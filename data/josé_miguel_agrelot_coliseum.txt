Puerto Rico Jos\xc3\xa9 Miguel Agrelot</b> (<a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>: <i>Jos\xc3\xa9 Miguel Agrelot Coliseum of Puerto Rico</i>) is the biggest indoor <a href="/wiki/Arena" title="Arena">arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> dedicated to entertainment. It is located at the <a href="/wiki/Milla_de_Oro" title="Milla de Oro">Golden Mile</a> of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, the island capital. It is usually referred by Puerto Ricans as the <i><b>Choliseo</b></i> or <i><b>Choli</b></i>, which is a <a href="/wiki/Portmanteau" class="mw-redirect" title="Portmanteau">portmanteau</a> of the words "Coliseo" and "Cholito", in reference to <i>Don Cholito</i>, one of <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Miguel_Agrelot" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Miguel Agrelot">Jos\xc3\xa9 Miguel Agrelot</a>\'s characters and Agrelot\'s own adopted nickname. It has consistently been ranked among the highest ticket-selling venues globally.<sup id="cite_ref-choliseo_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-choliseo-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-choliseo1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-choliseo1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n</p><p>The coliseum opened on September 4, 2004 after a prolonged construction financed by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Government of Puerto Rico">Government of Puerto Rico</a>. This venue is owned by the Puerto Rico Convention District Authority, a <a href="/wiki/Public_corporations_of_the_Government_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Public corporations of the Government of Puerto Rico">public corporation of Puerto Rico</a>, and managed by <a href="/wiki/ASM_Global" class="mw-redirect" title="ASM Global">ASM Global</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-smg_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smg-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It can accommodate up to 18,500 spectators and can be reached by the <a href="/wiki/Hato_Rey_(Tren_Urbano_station)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hato Rey (Tren Urbano station)">Hato Rey Station</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Tren_Urbano" title="Tren Urbano">Tren Urbano</a></i> system.\n</p><p>The arena hosted the first <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a> pay-per-view event outside the continental United States, Canada and the United Kingdom, since SummerSlam 1992 when <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Revolution_(2005)" title="New Year&#39;s Revolution (2005)">New Year\'s Revolution</a> was held there in 2005. On May 26, 2011, the arena was ranked 8th on the Top 50 Arena Venues of the world and second of the West Hemisphere in worldwide ticket sales by <a href="/wiki/Pollstar" title="Pollstar">Pollstar</a>. As of May 2013, the arena has received over 5 million spectators, hosting more than 600 events with a gross ticket revenue around $200 million.\n</p><p>After the hit from <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/2017_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2017 Atlantic hurricane season">2017 Atlantic hurricane season</a>, events after mid-September 2017 were cancelled. For a while, the Choliseo was used as a warehouse and recollection center by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Government of Puerto Rico">Government of Puerto Rico</a> to prepare and distribute food, water and basic necessities to those affected by the deadliest and costliest hurricane in Puerto Rican history. The arena resumed hosting events in March 2018.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_Miguel_Agrelot_Coliseum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Original_plans">Original plans</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_Miguel_Agrelot_Coliseum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Original plans"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Jose_Miguel_Agrelot_Coliseum.JPG" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Jose_Miguel_Agrelot_Coliseum.JPG/220px-Jose_Miguel_Agrelot_Coliseum.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="332" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Jose_Miguel_Agrelot_Coliseum.JPG/330px-Jose_Miguel_Agrelot_Coliseum.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/60/Jose_Miguel_Agrelot_Coliseum.JPG 2x" data-file-width="390" data-file-height="588" /></a><figcaption>Aerial view of the Jos\xc3\xa9 Miguel Agrelot Coliseum after construction.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The Coliseum was a project started during the administration of Governor <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Hern%C3%A1ndez_Col%C3%B3n" title="Rafael Hern\xc3\xa1ndez Col\xc3\xb3n">Rafael Hern\xc3\xa1ndez Col\xc3\xb3n</a>, initially as part of a <a href="/wiki/Bids_for_the_2004_Summer_Olympics" title="Bids for the 2004 Summer Olympics">bid to host the 2004 Olympic Games</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The facility was supposed to be constructed between the <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Clemente_Coliseum" title="Roberto Clemente Coliseum">Roberto Clemente Coliseum</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hiram_Bithorn_Stadium" title="Hiram Bithorn Stadium">Hiram Bithorn Stadium</a>, about a mile and a half away from the current arena site. The Coliseum would have been used for events such as Gymnastics and Olympic Basketball had the games been held in San Juan.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Controversy">Controversy</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_Miguel_Agrelot_Coliseum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Controversy"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>However, in 1997 the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a> selected <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a> to host the Olympics and the construction of the Coliseum was jeopardized. Recognizing the need for such a facility anyway, the government decided to continue the construction, this time at a site physically close to San Juan\'s financial district, the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Mile,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Golden Mile, Puerto Rico">Golden Mile</a>. Since the new site was adjacent to a future <a href="/wiki/Tren_Urbano" title="Tren Urbano">Tren Urbano</a> station, the Coliseum\'s planners decided not to incorporate parking facilities to the arena, as to induce visitors to use mass transportation to reach it.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Political opponents of <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Rossell%C3%B3" title="Pedro Rossell\xc3\xb3">Pedro Rossell\xc3\xb3</a>\'s administration raised various objections to the original plan, claiming that the lot of land purchased for the construction would benefit some of his political party\'s (<a href="/wiki/New_Progressive_Party_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="New Progressive Party of Puerto Rico">PNP</a>) heaviest financial contributors. They also objected to the lack of parking space, claiming that nearby private parking operators, supposedly also money donors to Rossell\xc3\xb3\'s party, would extort high parking fees from people who could not or would not use public transportation to the venue. At the time, the <a href="/wiki/Tren_Urbano" title="Tren Urbano">Tren Urbano</a> was in its initial stages of construction; by the time the facility opened it was still under construction. Objectors would also point out that a private operator would take control of the facility under contract from the government of Puerto Rico under what was perceived as a highly expensive contract. The Rossell\xc3\xb3 administration countered by stating that previous experience with public sports facilities in Puerto Rico, which would quickly fall into disrepair at an accelerated rate requiring constant remodeling, demanded that a private entity should manage the facility. The contract\'s expense would be justified by bringing in an operating partner with international experience managing world-class facilities, whose reputation would ensure that performers and sport events that had never been staged in Puerto Rico could visit the island.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The Coliseum\'s construction was stopped for close to two years during the administration of governor <a href="/wiki/Sila_Mar%C3%ADa_Calder%C3%B3n" title="Sila Mar\xc3\xada Calder\xc3\xb3n">Sila Mar\xc3\xada Calder\xc3\xb3n</a>, the leader of the opposition party (<a href="/wiki/Popular_Democratic_Party_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Popular Democratic Party of Puerto Rico">PPD</a>) whose administration followed Rossell\xc3\xb3\'s. She claimed that the Puerto Rican government had spent $242 million for a facility where only 42% of its scheduled project plan had been completed when she took office. The planners and constructors had overlooked the fact that a water pumping station next to the facility had been built over unstable land, and a portion of the building\'s foundations would have had to be redone because of this. There was even talk of demolishing what had been built and starting from scratch, which infuriated Rossell\xc3\xb3 supporters, who dismissed the suggestion as merely an excuse to spend more government money, this time to benefit the PPD\'s financial backers. Plans for a redesigned interior added during the Calder\xc3\xb3n administration added fuel to the intense public controversy.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Another controversy came about when <a href="/wiki/Banco_Popular_de_Puerto_Rico" title="Banco Popular de Puerto Rico">Banco Popular</a>\'s subsidiary Ticketpop was named the ticketing distributor through an exclusive contract, almost ignoring the bid by competitor TicketCenter. Ticketpop remained the sole ticketing distributor, from the venue\'s opening day until August 28, 2019 when rising competitor Ticketera was given the exclusive contract;<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Ticketpop\'s entire subsidiary operations were later sold to Ticketera in April 2021, whose founders also founded Ticketpop.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The original ticket vendor bidding controversy almost went to trial, as both parties argued over who would receive the contract, but the Government of Puerto Rico stood by their original contract with Ticketpop. Local producer <a href="/wiki/Angelo_Medina" title="Angelo Medina">Angelo Medina</a> spearheaded a group that wanted the legislature to curb the managing powers of <a href="/wiki/SMG_(property_management)" title="SMG (property management)">SMG</a> (before merging to ASM Global in 2019), recently-named to oversee operations and activities, because they feared that the company\'s relationship with non-local television companies would limit the participation of local talent in the arena.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The Coliseum was finally inaugurated on September 4, 2004. The first major show was <a href="/wiki/Van_Halen" title="Van Halen">Van Halen</a>, on September 13, 2004.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Dedication">Dedication</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_Miguel_Agrelot_Coliseum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Dedication"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Coliseo_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Coliseo_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg/220px-Coliseo_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="149" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Coliseo_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg/330px-Coliseo_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Coliseo_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg/440px-Coliseo_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="864" /></a><figcaption>The <i>Choliseo</i> from the northeast parking lot</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The naming of the structure caused a controversy by itself. During the construction phase, plans called for the coliseum to be named simply <i>El Coliseo de Puerto Rico</i> (The Coliseum of Puerto Rico). It was also suggested to have a private entity sponsor the facility and lend its name to it. Most local politicians objected to the idea, due to the <a href="/wiki/Enron_scandal" title="Enron scandal">2001 Enron financial scandal</a>, which made the company go bankrupt at a time when their corporate sponsorship named the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a>\' <a href="/wiki/Minute_Maid_Park" title="Minute Maid Park">home ballpark</a> as the Enron Field.\n</p><p>Some politicians argued over the naming the Coliseum after a Puerto Rican celebrity. Some of the names mentioned were that of pop star <a href="/wiki/Chayanne" title="Chayanne">Chayanne</a>, boxer <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9lix_Trinidad" title="F\xc3\xa9lix Trinidad">F\xc3\xa9lix "Tito" Trinidad</a> and actor <a href="/wiki/Ra%C3%BAl_Juli%C3%A1" class="mw-redirect" title="Ra\xc3\xbal Juli\xc3\xa1">Ra\xc3\xbal Juli\xc3\xa1</a>. Puerto Rican law demanded that no facility could be named after a living individual and there were talks of making an exception to the law should a living Puerto Rican icon be honored with the name. However, the name of beloved local comedian <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Miguel_Agrelot" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Miguel Agrelot">Jos\xc3\xa9 Miguel Agrelot</a> was chosen when the Coliseum was near its completion. Agrelot himself had suggested the name of <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Hern%C3%A1ndez_Mar%C3%ADn" title="Rafael Hern\xc3\xa1ndez Mar\xc3\xadn">Rafael Hern\xc3\xa1ndez Mar\xc3\xadn</a> to be used, and when someone suggested his name instead he was both surprised and concerned.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Parking_and_transportation">Parking and transportation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_Miguel_Agrelot_Coliseum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Parking and transportation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Coliseo_Jose_Miguel_Agrelot.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Coliseo_Jose_Miguel_Agrelot.jpg/220px-Coliseo_Jose_Miguel_Agrelot.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="141" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Coliseo_Jose_Miguel_Agrelot.jpg/330px-Coliseo_Jose_Miguel_Agrelot.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Coliseo_Jose_Miguel_Agrelot.jpg/440px-Coliseo_Jose_Miguel_Agrelot.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2237" data-file-height="1431" /></a><figcaption>Night view of the Coliseum of Puerto Rico from the <a href="/wiki/Hato_Rey_(Tren_Urbano_station)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hato Rey (Tren Urbano station)">Hato Rey Station</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Tren_Urbano" title="Tren Urbano">Tren Urbano</a></figcaption></figure>\n<p>A major public parking space or garage was not included in the planning or construction for the arena. Instead, only three parking lots were constructed, one for backstage entrance for authorized talent, production, equipment, props, etc., a medium-sized lot to the West entrance mostly for handicapped and the smallest lot, access-controlled, located across the box office and nearby the heavy rail station; privately-owned parking lots around the <i>Choliseo</i> can be found and charge a fee. The <a href="/wiki/Tren_Urbano" title="Tren Urbano">Tren Urbano</a> is by far the most used transportation mode to and from the <i>Choliseo</i>; the <a href="/wiki/Hato_Rey_(Tren_Urbano_station)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hato Rey (Tren Urbano station)">Hato Rey station</a>, located at a short walking distance, is the only station to extend operating hours after nighttime events end until all passengers using the system have exited at the station of their respective destinations.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Awards_and_recognitions">Awards and recognitions</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_Miguel_Agrelot_Coliseum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Awards and recognitions"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>On May 26, 2011, the <i>Coliseo</i> was ranked 8th on the Top 50 Arena Venues of the world and second of the West Hemisphere in worldwide ticket sales by <i><a href="/wiki/Pollstar" title="Pollstar">Pollstar</a></i>, surpassing the <a href="/wiki/Staples_Center" class="mw-redirect" title="Staples Center">Staples Center</a> in Los Angeles, the <a href="/wiki/American_Airlines_Arena" class="mw-redirect" title="American Airlines Arena">American Airlines Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Arena" title="Wembley Arena">Wembley Arena</a> in London, etc. In the first three months of 2011, the coliseum received an overwhelming number of people of over 205,000. During the first trimester, the coliseum was ranked 13 times in the 25 best arenas worldwide, according to <i>Pollstar</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> and <i>Venues Today</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Notable_events_held_at_the_venue">Notable events held at the venue</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_Miguel_Agrelot_Coliseum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Notable events held at the venue"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1236090951">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}@media print{body.ns-0 .mw-parser-output .hatnote{display:none!important}}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_events_at_Jos%C3%A9_Miguel_Agrelot_Coliseum" title="List of events at Jos\xc3\xa9 Miguel Agrelot Coliseum">List of events at Jos\xc3\xa9 Miguel Agrelot Coliseum</a></div>\n<p>The largest attendance record that the <i>Choliseo</i> has hosted was achieved on July 28, 2022, with <a href="/wiki/Bad_Bunny" title="Bad Bunny">Bad Bunny</a>\'s Un Verano Sin Ti. The singer established an all-time record of 18,749 spectators, 249 spectators over the venue\'s capacity, on the first night of his three-night performances, breaking a previous record that was also achieved by him in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-Record2022_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Record2022-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This was the first time a concert was fully broadcast live on national television in Puerto Rico, which was exclusively through the airwaves of <a href="/wiki/WKAQ-TV" title="WKAQ-TV">Telemundo Puerto Rico</a>. <a href="/wiki/Wisin_%26_Yandel" title="Wisin &amp; Yandel">Wisin &amp; Yandel</a> hold various records, some of which they have previously broken as well, including most performances at the <i>Choliseo</i>, by any artist, duo or group, increasing it to 39 and largest number of shows sold out in 24 hours. Their farewell concert tour, <a href="/wiki/La_%C3%9Altima_Misi%C3%B3n_World_Tour" title="La \xc3\x9altima Misi\xc3\xb3n World Tour">La \xc3\x9altima Misi\xc3\xb3n World Tour</a>, spun a 14-date run for the entire month of December 2022, a record previously held by <a href="/wiki/Daddy_Yankee" title="Daddy Yankee">Daddy Yankee</a>, who had a 12-date run, December 2019.\n</p>\n<div
