href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">Old San Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, stretching about 650 yards (600&#160;m) along the rocky <a Initially, the area was the site of a <a href="/wiki/Slaughterhouse" title="Slaughterhouse">slaughterhouse</a> because the law required the homes of slaves and homeless black and coloured servants &#8211; as well as <a href="/wiki/Cemetery" title="Cemetery">cemeteries</a> &#8211; to be established away from the main community center: in this case, outside the city walls. Perla is within two subbarrios, Mercado and San Cristobal</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Located inside the <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">Old San Juan Historic District</a>, <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">La Perla</i></span> (The Pearl) was originally the site of a slaughterhouse, called <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">El Matadero</i></span>.  It was built in the 18th century outside the walls of San Juan by slaves who were required to live outside the city. Slaves built homes and lived near the slaughterhouse and later <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">j\xc3\xadbaros</i></span>, (Puerto Rican farmers) who moved to San Juan, lived there.<sup id="cite_ref-Discover_PR_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Discover_PR-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>La Perla belongs to and consists of the northernmost stretches of  <a href="/wiki/Mercado,_Old_San_Juan" title="Mercado, Old San Juan">Mercado subbarrio</a> (west) and <a href="/wiki/San_Crist%C3%B3bal_(San_Juan_Antiguo)" class="mw-redirect" title="San Crist\xc3\xb3bal (San Juan Antiguo)">San Crist\xc3\xb3bal subbarrio</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Antiguo" title="San Juan Antiguo">San Juan Antiguo</a> <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Subdivisions of San Juan, Puerto Rico">barrio</a>. San Juan Antiguo barrio is located on <a href="/wiki/Isleta_de_San_Juan" class="mw-redirect" title="Isleta de San Juan">Isleta de San Juan</a>, an island off San Juan, the capital of Puerto Rico, and it is connected to the mainland by bridges and causeways. La Perla is in the Old San Juan Historic District which is managed by US <a href="/wiki/National_Park_Service" title="National Park Service">National Park Service</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NPS_2017_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NPS_2017-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>La Perla has sometimes been described as a slum and as a shantytown, because many <a href="/wiki/Informal_housing" title="Informal housing">homes have been built without proper permits</a>. While tourists have normally been deterred from visiting La Perla, some entrepreneurs have tried to change its image with art galleries, caf\xc3\xa9s and restaurants. <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">La Perla Impacto Comunitario</i></span>, a local community group has coordinated tours.<sup id="cite_ref-Discover_PR_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Discover_PR-1"><span Garbage pickup is done regularly in the neighborhood, which in 1973 consisted of about 900 houses and 3,300 residents.<sup id="cite_ref-Cordasco_Bucchioni_1973_p._109_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cordasco_Bucchioni_1973_p._109-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>La Perla has a community-oriented music recording studio, El Estudio D\' Oro, which serves as a free of cost music production workshop for all ages. Perla.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" id="Census">Census</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Perla,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit A population of 338 was reported, 198 housing units (29 unoccupied), and 169 households, on an area of 80,028 square yards (16.53 <a href="/wiki/Acre" title="Acre">acres</a>; 66,914 m\xc2\xb2).\n</p><p>La Perla belongs to and consists of the northernmost stretches of the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Subdivisions of San Juan, Puerto Rico">subbarrios</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Mercado,_Old_San_Juan" title="Mercado, Old San Juan">Mercado</a></i> (west) and <i><a href="/wiki/San_Crist%C3%B3bal_(San_Juan_Antiguo)" class="mw-redirect" title="San Crist\xc3\xb3bal (San Juan Antiguo)">San Crist\xc3\xb3bal</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Antiguo" title="San Juan Antiguo">San Juan Antiguo</a> barrio. The dividing line between the subbarrios is the imagined extension of <i>Calle San Justo</i> to the north beyond the old city wall to the Atlantic coast. The eastern part consists of Census blocks 3002, 3009 und 3010, with a population of 64, 35 housing units (4 unoccupied), and 31 households, on  41,348 square yards (8.5 <a href="/wiki/Acre" title="Acre">acres</a>; 34,572 m\xc2\xb2).\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Media_and_popular_culture">Media and popular culture</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Perla,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Media and popular culture"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Building_in_La_Perla,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Building_in_La_Perla%2C_San_Juan%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg/220px-Building_in_La_Perla%2C_San_Juan%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Building_in_La_Perla%2C_San_Juan%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg/330px-Building_in_La_Perla%2C_San_Juan%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Building_in_La_Perla%2C_San_Juan%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg/440px-Building_in_La_Perla%2C_San_Juan%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="800" /></a><figcaption>Building in La Perla, San Juan, Puerto Rico</figcaption></figure>\n<p>La Perla was the true site of the fictional "La Esmeralda" barrio depicted in <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Lewis" title="Oscar Lewis">Oscar Lewis</a>\'s sociological work "La vida: a Puerto Rican family in the culture of poverty--San Juan and New York", describing the lives of Puerto Rican slumdwellers and prostitutes. From La Perla, through taped interviews with dozens of intertwined family members who lived there in the 1940s-1960s, Lewis wrote an award-winning and controversial book. It was controversial because his award-winning book, written in 1967, would influence the way people viewed Puerto Rican women and Puerto Ricans, in general. "Many who read his book assumed the <a href="/wiki/Whores" class="mw-redirect" title="Whores">whores</a> of La Perla were typical of all Puerto Rican women."<sup id="cite_ref-Steiner_1974_p._17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Steiner_1974_p.-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1978, salsa singer <a href="/wiki/Ismael_Rivera" title="Ismael Rivera">Ismael Rivera</a> had a hit song, written by composer <a href="/wiki/Catalino_Curet_Alonso" class="mw-redirect" title="Catalino Curet Alonso">Catalino Curet Alonso</a>, in honor of this community, and named after it. In 2009 Urban Music group <a href="/wiki/Calle_13_(band)" title="Calle 13 (band)">Calle 13</a> released another tribute song, also  named <i>La Perla</i>, in collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Ruben_Blades" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruben Blades">Ruben Blades</a>. In the song, Blades references Rivera\'s early effort; in the song\'s video, Blades pays his respects to Curet by visiting his tomb at the community\'s Santa Mar\xc3\xada Madgalena de Pazzis cemetery.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Canadians" title="Canadians">Canadian</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Nelly_Furtado" title="Nelly Furtado">Nelly Furtado</a> along with Calle 13 band member <a href="/wiki/Residente" title="Residente">Residente</a> filmed the music video to the remix of Furtado\'s song <i><a href="/wiki/No_Hay_Igual" title="No Hay Igual">No Hay Igual</a></i>  on 26 June 2006 in La Perla. Residente said he hoped that the video would help them both reach new audiences: "It\'s a good opportunity for us both. More than the North American market, the European market really interests me and her because her family is Portuguese."<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The video was directed by <a href="/wiki/Israel_Lugo" title="Israel Lugo">Israel Lugo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Coss" title="Gabriel Coss">Gabriel Coss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Director_of_photography" class="mw-redirect" title="Director of photography">photographed</a> by Sonnel Velazquez and produced by Mar\xc3\xada Estades.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Giannina_Braschi" title="Giannina Braschi">Giannina Braschi</a>\'s political allegory <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_Banana" title="United States of Banana">United States of Banana</a> (2011), the Puerto Rican prisoner Segismundo is born in the dungeon of the Statue of Liberty as the illegitimate child of a woman from La Perla and the Governor of Puerto Rico <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Mar%C3%ADn" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The Puerto Rican classic play, <a href="/wiki/La_Carreta" title="La Carreta">La Carreta</a>, (1953) by <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Marqu%C3%A9s" title="Ren\xc3\xa9 Marqu\xc3\xa9s">Ren\xc3\xa9 Marqu\xc3\xa9s</a>, takes place in La Perla. A poor family moves from their farm to La Perla, and then to the <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">Bronx</a> for the better life that they never quite find.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The motion picture <a href="/wiki/The_Vessel_(film)" title="The Vessel (film)">The Vessel (film)</a>, starring <a href="/wiki/Martin_Sheen" title="Martin Sheen">Martin Sheen</a>, about a tidal wave that destroys a small town was filmed in La Perla in 2013.\n</p><p>With more than 8.3 billion views on YouTube, La Perla became world-known with the music video for the song <a href="/wiki/Despacito" title="Despacito">Despacito</a> being filmed there.<sup id="cite_ref-npr_year_end_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-npr_year_end-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup
