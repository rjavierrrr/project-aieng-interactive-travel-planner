former 2265-acre sugarcane plantation located in the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Manat%C3%AD" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Manat\xc3\xad">Manat\xc3\xad river valley</a> in the municipality of <a It remained operational from 1830 to 1880.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpdoc_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpdoc-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span 1873. The first mill on the plantation produced 100-150 tons of raw sugar every harvest. A second mill is estimated to have generated 200 tons while a third mill produced between 500-600 tons.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Two steam engines were purchased to mechanize sugar production at the hacienda: one in 1841 and another in 1861. The steam <a href="/wiki/Trapiche" title="Trapiche">trapiche</a> purchased in 1861 remains on the property making it the last known engine of its kind that remains preserved.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The <i>Hacienda La Esperanza</i> steam engine has been designated a <a href="/wiki/National_Historic_Mechanical_Engineering_Landmark" class="mw-redirect" title="National Historic Mechanical Engineering Landmark">National Historic Mechanical Engineering Landmark</a>. Today the entire property is owned by the <a href="/wiki/Conservation_Trust_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Conservation Trust of Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico Conservation Trust</a> (FCPR, for the initials in Spanish) which preserves and protects it, the property includes some of the only coastal forest left in the region. The organization <i>Para La Naturaleza</i> manages visitation to the historic site in addition to educational and cultural activities and tours into the surrounding nature reserve.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Owner">Owner</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hacienda_Azucarera_la_Esperanza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Owner"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Ramon_Fern%C3%A1ndez" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Ramon Fern\xc3\xa1ndez">Jos\xc3\xa9 Ramon Fern\xc3\xa1ndez</a> (1808\xe2\x80\x931883), an influential, conservative politician and businessman, owned Hacienda Azucarera la Esperanza. Ramon was named <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Marqu\xc3\xa9s de la Esperanza</i></span> by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> during a tumultuous time in Puerto Rico. The crisis allowed Ramon to purchase more land and expand the Hacienda.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Ramon, the 1st Marquis of La Esperanza was the wealthiest sugar baron in Puerto Rico in the 19th century. He was also one of the most powerful men of the entire <a href="/wiki/Spanish_West_Indies" title="Spanish West Indies">Spanish Caribbean</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Torres_2012_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Torres_2012-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></p><div style="clear:both;" class=""></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Hacienda_La_Esperanza_Nature_Reserve">Hacienda La Esperanza Nature Reserve</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hacienda_Azucarera_la_Esperanza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hacienda La Esperanza Nature Reserve"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p><b>Hacienda La Esperanza Nature Reserve</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Reserva Natural Hacienda La Esperanza</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Nature_reserve" title="Nature reserve">nature reserve</a> that preserves the grasslands and wetlands of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Manat%C3%AD" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Manat\xc3\xad">Manat\xc3\xad</a> <a href="/wiki/River_delta" title="River delta">river delta</a> in addition to coastal <a href="/wiki/Mogote" title="Mogote">karst hills</a> and cliffs, and numerous scenic beaches such as La Esperanza, Las Marias and Tombolo Beaches.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The 2,150-acre protected area surrounds the historic sugarcane plantation, while the historic manor house of Hacienda La Esperanza serves as the visitor center for the nature reserve.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The organization <i>Para La Naturaleza</i>, a nonprofit unit off the FCPR, manages and provides visitor services, volunteer activities and educational events in the nature reserve.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Ecology">Ecology</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hacienda_Azucarera_la_Esperanza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ecology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The nature reserve protects important ecosystems and several endangered and endemic species such as the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_boa" title="Puerto Rican boa">Puerto Rican boa</a> which can be found in the nearby mogotes.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <i>Para La Naturaleza</i> also promotes <a href="/wiki/Citizen_science" title="Citizen science">citizen science</a> and organizes related activities such as bird counting.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span
