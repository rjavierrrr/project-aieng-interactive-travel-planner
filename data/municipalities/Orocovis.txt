data-file-height="377" /></a><figcaption>Near the main plaza, 1941</figcaption></figure>\n<p><b>Orocovis</b> (from the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Taino</a> chief <a href="/wiki/Orocobix" title="Orocobix">Orocobix</a>) is a town and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> located in the center of the island. Founded by Juan Rivera de Santiago in 1825. Orocovis is spread over 17 barrios. It is part of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area">San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>. It\'s located north of <a href="/wiki/Villalba,_Puerto_Rico" title="Villalba, Puerto Rico">Villalba</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coamo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Coamo, Puerto Rico">Coamo</a>; south of <a href="/wiki/Morovis,_Puerto_Rico" title="Morovis, Puerto Rico">Morovis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Corozal,_Puerto_Rico" title="Corozal, Puerto Rico">Corozal</a>; southeast of <a href="/wiki/Ciales,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ciales, Puerto Rico">Ciales</a>; east of <a href="/wiki/Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico" title="Jayuya, Puerto Rico">Jayuya</a>; and west of <a href="/wiki/Barranquitas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Barranquitas, Puerto Rico">Barranquitas</a>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Orocovis,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Since before the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_colonization" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish colonization">Spanish colonization</a> in the <a href="/wiki/16th_century" title="16th century">16th century</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Ta\xc3\xadno people">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> were already established in the region. They were led by the <a href="/wiki/Cacique" title="Cacique">cacique</a> <a href="/wiki/Orocobix" title="Orocobix">Orocobix</a> and his tribe known as the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jatibonicu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jatibonicu (page does not exist)">Jatibonicu</a>. After <a href="/wiki/Spaniard" class="mw-redirect" title="Spaniard">Spaniards</a> settled in the island, the region was part of the south of Manat\xc3\xad and the north region of Coamo. By 1823 <b>Orocovis</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Barrio" title="Barrio">barrio</a> of <a href="/wiki/Barranquitas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Barranquitas, Puerto Rico">Barranquitas</a> while Morovis (previously part of Manat\xc3\xad) had a barrio called <b>Barros</b>.  Both Orocovis and Barros were eventually united to establish a new town.\n</p><p>In early 1825, <b>Juan Rivera de Santiago</b> sought and obtained legal power by the neighbors of the region to request the <a href="/wiki/Governor" title="Governor">Governor</a> authorization to found a <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> in the Barros area, where they had bought 14 <a href="/wiki/Acre" title="Acre">acres</a> of land from Eulalia (Olaya) de Rivera Melendez, who donated an additional acre to establish several municipal works. However, due to the distance from bodies of water, the settlement was moved to another place where it is today. On November 10, 1825, Governor <a href="/wiki/Miguel_de_la_Torre" title="Miguel de la Torre">Miguel de la Torre</a> granted permission to found the new municipality of Barros.\n</p><p>In 1838, the <a href="/wiki/Parish" title="Parish">parish</a> of <i>San Juan Bautista de Barros</i> was created and was blessed and inaugurated on October 29 of the same year. However, in 1875, a fire destroyed the church, the King\'s House, the priest\'s house and several homes.\n</p><p>Most of Orocovis nicknames stem from its location in the center of the island. Some of them are: "<i>Coraz\xc3\xb3n de Puerto Rico</i>" (Heart of Puerto Rico) and "<i>Centro Geogr\xc3\xa1fico de Puerto Rico</i>" (Geographical Center of Puerto Rico). Locals say the center of Puerto Rico is in a barrio called <a href="/wiki/Pellejas,_Orocovis,_Puerto_Rico" title="Pellejas, Orocovis, Puerto Rico">Pellejas</a>, on <a href="/w/index.php?title=PR-566&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="PR-566 (page does not exist)">Road 566</a>, Km 5.7 in Orocovis. That the center of Puerto Rico is in front of the Catholic church was determined to be false by engineers from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Mayag%C3%BCez" title="University of Puerto Rico at Mayag\xc3\xbcez">University of Puerto Rico, Mayag\xc3\xbcez Campus</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Puerto Rico was ceded by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_of_1898" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Paris of 1898">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became a territory of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. In 1899, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_War" title="United States Department of War">United States Department of War</a> conducted a <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of Puerto Rico finding that the population of Barros, as Orocovis was then called, was 14,845.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1928, the Legislative Assembly of Puerto Rico approved a resolution to change the name of Barros to <b>Orocovis</b>, to honor the Ta\xc3\xadno cacique of the same name, who lived in that region.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Orocovis,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The municipality Orocovis is mountainous with many valleys. Most of the Orocovis terrain runs through the <a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Cordillera Central, Puerto Rico">Cordillera Central</a> of the island. Most of the high peaks are in the southern region of the municipality within the limits of the municipality with <a href="/wiki/Villalba,_Puerto_Rico" title="Villalba, Puerto Rico">Villalba</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coamo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Coamo, Puerto Rico">Coamo</a>. The highest peak of the town is Cerro Mogote.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The rivers <a href="/wiki/Toro_Negro_River" title="Toro Negro River">Toro Negro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sana_Muerto_River" title="Sana Muerto River">Sana Muertos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orocovis_River" title="Orocovis River">Orocovis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Matrullas_River" title="Matrullas River">Matrullas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bauta_River" title="Bauta River">Bauta</a>, and the creeks Do\xc3\xb1a Juana, Palmar and Cacaos are among the water bodies in the town. There are two lakes: <a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Guineo_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="El Guineo Lake (page does not exist)">El Guineo</a>, a reservoir of the Toro Negro River, and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Matrullas_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Matrullas Lake (page does not exist)">Matrullas Lake</a> formed by the Bauta River. El Guineo is Puerto Rico\'s highest lake.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Barrios">Barrios</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Orocovis,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Barrios"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Orocovis,_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Orocovis%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/220px-Orocovis%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="177" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Orocovis%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/330px-Orocovis%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Orocovis%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/440px-Orocovis%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png 2x" data-file-width="1103" data-file-height="886" /></a><figcaption>Subdivisions of Orocovis.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Like all municipalities of Puerto Rico, Orocovis is subdivided into <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico">barrios</a>. id="Government">Government</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Orocovis,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit Rico</a></div>\n<p>All municipalities in Puerto Rico are administered by a mayor, elected every four years. <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_Col%C3%B3n_Berlingeri" title="Jes\xc3\xbas Col\xc3\xb3n Berlingeri">Jes\xc3\xbas Col\xc3\xb3n Berlingeri</a> (of the <a href="/wiki/New_Progressive_Party_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="New Progressive Party of Puerto Rico">New Progressive Party</a>) has served as mayor since 1998.\n</p><p>The city belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Senatorial_district_VI" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Senatorial district VI">Puerto Rico Senatorial district VI</a>, which is represented by two senators. In 2012, <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Pereira_Castillo" title="Miguel Pereira Castillo">Miguel Pereira Castillo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Angel_M._Rodr%C3%ADguez" class="mw-redirect" title="Angel M. Rodr\xc3\xadguez">Angel M. Rodr\xc3\xadguez</a> were elected as district senators.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Education">Education</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Orocovis,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Education"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Orocovis has several public and private schools and public education is administered by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Education" title="Puerto Rico Department of Education">Puerto Rico Department of Education</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Transportation">Transportation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Orocovis,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Transportation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>There is no direct <a href="/wiki/Highway" title="Highway">highway</a> connection to Orocovis. Roads <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_137" title="Puerto Rico Highway 137">#137</a> and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_155" title="Puerto Rico Highway 155">#155</a> lead from the north, while roads <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_138" title="Puerto Rico Highway 138">#138</a> and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_155" title="Puerto Rico Highway 155">#155</a> lead from the south. Orocovis lies at about an hour and a half from <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, the capital of Puerto Rico.\n</p><p>There are 17 bridges in Orocovis.<sup id="cite_ref-bridge_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bridge-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Symbols">Symbols</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Orocovis,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Symbols"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">municipio</i></span> has an official flag and coat of arms.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flag_of_Orocovis">Flag of Orocovis</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Orocovis,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Flag of Orocovis"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Flag of Orocovis is formed by five vertical stripes: Two green stripes at the ends, representing the territories of <a href="/wiki/Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico" title="Aibonito, Puerto Rico">Aibonito</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barranquitas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Barranquitas, Puerto Rico">Barranquitas</a>; and a blue one in the center representing the regional domain of the cacique <a href="/wiki/Orocobix" title="Orocobix">Orocobix</a> prior to the Spanish colonization. These stripes are divided by two narrower white stripes, representing the territories granted by Barranquitas and <a href="/wiki/Morovis,_Puerto_Rico" title="Morovis, Puerto Rico">Morovis</a> to form the municipality in 1825.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Over the blue portion there\'s an <a href="/wiki/Oval" title="Oval">oval</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a>. The Sun has seventeen rays that represent the different regions of the town. The Sun, being the center of the <a href="/wiki/Solar_System" title="Solar System">Solar System</a>, represents Orocovis as the center of the island of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Orocovis,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The oval is symbol of the letter "<a href="/wiki/O" title="O">O</a>", initial of the name of the town. In each quadrant appear representative elements of the town. In the left superior quadrant a Ta\xc3\xadno Indian bust, symbol of the great "Chief Orocobix" and the Indian heritage of the town, silhouetted in gold on a silver-plated background. The Indian bust faces to the star or sun that appears in the superior right quadrant, which is in gold on a blue background and represents the territorial municipality with its seventeen rays or demarcations. In the inferior right quadrant, a <a href="/wiki/Plantain_(cooking)" class="mw-redirect" title="Plantain (cooking)">plantain</a> plant is observed representing the <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a>, silhouetted in gold on a silver-plated background. A bridge also silhouetted in gold on a blue background, above the river appears in the left inferior quadrant.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div
