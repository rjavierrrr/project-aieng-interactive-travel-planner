.IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;\xc9\xa1u\xcb\x88\xc9\xbea\xce\xb2o&#93;</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Gurabo_barrio-pueblo" title="Gurabo barrio-pueblo">town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> in eastern <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. It is located in the central eastern region, north of <a href="/wiki/San_Lorenzo,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Lorenzo, Puerto Rico">San Lorenzo</a>; south of <a href="/wiki/Trujillo_Alto,_Puerto_Rico" title="Trujillo Alto, Puerto Rico">Trujillo Alto</a>; east of <a href="/wiki/Caguas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Caguas, Puerto Rico">Caguas</a>; and west of <a href="/wiki/Carolina,_Puerto_Rico" title="Carolina, Puerto Rico">Carolina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juncos,_Puerto_Rico" title="Juncos, Puerto Rico">Juncos</a>. Gurabo is spread over 9 barrios and <a href="/wiki/Gurabo_barrio-pueblo" title="Gurabo barrio-pueblo">Gurabo Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and the administrative center of the city). It is part of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area">San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurabo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Gurabo\'s history dates as far back as the 17th century, when it was actually part of <a href="/wiki/Caguas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Caguas, Puerto Rico">Caguas</a>. Then, the area was known as <i>Burabo</i>. By 1700, transportation, medical and economic troubles were crippling the population of the <i>Burabo</i> area; traveling to Caguas\' center for business and medical help was not easy and took hours. This led many of <i>Burabo\'</i>s citizens to seek autonomy for the area.\n</p><p>It would be long, however, before Gurabo was separated from Caguas. The separation movement was brought forward by an 1812 meeting of all 168 family leaders in Gurabo, who decided to have <a href="/w/index.php?title=Luis_del_Carmen_Echevarr%C3%ADa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Luis del Carmen Echevarr\xc3\xada (page does not exist)">Luis del Carmen Echevarr\xc3\xada</a> lead them in their quest for independence, based on the large number of residents in <i>Burabo</i>.\n</p><p>In 1815, Gurabo became a municipality. In 1822, the first <a href="/wiki/Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholic">Catholic</a> church in town was erected. In 1903, the first <a href="/wiki/Baptist_church" class="mw-redirect" title="Baptist church">Baptist church</a> opened its doors in Gurabo town.\n</p><p>Gurabo is also known as "<i>La Ciudad de las Escaleras</i>", or the "City of Stairs". Located in the town center district of <a href="/wiki/El_Cerro_(Gurabo,_Puerto_Rico)" title="El Cerro (Gurabo, Puerto Rico)">El Cerro</a>, the stairs (<a href="/wiki/Step_street" title="Step street">step streets</a>) are about twenty-two floors high, and they are painted in bright colors.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Gurabo\'s mayor is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosachely_Rivera_Santana&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rosachely Rivera Santana (page does not exist)">Rosachely Rivera Santana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PH_2018_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PH_2018-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The significant amount of rainfall from <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> triggered numerous landslides in Gurabo on September 20, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Many people had to be rescued from floods and 489 homes were destroyed.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading Rico</a></div>\n<p>Like all municipalities in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, Gurabo is administered by a mayor. The current mayor is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosachely_Rivera&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rosachely Rivera (page does not exist)">Rosachely Rivera</a>, from the <a href="/wiki/New_Progressive_Party_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="New Progressive Party of Puerto Rico">New Progressive Party</a> (PNP). Rivera was elected at the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gurabo_mayor_special_election,_2017&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gurabo mayor special election, 2017 (page does not exist)">Gurabo mayor special election, 2017</a>.\n</p><p>The city belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Senatorial_district_VII" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Senatorial district VII">Puerto Rico Senatorial district VII</a>, which is represented by two Senators. In 2016, <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Laureano" title="Miguel Laureano">Miguel Laureano</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Luis_Dalmau" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Luis Dalmau">Jos\xc3\xa9 Luis Dalmau</a> were elected as District Senators.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div
