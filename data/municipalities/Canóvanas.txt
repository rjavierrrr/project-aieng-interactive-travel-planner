.references .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;ka\xcb\x88no\xce\xb2anas&#93;</a></span>, <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1177148991"><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">locally</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;ka\xcb\x88no\xce\xb2ana\xca\x94&#93;</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Can%C3%B3vanas_barrio-pueblo" title="Can\xc3\xb3vanas barrio-pueblo">town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, located in the northeastern region, north of <a href="/wiki/Juncos,_Puerto_Rico" title="Juncos, Puerto Rico">Juncos</a> and <a href="/wiki/Las_Piedras,_Puerto_Rico" title="Las Piedras, Puerto Rico">Las Piedras</a>; south of <a href="/wiki/Lo%C3%ADza,_Puerto_Rico" title="Lo\xc3\xadza, Puerto Rico">Lo\xc3\xadza</a>; east of <a href="/wiki/Carolina,_Puerto_Rico" title="Carolina, Puerto Rico">Carolina</a>; and west of <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande,_Puerto_Rico" title="R\xc3\xado Grande, Puerto Rico">R\xc3\xado Grande</a>. Can\xc3\xb3vanas is spread over 6 barrios and <a href="/wiki/Can%C3%B3vanas_barrio-pueblo" title="Can\xc3\xb3vanas barrio-pueblo">Can\xc3\xb3vanas Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and administrative center). It is part of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area">San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Smithsonian_1901_map_of_Puerto_Rico_caciques.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Smithsonian_1901_map_of_Puerto_Rico_caciques.png/220px-Smithsonian_1901_map_of_Puerto_Rico_caciques.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="172" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Smithsonian_1901_map_of_Puerto_Rico_caciques.png/330px-Smithsonian_1901_map_of_Puerto_Rico_caciques.png 1.5x, data-file-height="410" /></a><figcaption>Early 20th c. map published by the Smithsonian Institution Bureau of American Ethnology showing the <i>Cayniabon</i> area under Chief Canobana</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The region of what is now Can\xc3\xb3vanas belonged to the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> region of <i>Cayniab\xc3\xb3n</i>, also the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno_language" title="Ta\xc3\xadno language">native name</a> of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Lo%C3%ADza" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza">Grande de Loiza River</a>, which stretched from the central eastern region of Puerto Rico to the northeast coast of the island.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The region was led by cacique <i>Canoban\xc3\xa1</i>, from which the actual name is derived, in the south half, and female Cacica <i>Loaiza</i> in the north (mostly modern day <a href="/wiki/Lo%C3%ADza,_Puerto_Rico" title="Lo\xc3\xadza, Puerto Rico">Lo\xc3\xadza</a>). During the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Spanish colonization of the Americas">Spanish colonization</a>, the region of Can\xc3\xb3vanas was granted to Miguel D\xc3\xadaz, who turned the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> yucayeque into a ranch. It is said that Can\xc3\xb3bana, along with Loaiza, were supporters of the Spanish regime and did not join the <a href="/wiki/Taino_rebellion_of_1511" class="mw-redirect" title="Taino rebellion of 1511">Taino rebellion of 1511</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-enciclopedia_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-enciclopedia-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Can\xc3\xb3vanas was a <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico"><i>barrio</i></a> (district) of the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Lo%C3%ADza,_Puerto_Rico" title="Lo\xc3\xadza, Puerto Rico">Lo\xc3\xadza</a> for over 400 years. In 1902, the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Legislative Assembly of Puerto Rico">Legislative Assembly of Puerto Rico</a> approved a law for the consolidation of certain municipalities. As a result, both Can\xc3\xb3vanas and Lo\xc3\xadza were incorporated into the municipality of <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande,_Puerto_Rico" title="R\xc3\xado Grande, Puerto Rico">R\xc3\xado Grande</a>. However, in 1905 a new law revoked the previous one, returning Can\xc3\xb3vanas to its previous state of <i>barrio</i> of Lo\xc3\xadza.<sup id="cite_ref-psh_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-psh-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1909, the Municipal administration of Lo\xc3\xadza was transferred to the barrio of Can\xc3\xb3vanas, which was more developed than the Lo\xc3\xadza region. Also, the construction of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_3" title="Puerto Rico Highway 3">PR-3</a> facilitated the communication with Can\xc3\xb3vanas. As a result of the transfer, land was acquired to build a new <a href="/wiki/City_hall" class="mw-redirect" title="City hall">city hall</a>, a town square, a <a href="/wiki/Slaughterhouse" title="Slaughterhouse">slaughterhouse</a>, and a cemetery. A 20-acre (8.1&#160;ha) plot of land was purchased by Don Luis Hernaiz Veronne, a townhall senator and local farmer. The site location was strategic, to intercept traffic from the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_3" title="Puerto Rico Highway 3">PR-3</a>, and from other nearby roads like the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_185" title="Puerto Rico Highway 185">PR-185</a>.\n</p><p>However, the transfer was not well received by the residents of the original town of Lo\xc3\xadza, then renamed <b>Loiza Aldea</b>. It was not until a law was passed on June 30, 1969, that both municipalities were recognized as having "clearly different population nuclei" recommending the establishment of two separate municipalities. The change was approved in 1970 by Governor <a href="/wiki/Luis_A._Ferr%C3%A9" title="Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9">Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-psh_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-psh-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Like other nearby towns, the proximity of Can\xc3\xb3vanas to the capital, <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, has allowed extraordinary urban and commercial development in the region. The municipality became renown in the 1990s for popularizing the urban legend of the <a href="/wiki/Chupacabra" title="Chupacabra">chupacabras</a>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> on September 20, 2017, triggered numerous landslides,<sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and caused catastrophic damages in Can\xc3\xb3vanas. Five cases of <a href="/wiki/Leptospirosis" title="Leptospirosis">Leptospirosis</a> were reported in Can\xc3\xb3vanas.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span href="/w/index.php?title=Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Can\xc3\xb3vanas sits on the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Northern_Coastal_Plain_(Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Northern Coastal Plain (Puerto Rico) (page does not exist)">Northern Coastal Plain</a> region of Puerto Rico. It is bordered by the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Lo%C3%ADza,_Puerto_Rico" title="Lo\xc3\xadza, Puerto Rico">Lo\xc3\xadza</a>, <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande,_Puerto_Rico" title="R\xc3\xado Grande, Puerto Rico">R\xc3\xado Grande</a>, <a href="/wiki/Las_Piedras,_Puerto_Rico" title="Las Piedras, Puerto Rico">Las Piedras</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juncos,_Puerto_Rico" title="Juncos, Puerto Rico">Juncos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gurabo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Gurabo, Puerto Rico">Gurabo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Carolina,_Puerto_Rico" title="Carolina, Puerto Rico">Carolina</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-psh_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-psh-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Can\xc3\xb3vanas covers only 28 square miles (72.8&#160;km<sup>2</sup>).<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Can\xc3\xb3vanas combines flat <a href="/wiki/Alluvial_plain" title="Alluvial plain">alluvial plains</a> in the center and north, areas with both gentle hills and rugged, deeply dissected mountainous areas made up of <a href="/wiki/Volcaniclastic" class="mw-redirect" title="Volcaniclastic">volcaniclastic</a> rocks (<a href="/wiki/Lava" title="Lava">lava</a> flows and exposed intrusive <a href="/wiki/Igneous_rock" title="Igneous rock">igneous rocks</a>) to the southeast and south. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cuchilla_de_Santa_In%C3%A9s&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cuchilla de Santa In\xc3\xa9s (page does not exist)">Cuchilla de Santa In\xc3\xa9s</a>, a karstic limestone hill (<a href="/wiki/Mogote" title="Mogote">mogote</a>) with an elevation of 328 feet, rises from coastal sediments on the northeast of the municipality near San Isidro, while the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cuchilla_El_Asomante&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cuchilla El Asomante (page does not exist)">Cuchilla El Asomante</a> lies at the south with elevations that range from 656 to 2,296 feet.\n</p><p>On the southeast, Can\xc3\xb3vanas features portions of the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_de_Luquillo" title="Sierra de Luquillo">Sierra de Luquillo</a> mountain range, with the Cerro El Negro being the tallest peak in the municipality at 2,592 feet. Other notable peaks are La Peregrina (1,903 feet) and Pitahaya (951 feet), both located at Barrio Hato Puerco.<sup id="cite_ref-enciclopedia_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-enciclopedia-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Water_features">Water features</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Water features"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Much of the flat plains are part of the flood-prone alluvial valley of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Lo%C3%ADza" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza">R\xc3\xado Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza</a> and its main tributaries, the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Can%C3%B3vanas" class="mw-redirect" title="R\xc3\xado Can\xc3\xb3vanas">R\xc3\xado Can\xc3\xb3vanas</a> and <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Canovanillas" class="mw-redirect" title="R\xc3\xado Canovanillas">R\xc3\xado Canovanillas</a>. <a href="/wiki/Flood" title="Flood">Floods</a> are typical during the storm season, between June and November. Also, 48.6% of the population identified themselves as males, and 51.4% as females. Finally, 26.7% of the population is under 18 years old. The next biggest percentage of population (21.5%) is between 35 and 49 years old.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Tourism">Tourism</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Tourism"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Although Can\xc3\xb3vanas is not particularly known for its touristic importance, there are several landmarks and places of interest to visit. The <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_T._Pi%C3%B1ero_House" title="Jes\xc3\xbas T. Pi\xc3\xb1ero House">Jes\xc3\xbas T. Pi\xc3\xb1ero House</a> is located along the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_3" title="Puerto Rico Highway 3">PR-3</a>. The residence, which was built around 1931, houses a museum dedicated to the life of <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_T._Pi%C3%B1ero" title="Jes\xc3\xbas T. Pi\xc3\xb1ero">Jes\xc3\xbas T. Pi\xc3\xb1ero</a>, first Puerto Rican governor of the island.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Hip%C3%B3dromo_Camarero" title="Hip\xc3\xb3dromo Camarero">Hip\xc3\xb3dromo Camarero</a> is also a tourist attraction for horserace enthusiasts of the island and the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Other places of interest are the ruins of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_Can%C3%B3vanas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Central Can\xc3\xb3vanas (page does not exist)">Can\xc3\xb3vanas Sugar Mill</a>, El Espa\xc3\xb1ol Bridge, the <a href="/wiki/Ceiba" title="Ceiba">Old Ceiba Tree</a>, and Villar\xc3\xa1n Park, which features the historic <a href="/wiki/Villar%C3%A1n_Bridge" title="Villar\xc3\xa1n Bridge">Villar\xc3\xa1n Bridge</a>, the only one of its kind in Puerto Rico and the United States.\n</p><p>To stimulate local tourism, the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Tourism_Company" title="Puerto Rico Tourism Company">Puerto Rico Tourism Company</a> launched the <i>Voy Turistiendo</i> ("I\'m Touring") campaign, with a passport book and website. The Can\xc3\xb3vanas page lists <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Pico el Toro</i></span>, <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">El Hip\xc3\xb3dromo</i></span> and the <a href="/wiki/Can%C3%B3vanas_barrio-pueblo" title="Can\xc3\xb3vanas barrio-pueblo">Pueblo</a> with its historic architecture, as places of interest.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Economy">Economy</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Economy"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Agriculture">Agriculture</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Agriculture"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The economy of Can\xc3\xb3vanas has traditionally relied on agriculture, primarily <a href="/wiki/Sugarcane" title="Sugarcane">sugarcane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coffea_arabica" title="Coffea arabica">coffee</a>. There was an important <a href="/wiki/Sugar_mill" class="mw-redirect" title="Sugar mill">sugar mill</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_185" title="Puerto Rico Highway 185">PR-951</a> from Can\xc3\xb3vanas to Lo\xc3\xadza. It belonged to <i>Lo\xc3\xadza Sugar Company</i>, and then to <i>Fajardo Sugar Company</i>. However, the mill closed in 1965.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1999, the structure was declared of historical importance by the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Legislative Assembly of Puerto Rico">Legislative Assembly of Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> There\'s also growth of minor vegetables and fruits, as well as a minor cattle and poultry industry. Most of Can\xc3\xb3vanas flat areas are subject to occasional flooding and are used as pastures for cattle.\n</p><p><i>Best Iguana Puerto Rico Meat</i> in Can\xc3\xb3vanas is the only company in Puerto Rico certified for processing, packaging and distributing <a href="/wiki/Iguana_meat" title="Iguana meat">iguana meat</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Green_iguana" title="Green iguana">green iguana</a> is an <a href="/wiki/Invasive_species_in_the_United_States" title="Invasive species in the United States">invasive species</a> of Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Commerce">Commerce</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Commerce"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In recent years, Can\xc3\xb3vanas economy has shifted to commerce and industry, supplemented by the production of fresh milk. There has also been an increase in retail businesses. There are three main shopping malls, located along the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_3" title="Puerto Rico Highway 3">PR-3</a> in the Can\xc3\xb3vanas region. These malls such as <a href="/wiki/The_Outlet_66_Mall" title="The Outlet 66 Mall">The Outlet 66 Mall</a> are the location of main stores like <a href="/wiki/Wal-Mart" class="mw-redirect" title="Wal-Mart">Wal-Mart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marshalls" title="Marshalls">Marshalls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burlington_(department_store)" title="Burlington (department store)">Burlington</a>, and others.\n</p><p>Can\xc3\xb3vanas is referred to as "The Door to the East" due to its location on the eastern edge of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Metropolitan_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan Metropolitan Area">San Juan Metropolitan Area</a> sprawl, and its proximity to the northeast region of Puerto Rico. Also the expansion of <a href="/wiki/PR-66" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-66">Route 66</a> has sparked new interest in Can\xc3\xb3vanas as an industrial and commercial sector. Recently the largest local supermarket chain brand <a href="/wiki/Supermercados_Econo" title="Supermercados Econo">Econo</a>, has started the construction of its consolidated operations facilities with the construction of a super complex which will have the administrative headquarters, cold storage warehouse and main storages.\n</p><p>The municipal government has an economic development plan called <i>Canovanas Opened for Business</i> in which it provides fast legal and permit assistance, tax breaks and incentives for new commercial opportunities moving or establishing their businesses in Can\xc3\xb3vanas. This has prompted the emergence of new small businesses and high demand for professional and technical services.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Industrial">Industrial</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Industrial"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The industrial sector is growing with large international pharmaceuticals like <a href="/wiki/AstraZeneca" title="AstraZeneca">AstraZeneca</a>, IPR Pharmaceuticals, QBD, and other manufacturing plants in Can\xc3\xb3vanas.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Culture">Culture</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Culture"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Festivals_and_events">Festivals and events</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Festivals and events"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Can\xc3\xb3vanas celebrates its <a href="/wiki/Fiestas_patronales_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Fiestas patronales in Puerto Rico">patron saint</a> festival in October. The <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Fiestas Patronales de Nuestra Sra. del Pilar</i></span> is a religious and cultural celebration that generally features parades, games, artisans, amusement rides, regional food, and live entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Other festivals and events celebrated in Can\xc3\xb3vanas include:\n</p>\n<ul><li>May\xe2\x80\x94Holy Cross Festival</li>\n<li>December\xe2\x80\x94<i>Christmas in the Country</i></li></ul>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Sports">Sports</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Sports"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Although Can\xc3\xb3vanas has no professional sports team currently active, several of its past teams have been notable. Traditionally, local sports teams bear the nickname of "Indios". The <a href="/wiki/Indios_de_Can%C3%B3vanas" title="Indios de Can\xc3\xb3vanas">Indios de Can\xc3\xb3vanas</a>, of the <a href="/wiki/Baloncesto_Superior_Nacional" title="Baloncesto Superior Nacional">Baloncesto Superior Nacional</a>, won the championship two years in a row (1983\xe2\x80\x931984) and reached the finals in 1988.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Guard <a href="/wiki/Angelo_Cruz" title="Angelo Cruz">Angelo Cruz</a> and center <a href="/wiki/Ram%C3%B3n_Ramos" title="Ram\xc3\xb3n Ramos">Ram\xc3\xb3n Ramos</a> were two of the key players of the team during that era. However, the team disappeared during the 1990s. There have been movements to reestablish the team, but they\'ve been unsuccessful.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The Indias of Can\xc3\xb3vanas team, from the <a href="/wiki/Liga_de_Voleibol_Superior_Femenino" title="Liga de Voleibol Superior Femenino">Liga de Voleibol Superior Femenino</a>, also won a number of championships.\n</p><p>Can\xc3\xb3vanas is also the location of <a href="/wiki/Hip%C3%B3dromo_Camarero" title="Hip\xc3\xb3dromo Camarero">Hip\xc3\xb3dromo Camarero</a>, Puerto Rico\'s only horse racetrack. The track, which was formerly named <a href="/wiki/El_Nuevo_Comandante" class="mw-redirect" title="El Nuevo Comandante">El Nuevo Comandante</a> was established in 1976.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Government">Government</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Government"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Canovanas_City_Hall.JPG" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Canovanas_City_Hall.JPG/220px-Canovanas_City_Hall.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="207" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Canovanas_City_Hall.JPG/330px-Canovanas_City_Hall.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Canovanas_City_Hall.JPG/440px-Canovanas_City_Hall.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2751" data-file-height="2592" /></a><figcaption>Can\xc3\xb3vanas City Hall</figcaption></figure>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1236090951"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mayoralty_in_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayoralty in Puerto Rico">Mayoralty in Puerto Rico</a></div>\n<p>All municipalities in Puerto Rico are administered by a mayor, elected every four years. The current mayor of Can\xc3\xb3vanas is <a href="/wiki/Lornna_Soto" title="Lornna Soto">Lornna Soto</a>, of the <a href="/wiki/New_Progressive_Party_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="New Progressive Party of Puerto Rico">New Progressive Party</a> (PNP). She is the daughter of long-serving former Mayor <a href="/wiki/Chemo_Soto" title="Chemo Soto">Jose "Chemo" Soto</a>, who distinguished himself for his eccentricities in clothing,<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and for successfully using the urban legend of the <a href="/wiki/Chupacabra" title="Chupacabra">Chupacabra</a> to promote the city.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> His daughter, <a href="/wiki/Lornna_Soto" title="Lornna Soto">Lornna</a>, also served as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Senate of Puerto Rico">Senate of Puerto Rico</a> from 2004 to 2013.\n</p><p>The city belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Senatorial_district_VIII" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Senatorial district VIII">Puerto Rico Senatorial district VIII</a>, which is represented by two Senators. In 2012, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_A._Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Pedro A. Rodr\xc3\xadguez">Pedro A. Rodr\xc3\xadguez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luis_Daniel_Rivera" title="Luis Daniel Rivera">Luis Daniel Rivera</a> were elected as District Senators.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>40<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Representatives <a href="/wiki/Javier_Aponte_Dalmau" title="Javier Aponte Dalmau">Javier Aponte Dalmau</a> (District 38) and <a href="/wiki/%C3%81ngel_Buler%C3%ADn" title="\xc3\x81ngel Buler\xc3\xadn">\xc3\x81ngel Buler\xc3\xadn</a> (District 37) represent different regions of Can\xc3\xb3vanas in the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_House_of_Representatives" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico House of Representatives">House of Representatives</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Symbols">Symbols</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Symbols"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">municipio</i></span> has an official flag and coat of arms.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flag">Flag</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Flag"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The flag of Can\xc3\xb3vanas features a purple background with a wide yellow band across, and the town\'s <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a> in the center.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-42"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>42<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The coat of arms features a shield with the same colors (purple background and a yellow band). The colors are taken from the banner of the "Hijos y Amigos Ausentes de Can\xc3\xb3vanas". A broken chain symbolizes the separation of Can\xc3\xb3vanas from <a href="/wiki/Loiza,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Loiza, Puerto Rico">Lo\xc3\xadza</a>. The crown in the middle represents the supremacy of Cacique Canoban\xc3\xa1. The laurels are a symbol of the 23 consecutive wins achieved by the Lo\xc3\xadza Indians basketball team, establishing a record in Puerto Rico, also represented by the basket in the middle. The rising sun, with its sixteen rays of light, indicate the sprouting of a new municipality in Puerto Rico and the number of incumbent mayors before Can\xc3\xb3vanas was separated from Lo\xc3\xadza.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-42"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>42<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The coat of arms also features a white banner below with the inscription "1130 1909, Canoban\xc3\xa1 del Cayniab\xc3\xb3n, 8\xe2\x80\x9316 1970". The first date, November 30, 1909, is the date of the installation of the municipal seat of Lo\xc3\xadza in Can\xc3\xb3vanas. The second date, August 15, 1970, is the date of the official founding of Can\xc3\xb3vanas as a separate municipality. The names of Canoban\xc3\xa1 and Cayniab\xc3\xb3n make reference to the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Ta\xc3\xadno people">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> heritage of the region. Finally, a <a href="/wiki/Coronet" title="Coronet">coronet</a> in the form of a three-tower mural crown stands above the shield.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_42-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-42"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>42<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Nicknames">Nicknames</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Nicknames"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Can\xc3\xb3vanas is known by various names. It is known as the "Pueblo Valeroso" after Cacique Yuira lost her life defending the Spanish people from her own people, the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Ta\xc3\xadnos</a>. It is also known as the "City of Indians" because of its important <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> heritage. Can\xc3\xb3vanas is also known as the "City of Races", because of the <a href="/wiki/Hip%C3%B3dromo_Camarero" title="Hip\xc3\xb3dromo Camarero">Hip\xc3\xb3dromo Camarero</a>, and the "<a href="/wiki/Chupacabras" class="mw-redirect" title="Chupacabras">Town of the Chupacabras</a>" because of the alleged sightings of the creature, and the beliefs in it of former mayor, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Chemo_Soto" class="mw-redirect" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Chemo Soto">Jos\xc3\xa9 Chemo Soto</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-psh_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-psh-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Transportation">Transportation</h2><span Distance from the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">capital</a> is roughly 15 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-psh_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-psh-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Other roads that lead to Can\xc3\xb3vanas are <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_185" title="Puerto Rico Highway 185">PR-185</a> that enters between the <a href="/wiki/Lomas,_Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Lomas, Can\xc3\xb3vanas, Puerto Rico">Lomas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hato_Puerco,_Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Hato Puerco, Can\xc3\xb3vanas, Puerto Rico">Hato Puerco</a> barrios, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_186" title="Puerto Rico Highway 186">PR-186</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Cubuy,_Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cubuy, Can\xc3\xb3vanas, Puerto Rico">Cubuy</a> ward, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=PR-957&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="PR-957 (page does not exist)">PR-957</a> of the Hato Puerco ward. In 2012, the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_66" title="Puerto Rico Highway 66">PR-66</a>, which starts in <a href="/wiki/Carolina,_Puerto_Rico" title="Carolina, Puerto Rico">Carolina</a>, was extended to lead directly into Can\xc3\xb3vanas.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>44<span cars.\n</p><p>Canovanas is reasonably close to <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Mar%C3%ADn_International_Airport" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn International Airport">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz
