data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1177148991">.mw-parser-output .IPA-label-small{font-size:85%}.mw-parser-output .references .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;na\xc9\xbea\xc5\x8b\xcb\x88xito&#93;</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Naranjito_barrio-pueblo" title="Naranjito barrio-pueblo">town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> located in the central region of the island, south of <a href="/wiki/Toa_Alta,_Puerto_Rico" title="Toa Alta, Puerto Rico">Toa Alta</a>; north of <a href="/wiki/Barranquitas,_Puerto_Rico" Naranjito is spread over 7 barrios and <a href="/wiki/Naranjito_barrio-pueblo" title="Naranjito barrio-pueblo">Naranjito Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and the administrative center). It is part of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area">San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naranjito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The intention to form the municipality of Naranjito began in 1810. After a series of major incidents with powerful political interests of the time, on December 3, 1824, Don Braulio Morales successfully founded the town of Naranjito. The town was founded in the neighborhood of the same name, on a land donated by Do\xc3\xb1a Manuela Rivera and Don Braulio Morales. Morales was named "Captain Settler" and at the same time was appointed mayor of the town in development. The name "Naranjito" is derived from a small sour orange tree that served as a reference point for travelers looking for in the shortest way to the town of <a href="/wiki/Toa_Alta,_Puerto_Rico" title="Toa Alta, Puerto Rico">Toa Alta</a>. At the time of its foundation, Naranjito consisted of five barrios/districts: <a href="/wiki/Lomas,_Naranjito,_Puerto_Rico" title="Lomas, Naranjito, Puerto Rico">Lomas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guadiana,_Naranjito,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guadiana, Naranjito, Puerto Rico">Guadiana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Achiote,_Naranjito,_Puerto_Rico" title="Achiote, Naranjito, Puerto Rico">Achiote</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nuevo,_Naranjito,_Puerto_Rico" title="Nuevo, Naranjito, Puerto Rico">Nuevo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cedro_Arriba" title="Cedro Arriba">Cedro</a>. Cedro was divided in 1853 in <a href="/wiki/Cedro_Arriba" title="Cedro Arriba">Cedro Arriba</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cedro_Abajo" title="Cedro Abajo">Cedro Abajo</a>. The municipality\'s main urban zone is composed by the San Miguel, San Antonio and San Cristobal districts.\n</p><p>Puerto Rico was ceded by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_of_1898" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Paris of 1898">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became a territory of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. In 1899, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_War" title="United States Department of War">United States Department of War</a> conducted a <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of Puerto Rico finding that the population of Naranjito was 8,101.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Puerto_Rico_and_South_Dakota_National_Guard_(37608787606).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Puerto_Rico_and_South_Dakota_National_Guard_%2837608787606%29.jpg/220px-Puerto_Rico_and_South_Dakota_National_Guard_%2837608787606%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Puerto_Rico_and_South_Dakota_National_Guard_%2837608787606%29.jpg/330px-Puerto_Rico_and_South_Dakota_National_Guard_%2837608787606%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Puerto_Rico_and_South_Dakota_National_Guard_%2837608787606%29.jpg/440px-Puerto_Rico_and_South_Dakota_National_Guard_%2837608787606%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2656" data-file-height="1771" /></a><figcaption>On October 11, 2017, the South Dakota National Guard distributed  drinking water to residents of Naranjito.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>On September 20, 2017 <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> struck Puerto Rico. The orange color in the flag symbolizes the town of Naranjito (little orange tree), while the green symbolizes its green mountains.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naranjito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a> is a red cross, the symbol of the angel <a href="/wiki/Michael_(archangel)" title="Michael (archangel)">Saint Michael</a>, patron of Naranjito. The globe with the cross symbolizes the power and sovereignty of God. The gold and red stripes that appear in the second and third quarters, constitute the primitive baton of the Guadiana lineage. The lily twigs are a tribute of Saint <a href="/wiki/Anthony_of_Padua" title="Anthony of Padua">Anthony of Padua</a>, confessor and doctor of the Church. The scene was considered difficult to shoot, as pathways were slippery from moist tropical heat and the scene involved actors and stunt doubles running while avoiding dogs, chickens and other stray animals loose in the area. To capture the scene, a 420-foot cable-camera rig was used to allow for a fast moving, birds-eye view of the action, and cameras on cranes were set up on rooftops and in alleyways. Walker and Brewster made multiple takes of the conclusion of the scene, requiring them to jump nearly 30 feet from a building onto a waiting safety mat.
