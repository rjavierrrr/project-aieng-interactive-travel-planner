.IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" barrio-pueblo">town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> located along the southern coast of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Guayama, Puerto Rico">Guayama</a> and northwest of the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Patillas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Patillas, Puerto Rico">Patillas</a>. Arroyo is spread over 5 barrios and <a href="/wiki/Arroyo_barrio-pueblo" title="Arroyo barrio-pueblo">Arroyo</a> barrio-pueblo (the downtown area and administrative center). It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Guayama_metropolitan_area" title="Guayama metropolitan area">Guayama Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Etymology_and_nicknames">Etymology and nicknames</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arroyo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology and nicknames"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>It is believed that the name of <i>Arroyo</i> (which means "<a href="/wiki/Stream" title="Stream">creek</a>" or "<a href="/wiki/Arroyo_(watercourse)" title="Arroyo (watercourse)">brook</a>" in <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>) is derived from a small water stream where travelers stopped to freshen up before continuing on their way.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_Founding_Symbols_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_Founding_Symbols-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>According to legend, one the town\'s first nicknames, <i>Pueblo Ingrato</i> ("Ungrateful Town"), comes from the locals in the 19th century who either burned or drowned a resident who contracted either <a href="/wiki/Cholera" title="Cholera">cholera</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Bubonic_plague" title="Bubonic plague">bubonic plague</a>, even though he was renowned for his generosity. In the last century, however, there has been a push to change the nickname to <i>Pueblo Grato</i> ("Grateful Town") in order to clean the town\'s modern, laid back and friendly reputation.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Residents of Arroyo are often referred to as <i>Bucaneros</i> ("<a href="/wiki/Buccaneer" title="Buccaneer">Buccaneers</a>"), a reference to the region\'s historic pirate and buccaneer activities. The main sports teams of the municipality, most notably its <a href="/wiki/Volleyball" title="Volleyball">volleyball</a> team, are also called <i>Los Bucaneros</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arroyo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The town of Arroyo was officially founded on December 25, 1855. However, some historians believe there were previous settlements in the area even previous to the <a href="/wiki/History_of_Puerto_Rico#Beginning_of_colonization" title="History of Puerto Rico">Spanish colonization</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Arroyo_history_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Arroyo_history-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> There are four different versions about the founding of the town, including that it was formed when a small group of people from the neighbor town of <a href="/wiki/Guayama,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guayama, Puerto Rico">Guayama</a> came to the region looking for a port to export and import merchandise.<sup id="cite_ref-Arroyo_history_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Arroyo_history-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Still, in 1868, a local newspaper called <i>La Gaceta de Puerto Rico</i> stated that Arroyo was founded in 1852. Between 1859 and 1860, City Hall signed agreements to open streets and build a <a href="/wiki/Town_square" title="Town square">town square</a> and a sewer system for the town.\n</p><p>\nIn 1858, <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Morse" title="Samuel Morse">Samuel Morse</a> introduced wired communication to <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a> when he established a <a href="/wiki/Telegraphy" title="Telegraphy">telegraph</a> system in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, then a <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Colony</a>. Morse\'s oldest daughter Susan Walker Morse (1821\xe2\x80\x931885), would often visit her uncle Charles Pickering Walker who owned the Hacienda Concordia in the town of <a href="/wiki/Guayama,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guayama, Puerto Rico">Guayama</a>. During one of her visits she met and later married Edward Lind, a <a href="/wiki/Danes" title="Danes">Danish</a> merchant who worked in the Hacienda La Henriqueta in Arroyo.<sup id="cite_ref-NYJ_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYJ-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Lind purchased the Hacienda from his sister when she became a widow. Morse, who often spent his winters at the Hacienda with his daughter and son-in-law, set a two-mile telegraph line connecting his son-in-law\'s Hacienda to their house in Arroyo. The line was inaugurated on March 1, 1859, in a ceremony flanked by the Spanish and American flags.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The first lines transmitted by Samuel Morse that day in Puerto Rico were:<sup id="cite_ref-NYJ_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYJ-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></p><blockquote><p> "Puerto Rico, beautiful jewel! When you are linked with the other jewels of the <a href="/wiki/Antilles" title="Antilles">Antilles</a> in the necklace of the world\'s telegraph, yours will not shine less brilliantly in the crown of your Queen!"</p></blockquote>\n<p>Puerto Rico was ceded by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_of_1898" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Paris of 1898">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became a territory of the United States. In 1899, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_War" title="United States Department of War">United States Department of War</a> conducted a <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of Puerto Rico finding that the population of Arroyo was 4,867.<sup id="cite_ref-OfficeSanger1900_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OfficeSanger1900-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1902, Puerto Rico\'s <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly" class="mw-redirect" title="Legislative Assembly">Legislative Assembly</a> approved a law to consolidate certain municipalities. According to it, Arroyo would be merged with <a href="/wiki/Guayama,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guayama, Puerto Rico">Guayama</a> starting on July 1 of that year. However, the law was revoked in 1905 returning Arroyo to its municipal status.\n</p><p>In early 1999, the U.S. Congressional record documented a commendation of Arroyo, on its 100-year relationship with the U.S., noting that many citizens of Arroyo, such as Virgilio S\xc3\xa1nchez and Raul Serrano, have fought and died in wars for the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-Congress1955_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Congress1955-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On September 20, 2017, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a>\'s wind and rainfall triggered numerous landslides in Arroyo.<sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The hurricane destroyed 1,550 structures in Arroyo. A bridge on PR-725 collapsed leaving 130 families trapped.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arroyo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Arroyo belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Alluvial_plain" title="Alluvial plain">alluvial plains</a> sub-region of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>-<a href="/wiki/Patillas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Patillas, Puerto Rico">Patillas</a> known as the Southern Coastal Valley. The area is very dry although its plains are productive thanks to artificial <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">irrigation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_General_Info_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_General_Info-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Barrios">Barrios</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arroyo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Barrios"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Arroyo,_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Arroyo%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/220px-Arroyo%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="164" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Arroyo%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/330px-Arroyo%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Arroyo%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/440px-Arroyo%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png 2x" data-file-width="847" data-file-height="630" /></a><figcaption>Subdivisions of Arroyo.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Like all municipalities of Puerto Rico, Arroyo is subdivided into <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico">barrios</a>.
