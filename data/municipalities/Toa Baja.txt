.IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;\xcb\x88toa<span class="wrap"> </span>\xcb\x88\xce\xb2axa&#93;</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Toa_Baja_barrio-pueblo" title="Toa Baja barrio-pueblo">town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> located in the northern coast, north of <a href="/wiki/Toa_Alta,_Puerto_Rico" title="Toa Alta, Puerto Rico">Toa Alta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bayam%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico" title="Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico">Bayam\xc3\xb3n</a>; east of <a href="/wiki/Dorado,_Puerto_Rico" title="Dorado, Puerto Rico">Dorado</a>; and west of <a href="/wiki/Cata%C3%B1o,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cata\xc3\xb1o, Puerto Rico">Cata\xc3\xb1o</a>. Toa Baja is spread over five <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico"> barrios</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Toa_Baja_barrio-pueblo" title="Toa Baja barrio-pueblo">Toa Baja Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and the administrative center of the city). Toa Baja is part of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area">San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>. Toa Baja is located fifteen minutes by car from <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a> and two hours from <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The region of what is now Toa Baja belonged to the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Ta\xc3\xadno people">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> region of Toa and Bayam\xc3\xb3n, which was located on the north coast of Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The regions were led by cacique Aramana and Majagua respectively. During the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Spanish colonization of the Americas">Spanish colonization</a>, a farm was settled in the region around 1511 where Spanish settlers tried to learn from the Ta\xc3\xadnos how to grow fruits and vegetables. Some of the families who settled in the region were Marrero, Salgado, and Mart\xc3\xadnez, from the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Central_Constancia,_Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Central_Constancia%2C_Toa_Baja%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg/220px-Central_Constancia%2C_Toa_Baja%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="169" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Central_Constancia%2C_Toa_Baja%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg/330px-Central_Constancia%2C_Toa_Baja%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Central_Constancia%2C_Toa_Baja%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg/440px-Central_Constancia%2C_Toa_Baja%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="786" /></a><figcaption>Train at <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Central Constancia</i></span>, a 900-acre sugar cane farm in Toa Baja</figcaption></figure>\n<p>In 1745, the town was officially organized, with the parish being built in 1749. It was dedicated to Nuestra Se\xc3\xb1ora de la Concepci\xc3\xb3n (<a href="/wiki/Immaculate_Conception" title="Immaculate Conception">Our Lady of the Immaculate Conception</a>). It is said that in 1776 there were six cattle ranches and 12 sugar cane <a href="/wiki/Hacienda" title="Hacienda">estates</a> in the region. However, rises in the flow of nearby rivers were problematic for the population. Because of this, in 1841 a group of residents requested the settlement to be transferred to what is now <a href="/wiki/Dorado,_Puerto_Rico" title="Dorado, Puerto Rico">Dorado</a>. The municipality of Dorado was officially founded the next year.<sup id="cite_ref-psh_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-psh-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Puerto Rico was ceded by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_of_1898" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Paris of 1898">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became a territory of the United States. In 1899, the United States conducted its first <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of Puerto Rico, finding that the population of Toa Baja was 4,030.\n</p><p>In 1902, the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Legislative Assembly of Puerto Rico">Legislative Assembly of Puerto Rico</a> approved the consolidation of several municipalities, which resulted in the annexation of Toa Baja to the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Bayam%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico" title="Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico">Bayam\xc3\xb3n</a>. However, in 1905 the law was annulled, and Toa Baja regained its status as an independent town.<sup id="cite_ref-psh_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-psh-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2010, Toa Baja was among the top ten municipalities in Puerto Rico, in terms of population, with 89,609 residents.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_de_la_Plata_(Puerto_Rico)" title="R\xc3\xado de la Plata (Puerto Rico)">La Plata River</a> rose more than 11 feet on September 20, 2017 as a result of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> and caused major infrastructure damage in Toa Baja.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span One of the radar systems is located at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Punta_Salinas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Punta Salinas (page does not exist)">Punta Salinas</a> in Toa Baja.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flood_control_project">Flood control project</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Flood control project"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In mid 2018, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Corps_of_Engineers" title="United States Army Corps of Engineers">United States Army Corps of Engineers</a> announced it would be undertaking a major <a href="/wiki/Flood_control" title="Flood control">flood control</a> project of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_de_la_Plata_(Puerto_Rico)" title="R\xc3\xado de la Plata (Puerto Rico)">R\xc3\xado de la Plata</a> river, which often causes flooding in Toa Baja.<sup id="cite_ref-2018_USACE_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2018_USACE-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Toa Baja is located in the Northern Coastal Plains of the island, in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Karst_(Puerto_Rico)" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern Karst (Puerto Rico)">Northern Karst</a> zone. It has an area of 24.0 miles<sup>2</sup> (62.4&#160;km<sup>2</sup>). The terrain consists mostly of alluvial deposits and clay-like surface as a result of the surrounding waters.<sup id="cite_ref-geo_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-geo-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The elevations range from 200 to 490 feet (60 to 150 meters) above sea level, with most of its highest parts located in the south. The rest of the area is mostly plain. The northern part of Toa Baja is characterized for its <a href="/wiki/Mangrove" title="Mangrove">mangroves</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marsh" title="Marsh">marshes</a>, while the southern part is classified as typical <a href="/wiki/Karst" title="Karst">karst</a> area.<sup id="cite_ref-geo_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-geo-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Updated flood zone maps (as of 2019) show that Toa Baja is extremely vulnerable to flooding, along with <a href="/wiki/Humacao,_Puerto_Rico" title="Humacao, Puerto Rico">Humacao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rinc%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico" title="Rinc\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico">Rinc\xc3\xb3n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barceloneta,_Puerto_Rico" title="Barceloneta, Puerto Rico">Barceloneta</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Corozal,_Puerto_Rico" title="Corozal, Puerto Rico">Corozal</a>. For its many rivers, almost the entire municipality of Toa Baja is in the flood zone area.<sup id="cite_ref-Le\xc3\xb3n_END_Flood_Zones_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Le\xc3\xb3n_END_Flood_Zones-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Homes in Toa Baja have historically been built a few feet above ground level to account for flooding. After <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a>, architects from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico" title="University of Puerto Rico">University of Puerto Rico</a> and <a href="/wiki/Polytechnic_University_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Polytechnic University of Puerto Rico">Polytechnic University of Puerto Rico</a> provided residents of Toa Baja with ideas on how to mitigate flooding.<sup id="cite_ref-Seguro_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Seguro-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Water_features">Water features</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Water features"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>There are three main rivers in Toa Baja: <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_la_Plata_(Puerto_Rico)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rio de la Plata (Puerto Rico)">R\xc3\xado de la Plata</a>, <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Cocal" class="mw-redirect" title="R\xc3\xado Cocal">R\xc3\xado Cocal</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hondo_River_(Bayam%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico)" title="Hondo River (Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico)">R\xc3\xado Hondo</a>. La Plata is the longest river on the island and crosses Toa Baja in the east. Cocal is the natural boundary between Toa Baja and Dorado. Boca Vieja Bay (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Ensenada Boca Vieja</i>) is a bay located in Toa Baja.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Barrios">Barrios</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Barrios"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Toa_Baja%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/220px-Toa_Baja%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="237" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Toa_Baja%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/330px-Toa_Baja%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Toa_Baja%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/440px-Toa_Baja%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png 2x" data-file-width="1100" data-file-height="1184" /></a><figcaption>Subdivisions of Toa Baja.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Like all municipalities of Puerto Rico, Toa Baja is subdivided into <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico">barrios</a>. The municipal buildings, central square and large Catholic church are located in a barrio referred to as <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">"el pueblo"</i></span>.<sup A map shows these communities occur in nearly every municipality of the commonwealth. Of the 742 places that were on the list in 2014, the following barrios, communities, sectors, or neighborhoods were in Toa Baja: Sector Villa Hostos (Campanillas), San Jos\xc3\xa9, Sector El 26, Toa Ville, Villa Albizu in Candelaria, Villa Calma, Villa del Sol, Villa Esperanza, Villa Marisol, and Villa Quintero.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wordpress.com_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wordpress.com-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2009, an order was given by Governor Luis G. Fortu\xc3\xb1o to shut off essential services, such as water and electricity, to Villas del Sol, a "Special Community", which consisted of homes built illegally on flood-prone, state land. The Federal Emergency Management Agency bought these homes from the Puerto Rican government in order to keep the land from being used further. In January 2010, the Puerto Rican government began demolishing some of the homes. The village had a large population of Dominicans, including illegal aliens. In 2013, the mayor and the community continued to argue about how to construct in Villas del Sol.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> By 2016, six years after the community had been promised, by the government of Toa Baja, that it would receive permits for the building of critical infrastructure, it had not.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Some families had dispersed, others had constructed humble homes on land obtained from a private donor.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a They do have a female <a href="/wiki/Volleyball" title="Volleyball">volleyball</a> team from the <a href="/wiki/Liga_de_Voleibol_Superior_Femenino" title="Liga de Voleibol Superior Femenino">Liga de Voleibol Superior Femenino</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Llaneras_de_Toa_Baja" title="Llaneras de Toa Baja">Llaneras de Toa Baja</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>42<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Each year, Toa Baja hosts both the <a href="/wiki/Pee_Wee_Reese_World_Series" class="mw-redirect" title="Pee Wee Reese World Series">Pee Wee Reese World Series</a> (for baseball players of ages 12 and under) and the <a href="/wiki/Willie_Mays_World_Series" class="mw-redirect" title="Willie Mays World Series">Willie Mays World Series</a> (for baseball players of ages 10 and under). The town has a team, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Llaneros_de_Levittown&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Llaneros de Levittown (page does not exist)">Llaneros de Levittown</a>, in the PeeWee Reese Category <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> team from the Pro-Deportes/American Amateur Baseball Congress.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Economy">Economy</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Economy"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>For many years, Toa Baja\'s economy relied in agriculture, particularly <a href="/wiki/Sugar_cane" class="mw-redirect" title="Sugar cane">sugar cane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cattle" title="Cattle">cattle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Because of this, the municipality was the site of many important <a href="/wiki/Hacienda" title="Hacienda">haciendas</a> like <a href="/wiki/Hacienda_Santa_Elena" title="Hacienda Santa Elena">Santa Elena</a> (founded in 1790), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_Constancia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Central Constancia (page does not exist)">Central Constancia</a> (founded in 1867), and Media Luna. In the middle of the 18th century, fishing also became a primary source of Toa Baja\'s economy. Cattle ranches were also among the best in the island, processing large amounts of milk. The growth of small fruits also continues to be one of Toa Baja\'s main products.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>44<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/1950s" title="1950s">1950s</a>, manufacturing started replacing agriculture as the main economic source. Currently, the industrial sector continues to be one of the most important factors of economic growth in the region. Some of the products manufactured in Toa Baja are metal, plastic, concrete, textile, electrical and electronic machinery, and rum.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Government">Government</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Government"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1236090951"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mayoralty_in_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayoralty in Puerto Rico">Mayoralty in Puerto Rico</a></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Anibal_Vega_Borges" class="mw-redirect" title="Anibal Vega Borges">Anibal Vega Borges</a>, a lawyer, was elected Mayor of the city in 2004 for the first time. He has been re-elected twice (2008 and 2012). Vega Borges\' election wins have been for wide margins, which led a newspaper to label him as one of the "most powerful" mayors in the island.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>45<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The city belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Senatorial_district_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Senatorial district II">Puerto Rico Senatorial district II</a>, which is represented by two Senators. <a href="/wiki/Migdalia_Padilla" title="Migdalia Padilla">Migdalia Padilla</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carmelo_R%C3%ADos_Santiago" title="Carmelo R\xc3\xados Santiago">Carmelo R\xc3\xados Santiago</a> have served as District Senators since 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>46<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2016 Vega Borges was challenged to a primary by a former representative of Toa Baja, <a href="/wiki/Bernardo_M%C3%A1rquez_Garc%C3%ADa" title="Bernardo M\xc3\xa1rquez Garc\xc3\xada">Bernardo "Betito" Marquez Garcia</a>. Marquez won the primary and subsequently won the general election.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>47<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> On January 15, 2017 Marquez Garcia was sworn in as mayor of Toa Baja.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>48<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Symbols">Symbols</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Symbols"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">municipio</i></span> has an official flag and coat of arms.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>49<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flag">Flag</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Flag"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Toa Baja\'s official flag features two vertical bands: the left side is green and the right side is gold. The green band represents the plains of the Toa River (now known as <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_la_Plata_(Puerto_Rico)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rio de la Plata (Puerto Rico)">La Plata</a>), and the golden band represents the sun. The green band features two keys, one across the other. One is gold and the other silver, symbolizing the <a href="/wiki/Saint_Peter" title="Saint Peter">Apostle Saint Peter</a>, the patron saint of the city.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>50<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-51"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>51<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Escudo_de_Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico.svg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Escudo_de_Toa_Baja%2C_Puerto_Rico.svg/150px-Escudo_de_Toa_Baja%2C_Puerto_Rico.svg.png" decoding="async" width="150" height="184" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Escudo_de_Toa_Baja%2C_Puerto_Rico.svg/225px-Escudo_de_Toa_Baja%2C_Puerto_Rico.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Escudo_de_Toa_Baja%2C_Puerto_Rico.svg/300px-Escudo_de_Toa_Baja%2C_Puerto_Rico.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="268" data-file-height="329" /></a><figcaption>Toa Baja\'s coat of arms</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Toa Baja\'s coat of arms features a green waving stripe, representing the <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_la_Plata_(Puerto_Rico)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rio de la Plata (Puerto Rico)">Toa River</a>, one of the first mentioned in the history of Puerto Rico. Like the flag, it features two keys, one across the other, symbolizing <a href="/wiki/Saint_Peter" title="Saint Peter">Saint Peter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patron_saint" title="Patron saint">patron saint</a> of the town. The coat has the letters "F" and "J" to each side of the keys, representing <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_II_of_Aragon" title="Ferdinand II of Aragon">Ferdinand II of Aragon</a> and <a href="/wiki/King_of_Castile" class="mw-redirect" title="King of Castile">V of Castile</a>, and his daughter, <a href="/wiki/Joanna_of_Castile" title="Joanna of Castile">Joanna of Castile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">sovereign of Castile and Le\xc3\xb3n</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_51-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-51"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>51<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The gold fields above and below the green stripe represent the wealth of the city. It also feature handfuls of <a href="/wiki/Yucca" title="Yucca">yucca</a>, one of the main crops of this area. It constitutes a symbol of the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Ta\xc3\xadnos</a> who cultivated it, and whose main food was cassava or bread of yucca. Two sugar cane stems surround the shield, representing the cultivation and industry of <a href="/wiki/Sugar_cane" class="mw-redirect" title="Sugar cane">sugar cane</a>, which was the main source of Toa Baja\'s economy.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_51-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-51"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>51<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Transportation">Transportation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Transportation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Toa Baja is approximately 25 minutes in non-rush hour from <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a> by car, however, it may take up to 90 minutes in <a href="/wiki/Rush_hour" title="Rush hour">rush hour</a>. The main road to the city is the <a href="/wiki/PR-22" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-22">PR-22</a> and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_165" title="Puerto Rico Highway 165">PR-165</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>52<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>A new <a href="/wiki/Bus_rapid_transit" title="Bus rapid transit">bus rapid transit</a> (BRT) system called <a href="/w/index.php?title=Metro_Urbano&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Metro Urbano (page does not exist)">Metro Urbano</a> operates in the center of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_22" title="Puerto Rico Highway 22">Jos\xc3\xa9 de Diego Expressway (PR-22)</a> in <a href="/wiki/High-occupancy_vehicle_lane" title="High-occupancy vehicle lane">high-occupancy vehicle lanes</a> (HOV) from <a href="/wiki/Candelaria,_Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico" title="Candelaria, Toa Baja, Puerto Rico">Candelaria</a> in Toa Baja to the <a href="/wiki/Bayam%C3%B3n_station" title="Bayam\xc3\xb3n station">Bayam\xc3\xb3n Station</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Tren_Urbano" title="Tren Urbano">Tren Urbano</a> metro system.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>53<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>There are 29 bridges in Toa Baja.<sup
