.IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;\xcb\x88a\xc9\xa3was<span class="wrap"> </span>\xcb\x88\xce\xb2wenas&#93;</a></span>, <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1177148991"><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">local pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA" title="Help:IPA">&#91;awa\xca\x94<span class="wrap"> </span>\xcb\x88\xce\xb2wena\xca\x94&#93;</a></span>), popularly known as "<i>La Ciudad de las Aguas Claras</i>" or "<i>The City of Clear Waters</i>", is a <a href="/wiki/Aguas_Buenas_barrio-pueblo" title="Aguas Buenas barrio-pueblo">town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico)">Central Mountain Range</a>, north of <a href="/wiki/Cidra,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cidra, Puerto Rico">Cidra</a>, south of <a href="/wiki/Bayam%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico" title="Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico">Bayam\xc3\xb3n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guaynabo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guaynabo, Puerto Rico">Guaynabo</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>; east of <a href="/wiki/Comer%C3%ADo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Comer\xc3\xado, Puerto Rico">Comerio</a>; and north-west of <a href="/wiki/Caguas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Caguas, Puerto Rico">Caguas</a>. Aguas Buenas is spread over 9 <i>barrios</i> and <a href="/wiki/Aguas_Buenas_barrio-pueblo" title="Aguas Buenas barrio-pueblo">Aguas Buenas Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and the administrative center of the city). It is part of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area">San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Etymology_and_nicknames">Etymology and nicknames</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aguas_Buenas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology and nicknames"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The name <i>Aguas Buenas</i> translates to "good waters". The <a href="/wiki/Aguas_Buenas_barrio-pueblo" title="Aguas Buenas barrio-pueblo">town</a>\'s and municipality\'s original names were <b>Aguabuena</b> ("good water"), originally a barrio or district of <a href="/wiki/Caguas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Caguas, Puerto Rico">Caguas</a>, and <b>Aguas Claras</b> ("clear waters"). These names and some of the municipality\'s nicknames such as <i>Ciudad de las Aguas Claras</i> ("city of clear waters") and <i>Oasis de Puerto Rico</i> ("<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Oasis" title="Oasis">oasis</a>") are a reference to the number of natural <a href="/wiki/Spring_(hydrology)" title="Spring (hydrology)">water springs</a> that can be found in the municipality, particularly those founds around the <a href="/wiki/Aguas_Buenas_Cave_System" title="Aguas Buenas Cave System">Aguas Buenas Cave and Caverns System Nature Reserve</a> (Spanish: <i>Reserva Natural Sistema de Cuevas y Cavernas de Aguas Buenas</i>). In addition to <i>aguasbonenses</i>, citizens and residents of Aguas Buenas are often referred to as Mulos ("mules") or \xc3\x91ocos ("amputees").<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aguas_Buenas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Aguas Buenas was originally a sector of <a href="/wiki/Caguas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Caguas, Puerto Rico">Caguas</a> known as <i>Aguabuena</i>. In 1798, a group of residents started establishing their houses near some of the rivers in the area and started calling the sector <i>Aguas Claras</i>.\n</p><p>The quantity of residents incremented with time and on July 25, 1832, they organized a meeting and commissioned Francisco de Salas Torres and Ram\xc3\xb3n D\xc3\xadaz to do the necessary arrangements for the region to be declared a municipality. A resident, Julian L\xc3\xb3pez, offered part of his estate to be used for the construction of a town square, a church, the city hall, and the priest house.\n</p><p>The town was officially founded on May 25, 1838, and the name was changed to <i>Aguas Buenas</i>. Francisco de Salas Torres was declared the first mayor. Initially, the economy of the town relied on coffee plantations and commerce. At the end of the 19th century, the town\'s population was close to 7,000.\n</p><p>In 1906, mayor Don Jos\xc3\xa9 E. Morales bought nearly six <a href="/wiki/Acres" class="mw-redirect" title="Acres">acres</a> of terrain from Don Guillermo D\xc3\xadaz Delgado. In these lands, the sectors of La Pajilla, El Pueblito and El Guan\xc3\xa1bano were established, expanding the town\'s area.\n</p><p>The epicenter of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Aguas_Buenas_earthquake" title="2010 Aguas Buenas earthquake">2010 Aguas Buenas earthquake</a>, popularly known as the <i>Nochebuena Earthquake</i>, was centered between the barrios <a href="/wiki/Juan_Asencio,_Aguas_Buenas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Juan Asencio, Aguas Buenas, Puerto Rico">Juan Asencio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mulita,_Aguas_Buenas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Mulita, Aguas Buenas, Puerto Rico">Mulita</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sonadora,_Aguas_Buenas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Sonadora, Aguas Buenas, Puerto Rico">Sonadora</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> on September 20, 2017, triggered numerous landslides in Aguas Buenas with the significant amount of rainfall.<sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Between1,000 and 3,000 homes in Aguas Buenas were destroyed or suffered significant damage as a result of the hurricane. A year after the hurricane struck, many older residents refused to leave their damaged homes and continued to live in their homes, without a reconstructed roof, but with a blue tarp for a roof.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The hurricane winds and rain damaged infrastructure and caused the entire electrical system to collapse.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aguas_Buenas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The terrain is moderately mountainous located in the northern edge of the <a href="/wiki/Cayey,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cayey, Puerto Rico">Cayey</a> mountain range (<a href="/wiki/Sierra_de_Cayey" title="Sierra de Cayey">Sierra de Cayey</a>), part of the <a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Cordillera Central (Puerto Rico)">Cordillera Central</a> in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The highest peaks are La Pe\xc3\xb1a, Santa B\xc3\xa1rbara, La Tisa, and La Marquesa.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_General_Info_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_General_Info-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Water_features">Water features</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aguas_Buenas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Water features"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Several rivers flow through Aguas Buenas. Some of them are the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Bayam%C3%B3n" class="mw-redirect" title="R\xc3\xado Bayam\xc3\xb3n">Bayam\xc3\xb3n River</a>, and several small rivers that are part of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Lo%C3%ADza" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza">R\xc3\xado Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza</a>, like <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Bairoa" class="mw-redirect" title="R\xc3\xado Bairoa">Bairoa</a>, <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Caguitas" class="mw-redirect" title="R\xc3\xado Caguitas">Caguitas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Ca%C3%B1as_(Caguas,_Puerto_Rico)" title="R\xc3\xado Ca\xc3\xb1as (Caguas, Puerto Rico)">Ca\xc3\xb1as</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Barrios">Barrios</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span The triangle has a solitary white star, like the national banner. A second triangle appears interposed with the first one, in yellow, with its point reaching the other end of the flag. The main field of the flag, under both triangles, is color green.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aguas_Buenas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Designed by Francisco Diaz Rivera in 1975, the coat of arms features a four-paneled shield. The upper left panel shows the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_of_Montserrat" title="Virgin of Montserrat">Virgin of Monserrate</a>, while the upper right panel features a <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Ta\xc3\xadno people">Ta\xc3\xadno</a>, a symbol of the original inhabitants of this region. At the bottom right panel, lies a <a href="/wiki/Cornucopia" title="Cornucopia">cornucopia</a> containing the native fruit of Aguas Buenas. This panel represents how the agriculture was the primary source of income for the town. In the lower left panel, there are four <a href="/wiki/Maracas" class="mw-redirect" title="Maracas">maracas</a> and a g\xc3\xbcicharo, typical instruments of the "jibaros" of Aguas Buenas and Puerto Rico. The crown, as with other municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, consists of three towers.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Anthem">Anthem</h3><span
