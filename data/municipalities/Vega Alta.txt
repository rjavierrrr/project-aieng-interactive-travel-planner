Alta</b> (<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1177148991">.mw-parser-output .IPA-label-small{font-size:85%}.mw-parser-output .references .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;\xcb\x88be\xc9\xa3a<span class="wrap"> </span>\xcb\x88alta&#93;</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Vega_Alta_barrio-pueblo" title="Vega Alta barrio-pueblo">town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. Vega Alta is on the northern coast of the island, north of <a href="/wiki/Morovis" class="mw-redirect" title="Morovis">Morovis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Corozal,_Puerto_Rico" title="Corozal, Puerto Rico">Corozal</a>; east of <a href="/wiki/Vega_Baja" class="mw-redirect" title="Vega Baja">Vega Baja</a>; and west of <a href="/wiki/Dorado,_Puerto_Rico" title="Dorado, Puerto Rico">Dorado</a> with an area of 28 square miles (73&#160;km<sup>2</sup>). Vega Alta is subdivided into seven barrios and <a href="/wiki/Vega_Alta_barrio-pueblo" title="Vega Alta barrio-pueblo">Vega Alta barrio-pueblo</a> (the downtown area and the administrative center of the city). It is part of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area">San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>.\n</p><p>Vega Alta is west of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, the capital of Puerto Rico. Vega Alta is known for the beach at Cerro Gordo. Other points of interest include the town\'s Catholic church, known as Inmaculada Concepci\xc3\xb3n, which was founded in 1813, and the <a href="/wiki/Vega_State_Forest" title="Vega State Forest">Vega Alta Forest</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>During the first week of December, Vega Alta celebrates its patron saint, the <a href="/wiki/Immaculate_Conception" title="Immaculate Conception">Immaculate Conception</a>.\n</p><p>\xe2\x80\x9dVega Alta\xe2\x80\x9d translates to \xe2\x80\x9chigh meadow\xe2\x80\x9d or "upper meadow" in English.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vega_Alta,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:VegaAlta4.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/VegaAlta4.jpg/220px-VegaAlta4.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/VegaAlta4.jpg/330px-VegaAlta4.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/VegaAlta4.jpg/440px-VegaAlta4.jpg 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="450" /></a><figcaption><a href="/wiki/Al-Faruq_Mosque_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Al-Faruq Mosque (Puerto Rico)">Al-Faruq Mosque</a> in Vega Alta, the largest in Puerto Rico, built in 1992</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Vega Alta was founded in 1775 by Francisco de los Olivos. It was first named <b>La Vega de Espinosa</b> and popularly known as <i>el pueblo de los \'\xc3\xb1angotaos</i> ("the town of the squatters", in reference to the <i>j\xc3\xadbaros</i> country folk who would wait for the train in a <a href="/wiki/Squatting_position" title="Squatting position">squatting position</a>, due to lack of benches). Before this, it was part of a group of towns known as <b>Las Vegas</b>. It was then separated into two municipalities: <a href="/wiki/Vega_Baja" class="mw-redirect" title="Vega Baja">Vega Baja</a> and the other Vega Alta.\n</p><p>Puerto Rico was ceded by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1898)" title="Treaty of Paris (1898)">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became a <a href="/wiki/Territories_of_the_United_States" title="Territories of the United States">territory</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. In 1899, the United States conducted its first census of Puerto Rico finding that the population of Vega Alta was 6,107.\n</p><p>On October 12, 1898, the city\'s mayor, Francisco Vega, received U.S. troops as part of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a>. On that same day, for the first time in Vega Alta\'s history, the <a href="/wiki/U.S._flag" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. flag">U.S. flag</a> was lifted on city hall. From 1902 to 1905, Vega Alta became once more part of the neighboring municipality of Vega Baja. In 1905, the government of Puerto Rico passed a law, allowing Vega Alta to become a municipality with its own limits.\n</p><p>Hacienda Carmen and Hacienda Ortega sugar plantations were owned by Juan Gualberto Landron y Martinez born in 1791 in Toa Baja . He owned slaves and purchased them, some <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_slave_trade" title="Atlantic slave trade">coming directly from Africa</a>.\n</p><p>The second-largest <a href="/wiki/Mosque" title="Mosque">mosque</a> in Puerto Rico, built in 1992, is located in Vega Alta.<sup id="cite_ref-Arab_News_2012_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Arab_News_2012-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> on September 20, 2017, triggered numerous landslides in Vega Alta and 90% of homes made of wood were destroyed.<sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides-4"><span blue in the center, with narrower green and white stripes on its sides.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vega_Alta,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>This municipality has a coat of arms which was designed by Roberto Biascochea Lota. It consists of three bars, the center one has a bouquet of natural lilies. It symbolizes the Immaculate Conception of Mary, who is represented by the silver and azure colors. On its sides are two olive branches, a symbol of the town\'s founder, Francisco de los Olivos. Two  sugarcane branches are featured, because it an important part of Vega Alta\'s economy.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Anthem">Anthem</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vega_Alta,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Anthem"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <a class="external text" href="https://es.wikisource.org/wiki/Himno_de_Vega_Alta_(Puerto_Rico)">anthem</a> used by Vega Alta is the musical composition created in 1958, with lyrics by Domingo Figueroa Ram\xc3\xadrez Arreglo and music by Domingo and Elliot A.
