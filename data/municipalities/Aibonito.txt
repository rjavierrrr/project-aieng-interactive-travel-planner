.IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;aj\xce\xb2o\xcb\x88nito&#93;</a></span>) is a small mountain <a href="/wiki/Aibonito_barrio-pueblo" title="Aibonito barrio-pueblo">town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_de_Cayey" title="Sierra de Cayey">Sierra de Cayey</a> mountain range, north of <a href="/wiki/Salinas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Salinas, Puerto Rico">Salinas</a>; south of <a href="/wiki/Barranquitas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Barranquitas, Puerto Rico">Barranquitas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Comer%C3%ADo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Comer\xc3\xado, Puerto Rico">Comer\xc3\xado</a>; east of <a href="/wiki/Coamo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Coamo, Puerto Rico">Coamo</a>; and west of <a href="/wiki/Cidra,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cidra, Puerto Rico">Cidra</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cayey,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cayey, Puerto Rico">Cayey</a>. Aibonito is spread over 8 <i>barrios</i> and <a href="/wiki/Aibonito_barrio-pueblo" title="Aibonito barrio-pueblo">Aibonito Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and the administrative center of the city). It is part of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area">San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>.\n</p><p>Aibonito is located at a relatively high elevation (its main square is the highest in the island at 732 metres (2,401&#160;ft) above sea level), which makes its climate cooler than most of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>\'s towns.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Etymology_and_nicknames">Etymology and nicknames</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology and nicknames"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The name <i>Aibonito</i> is possibly a combination of <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> and Ta\xc3\xadno from <i>Hatibon</i> or <i>Jatibon</i>, which the native name of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_de_Aibonito" title="R\xc3\xado de Aibonito">Aibonito River</a>. <i>Jatibon</i> most likely translates to "black river" or "night river", the indigenous name of the river which was later turned into a <a href="/wiki/Diminutive" title="Diminutive">diminutive</a> in Spanish (<i>Jatibon-ito</i>). It is common to have native place names in Puerto Rico be historically adopted and modified into the Spanish language such as <i>Mucarabones</i> for example, which is a plural of <i>Mucarabon</i>, meaning "<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_owl" title="Puerto Rican owl">owl</a> river" in Ta\xc3\xadno.\n</p><p>There\'s also a legend that tells of a Spanish soldier called Diego Alvarez who on May 17, 1615, reached one of the highest peaks in the area and upon watching the view, exclaimed "\xc2\xa1Ay, qu\xc3\xa9 bonito!" ("<i>Oh, how pretty!</i>") which eventually was turned into the name of the region. If this is the case, it would be considered <a href="/wiki/Folk_etymology" title="Folk etymology">folk etymology</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Some of the municipality\'s nicknames include <i>Ciudad de las Flores</i> ("City of Flowers") and <i>Jard\xc3\xadn de Puerto Rico</i> ("Puerto Rico\'s Garden") after the region\'s <a href="/wiki/Floral_industry" title="Floral industry">floral industry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aibonito_Festival_of_Flowers" title="Aibonito Festival of Flowers">annual festival</a>, and <i>Ciudad Fr\xc3\xada</i> ("Cold City") and <i>Nevera de Puerto Rico</i> ("Puerto Rico\'s <a href="/wiki/Refrigerator" title="Refrigerator">refrigerator</a>") after the fact that the municipality has recorded some of the island\'s lowest temperatures.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Before the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Spanish colonization of the Americas">Spanish colonization of the Americas</a>, it is believed that there were <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Ta\xc3\xadno people">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> settlements in the region that belonged to <a href="/wiki/Cacique" title="Cacique">Cacique</a> <a href="/wiki/Orocobix" title="Orocobix">Orocobix</a> domain.\n</p><p>After the Spanish arrived, it is believed that a ranch was established in the region by Pedro Zorascoechea in 1630, which led the development of a <a href="/wiki/Hamlet_(place)" title="Hamlet (place)">hamlet</a>. However, it wasn\'t until 1822 when Don Manuel Vel\xc3\xa9z presented himself before the government, representing the inhabitants of the area, to ask for Aibonito to be officially declared a town. This was authorized on March 13, 1824, by Governor Don <a href="/wiki/Miguel_de_la_Torre" title="Miguel de la Torre">Miguel de la Torre</a>. The first Catholic church in Aibonito was built in 1825. The building was replaced by the current church, which was started in 1887 and completed in 1897. After the town was officially constituted, barrios started developing in the area.\n</p><p>On the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> of 1898, around 800 <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Ricans" title="Puerto Ricans">Puerto Rican</a> soldiers were able to defeat the invading <a href="/wiki/United_States_Armed_Forces" title="United States Armed Forces">American troops</a> due to their strategic placement in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Asomante_Mountain&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Asomante Mountain (page does not exist)">Asomante Mountain</a>. This scrimmage came to an end when the Spanish government surrendered on August 12, 1898. The Spanish and Puerto Rican forces at Asomante never surrendered and would have held their position indefinitely if not for the buckling of the Spanish government in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>.\n</p><p>Puerto Rico was ceded by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_of_1898" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Paris of 1898">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became a territory of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. In 1899, the United States conducted its first <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of Puerto Rico finding that the population of Aibonito was 8,596.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> on September 20, 2017, triggered numerous landslides in Aibonito with the significant amount of rainfall.<sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Around 1,500 homes were completely or partially destroyed. The hurricane destroyed the <a href="/wiki/Poultry_industry" class="mw-redirect" title="Poultry industry">poultry industry</a> of Aibonito with nearly 1.8 million chickens killed. Known as "The Garden of Puerto Rico" Aibonito has celebrated a flower festival since 1969. Aibonito\'s <a href="/wiki/Floriculture" title="Floriculture">floriculture</a> industry was completely decimated by Hurricane Mar\xc3\xada.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Aibonito is located in the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_de_Cayey" title="Sierra de Cayey">Sierra de Cayey</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Cordillera Central (Puerto Rico)">Cordillera Central</a> in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Aibonito is the town with the highest elevation in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, located at 2,401 feet above sea level. Some of its mountains are La Sierra (2,394&#160;ft), Asomante (2,042&#160;ft) and Buena Vista (2,042&#160;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_General_Info_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_General_Info-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Barrios">Barrios</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Barrios"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Barrios_of_Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Barrios_of_Aibonito%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.jpg/220px-Barrios_of_Aibonito%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="230" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Barrios_of_Aibonito%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.jpg/330px-Barrios_of_Aibonito%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Barrios_of_Aibonito%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.jpg/440px-Barrios_of_Aibonito%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1100" data-file-height="1148" /></a><figcaption>Subdivisions of Aibonito.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Like all municipalities of Puerto Rico, Aibonito is subdivided into <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico">barrios</a>. The municipal buildings, central square and large Catholic church are located in a small barrio referred to as <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">"el pueblo"</i></span>, near the center of the municipality.<sup id="cite_ref-Pico_1969_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pico_1969-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup A map shows these communities occur in nearly every municipality of the commonwealth. Of the 742 places that were on the list in 2014, the following barrios, communities, sectors, or neighborhoods were in Aibonito: the Municipal neighborhood, Algarrobo barrio, Amoldadero and Los Muros in La Plata barrio, El Campito, El Coqu\xc3\xad, El Fresal, La Espa\xc3\xb1olita, La Represa, Las Bamb\xc3\xbaas, Los Cuadritos, Parcelas Nuevas in Pasto barrio, Parcelas Rabanal, Parcelas Viejas in Pasto, San Luis, Sector El Nueve, and Sector Gallera.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Water_features">Water features</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Water features"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Some of the rivers that flow through Aibonito are the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_de_Aibonito" title="R\xc3\xado de Aibonito">R\xc3\xado de Aibonito</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cuyon_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Cuyon River">R\xc3\xado Cuy\xc3\xb3n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_la_Plata_(Puerto_Rico)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rio de la Plata (Puerto Rico)">R\xc3\xado de la Plata</a> and <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Usab%C3%B3n" class="mw-redirect" title="R\xc3\xado Usab\xc3\xb3n">R\xc3\xado Usab\xc3\xb3n</a>. Rio Manu\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Climate">Climate</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Climate"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Aibonito features a rather mild <a href="/wiki/Tropical_rainforest_climate" title="Tropical rainforest climate">tropical rainforest climate</a>, owing to its elevation and location, close but still slightly removed from the equator, as well as being directly exposed to the wind and moisture of the <a href="/wiki/Trade_Winds" class="mw-redirect" title="Trade Winds">Trade Winds</a>. It borders on a <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_highland_climate" class="mw-redirect" title="Subtropical highland climate">subtropical highland climate</a>, and also a <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a>, though like the former, summers are not much hotter than the warm to cool winters, as it is typical of higher elevations near the equator. As the trade winds coming from the northeast go through <a href="/wiki/Orographic_lift" title="Orographic lift">orographic lift</a>, rainfall is both greater and more consistent throughout the year than in coastal areas (particularly the south coast only minutes away), with fog being a common occurrence. Aibonito holds the record for the lowest temperature in Puerto Rico. That is 40&#160;\xc2\xb0F (4&#160;\xc2\xb0C) on March 9, 1911.<sup id="cite_ref-NOAA_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NOAA-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The highest temperature record is 98&#160;\xc2\xb0F (37&#160;\xc2\xb0C) recorded on September 29, 1920.<sup id="cite_ref-NOAA_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NOAA-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Aibonito is among Puerto Rico\'s coolest towns.\n</p>\n<div>\n<table class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Tourism"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Landmarks_and_places_of_interest">Landmarks and places of interest</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Landmarks and places of interest"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Two of the older landmarks of Aibonito are the <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Old Encanto Theater</i></span>, and <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Moscoso Pharmacy</i></span>. Their building facades reflect early 20th century architecture. The Mirador Overlook allows for panoramic views of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_de_la_Plata_(Puerto_Rico)" title="R\xc3\xado de la Plata (Puerto Rico)">Plata Valley</a>. The Degetau Stone (in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">La Piedra de Degetau</i>) was where Federico Degetau, a writer, found his inspiration. <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Casa Museo Federico Degetau</i></span> is the restored, house museum of the writer. <a href="/wiki/Church_San_Jos%C3%A9_of_Aibonito" title="Church San Jos\xc3\xa9 of Aibonito">San Jos\xc3\xa9 Church</a>, which was designed by architect Pedro Cobreros between 1887 and 1897, was built in 1898 is located in the central plaza. There is a plaque commemoration the 100th anniversary of the Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War at Asomante Memorial, a place with a view of the <a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Cordillera Central (Puerto Rico)">Central Mountain Range</a>. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Trinchera_de_Asomante&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="La Trinchera de Asomante (page does not exist)">Asomante Trench</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">La Trinchera de Asomante</i> is where <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Aibonito_Pass" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Aibonito Pass">part</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> took place. The main square is named after <a href="/wiki/Segundo_Ruiz_Belvis" title="Segundo Ruiz Belvis">Segundo Ru\xc3\xadz Belvis</a>, a national <a href="/wiki/Abolitionism" title="Abolitionism">abolitionist</a> hero. The <a href="/wiki/San_Crist%C3%B3bal_Canyon" title="San Crist\xc3\xb3bal Canyon">San Crist\xc3\xb3bal Canyon</a>, a nine-kilometers-long <a href="/wiki/Canyon" title="Canyon">canyon</a> with <a href="/wiki/Waterfall" title="Waterfall">waterfalls</a> is located between Aibonito and Barranquitas. In <a href="/wiki/Robles,_Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico" title="Robles, Aibonito, Puerto Rico">Robles</a> barrio there is an iron bridge with <a href="/wiki/PR-176" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-176">PR-176</a> and was built in 1892. The annual <a href="/wiki/Aibonito_Festival_of_Flowers" title="Aibonito Festival of Flowers">Festival of Flowers</a> is celebrated each June in Aibonito.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_General_Info_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_General_Info-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>To stimulate local tourism during the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_Puerto_Rico" title="COVID-19 pandemic in Puerto Rico">COVID-19 pandemic in Puerto Rico</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Tourism_Company" title="Puerto Rico Tourism Company">Puerto Rico Tourism Company</a> launched the <i>Voy Turistiendo</i> (I\'m Touring) campaign in 2021. The campaign featured a passport book with a page for each municipality. The <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Voy Turisteando</i></span> Aibonito passport page lists <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Mirador Piedra Degetau</i></span>, <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Federico_Degetau_House_and_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Federico Degetau House and Museum (page does not exist)">Casa Museo Federico Degetau</a></i>, Meyer\'s Nurseries for <a href="/wiki/Agritourism" title="Agritourism">agritourism</a>, and several travel routes through Aibonito including the <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Ruta de Las Flores</i></span>, <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Ruta del Pollo</i></span>, and <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Ruta Cultural</i></span> as places of interest.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div had a <a href="/wiki/Baloncesto_Superior_Nacional" title="Baloncesto Superior Nacional">BSN</a> basketball franchise called the <a href="/wiki/Polluelos_de_Aibonito" title="Polluelos de Aibonito">Polluelos de Aibonito</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Isla_News_PR_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Isla_News_PR-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1986 they beat the defending champions, <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9ticos_de_San_Germ%C3%A1n" title="Atl\xc3\xa9ticos de San Germ\xc3\xa1n">Atl\xc3\xa9ticos Recently, the Polluelos have not been able to see action on the BSN\'s tournaments because of economic and team ownership problems.\nAlso Aibonito had a Double AA baseball. The franchise is also Polluelos de Aibonito. In 1966 they won their only baseball championship.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Festivals_and_events">Festivals and events</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Festivals and events"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Aibonito celebrates its <a href="/wiki/Fiestas_patronales_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Fiestas patronales in Puerto Rico">patron saint</a> festival in late July or early August. The <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Fiestas Patronales de Santiago Apostol</i></span> is a religious and cultural celebration that generally features parades, games, artisans, amusement rides, regional food, and live entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_General_Info_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_General_Info-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The festival has featured live performances by well-known artists such as <a href="/wiki/Ismael_Miranda" title="Ismael Miranda">Ismael Miranda</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Torres" title="Tommy Torres">Tommy Torres</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Fiestas_2014_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fiestas_2014-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Aibonito celebrates the <a href="/wiki/Aibonito_Festival_of_Flowers" title="Aibonito Festival of Flowers">Aibonito Festival of Flowers</a> between late June and early July.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_General_Info_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_General_Info-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> During this event, many visitors from other towns and countries come to Aibonito to see a huge display of various flowers and others. Aibonito also celebrates <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Festival de la Monta\xc3\xb1a</i></span> in November.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Economy">Economy</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Economy"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Agriculture">Agriculture</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Agriculture"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Aibonito has several plantations of <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">tobacco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a>. They are also known for their flower plantations. Also, a popular chicken producer in the island, To-Ricos, operates from Aibonito.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_General_Info_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_General_Info-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Business">Business</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Business"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Baxter_International" title="Baxter International">Baxter International</a> has one of its factories located in Aibonito. They produce medical tools for the hospitals and other medical uses.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Industrial">Industrial</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Industrial"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Other industries located in Aibonito are clothing, furniture and <a href="/wiki/Tapestry" title="Tapestry">tapestry</a> factories, as well as <a href="/wiki/Pottery" title="Pottery">pottery</a> and ceramics.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Current_projects">Current projects</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Current projects"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 2014, <a href="/wiki/William_Alicea_P%C3%A9rez" title="William Alicea P\xc3\xa9rez">William Alicea P\xc3\xa9rez</a>, the mayor of Aibonito stressed that many improvements had been made to the town and municipality of Aibonito and to its urban center. Several infrastructure projects were completed in 2014, including the inauguration of an assisted living center, and a stadium and he announced future plans for a gym, progress on another stadium and other projects that were under way for the benefit of Aibonito. P\xc3\xa9rez indicated that its urban center had been transformed and would continue to be improved upon. The construction of a gym for boxing and martial arts is underway with the help of <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Cotto" title="Miguel Cotto">Miguel Cotto</a>, a Puerto Rican boxing champion.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Government">Government</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Government"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1236090951"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mayoralty_in_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayoralty in Puerto Rico">Mayoralty in Puerto Rico</a></div>\n<p>All municipalities in Puerto Rico are administered by a mayor, elected every four years. On November 4, 2008, <a href="/wiki/William_Alicea_P%C3%A9rez" title="William Alicea P\xc3\xa9rez">William Alicea P\xc3\xa9rez</a> (of the <a href="/wiki/New_Progressive_Party_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="New Progressive Party of Puerto Rico">New Progressive Party</a>), won the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_general_election,_2008" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican general election, 2008">elections</a>, beating former mayor Jose Alberto Diaz (PPD) by 3,658 votes.\n</p><p>The city belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Senatorial_district_VI" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Senatorial district VI">Puerto Rico Senatorial district VI</a>, which is represented by two Senators. In 2012, <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Pereira_Castillo" title="Miguel Pereira Castillo">Miguel Pereira Castillo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Angel_M._Rodr%C3%ADguez" class="mw-redirect" title="Angel M. Rodr\xc3\xadguez">Angel M. Rodr\xc3\xadguez</a> were elected as District Senators.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Transportation">Transportation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Transportation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>There are 15 bridges in Aibonito.<sup id="cite_ref-bridge_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bridge-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Symbols">Symbols</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Symbols"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">municipio</i></span> has an official flag and coat of arms.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flag">Flag</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Flag"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The flag of Aibonito features four equal horizontal bands of blue, white, red, and yellow; a green isosceles triangle based on the hoist side bears the town\'s coat of arms.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The coat of arms features a four-paneled shield. The upper left corner features the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Asomante_Mountain&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Asomante Mountain (page does not exist)">Asomante Mountain</a> with a sword in front, representing the last battles of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> that took place there. The upper right corner features a white band on a blue field, symbolizing the fog that covers the region in winter. To each side of the band, a purple color flower and a golden lily. The lower left corner features the gold tower of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Casa_Manresa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Casa Manresa (page does not exist)">Casa Manresa</a>, to symbolize the spiritual value of the institution.
