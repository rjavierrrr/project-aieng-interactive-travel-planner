It has one barrio called Florida Adentro and two other subdivisions: Florida Zona Urbana and Pajonal <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">comunidad</i></span>. It is part of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area">San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Florida,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Florida was founded first as a <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico">barrio</a> of <a href="/wiki/Barceloneta,_Puerto_Rico" title="Barceloneta, Puerto Rico">Barceloneta</a> in 1881 when a priest named Father Carri\xc3\xb3n, the mayor of Barceloneta, and other dignitaries visited a tract of land of almost 4 acres. They decided to establish a new barrio. The owner of the land, Don Manuel Cintr\xc3\xb3n, granted the land while he retained a piece of it. The barrio was first called <b>Florida Adentro</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-Rico1977_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rico1977-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>During the 20th century, several efforts were made to declare Florida as a <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a>. First, on April 14, 1949, <a href="/wiki/House_of_Representatives_of_Puerto_Rico" title="House of Representatives of Puerto Rico">House Representative</a> Francisco D\xc3\xadaz Marchand presented a project to create a legislative commission that would study the economic and social conditions of the barrio, to determine the suitability of it as an independent municipality. The project was unsuccessful. In 1960, Manuel Fr\xc3\xadas Morales presented a law that would permit the study to establish the municipality but it was also unsuccessful.\n</p><p>Finally, on June 14, 1971, the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Senate of Puerto Rico">Senate of Puerto Rico</a> and Governor Don <a href="/wiki/Luis_A._Ferr%C3%A9" title="Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9">Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9</a> approved the law that officially created the municipality of Florida. It is thus the youngest municipality established in the island.\n</p><p>On September 20, 2017 <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> struck Puerto Rico. In Florida, 1400 homes were a total loss and 2,295 homes were partially damaged.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Florida,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Florida is the second smallest municipality of Puerto Rico, with an area of 10 square miles. As the only municipality in Puerto Rico that has its urban area within the northern <a href="/wiki/Karst" title="Karst">karst</a> region (sometimes referred as the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Karst_(Puerto_Rico)" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern Karst (Puerto Rico)">Northern Karst Hills</a>), it is surrounded by low elevation, red clay and <a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">limestone</a> haystack hills known in <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Spanish" title="Caribbean Spanish">Caribbean Spanish</a> as <i><a href="/wiki/Mogotes" title="Mogotes">mogotes</a></i>. The southern border of the municipality with <a href="/wiki/Ciales,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ciales, Puerto Rico">Ciales</a> remains one of the least developed areas on the island, due to the ruggedness of the karst. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%ADo_Encantado&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="R\xc3\xado Encantado (page does not exist)">R\xc3\xado Encantado</a>, a subterranean river, drains this area.<sup id="cite_ref-Encantado_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Encantado-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Several caves are found in the town, including Rom\xc3\xa1n Cave, Mir\xc3\xb3 Cave, and Juana G\xc3\xb3mez Cave.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Balcon Cave (<span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Cueva Balc\xc3\xb3n</i></span>) is located in Florida.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Florida,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Government"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1236090951"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mayoralty_in_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayoralty in Puerto Rico">Mayoralty in Puerto Rico</a></div>\n<p>After its initial establishment, Florida belonged to the <a href="/wiki/Barceloneta,_Puerto_Rico" title="Barceloneta, Puerto Rico">Barceloneta</a> region.<sup id="cite_ref-Rico1975_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rico1975-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1949 and 1960 there were some attempts to separate the barrio from Barceloneta, but these were unsuccessful. However, in 1974, Governor <a href="/wiki/Luis_A._Ferr%C3%A9" title="Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9">Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_Senate" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican Senate">Puerto Rican Senate</a> officially declared Florida an independent municipality. Its first mayor was Jorge L. P\xc3\xa9rez Pi\xc3\xb1eiro. The current mayor is <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Gerena_Polanco" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Gerena Polanco">Jos\xc3\xa9 Gerena Polanco</a>, of the <a href="/wiki/New_Progressive_Party_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="New Progressive Party of Puerto Rico">New Progressive Party</a> (PNP). He was elected in the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_general_election,_2012" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican general election, 2012">2012 general elections</a>.\n</p><p>The city belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Senatorial_district_III" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Senatorial district III">Puerto Rico Senatorial district III</a>, which is represented by two Senators. In 2008, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Emilio_Gonz%C3%A1lez_Vel%C3%A1zquez" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Emilio Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Vel\xc3\xa1zquez">Jos\xc3\xa9 Emilio Gonz\xc3\xa1lez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Angel_Mart%C3%ADnez_Santiago" class="mw-redirect" title="Angel Mart\xc3\xadnez Santiago">Angel Mart\xc3\xadnez</a> were elected as District Senators.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 Symbols"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">municipio</i></span> has an official flag and coat of arms.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flag">Flag</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Florida,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Flag"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>It consists of three horizontal stripes, with the following colors and widths: green the superior and white the inferior, with five modules of width each one, the center one red, with a width of one module.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Florida,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Field of silver, in an abyss, a <a href="/wiki/Gules" title="Gules">gules</a> (red) anchored cross, like the one in the <a href="/wiki/Asturias" title="Asturias">Asturian</a> district of <a href="/wiki/Llanes" title="Llanes">Llanes</a>. The cross is anchored between two branches of bloomed <a href="/wiki/Poinsettias" class="mw-redirect" title="Poinsettias">poinsettias</a> (<i>Poinsettia pulcherrima</i>). A green terrace represents the hilly terrain of the town, with a stripe forming waves outlined in silver which represents the underground river of Encantado. At the top, a three tower gold crown distinct in municipalities coat of arms. The shield can be surrounded, to its flanks and bottom by two crossed coffee tree branches with fruits.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Names">Names</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Florida,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Names"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Florida derives its name from the abundant flowers and natural resources on its land. It is also known as <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">La Tierra del R\xc3\xado Encantado</i></span> due to an underground river called Encantado.
