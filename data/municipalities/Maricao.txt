.IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;ma\xc9\xbei\xcb\x88kao&#93;</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Maricao_barrio-pueblo" title="Maricao barrio-pueblo">town</a> and the second-least populous <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" Central</a>. It is a small town set around a small square in hilly terrain, north of <a href="/wiki/San_Germ%C3%A1n,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Germ\xc3\xa1n, Puerto Rico">San Germ\xc3\xa1n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sabana_Grande,_Puerto_Rico" title="Sabana Grande, Puerto Rico">Sabana Grande</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yauco,_Puerto_Rico" title="Yauco, Puerto Rico">Yauco</a>; south of <a href="/wiki/Las_Marias,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Las Marias, Puerto Rico">Las Mar\xc3\xadas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lares,_Puerto_Rico" title="Lares, Puerto Rico">Lares</a>, southeast of <a href="/wiki/Mayag%C3%BCez,_Puerto_Rico" title="Mayag\xc3\xbcez, Puerto Rico">Mayag\xc3\xbcez</a>, and west of <a href="/wiki/Adjuntas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Adjuntas, Puerto Rico">Adjuntas</a>. Maricao is spread over 6 barrios and <a href="/wiki/Maricao_barrio-pueblo" title="Maricao barrio-pueblo">Maricao Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and the administrative center of the city).\n</p><p>The town borders the <a href="/wiki/Maricao_Fish_Hatchery" title="Maricao Fish Hatchery">Maricao Fish Hatchery</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Fish_hatchery" title="Fish hatchery">fish hatchery</a> made up of tanks and pools in a garden setting, where some 25,000 fish are raised yearly to stock farm fishponds and island lakes.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The hatchery is part of the <a href="/wiki/Maricao_State_Forest" title="Maricao State Forest">Maricao State Forest</a>, also known as <a href="/wiki/Monte_del_Estado" class="mw-redirect" title="Monte del Estado">Monte del Estado</a>. Though of dryer vegetation than the other mountain forest, Maricao is home to large number of <a href="/wiki/List_of_Puerto_Rican_birds" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Puerto Rican birds">bird species</a>. Its stone observation tower provides far-flung views to the coast and the <a href="/wiki/Mona_Passage" title="Mona Passage">Mona Passage</a>.\n</p><p>Maricao has a <a href="/wiki/2020_United_States_census" title="2020 United States census">2020 census</a> population of 4,455.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maricao,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Haciendajuanitamaricao.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Haciendajuanitamaricao.jpg/220px-Haciendajuanitamaricao.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Haciendajuanitamaricao.jpg/330px-Haciendajuanitamaricao.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Haciendajuanitamaricao.jpg/440px-Haciendajuanitamaricao.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="1600" /></a><figcaption>Hacienda Juanita in Maricao</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Maricao was founded on April 1, 1874, when Bernardo Collado, Juli\xc3\xa1n Ayala, Francisco M. Sojo, Napole\xc3\xb3n Pietri and Leoncio S. Mart\xc3\xadnez requested the Provincial Deputation authorization to establish an official town. It originally belonged to the district of <a href="/wiki/San_Germ%C3%A1n,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Germ\xc3\xa1n, Puerto Rico">San Germ\xc3\xa1n</a>, but they alleged the roads were too long and almost impassable. Their request made references to a <a href="/wiki/Parish" title="Parish">parish</a> already established in 1866.\n</p><p>During the 19th century <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a> boom, Maricao and other regions surged economically. When the spurt of coffee in the island ended by the start of the 20th century, the economic conditions deteriorated again. Many of the old plantation houses have been turned into museums to stimulate the tourism industry. While Puerto Rico still has a niche in the gourmet coffee market, the large scale coffee growing which built Maricao is no longer economically feasible.\n</p><p>Puerto Rico was ceded by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_of_1898" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Paris of 1898">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became a colony of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. In 1899, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_War" title="United States Department of War">United States Department of War</a> conducted a <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of Puerto Rico finding that the population of Maricao was 8,312.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On September 20, 2017 <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> struck Puerto Rico. Several major landslides covered roads into Maricao. Highways and bridges were washed away by flooding rivers. Residents were left incommunicado.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In some areas of Maricao there were more than 25 landslides per square mile.<sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Elderly were especially affected.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 title="Municipality">municipality</a> in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, above the island municipality of <a href="/wiki/Culebra,_Puerto_Rico" title="Culebra, Puerto Rico">Culebra</a>, with 6,449 inhabitants. The 2020 census shows the municipality remains the second least populated with a population of 4,455. The population of the town has decreased during the past decades in part due to the decline in coffee production and the deteriorating economy. In 1899, the population was 8,312. Making the <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> is 174.2 people per square mile (67.0/km<sup>2</sup>).\n</p><p>As a whole, Puerto Rico is mainly made up of people from a <a href="/wiki/Criollo_people" title="Criollo people">Criollo</a> (born on the Island of European descent) or <a href="/wiki/Spanish_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish people">Spanish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_groups_in_Europe" title="Ethnic groups in Europe">European</a> descent, with small groups of <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_groups_of_Africa" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethnic groups of Africa">African</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asian_people" title="Asian people">Asian people</a>. Statistics taken from the 2000 census shows that 90.3% of Maricae\xc3\xb1os have <a href="/wiki/Spanish_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish people">Spanish</a> or <a href="/wiki/White_people" title="White people">White</a> origin, 3.0% are <a href="/wiki/Black_people" title="Black people">black</a>, 0.2% are <a href="/wiki/Amerindian" class="mw-redirect" title="Amerindian">Amerindian</a> etc. The majority of the local population are partly descended from pre Columbian indigenous inhabitants.\n</p><p>There is a general consensus in Puerto Rico that the barrios of Indieras in Maricao have the most people of indigenous origin in Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-Ancestry_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ancestry-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1236090951"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mayoralty_in_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayoralty in Puerto Rico">Mayoralty in Puerto Rico</a></div>\n<p>Maricao\'s first mayor was Juan Ferrer y Arnijas, whose term ran from 1874 to 1876. <a href="/wiki/Gilberto_P%C3%A9rez_Valent%C3%ADn" title="Gilberto P\xc3\xa9rez Valent\xc3\xadn">Gilberto P\xc3\xa9rez Valent\xc3\xadn</a>, alias "El Enano" or the Dwarf, Senatorial district V</a>, which is represented by two senators. In 2016, <a href="/wiki/Luis_Berdiel" class="mw-redirect" title="Luis Berdiel">Luis Berdiel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Cruz" title="Nelson Cruz">Nelson Cruz</a>, from the <a href="/wiki/New_Progressive_Party_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="New Progressive Party of Puerto Rico">New Progressive Party</a>, were elected as district senators.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Symbols">Symbols</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maricao,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Symbols"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">municipio</i></span> has an official flag and coat of arms.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flag">Flag</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maricao,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Flag"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The flag of Maricao derives its colors, design and symbolism from the municipal shield. It consists of a green cloth, with the three usual dimensions of the municipal flags of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, crossing from an end to another a yellow band with three points. The color green symbolizes the vegetation of the municipality and the yellow band symbolizes the mountains of the region.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maricao,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In a silver field, resides an inverted V-shaped green band. Contained in the band are five golden huts. To either side and below the band are a total of three Maricao (<i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Byrsonima_spicata&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Byrsonima spicata (page does not exist)">Byrsonima spicata</a></i>) tree branches with flowers. Surrounding the shield below and to either side are two coffee tree branches. Above it is placed a gold mural crown with three towers outlined in black with green doors and windows.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Name">Name</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maricao,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Name"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Byrsonima_spicata_illustration.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Byrsonima_spicata_illustration.jpg/220px-Byrsonima_spicata_illustration.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="367" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Byrsonima_spicata_illustration.jpg/330px-Byrsonima_spicata_illustration.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Byrsonima_spicata_illustration.jpg/440px-Byrsonima_spicata_illustration.jpg 2x" data-file-width="648" data-file-height="1080" /></a><figcaption>Illustration of Byrsonima spicata or the Maricao tree</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The origin of its name has two versions. It is said to come from the name of the Maricao tree (<i>Byrsonima spicata</i>) which has yellow flowers and grows in the region. The second version is a legend about a <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Ta\xc3\xadno people">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> woman called Mar\xc3\xada that, during the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_colonization" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish colonization">Spanish colonization</a>, fell in love with a Spanish soldier. She informed him of an attack planned by her tribe and the Spanish took the offensive. Mar\xc3\xada was then taken prisoner by her people and tried for <a href="/wiki/Treason" title="Treason">treason</a>. As was the tradition, she was tied to a tree and sacrificed. Allegedly "cao" means "<a href="/wiki/Sacrifice" title="Sacrifice">sacrifice</a>" so Maricao means "Mar\xc3\xada\'s sacrifice"; this is considered <a href="/wiki/Folk_etymology" title="Folk etymology">folk etymology</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Education">Education</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maricao,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Education"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Endangered_Species_Posters_(7217683840).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Endangered_Species_Posters_%287217683840%29.jpg/220px-Endangered_Species_Posters_%287217683840%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Endangered_Species_Posters_%287217683840%29.jpg/330px-Endangered_Species_Posters_%287217683840%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Endangered_Species_Posters_%287217683840%29.jpg/440px-Endangered_Species_Posters_%287217683840%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3008" data-file-height="2000" /></a><figcaption>Kids gathering information on display about endangered species at a school in Maricao on May 4, 2012</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Maricao has several public and private schools distributed through several regions. Public education is handled by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Education" title="Puerto Rico Department of Education">Puerto Rico Department of Education</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Transportation">Transportation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maricao,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Transportation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>There is no direct <a href="/wiki/Highway" title="Highway">highway</a> connection to Maricao. <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_119" title="Puerto Rico Highway 119">Puerto Rico Highway 119</a> and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_120" title="Puerto Rico Highway 120">Puerto Rico Highway 120</a> lead from the north, while <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_121" title="Puerto Rico Highway 121">Puerto Rico Highway 121</a> and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_105" title="Puerto Rico Highway 105">Puerto Rico Highway 105</a> lead from the south.
