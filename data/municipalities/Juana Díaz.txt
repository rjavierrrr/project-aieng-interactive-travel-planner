.IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;\xcb\x88xwana<span class="wrap"> </span>\xcb\x88\xc3\xb0i.as&#93;</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Juana_D%C3%ADaz_barrio-pueblo" title="Juana D\xc3\xadaz barrio-pueblo">town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> located on the southern coast of the island, south of <a href="/wiki/Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico" title="Jayuya, Puerto Rico">Jayuya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ciales,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ciales, Puerto Rico">Ciales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orocovis,_Puerto_Rico" title="Orocovis, Puerto Rico">Orocovis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Villalba,_Puerto_Rico" Rico">Coamo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Santa_Isabel,_Puerto_Rico" title="Santa Isabel, Puerto Rico">Santa Isabel</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> to the south. Juana D\xc3\xadaz is spread over 13 barrios and <a href="/wiki/Juana_D%C3%ADaz_barrio-pueblo" title="Juana D\xc3\xadaz barrio-pueblo">Juana Diaz Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and the administrative center of the city). It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce Metropolitan Statistical Area">Ponce Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>.\n</p><p>Juana D\xc3\xadaz is known as "La Ciudad del Mav\xc3\xad" (Mav\xc3\xad City). <i><a href="/wiki/Mauby" title="Mauby">Mabi</a></i> is a fermented Ta\xc3\xadno beverage made out from the bark of the mavi tree <i><a href="/wiki/Colubrina_elliptica" title="Colubrina elliptica">Colubrina elliptica</a></i>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juana_D%C3%ADaz,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Juana D\xc3\xadaz was founded in 1798 and was known as <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Ciudad de Jacagua</i></span>, in honor of the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> <a href="/wiki/Cacique" title="Cacique">Cacique</a> Jacaguax.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The civil government of this territory was established on April 25, 1798.<sup id="cite_ref-JDHistoria_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JDHistoria-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Puerto Rico was ceded by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_of_1898" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Paris of 1898">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became a territory of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. In August of 1898, <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Crane" title="Stephen Crane">Stephen Crane</a>, a war correspondent for Hearst\'s <i>New York Journal</i>, single-handedly took possession of the town before an American regiment arrived. In 1899, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_War" title="United States Department of War">United States Department of War</a> conducted a <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of Puerto Rico finding that the population of Juana D\xc3\xadaz was 27,896.<sup id="cite_ref-OfficeSanger1900_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OfficeSanger1900-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On September 20, 2017 <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> struck the island of Puerto Rico. In Juana D\xc3\xadaz, 2,500 homes lost their roof.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juana_D%C3%ADaz,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Juana D\xc3\xadaz is located on the southern coast. Several rivers run through the Juana D\xc3\xadaz territory, among them, <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Inab%C3%B3n" title="R\xc3\xado Inab\xc3\xb3n">R\xc3\xado Inab\xc3\xb3n</a> and the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Jacaguas" class="mw-redirect" title="R\xc3\xado Jacaguas">R\xc3\xado Jacaguas</a>, from which Juana D\xc3\xadaz takes it\'s nickname, "Ciudad del Jacaguas". The Guayabal dam between Juana D\xc3\xadaz and Villalba is located in this river. Among its main tributaries are <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Toa_Vaca" class="mw-redirect" title="R\xc3\xado Toa Vaca">R\xc3\xado Toa Vaca</a> in Villalba, also dammed. Both <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Guayabal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lake Guayabal (page does not exist)">Guayabal</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Toa_Vaca&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lake Toa Vaca (page does not exist)">Toa Vaca</a> lakes are visible in the map. Lake Toa Vaca is also the main source of drinking water for Juana D\xc3\xadaz, Ponce and other towns.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The highest point in the municipality is located close to the top of <a href="/wiki/Cerro_Maravilla" title="Cerro Maravilla">Cerro Maravilla</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Barrios">Barrios</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juana_D%C3%ADaz,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Barrios"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Juana_D%C3%ADaz,_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Juana_D%C3%ADaz%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/220px-Juana_D%C3%ADaz%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="241" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Juana_D%C3%ADaz%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/330px-Juana_D%C3%ADaz%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Juana_D%C3%ADaz%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/440px-Juana_D%C3%ADaz%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png 2x" data-file-width="1100" data-file-height="1204" /></a><figcaption>Subdivisions of Juana D\xc3\xadaz.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Like all municipalities of Puerto Rico, Juana D\xc3\xadaz is subdivided into <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico">barrios</a>. The <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Fiestas Patronales de San Ram\xc3\xb3n Nonato</i></span> is a religious and cultural celebration that generally features parades, games, artisans, amusement rides, regional food, and live entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Other festivals and events celebrated in Juana D\xc3\xadaz include:\n</p>\n<ul><li>The Three Kings Festival in Juana D\xc3\xadaz is the largest in all of Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-nqe1r_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nqe1r-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Epiphany_(holiday)" title="Epiphany (holiday)">Three Kings Festival</a> is celebrated on January 6 - religious and cultural event which began in 1884 celebrating the visit of the <a href="/wiki/Biblical_Magi" title="Biblical Magi">Three Kings</a> to baby Jesus. The main event is a parade down Comercio Street to the town\'s plaza where a big altar is set up for the celebration of the Holy Eucharist. The Eucharist begins with an act of the Prophets announcing the coming of the <a href="/wiki/Messiah" title="Messiah">Messiah</a>. The prophesies are followed by the sighting of three kings by the shepherds. The <a href="/wiki/Mass_in_the_Catholic_Church" title="Mass in the Catholic Church">holy mass</a> follows and at the end the Three Kings find and adore baby Jesus. Some people come to the festival dressed up as shepherds.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li><span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Festival del Mab\xc3\xad</i></span> (<a href="/wiki/Mauby" title="Mauby">Mauby</a> Festival) - March</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/American_bullfrog" title="American bullfrog">Bull Frog</a> Festival - April</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Good_Friday" title="Good Friday">Good Friday</a> - Juana D\xc3\xadaz celebrates many activities during Holy Week each year. The most notable is the Good Friday procession that transits through many of the urban communities with representations of the Stations of the Cross. Thousands of juanadinos and people from other towns visit Juana D\xc3\xadaz on Good Friday.</li>\n<li>Llorensiana Week - May</li>\n<li>Puerto Rican Festivities - December</li></ul>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Government">Government</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juana_D%C3%ADaz,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Government"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1236090951"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mayoralty_in_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayoralty in Puerto Rico">Mayoralty in Puerto Rico</a></div>\n<p>Like all municipalities in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, Juana D\xc3\xadaz is administered by a mayor. In 2012, <a href="/wiki/Ram%C3%B3n_Ruiz" title="Ram\xc3\xb3n Ruiz">Ram\xc3\xb3n Ruiz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mart%C3%ADn_Vargas_Morales" title="Mart\xc3\xadn Vargas Morales">Mart\xc3\xadn Vargas Morales</a>, from the <a href="/wiki/Popular_Democratic_Party_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Popular Democratic Party of Puerto Rico">Popular Democratic Party</a>, were elected as district senators.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The other part of the city belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Senatorial_district_VI" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Senatorial district VI">Puerto Rico Senatorial district VI</a>, which has been represented by <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Pereira_Castillo" title="Miguel Pereira Castillo">Miguel Pereira Castillo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Angel_M._Rodr%C3%ADguez" class="mw-redirect" title="Angel M. Rodr\xc3\xadguez">Angel M. Rodr\xc3\xadguez</a> since 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Symbols">Symbols</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juana_D%C3%ADaz,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Symbols"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">municipio</i></span> has an official flag and coat of arms.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flag">Flag</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juana_D%C3%ADaz,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Flag"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The flag of Juana D\xc3\xadaz is rectangular in shape, formed by two triangles whose hypotenuse extends from the upper left corner to the lower right corner. The upper triangle is white, the symbol of silver and the lower triangle is yellow or gold. At its center is the coat of arms of Juana D\xc3\xadaz in its natural colors.<sup id="cite_ref-JD_bandera_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JD_bandera-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juana_D%C3%ADaz,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The coat of arms features The Nazarene Cross. It is in the center of the coat of arms. It stands out subtly between furrows and space, symbolizing the union of two races by means of the inalienable bond of the Christian faith. The cross also symbolizes the <a href="/wiki/Western_world" title="Western world">western</a> <a href="/wiki/Christians" title="Christians">Christian</a> culture.<sup id="cite_ref-JD_S\xc3\xadmbolos_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JD_S\xc3\xadmbolos-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The woman represents - Mrs. Juana D\xc3\xadaz, the towns namesake - with her hands the woman strews thirteen grains of corn into thirteen furrows, symbolizing the seeds that germinated and were the base for the foundation and growth of our town. The woman dresses as those of her time.<sup id="cite_ref-JD_S\xc3\xadmbolos_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JD_S\xc3\xadmbolos-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The sun symbolizes a new horizon, hope in the formation of a town. The sun within the coat of arms has thirteen rays, each represents one of the barrios of Juana D\xc3\xadaz.<sup id="cite_ref-JD_S\xc3\xadmbolos_38-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JD_S\xc3\xadmbolos-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_37-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Thirteen knolls stand out in the coat of arms symbolizing each of the thirteen barrios or barrios of Juana D\xc3\xadaz. The mountains also symbolize that Juana D\xc3\xadaz has been one of the richest mineral towns in Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-JD_S\xc3\xadmbolos_38-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JD_S\xc3\xadmbolos-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_37-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The Indians represents the natives who settled the village before the arrival of the white man. The native carries on his back thirteen sheathed arrows and a bow, and an arrow on his head. The bow and arrow represent the only effective means of defense useful for survival.<sup id="cite_ref-JD_S\xc3\xadmbolos_38-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JD_S\xc3\xadmbolos-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_37-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The shackle and the whip represent Juana D\xc3\xadaz as the martyr of 1887 because the most heinous, repressive institution known at the time was enforced in our town, "El Componte". It was in Juana D\xc3\xadaz where liberal politicians and dedicated patriots were martyred because they fought against the tyranny imposed by Spanish General <a href="/wiki/Romualdo_Palacio_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="Romualdo Palacio Gonz\xc3\xa1lez">Romualdo Palacios</a>. Palacios was the most tyrannical and cruel of all the governors of our nation. "El Componte", a military tribunal, was used to quiet the rebel voices of those who loved law and order.<sup id="cite_ref-JD_S\xc3\xadmbolos_38-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JD_S\xc3\xadmbolos-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_37-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The harp represents the music, the quill pen dipped in the ink well represents the verse, the poetry. Juana D\xc3\xadaz has generously given distinguished poets to Puerto Rican literature.<sup id="cite_ref-JD_S\xc3\xadmbolos_38-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JD_S\xc3\xadmbolos-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_37-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Ciudad del Jacaguas is the name by which Juana D\xc3\xadaz was known in the past. Many people knew Juana D\xc3\xadaz as the city of Jacaguas, but Luis Llor\xc3\xa9ns Torres dubbed it "La Versalles de Ponce" (Ponce\'s Versailles).<sup id="cite_ref-JD_S\xc3\xadmbolos_38-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JD_S\xc3\xadmbolos-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_37-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Four Castles Represent that Juana D\xc3\xadaz reached the stature of city. Cities have five castles in their coat of arms, villages have four and smaller towns have three.<sup id="cite_ref-JD_S\xc3\xadmbolos_38-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JD_S\xc3\xadmbolos-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_37-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Transportation">Transportation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juana_D%C3%ADaz,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Transportation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Among the most important roads in Juana D\xc3\xadaz are the Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9 Expressway (<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_52" title="Puerto Rico Highway 52">PR-52</a>) which connects Juana D\xc3\xadaz to <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a> and the airport in just minutes and <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a> in a little more than one hour. Other important roads include road <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_14" title="Puerto Rico Highway 14">PR-14</a> which runs through <a href="/wiki/Juana_D%C3%ADaz_barrio-pueblo" title="Juana D\xc3\xadaz barrio-pueblo">downtown</a> Juana D\xc3\xadaz on its route between <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cayey,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cayey, Puerto Rico">Cayey</a>, road <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_149" title="Puerto Rico Highway 149">PR-149</a> from Juana D\xc3\xadaz to <a href="/wiki/Manat%C3%AD,_Puerto_Rico" title="Manat\xc3\xad, Puerto Rico">Manat\xc3\xad</a> running through <a href="/wiki/Villalba,_Puerto_Rico" title="Villalba, Puerto Rico">Villalba</a> and the "<a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Cordillera Central (Puerto Rico)">Cordillera Central</a>" and road <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_1" title="Puerto Rico Highway 1">PR-1</a> which goes from Ponce to <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a> through the community of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pastillo_(Juana_D%C3%ADaz,_Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pastillo (Juana D\xc3\xadaz, Puerto Rico) (page does not exist)">Pastillo</a> in the south coast of Juana D\xc3\xadaz.\n</p><p>There are 51 bridges in Juana D\xc3\xadaz.<sup id="cite_ref-bridge_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bridge-39"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Notable_people">Notable people</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juana_D%C3%ADaz,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Notable people"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Natives of the Juana Diaz are referred to as "Juanadinos" within Puerto Rico.
