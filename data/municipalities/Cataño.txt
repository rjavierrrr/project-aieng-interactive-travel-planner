.IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;ka\xcb\x88ta\xc9\xb2o&#93;</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Cata%C3%B1o_barrio-pueblo" title="Cata\xc3\xb1o barrio-pueblo">town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> located on the northeastern coast of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, bordering the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Bay" title="San Juan Bay">San Juan Bay</a> and the Atlantic Ocean, and adjacent to the north and east by <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>; north of <a href="/wiki/Bayam%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico" title="Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico">Bayam\xc3\xb3n</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guaynabo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guaynabo, Puerto Rico">Guaynabo</a>; east of <a href="/wiki/Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico" title="Toa Baja, Puerto Rico">Toa Baja</a> and west of <a href="/wiki/Guaynabo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guaynabo, Puerto Rico">Guaynabo</a> and is part of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Metropolitan_Area#San_Juan_metropolitan_area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan Metropolitan Area">San Juan Metropolitan Area</a>. Cata\xc3\xb1o is spread over 7 barrios and <a href="/wiki/Cata%C3%B1o_barrio-pueblo" title="Cata\xc3\xb1o barrio-pueblo">Cata\xc3\xb1o Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and the administrative center of the city). It is part of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan%E2%80%93Caguas%E2%80%93Guaynabo_metropolitan_area" title="San Juan\xe2\x80\x93Caguas\xe2\x80\x93Guaynabo metropolitan area">San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cata%C3%B1o,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hernando_de_Cata%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hernando de Cata\xc3\xb1o (page does not exist)">Hernando de Cata\xc3\xb1o</a> was chosen to offer his medical services in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> during Francisco Bahamonde de Lugo\'s tenure as <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Governor of Puerto Rico">Governor of Puerto Rico</a> (1564\xe2\x80\x931568). He was one of the first physicians who arrived in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> during its <a href="/wiki/Colonization" title="Colonization">colonization</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and, upon accepting his position, received as payment a piece of land across the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a> islet. From that time, the region started to be recognized by the name of its original owner. As people started establishing in the area, Cata\xc3\xb1o was declared as a <a href="/wiki/Barrio" title="Barrio">barrio</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bayam%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico" title="Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico">Bayam\xc3\xb3n</a>. However, there wasn\'t much success in the town\'s development during these years due to its <a href="/wiki/Swamp" title="Swamp">swamp</a>-like terrain. Still, around 1690, a hermitage was established to allow residents to receive religious services without having to travel to Bayam\xc3\xb3n.\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Houses_in_the_slum_area_known_as_El_Fangitto_(The_Mud)_in_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico.tif" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Houses_in_the_slum_area_known_as_El_Fangitto_%28The_Mud%29_in_San_Juan%2C_Puerto_Rico.tif/lossy-page1-200px-Houses_in_the_slum_area_known_as_El_Fangitto_%28The_Mud%29_in_San_Juan%2C_Puerto_Rico.tif.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="141" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Houses_in_the_slum_area_known_as_El_Fangitto_%28The_Mud%29_in_San_Juan%2C_Puerto_Rico.tif/lossy-page1-300px-Houses_in_the_slum_area_known_as_El_Fangitto_%28The_Mud%29_in_San_Juan%2C_Puerto_Rico.tif.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Houses_in_the_slum_area_known_as_El_Fangitto_%28The_Mud%29_in_San_Juan%2C_Puerto_Rico.tif/lossy-page1-400px-Houses_in_the_slum_area_known_as_El_Fangitto_%28The_Mud%29_in_San_Juan%2C_Puerto_Rico.tif.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4320" data-file-height="3043" /></a><figcaption>Houses in the slum area known as El Fanguito (<span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">fango</i></span> means mud) in San Juan, Puerto Rico</figcaption></figure>\n<p>In the middle of the 19th century, a ferry company was founded to facilitate the transportation of merchandise and passengers through the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Bay" title="San Juan Bay">San Juan Bay</a>. This spurred a growth in the population of Cata\xc3\xb1o, transforming it into one of the most prosperous barrios of Bayam\xc3\xb3n. Still, attempts to separate themselves from Bayam\xc3\xb3n in 1839 were unsuccessful. On June 26, 1893, Bishop Antonio Puig y Montserrat separated the barrios of <a href="/wiki/Cata%C3%B1o_barrio-pueblo" title="Cata\xc3\xb1o barrio-pueblo">Cata\xc3\xb1o</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palo_Seco,_Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico" title="Palo Seco, Toa Baja, Puerto Rico">Palo Seco</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Palmas,_Cata%C3%B1o,_Puerto_Rico" title="Palmas, Cata\xc3\xb1o, Puerto Rico">Palmas</a> from Bayam\xc3\xb3n\'s parish and established an independent parish for the residents of these sectors. In 1927, Cata\xc3\xb1o was officially declared a municipality with the name <b>Hato de Palmas de Cata\xc3\xb1o</b>, which overtime was simply shortened to Cata\xc3\xb1o.\n</p><p>Politics played a crucial part in the foundation of the town, since Bayam\xc3\xb3n was controlled by an administration with opposing ideologies to those of the island\'s Legislature. The separation of Cata\xc3\xb1o from Bayam\xc3\xb3n was a strategy to weaken that opposition.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>With only 12.5 square kilometres (4.8&#160;sq&#160;mi) of territory, Cata\xc3\xb1o is the smallest municipality in Puerto Rico. It is less than half the size of <a href="/wiki/Hormigueros,_Puerto_Rico" title="Hormigueros, Puerto Rico">Hormigueros</a>, the next-smallest in area.\n</p><p>The people of Cata\xc3\xb1o were left in despair when <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Mar\xc3\xada</a> struck on September 20, 2017, destroying their infrastructure and homes. With winds of 240 kilometres per hour (150&#160;mph) the hurricane destroyed and flooded an estimated 650 homes and the roads became rivers (flooded).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> An estimated 80% of homes in the Juana Matos area of Cata\xc3\xb1o were destroyed.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cata%C3%B1o,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Cata\xc3\xb1o consists mostly of flat plains that belong to the Northern region of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. Its northern shore falls on the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Bay" title="San Juan Bay">San Juan Bay</a> of the Atlantic Ocean.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_6-0" class="reference"><a The statue Columbus by Tsereteli was designed by artist <a href="/wiki/Zurab_Tsereteli" title="Zurab Tsereteli">Zurab Tsereteli</a> and would measure 350 feet (110&#160;m) when erected. Tsereteli had offered the statue to the United States as a gift in 1992 with the intention to use it for the celebrations of the 500th year of its voyage. However, it was rejected. The transportation of the statue from Russia to Cata\xc3\xb1o cost $2.4&#160;million. After arriving on the island, the 2,700 bronze pieces of the statue were scattered in a terrain awaiting for funds for the project, but Rivera Sierra was unable to garner enough public support or funding for it. class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cata%C3%B1o,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Government"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1236090951"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mayoralty_in_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayoralty in Puerto Rico">Mayoralty in Puerto Rico</a></div>\n<p>After its initial establishment, Cata\xc3\xb1o belonged to the Bayam\xc3\xb3n region. From 1839 to 1845, there were some attempts to separate the barrio from Bayam\xc3\xb3n, but these were unsuccessful. However, on late 19th century, Bishop Antonio Puig y Montserrat managed to separate Cata\xc3\xb1o establishing their own parish. Cata\xc3\xb1o was finally declared a municipality on April 25, 1927, being its first mayor Alberto D\xc3\xa1vila.\n</p><p>In 1987, Edwin Rivera Sierra was elected as Mayor of Cata\xc3\xb1o. He remained in the position for 16 years, quitting in 2003. He was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Wilson_Soto" title="Wilson Soto">Wilson Soto</a>, who was then officially elected at the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_general_election,_2004" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican general election, 2004">2004 elections in Puerto Rico</a>. After losing a reelection bid in 2008 against <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Rosario" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Rosario">Jos\xc3\xa9 Rosario</a>, Soto was indicted on nine charges.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The city belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Senatorial_district_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Senatorial district II">Puerto Rico Senatorial district II</a>, which is represented by two senators. <a href="/wiki/Migdalia_Padilla" title="Migdalia Padilla">Migdalia Padilla</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carmelo_R%C3%ADos_Santiago" title="Carmelo R\xc3\xados Santiago">Carmelo R\xc3\xados Santiago</a> have served as district senators since 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Symbols">Symbols</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cata%C3%B1o,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Symbols"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">municipio</i></span> has an official flag and coat of arms.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flag">Flag</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cata%C3%B1o,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Flag"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The flag consists of nine horizontal stripes: four blue stripes and five white stripes (substituting for the silver color on the coat of arms). A white and green band traverses diagonally the drape in all its extension, from the upper hoist to the lower fly.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The green color represents the <a href="/wiki/Palm_tree" class="mw-redirect" title="Palm tree">palm trees</a> that are also present in the <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a>. The flag was officially adopted during Jos\xc3\xa9 Alvarez Brunet tenure as mayor on September 5, 1974.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cata%C3%B1o,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The coat of arms of Cata\xc3\xb1o consists of nine horizontal stripes of same the width: four blue and five silver. The colors of the coat and the flag represent the coat of arms of the family of Don Hernando de Cata\xc3\xb1o, an <a href="/wiki/Hidalgo_(Spanish_nobility)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hidalgo (Spanish nobility)">Hidalgo</a> to whom the town owes its name. The color silver represents nobility and the color blue was used by hidalgos on their armories. It symbolizes royalty and serenity.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On top of the coat of arms, there\'s a crown with three towers distinct of others coat of arms. The coat itself is surrounded by two green <a href="/wiki/Palm_tree" class="mw-redirect" title="Palm tree">palm trees</a>, an allusion to one of the original names of the town: Hato de las Palmas de Cata\xc3\xb1o.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_34-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Name">Name</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cata%C3%B1o,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Name"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Aside of its name, derived from its original owner, Cata\xc3\xb1o has several <a href="/wiki/Nicknames" class="mw-redirect" title="Nicknames">nicknames</a>. The city is known as "La Antesala de la Capital" (the <a href="/wiki/Foyer" class="mw-redirect" title="Foyer">Foyer</a> of the Capital) because of its location across the bay from the capital-city of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>. In the 1960s, the residents of Cata\xc3\xb1o jokingly called it "Fanguito Town" because of its \nmany muddy streets and shacks built on stilts over tidal flats.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Education">Education</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cata%C3%B1o,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Education"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Cata\xc3\xb1o has several public and private schools managed by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Education" title="Puerto Rico Department of Education">Puerto Rico Department of Education</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Transportation">Transportation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span Like most other towns in the island, it has a public transportation system consisting of public cars. Taxis are also available around the town.\n</p><p>Cata\xc3\xb1o also has a ferry service known as <i><a href="/wiki/La_Lancha_de_Cata%C3%B1o" class="mw-redirect" title="La Lancha de Cata\xc3\xb1o">La Lancha de Cata\xc3\xb1o</a></i>, or the Ferry of Cata\xc3\xb1o. The ferry service, which has been working since 1853, operates a five-minute harbor route between Cata\xc3\xb1o and <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">Old San Juan</a>, and vice versa daily. There is a large ferry terminal at Cata\xc3\xb1o, and tourists can enjoy the view of the <a href="/wiki/El_Morro_Castle" class="mw-redirect" title="El Morro Castle">Castillo del Morro</a> and the large cruise ships docked at the old San Juan terminal during this journey.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>There are 16 bridges in Cata\xc3\xb1o.<sup id="cite_ref-bridge_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bridge-8"><span
