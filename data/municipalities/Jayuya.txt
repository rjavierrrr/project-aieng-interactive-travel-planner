.IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;xa\xcb\x88\xca\x9du\xca\x9da&#93;</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Jayuya_barrio-pueblo" title="Jayuya barrio-pueblo">town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Cordillera Central (Puerto Rico)">mountainous center region</a> of the island, north of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>; east of <a href="/wiki/Utuado,_Puerto_Rico" title="Utuado, Puerto Rico">Utuado</a>; and west of <a href="/wiki/Ciales,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ciales, Puerto Rico">Ciales</a>. Jayuya is spread over 10 <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico">barrios</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jayuya_barrio-pueblo" title="Jayuya barrio-pueblo">Jayuya Pueblo</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> (the downtown and administrative center of the city). It is the principal city of the Jayuya <a href="/wiki/Micropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Micropolitan Statistical Area">Micropolitan Statistical Area</a>.\n</p><p>Named after the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> tribal leader, <a href="/wiki/Hayuya" title="Hayuya">Hayuya</a>, Jayuya is known for its skilled <a href="/wiki/Wood_carving" title="Wood carving">wood carvers</a>. Among its monuments are the statue of <a href="/wiki/Nemesio_Canales" title="Nemesio Canales">Nemesio Canales</a> and the bust of Hayuya, both by the Puerto Rican sculptor <a href="/wiki/Tom%C3%A1s_Batista" title="Tom\xc3\xa1s Batista">Tom\xc3\xa1s Batista</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The town of Jayuya was founded in 1911, but history traces non-indigenous settlers living in the region as far back as 1878. At that time, a small community was established there, separated from the larger cities of the coasts with little to no communication. Near the end of the 19th century, the town focused on the production of coffee, which greatly boosted the local economy.\n</p><p>In 1911, the town was officially declared a municipality with a population of more than 9,000.\n</p><p>In 1950, the town was the setting of the <a href="/wiki/Jayuya_Uprising" title="Jayuya Uprising">Jayuya Uprising</a>, in which <a href="/wiki/Nationalist_Party_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Nationalist Party of Puerto Rico">Nationalists</a> started a <a href="/wiki/Revolt" class="mw-redirect" title="Revolt">revolt</a> against the Government of the United States led by <a href="/wiki/Blanca_Canales" title="Blanca Canales">Blanca Canales</a>, a social worker, and her cousins, Elio and Doris Torresola. Canales led the group into the main town square and gave a speech, declaring Puerto Rico an <a href="/wiki/Independence_movement_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Independence movement in Puerto Rico">independent republic</a>. The police station was later attacked, telephone lines cut, and the post office burned to the ground. One police officer was killed and three others wounded in these attacks. The group held the town for three days, until it was bombed by U.S. planes supporting a ground attack by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_National_Guard" title="Puerto Rico National Guard">Puerto Rico National Guard</a>. Even though an extensive part of the town was destroyed, the news of the American bombing was not reported outside of Puerto Rico. Instead, it was reported by the American media stateside as an incident between Puerto Ricans.\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Jayuya is located in the center of the island of Puerto Rico, in a valley in the middle of the <a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Cordillera Central (Puerto Rico)">Cordillera Central</a>. Due to this location, its terrain is mostly rough and steep. Some of the highest mountains of the island are located in this region, including <a href="/wiki/Monte_Jayuya" title="Monte Jayuya">Monte Jayuya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cerro_Rosa" title="Cerro Rosa">Cerro Rosa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tres_Picachos" class="mw-redirect" title="Tres Picachos">Tres Picachos</a> and the highest mountain of the island, <a href="/wiki/Cerro_de_Punta" title="Cerro de Punta">Cerro Punta</a>, at 4,389&#160;ft (1,338&#160;m).<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The town\'s total area is estimated at 39.4 square miles (102.05&#160;km<sup>2</sup>)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. Part of the <a href="/wiki/Toro_Negro_State_Forest" title="Toro Negro State Forest">Toro Negro State Forest</a> is located within the municipality of Jayuya. /></a><figcaption>Hacienda San Pedro</figcaption></figure>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Festivals_and_events">Festivals and events</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Festivals and events"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Jayuya celebrates its <a href="/wiki/Fiestas_patronales_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Fiestas patronales in Puerto Rico">patron saint</a> festival in September. The <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Fiestas Patronales Nuestra Se\xc3\xb1ora de la Monserrate</i></span> is a religious and cultural celebration that generally features parades, games, artisans, amusement rides, regional food, and live entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The festival has featured live performances by well-known artists such as <a href="/wiki/Victor_Manuelle" class="mw-redirect" title="Victor Manuelle">Victor Manuelle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Fiestas_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fiestas-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Other festivals and events celebrated in Jayuya include:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Epiphany_(holiday)" title="Epiphany (holiday)">Three Kings</a> Festival- January</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/J%C3%ADbaro_(Puerto_Rico)" title="J\xc3\xadbaro (Puerto Rico)">J\xc3\xadbaro</a> Tomato Festival- February</li>\n<li>National Indigenous Festival- November</li>\n<li>La Monserrate Marathon- September</li></ul>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Tourism">Tourism</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Tourism"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Due to its location, Jayuya is usually mentioned as the obligatory municipality to visit when visiting the center of the island of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. There are several landmarks for tourists to consider like the <a href="/wiki/Nemesio_Canales" title="Nemesio Canales">Nemesio Canales</a> Museum at his <a href="/wiki/Casa_Nemesio_Canales" title="Casa Nemesio Canales">historic home</a> and El Cem\xc3\xad Museum,<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> both located in the same area on a small valley to the west of the <a href="/wiki/Jayuya_barrio-pueblo" title="Jayuya barrio-pueblo">downtown district</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Also, the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Ta\xc3\xadno people">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> tomb in the city\'s downtown has the remains of a Ta\xc3\xadno Indian found in an excavation. Nearby, there\'s a monument to Chief <a href="/wiki/Hayuya" title="Hayuya">Hayuya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cacique" title="Cacique">cacique</a> of the region.\n</p><p>\nIn the outskirts of the city, tourists can visit the <a href="/wiki/Cerro_de_Punta" title="Cerro de Punta">Cerro de Punta</a>, highest peak in the island of Puerto Rico, as well as the other mountains mentioned (<a href="/wiki/Tres_Picachos" class="mw-redirect" title="Tres Picachos">Tres Picachos</a>, for example). The <a href="/wiki/Toro_Negro_State_Forest" title="Toro Negro State Forest">Toro Negro State Forest</a> can also be reached through the city.</p><figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:CasaMuseoBC.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/CasaMuseoBC.jpg/250px-CasaMuseoBC.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/CasaMuseoBC.jpg/375px-CasaMuseoBC.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/0c/CasaMuseoBC.jpg 2x" data-file-width="411" data-file-height="271" /></a><figcaption>Casa Museo Canales</figcaption></figure><p>Jayuya is home to the first <a href="/wiki/Aerostat" title="Aerostat">aerostat</a> in Puerto Rico and one of the world\'s largest. It is located in barrio Mameyes, at 3,200 feet above sea level. When the aerostat lifts, it reaches 3,500 feet where it has a panoramic view of 360 degrees.<sup id="cite_ref-aerostat_balloon_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aerostat_balloon-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Coffee_production" title="Coffee production">Coffee production</a> can be seen at <a href="/wiki/Hacienda" title="Hacienda">Hacienda</a> San Pedro located in Jayuya.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Economy">Economy</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Economy"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Agriculture">Agriculture</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Agriculture"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Jayuya\'s economy has always depended greatly on agriculture. Products like coffee, tomato, and other vegetables are produced and distributed in the region. Livestock is another part of the city\'s economy.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Industry">Industry</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Industry"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In the middle of the 20th century, several manufacturing industries established in Jayuya, aiming to revitalize the economy as well as provide work for the residents. Some of the companies currently in the city are <a href="/wiki/Baxter_Healthcare" class="mw-redirect" title="Baxter Healthcare">Baxter Healthcare</a> and <a href="/wiki/Abbott_Laboratories" title="Abbott Laboratories">Abbott Laboratories</a>. A bridge used by these companies for the delivery of their materials to their plants was destroyed by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a>. Work was set to begin on the bridge in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-Femmy_2019_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Femmy_2019-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Government">Government</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Government"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1236090951"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mayoralty_in_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayoralty in Puerto Rico">Mayoralty in Puerto Rico</a></div>\n<p>Like all municipalities in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, Jayuya is administered by a mayor. The current mayor is <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Gonz%C3%A1lez_Otero" title="Jorge Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Otero">Jorge Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Otero</a>, from the <a href="/wiki/Popular_Democratic_Party_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Popular Democratic Party of Puerto Rico">Popular Democratic Party</a> (PPD). Gonz\xc3\xa1lez was elected at the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_general_election,_1996" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican general election, 1996">1996 general election</a>.\n</p><p>The city belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Senatorial_district_V" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Senatorial district V">Puerto Rico Senatorial district V</a>, which is represented by two senators. In 2012, <a href="/wiki/Ram%C3%B3n_Ruiz" title="Ram\xc3\xb3n Ruiz">Ram\xc3\xb3n Ruiz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mart%C3%ADn_Vargas_Morales" title="Mart\xc3\xadn Vargas Morales">Mart\xc3\xadn Vargas Morales</a>, from the <a href="/wiki/Popular_Democratic_Party_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Popular Democratic Party of Puerto Rico">Popular Democratic Party</a>, were elected as district senators.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Transportation">Transportation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Transportation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>There are 34 bridges in Jayuya.<sup id="cite_ref-bridge_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bridge-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Education">Education</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Education"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>As all other municipalities in the island, Jayuya is part of the Puerto Rico Department of Education. It has several elementary schools distributed throughout the city\'s barrios. However, there\'s currently only one public high school, Josefina Le\xc3\xb3n Zayas High School, located in the city\'s downtown. The high school is <a href="/wiki/LEED" title="LEED">LEED</a> certified, for having a green-building design.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The Metropolitan University, a private institution, has a campus in the municipality\'s downtown area.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Former_mayors">Former mayors</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Former mayors"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<ul><li>1883 Julio Grau Battle \xe2\x80\x93 District Commissioner</li>\n<li>1911\xe2\x80\x9316 Rosario Canales</li>\n<li>1916\xe2\x80\x9320 Juan de Jes\xc3\xbas L\xc3\xb3pez</li>\n<li>1920\xe2\x80\x9324 Ram\xc3\xb3n B. It symbolizes the spirit of people proud of their <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Ta\xc3\xadno people">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> heritage and their hospitality.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Jayuya\'s coat of arms consists of a shield with a three tower crown above, which is standard for municipalities. Inside the shield, centered at the top, a <a href="/wiki/Heraldic_crown" class="mw-redirect" title="Heraldic crown">crown</a> representing Chief <a href="/wiki/Hayuya" title="Hayuya">Hayuya</a>\'s monarchy. In the center a green band with a white border representing the <a href="/wiki/Tres_Picachos" class="mw-redirect" title="Tres Picachos">Tres Picachos</a> (Three Peaks) mountains. The green color symbolizes the countryside and the town\'s matron, <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_of_Montserrat" class="mw-redirect" title="Our Lady of Montserrat">La Virgen de la Monserrate</a>. At the bottom of the shield, is a <a href="/wiki/Petroglyph" title="Petroglyph">petroglyph</a> known as the <i>Ta\xc3\xadno Sun</i> found in Jayuya.<sup
