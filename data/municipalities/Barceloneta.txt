.IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;ba\xc9\xbeselo\xcb\x88neta&#93;</a></span>, <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1177148991"><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">locally</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;ba\xc9\xadselo\xcb\x88neta&#93;</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Barceloneta_barrio-pueblo" title="Barceloneta barrio-pueblo">town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> located in the north region, bordering the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>, north of <a href="/wiki/Florida,_Puerto_Rico" title="Florida, Puerto Rico">Florida</a> (city and municipality in Puerto Rico), east of <a href="/wiki/Arecibo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Arecibo, Puerto Rico">Arecibo</a>, and west of <a href="/wiki/Manat%C3%AD,_Puerto_Rico" title="Manat\xc3\xad, Puerto Rico">Manat\xc3\xad</a>. Barceloneta is spread over 3 barrios and <a href="/wiki/Barceloneta_barrio-pueblo" title="Barceloneta barrio-pueblo">Barceloneta Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and the administrative center of the city). It is part of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area">San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Etymology">Etymology</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barceloneta,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The name of <i>Barceloneta</i> ("Little <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>") is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> city, from where the town\'s founder originated. <i><a href="/wiki/La_Barceloneta,_Barcelona" title="La Barceloneta, Barcelona">Barceloneta</a></i> is also the name of a beach and neighborhood of Barcelona.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_Founding_Symbols_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_Founding_Symbols-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barceloneta,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Barceloneta was founded on July 1, 1881 by Bonocio Llenza Feli\xc3\xba,<sup id="cite_ref-El_Portal_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-El_Portal-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> an immigrant from <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. Barceloneta was one of the last municipalities created by the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish Government">Spanish Government</a> in Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_Founding_Symbols_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_Founding_Symbols-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a decoding="async" width="220" height="144" class="mw-file-element" 2x" data-file-width="1602" data-file-height="1046" /></a><figcaption>Homes in the countryside of Barceloneta in the early 20th century</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Puerto Rico was ceded by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_of_1898" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Paris of 1898">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became a territory of the United States. In 1899, the United States conducted its first <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of Puerto Rico finding that the population of Barceloneta was 9,357.\n</p><p>About a decade after its foundation, the town had a significant economic development led by <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a> and industry. By 1894, there were three <a href="/wiki/Sugarcane" title="Sugarcane">sugarcane</a> estates, 93 sugar mills, and the production of other fruits.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\nBecause of its location next to the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Manat%C3%AD" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Manat\xc3\xad">R\xc3\xado Grande de Manat\xc3\xad</a>, Barceloneta was known for many years as a site of important port activity, for shipment of <a href="/wiki/Sugar" title="Sugar">sugar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salt" title="Salt">salt</a>, as well as passengers to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>. Bonocio\'s son was the owner of a <a href="/wiki/Sail_ship" class="mw-redirect" title="Sail ship">sail ship</a> that made those routes.<sup id="cite_ref-El_Portal_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-El_Portal-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1899, Barceloneta requested to be annexed to the town of <a href="/wiki/Manat%C3%AD,_Puerto_Rico" title="Manat\xc3\xad, Puerto Rico">Manat\xc3\xad</a>. An election was scheduled for August 14, 1899 to decide the matter. However, on August 8, the island suffered the effects of <a href="/wiki/1899_San_Ciriaco_hurricane" title="1899 San Ciriaco hurricane">Hurricane San Ciriaco</a>, which prevented the elections from being held. The following year, Governor <a href="/wiki/George_Whitefield_Davis" title="George Whitefield Davis">George Whitefield Davis</a> ordered the annexation of Barceloneta into Manat\xc3\xad. The annexation was short-lived. Eleven years later, the government reinstated Barceloneta as a municipality.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>After <a href="/w/index.php?title=Plazuela_Sugarcane_Refinery&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Plazuela Sugarcane Refinery (page does not exist)">Central Plazuela</a>, a large sugar cane mill, closed in 1963, Barceloneta started reinforcing its agricultural economy with <a href="/wiki/Pineapple" title="Pineapple">pineapples</a> and other minor fruits. Efforts to revive the economy were not too successful, until pharmaceutical industries established themselves in the town during the 1970s.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_General_Info_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_General_Info-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> For a time, Barceloneta supplied all of the <a href="/wiki/Viagra" class="mw-redirect" title="Viagra">Viagra</a> for North America.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> struck Puerto Rico on September 20, 2017 causing large-scale damage and destruction to infrastructure.<sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In Barceloneta, some homes and establishments along the coast were flooded with up to 15 feet of water, and nearly all wooden homes were destroyed.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barceloneta,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Municipality of Barceloneta sits on the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Karst_(Puerto_Rico)" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern Karst (Puerto Rico)">Northern Karst</a> region of Puerto Rico, on the shores of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. It is bordered by the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Arecibo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Arecibo, Puerto Rico">Arecibo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida,_Puerto_Rico" title="Florida, Puerto Rico">Florida</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Manat%C3%AD,_Puerto_Rico" title="Manat\xc3\xad, Puerto Rico">Manat\xc3\xad</a>. In terms of physical features, the municipality occupies a roughly rectangular area in the northern coast of the island. It has a surface area of 36.4 square miles (94.24 km2).<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_General_Info_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_General_Info-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Barceloneta\'s terrain is predominantly plain. Since it is located in the karst region, it features hills, <a href="/wiki/Ponor" title="Ponor">ponors</a>, and caves. Near the coast, the terrain is occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Ca%C3%B1o_Tiburones" title="Ca\xc3\xb1o Tiburones">Ca\xc3\xb1o Tiburones</a> wetlands.<sup id="cite_ref-boricuaonline_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boricuaonline-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Updated flood zone maps (as of 2019) show that Barceloneta is extremely vulnerable to <a href="/wiki/Flood" title="Flood">flooding</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Humacao,_Puerto_Rico" title="Humacao, Puerto Rico">Humacao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rinc%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico" title="Rinc\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico">Rinc\xc3\xb3n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico" title="Toa Baja, Puerto Rico">Toa Baja</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Corozal,_Puerto_Rico" title="Corozal, Puerto Rico">Corozal</a>. For its many wetlands, Barceloneta ends up being extremely vulnerable when hit by a major hurricane.<sup id="cite_ref-Le\xc3\xb3n_END_Flood_Zones_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Le\xc3\xb3n_END_Flood_Zones-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Water_features">Water features</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barceloneta,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Water features"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Manat%C3%AD" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Manat\xc3\xad">R\xc3\xado Grande de Manat\xc3\xad</a> travels across the municipalities of Manat\xc3\xad and Barceloneta and drains into the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. Barceloneta also features the <a href="/wiki/Ca%C3%B1o_Tiburones" title="Ca\xc3\xb1o Tiburones">Ca\xc3\xb1o Tiburones</a>, a natural reserve.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_General_Info_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_General_Info-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-boricuaonline_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boricuaonline-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Barrios">Barrios</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barceloneta,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Barrios"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Barceloneta,_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Barceloneta%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/220px-Barceloneta%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="164" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Barceloneta%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/330px-Barceloneta%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Barceloneta%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/440px-Barceloneta%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png 2x" data-file-width="847" data-file-height="630" /></a><figcaption>Barrios of Barceloneta</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Like all municipalities of Puerto Rico, Barceloneta is subdivided into <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico">barrios</a>. The municipal buildings, central square and large Catholic church are located in a small barrio referred to as <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">"el pueblo"</i></span>, near the center of the municipality.<sup id="cite_ref-Law2015_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Law2015-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup These companies have chosen Barceloneta to establish their facilities for its underground water reservoirs. The water in Barceloneta requires very little treatment for use in the manufacturing of pharmaceutical products.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_General_Info_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_General_Info-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The employment created by these companies, along with a well-developed plan for urban growth, has allowed Barceloneta to be more than simply a town where people go to work. <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Premium_Outlets" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Premium Outlets">Puerto Rico Premium Outlets</a>, near the <a href="/wiki/PR-22" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-22">expressway</a>, have made the town into a destination for shopping and consumerism and consequently a place for investment in food chains and entertainment. Increased employment opportunities have also caused an increase in population in Barceloneta and its surrounding municipalities. \n</p><p>Barceloneta is also known for its black sand beaches.<sup class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barceloneta,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Government"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Barceloneta is known for its political stability. Mayor <a href="/wiki/Sol_Luis_Fontanes" title="Sol Luis Fontanes">Sol Luis Fontanes</a> held the mayor\'s seat for the <a href="/wiki/Popular_Democratic_Party_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Popular Democratic Party of Puerto Rico">Popular Democratic Party</a> (PPD) from 1986 until 2012. He assumed the role after the sudden death of elected mayor <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9ctor_Ru%C3%ADz_Mart%C3%ADnez" class="mw-redirect" title="H\xc3\xa9ctor Ru\xc3\xadz Mart\xc3\xadnez">H\xc3\xa9ctor Ru\xc3\xadz Mart\xc3\xadnez</a>, also of the PPD. After that, Font\xc3\xa1nes was elected by only a narrow margin (49.2% vs. 48.6%), but he gained popularity with time. At the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_general_election,_2008" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican general election, 2008">2008 general elections</a>, Font\xc3\xa1nes received more than 70% of the votes and his margin of victory was one of the biggest of that election.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> However, on 2012, Font\xc3\xa1nes was arrested on charges of <a href="/wiki/Extortion" title="Extortion">extortion</a>, after 24 years as mayor. He resigned on March 1, 2012 while in prison.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2012, <a href="/wiki/Wanda_Soler" class="mw-redirect" title="Wanda Soler">Wanda Soler</a> was elected as Font\xc3\xa1nes replacement.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_Mayor,_Festivals,_Places_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_Mayor,_Festivals,_Places-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> She was reelected in 2020 for her third term with 75% of the vote.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>40<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The city belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Senatorial_district_III" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Senatorial district III">Puerto Rico Senatorial district III</a>, which is represented by two senators. In 2008, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Emilio_Gonz%C3%A1lez_Vel%C3%A1zquez" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Emilio Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Vel\xc3\xa1zquez">Jos\xc3\xa9 Emilio Gonz\xc3\xa1lez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Angel_Mart%C3%ADnez_Santiago" class="mw-redirect" title="Angel Mart\xc3\xadnez Santiago">Angel Mart\xc3\xadnez</a> were elected as district senators.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Names_and_symbols">Names and symbols</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barceloneta,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Names and symbols"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">municipio</i></span> has an official flag and coat of arms.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>42<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flag">Flag</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barceloneta,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Flag"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The flag of Barceloneta is a variation of the flag of <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, from where the town borrows its name. It features four fields, two of them bearing the <a href="/wiki/St_George%27s_Cross" class="mw-redirect" title="St George&#39;s Cross">cross of Saint George</a> (in Catalan, <i>Sant Jordi</i>), with five <a href="/wiki/Senyera" title="Senyera">red and yellow bars</a> in vertical direction to the right of each cross. These are all symbols pertaining to the city of <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, and the community of <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>, where it is located, since the town was founded by a <a href="/wiki/Catalan_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Catalan people">Catalan</a> immigrant. The remaining two fields in the flag are blue, representing the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>, which lies to the north of Barceloneta.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_Founding_Symbols_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_Founding_Symbols-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-boricuaonline_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boricuaonline-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-44"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>44<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barceloneta,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Barceloneta\'s coat of arms is similar to the flag, since it features the same <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalan</a> symbols. However, it features three <a href="/wiki/Sugarcane" title="Sugarcane">sugarcane flowers</a> (<i>guajanas</i>) on top of the blue fields. The sugarcane flowers symbolize the source of life of the old <a href="/wiki/Economy_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Economy of Puerto Rico">agricultural economy</a> of Barceloneta. The blue symbolizes the beaches and marine ports that contributed to the industry and commerce of the town. Like many coat of arms, it features a crown with three towers.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_Founding_Symbols_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_Founding_Symbols-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Nicknames">Nicknames</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barceloneta,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Nicknames"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Barceloneta is referred to as <i>"La Ciudad de las Pi\xc3\xb1as"</i> (Pineapple City) for its many <a href="/wiki/Pineapple" title="Pineapple">pineapple</a> plantations. It is also known as <i>"La Ciudad Industrial"</i> (Industrial City) for its many industries, mainly <a href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical" class="mw-redirect" title="Pharmaceutical">pharmaceutical</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>45<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Other names are <i>"Ciudad de los Indios"</i> (City of the Indians) and <i>"Pueblo de <a href="/wiki/Sixto_Escobar" title="Sixto Escobar">Sixto Escobar</a>"</i> (Town of Sixto Escobar) for being the birthplace of the Puerto Rican boxer.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
