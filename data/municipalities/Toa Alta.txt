.IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;\xcb\x88toa<span class="wrap"> </span>\xcb\x88alta&#93;</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Toa_Alta_barrio-pueblo" title="Toa Alta barrio-pueblo">town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> located in the northern coast of the island, north of <a href="/wiki/Naranjito,_Puerto_Rico" title="Naranjito, Puerto Rico">Naranjito</a>; south of <a href="/wiki/Dorado,_Puerto_Rico" title="Dorado, Puerto Rico">Dorado</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico" title="Toa Baja, Puerto Rico">Toa Baja</a>; east of <a href="/wiki/Vega_Alta,_Puerto_Rico" title="Vega Alta, Puerto Rico">Vega Alta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Corozal,_Puerto_Rico" title="Corozal, Puerto Rico">Corozal</a>; and west of <a href="/wiki/Bayam%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico" title="Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico">Bayam\xc3\xb3n</a>. Toa Alta is spread over eight barrios and <a href="/wiki/Toa_Alta_barrio-pueblo" title="Toa Alta barrio-pueblo">Toa Alta Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and the administrative center of the city). It is part of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan%E2%80%93Caguas%E2%80%93Guaynabo_metropolitan_area" title="San Juan\xe2\x80\x93Caguas\xe2\x80\x93Guaynabo metropolitan area">San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo metropolitan statistical area</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> There are nine barrios in Toa Alta and altogether in 2020 had a population of 66,852. R\xc3\xado de la Plata, is a large river that runs through Toa Alta. Toa Alta celebrates its patron saint festival in May.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toa_Alta,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Toa Alta is located west of the capital city of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a> and was founded in 1751, making it one of the oldest towns on the main island of Puerto Rico. The construction of the San Fernando Rey Church in the main town square began in 1752. It is popularly said that the name Toa Alta comes from the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno_language" title="Ta\xc3\xadno language">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> word for mother or fertility, <i>Thoa</i>. Most likely the word <i>toa</i> comes from the Taino word for <i>valley</i> or <i>mountain</i>; the region is also known as <i>Valle del Toa</i> (Toa Valley).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Over the years, agriculture became an important economic force in the area. At the peak of the agricultural economy, the town was also known as the <i>"Granja de los Reyes Cat\xc3\xb3licos"</i> (the Farm of the Catholic Monarchs). The town is also called <i>Ciudad del Josco</i>. The town is commonly known by its nickname <i>La Cuna de los Poetas</i>, or "Cradle of Poets", due to the numerous <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish-language</a> poets born there, such as <a href="/wiki/Abelardo_D%C3%ADaz_Alfaro" title="Abelardo D\xc3\xadaz Alfaro">Abelardo D\xc3\xadaz Alfaro</a> and the musician Tom\xc3\xa1s "Masso" Rivera.<sup id="cite_ref-Plan_2012_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Plan_2012-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> on September 20, 2017 triggered numerous landslides in Toa Alta.<sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Many homes and roads in Toa Alta were destroyed by the hurricane.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div
