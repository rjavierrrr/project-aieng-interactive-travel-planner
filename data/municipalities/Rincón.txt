.IPA-label-small{font-size:85%}.mw-parser-output .references .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> founded in 1771 by Don Luis de A\xc3\xb1asco, who previously founded <a href="/wiki/A%C3%B1asco,_Puerto_Rico" title="A\xc3\xb1asco, Puerto Rico">A\xc3\xb1asco</a> in 1733. It is located in the Western Coastal Valley, west of <a href="/wiki/A%C3%B1asco,_Puerto_Rico" title="A\xc3\xb1asco, Puerto Rico">A\xc3\xb1asco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aguada,_Puerto_Rico" title="Aguada, Puerto Rico">Aguada</a>. Rinc\xc3\xb3n is spread over 9 barrios and <a href="/wiki/Rinc%C3%B3n_barrio-pueblo" title="Rinc\xc3\xb3n barrio-pueblo">Rinc\xc3\xb3n Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and the administrative center of the city). It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Aguadilla-Isabela-San_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Aguadilla-Isabela-San Sebasti\xc3\xa1n Metropolitan Statistical Area">Aguadilla-Isabela-San Sebasti\xc3\xa1n Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>.\n</p><p>The municipality is home to many of the <a href="/wiki/Surfing" title="Surfing">surfing</a> beaches in Puerto Rico, including <a href="/wiki/Domes_Beach" title="Domes Beach">Domes</a>, Marias,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Tres Palmas, Sandy Beach, Pools Beach, C\xc3\xb3rcega Beach and Rinc\xc3\xb3n Town Beach. The word "Rinc\xc3\xb3n" means "nook" in <a href="/wiki/Castilian_Spanish" title="Castilian Spanish">Castilian Spanish</a>. Rinc\xc3\xb3n is in the north, on the westernmost tip of Puerto Rico.\n</p><p>Rincon has a large population of Non-Hispanic whites(most from the mainland United States and United Kingdom) when compared to the rest of the Island. Its known to suffer strong gentrification from the increasing Mainland US population moving to the Municipality(Puerto Ricans &amp; Non-Hispanic combined) leading to higher rent prices and higher In 1885, Benito Cumpiano was mayor of R\xc3\xadncon and in 1890, Victor Cumpiano was R\xc3\xadncon\'s mayor.<sup id="cite_ref-Puerto_Rico._Office_of_Historian_1949_p._306_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Puerto_Rico._Office_of_Historian_1949_p._306-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Puerto Rico was ceded by Spain in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_of_1898" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Paris of 1898">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became a territory of the United States. In 1899, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_War" title="United States Department of War">United States Department of War</a> conducted a <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of Puerto Rico finding that the population of Rinc\xc3\xb3n was 6,641.\n</p><p>On September 20, 2017 <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> struck the island of Puerto Rico. In Rinc\xc3\xb3n, infrastructure was damaged and about 400 homes were damaged or completely destroyed. Intense rainfall and winds triggered numerous landslides.<sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The storm surge bought 4 feet of water into homes on the coast.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rinc%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Rinc\xc3\xb3n is located on the western coast of Puerto Rico,<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> on the westernmost tip.<sup id="cite_ref-Places_2021_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Places_2021-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Updated flood zone maps (as of 2019) show that Rinc\xc3\xb3n is extremely vulnerable to <a href="/wiki/Flood" title="Flood">flooding</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Humacao,_Puerto_Rico" title="Humacao, Puerto Rico">Humacao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico" title="Toa Baja, Puerto Rico">Toa Baja</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barceloneta,_Puerto_Rico" title="Barceloneta, Puerto Rico">Barceloneta</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Corozal,_Puerto_Rico" title="Corozal, Puerto Rico">Corozal</a>. For its high levels of erosion, Rinc\xc3\xb3n is vulnerable in the case of a major <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">hurricane</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Le\xc3\xb3n_END_Flood_Zones_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Le\xc3\xb3n_END_Flood_Zones-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Barrios">Barrios</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rinc%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Barrios"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Like all municipalities of Puerto Rico, Rinc\xc3\xb3n is subdivided into <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico">barrios</a>. The construction of BONUS started in 1960, and the <a href="/wiki/Reactor_pressure_vessel" title="Reactor pressure vessel">reactor</a> had its first controlled nuclear chain reaction on April 13, 1964, achieving full power operation in September 1965. Operation of the BONUS reactor was terminated in June 1968 because of technical difficulties and the ensuing need for high-cost modifications.<sup id="cite_ref-DOE_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DOE-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> General decontamination of the reactor was performed with the goal of meeting unrestricted use criteria in all accessible areas of the building. Residual radioactive materials remaining in the structure were isolated or shielded to protect site visitors and workers. During subsequent years, more <a href="/wiki/Radioactive_contamination" title="Radioactive contamination">radioactive contamination</a> was identified in portions of the building, and additional clean-up and shielding activities were conducted in the 1990s and early 2000s.<sup id="cite_ref-DOE_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DOE-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was Puerto Rico\'s only nuclear reactor.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Demographics">Demographics</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rinc%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Demographics"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Rinc\xc3\xb3n reported the smallest decline in population of any Puerto Rican municipality between the 2010 and 2020 censuses (no municipality recorded an increase). Rinc\xc3\xb3n rose to international recognition through the 1968 <a href="/wiki/World_Surfing_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="World Surfing Championship">World Surfing Championship</a>, which was held at <a href="/wiki/Domes_Beach" title="Domes Beach">Domes</a> and Maria\'s Beaches. The winter surf along Rinc\xc3\xb3n\'s coast is some of the best in the region. Generally regarded as one of the best surf spots across the globe, Rinc\xc3\xb3n draws surfers from around the world and is the center of the island\'s surf scene. Dubbed the "<a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>\'s Hawaii," winter waves here can approach 25\xe2\x80\x9330 feet (6.7m) in height, sometimes equaling the force of the surf on <a href="/wiki/Oahu" title="Oahu">Oahu</a>\'s north shore. class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>There are 8 bridges in Rinc\xc3\xb3n.<sup id="cite_ref-bridge_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bridge-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Symbols">Symbols</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rinc%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Symbols"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">municipio</i></span> has an official flag and coat of arms.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flag">Flag</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rinc%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Flag"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Created and designed by Evaristo Cardona Moreno and art performed by Jose Luis Cardona Martinez (his eldest son). The colors of the Rinc\xc3\xb3n flag are red, orange, yellow, green and white. The triangle has a five-pointed white star representing the urban zone. There are white stars of equal size which represent the 9 barrios. The red and orange symbolize the vitality and vigor of its people. The yellow symbolizes Christianity, the green symbolizes vegetation and hope for progress and the white symbolizes the integrity between its barrios and urban zone.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rinc%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Created and designed by Angel L. Cardona Moreno, the shield consists of a green and orange Spanish blazon, with a yellow band inclined left to right. To the right and on a green background a cross, symbol of Christendom. To the left and on an orange background a Spanish ship, symbol of the discovery of Puerto Rico, in the coasts of Rinc\xc3\xb3n (this is greatly disputed, as the towns of <a href="/wiki/Aguada,_Puerto_Rico" title="Aguada, Puerto Rico">Aguada</a>, Rinc\xc3\xb3n, and <a href="/wiki/A%C3%B1asco,_Puerto_Rico" title="A\xc3\xb1asco, Puerto Rico">A\xc3\xb1asco</a> all claim entry-point status).<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-34"><span
