Salinas is spread over 5 barrios and <a href="/wiki/Salinas_barrio-pueblo" title="Salinas barrio-pueblo">Salinas Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and the administrative center of the city).\n</p><p>It has long been a fishing spot for <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Ricans</a>, known for its beaches, fish variety and the birthplace of the famous "<a href="/wiki/Mojo_(sauce)" title="Mojo (sauce)">mojo</a> isle\xc3\xb1o".\n</p><p>Although Salinas doesn\'t have any commercial airports, there is a military training area there, <a href="/wiki/Camp_Santiago" class="mw-redirect" title="Camp Santiago">Camp Santiago</a>, which is one of the training centers of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_National_Guard" title="Puerto Rico National Guard">Puerto Rico National Guard</a>. <a href="/wiki/Army_National_Guard" title="Army National Guard">Army National Guard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Air_National_Guard" title="Air National Guard">Air National Guard</a>, State Guard, U.S. Army <a href="/wiki/Reserve_Officers%27_Training_Corps" title="Reserve Officers&#39; Training Corps">ROTC</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Reserve" title="United States Army Reserve">U.S. Army Reserve</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">U.S. Army</a> also conduct military training at Camp Santiago.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salinas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Salinas was founded in 1840. On July 22, 1841, its first municipal council was established by Don Agust\xc3\xadn Col\xc3\xb3n Pacheco as Mayor, Don Jose Maria Cadavedo as Sargent of Arms, Don Juan Colon as Captain of the Civil Guard and five hacendados which were Don Antonio Semidey, Don Antonio Morelli, Don Francisco Secola, Don Julio Delannoy and Don Jose Antonio Torres. In 1847 it was annexed to the municipality of Guayama until 1851 when it regained its status as a municipality.\n</p><p>Puerto Rico was ceded by Spain in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1898)" title="Treaty of Paris (1898)">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became a territory of the United States. In 1899, the United States conducted its first census of Puerto Rico finding that the population of Salinas was 5,731\n</p><p>In the 21st century the availability of clean drinking water has become an issue for Puerto Rico and especially for Salinas which is located in a dry region of the island.<sup id="cite_ref-Kaufman_2019_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kaufman_2019-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On September 20, 2017 <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> struck the island of Puerto Rico. In Salinas, 2800 homes were destroyed by the winds, flooded rivers, and ocean surge.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span (30&#160;in)  of rain caused the river to flood neighborhoods and destroy homes in Salinas.<sup id="cite_ref-NPR.org_2022_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NPR.org_2022-4"><span data-file-width="558" data-file-height="516" /></a><figcaption>Climate chart for Salinas</figcaption></figure>\n<p>According to data provided by the <a href="/wiki/Aguirre,_Salinas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Aguirre, Salinas, Puerto Rico">Aguirre weather station</a>, Salinas has a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_savanna_climate" title="Tropical savanna climate">tropical savanna climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="K\xc3\xb6ppen climate classification">K\xc3\xb6ppen</a>: <i>Aw</i>). The annual average temperature of Salinas is 78.3&#160;\xc2\xb0F (25.7&#160;\xc2\xb0C), of which August is the hottest month with an average temperature of 81.0&#160;\xc2\xb0F (27.2&#160;\xc2\xb0C), and January is the coolest month with an average temperature of 75.2&#160;\xc2\xb0F (24.0&#160;\xc2\xb0C).<sup id="cite_ref-NCEI_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NCEI-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> There are about 105 days with a temperature above 90&#160;\xc2\xb0F (32&#160;\xc2\xb0C) every year, and there are 4 hot days with a temperature above 95&#160;\xc2\xb0F (35&#160;\xc2\xb0C) every year; at the same time, there are also 4 nights with a temperature below 60&#160;\xc2\xb0F (16&#160;\xc2\xb0C) every year.<sup id="cite_ref-NOWData_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NOWData-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The annual precipitation in Salinas is 40.37 inches (1,025&#160;mm), of which the rainy season is from May to November, and the precipitation accounts for about 82.5% of the whole year.<sup id="cite_ref-NCEI_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NCEI-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Among them, September is the wettest, and the precipitation can reach 6.85 inches (174&#160;mm).<sup id="cite_ref-NCEI_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NCEI-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The temperature extremes ever recorded in Salinas ranged from 51&#160;\xc2\xb0F (11&#160;\xc2\xb0C) on December 3, 2013 to 100&#160;\xc2\xb0F (38&#160;\xc2\xb0C) on September 18, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-NOWData_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NOWData-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span
