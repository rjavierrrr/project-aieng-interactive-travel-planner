.references .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish </span>\xcb\x88\xc9\xa3\xc9\xbeande&#93;</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_barrio-pueblo" title="R\xc3\xado Grande barrio-pueblo">town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> located on the eastern edge of the R\xc3\xado Grande is spread over eight barrios and <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_barrio-pueblo" title="R\xc3\xado Grande barrio-pueblo">R\xc3\xado Grande Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and the administrative center of the city). A big portion of <a href="/wiki/El_Yunque_National_Forest" title="El Yunque National Forest">El Yunque National Forest</a>, including <a href="/wiki/El_Yunque_(Puerto_Rico)" title="El Yunque (Puerto Rico)">El Yunque</a>\'s peak, is located within the municipality.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%ADo_Grande,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>R\xc3\xado Grande was founded on July 16, 1840, by Desiderio and Quilimaco Escobar, with the approval of Governor Miguel L\xc3\xb3pez. It was named after the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_(Puerto_Rico)" title="R\xc3\xado Grande (Puerto Rico)">R\xc3\xado Grande</a> and is located where the R\xc3\xado Grande and the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Esp%C3%ADritu_Santo" title="R\xc3\xado Esp\xc3\xadritu Santo">R\xc3\xado Esp\xc3\xadritu Santo</a> join.\n</p><p>In 1894, there were two sugar cane <a href="/wiki/Hacienda" title="Hacienda">haciendas</a> and 256 <a href="/wiki/Estancias" class="mw-redirect" title="Estancias">estancias</a> growing minor fruits.<sup id="cite_ref-1894_directory_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1894_directory-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> on September 20, 2017, triggered numerous landslides in R\xc3\xado Grande with the significant amount of rainfall.<sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Infrastructure was destroyed, trees were ripped out of the ground, and rivers were breached causing property losses to approximately 5000 people. Of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_amazon" title="Puerto Rican amazon">Puerto Rican amazon</a>, 230 in captivity did survive.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span de Luquillo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> A large
