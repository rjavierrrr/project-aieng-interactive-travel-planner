.IPA-label-small{font-size:85%}.mw-parser-output .references .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> located on the northeast coast of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. It lies immediately east of the capital <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trujillo_Alto,_Puerto_Rico" title="Trujillo Alto, Puerto Rico">Trujillo Alto</a>; north of <a href="/wiki/Gurabo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Gurabo, Puerto Rico">Gurabo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juncos,_Puerto_Rico" title="Juncos, Puerto Rico">Juncos</a>; and west of <a href="/wiki/Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Can\xc3\xb3vanas, Puerto Rico">Can\xc3\xb3vanas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lo%C3%ADza,_Puerto_Rico" title="Lo\xc3\xadza, Puerto Rico">Lo\xc3\xadza</a>. Carolina is spread over 12 barrios plus <a href="/wiki/Carolina_barrio-pueblo" title="Carolina barrio-pueblo">Carolina Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and administrative center). It is part of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area">San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>, and home to Puerto Rico\'s main airport, the <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Mar%C3%ADn_International_Airport" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn International Airport">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn International Airport</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carolina,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The town was founded by <a href="/wiki/Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Spanish colonization of the Americas">Spanish</a> colonists in 1816 as <b>Trujillo Bajo</b> ("lower Trujillo"), along with its counterpart <a href="/wiki/Trujillo_Alto,_Puerto_Rico" title="Trujillo Alto, Puerto Rico">Trujillo Alto</a> after <a href="/wiki/Trujillo,_Caceres" class="mw-redirect" title="Trujillo, Caceres">Trujillo, Spain</a>. In 1857 it was renamed to <b>San Fernando de la Carolina</b>, later shortened to <i>Carolina</i>, after <a href="/wiki/Charles_II_of_Spain" title="Charles II of Spain">Charles II of Spain</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The city is known as <i>"Tierra de Gigantes"</i> (Land of Giants), not only for well-known Carolina resident <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Birriel" title="Felipe Birriel">Don Felipe Birriel Gonz\xc3\xa1lez</a> (who was 7\'11"), but also in honor of other people from Carolina, including <a href="/wiki/Poet" title="Poet">poet</a> <a href="/wiki/Julia_de_Burgos" title="Julia de Burgos">Julia de Burgos</a> and most notably the first <a href="/wiki/Latin_Americans" title="Latin Americans">Latin American</a> player named to <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Hall_of_Fame" class="mw-redirect" title="Hall of Fame">Hall of Fame</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Clemente" title="Roberto Clemente">Roberto Clemente</a>. Carolina was also home to <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_T._Pi%C3%B1ero" title="Jes\xc3\xbas T. Pi\xc3\xb1ero">Jes\xc3\xbas T. Pi\xc3\xb1ero</a>, the first Puerto Rican to be appointed as <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Governor of Puerto Rico">governor</a> by the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> government.\nThe city is also known as <i>"El Pueblo de los Tumba Brazos"</i> (The Arm Hackers\' Town). During the late 1800s, the town\'s major export was <a href="/wiki/Sugarcane" title="Sugarcane">sugarcane</a>.\n</p><p>Puerto Rico was ceded by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_of_1898" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Paris of 1898">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became a territory of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. In 1899, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_War" title="United States Department of War">United States Department of War</a> conducted a <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of Puerto Rico finding that the population of Carolina was 11,965.<sup id="cite_ref-OfficeSanger1900_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OfficeSanger1900-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Flooding_i_n_Carolina_(37322170706).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Flooding_i_n_Carolina_%2837322170706%29.jpg/220px-Flooding_i_n_Carolina_%2837322170706%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="109" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Flooding_i_n_Carolina_%2837322170706%29.jpg/330px-Flooding_i_n_Carolina_%2837322170706%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Flooding_i_n_Carolina_%2837322170706%29.jpg/440px-Flooding_i_n_Carolina_%2837322170706%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2160" data-file-height="1066" /></a><figcaption>Flooding in Carolina after Hurricane Maria on September 20, 2017.</figcaption></figure>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> on September 20, 2017 caused flooding in Carolina.<sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The R\xc3\xado Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza floods left around 500 homes uninhabitable. The Roberto Clemente stadium lost its roof and many other structures, bridges and roads were damaged to a toll of $87 million. Given the Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn International Airport is in Carolina, the destruction in Carolina created a challenge and the airport remained closed for three days.<sup id="cite_ref-6" elected every four years. The current mayor of Carolina is <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Aponte_Dalmau" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Aponte Dalmau">Jos\xc3\xa9 Aponte Dalmau</a>, of the <a href="/wiki/Popular_Democratic_Party_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Popular Democratic Party of Puerto Rico">Popular Democratic Party</a> (PPD). He was elected in 2007, after a special election, succeeding his late father, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Aponte_de_la_Torre" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Aponte de la Torre">Jos\xc3\xa9 Aponte de la Torre</a>. Aponte de la Torre was elected mayor in 1984 and served for 23 years.\n</p><p>The city belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Senatorial_district_VIII" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Senatorial district VIII">Puerto Rico Senatorial district VIII</a>, which is represented by two Senators. In 2012, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_A._Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Pedro A. Rodr\xc3\xadguez">Pedro A. Rodr\xc3\xadguez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luis_Daniel_Rivera" title="Luis Daniel Rivera">Luis Daniel Rivera</a> were elected as District Senators.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p><span class="anchor" id="Police"></span>The Carolina Police Department, with most of its precincts in the northern half of the city due to the density of the population, handle law enforcement responsibilities. <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Police_Department" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Police Department">Puerto Rico Police Department</a> also has jurisdiction in Carolina, especially for <a href="/wiki/Narcotics" class="mw-redirect" title="Narcotics">narcotics</a> enforcement, with four precincts positioned on all four points of the city.\n</p><p>Carolina created the first municipal fire department in Puerto Rico. The Carolina Fire Department in collaboration with the Carolina Municipal Emergency Management use two engine trucks, one ladder track and one special hazard engine truck. Their headquarters are located in the tourist district of Isla Verde. Also, the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Fire_Department" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Fire Department">Puerto Rico Fire Department</a> have a fire station and regional office in town, located Symbols"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">municipio</i></span> has an official flag and coat of arms.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>40<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flag">Flag</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carolina,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Flag"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The flag consists of three vertical bands, the laterals white and the middle red. The laterals are seeded with black ermine tails in the <a href="/wiki/Heraldic" class="mw-redirect" title="Heraldic">heraldic</a> way as for the <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a>. The middle band of the flag shows a red field with the sword and crown of the coat of arms.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carolina,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The crown over the sword is the main attribute for royalty and for that reason it occupies a privileged position in the coat of arms. The sword is not only a symbol for military service, but also one of justice, recalling the virtues of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Ferdinand" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Ferdinand">San Fernando</a>. The coat of arms has a wide edge of silver, a cultivated field of small tails of ermine shown in the conventional heraldic manner. The red symbolizes the first patriotic developments made for Puerto Rico\'s freedom under Spanish dominion.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div
