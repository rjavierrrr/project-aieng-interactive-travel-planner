.IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;a\xcb\x88ti\xca\x8eo&#93;</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Hatillo_barrio-pueblo" title="Hatillo barrio-pueblo">town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>\'s north coast, bordered by the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> to the north, <a href="/wiki/Lares,_Puerto_Rico" title="Lares, Puerto Rico">Lares</a> and <a href="/wiki/Utuado,_Puerto_Rico" title="Utuado, Puerto Rico">Utuado</a> to the south, <a href="/wiki/Camuy,_Puerto_Rico" title="Camuy, Puerto Rico">Camuy</a> to the west, and <a href="/wiki/Arecibo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Arecibo, Puerto Rico">Arecibo</a> to the east. According to the 2000 US Census Hatillo is spread over nine barrios and <a href="/wiki/Hatillo_barrio-pueblo" title="Hatillo barrio-pueblo">Hatillo Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and the administrative center of the city). It is part of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area">San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hatillo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Agust\xc3\xadn Ruiz Miranda, a <a href="/wiki/Canarian" class="mw-redirect" title="Canarian">Canarian</a> immigrant, founded Hatillo on approximately ten <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">cuerdas</i></span> (a <a href="/wiki/Cuerda" title="Cuerda">cuerda</a> is 0.97 acre, also called a Spanish acre) in 1823. Miranda granted this land on the condition that public buildings be erected and wide streets be built, and that the remaining land be sold or used for homes.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_Founding_Symbols_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_Founding_Symbols-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In its first year, Hatillo had 910 people; increasing to 2,663 inhabitants the following year distributed among the central town and the barrios of <a href="/wiki/Carrizales,_Hatillo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Carrizales, Hatillo, Puerto Rico">Carrizales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cap%C3%A1ez,_Hatillo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cap\xc3\xa1ez, Hatillo, Puerto Rico">Cap\xc3\xa1ez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Naranjito,_Hatillo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Naranjito, Hatillo, Puerto Rico">Naranjito</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corcovado,_Hatillo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Corcovado, Hatillo, Puerto Rico">Corcovado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buena_Vista,_Hatillo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Buena Vista, Hatillo, Puerto Rico">Buena Vista</a> (formerly Yeguada Occidental), <a href="/wiki/Campo_Alegre,_Hatillo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Campo Alegre, Hatillo, Puerto Rico">Campo Alegre</a> (formerly Yeguada Oriental), Pajuil, <a href="/wiki/Bayaney,_Hatillo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Bayaney, Hatillo, Puerto Rico">Bayaney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aibonito,_Hatillo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Aibonito, Hatillo, Puerto Rico">Aibonito</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Hatillo_barrio-pueblo" title="Hatillo barrio-pueblo">Pueblo</a>. Barrio Pajuil had disappeared by the 1940 census being divided up between Buena Vista, Naranjito, Corcovado and Campo Alegre barrios. There were also two sugarcane plantations named "Hacienda Santa Rosa" measuring 150 <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">cuerdas</i></span> and "Hacienda Perseverancia" at 50 <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">cuerdas</i></span>.\n</p><p>Puerto Rico was ceded by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_of_1898" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Paris of 1898">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became a territory of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.  In 1899, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_War" title="United States Department of War">United States Department of War</a> conducted a <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of Puerto Rico finding that the population of Hatillo was 1,148.<sup id="cite_ref-OfficeSanger1900_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OfficeSanger1900-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Hatillo, like several other municipalities on the island, experienced boundary changes from the 1902 municipality consolidation law (Consolidaci\xc3\xb3n de Ciertos T\xc3\xa9rminos Municipales de Puerto Rico) in which Hatillo was annexed temporarily into neighboring <a href="/wiki/Camuy,_Puerto_Rico" title="Camuy, Puerto Rico">Camuy</a>. Three years later the territorial legislature approved the reformation of Hatillo as a separate municipality (independent town) from Camuy in 1905.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1910, Barrio Pueblo (rural) changed its name to Hatillo barrio (rural). In 1930, Yeguadilla Occidental and Yeguadilla Oriental barrios\' names were changed to Buena Vista and Campo Alegre, respectively. As mentioned before, Pajuil barrio disappeared by the 1940 census being divided up between barrios Buena Vista, Naranjito, Corcovado and Campo Alegre. In 1947 the Planning Commission of Puerto Rico issued a new map of Hatillo municipality and its barrios. As a result of this new map, the central town was expanded to include part of Hatillo barrio (rural) and the name of "Corcovados" was changed to "Corcovado".\n</p><p>On September 20, 2017 <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> struck Puerto Rico.  In Hatillo,  residences and municipal buildings sustained damage. The 75 dairy farms which produce much of the milk consumed in Puerto Rico were greatly affected. Ranchers said milk production would decrease by up to 45% in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The hurricane triggered numerous landslides in Hatillo.<sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 The <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Fiestas Patronales de Nuestra Se\xc3\xb1ora del Carmen</i></span> is a religious and cultural celebration that generally features parades, games, artisans, amusement rides, regional food, and live entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_Mayor,_Festivals,_Places_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_Mayor,_Festivals,_Places-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The festival has featured live performances by well-known artists such as <a href="/wiki/Sonora_Ponce%C3%B1a" class="mw-redirect" title="Sonora Ponce\xc3\xb1a">Sonora Ponce\xc3\xb1a</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andy_Monta%C3%B1ez" title="Andy Monta\xc3\xb1ez">Andy Monta\xc3\xb1ez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Cap%C3%B3" title="Pedro Cap\xc3\xb3">Pedro Cap\xc3\xb3</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sie7e" title="Sie7e">Sie7e</a> and <a href="/wiki/El_Gran_Combo_de_Puerto_Rico" title="El Gran Combo de Puerto Rico">El Gran Combo de Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Fiestas_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fiestas-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Other festivals and events celebrated in Hatillo include:\n</p>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1184024115"><div class="div-col">\n<ul><li>Fiestas de la Cruz - May</li>\n<li><span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Festival de la Herencia Canaria</i></span> - May</li>\n<li>Hatillo Music And Culinary Fest - May</li>\n<li><span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Festival de la Ca\xc3\xb1a de Azucar</i></span>- May</li>\n<li><span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Festival de la Leche Fresca</i></span> - June</li>\n<li><span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Hatillo es Bomba y Plena</i></span> - October</li>\n<li>Cooperative Movement Traditional festival- October</li>\n<li><span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Festival de las M\xc3\xa1scaras</i></span> (Mask Festival)- December</li>\n<li>Christmas festival - December</li></ul>\n</div>\n<p>The Mask Festival began in 1823, and was imported by the immigrants from the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>, where the traditional festival originated. The early tradition of the festival required that the male population dress as women and they would visit each residence where the owners would offer them food and drinks. Currently the festival is celebrated every year on December 28. The Masks are fashioned and based on the biblical story of King Herod (<a href="/wiki/Herod_the_Great" title="Herod the Great">Herod the Great</a>). The costumes used are very elaborate and the Masks represent the soldiers which were sent by the King Herod to kill all boys age three and younger, after hearing about a new king being born as told by the <a href="/wiki/Biblical_Magi" title="Biblical Magi">Three Wise Men</a>. The festival, however is presented in humor and said soldiers only joke around and ride on chariots.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_Mayor,_Festivals,_Places_23-2" class="reference"><a class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flag">Flag</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hatillo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Flag"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The flag consists of three broad stripes - Blue, Yellow and Green. Blue represents the sea, yellow represents the material and artistic wealth of the town, and green represents the vegetation of its fields in all its territorial extension.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_Founding_Symbols_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency_Founding_Symbols-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hatillo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>On top of the shield is a gold crown with three towers over a silver field a Custard apple tree (<i><a href="/wiki/Annona_reticulata" title="Annona reticulata">Annona reticulata</a></i>) and a field with two cows in gold which is over eight blue and silver-plated waves. At the center is a shield of "<i>La Orden del Carmen</i>".
