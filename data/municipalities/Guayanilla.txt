(<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1177148991">.mw-parser-output .IPA-label-small{font-size:85%}.mw-parser-output .references .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;\xc9\xa1wa\xca\x9da\xcb\x88ni\xca\x8ea&#93;</a></span>, <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1177148991"><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">locally</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> located on the southern coast of the island, bordering the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>, south of <a href="/wiki/Adjuntas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Adjuntas, Puerto Rico">Adjuntas</a>, east of <a href="/wiki/Yauco,_Puerto_Rico" title="Yauco, Puerto Rico">Yauco</a>; and west of <a href="/wiki/Pe%C3%B1uelas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Pe\xc3\xb1uelas, Puerto Rico">Pe\xc3\xb1uelas</a> and about 12 miles (19&#160;km) west of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>. Guayanilla is spread over 16 barrios and <a href="/wiki/Guayanilla_barrio-pueblo" title="Guayanilla barrio-pueblo">Guayanilla Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and the administrative center of the city). It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Yauco_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Yauco Metropolitan Statistical Area">Yauco Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guayanilla,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Guayanilla,_Puerto_Rico._One_of_the_houses_in_the_company_village_for_sugar_cane_workers.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Guayanilla%2C_Puerto_Rico._One_of_the_houses_in_the_company_village_for_sugar_cane_workers.jpg/220px-Guayanilla%2C_Puerto_Rico._One_of_the_houses_in_the_company_village_for_sugar_cane_workers.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="163" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Guayanilla%2C_Puerto_Rico._One_of_the_houses_in_the_company_village_for_sugar_cane_workers.jpg/330px-Guayanilla%2C_Puerto_Rico._One_of_the_houses_in_the_company_village_for_sugar_cane_workers.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Guayanilla%2C_Puerto_Rico._One_of_the_houses_in_the_company_village_for_sugar_cane_workers.jpg/440px-Guayanilla%2C_Puerto_Rico._One_of_the_houses_in_the_company_village_for_sugar_cane_workers.jpg 2x" data-file-width="943" data-file-height="698" /></a><figcaption>"Guayanilla, Puerto Rico. One of the houses in the company village for sugar cane workers" (1941 photograph by Jack Delano)</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Guayanilla was founded by Puerto Rican <a href="/wiki/Criollo_(people)" class="mw-redirect" title="Criollo (people)">criollos</a>. The original name was <b>Guadianilla</b> in memory of a river and town of the same name in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. However, it was changed to <i>Guayanilla</i> to resemble a native word in the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno_language" title="Ta\xc3\xadno language">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> language. The name Guayanilla is derived from a combination of <a href="/wiki/Guaynia" title="Guaynia">Guaynia</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Santa_Maria_de_Guadianilla&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Santa Maria de Guadianilla (page does not exist)">Santa Maria de Guadianilla</a>.\n</p><p>The first Europeans settled in this area in 1511. In 1756, <a href="/wiki/Yauco,_Puerto_Rico" title="Yauco, Puerto Rico">Yauco</a> was founded as a <a href="/wiki/Town" title="Town">town</a>. Then Guayanilla was a borough of Yauco. Due to the very fertile lands and access to the local port where most of the local commerce occurred, Guayanilla became an important agricultural center where <a href="/wiki/Sugarcane" title="Sugarcane">sugarcane</a> was cultivated. Guayanilla grew quickly and was established as a separate municipality on February 27, 1833 by <a href="/wiki/Governor" title="Governor">Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Miguel_de_la_Torre" title="Miguel de la Torre">Miguel de la Torre</a>.\n</p><p>Puerto Rico was ceded by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_of_1898" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Paris of 1898">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became a territory of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. In 1899, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_War" title="United States Department of War">United States Department of War</a> conducted a <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of Puerto Rico finding that the population of Guayanilla was 9,540.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On September 20, 2017 <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> struck Puerto Rico.  In Guayanilla, the hurricane triggered numerous landslides and caused major destruction with an estimated 600 homes losing their roof and 300 homes completely destroyed. Roads, crops and structures were destroyed by the winds and flooding.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="2019_-_2020_Earthquakes"><a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Puerto_Rico_earthquakes" title="2019\xe2\x80\x9320 Puerto Rico earthquakes">2019 - 2020 Earthquakes</a></h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guayanilla,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2019 - 2020 Earthquakes"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Playa_Ventana.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Playa_Ventana.jpg/220px-Playa_Ventana.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Playa_Ventana.jpg/330px-Playa_Ventana.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Playa_Ventana.jpg/440px-Playa_Ventana.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a><figcaption>This Guayanilla landmark called Ventana was destroyed by the earthquakes.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>On January 6, 2020 a 5.8 magnitude earthquake was felt in Guayanilla and several structures and cars were destroyed. A family of eight escaped a home that was destroyed by the earthquake.<sup id="cite_ref-Jan_6_2020_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jan_6_2020-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On January 7, 2020 a 6.4 magnitude earthquake destroyed the Catholic church in <a href="/wiki/Guayanilla_barrio-pueblo" title="Guayanilla barrio-pueblo">Guayanilla Pueblo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Jan_7_2020_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jan_7_2020-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guayanilla,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Guayanilla is located on the southern coast. The coastline forms the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Guayanilla_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Guayanilla Bay (page does not exist)">Guayanilla Bay</a>, one of the best natural harbors in Puerto Rico, to the south, also. The nearest city is <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>, which is 12 miles (19&#160;km) to the east. The northern regions are bordered by mountains that reach 3,300 feet (1,000&#160;m) at the <a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Cordillera Central (Puerto Rico)">Cordillera Central</a>. In the central regions, the terrain descends where it does not exceed 1,410 feet (430 m). Finally in the coastal plain, the elevations do not exceed 951 feet (290&#160;m). The <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Yauco" class="mw-redirect" title="R\xc3\xado Yauco">Yauco</a>, <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Guayanilla" class="mw-redirect" title="R\xc3\xado Guayanilla">Guayanilla</a>, and <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Macan%C3%A1" class="mw-redirect" title="R\xc3\xado Macan\xc3\xa1">Macan\xc3\xa1</a> rivers all run through the municipality. The <a href="/wiki/Yauco_River" title="Yauco River">Yauco River</a> briefly runs through the Boca borough, where its exit into the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> and accompanying marshlands are located.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Barrios">Barrios</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guayanilla,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Barrios"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Guayanilla,_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Guayanilla%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/220px-Guayanilla%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="238" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Guayanilla%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/330px-Guayanilla%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Guayanilla%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/440px-Guayanilla%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png 2x" data-file-width="1100" data-file-height="1188" /></a><figcaption>Subdivisions of Guayanilla</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Like all municipalities of Puerto Rico, Guayanilla is subdivided into <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico">barrios</a>. Nazario (and which popular culture links to <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> chief <a href="/wiki/Ag%C3%BCeyban%C3%A1_II" title="Ag\xc3\xbceyban\xc3\xa1 II">Ag\xc3\xbceyban\xc3\xa1 II</a>), has become a cultural symbol for the municipality.
