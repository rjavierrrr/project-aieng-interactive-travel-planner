.IPA-label-small{font-size:85%}.mw-parser-output .references .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;ke\xce\xb2\xc9\xbea\xcb\x88\xc3\xb0i\xca\x8eas&#93;</a></span>, <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1177148991"><span class="IPA-label href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> of the island of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> located in the northern shore bordering the Atlantic Ocean, north of <a href="/wiki/San_Sebasti%C3%A1n,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Sebasti\xc3\xa1n, Puerto Rico">San Sebasti\xc3\xa1n</a>; east of <a href="/wiki/Isabela,_Puerto_Rico" title="Isabela, Puerto Rico">Isabela</a>; and west of <a href="/wiki/Camuy,_Puerto_Rico" title="Camuy, Puerto Rico">Camuy</a>. Quebradillas is spread over seven barrios and <a href="/wiki/Quebradillas_barrio-pueblo" title="Quebradillas barrio-pueblo">Quebradillas Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and the administrative center of the city). It is part of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area">San Juan-Caguas-Guaynabo Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>.\n</p><p>Quebradillas is called "<i>La Guarida del Pirata</i>" (The Pirate\'s Hideout). A well-known beach in the area, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Puerto_Hermina&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Puerto Hermina (page does not exist)">Puerto Hermina</a>, is home to an old structure known to have been a hiding place for pirates and their contraband.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quebradillas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The town was founded in 1823 by Felipe Ruiz.  There were many factors that cause the inhabitants of this territory of Puerto Rico to be independent from the towns that formed the Hato de la Tuna resulting in the formation of a new municipality. The town lacked chapels or churches, religious services nor priests, a place to bury the dead, a post office, and quality roads to transport. They relied on the neighboring municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Camuy,_Puerto_Rico" title="Camuy, Puerto Rico">Camuy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hatillo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Hatillo, Puerto Rico">Hatillo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Isabela,_Puerto_Rico" title="Isabela, Puerto Rico">Isabela</a>. This would however be a problem during the rainy seasons when the <a href="/wiki/Camuy_River" title="Camuy River">R\xc3\xado Camuy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guajataca_River" title="Guajataca River">R\xc3\xado Guajataca</a> water level rose resulting in closed off passage to the other municipalities and would sometimes take up to a week for the water level to return to normal. This led the residents of the area to organize a corporation that would look after the political, economic and administrative interests of <i>"Las Quebradillas"</i> and its jurisdiction.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This town derives its name from the large number of streams flowing through it. Quebradillas means "small streams".\n</p><p>Puerto Rico was ceded by Spain in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_of_1898" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Paris of 1898">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became a territory of the United States.  In 1899, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_War" title="United States Department of War">United States Department of War</a> conducted a <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of Puerto Rico finding that the population of Quebradillas was 7,432.\n</p><p>On September 20, 2017 <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> struck the island of Puerto Rico.  In Quebradillas, 800 concrete barriers had to be built to stabilize the <a href="/wiki/Guajataca_Lake" title="Guajataca Lake">Guajataca reservoir</a> which suffered a structural failure and was causing a "life-threatening situation".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-guardian1722_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian1722-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> "It\xe2\x80\x99s a structural failure. I don\xe2\x80\x99t have any more details," Governor <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Rossell%C3%B3" title="Ricardo Rossell\xc3\xb3">Ricardo Rossell\xc3\xb3</a> stated. "We\xe2\x80\x99re trying to evacuate as many people as possible."<sup id="cite_ref-guardian1722_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian1722-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Rossell\xc3\xb3 ordered the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_National_Guard" title="Puerto Rico National Guard">Puerto Rico National Guard</a> and the Police to help assist in the evacuation effort downstream.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The dam lies across the Guajataca River to form a reservoir that can hold roughly 11 billion gallons of water.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In Quebradillas, infrastructure and about 2200 homes were damaged or completely destroyed.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quebradillas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Guajatacatunel.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Guajatacatunel.jpg/220px-Guajatacatunel.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="154" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Guajatacatunel.jpg/330px-Guajatacatunel.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Guajatacatunel.jpg/440px-Guajatacatunel.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="449" /></a><figcaption>The coastline and <a href="/wiki/Guajataca_Tunnel" title="Guajataca Tunnel">Guajataca Tunnel</a> at Guajataca Beach</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Quebradillas, located in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Karst_(Puerto_Rico)" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern Karst (Puerto Rico)">Northern Karst</a>, borders on the Atlantic Ocean, is north of <a href="/wiki/San_Sebasti%C3%A1n,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Sebasti\xc3\xa1n, Puerto Rico">San Sebasti\xc3\xa1n</a>; east of <a href="/wiki/Isabela,_Puerto_Rico" title="Isabela, Puerto Rico">Isabela</a>, and west of <a href="/wiki/Camuy,_Puerto_Rico" title="Camuy, Puerto Rico">Camuy</a>. Quebradillas is home to one of the 20 designated forest preserves in Puerto Rico, the <a href="/wiki/Guajataca_State_Forest" title="Guajataca State Forest">Guajataca State Forest</a>. The forest serves as a great example of an unusual topography known as <a href="/wiki/Karst" title="Karst"><i>karst country</i></a>. Karst is characterized by dissolved limestone formations such as <a href="/wiki/Sinkholes" class="mw-redirect" title="Sinkholes">sinkholes</a> and haystack-shaped hills known as <a href="/wiki/Mogote" title="Mogote">"mogotes"</a>. It is also home to the beautiful man-made reservoir, <a href="/wiki/Guajataca_Lake" title="Guajataca Lake">Guajataca Lake</a> (2.5&#160;mi or 4.0&#160;km long), where you can fish for <a href="/wiki/Largemouth_bass" title="Largemouth bass">largemouth bass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peacock_bass" title="Peacock bass">peacock bass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tilapia" title="Tilapia">tilapia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bluegill" title="Bluegill">bluegill</a> (in Spanish known as "chopa"). You can also go <a href="/wiki/Hiking" title="Hiking">hiking</a> or <a href="/wiki/Camping" title="Camping">camping</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Scouting_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Scouting in Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico Council</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Boy_Scouts_of_America" title="Boy Scouts of America">Boy Scouts of America</a> maintains a campground on the lake known as <a href="/wiki/Camp_Guajataka" class="mw-redirect" title="Camp Guajataka">Camp Guajataka</a>. The name Guajataca comes from the name <i>Wahataka</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Ta\xc3\xadno people">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> chief who lived in this area. This Indian chief also gives his name to <a href="/w/index.php?title=Guajataca_Beach&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Guajataca Beach (page does not exist)">Guajataca Beach</a> to the north where <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Guajataca" class="mw-redirect" title="R\xc3\xado Guajataca">R\xc3\xado Guajataca</a> flowing from <a href="/wiki/Guajataca_Lake" title="Guajataca Lake">Guajataca Lake</a> meets the Atlantic Ocean. Guajataca Beach is popular with <a href="/wiki/Surfing" title="Surfing">surfers</a> and is known for its white sands and wild waves. This beach is ideal for sunning and collecting seashells.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Barrios">Barrios</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quebradillas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Barrios"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Quebradillas,_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Quebradillas%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/220px-Quebradillas%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="164" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Quebradillas%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/330px-Quebradillas%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Quebradillas%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png/440px-Quebradillas%2C_Puerto_Rico_locator_map.png 2x" data-file-width="847" data-file-height="630" /></a><figcaption>Subdivisions of Quebradillas.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Like all municipalities of Puerto Rico, Quebradillas is subdivided into <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico">barrios</a>. class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Business">Business</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quebradillas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Business"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>A small shopping center called Quebradillas Plaza is located in this municipality.\n</p><p>An event which negatively affected the region in the decade of the 1990s was the disappearance of tax exemptions to the private corporations Section 936 of the code of the US <a href="/wiki/Internal_Revenue_Service" title="Internal Revenue Service">Internal Revenue Service</a>, which at the time was the resulted in the closure of one of the greater manufacturers of textiles in the northwest area of the island. This decline of the industry of the needle occurred similarly in the bordering cities like <a href="/wiki/Isabela,_Puerto_Rico" title="Isabela, Puerto Rico">Isabela</a> and <a href="/wiki/Camuy" class="mw-redirect" title="Camuy">Camuy</a>, generating a regional economic decline as the locals greatly depended on these jobs. In nearby towns like <a href="/wiki/Hatillo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Hatillo, Puerto Rico">Hatillo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mayag%C3%BCez" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayag\xc3\xbcez">Mayag\xc3\xbcez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aguadilla" class="mw-redirect" title="Aguadilla">Aguadilla</a>, the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Megastores" class="mw-redirect" title="Megastores">mega stores</a> and new shopping centers attracted the jobs that used to be in Quebradillas.\n</p><p>The island lacks mass public transportation, people must resort to private cars. The only road to access the important cities, the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_2" title="Puerto Rico Highway 2">Puerto Rico Highway 2</a> suffers from congestion. The town possesses one tunnel that at the beginning of the 20th century was used by steam driven trains that traveled throughout the Island. This absence aggravates the problem of mass transit and the dependence on privately owned vehicles as these are the only method of transportation.<sup id="cite_ref-OS_scenic_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OS_scenic-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The economy, entering into the 21st century, is based on retail sales. Many small businesses are located along the two main thoroughfares that cross Quebradillas. These consist mainly of light hardware, bakeries, pharmacies, seafood restaurants, American fast food chains and automobile car repair shops.\n</p><p>The town has bank branches for <a href="/wiki/Popular,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Popular, Inc.">Banco Popular</a> and local savings and credit <a href="/wiki/Cooperative_banking" title="Cooperative banking">Unions</a>. id="Flag">Flag</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quebradillas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Flag"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>It has two red quarters. The red stands for struggle, effort and sacrifice. The other two-quarters have each five green and white wavy stripes, similar to those that appear in the shield.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quebradillas,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The three waved stripes represent the <i>quebradillas</i> (Spanish for creeks or streams), over the green background of the vegetation. There is also a public electronic library near <i>Los Chorritos Pirata</i> water park.
