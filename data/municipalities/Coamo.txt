class="mw-redirect" title="Plaza">plaza</a> area</figcaption></figure>\n<p><b>Coamo</b> (<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1177148991">.mw-parser-output .IPA-label-small{font-size:85%}.mw-parser-output .references .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;ko\xcb\x88amo&#93;</a></span>, <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1177148991"><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">locally</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;\xcb\x88kwamo&#93;</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Coamo_barrio-pueblo" title="Coamo barrio-pueblo">town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> founded in 1579 in the south-central region of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, located north of <a href="/wiki/Santa_Isabel,_Puerto_Rico" title="Santa Isabel, Puerto Rico">Santa Isabel</a>; south of <a href="/wiki/Orocovis,_Puerto_Rico" title="Orocovis, Puerto Rico">Orocovis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barranquitas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Barranquitas, Puerto Rico">Barranquitas</a>; east of <a href="/wiki/Villalba,_Puerto_Rico" title="Villalba, Puerto Rico">Villalba</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juana_D%C3%ADaz,_Puerto_Rico" title="Juana D\xc3\xadaz, Puerto Rico">Juana D\xc3\xadaz</a>; and west of <a href="/wiki/Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico" title="Aibonito, Puerto Rico">Aibonito</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salinas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Salinas, Puerto Rico">Salinas</a>. The municipality of Coamo is spread over 10 barrios and <a href="/wiki/Coamo_barrio-pueblo" title="Coamo barrio-pueblo">Coamo Pueblo</a> \xe2\x80\x93 the town or downtown area and administrative center of the city. The Coamo municipality is also a <a href="/wiki/Micropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Micropolitan Statistical Area">Micropolitan Statistical Area</a> and as such is part of the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_metropolitan_area#Combined_Statistical_Area" title="Ponce metropolitan area">Ponce-Yauco-Coamo Combined Statistical Area</a>.\n</p><p>The town of Coamo and parts of its barrios are nestled in a <a href="/wiki/Valley" title="Valley">valley</a> about 20 miles (32&#160;km) east of the town of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a> (about 40 minutes by car). It was named <b>San Blas Illescas de Coamo</b> by Spanish settlers in 1579. <a href="/wiki/Saint_Blaise" title="Saint Blaise">Saint Blaise</a> (<i>San Blas</i>) was designated by the Catholic Church as the patron saint of the town, and so it remains presently. <i><a href="/wiki/Illescas,_Toledo" title="Illescas, Toledo">Illescas</a></i> is the Spanish town where some of the town founders originated (nowadays in <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Toledo" title="Province of Toledo">Toledo province</a>, Castile-La Mancha, Spain).\n</p><p>There are several theories regarding the origin of the word <i>Coamo</i>. One theory is that it comes from an indigenous word that means "valley". Another theory is that Coamo derives its name from <i>Coamex</i> (or <i>Coamey</i>), who was a celebrated local <a href="/wiki/Cacique" title="Cacique">cacique</a>. Archeological digs in the municipality of Coamo have produced extensive evidence of <a href="/wiki/Pre-Columbian" class="mw-redirect" title="Pre-Columbian">pre-Columbian</a> inhabitants of the area. \n</p><p>Coamo is famous for its natural hot springs, <a href="/wiki/Los_Ba%C3%B1os_de_Coamo" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Ba\xc3\xb1os de Coamo">Los Ba\xc3\xb1os de Coamo</a><i>,</i> and for its annual <a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Blas_Half_Marathon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="San Blas Half Marathon (page does not exist)">San Blas Half Marathon</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Coamo" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Coamo">Battle of Coamo</a> was a decisive battle of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> (1898).\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coamo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>When Spanish settlers arrived, Coamo was inhabited by the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Taino</a>. Modern archeology has found evidence of at least four pre-columbian villages in the territory that is today the Coamo municipality:<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>"Buenos Aires" village was located in the current downtown southern section, were today is Buenos Aires Street</li>\n<li>"Ba\xc3\xb1os de Coamo" village was located by the hot springs, across the river from the modern baths.</li>\n<li>"Cuy\xc3\xb3n" village was located in what is now <a href="/wiki/Cuy%C3%B3n,_Coamo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cuy\xc3\xb3n, Coamo, Puerto Rico">Cuy\xc3\xb3n barrio</a></li>\n<li>"Las Flores" village was located west of the Las Flores neighborhood in <a href="/wiki/San_Ildefonso,_Coamo,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Ildefonso, Coamo, Puerto Rico">San Ildefonso barrio</a>. Artifacts found here suggest a high status <a href="/wiki/Cacique" title="Cacique">cacique</a> lived here.</li></ul>\n<p>It is believed the Coamo villages had  strong ties with the coastal village "El Cayito" (in what is now <a href="/wiki/Santa_Isabel,_Puerto_Rico" title="Santa Isabel, Puerto Rico">Santa Isabel municipality</a>) and the "To\xc3\xadta" village (in what is now <a href="/wiki/Cayey,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cayey, Puerto Rico">Cayey municipality</a>) due to the large quantities of marine shellfish remains found in the Coamo and <a href="/wiki/Cayey,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cayey, Puerto Rico">Cayey</a> villages.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93Ta%C3%ADno_War_of_San_Juan%E2%80%93Borik%C3%A9n" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93Ta\xc3\xadno War of San Juan\xe2\x80\x93Borik\xc3\xa9n">Taino rebellion of 1511</a> was defeated by the Spanish fuerzas espa\xc3\xb1olas, the hard labor in <a href="/wiki/Encomienda" title="Encomienda">encomiendas</a>, epidemics of <a href="/wiki/Smallpox" title="Smallpox">smallpox</a> and other European diseases, and conversion to Catholicism and intermarriage with Spanish colonist contributed to the assimilation of the Taino into the Spanish society and culture.\n</p><p>Founded on July 15, 1579, the town of <a href="/wiki/Coamo_barrio-pueblo" title="Coamo barrio-pueblo">Coamo</a> is the third-oldest settlement of the island\'s post-Columbian period (after <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a> in the north and <a href="/wiki/San_Germ%C3%A1n,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Germ\xc3\xa1n, Puerto Rico">San Germ\xc3\xa1n</a> in the west). At that time, Coamo was the administrative center for a larger area that would eventually be subdivided into several municipalities:  <a href="/wiki/Guayama,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guayama, Puerto Rico">Guayama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cayey,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cayey, Puerto Rico">Cayey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juana_D%C3%ADaz,_Puerto_Rico" title="Juana D\xc3\xadaz, Puerto Rico">Juana D\xc3\xadaz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orocovis,_Puerto_Rico" title="Orocovis, Puerto Rico">Orocovis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barranquitas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Barranquitas, Puerto Rico">Barranquitas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cidra,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cidra, Puerto Rico">Cidra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patillas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Patillas, Puerto Rico">Patillas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico" title="Aibonito, Puerto Rico">Aibonito</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santa_Isabel,_Puerto_Rico" title="Santa Isabel, Puerto Rico">Santa Isabel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salinas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Salinas, Puerto Rico">Salinas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arroyo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Arroyo, Puerto Rico">Arroyo</a>.\n</p><p>By 1582, there were twenty families living in Coamo, in the same area where the Tainos had had their village of <a href="/wiki/Guayama,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guayama, Puerto Rico">Guayama</a> <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>. Coamo officially became a town in 1616, and was given the title of "Villa" by Spanish Royal Decree in 1778.\n</p><p>Coamo was the administrative center that encompassed most of the southern half of the island during the early colonial period. As the agricultural and sugar industries grew and became the mainstays of the colony\'s economy, the province would eventually subdivide into several distinct municipalities, and the administrative center of the region would later shift west to the coastal town of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>.\n</p><p>Coamo is the home of a series of natural <a href="/wiki/Hot_spring" title="Hot spring">hot springs</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Ba%C3%B1os_de_Coamo" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Ba\xc3\xb1os de Coamo">Los Ba\xc3\xb1os de Coamo</a></i>, which have attracted visitors since before the Spaniards landed.<sup id="cite_ref-JetBlue_2010_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JetBlue_2010-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> These springs were once rumored to have been <a href="/wiki/Juan_Ponce_de_Le%C3%B3n" title="Juan Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n">Juan Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n</a>\'s legendary <a href="/wiki/Fountain_of_Youth" title="Fountain of Youth">fountain of youth</a>. In the early nineteenth century, a system of pools of varying depths, sizes and temperatures was constructed at the site of these springs to serve as a spa for the colonials. During the American invasion in the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> (1898), this site was the scene of one of the decisive battles of that conflict (the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Coamo" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Coamo">Battle of Coamo</a>). The American troops took possession of the island, and the spa was subsequently abandoned. Though the site lay in ruins for most of the twentieth century, it continued to be a landmark to the <i>Coame\xc3\xb1os</i>, who would often go to bathe in its healing thermal waters. The pools remain, but the old buildings which once hosted the island\'s affluent and colonial soldiers are gone, except for the remains of one central wall structure. It has been preserved and incorporated into a fountain courtyard on the grounds of a popular tourist hotel and rest area and has replaced the ancient Spanish ruins.\n</p><p>Puerto Rico was ceded by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_of_1898" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Paris of 1898">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became a territory of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. In 1899, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_War" title="United States Department of War">United States Department of War</a> conducted a <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of Puerto Rico finding that the population of Coamo was 15,144.<sup id="cite_ref-OfficeSanger1900_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OfficeSanger1900-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> on September 20, 2017 triggered numerous landslides in Coamo with the significant amount of rainfall.<sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> As of October 9, no one in Coamo had electrical service, only 15% of Coamo had access to clean drinking water, and several people on dialysis had died. Around 2,000 homes were partially or completely destroyed. class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flag">Flag</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coamo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Flag"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The flag of Coamo derives its colors from the coat of arms. Its colors are red, yellow, and black.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coamo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The top left and the lower right have a red background with a gold Episcopal hat each. These parts of the coat of arms represent the old seat of <i>San Blas de Illescas</i>. The <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horse</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Bull" title="Bull">bull</a> represent the cattle wealth of the population. The gold color that serves as background in contrast with the black color, recalls the yellowish reddish tone of the fields of Coamo during the <a href="/wiki/Drought" title="Drought">droughts</a>. The heavy border of the coat of arms contains the following figures: two flames; three bell towers with gold bells outlined in red; two red crosses with arms ending in three petals; and a circle with a surface divided by horizontal blue and silver-plated stripes.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_38-1" class="reference"><a
