0;">Designated&#160;NHLDCP</th><td class="infobox-data" style="border: 0;">October 10, 1972</td></tr></tbody></table><p><b>San Jos\xc3\xa9 Church</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Iglesia de San Jos\xc3\xa9</i>), located in <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">Old San Juan</a> within the historic colonial zone of the capital of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, is one of the first significant works of architecture on the island.<sup id="cite_ref-Pariser_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pariser-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-American_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-American-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The church is one of the earliest surviving examples of 16th-century Spanish <a href="/wiki/Gothic_architecture" title="Gothic architecture">Gothic architecture</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Western_hemisphere" class="mw-redirect" title="Western hemisphere">Western hemisphere</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Yale_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Yale-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MarvelMoreno1994_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MarvelMoreno1994-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2013 it was added to the <a href="/wiki/National_Trust_for_Historic_Preservation" title="National Trust for Historic Preservation">National Trust for Historic Preservation</a>\'s list of 11 Most Endangered Historic Places of 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Jos%C3%A9_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The church was constructed from 1528 to 1735 by the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Order" title="Dominican Order">Dominican Order</a> as part of the Saint Aquinas Monastery. It was renamed by the Jesuits who took over the monastery on September 2, 1858.<sup id="cite_ref-Revista_del_ICP_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Revista_del_ICP-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Juan_Ponce_de_Le%C3%B3n" title="Juan Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n">Juan Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n</a>, the first governor of Puerto Rico, was buried in the crypt of the church from 1559 to 1836, when his remains were exhumed and later transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Catedral_Metropolitana_Bas%C3%ADlica_de_San_Juan_Bautista_(San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico)" class="mw-redirect" title="Catedral Metropolitana Bas\xc3\xadlica de San Juan Bautista (San Juan, Puerto Rico)">Cathedral of San Juan Bautista</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Yale_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Yale-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> However, his coat of arms is still located near the main altar. His grandson, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Ponce_de_Leon_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Juan Ponce de Leon II">Juan Ponce de Leon II</a> is buried in the crypt beneath the Sanctuary\'s floor. Puerto Rican painter <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Campeche" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Campeche">Jos\xc3\xa9 Campeche</a> is also buried in the church.\n</p><p>In 1972, the 15th-century painting <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_of_Bethlehem" class="mw-redirect" title="Our Lady of Bethlehem">Our Lady of Bethlehem</a> disappeared from the church. In 2002, a restoration project on the structure began and several painted murals were discovered including a mid-19th century depiction of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lepanto" title="Battle of Lepanto">Battle of Lepanto</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Silva_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Silva-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2004, the church was listed on the <a href="/wiki/2004_World_Monuments_Watch" title="2004 World Monuments Watch">2004 World Monuments Watch</a> by the <a href="/wiki/World_Monuments_Fund" title="World Monuments Fund">World Monuments Fund</a>, which helped the conservation effort.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Restoration of the church was completed in March 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-Reopen_2021_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reopen_2021-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Gallery">Gallery</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
