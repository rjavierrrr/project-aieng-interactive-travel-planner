style="font-size:85%" title="literal translation">lit.</abbr><span style="white-space: nowrap;">&#8201;</span><span class="gloss-quot">\'</span><span class="gloss-text">Chest of the Dead</span><span class="gloss-quot">\'</span>;<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> also in English: <i>Deadman\'s Chest</i> or <i>Coffin Island</i>) is an uninhabited <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a> off the southern coast of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>. The island and its surrounding waters are protected by the <a href="/wiki/Caja_de_Muertos_Nature_Reserve" title="Caja de Muertos Nature Reserve">Caja de Muertos Nature Reserve</a>, because of its native <a href="/wiki/Turtle" title="Turtle">turtle</a> traffic and ecological value of its dry forests and reefs. <a href="/wiki/Hiker" class="mw-redirect" title="Hiker">Hikers</a> and beachgoers are often seen in the island, which can be reached by <a href="/wiki/Ferry" title="Ferry">ferry</a> from the <a href="/wiki/La_Guancha_Boardwalk" class="mw-redirect" title="La Guancha Boardwalk">La Guancha Boardwalk</a> sector of <a href="/wiki/Playa_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Playa (Ponce)">Ponce Playa</a>. Together with <a href="/wiki/Isla_Cardona" title="Isla Cardona">Cardona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ratones" class="mw-redirect" title="Ratones">Ratones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morrillito" title="Morrillito">Morrillito</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isla_del_Frio" class="mw-redirect" title="Isla del Frio">Isla del Frio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isla_de_Gatas" class="mw-redirect" title="Isla de Gatas">Gatas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Isla_de_Jueyes" title="Isla de Jueyes">Isla de Jueyes</a>, Caja de Muertos is one of seven islands ascribed to the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caja_de_Muertos_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>It is believed the island is only some 4,000 years old, and stone art left on the island attests it has been visited by man over milennnia.<sup id="cite_ref-Muerto_2016_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Muerto_2016-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> natives made frequent trips to the island on fishing expeditions, and when they were defeated by the Spanish during the 1511 battle of <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93Ta%C3%ADno_War_of_San_Juan%E2%80%93Borik%C3%A9n" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93Ta\xc3\xadno War of San Juan\xe2\x80\x93Borik\xc3\xa9n">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93Ta\xc3\xadno War for Borik\xc3\xa9n</a>, the natives attempted a retreat to the Caja de Muertos but shortly afterwards they were either captured or killed.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> During much of the 17th century, the island is known to have been used as a hideout by pirates and mutineers. For example, in 1599-1600 it was used by a group merchants-turned-pirates who, after learning of a Portuguese ship filled with gold, silver, precious stones, other treasures, attacked it and appropriated its contents. As they attempted to travel back to England, though, they faced an attempted mutiny and, surviving it, they took refuge at Caja de Muertos. Venturing to the Puerto Rico mainland for provisions, they were killed, except for one, who later confessed to the existence of the hidden treasure. The Spanish colonial governor then appropriated the remaining treasure for construction projects among other uses.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1842, the first known map was drafted using the name of the island as it is known today.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1880 the Spanish government started construction of a lighthouse on the island, completing it in 1887.<sup id="cite_ref-enciclopediapr.org_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-enciclopediapr.org-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  During the 19th century, the island was also hideout for Puerto Rican patriots who sought independence from Spain. Some of the patriots who hid there include <a href="/wiki/Ram%C3%B3n_Emeterio_Betances" title="Ram\xc3\xb3n Emeterio Betances">Ram\xc3\xb3n Emeterio Betances</a> and <a href="/wiki/Segundo_Ruiz_Belvis" title="Segundo Ruiz Belvis">Segundo Ruiz Belvis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Muertos._2014_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Muertos._2014-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> During these decades it was the secret meeting places of <a href="/wiki/Freemasonry" title="Freemasonry">masons</a> when they were persecuted on the mainland.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Muerto_2016_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Muerto_2016-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1928, <a href="/wiki/Cayetano_Coll_y_Toste" title="Cayetano Coll y Toste">Cayetano Coll y Toste</a> wrote a novel titled "El Tesoro del Pirata Almeida" (The Treasure of Pirate Almeida) inspired by Caja de Muertos island, and in 1883, it inspired <a href="/wiki/Robert_Louis_Stevenson" title="Robert Louis Stevenson">Robert Louis Stevenson</a> to write the novel "Treasure Island".<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1898, at the time of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_campaign" title="Puerto Rico campaign">American invasion</a>, the island had a population of 64 permanent residents, and a decade later only 16 residents remained.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> These residents had no fresh water in the island, so water was brought in from the mainland when, during periods of little rain, it could not be collected from the rainfall.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The island eventually became uninhabited, except for the lightkeepers. In 1945 when the island\'s lighthouse was automated,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> there was no longer a need to have lighthouse keepers and the island became deserted.  After this, the island became a day-trip vacationing spot for some owners of private vessels who might venture to the island for a day trip or for fishing.\n</p><p>On January 2, 1980<sup id="cite_ref-Muertos._2014_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Muertos._2014-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> the island was declared a nature reserve by act of the Puerto Rico Legislature and entrusted to the Puerto Rico Department of Natural Resources to be guarded and administered. It is administered in conjunction of the Ponce Municipal Government.<sup id="cite_ref-enciclopediapr.org_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-enciclopediapr.org-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1985, the Government of Puerto Rico gave its approval for the development of a tourism center at Caja de Muertos.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The lighthouse was restored by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> Department of Natural Resources and for a while it housed a museum.<sup id="cite_ref-primerahora.com_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-primerahora.com-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Ports_Authority" title="Puerto Rico Ports Authority">Puerto Rico Ports Authority</a> had public transportation to the Island until 1993; shortly thereafter a company called \xe2\x80\x9cIsland Venture\xe2\x80\x9d started offering daily trips.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Service was interrupted twice, after <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> and after the <a href="/wiki/2020_Puerto_Rico_earthquakes" class="mw-redirect" title="2020 Puerto Rico earthquakes">2020 Puerto Rico earthquakes</a>, both of which badly damaged <a href="/wiki/La_Guancha_Boardwalk" class="mw-redirect" title="La Guancha Boardwalk">La Guancha</a>, the area on the mainland where passengers boarded the <a href="/wiki/Catamaran" title="Catamaran">catamarans</a> to the island. The <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Natural_and_Environmental_Resources" title="Puerto Rico Department of Natural and Environmental Resources">Puerto Rico Department of Natural and Environmental Resources</a> built a dock, barracks for reserve personnel, as well as a small museum, trail signs, and compost bathrooms for tourists.\n</p><p>In 2009, the government of Puerto Rico announced plans to make development of the island and its restoration of its lighthouse, both for tourism purposes, a priority of the DRNA.<sup id="cite_ref-primerahora.com_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-primerahora.com-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> By the summer of 2012, there were three businesses making tour trips there, including one for charter trips only.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2010, Playa Pel\xc3\xadcano received the <a href="/wiki/Blue_Flag_beach" title="Blue Flag beach">Blue Flag beach</a> certification.<sup id="cite_ref-CDM22_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CDM22-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2017, much of what DRNA had built was destroyed by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> and the tourist ferry was halted, but restarted about a year later.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>However, government inaction following the <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Puerto_Rico_earthquakes" title="2019\xe2\x80\x9320 Puerto Rico earthquakes">2019\xe2\x80\x9320 Puerto Rico earthquakes</a> left the reserve without DRNA rangers for over a year, since the housing structure was severely damaged and not timely repaired or replaced and they limited to maritime patrolling despite having obsolete equipment and expired bulletproof vests.<sup id="cite_ref-CDM22_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CDM22-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2021, the last of three vehicles assigned for this task broke down and the rounds stopped altogether. This had several repercussions on the natural reserve, leading to tourists illegally breaching the perimeter, anchoring in the sand, riding jet skis, camping, hosting parties and vandalizing the remains of the fallen structure.<sup id="cite_ref-CDM22_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CDM22-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The preservation program that monitors and protect the reproduction habitat of the <a href="/wiki/Hawksbill_sea_turtle" title="Hawksbill sea turtle">hawksbill sea turtle</a> was halted, due to the rangers being unable to follow up on their nesting habits, leaving 25 stations unattended.<sup id="cite_ref-CDM22_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CDM22-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>When queried about the situation in April 2022, the DRNA claimed that the housing structure would be eventually reconstruct since disaster mitigation funds had been approved for that purpose.<sup id="cite_ref-CDM22_24-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CDM22-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> However, the process was still in the early stage, since the government had yet to name a contractor for the project. The agency also claimed that patrolling had taken place when the boat \xe2\x80\x9cwas available\xe2\x80\x9d.<sup id="cite_ref-CDM22_24-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CDM22-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caja_de_Muertos_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Caja de Muertos is located 5.2&#160;mi (8.4&#160;km) south of the Puerto Rican mainland and is part of the <a href="/wiki/Playa_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Playa (Ponce)">Playa</a><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Barrio" title="Barrio">barrio</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>, Puerto Rico, <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography_and_climate">Geography and climate</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caja_de_Muertos_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography and climate"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The island measures 2&#160;mi (3&#160;km) long northeast-southwest, and up to 2,822&#160;ft (860&#160;m) wide [1,837&#160;ft (560&#160;m) on the average]. It has an area of 0.59&#160;sq&#160;mi (1.53&#160;km<sup>2</sup>). Close by are <a href="/wiki/Morrillito" title="Morrillito">Morrillito</a> Key [591&#160;ft (180&#160;m) off the southwest point, 0.02&#160;sq&#160;mi (0.05&#160;km<sup>2</sup>)] and Berber\xc3\xada Key [3.9&#160;mi (6.3&#160;km) to the northeast, 0.12&#160;sq&#160;mi (0.31&#160;km<sup>2</sup>)], both part of the <i>Caja de Muertos Natural Reserve</i>. Berber\xc3\xada Key belongs to <i>Rio Canas Abajo</i> <a href="/wiki/Barrio" title="Barrio">barrio</a> of <a href="/wiki/Juana_Diaz,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Juana Diaz, Puerto Rico">Juana D\xc3\xadaz</a> <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a>.\n</p><p>The climate is dry and the island supports <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_dry_forests" title="Puerto Rican dry forests">dry forest</a>. A still-functioning <a href="/wiki/Lighthouse" title="Lighthouse">lighthouse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caja_de_Muertos_Light" title="Caja de Muertos Light">Caja de Muertos Light</a>, established in 1887 and automated in 1945, sits atop the highest hill on the island. This 170 foot high hill is located at the southwest extreme of the island.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Features">Features</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caja_de_Muertos_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Features"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The island has four beaches: Pelicano, Playa Larga, Carrucho, and Coast Guard. The island also possesses fauna and flora not found anywhere else in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The island has no permanent residents, but the Government of Puerto Rico maintains Department of Natural Resources staff and security personnel there. In 1899, it registered a permanent population of 64 residents, but it since declined to zero.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Etymology">Etymology</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caja_de_Muertos_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Etymology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Cactus_Caja_de_Muerto.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Cactus_Caja_de_Muerto.jpg/200px-Cactus_Caja_de_Muerto.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="150" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Cactus_Caja_de_Muerto.jpg/300px-Cactus_Caja_de_Muerto.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5a/Cactus_Caja_de_Muerto.jpg 2x" data-file-width="400" data-file-height="300" /></a><figcaption>Cactus in the island\'s dry forest</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Since 1511, the island has been called by different names including Isla Abeiranas, Abairianay, Ant\xc3\xadas, Jut\xc3\xadas, Yaut\xc3\xadas, Ut\xc3\xadas, Abeianay, Angulo, Isla Bestia, and Isla del Tesoro, in addition to the translations as <i>Coffre A\xe2\x80\x99morr</i>, <i>Deadman\xe2\x80\x99s Chest</i>, and <i>Coffin Island</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Caja de Muertos can be translated into English as "Coffin" or "Dead Man\'s Chest", there is no consensus on how the island got its current name. The official version of the origin of the name, as given by the Puerto Rico Department of Natural Resources, and under whose care the island is currently entrusted, is as follows: "Its name is attributed to the 18th-century French writer <a href="/wiki/Jean-Baptiste_Labat" title="Jean-Baptiste Labat">Jean-Baptiste Labat</a> who called it <i>Coffre \xc3\xa0 mort</i> (Caja de Muertos) or <i>Bomba d\'Infierno</i>, making reference to the fact that when the island is seen from certain places in southern Puerto Rico, it gives the impression of seeing a dead person laying on a plateau."<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Etymological_theories">Etymological theories</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caja_de_Muertos_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Etymological theories"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="Maritime_fiction">Maritime fiction</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caja_de_Muertos_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Maritime fiction"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1901, it was suggested by one A. W. Van Buren of Yale University that the island\'s name may be related to the <a href="/wiki/Sea_shanty" title="Sea shanty">sea shanty</a> "<a href="/wiki/Dead_Man%27s_Chest" title="Dead Man&#39;s Chest">Dead Man\'s Chest</a>", first written by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Louis_Stevenson" title="Robert Louis Stevenson">Robert Louis Stevenson</a> for his novel <i><a href="/wiki/Treasure_Island" title="Treasure Island">Treasure Island</a></i>;<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> However, no scholar since Van Buren has made this connection nor is there any evidence to support such a claim. Further, Van Buren says that Stevenson did not write the song "<a href="/wiki/Dead_man%27s_chest" class="mw-redirect" title="Dead man&#39;s chest">Dead man\'s chest</a>" himself, that he "picked it up somewhere", which is another baseless claim (see "<a href="/wiki/Dead_Man%27s_Chest#Background" title="Dead Man&#39;s Chest">Dead Man\'s Chest</a>" for more information on the fakelore surrounding this song).<sup id="cite_ref-bc_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bc-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Finally, the shanty invented by Stevenson was most likely inspired by a different island, <a href="/wiki/Dead_Chest_Island,_British_Virgin_Islands" title="Dead Chest Island, British Virgin Islands">Dead Chest Island, British Virgin Islands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Kingsley_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kingsley-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="Jos\xc3\xa9_Joaquim_Almeida"><span id="Jos.C3.A9_Joaquim_Almeida"></span>Jos\xc3\xa9 Joaquim Almeida</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caja_de_Muertos_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Jos\xc3\xa9 Joaquim Almeida"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>One story given by Kurt Pitzer and Tara Stevens is that of a Portuguese pirate, Jos\xc3\xa9 Almeida.<sup id="cite_ref-books.google.com_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-books.google.com-39"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> A former merchant sailor, Almeida fell in love with a Basque woman in <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Cura\xc3\xa7ao">Cura\xc3\xa7ao</a>, married her in <a href="/wiki/St._Thomas,_US_Virgin_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Thomas, US Virgin Islands">St. Thomas, US Virgin Islands</a> and took her pirating with him around the Caribbean. On the first raid, she was killed by a stray bullet. Distraught, Almeida had her embalmed and placed in a glass box inside a copper coffin. He buried her in a cave in a deserted island near Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>40<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> He would come every month to gaze over her preserved body and leave half of his treasure in her grave. Almeida, however, was caught in the Puerto Rico mainland, tried, and executed in <a href="/wiki/Castillo_San_Felipe_del_Morro" title="Castillo San Felipe del Morro">Castillo San Felipe del Morro</a> in 1832. Many years later, a Spanish engineer discovered the glass and copper coffin, and identifying the cay on a map gave it its present name.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The treasure found, if any, was kept secret.<sup id="cite_ref-books.google.com_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-books.google.com-39"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Caja_de_muerto_playa.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Caja_de_muerto_playa.jpg/150px-Caja_de_muerto_playa.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="200" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Caja_de_muerto_playa.jpg/225px-Caja_de_muerto_playa.jpg 1.5x, Beach in Caja de Muertos</figcaption></figure>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="British_shipwreck">British shipwreck</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caja_de_Muertos_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: British shipwreck"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1599, several English merchants departed to China with a letter from Queen Elizabeth to the Chinese government.<sup id="cite_ref-Cardona204_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cardona204-42"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>42<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> However, the expedition was dangerous and a ship was lost to the weather.<sup id="cite_ref-Cardona205_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cardona205-43"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Off the Indian coast the group turned to piracy and after learning of a Portuguese ship filled with gold, silver, precious stones, other treasures and other valuables (spices, silk, some clothing), they attacked and captured the vessel.<sup id="cite_ref-Cardona205_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cardona205-43"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The fleet managed to capture another vessel, but in the process lost its flagship and a large part of the crew was fatally injured, including the fleet commander.<sup id="cite_ref-Cardona205_43-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cardona205-43"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Shorthanded, the group decided to return to England aboard a smaller ship, taking part of the loot with them.<sup id="cite_ref-Cardona205_43-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cardona205-43"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> After surviving an attempted mutiny, the Englishmen decided to take a canoe left behind by a frigate off Puerto Rico\'s eastern coast from where they took refuge at Caja de Muertos.<sup id="cite_ref-Cardona205_43-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cardona205-43"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Losing the smaller vessel, the Englishmen traveled to the main island in search of provisions, but one of them left behind to mind the canoe was captured and let the presence of the treasure known to the local authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-Cardona207_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cardona207-44"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>44<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>This led to an incursion to take the treasure, where all but one of the Englishmen were suddenly executed despite negotiations and the Spanish took the treasure with them and reported it as being captured in a battle with the English.<sup id="cite_ref-Cardona207_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cardona207-44"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>44<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The lone survivor, named Thomas, managed to build a makeshift boat out of wood remnants and drifted to Puerto Rico, being taken to San Germ\xc3\xa1n and telling his version of the events.<sup id="cite_ref-Cardona208_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cardona208-45"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>45<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The governor of Puerto Rico grew suspicious and an investigation was held between the autumn of 1599 and early 1600, which lead to a confession by one of the murderers.<sup id="cite_ref-Cardona208_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cardona208-45"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>45<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The authorities then began confiscating the treasure seized during the incursion, with other parts disappearing and reportedly ending in the hands of the governor\'s own nephew.<sup id="cite_ref-Cardona210_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cardona210-46"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>46<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The treasure confiscated was auctioned, with about 4,000 ducats of Indian silver remaining by 1602.<sup id="cite_ref-Cardona210_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cardona210-46"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>46<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The following governor employed another part in construction, with a collection of jewels and bars that were left behind being sold.<sup id="cite_ref-Cardona210_46-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cardona210-46"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>46<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The last piece, a large gemstone, was sent abroad for transport to Spain.<sup id="cite_ref-Cardona212_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cardona212-47"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>47<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> From these events, the island would gain the moniker of "Isla de Caja de Muertos" ("Dead Man\xe2\x80\x99s Chest").<sup id="cite_ref-Cardona207_44-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cardona207-44"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>44<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Natural_reserve">Natural reserve</h2><span
