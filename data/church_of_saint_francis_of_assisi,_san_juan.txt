0;">Designated&#160;NHLDCP</th><td class="infobox-data" style="border: 0;">October 10, 1972</td></tr></tbody></table><p>The <b>Church of Saint Francis of Assisi</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Iglesia de San Francisco de As\xc3\xads</i>) is a historic <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Parish" title="Parish">parish</a> church located in the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_Old_San_Juan" title="San Francisco, Old San Juan">San Francisco</a> sub-barrio of <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">Old San Juan</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>. The church has gone numerous transformations throughout its history and its original brick and masonry structure can still be admired. The church is also renowned for its crypt which is opened to visitors and at some point it contained the remains of Puerto Rican <a href="/wiki/Impressionism" title="Impressionism">Impressionist</a> painter <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Oller" title="Francisco Oller">Francisco Oller</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Church_of_Saint_Francis_of_Assisi,_San_Juan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The original Church of Saint Francis of Assisi was originally built by the <a href="/wiki/Third_Order_of_Saint_Francis" title="Third Order of Saint Francis">Third Order of Franciscans</a> in 1756 in what is today San Francisco Square (Spanish: <i>Plaza San Francisco</i>) in front of the contemporary church. The modern church was originally an expansion of the original church, which went through numerous transformations after its construction, and it dates to 1876. The mural at the church\'s altar dates to the 1940s. The church and its mural were meticulously restored in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The church is open to visitors and still offers religious services.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Gallery">Gallery</h2><span
