style="font-weight:bold; border: 0;">Designated&#160;RNSZH</th><td class="infobox-data" style="border: 0;">December 21, 2000</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>El Parterre</b> is a landscaped park in <a href="/wiki/Aguadilla,_Puerto_Rico" title="Aguadilla, Puerto Rico">Aguadilla, Puerto Rico</a>, that was built in 1851. The park encloses the <b>Ojo de Agua</b> (<abbr style="font-size:85%" title="literal translation">lit.</abbr><span style="white-space: nowrap;">&#8201;</span><span class="gloss-quot">\'</span><span class="gloss-text">water eye</span><span class="gloss-quot">\'</span>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_Spanish" title="Puerto Rican Spanish">Spanish</a> for \'<a href="/wiki/Spring_(hydrology)" title="Spring (hydrology)">spring</a>\' or \'water source\'), also referred to as <b>Manantial Ojo de Agua</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> a natural spring which was a source of water for Spanish soldiers, and the source of a small <a href="/wiki/Stream" title="Stream"><i>rivulet</i></a> locally called Chico River (\'little river\') which empties into the Aguadilla Bay.<sup id="cite_ref-PartOjoNom_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PartOjoNom-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n</p><p>The park is located in <a href="/wiki/Aguadilla_barrio-pueblo" title="Aguadilla barrio-pueblo">downtown Aguadilla</a> and, in addition to the Ojo de Agua, it commemorates historic figures from the history of Aguadilla such as poet Jos\xc3\xa9 de Diego.<sup id="cite_ref-PartOjoNom_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PartOjoNom-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Parterre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Before the establishment of El Parterre, the Ojo de Agua served as a water source for the first European settlers of modern-day Aguadilla. Before the founding of Aguadilla, the site around the water source was used by <a href="/wiki/Francis_Drake" title="Francis Drake">Sir Francis Drake</a> as a resupplying base in 1595. The spring kept being used as a primary water source for Spanish soldiers and later on by the first European settlers of the region between the 16th and 18th centuries, who named the new town <i>San Carlos de La Aguadilla</i> partially after the Ojo de Agua (Aguadilla is also the diminutive of <a href="/wiki/Aguada,_Puerto_Rico" title="Aguada, Puerto Rico">Aguada</a>, the name of the older town located directly to the south). The site was also visited and mentioned by the <a href="/wiki/Benedictines" title="Benedictines">Benedictine</a> monk and historian <a href="/wiki/Fray_%C3%8D%C3%B1igo_Abbad_y_Lasierra" title="Fray \xc3\x8d\xc3\xb1igo Abbad y Lasierra">Fray Inigo Abad y La Sierra</a>, who described it as a site of great beauty.<sup id="cite_ref-PartOjoNom_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PartOjoNom-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The first foundations of El Parterre were established by <a href="/wiki/German_immigration_to_Puerto_Rico" title="German immigration to Puerto Rico">German</a> engineer Enrique Hau (Heinrich Hau) who built a mortar and stone enclosure around the spring and a shallow brick pool and stone channel along the stream in 1852. The <i>Puente de la Reina</i> (\'the Queen\'s Bridge\') was added to the plaza by orders of Spanish magistrate Adolfo Ruiz in 1865, and the rest of the park was completed in 1882, by then mayor Don Ram\xc3\xb3n M\xc3\xa9ndez de Arcay.<sup id="cite_ref-PartOjoNom_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PartOjoNom-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The park and spring were listed on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1986,<sup id="cite_ref-nris_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WkList_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WkList-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and on the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Register_of_Historic_Sites_and_Zones" title="Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones">Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones</a> in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Gallery">Gallery</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
