mall</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>. At the time of its inauguration in 1962,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> it was Puerto Rico\'s largest mall and \xe2\x80\x9cthe most modern mall in the Caribbean.\xe2\x80\x9d<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is located at the intersection of Puerto Rico routes <a href="/wiki/PR-163" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-163">PR-163</a> (<a href="/wiki/Avenida_Las_Am%C3%A9ricas" title="Avenida Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas">Avenida Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_1" title="Puerto Rico Highway 1">PR-1</a> (<a href="/wiki/Miguel_Pou" title="Miguel Pou">Bulevar Miguel Pou</a>). Its original building cost was <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">$</a>2,500,000 ($43&#160;million in 2023 dollars<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-US_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-US-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It has been enlarged several times; including in 1991, in 2005, and again in 2010; this last time to accommodate the megastore <a href="/wiki/Burlington_(department_store)" title="Burlington (department store)">Burlington</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centro_del_Sur_Mall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Opening:_1960s">Opening: 1960s</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centro_del_Sur_Mall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Opening: 1960s"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>On June 27, 1962, developed by IBEC Realty Company,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Centro del Sur inaugurated with anchors <a href="/wiki/Kmart" title="Kmart">Kresge\xe2\x80\x99s</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barker%27s_Discount_Department_Stores" title="Barker&#39;s Discount Department Stores">Barker\xe2\x80\x99s</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Grand_Union_(supermarket)" title="Grand Union (supermarket)">Grand Union</a>. At the time it consisted of 120,000 square feet of retail space with 18 establishments such as <a href="/wiki/Farmacias_Moscoso" title="Farmacias Moscoso">Farmacias Moscoso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thom_McAn" title="Thom McAn">Thom McAn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Banco_de_Ponce" title="Banco de Ponce">Banco de Ponce</a>, Elegance, Mayfair, and One-Hour Martinizing. The $2,500,000 shopping center which took two years to be built, being in plans since 1960, it was considered at the time the most modern shopping center in the Caribbean. It was constructed by engineer Santiago Castrodad, and featured a 436 foot long shopping corridor with benches, gardens, and a central fountain with colored lighting that served as main attractions at the mall. The shopping corridor was about 40 feet wide and over 21 feet high, with its drastically angled accordion-type roof. Barker\xe2\x80\x99s made its debut on the island with this new store at the shopping center.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Centro_del_Sur.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img explains the main details of the new Centro del Sur in 1962.</figcaption></figure>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="1980s-1990s">1980s-1990s</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centro_del_Sur_Mall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1980s-1990s"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In June 1987, the <a href="/wiki/Kmart" title="Kmart">Kresge\xe2\x80\x99s</a> store at the shopping center would close, leaving employees who had worked at the store for over 20 years without jobs.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n</p><p>In 1994, a space previously occupied by <a href="/wiki/New_York_Department_Stores" title="New York Department Stores">New York Department Stores</a> became an extension of the main mall which then accommodated <a href="/wiki/Marshalls" title="Marshalls">Marshalls</a>, Tiendas Ferco, Me Salv\xc3\xa9, Post Net, and six other local tenants. This also triggered a complete reconstruction of the mall\'s entrance and facade. The combination of the re-tenanting and remodeling the center proved to be very successful as sales figures increased consistently.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1995, the Malec\xc3\xb3n Ave. overpass was completed providing new and better access to the shopping center and approximately 150 additional parking spaces for a total of 1,350 parking spaces.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="2000s">2000s</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centro_del_Sur_Mall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2000s"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 2005, a 20,000 square foot expansion for the <a href="/wiki/Marshalls" title="Marshalls">Marshalls Megastore</a> was to be completed by July of that year.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In the late 2000s, the mall was anchored by a <a href="/wiki/Marshalls" title="Marshalls">Marshalls Megastore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Farmacias_El_Amal" title="Farmacias El Amal">Farmacias El Amal</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Pitusa" title="Pitusa">Pitusa</a>. Rinc\xc3\xb3n del Sabor the food court at the mall had food options for all tastes, such as <a href="/wiki/Subway_(restaurant)" title="Subway (restaurant)">Subway</a>, AKA Los Tacos, Mr. Pretzels, Crave, Goodies, Charlie\xe2\x80\x99s Pizza, Country Deli, and Villa China\xe2\x80\x99s chinese exquisite food. Tenants at the time were establishments such as Me Salv\xc3\xa9, <a href="/wiki/S._H._Kress_%26_Co." title="S. H. Kress &amp; Co.">Almacenes Kress</a>, Marianne, Rave, Madison, Jeans.com, <a href="/wiki/Payless_(footwear_retailer)" title="Payless (footwear retailer)">Payless Shoe Source</a>, <a href="/wiki/RadioShack" title="RadioShack">Radio Shack</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foot_Locker" title="Foot Locker">Foot Locker</a>, La Defensa,  <a href="/wiki/KB_Toys" title="KB Toys">Kay-Bee Toys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rent-A-Center" title="Rent-A-Center">Rent-A-Center</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Western_Auto" title="Western Auto">Western Auto</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="2010s,_and_on"><span id="2010s.2C_and_on"></span>2010s, and on</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centro_del_Sur_Mall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010s, and on"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>On June 24, 2010, it was reported that a new 69,595 square foot <a href="/wiki/Burlington_(department_store)" title="Burlington (department store)">Burlington Coat Factory</a> store would be located in the section of the mall that formerly housed <a href="/wiki/Western_Auto" title="Western Auto">Western Auto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pitusa" title="Pitusa">Pitusa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Farmacias_El_Amal" title="Farmacias El Amal">Farmacia El Amal</a>, Madison and <a href="/wiki/Rent-A-Center" title="Rent-A-Center">Rent-A-Center</a>, with the latter two stores being relocated to another section of the mall. Located in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>, the 220,304 square foot Centro del Sur Mall had enjoyed additional exposure and traffic as the result of renewal efforts in the southern city\'s historic section at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n</p><p>On March 27, 2011, the <a href="/wiki/Burlington_(department_store)" title="Burlington (department store)">Burlington Coat Factory</a> store at the mall finally inaugurated.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In October 2012, a <a href="/wiki/Burlington_(department_store)" title="Burlington (department store)">Burlington Baby Depot</a> store opened at the mall.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a closest to the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce Historic Zone</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Design">Design</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centro_del_Sur_Mall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Design"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>A unique characteristic of this mall is its zig-zag <a href="/wiki/Prefab" class="mw-redirect" title="Prefab">prefab</a> reinforced concrete roof which is not found in any other mall in Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The mall\'s main corridor is oriented in a north-south axis, with a shorter east-west axis and a smaller east-west hallway.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Centro del Sur is the first and only mall in Puerto Rico with a truss zig-zag prefab concrete roof system.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Tenants">Tenants</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centro_del_Sur_Mall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Tenants"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Centro del Sur has over 50 stores and more than 30 kiosks and food court vendors.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> An area for community events at the mall is unique among malls in Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Mall has been southern Puerto Rico\'s regional outlet for a number of major national departmental chains including <a href="/wiki/Kmart_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kmart (United States)">Kresge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barker%27s" class="mw-redirect" title="Barker&#39;s">Barker\'s</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marshalls" title="Marshalls">Marshalls</a> and <a href="/wiki/Burlington_Coat_Factory" class="mw-redirect" title="Burlington Coat Factory">Burlington</a>. The mall is managed by Commercial Centers Management.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centro_del_Sur_Mall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The mall is located on the southwestern corner of Miguel Pou Boulevard (<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_1" title="Puerto Rico Highway 1">PR-1</a>) and Las Americas Avenue (<a href="/wiki/PR-163" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-163">PR-163</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The main branch of the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Public_Library" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce Public Library">Ponce Public Library</a> is within walking distance west of the mall, and Urbanizacion La Alhambra, Puerto Rico\'s first upper-class suburban development,<sup id="cite_ref-ToroNom_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ToroNom-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> is located diagonally across from the Mall to the northwest. This puts it just outside the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce Historic Zone</a>.\n</p><p>A limited-access highway, <a href="/wiki/PR-12" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-12">PR-12</a>, also known as Avenida Santiago de los Caballeros, and which opened to the public in 1995, was built just west of the mall providing easy access to it. The mall is flanked by several outlying banks, and restaurants which together bring the total effective retail area within the plaza to over 350,000 square feet<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The mall is also readily accessible via taxi service with the Union Taxi terminal depot located at the mall\'s main eastern entrance.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Accolades">Accolades</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centro_del_Sur_Mall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Accolades"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Centro del Sur Mall was the venue used by <a href="/wiki/Melina_Le%C3%B3n" title="Melina Le\xc3\xb3n">Melina Le\xc3\xb3n</a> to debut her new album \xe2\x80\x9cCoraz\xc3\xb3n de Mujer\xe2\x80\x9d<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Other talent shows have also taken place here.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> On 24 December 1966, the mall became the first public place in Puerto Rico where <a href="/wiki/Santa_Claus" title="Santa Claus">Santa Claus</a> landed via a helicopter to hand out gifts to waiting children, a marketing and advertising stunt.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Today, the yearly \xe2\x80\x9cMiss Centro del Sur\xe2\x80\x9d event takes place instead.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="References">References</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
