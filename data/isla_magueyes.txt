ul{line-height:inherit;list-style:none;margin:0;padding:0}.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol li,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul li{margin-bottom:0}</style><div class="plainlist"><ul><li><a title="UTC-4">UTC-4</a>)</li></ul></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Isla Magueyes</b> (<i>Isle of Maguey</i>) is a 7.2 hectares (0.028&#160;sq&#160;mi; 0.072&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) island 50 metres (160&#160;ft) from the southwest coast of the island of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-b1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-b1-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is encircled with <a href="/wiki/Mangrove" title="Mangrove">mangrove</a> and has an interior of <a href="/wiki/Xeric" class="mw-redirect" title="Xeric">dry scrub</a> habitat, where it gets its name.<sup id="cite_ref-Cohen_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cohen-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is named for the presence of many <a href="/wiki/Century_plant" class="mw-redirect" title="Century plant">century plants</a> or maguey (<i><a href="/wiki/Agave_americana" title="Agave americana">Agave americana</a></i>). The surrounding shelf of the island is mostly <a href="/wiki/Coral_reef" title="Coral reef">coral reef</a>. There are buildings on the western end of the island associated with the Department of Marine Sciences, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Mayag%C3%BCez" title="University of Puerto Rico at Mayag\xc3\xbcez">University of Puerto Rico at Mayag\xc3\xbcez</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DMS_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DMS-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> UPR- Mayag\xc3\xbcez is an important center in the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a> region for the study of tropical marine science due to its location, facilities, and first-rate researchers.<sup id="cite_ref-ccri_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccri-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The research facilities includes the Puerto Rico Water Resources and Environmental Research Institute, the Caribbean Coral Reef Institute (CCRI), the Research and Development Center, the Agricultural Research Station and the Caribbean Atmospheric Research Center (ATMOSCarib).<sup id="cite_ref-DMS_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DMS-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ccri_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccri-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Fauna">Fauna</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isla_Magueyes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Fauna"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>A free-ranging colony of <a href="/wiki/Feral" title="Feral">feral</a> <a href="/wiki/Cyclura_nubila" title="Cyclura nubila">Cuban iguanas</a>, released from a <a href="/wiki/Dr._Juan_A._Rivero_Zoo" title="Dr. Juan A. Rivero Zoo">zoo</a> that was closed on the island in the <a href="/wiki/1950s" title="1950s">1950s</a> inhabit all parts of the island.<sup id="cite_ref-b1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-b1-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Cohen_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cohen-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-christian_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-christian-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> While the iguanas are <a href="/wiki/Endangered_species" title="Endangered species">endangered</a> in their native <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> and are currently protected by the US <a href="/wiki/Endangered_Species_Act" class="mw-redirect" title="Endangered Species Act">Endangered Species Act</a>, there has been talk of eliminating or reducing the population here as they are considered an <a href="/wiki/Invasive_species" title="Invasive species">invasive species</a>. Currently dogs, cats, and most tourists are barred from the island to protect the iguanas.<sup id="cite_ref-bird_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bird-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This has turned the island into somewhat of an unofficial <a href="/wiki/Bird_sanctuary" class="mw-redirect" title="Bird sanctuary">bird sanctuary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brown_pelican" title="Brown pelican">brown pelicans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cattle_egret" title="Cattle egret">cattle egrets</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Heron" title="Heron">herons</a> are commonly observed here.<sup id="cite_ref-bird_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bird-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The iguana colony has been used as a research control group for various experiments concerning <a href="/wiki/Animal_communication" title="Animal communication">animal communication</a> and <a href="/wiki/Evolution" title="Evolution">evolution</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Martins_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Martins-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="References">References</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
