Carnegie">Carnegie Library</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Capitol_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Capitol of Puerto Rico">Capitol complex</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Olympic_Committee" title="Puerto Rico De Castro\'s tropical and mediterranean conscience objected to the architecture imported from the north, giving preference to the luminosity of the Caribbean, to the visual continuity of the spaces, to the galleries and balconies that served as a transition between interior and exterior spaces, shaped by the urban environment, the countryside or the inner courtyards.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="The_Athenaeum_Art_Gallery">The Athenaeum Art Gallery</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ateneo_Puertorrique%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Athenaeum Art Gallery"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Athenaeum\'s pride is its gallery of art, which consists of 459 artworks.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Among the artworks held at the Atheneum one of its most recognizable is <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Oller" title="Francisco Oller">Francisco Oller\'s</a> 1890 painting "<i>La Escuela del Maestro <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Cordero_(educator)" title="Rafael Cordero (educator)">Rafael Cordero</a>".</i><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. The Experimental Theatre of the Puerto Rican Antheneaum was founded in 1952 as the first incumbent company of the institution. As such, one of the greatest Puerto Rican playwrights of the twentieth century, <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Marqu%C3%A9s" title="Ren\xc3\xa9 Marqu\xc3\xa9s">Ren\xc3\xa9 Marqu\xc3\xa9s</a>, commences a dissemination and creative project of drama that continues to this day, totaling over 500 productions and 26 Avant-Garde Theatre Festivals.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Many participants of the Festivals are members of Puerto Rico\'s thespian community. The Experimental Theatre Hall, which has capacity for 200, has been the premiere of many works that are now considered classical Puerto Rican drama. The most well-known of these is Ren\xc3\xa9 Marqu\xc3\xa9s\' <a href="/wiki/La_Carreta" title="La Carreta">La Carreta</a>, which premiered in 1953 under the patronage of the Athenaeum. Between 1973 and 2003 there were 36 editions of the Theatre Festival. During each edition approximately 10 productions were presented, except in the XIX Edition, where twenty works took place, of which fifteen young authors or Puerto Rican authors of note, and the XX edition, which premiered 26 plays.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
