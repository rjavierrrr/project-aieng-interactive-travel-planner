scope="row" class="infobox-label" style="font-weight:bold; border: 0;">Designated&#160;NHLDCP</th><td class="infobox-data" style="border: 0;">February 27, 2013</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Plaza Col\xc3\xb3n</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> for <i>Columbus square</i> or <i>plaza</i>), formerly called <b>Santiago Square</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Town_square" title="Town square">plaza</a> or public town square located in <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">Old San Juan</a> where the easternmost city walls and main city gate (Puerta de Santiago) used to be located. The square today is a popular meeting place and is often used as a starting point for sightseeing in Old San Juan as it is located close to the main cruise ship docks, numerous restaurants, caf\xc3\xa9s and gift shops, and important landmarks such as <a href="/wiki/Castillo_San_Crist%C3%B3bal_(San_Juan)" title="Castillo San Crist\xc3\xb3bal (San Juan)">San Crist\xc3\xb3bal Castle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Teatro_Tapia" title="Teatro Tapia">Tapia Theater</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Antiguo_Casino_de_Puerto_Rico" title="Antiguo Casino de Puerto Rico">Old Casino</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Capitol_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Capitol of Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico Capitol</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Col%C3%B3n_(San_Juan)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:PlazadeSantiago.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/PlazadeSantiago.jpg/220px-PlazadeSantiago.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="181" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/PlazadeSantiago.jpg/330px-PlazadeSantiago.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/PlazadeSantiago.jpg/440px-PlazadeSantiago.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2668" data-file-height="2197" /></a><figcaption>View of Santiago Square in 1892.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The area was originally considered an important transportation and social center in San Juan. The Tapia Theater was built around this time and inaugurated in 1832.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Christopher Columbus statue was first erected in the area in 1893 to celebrate the 400th anniversary of the European "discovery" of Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The statue of Juan Ponce de Leon that used to be located in the center of the square was moved to Plaza San Jose (next to <a href="/wiki/San_Jos%C3%A9_Church" title="San Jos\xc3\xa9 Church">San Jose Church</a>). Before its construction in 1897, the plaza used to be the eastern end of the defensive wall system that surrounded San Juan. It used to be the location of one of the main city gates, <b>Santiago Gate</b> (Spanish: <i>Puerta de Santiago</i>). This section of the wall was torn down in the May of 1897 as it was deemed unnecessary and it was considered an obstacle to San Juan\'s economic growth as the walled district was isolated from the rest of the modern city. The statue of Columbus atop its column at the center of the square caused people to start referring to the plaza as Plaza Col\xc3\xb3n rather than Plaza Santiago.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The square today is paved with marble tiles and lightened by adorned lamp posts which were installed during the revitalization of the area in the latter half of the 20th century.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Gallery">Gallery</h2><span
