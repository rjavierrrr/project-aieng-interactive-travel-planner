href="/wiki/Carolina,_Puerto_Rico" title="Carolina, Puerto Rico">Carolina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. Anchored by <a href="/wiki/JCPenney" title="JCPenney">JCPenney</a>, <a href="/wiki/TJ_Maxx" title="TJ Maxx">TJ Maxx</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Cinemas" title="Caribbean Cinemas">Caribbean Cinemas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burlington_Coat_Factory" class="mw-redirect" title="Burlington Coat Factory">Burlington</a> <a href="/wiki/Forever_21" title="Forever 21">Forever 21</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chili%27s_Grill_%26_Bar" class="mw-redirect" title="Chili&#39;s Grill &amp; Bar">Chili\'s Grill &amp; Bar</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Supermercados_Econo" title="Supermercados Econo">Supermercados Econo</a> it features 161 stores, making it the second largest mall in Puerto Rico and the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The mall has a <a href="/wiki/Food_court" title="Food court">food court</a> in its second floor and several offices in its third floor.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Development_and_opening">Development and opening</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Development and opening"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Starting development as early as 1972 and being scheduled for completion by 1976, it was estimated to generate at least $75 million dollars in retail sales annually during its first 3 years of operation and over $100 million thereafter.<sup id="cite_ref-miami_1972_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-miami_1972-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The mall was developed and was owned at the time by Plaza Carolina Associates, a coordinated project between <a href="/wiki/GGP_Inc." title="GGP Inc.">General Growth Properties</a> and IBEC. Eduardo Molinari was the architect for the project.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On September 27, 1978, <a href="/wiki/JCPenney" title="JCPenney">JCPenney</a> would officially inaugurate its store at the mall. The JCPenney store, whose physical extension covered 270,000 square feet (25,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup>), was the first store to open its doors in Plaza Carolina, and according to a spokesperson, it was the largest store in sales volume that the chain had ever opened in its entire history. It would create more than 400 new jobs in the area.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>First having a soft opening with its anchors on the 9th, the mall finally opened on October 11, 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It opened with more than 100 stores, including 3 anchor stores, and parking for 5,000 cars.<sup id="cite_ref-miami_1972_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-miami_1972-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Its three main anchor stores at the time were, <a href="/wiki/Sears" title="Sears">Sears</a>, <a href="/wiki/JCPenney" title="JCPenney">JCPenney</a>, and a 88,000-square-foot (8,200&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) <a href="/wiki/Gonz%C3%A1lez_Pad%C3%ADn" title="Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Pad\xc3\xadn">Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Pad\xc3\xadn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It also opened with a 2-level, 37,000&#160;sq&#160;ft (3,400&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) <a href="/wiki/F._W._Woolworth_Company" title="F. W. Woolworth Company">Woolworth</a> store, which would additionally open with a "Harvest House" restaurant.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Later, a 26,379&#160;sq&#160;ft (2,450.7&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) Tiendas Capri was added.<sup id="cite_ref-dmm_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dmm-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Velasco_arrival_and_the_eighties">Velasco arrival and the eighties</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Velasco arrival and the eighties"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>On September 28, 1979,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> a 2-level, 65,000-square-foot (6,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) <a href="/wiki/Es_de_Velasco" title="Es de Velasco">Velasco</a> department store opened at the mall.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was supposed to be a high point for the chain, but it proved unsuccessful. The store was located in an area of families with modest means, and its high prices were not compatible with the economic situation of the time, which caused low sales. The store announced its closure on August 26, 1981.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was liquidated and closed by August 29, 1981.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The first level of the store was turned into what is now known as "La Plazoleta", the food court in the mall, and the second level of the former store was turned into office space. One of the first spaces to open at La Plazoleta was a Tex Critter\'s Pizza Jamboree restaurant, the first for the chain outside the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-primeros_en_ventas_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-primeros_en_ventas-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In October 1981, Plaza Carolina celebrated its third anniversary with a special appearance by <a href="/wiki/Sophia_Loren" title="Sophia Loren">Sophia Loren</a> on October 24. Recent additions to the center and upcoming new stores were showcased by the assistant manager Norman Mel\xc3\xa9ndez. He highlighted Marki-II, a new children\'s store in operation in the second level of the mall offering children\'s clothing, and various children\'s items and accessories. Preciosa, which specialized in cosmetics and perfumes, had also opened its second store, located on the lower level, just east of the sun sculpture in the center of the mall. Land of Oz, an electronic games room had just finished its third expansion. Another new store was El Criollito, located in the "La Plazoleta" food court, specializing in Creole foods for Puerto Rican tastes. Several other stores were also under construction including: <a href="/wiki/Radio_Shack" class="mw-redirect" title="Radio Shack">Radio Shack</a>, Fashion Warehouse, Value Slacks, and the Sandwich Marker, an addition to La Plazoleta. These eventually opened to the public. Part of the space formerly occupied by Velasco would be remodeled to accommodate several small commercial spaces. At the same time, the attached food court would be expanded to offer a restaurant area.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In August 1982, in sales per square foot, it was among the highest 10 percent of mall-type shopping centers in the entire United States. It had never gone through any economic problems since it had opened its doors; at this point, it had always increased sales, year-on-year.<sup id="cite_ref-primeros_en_ventas_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-primeros_en_ventas-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n</p><p>In December 1989, it was announced that the mall would be undergoing a massive renovation to begin by January 1990. EQK Partners which had acquired the mall a year earlier planned these renovations to be completed by November 1990. Costs for these ambitious renovations were said to go over $7 million dollars, with around 200 construction workers working on the massive project. This renovation included a full facelift for the mall, with new entrances, a new fountain, and a new color scheme.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Reinauguration_and_the_nineties">Reinauguration and the nineties</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reinauguration and the nineties"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>On November 15, 1990, Plaza Carolina was officially reinaugurated after the $7 million renovations. Among the changes, Richard Gelber, architect of the Shapiro, Petrauskas and Gelber company, in charge of remodeling project, indicated that new colors were used on facades to reflect the tropical environment of the island. New entrances were created with glazed ceramic tiles, pink stucco, diagonal candle-shaped walls, and neon lights that illuminate the interior and exterior of the center. They also installed skylights in the ceilings over the central areas with a lattice to let in natural light. A fountain surrounded by tropical plants was built in the central area that could be covered and become a stage for special events, since it had seats for the public placed in the form of an amphitheater. The decoration included the installation of glazed tiles topped with shiny metal and neon bands on the corridor columns. Reopening celebration activities for the public were announced by the marketing director, Josefina Cantellops. During this time the mall had a total of 1.1 million square feet of retail space with 170 stores, and it was primarily anchored by <a href="/wiki/JCPenney" title="JCPenney">JCPenney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sears" title="Sears">Sears</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gonz%C3%A1lez_Pad%C3%ADn" title="Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Pad\xc3\xadn">Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Pad\xc3\xadn</a>, <a href="/wiki/F._W._Woolworth_Company" title="F. W. Woolworth Company">Woolworth</a>, Tiendas Capri, and a <a href="/wiki/Pueblo_Supermarkets" title="Pueblo Supermarkets">Pueblo Xtra</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In October 1995, <a href="/wiki/Gonz%C3%A1lez_Pad%C3%ADn" title="Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Pad\xc3\xadn">Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Pad\xc3\xadn</a> ceased operations after a failed buyout by the retailer <a href="/wiki/Dillard%27s" title="Dillard&#39;s">Dillard\'s</a> which was supposed to save the department store; for unknown reasons the deal never happened, causing the permanent closure of Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Pad\xc3\xadn.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Over the passing years the former space of Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Pad\xc3\xadn was subdivided into a mall entrance, and a <a href="/wiki/Forever_21" title="Forever 21">Forever 21</a>.\n</p><p>In October 1998, under the <a href="/wiki/Lend_Lease_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Lend Lease Corporation">Lend Lease Corporation</a> an agreement was made with <a href="/wiki/JLL_(company)" title="JLL (company)">LaSalle Partners Incorporated</a> to acquire from Lend Lease the then current owners of the mall Compass Retail Management and Leasing operations for an estimated $180 million dollars with provisions for an earnout payment of up to $77.5 million dollars over 5 years, of which Plaza Carolina was included.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="1999_expansion_and_the_2000s">1999 expansion and the 2000s</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1999 expansion and the 2000s"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1999, the mall underwent a $25 million expansion and renovation which added over 60,000 square feet (5,600&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) in retail, a 12-screen <a href="/wiki/CineVista_Theatres" title="CineVista Theatres">CineVista Theatres</a>, and a 510,000-square-foot (47,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) parking deck.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In August 2000, after its major expansion, Plaza Carolina reported that it continued to add stores to its tenant mix. Its most recent arrivals at the time included island-newcomers <a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Shops" title="Rainbow Shops">Rainbow</a>, Styles, and d.e.m.o. as well as Frank Mora, which already had a local presence. <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Russe_(retailer)" title="Charlotte Russe (retailer)">Charlotte Russe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Old_Navy" title="Old Navy">Old Navy</a> were scheduled to open in September and November, respectively. The 3,857&#160;sq&#160;ft (358.3&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) Rainbow store featured clothing, accessories, and footwear for women. Styles would also offer fashionable clothing and accessories for women and juniors in its 3,369&#160;sq&#160;ft (313.0&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) store. Specializing in hip-hop style, d.e.m.o. would include the Sean John line from singer-producer <a href="/wiki/Puff_Daddy" class="mw-redirect" title="Puff Daddy">Puff Daddy</a> in its Plaza Carolina store, which covered 2,641&#160;sq&#160;ft (245.4&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) and was the largest of the U.S.-based chain, according to Juliana Castillo, the mall\'s marketing manager. The Frank Mora store, with 2,265&#160;sq&#160;ft (210.4&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) of retail space, would be the only one its stores that catered to both men and women. The Old Navy store would measure 17,313&#160;sq&#160;ft (1,608.4&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) and would occupy the site once used by Woolworth on the first level. Charlotte Russe, also to be on the mall\'s first level, would measure about 9,000&#160;sq&#160;ft (840&#160;m<sup>2</sup>).<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In May 2004, <a href="/wiki/Simon_Property_Group_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Simon Property Group Inc.">Simon Property Group Inc.</a> completed its 100 percent interest from LaSalle Partners in Plaza Carolina for $309 million dollars. At the time, the mall had maintained a 98-percent-average occupancy rate over the last five years, generating total annual sales of approximately $275 million and sales per square foot of over $450.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In January 2008, the <a href="/wiki/CineVista_Theatres" title="CineVista Theatres">CineVista Theatres</a> shuttered due to bankruptcy.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Later in May 2017, it was announced that a new <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Cinemas" title="Caribbean Cinemas">Caribbean Cinemas</a> would be opening in Spring 2018, bringing the mall back to having a cinema.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Caribbean Cinemas previously had a presence at the mall from 1996 until 1998 when it was moved to <a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Escorial&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Plaza Escorial (page does not exist)">Plaza Escorial</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="From_2010">From 2010</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: From 2010"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In Spring 2011, Simon Property Group announced that the plaza, a major shopping destination for 33 years, would be completely renovated beginning the Spring of 2011.
