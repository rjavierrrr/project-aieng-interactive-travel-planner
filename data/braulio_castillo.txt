His two sons, <a href="/wiki/Braulio_Castillo,_hijo" class="mw-redirect" title="Braulio Castillo, hijo">Braulio Castillo, Jr.</a> and Jorge Castillo, are also actors. He was born in <a href="/wiki/Bayam%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico" title="Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico">Bayam\xc3\xb3n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.\n</p><p>Castillo played in the <i>Orquesta Mandolina</i> in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, before working as an actor. He graduated from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico" title="University of Puerto Rico">University of Puerto Rico</a> with a major in the performing arts. He had roles in Puerto Rican television <a href="/wiki/Soap_operas" class="mw-redirect" title="Soap operas">soap operas</a>, during the 1950s, including <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=El_derecho_de_nacer_(1959_telenovela)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="El derecho de nacer (1959 telenovela) (page does not exist)">El derecho de nacer</a></i>; he worked at <a href="/wiki/Telemundo" title="Telemundo">Telemundo</a>\n<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> productions.\n</p><p>Castillo later relocated to <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>, where he performed the leading role in the <a href="/wiki/Telenovela" title="Telenovela">telenovela</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Simplemente_Mar%C3%ADa_(Peru)" class="mw-redirect" title="Simplemente Mar\xc3\xada (Peru)">Simplemente Mar\xc3\xada</a>" ("Simply Mary"), with Peruvian actress <a href="/wiki/Saby_Kamalich" title="Saby Kamalich">Saby Kamalich</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SinghalRogers1999_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SinghalRogers1999-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> There, Castillo was the first <a href="/wiki/MC" class="mw-redirect" title="MC">MC</a> of the international children\'s puppet show based on the Italian fictional character of <a href="/wiki/Topo_Gigio" title="Topo Gigio">Topo Gigio</a>.\n</p><p>He then moved to Mexico during the 1960s, and performed in <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Mexico" title="Cinema of Mexico">Mexican</a> films such as "El Cielo y Tu" ("Heaven and You"), among others, and working in several <a href="/wiki/Televisa" title="Televisa">Televisa</a> productions.\n</p><p>In 1970 Castillo was hit in the head by a rock, while filming a scene for a movie. He survived, but retired from the entertainment business. He still occasionally made appearances on television.\n</p><p>He died at the age of 81 of natural causes at Santa Teresa Jornet elderly home in <a href="/wiki/Trujillo_Alto,_Puerto_Rico" title="Trujillo Alto, Puerto Rico">Trujillo Alto, Puerto Rico</a>. He was buried at Porta Coeli Cemetery in Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span
