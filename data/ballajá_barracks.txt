<b>Ballaj\xc3\xa1 Barracks</b> (<i><b>Cuartel de Ballaj\xc3\xa1</b></i> in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>) is a historic building and former military <a href="/wiki/Barracks" title="Barracks">barracks</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Ballaj%C3%A1,_Old_San_Juan" title="Ballaj\xc3\xa1, Old San Juan">Ballaj\xc3\xa1</a> section of <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">Old San Juan</a>, in the city of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. It is located close to <a href="/wiki/Castillo_San_Felipe_del_Morro" title="Castillo San Felipe del Morro">El Morro</a> and its esplanade, between the Morovis, Beneficiencia and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Norzagaray_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Norzagaray Street (page does not exist)">Norzagaray</a> streets. It was built from 1854 to 1864 to house Spanish and Puerto Rican regiments. The Ballaj\xc3\xa1 Barracks were the last large-scale construction project made by the <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_Spain" title="Monarchy of Spain">Spanish Crown</a> in the Americas.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The building has been owned by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Government of Puerto Rico">government of Puerto Rico</a> since 1976 and today it houses the <a href="/wiki/Museo_de_las_Am%C3%A9ricas" title="Museo de las Am\xc3\xa9ricas">Museum of the Americas</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Academia_Puertorrique%C3%B1a_de_la_Lengua_Espa%C3%B1ola" title="Academia Puertorrique\xc3\xb1a de la Lengua Espa\xc3\xb1ola">Puerto Rican Academy of the Spanish Language</a>, in addition to several businesses and institutions such as a dance school, exhibition spaces, restaurants and the State Office of Historic Conservation.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ballaj%C3%A1_Barracks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Ballaj\xc3\xa1 Barracks were built by the <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> <a href="/wiki/Militia" title="Militia">militia</a> from 1854 to 1864. To build it, six blocks from the <a href="/wiki/Ballaj%C3%A1,_Old_San_Juan" title="Ballaj\xc3\xa1, Old San Juan">Ballaj\xc3\xa1 subbarrio</a> (in Old San Juan) were expropriated from the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Order" title="Dominican Order">Dominican Order</a> and demolished in 1853. The three-story structure was completed in 1864, occupying an area of 7,700 square meters (83,000&#160;sq&#160;ft). It could accommodate over 1,000 citizens, originally Spanish soldiers and their families. Its vast interior <a href="/wiki/Patio" title="Patio">patio</a> is one of the best examples of 19th-century Spanish <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">architecture</a> in Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-Serial_set_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Serial_set-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Other than the housing facilities, the barracks had storage rooms, kitchens, <a href="/wiki/Mess_hall" class="mw-redirect" title="Mess hall">mess halls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dungeon" title="Dungeon">dungeons</a>, and horse <a href="/wiki/Stables" class="mw-redirect" title="Stables">stables</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American_War"><span id="Spanish.E2.80.93American_War"></span>Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ballaj%C3%A1_Barracks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The barracks were heavily used during the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> near the end of the 19th century. On May 12, 1898, during the American bombing of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, the fleet led by <a href="/wiki/Admiral" title="Admiral">Admiral</a> <a href="/wiki/William_T._Sampson" title="William T. Sampson">William T. Sampson</a> damaged the northeast side of the barracks.<sup id="cite_ref-Spanish-American_War_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spanish-American_War-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>After the war ended, and with the change of sovereignty, the barracks became the quarters for the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a> until 1939.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span /></a><figcaption>Ballaj\xc3\xa1 in 1920.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>During the Second World War, the barracks were used as a <a href="/wiki/Military_hospital" title="Military hospital">military hospital</a>, under the name of <b>Army General Hospital of <a href="/wiki/Fort_Brooke" title="Fort Brooke">Fort Brooke</a></b>. On August 31, 1944, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_War" title="United States Department of War">United States Department of War</a> issued <i>General Order No. 71</i> which renamed the hospital to <b>Rodriguez (161st) General Hospital</b>, in honor of Major <a href="/wiki/Fernando_E._Rodr%C3%ADguez_Vargas" title="Fernando E. Rodr\xc3\xadguez Vargas">Fernando E. Rodr\xc3\xadguez Vargas</a>. \n</p><p>After the end of the war, the building was abandoned and it rapidly decayed. Although it was in disrepair, the building was included in the first heritage list of historic structures and monuments prepared by academic architect Mario J. Buschiazzo for the Puerto Rico Planning Board in 1955.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Scholar and archaeologist <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Alegr%C3%ADa" title="Ricardo Alegr\xc3\xada">Ricardo Alegr\xc3\xada</a> proposed revitalization of the building for it to be used as an academic or educational institution.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Today">Today</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ballaj%C3%A1_Barracks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Today"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Ballaj\xc3\xa1 Barracks today house several educational and cultural organizations, namely the<i> <a href="/wiki/Museo_de_las_Am%C3%A9ricas" title="Museo de las Am\xc3\xa9ricas">Museo de las Am\xc3\xa9ricas</a></i> on the second floor of the building since 1992. This museum is dedicated to the history, culture and heritage of the American continent. The <i>Museo de Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas</i> has three permanent collections: African Heritage, the Indian in America, and Popular Arts in America. In addition to the museum on the second floor, the first floor houses a dance school specializing in <a href="/wiki/Tablao" title="Tablao">tablao</a> <a href="/wiki/Flamenco" title="Flamenco">flamenco</a>, a bar, a movie theater, a coffee exhibition and caf\xc3\xa9, and a Spanish restaurant called Rinc\xc3\xb3n Ib\xc3\xa9rico, while the second-floor hosts offices and academic institutions such as the <a href="/wiki/Academia_Puertorrique%C3%B1a_de_la_Lengua_Espa%C3%B1ola" title="Academia Puertorrique\xc3\xb1a de la Lengua Espa\xc3\xb1ola">Puerto Rican Academy of the Spanish Language</a> and the State Office of Historic Conservation.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The building sustained damages after <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> but restoration was successful with help of more than $17.5 million in <a href="/wiki/Federal_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Federal Emergency Management Agency">FEMA</a> grants.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> After careful structural analysis and studies from the State Historic Preservation Office, a sedum <a href="/wiki/Green_roof" title="Green roof">green roof</a> (or living roof) was installed in the barracks rooftop. This area is open to visitors, and it consists of observation and sitting areas, walkways and solar panels.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Ownership">Ownership</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ballaj%C3%A1_Barracks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ownership"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Initially, when Puerto Rico was ceded by Spain to the United States, the federal government paid the Catholic Church for this property, among others in the area.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Ownership of the property, claimed by the Catholic Church, was not a straightforward matter and had to be resolved by the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Supreme Court of Puerto Rico">Supreme Court</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Annual_Report_1909_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Annual_Report_1909-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PR_Supreme_Court_1906_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Supreme_Court_1906-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Root_United_States_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Root_United_States-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Government of Puerto Rico">Government of Puerto Rico</a> acquired the building in 1976 through a transfer from the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_the_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="Government of the United States">Government of the United States</a> with the commitment of restoring it and using it for cultural, educative, and touristic purposes. In 1986, a reform plan for the <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">San Juan Historic Zone</a> was sketched and the building was restored from 1990 to 1993.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading
