href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>, dedicated to the seven fire fighters and one civilian that subdued the flames of the "El Polvorin" fire on the night of <a href="/wiki/Calle_25_de_Enero" title="Calle 25 de Enero">25 January 1899</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monumento_a_los_h%C3%A9roes_de_El_Polvor%C3%ADn_(obelisk)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The monument is located in a shady area on the eastern half of <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Degetau" title="Plaza Degetau">Plaza Degetau</a>, the southern plaza at <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Delicias" title="Plaza Las Delicias">Plaza Las Delicias</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>, and it honors the following seven firemen and one civilian:<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Rafael_Rivera_Esbr%C3%AD" title="Rafael Rivera Esbr\xc3\xad">Rafael Rivera Esbr\xc3\xad</a> (civilian)</li>\n<li>Pedro Sabater (2nd Corporal)</li>\n<li>Rafael del Valle (1st Corporal)</li>\n<li>Cayetano Casals (2nd Corporal)</li>\n<li>Pedro Ruiz (1st Sargent)</li>\n<li>Juan Romero (1st Corporal)</li>\n<li>Gregorio Rivera (Gastador)</li>\n<li>Tomas Rivera (2nd Corporal)</li></ul>\n<p>Its coordinates are 18.011503\xc2\xb0N 66.613846\xc2\xb0W (18\xc2\xb000\xe2\x80\xb241\xe2\x80\xb3N 66\xc2\xb036\xe2\x80\xb250\xe2\x80\xb3W).\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Background">Background</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monumento_a_los_h%C3%A9roes_de_El_Polvor%C3%ADn_(obelisk)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>On 25 January 1899, a large fire (later dubbed "El Polvorin") threatened the lives of the residents of the city of Ponce and, given Ponce\'s <i>de facto</i> role as Puerto Rico\'s banking and agricultural capital, it threatened the economy of Puerto Rico as a whole. The fire started at the horse barn of the recently deployed U.S. Army artillery encampment one block from the center of town, on Calle Salud between Calle Cristina and Calle Comercio, and quickly spread towards the army\'s artillery munitions storage area.  By the time the local commander realized there was a fire near the munitions compound, he judged it too risky for his men to try to put it out and ordered all personnel to move away from the area to let the fire extinguish itself, even if it meant explosions at the munitions depot. The commander also instructed the local firemen (then numbering about 40) not to enter the area or attempt to extinguish the fire. However, seven of the firemen and one civilian disobeyed the orders of the commander and decided to fight the fire. They were able to suppress the fire before it could get to the gunpowder reserves, without any loss of life or additional loss of property. Due to their efforts, disaster upon the then mostly wooden homes and businesses in town was averted. For their success, the group was honored many times both in Ponce and the rest of Puerto Rico. The <a href="/wiki/Obelisk" title="Obelisk">obelisk</a> was built to honor their memory.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="The_structure">The structure</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monumento_a_los_h%C3%A9roes_de_El_Polvor%C3%ADn_(obelisk)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The structure"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The obelisk was built in 1948.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It replaced the original obelisk built shortly after the <a href="/wiki/Calle_25_de_Enero" title="Calle 25 de Enero">25 January 1899</a> fire, but which was destroyed by the <a href="/wiki/1918_San_Ferm%C3%ADn_earthquake" title="1918 San Ferm\xc3\xadn earthquake">1918 San Ferm\xc3\xadn earthquake</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-explore_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-explore-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The obelisk was erected by a city board consisting of <a href="/wiki/Jaime_L._Drew" title="Jaime L. Drew">Jaime L. Drew</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Arias_(Mayor)" class="mw-redirect" title="Antonio Arias (Mayor)">Antonio Arias</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Mirabal" title="Antonio Mirabal">Antonio Mirabal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-explore_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-explore-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This monument is different from <a href="/wiki/Monumento_a_los_heroes_de_El_Polvor%C3%ADn_(tomb)" class="mw-redirect" title="Monumento a los heroes de El Polvor\xc3\xadn (tomb)">the obelisk/tomb built in 1911</a>, and dedicated to the same men, and which stands at the <a href="/wiki/Cementerio_Civil_de_Ponce" title="Cementerio Civil de Ponce">Cementerio Civil de Ponce</a> (Ponce Civil Cemetery).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span
