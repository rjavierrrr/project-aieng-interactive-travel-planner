href="/wiki/Guajataka_Scout_Reservation" title="Guajataka Scout Reservation">Guajataka Scout Reservation</a></div></div></div>\n<p><b>Guajataca Lake</b>, or <b>Lago Guajataca</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Reservoir" title="Reservoir">reservoir</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Guajataca_River" title="Guajataca River">Guajataca River</a> created by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Electric_Power_Authority" title="Puerto Rico Electric Power Authority">Puerto Rico Electric Power Authority</a> in 1929. It is located between the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/San_Sebasti%C3%A1n,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Sebasti\xc3\xa1n, Puerto Rico">San Sebasti\xc3\xa1n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebradillas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Quebradillas, Puerto Rico">Quebradillas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Isabela,_Puerto_Rico" title="Isabela, Puerto Rico">Isabela</a> in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and receives most of its water from the Rio Guajataca and Rio Chiquito de Cibao rivers. The lake primarily functions as a water reservoir as well as for recreational activities such as boating and fishing.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Various species of fish such as <a href="/wiki/Peacock_bass" title="Peacock bass">peacock bass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Largemouth_bass" title="Largemouth bass">largemouth bass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Centrarchidae" title="Centrarchidae">sunfish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Perch" title="Perch">perch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catfish" title="Catfish">catfish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tilapia" title="Tilapia">tilapia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Threadfin_shad" title="Threadfin shad">threadfin shad</a> can be found in the lake.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Guajataka_Scout_Reservation" title="Guajataka Scout Reservation">Guajataka Scout Reservation</a> partially borders the southern portion of the lake. The dam at Guajataca Lake experienced a structural failure on September 22, 2017, due to the hit from <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Lago_Guajataca_at_Damsite_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lago_Guajataca_at_Damsite-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n</p><p>The reservoir is considered a touristic area.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Guajataca_Dam">Guajataca Dam</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a Lake</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The Guajataca Dam is an earthen dam<sup id="cite_ref-NID-detail_12-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NID-detail-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> is currently used for irrigation and potable water purposes. A <a href="/wiki/Hydroelectric_power" class="mw-redirect" title="Hydroelectric power">hydroelectric power</a> station was built, but not longer in use.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The reservoir has a normal surface area of 1.321 square miles (3.42 square kilometres),<sup id="cite_ref-:0_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> its length is 2.5 miles (4.0 kilometres), its maximum width is 1 mile (1.6 kilometres),  the mean depth is 12 m and the maximum depth is 27 m, located near the dam.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> its maximum discharge is 28,954 cubic feet (819.9 cubic metres) per second. Its normal storage capacity is 34,050 <a href="/wiki/Acre-foot" title="Acre-foot">acre-feet</a> (42,000,000 <a href="/wiki/Cubic_metre" title="Cubic metre">cubic metres</a>), and its <a href="/wiki/Drainage_basin" title="Drainage basin">drainage basin</a> is 24.58 square miles (63.7&#160;km<sup>2</sup>).<sup id="cite_ref-:0_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-permit_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-permit-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Dam_construction">Dam construction</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guajataca_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Dam construction"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The construction of the dam was authorized by act 63 of the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Legislative Assembly of Puerto Rico">Legislature of Puerto Rico</a>, known as the "Isabela public irrigation law," approved April 19, 1919.<sup id="cite_ref-Governor1922_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Governor1922-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The dam was constructed starting in 1928. The reservoir had an initial storage capacity of 39,286 acre-feet (48&#160;million cubic metres), but by 1999 (71 years later), the capacity had been reduced to 34,276 acre-feet (42&#160;million cubic metres), as about 13% less, attributed to sediment erosion.<sup id="cite_ref-Lago_Guajataca_at_Damsite_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lago_Guajataca_at_Damsite-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\nThe surface area of the reservoir was 3.42&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (1.32&#160;sq&#160;mi) in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-Soler_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Soler-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/National_Inventory_of_Dams" title="National Inventory of Dams">National Inventory of Dams</a>, Guajataca Dam was designed by and is owned by the Puerto Rico Electric Power Authority.<sup id="cite_ref-NID-detail_12-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NID-detail-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Guajataca_dam_failure_risk">Guajataca dam failure risk</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guajataca_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Guajataca dam failure risk"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>On September 22, 2017, at 18:10 GMT, following <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a>, operators at Guajataca Dam announced that the dam\'s spillway was failing at the northern end of the lake and it could result in the whole dam collapsing.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> a few minutes later urged all 70,000 residents in the flood area to be evacuated. The <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> stated the dam was a "life-threatening situation".<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-guardian1722_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian1722-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> "It\xe2\x80\x99s a structural failure. I don\xe2\x80\x99t have any more details," Governor <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Rossell%C3%B3" title="Ricardo Rossell\xc3\xb3">Ricardo Rossell\xc3\xb3</a> stated. "We\xe2\x80\x99re trying to evacuate as many people as possible."<sup id="cite_ref-guardian1722_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian1722-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Rossell\xc3\xb3 ordered the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_National_Guard" title="Puerto Rico National Guard">Puerto Rico National Guard</a> and the Police to help assist in the evacuation effort downstream.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The dam lies across the Guajataca River to form a reservoir that can hold roughly 11 billion gallons of water.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>As of September 2017<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Guajataca_Lake&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, the dam was last inspected on October 23, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-NID-detail_12-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NID-detail-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The first phase of repairs to avoid the threat of flooding were completed on November 17, 2017. Since then about 10,000 residents, including farmers, who depend on the waters of the reservoir, have been struggling with the rationing of water. There is confusion and little transparency as to how the issue is being handled. Final repairs will be ongoing, until 2028.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2
