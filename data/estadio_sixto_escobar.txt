colspan="2">\n</th></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Estadio Sixto Escobar</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Multi-purpose_stadium" title="Multi-purpose stadium">multi-purpose stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The stadium was named after boxer <a href="/wiki/Sixto_Escobar" title="Sixto Escobar">Sixto Escobar</a>, the first champion of Puerto Rico, in 1938.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Originally built as a <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> venue, the stadium then became a frequent venue for <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> matches. In 1979, it was one of the three stadiums that served for the <a href="/wiki/1979_Pan_American_Games" title="1979 Pan American Games">VIII Pan American Games</a> held in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>. Nowadays, Sixto Escobar Stadium is the current home venue for C.A. San Juan.<sup id="cite_ref-bori_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bori-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estadio_Sixto_Escobar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The stadium was inaugurated on November 12, 1932. Originally it was a <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> venue, and was built in an area then administrated by the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Army" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Army">U.S. Army</a>. By the 1940s, a sector of the stadium, behind the baseball pitch, began to be used by some football enthusiasts. They played in a 100-metre length field known as <i>can\xc3\xb3dromo</i> (a track used for <a href="/wiki/Sighthound" title="Sighthound">sighthound</a> races). Some of the most notable Puerto Rican footballers of those times, started playing there.<sup id="cite_ref-bori_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bori-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Sixto_Escobar_and_Whitey_Brimstein_c1930cr.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Sixto_Escobar_and_Whitey_Brimstein_c1930cr.jpg/130px-Sixto_Escobar_and_Whitey_Brimstein_c1930cr.jpg" decoding="async" width="130" height="184" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Sixto_Escobar_and_Whitey_Brimstein_c1930cr.jpg/195px-Sixto_Escobar_and_Whitey_Brimstein_c1930cr.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Sixto_Escobar_and_Whitey_Brimstein_c1930cr.jpg/260px-Sixto_Escobar_and_Whitey_Brimstein_c1930cr.jpg 2x" data-file-width="618" data-file-height="876" /></a><figcaption>Boxer Sixto Escobar, in honor of whom the stadium was named in 1938</figcaption></figure>\n<p>In baseball, the <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Reds" title="Cincinnati Reds">Cincinnati Reds</a> conducted spring training at the stadium in 1935 and 1936.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In April 1938, the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Legislative Assembly of Puerto Rico">Legislative Assembly of Puerto Rico</a> voted to name the stadium and surrounding park in honor of <a href="/wiki/Sixto_Escobar" title="Sixto Escobar">Sixto Escobar</a>, a Puerto Rican professional boxer who became Puerto Rico\'s first world champion.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  A statue of Escobar stands on the grounds.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Miami_Marlins_(IL)" class="mw-redirect" title="Miami Marlins (IL)">Miami Marlins</a> were transferred to San Juan for the 1961 season and opened the season at Sixto Escobar Stadium.  Attendance was poor and the team was moved to Charleston, WV on May 19, 1961. The <a href="/wiki/Cangrejeros_de_Santurce_(baseball)" title="Cangrejeros de Santurce (baseball)">Cangrejeros de Santurce</a> of the professional <a href="/wiki/Liga_de_B%C3%A9isbol_Profesional_Roberto_Clemente" title="Liga de B\xc3\xa9isbol Profesional Roberto Clemente">Liga de B\xc3\xa9isbol Profesional Roberto Clemente</a> moved out to the newly constructed <a href="/wiki/Hiram_Bithorn_Stadium" title="Hiram Bithorn Stadium">Hiram Bithorn Stadium</a> in 1962.\n</p><p>By the 1970s the Sixto Escobar Stadium consolidated its predominance as a Puerto Rican football venue. In 1976 it was refurbished to host the <a href="/wiki/1976_CONCACAF_U-20_Tournament" title="1976 CONCACAF U-20 Tournament">1976 CONCACAF U-20 Tournament</a>, that also served as qualification for the <a href="/wiki/1977_FIFA_World_Youth_Championship" title="1977 FIFA World Youth Championship">1977 FIFA World Youth Championship</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mexico_national_under-20_football_team" title="Mexico national under-20 football team">Mexico</a> and <a href="/wiki/Honduras_national_under-20_football_team" title="Honduras national under-20 football team">Honduras</a> played the final at Sixto Escobar stadium on October 6.<sup id="cite_ref-bori_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bori-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Likewise, many events of the <a href="/wiki/1979_Pan_American_Games" title="1979 Pan American Games">1979 Pan American Games</a> were held at the stadium. On July 4, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_national_football_team" title="Puerto Rico national football team">Puerto Rico</a> beat <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic_national_football_team" title="Dominican Republic national football team">Dominican Republic</a> 1\xe2\x80\x930 in front of a full attendance.<sup id="cite_ref-bori_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bori-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1981, the stadium hosted the "Torneo 5 Estrellas", an international football tournament where <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF" title="Real Madrid CF">Real Madrid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Millonarios_F.C." title="Millonarios F.C.">Millonarios</a>, <a href="/wiki/Club_Universidad_Nacional" title="Club Universidad Nacional">Pumas UNAM</a>, a regional side of United States and local club Internacional de Puerto Rico participated. From the 1970s to early 2000s the stadium was a frequent venue of Puerto Rico national football team matches.<sup id="cite_ref-bori_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bori-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2000 the Sixto Escobar Stadium hosted all six matches of the inaugural <a href="/wiki/Torneo_de_Copa_de_Puerto_Rico" title="Torneo de Copa de Puerto Rico">Torneo de Copa de Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Copa featured a mix of club and national teams and was hosted by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_Football_Federation" title="Puerto Rican Football Federation">Puerto Rican Football Federation</a> and the Puerto Rico national team.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The international competition was played over the course of a week\'s time, consisting of three nights of double-headers. The <a href="/wiki/MLS" class="mw-redirect" title="MLS">MLS</a> side, <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Mutiny" title="Tampa Bay Mutiny">Tampa Bay Mutiny</a> were the winners.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2011 the stadium was repaired and improved to accommodate the new tenant football team <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_River_Plate_Puerto_Rico" title="Club Atl\xc3\xa9tico River Plate Puerto Rico">River Plate Puerto Rico</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> but the club never played there.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading
