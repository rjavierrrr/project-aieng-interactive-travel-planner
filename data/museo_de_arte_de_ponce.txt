The museum contains one of the most important <a href="/wiki/Pre-Raphaelite_Brotherhood" title="Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood">Pre-Raphaelite</a> collections in the <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">Western Hemisphere</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> holding some 4,500 pieces of art<sup id="cite_ref-Museo_Caramelo_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Museo_Caramelo-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-museoarteponce.org_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-museoarteponce.org-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> distributed among fourteen <a href="/wiki/Art_gallery" title="Art gallery">galleries</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Museo de Arte de Ponce is the finest art museum in Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The largest art museum in the Caribbean,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> it has also been called one of the best in the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PR_Herald_Ferre_death_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Herald_Ferre_death-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LAT_Statehood_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LAT_Statehood-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  It was the first museum in Puerto Rico accredited by the <a href="/wiki/American_Alliance_of_Museums" title="American Alliance of Museums">American Alliance of Museums</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-museoarteponce.org_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-museoarteponce.org-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>It was founded in 1959 by <a href="/wiki/Business_magnate" title="Business magnate">industrialist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philanthropy" title="Philanthropy">philanthropist</a> <a href="/wiki/Luis_A._Ferr%C3%A9" title="Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9">Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9</a> at a location in the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce Historic Zone</a>. The museum moved to its current building location on <a href="/wiki/Avenida_Las_Am%C3%A9ricas" title="Avenida Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas">Avenida Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas</a> in 1965. In 2010, the museum increased its size significantly after a $30M expansion.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_Arte_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Museo_de_Arte_de_Ponce,_Barrio_Canas_Urbano,_Ponce,_Puerto_Rico,_mirando_hacia_el_norte.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Museo_de_Arte_de_Ponce%2C_Barrio_Canas_Urbano%2C_Ponce%2C_Puerto_Rico%2C_mirando_hacia_el_norte.jpg/220px-Museo_de_Arte_de_Ponce%2C_Barrio_Canas_Urbano%2C_Ponce%2C_Puerto_Rico%2C_mirando_hacia_el_norte.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="144" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/38/Museo_de_Arte_de_Ponce%2C_Barrio_Canas_Urbano%2C_Ponce%2C_Puerto_Rico%2C_mirando_hacia_el_norte.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="305" data-file-height="199" /></a><figcaption>Main hall of the museum</figcaption></figure>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389"><table class="infobox" style="clear: left; float:left;margin:0 1.5em 1.5em 0;"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="font-size:115%">External audio</th></tr><tr><td colspan="2" class="infobox-full-data" style="text-align: left"><span typeof="mw:File"><span><img alt="audio icon" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Nuvola_apps_arts.svg/16px-Nuvola_apps_arts.svg.png" decoding="async" width="16" height="16" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Nuvola_apps_arts.svg/24px-Nuvola_apps_arts.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Nuvola_apps_arts.svg/32px-Nuvola_apps_arts.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="128" data-file-height="128" /></span></span> You may see a video of Museo de Arte de Ponce <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Vw5h7ci5NVE"><b>HERE</b></a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The project of the museum began in 1956 when <a href="/wiki/Luis_A._Ferr%C3%A9" title="Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9">Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9</a> traveled to <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and acquired various European art pieces, including many <a href="/wiki/Pre-Raphaelite_Brotherhood" title="Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood">Pre-Raphaelite works</a>, which encouraged him to start a project for a museum in the city of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>, his birthplace. With the advice of two experts \xe2\x80\x93 Julius S. Held, specialist on <a href="/wiki/Rubens" class="mw-redirect" title="Rubens">Rubens</a> and professor of <a href="/wiki/Art_History" class="mw-redirect" title="Art History">Art History</a> at <a href="/wiki/Barnard_College" title="Barnard College">Barnard College</a> and <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a>, and Ren\xc3\xa9 Taylor, art and architecture enthusiast and professor at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Granada" title="University of Granada">University of Granada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yale" class="mw-redirect" title="Yale">Yale</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia</a> \xe2\x80\x93 Ferr\xc3\xa9 compiled a collection of works of art based on their value instead of their popularity.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Ferr\xc3\xa9 would state in a <i>Forbes</i> interview that "the scholars and critics all called it <a href="/wiki/Kitsch" title="Kitsch">kitsch</a>, everyone thought I was crazy to buy them."<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_Ferre_death_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_Ferre_death-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On 3 January 1959, Ferr\xc3\xa9 opened the museum at a house at 70 Cristina Street in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a> at what is today the <a href="/wiki/Centro_Cultural_de_Ponce" class="mw-redirect" title="Centro Cultural de Ponce">Centro Cultural de Ponce</a>, with an exhibit of 72 works of art.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Herald_Ferre_death_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Herald_Ferre_death-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Some of these original paintings continue to be on display today. As time passed and the museum gained popularity, additional works of art were added to the collections. In 1962, the <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Henry_Kress" title="Samuel Henry Kress">Samuel H. Kress Foundation</a> donated 15 paintings to the museum.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Herald_Ferre_death_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Herald_Ferre_death-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> By 1989, the museum\'s collection had grown to 500 pieces, with a then estimated value of $50 million,<sup id="cite_ref-LAT_Statehood_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LAT_Statehood-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> equivalent to $123&#160;million in present-day terms.\n</p><p>Fearing a fire in the Cristina Street building would destroy its collections, Ferr\xc3\xa9 acquired a tract of land on Las Americas Avenue in Ponce to build the current museum, and recruited <a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">architect</a> <a href="/wiki/Edward_Durell_Stone" title="Edward Durell Stone">Edward Durell Stone</a> for its design.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Herald_Ferre_death_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Herald_Ferre_death-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> On 23 April 1964 the first stone was placed and the construction of the museum began. It was finished in 1965 and officially opened on 28 December 1965. One of the main features of the museum is its <a href="/wiki/Hexagonal" class="mw-redirect" title="Hexagonal">hexagonal</a> galleries, which allow natural light to enter through its corners bringing an illumination to exhibitions. The museum contained a total of 14 galleries, two <a href="/wiki/Gardens" class="mw-redirect" title="Gardens">gardens</a>, and an <a href="/wiki/Amphitheater" class="mw-redirect" title="Amphitheater">amphitheater</a>, and its main entrance with bifurcated ladders.\n</p><p><i>Pinceladas en Vuelo</i> ("Brushstokes in Flight"), a 28-foot-high aluminum structure created in 1984 by New York sculptor <a href="/wiki/Roy_Lichtenstein" title="Roy Lichtenstein">Roy Lichtenstein</a> sits on the front yard of the museum, which was then considered "the largest public [display art] work in Latin America and the Caribbean."<sup id="cite_ref-Museo_Caramelo_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Museo_Caramelo-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The museum was closed from 2008 to 2010 while undergoing renovations, re-opening in time for the celebration of its 50th anniversary on 13 November 2010, after a $30 million in improvements.<sup id="cite_ref-Museo_Caramelo_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Museo_Caramelo-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In the meantime, the museum held exhibitions in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Am%C3%A9ricas_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Plaza Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas (Puerto Rico)">Plaza Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas</a>, and loaned some of its best pieces out to traveling exhibitions at fine arts institutions around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The construction work was both a renovation of the existing structure as well as an expansion, increasing the size of the museum by more than 40%.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The $20-turn-$30 million renovations also included a new building to house a historic archive and a library.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The new 37,745-square-foot (3,506.6&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) annex to the museum\'s main building houses an educational space, a library specializing in art history, the Don Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9 Archives, a laboratory for the conservation of artworks, an artwork storage area, a museum shop, a restaurant, and administrative offices.<sup id="cite_ref-museoarteponce.org_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-museoarteponce.org-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> After the expansion, the total square footage of the museum came to 77,745 square feet.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The expansion intended to provide facilities for educational purposes and an art history library as well as to provide room for a museum shop and a restaurant for guests.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Thoroughfare" title="Thoroughfare">thoroughfare</a> on which the museum is located, a major <a href="/wiki/Arterial_road" title="Arterial road">road artery</a> in Ponce, was renamed the "<a href="/wiki/Luis_A._Ferr%C3%A9" title="Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9">Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9</a> Boulevard" in honor of the founder of the museum.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Ferre_Bulevar_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferre_Bulevar-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2021, the museum was named as a recipient for the <a href="/wiki/National_Medal_for_Museum_and_Library_Service" title="National Medal for Museum and Library Service">National Medal for Museum and Library Service</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Artworks">Artworks</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_Arte_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Artworks"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Herald_Ferre_death-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The <i><a href="/wiki/Financial_Times" title="Financial Times">Financial Times</a></i> of London states that the museum holds "one of the most distinguished private collections in the Western Hemisphere outside the United States."<sup id="cite_ref-LAT_Statehood_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LAT_Statehood-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It has  an important collection of almost 4,000 pieces of art that range from the 14th to the 20th century, Italian <a href="/wiki/Baroque" title="Baroque">Baroque</a>, British <a href="/wiki/Pre-Raphaelite" class="mw-redirect" title="Pre-Raphaelite">Pre-Raphaelite</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Spanish <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Golden_Age" title="Spanish Golden Age">Golden Age</a> and contemporary <a href="/wiki/Latin_American_art" title="Latin American art">Latin-American</a> art.\n</p><p>Some of the artists whose paintings and works are exhibited at the museum are <a href="/wiki/Peter_Paul_Rubens" title="Peter Paul Rubens">Peter Paul Rubens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lucas_Cranach_the_Elder" title="Lucas Cranach the Elder">Lucas Cranach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bartolom%C3%A9_Esteban_Murillo" title="Bartolom\xc3\xa9 Esteban Murillo">Bartolom\xc3\xa9 Esteban Murillo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eug%C3%A8ne_Delacroix" title="Eug\xc3\xa8ne Delacroix">Eug\xc3\xa8ne Delacroix</a>, and Sir <a href="/wiki/Edward_Burne-Jones" title="Edward Burne-Jones">Edward Burne-Jones</a>, among others. The main masterpiece of the museum is <i><a href="/wiki/Flaming_June" title="Flaming June">Flaming June</a></i>, painted by <a href="/wiki/Frederic_Leighton" title="Frederic Leighton">Frederic Leighton</a>.  Ferr\xc3\xa9 bought this piece for less than $1,000 in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a> in 1960,<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and it was his favorite.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Last_Sleep_of_Arthur_in_Avalon" title="The Last Sleep of Arthur in Avalon">The Last Sleep of Arthur in Avalon</a></i>, the final masterpiece and crowning achievement of Sir <a href="/wiki/Edward_Burne-Jones" title="Edward Burne-Jones">Edward Burne-Jones</a>, is another of the main pieces of the museum\'s collection, originally acquired by Ferr\xc3\xa9 for just 1,600 <a href="/wiki/Guinea_(British_coin)" class="mw-redirect" title="Guinea (British coin)">British guineas</a> in 1963.<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The enormous painting was started in 1881 and left unfinished at the artist\'s death in 1898. In 2009, both <i>Flaming June</i> and <i>The Last Sleep of Arthur in Avalon</i> were loaned to <a href="/wiki/Tate_Britain" title="Tate Britain">Tate Britain</a> while the museum underwent a two-year refurbishment.<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Other paintings were loaned to the <a href="/wiki/Museo_del_Prado" title="Museo del Prado">Museo del Prado</a> in Madrid.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>"Equally important is the Puerto Rican art collection, which ranges from the 18th century to the present day and includes great masters such as <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Campeche" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Campeche">Jos\xc3\xa9 Campeche</a>, <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Oller" title="Francisco Oller">Francisco Oller</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Pou" title="Miguel Pou">Miguel Pou</a>, as well as the best contemporary talent such as <a href="/wiki/Myrna_B%C3%A1ez" title="Myrna B\xc3\xa1ez">Myrna B\xc3\xa1ez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Rod%C3%B3n" title="Francisco Rod\xc3\xb3n">Francisco Rod\xc3\xb3n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Martorell" title="Antonio Martorell">Antonio Martorell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arnaldo_Roche_Rabell" title="Arnaldo Roche Rabell">Arnaldo Roche Rabell</a>, among others."<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>40<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The museum also displays works by international artists. In March 2006, the museum exhibited the work of Mexican artist <a href="/wiki/Frida_Kahlo" title="Frida Kahlo">Frida Kahlo</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The Museum charges a small admission fee to the public, but most revenues come from substantial donations made by Puerto Rican individuals and businesses. Some have made single donations for the sole purpose of acquiring art to be exhibited in the museum, while others donate for the maintenance and operational expenditures of the museum.
