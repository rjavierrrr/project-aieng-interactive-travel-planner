The structure is considered a historic landmark and a <a href="/wiki/National_icon" class="mw-redirect" title="National icon">national icon</a> in the city of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a> and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The property is currently owned by Misla Hospitality Group, a family of local Ponce investors who bought it from CBC Development. Its architecture is <a href="/wiki/Modern_architecture" title="Modern architecture">classical modern</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> When it opened, in 1960, it became the first <a href="/wiki/Modern_architecture" title="Modern architecture">modern</a> hotel in the city.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location_and_features">Location and features</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel_Ponce_Intercontinental&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location and features"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The hotel is located in the northern section of the city of Ponce, on a hill just north of the <i>El Vigia Hill</i>, behind <a href="/wiki/Cruceta_del_Vig%C3%ADa" title="Cruceta del Vig\xc3\xada">Cruceta del Vig\xc3\xada</a> and <a href="/wiki/Castillo_Serralles" class="mw-redirect" title="Castillo Serralles">Castillo Serralles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The hotel had a large circular outdoor swimming pool, a <a href="/wiki/Ballroom" title="Ballroom">ballroom</a> named <i>Sal\xc3\xb3n Ponciana</i>, a cocktail bar named <i>Bar Coqu\xc3\xad</i>; and a restaurant called <i>El Cafetal</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The hotel also had a casino that operated until 1973.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> On the top of the hotel, <a href="/wiki/WSTE-DT#Early_History,_WRIK-TV_(1958\xe2\x80\x931969)" title="WSTE-DT">WRIK-TV</a> had its transmission antenna.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The hotel is on a lot measuring approximately 25 <i><a href="/wiki/Cuerda" title="Cuerda">cuerdas</a></i> (roughly 25 acres).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The land area is 22.5 <a href="/wiki/Cuerda" title="Cuerda">cuerdas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Design">Design</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel_Ponce_Intercontinental&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Collectie_Nationaal_Museum_van_Wereldculturen_TM-20016605_Hotel_El_Ponce_Puerto_Rico_Boy_Lawson_%28Fotograaf%29.jpg/440px-Collectie_Nationaal_Museum_van_Wereldculturen_TM-20016605_Hotel_El_Ponce_Puerto_Rico_Boy_Lawson_%28Fotograaf%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1915" data-file-height="1752" /></a><figcaption>One of the hotel\'s fa\xc3\xa7ades in 1964</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The hotel was designed in 1957-58 by <a href="/wiki/American_architecture" class="mw-redirect" title="American architecture">American architect</a> <a href="/wiki/William_B._Tabler" title="William B. Tabler">William B. Tabler</a>, <a href="/wiki/FAIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FAIA">FAIA</a>.  Tabler, whose offices were in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, designed hotels worldwide for the Statler chain, Hilton, and Intercontinental.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Tabler designed the Ponce Intercontinental in a modern style with ample space for cross ventilation and light, interior details, and quasi-futuristic traits. The design takes advantage of the location of the building for natural ventilation and exposure to large and spacious panoramic views of the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> from the top sector of the El Vigia Hill in the municipality of Ponce, Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The architectural design of this hotel is simple but consistent with a curvilinear theme which is unique in Puerto Rico. The concrete shells that housed the restaurant, and activities rooms are geared to take advantage of large open spaces with majestic views of the Caribbean Sea.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The use of <a href="/wiki/Ornament_(architecture)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ornament (architecture)">ornamental</a> roofs is typical of modern architecture of the mid-1950s era.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel_Ponce_Intercontinental&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Abandoned_Intercontinental_Hotel,_Ponce.JPG" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Abandoned_Intercontinental_Hotel%2C_Ponce.JPG/200px-Abandoned_Intercontinental_Hotel%2C_Ponce.JPG" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Abandoned_Intercontinental_Hotel%2C_Ponce.JPG/400px-Abandoned_Intercontinental_Hotel%2C_Ponce.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2304" data-file-height="1728" /></a><figcaption>2007 photo of the front facade of the derelict structure</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The first stone of the Hotel was placed on January 6, 1958, under the project name \xe2\x80\x9cHotel El Ponce\xc3\xb1o\xe2\x80\x9d,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and the Hotel opened as a luxury hotel on 1 February 1960.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The cost of the construction of the hotel was $3.75 million USD ($40 million today).<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Hotel operated for 15 years (from 1960 to 1975) and was of particular importance in the collective memory of the Ponce\'s popular society in the 1960s through the 1970s.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The hotel was a bustling center of entertainment that stood out as a center of large musical events of those years. The Ponce Intercontinental is remembered for being the birthplace of great artistic, social and political events of its time.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Celebrities, such as <a href="/wiki/Chucho_Avellanet" title="Chucho Avellanet">Chucho Avellanet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iris_Chacon" class="mw-redirect" title="Iris Chacon">Iris Chacon</a>, <a href="/wiki/El_Gran_Combo_de_Puerto_Rico" title="El Gran Combo de Puerto Rico">El Gran Combo de Puerto Rico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Celia_Cruz" title="Celia Cruz">Celia Cruz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marco_Antonio_Mu%C3%B1iz" title="Marco Antonio Mu\xc3\xb1iz">Marco Antonio Mu\xc3\xb1iz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Jos%C3%A9" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Jos\xc3\xa9">Jos\xc3\xa9 Jos\xc3\xa9</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sandro_de_America" class="mw-redirect" title="Sandro de America">Sandro de America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Camilo_Sesto" title="Camilo Sesto">Camilo Sesto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raphael_(singer)" title="Raphael (singer)">Raphael</a>, and others, stayed or performed at the hotel.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Closing">Closing</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel_Ponce_Intercontinental&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Closing"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The hotel\'s definitive closure was first announced on April 21, 1975, slated for May 30, due to the hotel\'s profound economical crisis as losses were accumulating over $1.7 millions ($10 millions today).<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The hotel\'s management were disposed to accept offers to avoid the hotel\'s closure, requiring $1 million ($6 millions today) investment.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> A group of local Ponce investors were interested in leasing the hotel to continue operating, once <a href="/wiki/InterContinental" title="InterContinental">Inter\xc2\xb7Continental Hotels</a> ceases operations.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n</p><p>However, the interested group didn\'t have the necessary economical resources to continue operating the hotel, and the hotel was finally closed on May 31, 1975 at 11 pm, a day after the anticipated date, losing over 145 jobs. 7 guests were staying at the hotel on the last day of operations.<sup id="cite_ref-closing_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-closing-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> After the hotel\'s closure, Inter\xc2\xb7Continental Hotels continued in charge of the building\'s maintenance until 1980.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The hotel\'s location, high costs, labor conflicts, and the lack of appropriated road access were considered factors for the hotel\'s economical problems that caused the closure.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The hotel\'s only access road was through a narrow one-way, one-lane <a href="/wiki/Alley" title="Alley">alley</a> in a financially deprived neighborhood north of the city. The lack of touristic promotion outside <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Metropolitan_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan Metropolitan Area">San Juan Metropolitan Area</a> and the hotel\'s bad administration were also blamed for the hotel\'s failure.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Re-construction">Re-construction</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hotel_Ponce_Intercontinental&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Re-construction"><span>edit</span></a><span href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_G._Tormos_Vega" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 G. Tormos Vega">Jos\xc3\xa9 G. Tormos Vega</a> announced that the hotel would reopen under new owners for the <a href="/wiki/1979_Pan_American_Games" title="1979 Pan American Games">1979 Pan American Games</a>, which was held in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>. However, the hotel didn\xe2\x80\x99t reopen and in 1981, a group of Puerto Rican and American investors were reportedly looking into purchasing the property. No other developments were subsequently reported.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1985, and under Government of Puerto Rico guidelines, Puerto Rico\'s <i><a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Industrial_Development_Company" title="Puerto Rico Industrial Development Company">Compa\xc3\xb1ia de Fomento Industrial</a></i> (<i>"PRIDCO"</i>) decided to buy the structure on 7 November 1985, with the goal of remodeling it, expanding its facilities, and providing the city of Ponce with world-class accommodations. PRIDCO commissioned the architectural firm of Pablo Quinones &amp; Associates to initiate investigations and studies on the condition of the main structure with the intention of upgrading it to modern codes. The design team generated its recommendations, which were delivered to the PRIDCO. Simultaneously, the team traveled to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> in 1985-86, where it located the original plans at the offices of <a href="/wiki/William_B._Tabler" title="William B. Tabler">William B. Tabler</a>. As a design strategy for its architectural features, they decided to remodel the essence of its main structure and proposed an enlargement to accommodate a modern <a href="/wiki/Convention_center" title="Convention center">convention center</a> within the premises.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On 7 October 1985, as a result of the landslide in <a href="/wiki/Mameyes" class="mw-redirect" title="Mameyes">Mameyes</a>, the hotel was used as temporary accommodation for people affected by the floods. As the time of the relocation of the affected tenants became longer than anticipated, plans to enlarge and re-model the hotel were abandoned.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1999, George Philip Rivera, a local businessman and entrepreneur, became interested in buying and in remodeling property, and have it operate as El Vig\xc3\xada Hotel &amp; Casino. He envisioned the construction of villas, a multilevel parking garage and banquet facilities.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>40<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> However, by 2001 the sale had fallen through and PRIDCO put the property for sale again. CBC Development won the auction for the property and planned to demolish the structure and build a 365-unit walk-up apartment complex.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2006, PRIDCO sold the property to CBC Development, however, CBC changed the plans. It then planned to remodel the hotel, build an 80-room condo hotel, a 15,200&#160;ft. square convention center, and 80 villas. The project was named Vista Magna Hotel &amp; Resort. Two years later, however, in 2008, due to the estructural damage deemed too extensive, CBC decided it would instead demolish the structure and build a new hotel having some elements of the nostalgic original building.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>42<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In any event, neither repairs nor demolition took place and, in 2012, CBC Development along with the Integrated Development Commission of the South Region from the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Representatives_of_Puerto_Rico" title="House of Representatives of Puerto Rico">House of Representatives of Puerto Rico</a> began considering the possibility to rehabilitate the structure to operate as a senior citizen housing complex. The plan called for 126 senior housing units.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>By 2016, however, a new player surfaced. Juan Jose Acosta won approval to convert the building into a housing complex for homeless youth members of the LGBT community.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>44<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2018, Grupo Misla Villalba, owners of <a href="/wiki/Hotel_Meli%C3%A1" title="Hotel Meli\xc3\xa1">Hotel Meli\xc3\xa1</a>, bought the property from CBC with plans to rehabilitate the structure as a hotel.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>45<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On 28 July 2020, Misla Hospitality Group announced that the structure will be rehabilitated to convert it into the Puerto Rico\'s first <a href="/wiki/Tribute_Portfolio" class="mw-redirect" title="Tribute Portfolio">Tribute Portfolio</a> by <a href="/wiki/Marriott_International" title="Marriott International">Marriott International</a>. The hotel was preliminarily expected to open in March 2022 after an investment of $20 million. The new hotel will feature a casino, spa, night club, swimming pools, and three restaurants. It would be the second under the Marriott flag in Ponce, after the <a href="/wiki/Aloft_Hotels" title="Aloft Hotels">Aloft</a> hotel located on the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Bypass" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce Bypass">Ponce Bypass</a> and <a href="/wiki/PR-12" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-12">PR-12</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>46<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="See_also">See also</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
