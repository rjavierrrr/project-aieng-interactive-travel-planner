Rico">Ponce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The cathedral lies in the middle of Ponce\'s <a href="/wiki/Town_square" title="Town square">town square</a>, known as <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Delicias" title="Plaza Las Delicias">Plaza Las Delicias</a>, located at the center of the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce Historic Zone</a>. For its historic significance, the cathedral was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1984.<sup id="cite_ref-nris_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is the seat of the Bishop of Ponce, currently <a href="/wiki/Rub%C3%A9n_Gonz%C3%A1lez_Medina" title="Rub\xc3\xa9n Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Medina">Rub\xc3\xa9n Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Medina</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-daily_sun_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-daily_sun-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The cathedral has a history that dates to 1670. It has been damaged several times by fires and earthquakes. It stands out among Puerto Rico\'s other four cathedrals for its intricate design. It has a large pipe organ that was played by <a href="/wiki/Danza" title="Danza">danza</a> master and composer <a href="/wiki/Juan_Morel_Campos" title="Juan Morel Campos">Juan Morel Campos</a>. Architecturally, it is designed in the neoclassical style. Structurally, it follows a cruciform plan, with a large dome at the <a href="/wiki/Crossing_(architecture)" title="Crossing (architecture)">crossing</a>. The interior consists of a main nave and two large aisles separated by a series of eight arcades. There are two small chapels in its interior.  Two three-story square towers decorate the front facade.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catedral_de_Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_Guadalupe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The cathedral has over 300 years of history. In 1670, a small <a href="/wiki/Chapel" title="Chapel">chapel</a> was built in the center of the colonial settlement of Ponce on the site of the present cathedral.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> On 17 September 1692, the King of Spain, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Carlos II">Carlos II</a>, issued a <i>C\xc3\xa9dula Real</i> (Royal Permit) designating the chapel as a <a href="/wiki/Parish_church" title="Parish church">parish church</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1835, the original parish church was demolished to build a larger church, which was completed and opened for worship in 1839.<sup id="cite_ref-nris_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Among its new features were two octagonal towers on the corners of the front <a href="/wiki/Facade" class="mw-redirect" title="Facade">facade</a>.  The original (pre-1918) towers were also three stories high and had windows on each of the sides. They were each topped with a Christian cross.\n</p><p>The cathedral contains a number of burials within its interior.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was among the first burial places in Ponce. However, in 1814 the practice was discontinued due to potential health risks on church worshipers, and based on the edict promulgated in 1784 by <a href="/wiki/Charles_III_of_Spain" title="Charles III of Spain">King Charles III</a>. On that year the first cemetery in the city located outside a religious structure was built at the block bounded by today\'s Calle Victoria, Union, Vives and Mendez Vigo. That block is now (2019) occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Terminal_de_Transportaci%C3%B3n_P%C3%BAblica_Carlos_Garay" title="Terminal de Transportaci\xc3\xb3n P\xc3\xbablica Carlos Garay">Terminal de Transportaci\xc3\xb3n P\xc3\xbablica Carlos Garay</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span data-file-height="652" /></a><figcaption>The cathedral, circa 1910</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The building was damaged by several fires and <a href="/wiki/Earthquake" title="Earthquake">earthquakes</a> during the 19th and 20th centuries including the <a href="/wiki/1918_San_Ferm%C3%ADn_earthquake" title="1918 San Ferm\xc3\xadn earthquake">1918 San Ferm\xc3\xadn earthquake</a>, which caused extensive The cathedral\'s first <a href="/wiki/Bishop" title="Bishop">bishop</a> was <a href="/wiki/Edwin_Byrne" title="Edwin Byrne">Edwin Byrne</a>, who took charge in 1925.<sup id="cite_ref-PRE_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PRE-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>From 1931 to 1937, the church was reconstructed to a design by <a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">architect</a> <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Porrata_Doria" class="mw-redirect" title="Francisco Porrata Doria">Francisco Porrata Doria</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The current facade bears a French <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">neoclassical</a> style. Among these improvements were the addition of two new chapels, a new roof, the remodelling of the upper storey of the facade and the construction of two new, richly decorated square towers. A <a href="/wiki/Pipe_organ" title="Pipe organ">pipe organ</a> was installed in 1934 and re-inaugurated in 1989 after a nine-year restoration.<sup id="cite_ref-travel_ponce_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travel_ponce-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The building was <a href="/wiki/Dedication_of_churches" title="Dedication of churches">dedicated</a> by <a href="/wiki/James_Edward_McManus" title="James Edward McManus">Bishop James E. McManus</a> on 11 April 1950.<sup id="cite_ref-arteagainteractivo1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-arteagainteractivo1-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div style="clear:left;" class=""></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catedral_de_Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_Guadalupe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Plan_of_Plaza_Las_Delicias_in_Ponce,_Puerto_Rico.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Plan_of_Plaza_Las_Delicias_in_Ponce%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg/220px-Plan_of_Plaza_Las_Delicias_in_Ponce%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="365" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Plan_of_Plaza_Las_Delicias_in_Ponce%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg/330px-Plan_of_Plaza_Las_Delicias_in_Ponce%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Plan_of_Plaza_Las_Delicias_in_Ponce%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg/440px-Plan_of_Plaza_Las_Delicias_in_Ponce%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg 2x" data-file-width="588" data-file-height="976" /></a><figcaption><b>Diagram of Plaza Las Delicias</b> showing the central location of the cathedral within the Plaza</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The cathedral sits in the center of Ponce\'s central square, <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Delicias" title="Plaza Las Delicias">Plaza Las Delicias</a>, oriented east-to-west, with its front entrance facing west. Immediately behind the cathedral, to the east, is the historic <a href="/wiki/Fire_house" class="mw-redirect" title="Fire house">fire house</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parque_de_Bombas" title="Parque de Bombas">Parque de Bombas</a>. The two buildings effectively divide Plaza Las Delicias into two smaller plazas, named <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Mu%C3%B1oz_Rivera" title="Plaza Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera">Plaza Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera</a> and <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Degetau" title="Plaza Degetau">Plaza Degetau</a>. Low undulating walls line the cathedral\'s northern and southern sides, and serve to separate it from the Plazas.<sup id="cite_ref-nris_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Plaza Las Delicias is in <a href="/wiki/Barrio" title="Barrio">barrio</a> <a href="/wiki/Segundo_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Segundo (Ponce)">Segundo</a>, one of the six core barrios of Ponce that compose Historic Zone designated by the Government of Puerto Rico to preserve the colonial character of the city.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="The_building">The building</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catedral_de_Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_Guadalupe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The building"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The building\'s exterior architecture is <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">Neoclassical</a>, while its interior is mostly <a href="/wiki/American_colonial_architecture" title="American colonial architecture">colonial</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gothic_architecture" title="Gothic architecture">Gothic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-arteagainteractivo1_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-arteagainteractivo1-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The current structure is larger than the 1839 structure, having been extended on the south side in 1911 with the addition of the side chapel. It covers an area of 3,540 square yards (2,960&#160;m<sup>2</sup>).<sup id="cite_ref-nris_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The architecture of the cathedral is <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">neoclassical</a> in style with the exterior painted pale blue and gray and the interior in blue and beige.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The building has many stained glass windows depicting religious scenes and two bell towers.<sup id="cite_ref-Municipio_de_Ponce_17-0" class="reference"><a class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The footprint of the cathedral is of a <a href="/wiki/Cruciform" title="Cruciform">cruciform</a> plan. It features a large dome at the crossing of the <a href="/wiki/Transept" title="Transept">transept</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Nave" title="Nave">nave</a>. The interior consists of eight bays plus the <a href="/wiki/Apse" title="Apse">apse</a>.  The westernmost bay accommodates the <a href="/wiki/Vestibule_(architecture)" title="Vestibule (architecture)">vestibule</a>. Above this vestibule is a second level <a href="/wiki/Choir_loft" class="mw-redirect" title="Choir loft">choir loft</a>, accessible via a wooden stairway connected to the front wall. A large pipe organ, said to have been played by <a href="/wiki/Juan_Morel_Campos" title="Juan Morel Campos">Juan Morel Campos</a> every Sunday for many years, is also on this second level loft.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The cathedral measures 42.2m long by 19.4m wide.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Arcade_(architecture)" title="Arcade (architecture)">Arcades</a> of semi-circular arches divide the nave from the <a href="/wiki/Aisle" title="Aisle">aisles</a>. On the south side of the cathedral, a large chapel, built in 1911, extends three bays parallel to the aisle. This chapel provides a more intimate place of worship than the main church or its aisles. The nave has a <a href="/wiki/Cross_vault" class="mw-redirect" title="Cross vault">cross vaulted</a> ceiling with <a href="/wiki/Clerestory" title="Clerestory">clerestory</a> windows above the arcades. id="The_front_facade">The front facade</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catedral_de_Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_Guadalupe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The front facade"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The western front facade is flanked by two square towers. They are both three stories high topped with silver domes and a <a href="/wiki/Christian_cross" title="Christian cross">Christian cross</a> on top of these. Both towers have windows at each level. Access to the towers is through either of two small wooden doors located in the interior northwest and southwest corners of the church vestibule.<sup id="cite_ref-nris_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Entry to the cathedral is through a tall pair of wooden doors in a style reminiscent of church doors of <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> Europe. There are two round columns to each side of these doors, as well as rectangular wooden windows to both sides.  Atop the second level of the front facade there is a large triangular <a href="/wiki/Pediment" title="Pediment">pediment</a>, and on top of this pediment sits a Christian cross considerably larger than the two atop the tower domes. There are also two statues standing at each of the two lower ends of the large triangular pediment. Except for the wooden windows and doors, all elements on the front facade are of masonry construction.<sup id="cite_ref-nris_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div style="clear:left;" class=""></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Events">Events</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catedral_de_Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_Guadalupe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Events"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>On 12 December, the cathedral hosts the annual event known as "<a href="/wiki/Las_Ma%C3%B1anitas_(celebration)" title="Las Ma\xc3\xb1anitas (celebration)">Las Ma\xc3\xb1anitas</a>", named after the <a href="/wiki/Las_Ma%C3%B1anitas" title="Las Ma\xc3\xb1anitas">Mexican birthday song</a> (English: "Early Mornings"), which is sung during a pre-dawn religious procession in the downtown area to celebrate the <a href="/wiki/General_Roman_Calendar#December_(General_Calendar)" title="General Roman Calendar">day</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_of_Guadalupe" class="mw-redirect" title="Virgin of Guadalupe">Virgin of Guadalupe</a>, the patron saint of Ponce. The event started in 1965.<sup id="cite_ref-travel_ponce_2_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travel_ponce_2-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> After the procession candle bearers attend <a href="/wiki/Holy_Mass" class="mw-redirect" title="Holy Mass">Holy Mass</a> at the Cathedral at 5:00&#160;am. Up to 10,000 people attend the event.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  On 24 December there is also a midnight Mass known as <i>Misa de Gallo</i> (English: Rooster\'s Mass).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
