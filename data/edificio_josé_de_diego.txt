<b>Edificio Jos\xc3\xa9 de Diego</b> (English: <span lang="en">Jos\xc3\xa9 de Diego Building</span>) in <a href="/wiki/Mayag%C3%BCez,_Puerto_Rico" title="Mayag\xc3\xbcez, Puerto Rico">Mayag\xc3\xbcez, Puerto Rico</a>, also known as the <b>Rector\xc3\xada</b>, is a historic building on the grounds of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Mayag%C3%BCez" title="University of Puerto Rico at Mayag\xc3\xbcez">University of Puerto Rico at Mayag\xc3\xbcez</a>. It was built in 1913. It was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1977<sup id="cite_ref-FRto78_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FRto78-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and on the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Register_of_Historic_Sites_and_Zones" title="Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones">Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones</a> in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edificio_Jos%C3%A9_de_Diego&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Its construction began in 1913 and concluded in 1916 as the Science Building.  It was slightly affected by the <a href="/wiki/1918_San_Ferm%C3%ADn_earthquake" title="1918 San Ferm\xc3\xadn earthquake">1918 San Ferm\xc3\xadn earthquake</a>, it housed the administrative offices, the library and nine class rooms.  As a result of a 1921 remodeling its new tower was added where later the clock was placed. The carillon was donated by Association and Foundation Alumni Colegial, Inc.  Since then the rectory building is named the <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_de_Diego" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 de Diego">Jos\xc3\xa9 de Diego</a> building in honor of the campus\'s founder.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Architecture">Architecture</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edificio_Jos%C3%A9_de_Diego&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Architecture"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Although its constructive aesthetics presents a great eclecticism, is important to indicate the entrance of the great facade.<sup id="cite_ref-Alvarez_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alvarez-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Follow the steps of the central staircase regular service, stand two columns - "d\xc3\xadstilo" smooth shaft and reduced \xc3\xa9ntasis, with its pedestal, \xe2\x80\x9cbasa\xe2\x80\x9d, toscano-romano capital marked \xe2\x80\x9castragalo\xe2\x80\x9d.<sup id="cite_ref-Alvarez_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alvarez-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  This is the most estimable attention to aesthetic impact section.  On the two abacus terminals holds a short frieze with the inscription in English the name of the school. class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The construction is of two floors and irregular <a href="/wiki/Masonry" title="Masonry">masonry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Alvarez_4-4" central rectangular.<sup id="cite_ref-Alvarez_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alvarez-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  In the middle section rises the Tower surrounded by <a href="/wiki/Balustrades" class="mw-redirect" title="Balustrades">balustrades</a> and four campaniformes floreados pinnacles below of the cantilever roof supported by plates; inside lies the "Carillon" service, with external exits to their respective clocks.<sup id="cite_ref-Alvarez_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alvarez-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The turret is covered with a coverage to four aspects; its Center highlights a "chapitelillo" tip.<sup
