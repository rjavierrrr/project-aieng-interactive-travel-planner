class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></td></tr><tr style="display:none"><th colspan="2">\n</th></tr><tr style="display:none"><th colspan="2">\n</th></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Castillo San Crist\xc3\xb3bal</b> (<a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>: <i>Saint Christopher Castle</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Medieval_fortification" title="Medieval fortification">fortress</a> in the historic district of <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">Old San Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, known as the largest <a href="/wiki/Medieval_fortification" title="Medieval fortification">fortification</a> built by the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish</a> in the <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Dating back to <a href="/wiki/Medieval_fortification" title="Medieval fortification">defense</a> expansions following attacks by the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_San_Juan_(1598)" title="Battle of San Juan (1598)">English in 1598</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_San_Juan_(1625)" title="Battle of San Juan (1625)">Dutch in 1625</a>, it was first built in 1634 as the <i>Garita del Diablo</i> ("devil\xe2\x80\x99s <a href="/wiki/Bartizan" title="Bartizan">sentry box</a>") and an <a href="/wiki/Artillery_battery" title="Artillery battery">artillery platform</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Over the next 150 years, especially in the reign of <a href="/wiki/Charles_III_of_Spain" title="Charles III of Spain">King Charles III of Spain</a>, it continued to be developed into a <a href="/wiki/Bastion_fort" title="Bastion fort">bastion fort</a> to reach its largest size in 1790.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Rising 150 ft from the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a> shoreline, the three-level edifice stands on a <a href="/wiki/Hill" title="Hill">hill</a> at the northern coastline of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Islet" title="San Juan Islet">San Juan Islet</a>, guarding the land entry to <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">Old San Juan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <i>San Crist\xc3\xb3bal</i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Castillo_San_Felipe_del_Morro" title="Castillo San Felipe del Morro">El Morro</a>, <a href="/wiki/La_Fortaleza" title="La Fortaleza">La Fortaleza</a>, and other forts part of the <a href="/wiki/Walls_of_Old_San_Juan" title="Walls of Old San Juan">Walls of Old San Juan</a>, protected <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_Puerto_Rico#Strategic_geography" title="Geography of Puerto Rico">strategically and militarily</a> important <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, or <i>La Llave de las Indias (The Key to the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_the_Indies" title="Council of the Indies">Indies</a>)</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> from invasion by competing <a href="/wiki/List_of_modern_great_powers#Early_modern_powers" title="List of modern great powers">world powers</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Age_of_Sail" title="Age of Sail">Age of Sail</a>. It was designated a <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a> by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> in 1983.<sup id="cite_ref-nps.gov2_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nps.gov2-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Castillo_San_Crist%C3%B3bal_(San_Juan)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Castillo_de_San_Crist%C3%B3bal_-_22592955479.jpg/400px-Castillo_de_San_Crist%C3%B3bal_-_22592955479.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5184" data-file-height="3456" /></a><figcaption>The flags of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" class="mw-redirect" title="United States of America">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a> fly over <i>San Crist\xc3\xb3bal</i></figcaption></figure>\n<p><i>Castillo San Crist\xc3\xb3bal</i> was built on a hill originally known as the <i>Cerro de la Horca</i> ("gallows Hill") or the <i>Cerro del Quemadero</i> ("burner\xe2\x80\x99s hill"), changed to <i>Cerro de San Crist\xc3\xb3bal</i> in honor of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Christopher" title="Saint Christopher">Saint Christopher</a>, the patron of travelers. The fortress is considered the largest fortification built by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in the <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a>. When it was completed in 1783, it covered about 27 acres. The entrances to it were protected by two <a href="/wiki/Gate" title="Gate">gates</a> and drawbridges in what was called the <i>Revell\xc3\xadn de Santiago</i> (Saint James <a href="/wiki/Ravelin" title="Ravelin">Ravelin</a>) and the <i>Basti\xc3\xb3n de Santiago</i> (Saint James <a href="/wiki/Bastion" title="Bastion">Bastion</a>). After close to a hundred years of relative peace in the area, this section of the fortification (about a third) was demolished in 1897 to help ease the flow of traffic in and out of the <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">walled city</a>.\n</p>\n<figure typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Fortalezas_San_Juan_SJU_06_2019_8149.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Fortalezas_San_Juan_SJU_06_2019_8149.jpg/150px-Fortalezas_San_Juan_SJU_06_2019_8149.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="188" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Fortalezas_San_Juan_SJU_06_2019_8149.jpg/225px-Fortalezas_San_Juan_SJU_06_2019_8149.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Fortalezas_San_Juan_SJU_06_2019_8149.jpg/300px-Fortalezas_San_Juan_SJU_06_2019_8149.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2400" data-file-height="3000" /></a><figcaption><i>Garita del Diablo</i> immediately next to the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a></figcaption></figure>\n<p>In 1634, the first defensive structure had been built in the area, the <i>Fort\xc3\xadn del Espig\xc3\xb3n</i> ("breakwater fort")or the <i>Garita del Diablo (</i>"devil\xe2\x80\x99s sentry box"<i>)</i>. In 1765, San Crist\xc3\xb3bal was conformed by a hornwork surrounded by a dry moat, which gave continuity to the walls of the city, and at the highest point of it, there was a artillery platform, or <i>Caballero de San Miguel</i> (Knight of Saint Michael)<i>,</i> for the emplacement of cannons. <i>San Crist\xc3\xb3bal</i> derives its name from the hill on which the main defenses were built against the <a href="/wiki/Invasion" title="Invasion">invasion</a> of the city by land, although it also defended the north coast. By the time the work was completed, it became, the most extensive <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish</a> fortification in all of the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>.\n</p>\n<figure typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Tunnel_system_-_Fort_San_Crist%C3%B3bal_(5420913529).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Tunnel_system_-_Fort_San_Crist%C3%B3bal_%285420913529%29.jpg/175px-Tunnel_system_-_Fort_San_Crist%C3%B3bal_%285420913529%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="175" height="116" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Tunnel_system_-_Fort_San_Crist%C3%B3bal_%285420913529%29.jpg/263px-Tunnel_system_-_Fort_San_Crist%C3%B3bal_%285420913529%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Tunnel_system_-_Fort_San_Crist%C3%B3bal_%285420913529%29.jpg/350px-Tunnel_system_-_Fort_San_Crist%C3%B3bal_%285420913529%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4288" data-file-height="2848" /></a><figcaption>Tunnel passageway connecting the different sections of the fortress</figcaption></figure>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Charles_III_of_Spain" title="Charles III of Spain">Charles III of Spain</a> came to the Spanish throne in 1759, and in 1764 ordered Field Marshal <a href="/wiki/Alejandro_O%27Reilly" title="Alejandro O&#39;Reilly">Alexander O\'Reilly</a> and the engineers, Colonel Tom\xc3\xa1s O\'Daly and Juan Francisco Mestre, to make the island a stronghold, a defense of the first order. The plans constituted the preparation for a war with <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, which seemed inevitable. The order coincided with the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Havana" title="Siege of Havana">Siege of Havana</a> temporarily by the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">English</a>. As a result of O\'Reily\'s reforms, the <i>Revell\xc3\xadn de San Carlos</i> (Saint Charles <a href="/wiki/Ravelin" title="Ravelin">Ravelin</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/Counterguard" title="Counterguard">counterguard</a> of <i>La Trinidad</i> (The Trinity) were built. Further east, a fort called <i>El Abanico</i> (The Fan) and a <a href="/wiki/Artillery_battery" title="Artillery battery">battery</a> overlooking the sea, <i>La Princesa</i> (The Princess), were built. By 1788, the year of the king\'s death, the reforms were complete. The defenses proved their effectiveness since in 1797 they helped to repel the invasion of seven thousand English soldiers commanded by the British general <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Abercromby" title="Ralph Abercromby">Ralph Abercromby</a>.\n</p>\n<figure typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:San_Cristobal_cistern_well_-_San_Juan_NHS_Puerto_Rico.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/San_Cristobal_cistern_well_-_San_Juan_NHS_Puerto_Rico.jpg/150px-San_Cristobal_cistern_well_-_San_Juan_NHS_Puerto_Rico.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="200" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/San_Cristobal_cistern_well_-_San_Juan_NHS_Puerto_Rico.jpg/225px-San_Cristobal_cistern_well_-_San_Juan_NHS_Puerto_Rico.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/San_Cristobal_cistern_well_-_San_Juan_NHS_Puerto_Rico.jpg/300px-San_Cristobal_cistern_well_-_San_Juan_NHS_Puerto_Rico.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3000" data-file-height="4000" /></a><figcaption>Opening of cistern in the fortress</figcaption></figure> \n<p><i>Castillo San Crist\xc3\xb3bal</i> also contains five <a href="/wiki/Cistern" title="Cistern">cisterns</a> that were used for the storage of water during the ages of the Spanish Colony. They are extremely large (24&#160;ft tall, 17&#160;ft wide, and 57&#160;ft long) and were used as bomb shelters during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Historical_gallery">Historical gallery</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Castillo_San_Crist%C3%B3bal_(San_Juan)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Historical gallery"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<ul class="gallery mw-gallery-packed">\n\t\t<li class="gallerybox" style="width: 127.33333333333px">\n\t\t\t<div class="thumb" style="width: 125.33333333333px;"><span typeof="mw:File"><a href="/wiki/File:Collectie_Nationaal_Museum_van_Wereldculturen_TM-20016575_San_Juan._Fort_San_Cristobal_Puerto_Rico_Boy_Lawson_(Fotograaf).jpg" class="mw-file-description" title="Castillo de San Crist\xc3\xb3bal in 1964.
