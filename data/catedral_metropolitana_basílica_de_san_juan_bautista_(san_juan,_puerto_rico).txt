Bas\xc3\xadlica Metropolitana  de San Juan Bautista</b>, or in English, <b>Metropolitan Cathedral Basilica of Saint John the Baptist</b>, is the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Cathedral" title="Cathedral">cathedral</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_San_Juan_de_Puerto_Rico" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of San Juan de Puerto Rico">Archdiocese of San Juan de Puerto Rico</a>. It is one of the oldest buildings in <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Old San Juan, Puerto Rico">Old San Juan</a>, the oldest cathedral in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, and the second-oldest cathedral in the Americas.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Although the <a href="/wiki/Cathedral_of_Santo_Domingo" title="Cathedral of Santo Domingo">Cathedral of Santa Mar\xc3\xada la Menor</a> in <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo" title="Santo Domingo">Santo Domingo, Dominican Republic</a>, is an older church building, the Cathedral holds the distinction of being the first cathedral church in the Americas.<sup id="cite_ref-Herbermann_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Herbermann-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This is because San Juan, then known as the City of Puerto Rico, was the first diocese of the New World, with Bishop Don Alonso Manso appointed in 1511.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>A private Puerto Rican foundation known as <i>Fundaci\xc3\xb3n Protectora de la Catedral Metropolitana de San Juan, Inc.</i> was established to fund the historical restoration of the building and its art treasures for its 500th anniversary in 2021, and to protect it for the coming centuries.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cathedral_of_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The original cathedral in what was the city of Puerto Rico (changed to San Juan Bautista in 1521) was constructed from wood in 1521. It was destroyed by a hurricane and the current church construction began in 1535, being practically completed in 1802, later in 1905 an upper portion to the facade was added.<sup id="cite_ref-Mari_Mut_2013_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mari_Mut_2013-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The first school in Puerto Rico was the <i>Escuela de gram\xc3\xa1tica</i> (Grammar School). The school was established by Bishop <a href="/wiki/Alonso_Manso" title="Alonso Manso">Alonso Manso</a> in 1513, in the area where the cathedral would later be constructed. The school was free of charge and the courses taught were Latin language, literature, history, science, art, philosophy and theology.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The cathedral contains the <a href="/wiki/Tomb" title="Tomb">tomb</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish people">Spanish</a> explorer and settlement founder <a href="/wiki/Juan_Ponce_de_Le%C3%B3n" title="Juan Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n">Juan Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n</a>. It also has a shrine to the <a href="/wiki/Beatification" title="Beatification">Blessed</a> <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Manuel_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Santiago" title="Carlos Manuel Rodr\xc3\xadguez Santiago">Carlos Manuel Rodr\xc3\xadguez Santiago</a>, the first <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican people">Puerto Rican</a> and first <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>-born layperson in history to be beatified.<sup id="cite_ref-VaticanList_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VaticanList-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The first organist of the Cathedral of San Juan was the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canarian</a> <a href="/wiki/Domingo_Crisanto_Delgado_G%C3%B3mez" title="Domingo Crisanto Delgado G\xc3\xb3mez">Domingo Crisanto Delgado G\xc3\xb3mez</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> who came from the island of <a href="/wiki/Tenerife" title="Tenerife">Tenerife</a> and managed to take this position in <a href="/wiki/1836" title="1836">1836</a>, having been a composer in <a href="/wiki/La_Laguna_Cathedral" title="La Laguna Cathedral">Cathedral Our Lady of Los Remedios of San Crist\xc3\xb3bal de La Laguna</a> in his native island.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span
