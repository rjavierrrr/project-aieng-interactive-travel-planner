It is located in front of the <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Col%C3%B3n" title="Plaza Col\xc3\xb3n">Col\xc3\xb3n Main Square</a> facing the <a href="/wiki/Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_la_Candelaria_Cathedral" class="mw-redirect" title="Nuestra Se\xc3\xb1ora de la Candelaria Cathedral">Nuestra Se\xc3\xb1ora de la Candelaria Cathedral</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayag%C3%BCez_City_Hall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The first building was built in 1845 but was destroyed by the <a href="/wiki/1918_San_Ferm%C3%ADn_earthquake" title="1918 San Ferm\xc3\xadn earthquake">earthquake of 1918</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Alvarez_Cervela_1983_2\xe2\x80\x933_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alvarez_Cervela_1983_2\xe2\x80\x933-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The original building housed the municipal offices, a jail, a telegraph station and the guard corps.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The current city hall was built in 1926. Its main entrance consists of a <a href="/wiki/Portico" title="Portico">portico</a> and a tower with a clock.<sup id="cite_ref-Alvarez_Cervela_1983_2\xe2\x80\x933_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alvarez_Cervela_1983_2\xe2\x80\x933-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Its fa\xc3\xa7ade is of the <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">neoclassical</a> style, classified as such because of the building\'s Corinthian capitals and its grecoroman columns.<sup id="cite_ref-Alvarez_Cervela_1983_2\xe2\x80\x933_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alvarez_Cervela_1983_2\xe2\x80\x933-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The architects were Carmoega and the Ing. Font Jim\xc3\xa9nez, it was built by Ignacio Flores Lorenzo, and Adriano Gonz\xc3\xa1lez was the contractor of the work.<sup id="cite_ref-Alvarez_Cervela_1983_2\xe2\x80\x933_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alvarez_Cervela_1983_2\xe2\x80\x933-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Mayors_of_Mayag%C3%BCez" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayors of Mayag\xc3\xbcez">mayor</a> that ordered the new city hall was <a href="/wiki/Juan_Rull%C3%A1n_Rivera" title="Juan Rull\xc3\xa1n Rivera">Juan Rull\xc3\xa1n Rivera</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GENE_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GENE-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Numerous parlors, rooms, offices and other services are situated in the building.<sup id="cite_ref-Alvarez_Cervela_1983_2\xe2\x80\x933_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alvarez_Cervela_1983_2\xe2\x80\x933-1"><span
