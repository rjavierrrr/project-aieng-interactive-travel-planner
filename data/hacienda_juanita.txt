Website</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Hacienda Juanita</b> (built 1833-34) is a <a href="/wiki/Coffee_plantation" class="mw-redirect" title="Coffee plantation">coffee plantation</a> <a href="/wiki/Hacienda" title="Hacienda">hacienda</a> in the town of <a href="/wiki/Maricao,_Puerto_Rico" title="Maricao, Puerto Rico">Maricao, Puerto Rico</a>. The  design is based on typical <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Culture of Puerto Rico">Puerto Rican culture</a>, and was commissioned by the wife of a Spanish official.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Coffee production at the hacienda declined from the 1960s.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Hotel">Hotel</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hacienda_Juanita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Hotel"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>From 1976 it was converted, with government assistance,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> into one of Puerto Rico\'s earliest <a href="/wiki/Parador" title="Parador">paradores</a>, and ran, administered by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Tourism_Company" title="Puerto Rico Tourism Company">Puerto Rico Tourism Company</a>, as a 21-room agro-<a href="/wiki/Hotel" title="Hotel">hotel</a>. At 1,600&#160;ft (490&#160;m) above sea level, visitors could enjoy the view and the peacefulness of the Puerto Rican mountains.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The parador passed to new private owners in 2005 but closed in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The hotel had hosted a <i>Sala del Parador</i>, a permanent exhibit of antique artifacts related to <a href="/w/index.php?title=Puerto_Rican_coffee&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Puerto Rican coffee (page does not exist)">Puerto Rican coffee</a> production.\n</p>\n<div
