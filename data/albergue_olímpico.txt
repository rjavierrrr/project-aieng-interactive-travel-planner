class="mw-body-content"><div class="mw-content-ltr mw-parser-output" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Athletic training and recreational center located in Puerto Rico</div>\n<p>The <b>Albergue Ol\xc3\xadmpico Germ\xc3\xa1n Rieckehoff</b> (<a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>: <b>Olympic Hostel</b>), often referred as <i>El Albergue</i>, is an athletic training and recreational center located in <a href="/wiki/Salinas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Salinas, Puerto Rico">Salinas, Puerto Rico</a> founded in 1985 by <a href="/wiki/Germ%C3%A1n_Rieckehoff" title="Germ\xc3\xa1n Rieckehoff">Germ\xc3\xa1n Rieckehoff</a>, former president of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Olympic_Committee" title="Puerto Rico Olympic Committee">Puerto Rico Olympic Committee</a>. It is the most complete sports center in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> used by professional athletes and by the general public for health, educational, and recreational activities.<sup id="cite_ref-copur_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-copur-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Albergue Ol\xc3\xadmpico was also the host of the Shooting competitions for the <a href="/wiki/2010_Central_American_and_Caribbean_Games" title="2010 Central American and Caribbean Games">2010 Central American and Caribbean Games</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Facilities">Facilities</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albergue_Ol%C3%ADmpico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Facilities"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Salinas,_PR.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Salinas%2C_PR.jpg/220px-Salinas%2C_PR.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Salinas%2C_PR.jpg/330px-Salinas%2C_PR.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Salinas%2C_PR.jpg/440px-Salinas%2C_PR.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2592" data-file-height="1944" /></a><figcaption>View near Albergue Ol\xc3\xadmpico</figcaption></figure>\n<p>It has a territorial expansion of 1,500 acres (6.1&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) and has 17 sports installations, including multiple use courts, <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a>, aquatic, and <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a> complexes, gymnasiums, different sports halls, two Conference Halls and a Sports Library named after <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Pont_Flores" title="Rafael Pont Flores">Rafael Pont Flores</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-copur_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-copur-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> used for training by the different sports federations of the Puerto Rico Olympic Committee as well as individual professionals, national and international
