Romero Garcia</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Enrique Arturo Laguerre V\xc3\xa9lez</b> (July 15, 1905 \xe2\x80\x93 June 16, 2005) was a teacher, novelist, playwright, <a href="/wiki/Critic" title="Critic">critic</a>, and newspaper columnist from <a href="/wiki/Moca,_Puerto_Rico" title="Moca, Puerto Rico">Moca, Puerto Rico</a>. He is the author of the 1935 novel <i>La Llamarada</i> (<abbr style="font-size:85%" title="literal translation">lit.</abbr><span style="white-space: nowrap;">&#8201;</span><span class="gloss-quot">\'</span><span class="gloss-text">The blaze</span><span class="gloss-quot">\'</span>), which has been for many years obligatory reading in many literature courses in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Biography">Biography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enrique_Laguerre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Laguerre studied at various universities, obtaining degrees in arts from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico" title="University of Puerto Rico">University of Puerto Rico</a> and <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a>.\n</p><p>In 1924, he took courses on teaching in rural areas in the town of <a href="/wiki/Aguadilla" class="mw-redirect" title="Aguadilla">Aguadilla</a>. The courses were taught by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carmen_G%C3%B3mez_Tejera&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Carmen G\xc3\xb3mez Tejera (page does not exist)">Carmen G\xc3\xb3mez Tejera</a>. After this, he taught from 1925 to 1988, both at the elementary school and university levels.\n</p><p>Laguerre was known to use the pen-names of <b>Trist\xc3\xa1n Ronda</b>, <b>Luis Urayo\xc3\xa1n</b>, <b>Motial</b> and <b>Alberto Prado</b>, among others. Married for many years to the well-respected writer <a href="/w/index.php?title=Luz_V._Romero_Garc%C3%ADa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Luz V. Romero Garc\xc3\xada (page does not exist)">Luz V. Romero Garc\xc3\xada</a>, he also worked in many Puerto Rican publications before joining the staff of <i><a href="/wiki/El_Vocero" title="El Vocero">El Vocero</a></i>.\n</p><p>In 1998, his peers as well as former governors <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Hern%C3%A1ndez_Col%C3%B3n" title="Rafael Hern\xc3\xa1ndez Col\xc3\xb3n">Rafael Hern\xc3\xa1ndez Col\xc3\xb3n</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luis_A._Ferr%C3%A9" title="Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9">Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9</a>, advocated for Laguerre to be considered for the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Literature" title="Nobel Prize in Literature">Nobel Prize in Literature</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Despite their efforts, Laguerre was not awarded the prestigious award.\n</p><p>Laguerre was an <a href="/wiki/Emeritus" title="Emeritus">emeritus</a> member of the <a href="/wiki/Center_for_Advanced_Studies_on_Puerto_Rico_and_the_Caribbean" title="Center for Advanced Studies on Puerto Rico and the Caribbean">Center for Advanced Studies on Puerto Rico and the Caribbean</a>.\n</p><p>Enrique Laguerre died on June 16, 2005, at the age of 99.  His body was buried on the grounds of the <a href="/wiki/Palacete_Los_Moreau" title="Palacete Los Moreau">Palacete Los Moreau</a>, an old hacienda restored as a museum, in his native town of Moca.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Writings">Writings</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enrique_Laguerre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Writings"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Laguerre was one of the most prolific novelists of Puerto Rico and was nominated for a Nobel Prize in Literature.<sup id="cite_ref-PRPOP_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PRPOP-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Following in the steps of <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Zeno_Gand%C3%ADa" title="Manuel Zeno Gand\xc3\xada">Manuel Zeno Gand\xc3\xada</a>, Laguerre\'s most influential work focused on the problems of the colonized society. His novel <i>La Llamarada</i> offers a comprehensive view of rural Puerto Rico during the <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a>. Most of his novels are essential readings in Puerto Rican literature courses.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Works">Works</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enrique_Laguerre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Works"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Visittomoca.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Visittomoca.jpg/300px-Visittomoca.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="225" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Visittomoca.jpg/450px-Visittomoca.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Visittomoca.jpg/600px-Visittomoca.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2304" data-file-height="1728" /></a><figcaption>The Labadie Mansion inspired Enrique Laguerre to write <i>La Llamarada</i>.
