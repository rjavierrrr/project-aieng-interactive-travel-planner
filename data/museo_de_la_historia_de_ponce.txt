href="http://ponce.inter.edu/cai/connections/html/museo.html">Website</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Museo de la Historia de Ponce</b> (<b>Museum of the History of Ponce</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Local_history_museum" class="mw-redirect" title="Local history museum">local history museum</a> located in the historic <a href="/wiki/Casa_Salazar-Candal" title="Casa Salazar-Candal">Casa Salazar-Candal</a> in the city of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>. The museum depicts the city\'s ecology, economy, architecture, government, and elements of daily life. It seeks to promote the research, conservation, and dissemination of the historic heritage of Ponce and Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Inaugurated on 12 December 1992,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> it was the first museum in Puerto Rico established to cover the history of the people of a town or city.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  It traces the city\'s history from the <a href="/wiki/Taino_Indians" class="mw-redirect" title="Taino Indians">Taino Indians</a> to today. The museum was inaugurated under the administration of Mayor <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Cordero_Santiago" title="Rafael Cordero Santiago">Rafael Cordero Santiago</a>, as part of the tricentennial celebration of the founding of the city.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>It is located in the <a href="/wiki/Historic_district" title="Historic district">historic district</a> of the city, a short two-block walk from the central <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Delicias" title="Plaza Las Delicias">Plaza Las Delicias</a> town square, at the southeast corner of Isabel and Mayor Cantera streets. The museum proper is housed in the historic <a href="/wiki/Casa_Salazar-Candal" title="Casa Salazar-Candal">Casa Salazar-Candal</a> but also includes the neighboring Rosali-Zapater house, which houses administratives offices of the museum, in addition to the empty lot that once held the residence of the Schuck Gelp\xc3\xad family.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2012, plans were announced to expand the museum into the <a href="/wiki/Casa_Rosita_Serrall%C3%A9s" title="Casa Rosita Serrall\xc3\xa9s">Casa Rosita Serrall\xc3\xa9s</a>, an adjacent property.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2014, Casa Rosita Serralles (Calle Salud street #67) joined Museo de la Historia de Ponce and opened as "<a href="/wiki/Mameyes_Landslide" class="mw-redirect" title="Mameyes Landslide">Museo de la Recordacion Barrio Mameyes</a>" (<a href="/wiki/Mameyes_Landslide" class="mw-redirect" title="Mameyes Landslide">Barrio Mameyes Memorial Museum</a>) under Ponce mayor <a href="/wiki/Mar%C3%ADa_Mel%C3%A9ndez" title="Mar\xc3\xada Mel\xc3\xa9ndez">Mar\xc3\xada Mel\xc3\xa9ndez</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is also known as "<a href="/wiki/Mameyes_Landslide" class="mw-redirect" title="Mameyes Landslide">Sala Memorial del Barrio Mameyes</a>" as it operates as a part of the Museo de la Historia de Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History_of_the_museum">History of the museum</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History of the museum"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce,_Ponce,_Puerto_Rico.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce%2C_Ponce%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg/400px-Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce%2C_Ponce%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg" decoding="async" width="400" height="249" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce%2C_Ponce%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg/600px-Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce%2C_Ponce%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce%2C_Ponce%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg/800px-Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce%2C_Ponce%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="638" /></a><figcaption>Part of the front facade of the Museo de la Historia de Ponce at Calle Mayor and Calle Isabel streets</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The idea of creating a Ponce history museum dates back to the 1930s, following a citizens group\'s initiative, but the project did not materialize then.  Interest in the proposal revived years later under Ponce\'s Secretariat of Culture.  The plan visualized a museum to be constructed on the block bordered by Isabel, Mayor, Cristina, and Salud streets, and Casa Salazar and Casa Zapater, two historic homes, were acquired, as was the empty lot where Casa Shuck Gelp\xc3\xad had once stood. However, the project came to a standstill once again.\n</p><p>The plan was revived once again, but with modifications, at the end of the 1980s. This time the project came under the direction of Ms. Maruja Candal Salazar and Dr. Neysa Rodr\xc3\xadguez Deynes (curator and founder). They worked in conjunction with a team of curators that included Lizette Cabrera Salcedo, Dr. Jos\xc3\xa9 Molinelli, Alberto del Toro, and J. A. Figueroa Irizarry, as well as museum specialists An\xc3\xadbal Sep\xc3\xbalveda, N\xc3\xa9stor Barretto, and Jorge Carbonell, members of the Carimar Research Center. The four-year period from 1989 to 1992 saw the restoration of the Salazar and Zapater houses to serve as home to the museum, and the completion of the research, planning, and design of its first four permanent exhibit halls of the museum, namely, ecology, panorama, economic activity, and architecture.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1994, the second phase of the project was initiated, under the direction of Lizette Cabrera Salcedo: the creation of two permanent exhibits, one focused on health and one entitled "Ponce in Puerto Rican Political Life", in addition to a multimedia presentation "Ponce is Ponce." In 1998, the <a href="/wiki/Ernesto_Ramos_Antonini" title="Ernesto Ramos Antonini">Ernesto Ramos Antonini</a> auditorium, designed by architect Jos\xc3\xa9 Berm\xc3\xbadez, was inaugurated under the direction of Lizette Cabrera Salcedo, at the location of the old Shuck Gelp\xc3\xad House.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Currently">Currently</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Currently"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>By 2002, ten years after its founding, the Museum of the History of Ponce had become one of Puerto Rico\'s most active cultural centers,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and Puerto Rico\'s most complete history museum.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The museum provides a variety of cultural offerings intended to strengthen and increase awareness of the study of Puerto Rican and Ponce\xc3\xb1o history. As part of the 10th anniversary celebrations, the third and last phase of the museum\'s development was begun.  It included two permanent exhibits on education and daily life, and the creation of a general museum catalog.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Building">Building</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Building"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The main building that houses the museum has stained-glass windows, mosaics, and interior patios.  Both buildings where the museum is housed are turn-of-the-20th-century homes. The Salazar House dates from 1911 and was designed by <a href="/wiki/Blas_C._Silva_Boucher" title="Blas C. Silva Boucher">Blas Silva Boucher</a> in an eclectic neoclassical style. It is listed in the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>. The Zapater home, on the other hand, represents the criollo townhouse typology. It was built in 1880 and rebuilt in 1925. The Zapater residence was home to some of Ponce\'s most notable families, including the <a href="/wiki/Segundo_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Segundo (Ponce)">Clausells</a>, Dapena, Zapater, and Rosaly families. It was also designed by Blas Silva.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Contents">Contents</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Contents"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The museum consists of 10 exhibition halls. It showcases facts, documents, objects, and <a href="/wiki/Relics" class="mw-redirect" title="Relics">relics</a> of Ponce\'s history, demonstrating the importance of the city in the development of the history of Puerto Rico, including its intellectual, political, social, cultural, and economic aspects. It also covers the role of the city in the artistic development of Puerto Rican culture in the areas of music, theater, opera, literature, and journalism.  Included in the exhibits are depictions of the ethnic cultures that influenced southern Puerto Rico culture, such as Spanish, African and non-European immigrants.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="The_permanent_exhibits_of_the_museum">The permanent exhibits of the museum</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The permanent exhibits of the museum"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Ecology">Ecology</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Ecology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The first exhibition hall depicts the land and climate of the municipality: its coast, plains, and mountains. It shows how the city\'s natural surroundings are the departure point for learning about its history.  It includes a scale model of the municipality, with illustrations of its fertile soils, natural resources, and native <a href="/wiki/Fauna" title="Fauna">fauna</a>. The curator in charge of this exhibit was Dr. Jos\xc3\xa9 Mollinelli, with sponsorship by CARIMAR.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Panorama">Panorama</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Panorama"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Human beings make an entrance in this exhibit describing how humans interact with their environment.  The Panorama hall presents the origins of the settlement at Ponce and the evolution of its population from <a href="/wiki/Pre-Hispanic" class="mw-redirect" title="Pre-Hispanic">pre-Hispanic</a> times through the beginnings of the 20th century. It includes the regions and countries from which the present inhabitants originated, emphasizing Ponce\'s heterogeneous populace.  A scale model of the city shows Ponce in its greatest era of development, at the end of the 19th century. The curator in charge of this exhibit was Dra. Neysa Rodr\xc3\xadguez Deynes, with sponsorship by CARIMAR.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Politics">Politics</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Politics"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>This exhibit focuses on Ponce\'s role in Puerto Rico\'s politics and the local political life of the people of Ponce. The exhibit occupies two rooms.  The first illustrates the political development of Puerto Rico from 1508 until the 1950s.  Photographic murals, documents, photographs, and objects reveal the most significant periods in Puerto Rican political life and Ponce\'s leading role in this development. The second room explains the creation of the <a href="/wiki/Estado_Libre_Asociado" class="mw-redirect" title="Estado Libre Asociado">Estado Libre Asociado</a>, and the life and work of governors native to Ponce: <a href="/wiki/Luis_A._Ferr%C3%A9" title="Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9">Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Hern%C3%A1ndez_Col%C3%B3n" title="Rafael Hern\xc3\xa1ndez Col\xc3\xb3n">Rafael Hern\xc3\xa1ndez Col\xc3\xb3n</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Roberto_S%C3%A1nchez_Vilella" title="Roberto S\xc3\xa1nchez Vilella">Roberto S\xc3\xa1nchez Vilella</a>, who claimed Ponce as his adoptive city.  Among the most significant pieces in this exhibition hall is the Prince of Asturias of Literature Prize (Premio Pr\xc3\xadncipe de Asturias de las Letras), a small bronze statue created by the famous Spanish sculptor <a href="/wiki/Joan_Mir%C3%B3" title="Joan Mir\xc3\xb3">Juan Mir\xc3\xb3</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Finance_and_Economics">Finance and Economics</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Finance and Economics"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>One of the most significant themes in the museum, economic development, is the largest factor in the city\'s growth from its founding through the present day. The development of the port, agriculture, industry, and banking has supported Ponce\'s economic development, which in turn has significantly nourished its cultural, social, and intellectual development are evident in this exhibition hall. The curator in charge of this exhibit was Lizette Cabrera Salcedo, with sponsorship by CARIMAR.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Architecture_and_Urban_Development">Architecture and Urban Development</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Architecture and Urban Development"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The evolution of Ponce\'s urban landscape is illustrated in successive periods of development. The exhibit presents the city\'s architects and highlights typical elements of Ponce\'s native architecture. It includes domestic, <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a>, and institutional architecture, public works and the evolution of <a href="/wiki/Public_space" title="Public space">open space</a> that define this city as a traditional urban center of importance.  The hall includes a scale model of the city\'s urban development, and a photographic montage including plans, buildings, architects, and art installations. The curator in charge of this exhibit was Architect  Alberto del Toro, with sponsorship by CARIMAR.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Health_and_Medicine">Health and Medicine</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Health and Medicine"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>This exhibit shows the creation and evolution of Ponce\'s principal health institutions from 1863 until the present day and the medical community from the early 20th century until the 1940s. It also presents significant events in health, such as Dr. <a href="/wiki/Bailey_K._Ashford" class="mw-redirect" title="Bailey K. Ashford">Bailey K. Ashford</a>\'s discovery of the parasite that causes <a href="/wiki/Ancylostomiasis" title="Ancylostomiasis">uncinaria ancylostomiasis</a> (local ancylostomiasis) to the country\'s first kidney transplant. Included in this exhibit are  documents, photographs, prescriptions, professional awards, and personal artifacts once belonging to various Ponce physicians.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Collections">Collections</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Collections"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The process of putting together the collections involved over 200 of Ponce\'s own citizens who lent or donated the photographs, documents, and objects that make up the collections. The current collection has over 3,000 photographs, documents, pieces of furniture, and objects on display in the exhibition halls, or conserved in the museum\'s storage areas.\n</p><p>The most important collections include:\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="The_Dr._Manuel_de_la_Pila_Iglesias_collection">The Dr. Manuel de la Pila Iglesias collection</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: The Dr. Manuel de la Pila Iglesias collection"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Covering the period from 1898 to 1950, this collection consists of documents, photographs, and personal objects of <a href="/wiki/Dr._Pila" class="mw-redirect" title="Dr. Pila">Dr. Manuel de la Pila Iglesias</a> that show the evolution of health and <a href="/wiki/Medicine" title="Medicine">medicine</a> in Ponce during the early-to-middle 20th century.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="The_Juan_Sisco_Santiago_collection">The Juan Sisco Santiago collection</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: The Juan Sisco Santiago collection"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Covering the period from 1930 to 1980, this collection includes documents, publications, objects, and photographs by the famous Ponce photographer Juan Sisco Santiago, and illustrating the <a href="/wiki/Politics" title="Politics">political</a>, <a href="/wiki/Society" title="Society">social</a>, cultural and economic daily life of Ponce during the second half of the 20th century.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="The_Dr._Ana_Dolores_P\xc3\xa9rez_Marchand_collection"><span id="The_Dr._Ana_Dolores_P.C3.A9rez_Marchand_collection"></span>The Dr. Ana Dolores P\xc3\xa9rez Marchand collection</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: The Dr. Ana Dolores P\xc3\xa9rez Marchand collection"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Covering the period from 1911 to 1950, this collection includes photographs, documents, publications, and personal items of Ponce\'s first <a href="/wiki/Woman" title="Woman">female</a> physician, <a href="/wiki/Ana_Dolores_P%C3%A9rez_Marchand" title="Ana Dolores P\xc3\xa9rez Marchand">Ana Dolores P\xc3\xa9rez Marchand</a>. Dr. P\xc3\xa9rez Marchand was also one of the first three female physician in Puerto Rico. This collection provides a valuable resource for studying the role of women during the early 20th century, and the development of health and medicine in Puerto Rico in general.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="The_Emilio_J._Pasarell_collection">The Emilio J. Pasarell collection</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: The Emilio J. Pasarell collection"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Covering the period from 1900 to 1920, this collection includes postcards by Ponce photographer Jos\xc3\xa9 Rodr\xc3\xadguez Serra. The collection was donated by the <a href="/wiki/Emilio_J._Pasarell" title="Emilio J. Pasarell">Emilio J. Pasarell</a> succession. These offer a visual panorama of Ponce\'s streets, public and private buildings, plazas, and businesses in the first two decades of the 20th century.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="The_Mariana_Su\xc3\xa1rez_de_Longo_collection"><span id="The_Mariana_Su.C3.A1rez_de_Longo_collection"></span>The Mariana Su\xc3\xa1rez de Longo collection</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: The Mariana Su\xc3\xa1rez de Longo collection"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>This collection covers the period from 1949 to 1970. It consists of correspondence, invitations, graduation cards, newspaper clippings, telegrams, and photographs of <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">educational</a> activities in Ponce between 1949 and 1970.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="The_Ram\xc3\xb3n_L\xc3\xb3pez_Crespo_collection"><span id="The_Ram.C3.B3n_L.C3.B3pez_Crespo_collection"></span>The Ram\xc3\xb3n L\xc3\xb3pez Crespo collection</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: The Ram\xc3\xb3n L\xc3\xb3pez Crespo collection"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Ram\xc3\xb3n L\xc3\xb3pez Crespo collection covers the period from 1960 to 1980. It consists of photographs, posters, programs of cultural events, magazines, letters, newspapers, postcards, flyers for musical and theatrical events, illustrating <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">cultural life</a> and events in Ponce between 1960 and 1980.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="See_also">See also</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
