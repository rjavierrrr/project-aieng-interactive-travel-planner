The <a href="/wiki/Park" title="Park">park</a> is one of the largest <a href="/wiki/Water_park" title="Water park">water parks</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> region. The park is owned and administered by Aguadilla\'s municipal government.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p><i>Las Cascadas</i> opened its doors in 1985 as the only facility of its kind in the Caribbean. The park was built under the <a href="/wiki/Municipal" class="mw-redirect" title="Municipal">municipal</a> administration of <b>Alfredo Gonzalez</b>. The park is now part of the <b>Authority of Municipal Enterprises</b> (AME) of Aguaddilla. AME also administers the <a href="/wiki/Aguadilla_Ice_Skating_Arena" title="Aguadilla Ice Skating Arena">Aguadilla Ice Skating Arena</a>. \n</p><p>The water park was closed since after <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> struck Puerto Rico on September 20, 2017. In 2020, <a href="/wiki/Federal_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Federal Emergency Management Agency">Federal Emergency Management Agency</a> assigned over $917,000 in funds to repair the park.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In early 2022, phase 1 of repairs had been completed and the park was set to reopen.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In May 2022, a portal for purchasing tickets was available to the public.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span
