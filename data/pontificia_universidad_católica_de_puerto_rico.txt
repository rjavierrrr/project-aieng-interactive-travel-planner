Universidad Cat\xc3\xb3lica de Puerto Rico</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Private_university" title="Private university">private</a> <a href="/wiki/Catholic_church" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholic church">Roman Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a> with its main campus in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>. It provides courses leading to Bachelor\'s, Master\'s and Doctorate degrees in education, business administration, the sciences, and arts and humanities. It also has campuses in <a href="/wiki/Arecibo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Arecibo, Puerto Rico">Arecibo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mayag%C3%BCez,_Puerto_Rico" title="Mayag\xc3\xbcez, Puerto Rico">Mayag\xc3\xbcez</a>, as well as a satellite extension in <a href="/wiki/Coamo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Coamo, Puerto Rico">Coamo</a>. It is also home to a <a href="/wiki/Pontifical_Catholic_University_of_Puerto_Rico_School_of_Law" title="Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Law">School of Law</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Pontifical_Catholic_University_of_Puerto_Rico_School_of_Architecture" title="Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Architecture">School of Architecture</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The university also founded a medical school, <a href="/wiki/Ponce_School_of_Medicine" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce School of Medicine">Escuela de Medicina de Ponce</a>, in 1977, but in 1980 became an independent entity that eventually became the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Health_Sciences_University" title="Ponce Health Sciences University">Ponce Health Sciences University</a>.\n</p><p>The Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico was founded in 1948 as <b>Universidad Cat\xc3\xb3lica de Santa Mar\xc3\xada</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> but its name was changed to <a href="/wiki/Catholic_University_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholic University of Puerto Rico">Universidad Cat\xc3\xb3lica de Puerto Rico</a> with the graduation of its first class in 1950.<sup id="cite_ref-Cristobal_Col\xc3\xb3n_1993._Page_8_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cristobal_Col\xc3\xb3n_1993._Page_8-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> On 25 January 1991, the name was changed again to its current name, after <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_Paul_II" title="Pope John Paul II">Pope John Paul II</a> bestowed the title of <a href="/wiki/Pontifical_university" title="Pontifical university">pontifical</a> on the university.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pontifical_Catholic_University_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The university was founded in spring of 1948 by <a href="/wiki/James_Edward_McManus" title="James Edward McManus">James E. McManus</a>, C.S.S.R., Bishop of the <a href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Ponce" class="mw-redirect" title="Diocese of Ponce">Diocese of Ponce</a>, and <a href="/wiki/James_Peter_Davis" title="James Peter Davis">James Peter Davis</a>, Bishop of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_San_Juan_de_Puerto_Rico" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of San Juan de Puerto Rico">Diocese of San Juan</a>. It was founded as the Catholic University of Santa Mar\xc3\xada.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> "The name Santa Mar\xc3\xada was chosen to honor the Mother of God and to implore her protection and help."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  Its first president was Monse\xc3\xb1or Vicente Murga.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The original campus consisted of a few classrooms provided by the <a href="/wiki/Capuchin_Friars" class="mw-redirect" title="Capuchin Friars">Capuchin Friars</a> (OFM) and the <a href="/wiki/Sisters_of_Saint_Joseph" class="mw-redirect" title="Sisters of Saint Joseph">Sisters of Saint Joseph</a>, catholic clergymen and nuns, respectively, in the <a href="/wiki/Colegio_San_Conrado" title="Colegio San Conrado">Colegio San Conrado</a> in Ponce, a catholic primary educational school. During 1949, the university acquired 118 cuerdas (115 acres) from the Government of Puerto Rico (administered by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Autoridad_de_Tierras&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Autoridad de Tierras (page does not exist)">Autoridad de Tierras</a>). This plot of land was located south of the city center proper and was, at the time, part of the <i>Ingenio La Reparada</i> sugar cane plantation, and being used to feed <a href="/wiki/Hacienda_Mercedita" title="Hacienda Mercedita">Central Mercedita</a> sugar mill.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The University has been accredited by the <a href="/wiki/Middle_States_Association_of_Colleges_and_Schools" title="Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools">Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools</a> since 1953.<sup id="cite_ref-Cristobal_Col\xc3\xb3n_1993._Page_8_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cristobal_Col\xc3\xb3n_1993._Page_8-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Presidents">Presidents</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pontifical_Catholic_University_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Presidents"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<ul><li>Jos\xc3\xa9 A. Frontera Agenjo</li>\n<li>Jorge Iv\xc3\xa1n V\xc3\xa9lez Arocho (2010\xe2\x80\x932024)</li>\n<li>Marcelina Velez de Santiago (2006 - 2010)</li>\n<li>Jose Alberto Morales<sup id="cite_ref-mora1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mora1-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> (2001 - 2006)</li>\n<li>Tossello Giangiacono<sup id="cite_ref-mora1_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mora1-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> (1984 - 2001)</li>\n<li>Jaime B. Fuster (1981 - 1984)<sup id="cite_ref-colon_9_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colon_9-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>Francisco Carreras (1969 - 1981)<sup id="cite_ref-colon_9_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colon_9-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>Theodore McCarrick (1965 - 1969)<sup id="cite_ref-colon_9_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colon_9-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>John F. Mueller (1961 - 1965)<sup id="cite_ref-colon_9_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colon_9-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>Thomas Stanley (1956 - 1961)<sup id="cite_ref-colon_9_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colon_9-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>William Ferree (1953 - 1956)<sup id="cite_ref-colon_9_12-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colon_9-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>Vicente Murga (1948 - 1953)<sup id="cite_ref-colon_9_12-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colon_9-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li></ul>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Schools">Schools</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pontifical_Catholic_University_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Schools"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1961, the university started the <a href="/wiki/Pontifical_Catholic_University_of_Puerto_Rico_School_of_Law" title="Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Law">School of Law</a> providing graduate law studies and student exchange programs with other law schools in the United States and in Spain. The school has been host to famous and respected Puerto Rican law professors, including former Puerto Rico governor <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Hern%C3%A1ndez_Col%C3%B3n" title="Rafael Hern\xc3\xa1ndez Col\xc3\xb3n">Rafael Hern\xc3\xa1ndez Col\xc3\xb3n</a>.\n</p><p>In 1976, the university started the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_School_of_Medicine" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce School of Medicine">School of Medicine</a> as a graduate medical education program, which, when reorganized, became an independent private institution now known as the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Health_Sciences_University" title="Ponce Health Sciences University">Ponce Health Sciences University</a>.\n</p><p>In 2009, the university opened a <a href="/wiki/Pontifical_Catholic_University_of_Puerto_Rico_School_of_Architecture" title="Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Architecture">School of Architecture</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2011, the university announced it was engaging in a $20 million expansion plan that would result in the creation of three new schools over a ten-year period. The three new schools were reported to be a School of Graphical Design, a School of Fashion, and a School of Cinematic Arts.<sup id="cite_ref-periodicolaperla.com_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-periodicolaperla.com-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Campuses">Campuses</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pontifical_Catholic_University_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Campuses"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Aside from its main campus in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>, specifically on barrio <a href="/wiki/Canas_Urbano" title="Canas Urbano">Canas Urbano</a>, on Avenida Las Americas (<a href="/wiki/PR-163" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-163">PR-163</a>), the university has two additional campuses: the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pontifical_Catholic_University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Arecibo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico at Arecibo (page does not exist)">Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico at Arecibo</a> in Arecibo and the <a href="/wiki/Pontifical_Catholic_University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Mayag%C3%BCez" title="Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico at Mayag\xc3\xbcez">Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico at Mayag\xc3\xbcez</a> in Mayag\xc3\xbcez. It also has a satellite extension in <a href="/wiki/Coamo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Coamo, Puerto Rico">Coamo</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Pontifical_Catholic_University_of_Puerto_Rico_School_of_Architecture" title="Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Architecture">Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Architecture</a> is also a separate campus and is located across from <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Delicias" title="Plaza Las Delicias">Plaza Las Delicias</a> at the historic <a href="/w/index.php?title=Forteza_Building&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Forteza Building (page does not exist)">Forteza Building</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">downtown Ponce</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span
