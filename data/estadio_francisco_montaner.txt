(1950-1971)</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Estadio Francisco ("Paquito") Montaner</b> (English: <i>Francisco ("Paquito") Montaner Stadium</i>) is a multi-use <a href="/wiki/Stadium" title="Stadium">stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. It is home to the <a href="/wiki/Leones_de_Ponce_(baseball)" title="Leones de Ponce (baseball)">Leones de Ponce</a> team of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Baseball_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Baseball League">Puerto Rico Baseball League</a> (LBPPR) and <a href="/wiki/FC_Leones" title="FC Leones">FC Leones</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Soccer_League" title="Puerto Rico Soccer League">Puerto Rico Soccer League</a> (PRSL). The stadium has a capacity of 16,000 seats.<sup id="cite_ref-PR.com_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR.com-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-noticel1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-noticel1-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sporting_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Construction of the stadium began in 1947 and it opened on 15 October 1949.<sup id="cite_ref-periodicolaperla.com_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-periodicolaperla.com-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Montaner is the first stadium in Puerto Rico with an artificial surface field.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The stadium lies next to the <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pach%C3%ADn_Vic%C3%A9ns_Auditorium" class="mw-redirect" title="Juan Pach\xc3\xadn Vic\xc3\xa9ns Auditorium">Juan Pach\xc3\xadn Vic\xc3\xa9ns Auditorium</a>, where the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Lions_(basketball)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce Lions (basketball)">Ponce Lions (basketball)</a> hold their games. The Stadium was named to honor <a href="/wiki/Francisco_%22Paquito%22_Montaner" title="Francisco &quot;Paquito&quot; Montaner">Francisco "Paquito" Montaner</a>, one of the greatest <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican people">Puerto Rican</a> pitchers of all times.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estadio_Francisco_Montaner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>While baseball had been practiced in the city prior to this, baseball in Ponce started in earnest upon the arrival of Americans to the island after the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> of 1898.  The first non-professional baseball leagues played at <a href="/wiki/Campo_Atl%C3%A9tico_Charles_H._Terry" title="Campo Atl\xc3\xa9tico Charles H. Terry">Campo Atl\xc3\xa9tico Charles H. Terry</a>. After the formation of the <i>Liga de B\xc3\xa9isbol Semiprofesional de Puerto Rico</i> (Puerto Rico Semiprofessional Baseball League) in 1938,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> the sport had matured enough that it became necessary to consider building a new stadium. This need was strengthened on 14 September 1941 when the league became professional-level.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Construction of the stadium began in 1947 and it opened two years later on 15 October 1949.<sup id="cite_ref-periodicolaperla.com_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-periodicolaperla.com-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In addition to baseball, the stadium was also used for basketball games. These were played on a convertible basketball court that was stowed away when the basketball games were over.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> has killed?\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Uses">Uses</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estadio_Francisco_Montaner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Uses"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The PRPBL\'s regular playing season runs from November through January. From February to August, the venue is transformed into a <a href="/wiki/Track_and_field" title="Track and field">track and field</a> stadium, and also serves as a venue for other activities such as a host of the Mustang Auto Daredevils. It has also been used for <a href="/wiki/Intramural" class="mw-redirect" title="Intramural">intramural</a> competitions and other academic sporting events.  The track is completely covered during baseball season and then it is elevated via a hydraulic system for the track and field events.\n</p><p>The stadium is also used for winter training by many <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">United States Major Baseball League</a> players.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  In its early days, in particular, the stadium was used as the winter training stadium by the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a>. In 1947, the game at the opening day of training was won 12 to 8 by the <a href="/wiki/Leones_de_Ponce_(baseball)" title="Leones de Ponce (baseball)">Leones de Ponce</a><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>a<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> v. New York Yankees.  News of the event first broke at the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Daily_News" title="New York Daily News">New York Daily News</a></i>, followed by <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Herald" class="mw-redirect" title="The New York Herald">The New York Herald</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> .<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Major_events">Major events</h2><span events"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Championships">Championships</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estadio_Francisco_Montaner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Championships"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Montaner has seen a total of 12 championships: four in <a href="/wiki/Baloncesto_Superior_Nacional" title="Baloncesto Superior Nacional">BSN</a> basketball (1952, 1961, 1964 y 1965), one in Double A (AA) baseball in 1957, six in Professional Baseball (68-69, 69-70, 71-72, 81-82, 03-04 and 08-09), and one in <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-periodicolaperla.com_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-periodicolaperla.com-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Athletics">Athletics</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estadio_Francisco_Montaner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Athletics"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Montaner has also seen some great events in athletics. The <i>Primeros Juegos Ponce\xc3\xb1os</i>, the Frankie Col\xc3\xb3n Memorial, the <i>Justas Intercolegiales</i> (intercollegiate games), the <a href="/wiki/1993_Central_American_and_Caribbean_Games" title="1993 Central American and Caribbean Games">Central American and Caribbean Games</a> ("Ponce 93"), and the Iberoamerican Games, all took place here. There have also been athletics competitions at the Masters category and, more recently, the famed <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Grand_Prix" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce Grand Prix">Ponce Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-periodicolaperla.com_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-periodicolaperla.com-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Other_events">Other events</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Estadio_Francisco_Montaner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other events"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>For many years Montaner was the venue of the finals of the <i>Guantes Dorados de Boxeo Aficionado</i> (<a href="/wiki/Amateur_Boxing" class="mw-redirect" title="Amateur Boxing">Amateur Boxing</a>\'s Golden Gloves), national as well as U.S.-based <a href="/wiki/Professional_Wrestling" class="mw-redirect" title="Professional Wrestling">Professional Wrestling</a> <a href="/wiki/Billboard" title="Billboard">billboards</a>, bull runs, concerts by internationally renowned artists, as well as the site of various major religious conventions.<sup id="cite_ref-periodicolaperla.com_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-periodicolaperla.com-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In February 1958, evangelist <a href="/wiki/Billy_Graham" title="Billy Graham">Billy Graham</a> hosted a religious event at this stadium.<sup
