href="/wiki/Shopping_mall" title="Shopping mall">shopping mall</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. It is owned by Empresas Fonalledas, and is the largest mall in southern Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The mall is located at the intersection of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_2" title="Puerto Rico Highway 2">Puerto Rico Highway 2</a> and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_12" title="Puerto Rico Highway 12">Highway 12</a>. The anchor stores are <a href="/wiki/JCPenney" title="JCPenney">JCPenney</a> and <a href="/wiki/Macy%27s" title="Macy&#39;s">Macy\'s</a>. A former third anchor store, <a href="/wiki/Sears" title="Sears">Sears</a>, closed in February 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_del_Caribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Early_development:_1960s-1970s">Early development: 1960s-1970s</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_del_Caribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early development: 1960s-1970s"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>On December 28, 1969, the Kislak Organization advertised the development of a new 600,000 square feet (56,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) regional enclosed mall named \xe2\x80\x9cPlaza del Caribe\xe2\x80\x9d in Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On November 6, 1971, <a href="/wiki/JCPenney" title="JCPenney">J. C. Penney Co. Inc.</a>, one of America\'s largest retail chains at the time, was to open its second major department store in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> by November 1973. The J. I. Kislak Realty Corp. of <a href="/wiki/Newark,_New_Jersey" title="Newark, New Jersey">Newark, New Jersey</a>, and <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, had announced that negotiations had been concluded for a two-story,174,400-square-foot (16,200&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) department store to be built for Penney\'s occupancy in Plaza Del Caribe, a regional shopping center that was rising on a 62-acre (25&#160;ha) tract on Exterior Avenue in Ponce. Plaza Del Caribe, an enclosed, all-weather mall complex was scheduled to comprise more than 500,000 square feet of stores with parking provided for 3,600 cars in its initial stage. Provisions were being made for expansion, and the center was expected to reach 725,000 square feet of stores in its final stage. The center was being developed by Sabari Estates, Inc., headed by Jose Sauri Jr. of Ponce. Architects for the project were Passalacqua &amp; Cia. Development consultant and exclusive leasing broker for Plaza Del Caribe was the J. I. Kislak Realty Corp. which maintained a Caribbean regional office in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>. The same broker was an exclusive leasing agent for the <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Am%C3%A9ricas_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Plaza Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas (Puerto Rico)">Plaza Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas</a> in San Juan, the first regional shopping center in Latin America.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On May 21, 1972, it was reported that construction of the shopping center would begin work on July 1 of that year. The center was expected to have 680,000 square feet and 60 stores at the time, with a completion date set for 1974.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On June 25, 1975, it was announced that Plaza del Caribe, projected to cost $25 million, would begin construction in Ponce next September that year. This was reported by the firms Sabari Estates and Kislak Realty Corporation, developer and broker of the project, respectively. Final approval for the channeling of the R\xc3\xado Portugu\xc3\xa9s, which served as the border for the new shopping center, had been granted by the Department of Transportation and Public Works and by the Army Corps of Engineers, which, according to the firms, paved the way at the time for the start of construction of the project. The Kislak organization reported that it had already signed lease agreements with <a href="/wiki/Pueblo_Supermarkets" title="Pueblo Supermarkets">Pueblo Supermarkets</a>, <a href="/wiki/JCPenney" title="JCPenney">J.C. Penney</a>, <a href="/wiki/F._W._Woolworth_Company" title="F. W. Woolworth Company">F.W. Woolworth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gordon%27s_Jewelers" class="mw-redirect" title="Gordon&#39;s Jewelers">Gordon\'s Jewelers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gonz%C3%A1lez_Pad%C3%ADn" title="Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Pad\xc3\xadn">Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Pad\xc3\xadn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thom_McAn" title="Thom McAn">Thom McAn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kinney_Shoes" title="Kinney Shoes">Kinney Shoes</a> stores, which would occupy premises in the shopping center. Kislak Realty president Joseph Aramanda said Plaza del Caribe was expected to provide 2,000 jobs when completed.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On April 15, 1976, it was reported that Plaza del Caribe Shopping Center was about to begin construction. An executive with J.I. Kislak Realty Corp., the exclusive leasing agents at the time for Plaza del Caribe, said the mall would have approximately 85 stores. Located on the Ponce highway, Plaza del Caribe would have nearly 700,000 square feet (65,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) of commercial space and service areas. It was to be located on about 65 acres of land. The developer of the Shopping Center was Sabari Estates, from Ponce, whose president was Dr. Francisco S\xc3\xa1nchez Ar\xc3\xa1n. Atilio Zepeda, from the Kislak Realty Corporation for the Caribbean, said that among the most important department stores that had signed a lease for the mall was <a href="/wiki/JCPenney" title="JCPenney">J. C. Penney</a>. "This will be the largest mall ever built outside of the San Juan area, and we expect to have a large number of major department stores as well as other well known stores when Plaza del Caribe opens. We are currently signing new tenants," Zepeda said.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Opening_and_success:_1990s-2000s">Opening and success: 1990s-2000s</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_del_Caribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Opening and success: 1990s-2000s"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>On June 30, 1990, it was reported that one of the most modern and complete shopping centers in the southern area of the Island, would serve more than 470,000 consumers in the municipalities of the region. The project was under construction on a 70-acre property on highway number 2 in Ponce and represented an investment of over $50 million. "The city of Ponce is now experiencing a renewed effervescence. We feel very proud to share in their enthusiasm and to contribute, through Plaza del Caribe, to the economic development of Ponce, as well as the entire southern area," said <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Fonalledas" title="Jaime Fonalledas">Jaime Fonalledas</a>, son, president of the Board of the center. Fonalledas added that even with two years to go before the opening of the center, there was already a palpable interest on the part of the business community. He reported that preliminary agreements had been reached with the main department store chains for their participation in Plaza del Caribe. The businessman also highlighted that considerations have been taken in the architectural design of the center so that it contributed to the cultural richness of Ponce and complemented the city\'s tourist offerings.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On November 9, 1992, Plaza del Caribe, opened its doors to the public, and was already in the process of starting a third phase of construction. It was not disclosed what types of stores or businesses would be located in the three planned buildings, the largest of which would be 400,870 square feet. The remaining two would be 60,000 and 44,000 square feet, respectively. The projected cost of the new investment in Plaza del Caribe was also unclear. The two initial phases, the first of which began in April 1989, required an investment of $74 million. At the time, 1,500 to 2,000 jobs had been created. The three buildings would be built under the concept of a "<a href="/wiki/Power_center_(retail)" title="Power center (retail)">power center</a>\xe2\x80\x9d. Something similar to this type of concept was the <a href="/wiki/Toys_%22R%22_Us" title="Toys &quot;R&quot; Us">Toys "R" Us</a> store in <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Am%C3%A9ricas_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Plaza Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas (Puerto Rico)">Plaza Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas</a> at the time. Plaza del Caribe, whose main tenants were <a href="/wiki/Sears" title="Sears">Sears</a>, <a href="/wiki/JCPenney" title="JCPenney">JCPenney</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gonz%C3%A1lez_Pad%C3%ADn" title="Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Pad\xc3\xadn">Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Pad\xc3\xadn</a>, opened to the public with 32 stores already ready, representing 66 percent of stores or 84 percent of leased space. It was expected that by December of that year, 87 percent of them would be open, which meant that 90 percent of the space would already be rented. In total, Plaza del Caribe would have 118 stores, including the three main anchor stores, food outlets, movie theaters and stores such as <a href="/wiki/Kinney_Shoes" title="Kinney Shoes">Kinney Shoes</a>, Oak Tree, <a href="/wiki/Foot_Locker" title="Foot Locker">Foot Locker</a> and <a href="/wiki/LensCrafters" title="LensCrafters">LensCrafters</a>. This mix of establishments was made to follow the tastes of consumers who preferred clothing and shoe stores. They were followed in importance by service establishments; entertainment, music, and home goods stores; and then those of gifts and jewelry.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 2003, Plaza del Caribe would have establishments such as <a href="/wiki/Gap_Inc." title="Gap Inc.">Gap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Old_Navy" title="Old Navy">Old Navy</a>, Tiendas Capri, <a href="/wiki/5-7-9" title="5-7-9">5-7-9</a>, <a href="/wiki/Claire%27s" title="Claire&#39;s">Claire\'s</a>, <a href="/wiki/Champs_Sports" title="Champs Sports">Champs Sports</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foot_Locker" title="Foot Locker">Foot Locker</a>, <a href="/wiki/KB_Toys" title="KB Toys">KB Toys</a>, <a href="/wiki/EB_Games" title="EB Games">Electronics Boutique</a>, <a href="/wiki/RadioShack" title="RadioShack">Radio Shack</a>, Rave, Naturalizer, <a href="/wiki/Regis_Corporation" title="Regis Corporation">Regis Hairstylists</a>, Time Out, <a href="/wiki/Zale_Corporation" title="Zale Corporation">Zales Jewelers</a>, and others.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On January 6, 2007, It was reported that <a href="/wiki/Circuit_City" title="Circuit City">Circuit City</a> would be opening a store in Plaza del Caribe. The approximately 20,000 square foot store was expected to open by 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="2010s,_and_on"><span id="2010s.2C_and_on"></span>2010s, and on</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_del_Caribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010s, and on"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>During the 2010 Christmas holiday season, the in-mall 6-screen cinema was replaced by a new 10-screen <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Cinemas" title="Caribbean Cinemas">Caribbean Cinemas</a> multiplex located on mall property but physically detached from the main mall structure. It is located immediately south of the main mall complex bounded by <a href="/wiki/PR-52" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-52">Autopista Luis A. Ferre (PR-52)</a>.\n</p><p>On April 15, 2012, <a href="/wiki/CompUSA" title="CompUSA">CompUSA</a> announced the grand opening of its second branch in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The 30,000 square foot store incorporated a total consumer shopping experience through its "Retail 2.0" system. This system combined the best of shopping through traditional stores and online. A grand opening ceremony would be held on April 27, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The store under the nameplate at the time of <a href="/wiki/TigerDirect" title="TigerDirect">TigerDirect</a> would close by June 30, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On July 20, 2012, it was announced that Plaza del Caribe would soon begin a $3.5 million remodeling and expansion of its Terraza del Caribe food court and the construction of the second <a href="/wiki/Zara_(retailer)" title="Zara (retailer)">Zara</a> store in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Zara_(retailer)" title="Zara (retailer)">Zara</a> store would be located in the space previously occupied by the original location of <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Cinemas" title="Caribbean Cinemas">Caribbean Cinemas</a>, and was expected to open at the end of that year. Upon completion, the food court would have some 10,500 square feet of new space and its new design would be \xe2\x80\x9cmodern, bright, with open spaces, and will feature spaces for larger groups of people and counter tables, in addition to the traditional food court tables,\xe2\x80\x9d mall officials said. \xe2\x80\x9cThis year, when we celebrate the 20th anniversary of Plaza Del Caribe, we begin our preparations for the next 20 years of commitment and service to the market that has welcomed and supported us,\xe2\x80\x9d said Arturo Valldejuly, general manager at the time of Plaza Del Caribe. \xe2\x80\x9cWe now start the complete renovation and expansion of the food court, where we will be changing the floor, acoustic ceilings, furniture, facades of the eateries and the lighting of the area,\xe2\x80\x9d said Valldejuly, adding that although the construction work would begin then, it would be coordinated in a way that did not disrupt the everyday operation of the shopping center. It was expected that the project would be completed during the first half of that following year. Part of the food court project included relocating the mall\'s emblematic \xe2\x80\x9cVenus\xe2\x80\x9d sculpture from the base of the central atrium stairs. where it had been located for two decades at the time to the center of the food court.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On May 13, 2013, a <a href="/wiki/P._F._Chang%27s" title="P. F. Chang&#39;s">P. F. Chang\'s</a> restaurant would open at the mall, the 7,400 square foot structure had a capacity for 268 patrons.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On May 31, 2013, twelve years after making its local debut, retailer <a href="/wiki/Macy%27s" title="Macy&#39;s">Macy\'s</a>, announced planned to open a new store in Ponce, in the fall of 2015. The two-level, 150,000 square-foot store would be located in Plaza del Caribe, owned by <a href="/wiki/Empresas_Fonalledas" class="mw-redirect" title="Empresas Fonalledas">Empresas Fonalledas</a>. <a href="/wiki/Macy%27s" title="Macy&#39;s">Macy\'s</a> Ponce store would include an assortment of fashion goods for women, men, children and home that would be \xe2\x80\x9ctailored to the preferences of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Ricans" title="Puerto Ricans">Puerto Rican</a> customer,\xe2\x80\x9d store officials said. Construction was expected to begin in 2014. <a href="/wiki/Macy%27s" title="Macy&#39;s">Macy\'s</a> store was expected to employ a workforce of approximately 275 associates. The investment required to build the new store was not revealed.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In October 2013, <a href="/wiki/Sports_Authority" title="Sports Authority">Sports Authority</a>, one of the largest sporting goods retailers at the time in the mainland, marked its entrance into the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Ricans" title="Puerto Ricans">Puerto Rican</a> market with the grand opening of its flagship store in Ponce. The sporting goods store, located in Plaza del Caribe, boasted 35,000 square feet of retail space and featured a wide assortment of quality sporting goods.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The store would be announced to close in 2016 due to the bankruptcy of the chain.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On January 24, 2014, it was announced that after more than 20 years of being founded, the Plaza del Caribe shopping center, in Ponce, would be carrying out its first expansion, in which it would add a two-story building and more than 200,000 square feet of commercial space, at a cost of more than $30 million. The expansion, had not gone without controversy due to the removal of more than 300 trees on the land where the buildings will be built. The mall would have <a href="/wiki/Macy%27s" title="Macy&#39;s">Macy\'s</a> as its new anchor. This would be the second store that the chain would open on the island, the first one had opened in the year 2000 in <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Am%C3%A9ricas_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Plaza Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas (Puerto Rico)">Plaza Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas</a>. In an exclusive interview with Jaime Fonalledas, owner of Plaza del Caribe, he indicated that the construction would start during the third quarter of this year, and was expected to be completed by the end of 2015. It would be carried out in the southern area of the mall, in front of where the <a href="/wiki/Sports_Authority" title="Sports Authority">Sports Authority</a> and <a href="/wiki/CompUSA" title="CompUSA">CompUSA</a> stores were. The expansion would consist of a new two-story, 57,000 square foot building, connecting the existing mall building with the new <a href="/wiki/Macy%27s" title="Macy&#39;s">Macy\'s</a> anchor store. This addition would be made on the south side, next to <a href="/wiki/Popular,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Popular, Inc.">Banco Popular</a> on the first level and Loft on the second. Said building would accommodate a total of between 15 and 18 commercial establishments, and on its roof it would feature a large skylight that would allow sunlight to enter and give the two levels a lot of clarity. The <a href="/wiki/Macy%27s" title="Macy&#39;s">Macy\'s</a> store would have two levels and 150,000 square feet. With the expansion, more than 400 additional parking spaces would be built. \xe2\x80\x9cPlaza del Caribe has been successful from day one. <a href="/wiki/Macy%27s" title="Macy&#39;s">Macy\'s</a> was interested in opening a second store in Puerto Rico, and many merchants have confidence in the trajectory of this mall. Imagine, without announcing much, new tenants have arrived," Fonalledas commented when asked why he decided to expand the shopping center at this time. Among those tenants who had opted for Plaza del Caribe, he mentioned <a href="/wiki/Sports_Authority" title="Sports Authority">Sports Authority</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zara_(retailer)" title="Zara (retailer)">Zara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Forever_21" title="Forever 21">Forever 21</a>, <a href="/wiki/Express,_Inc." title="Express, Inc.">Express</a>, <a href="/wiki/P._F._Chang%27s" title="P. F. Chang&#39;s">PF Chang\'s</a>, <a href="/wiki/LongHorn_Steakhouse" title="LongHorn Steakhouse">Longhorn</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pandora_(jewelry)" title="Pandora (jewelry)">Pandora</a>, in addition to <a href="/wiki/Macy%27s" title="Macy&#39;s">Macy\'s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On October 22, 2015, it was reported that Plaza del Caribe had been the subject of a significant overhaul that included the construction of a new building featuring Puerto Rico\'s second ever <a href="/wiki/Macy%27s" title="Macy&#39;s">Macy\'s</a> department store, being scheduled to open on November 5 of that year. The expansion project, which entailed an investment of around $40 million also added about 60,000 square feet of space to the original mall structure, which in turn allowed the inclusion of about 18 new stores to the <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a> shopping center. Some of the retail tenants that had  recently opened or were slated to open during those next few weeks included <a href="/wiki/Victoria%27s_Secret" title="Victoria&#39;s Secret">Victoria\'s Secret</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bath_%26_Body_Works" title="Bath &amp; Body Works">Bath &amp; Body Works</a>, House of Hoops by <a href="/wiki/Foot_Locker" title="Foot Locker">Foot Locker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Starbucks" title="Starbucks">Starbucks</a>.  The expansion project at the mall, which had begun its most intense stage two years earlier, also involved constructing a structure connecting the Macy\'s building to the rest of the mall, as well as replacing the mall\'s floor and roof elements, furniture and d\xc3\xa9cor.The shopping center\'s redesigned look resembled that of certain areas of <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Am%C3%A9ricas_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Plaza Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas (Puerto Rico)">Plaza Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas</a>, particularly the hallways between anchor stores Macy\'s and <a href="/wiki/JC_Penney%27s" class="mw-redirect" title="JC Penney&#39;s">JC Penney\'s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On November 5, 2015, the mall would officially inaugurate its new renovations, which also included the opening of the <a href="/wiki/Macy%27s" title="Macy&#39;s">Macy\'s</a> store.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On November 7, 2019, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Sears" title="Sears">Sears</a> would be closing this location a part of a plan to close 96 stores nationwide. The store would close by February 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Mall_design">Mall design</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_del_Caribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mall design"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Plaza del Caribe is notable for its sophisticated design, featuring artwork scattered throughout the mall, especially in its central <a href="/wiki/Atrium_(architecture)" title="Atrium (architecture)">atrium</a>.
