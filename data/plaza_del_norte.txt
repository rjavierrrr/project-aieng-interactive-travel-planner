href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Shopping_center" title="Shopping center">shopping center</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Hatillo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Hatillo, Puerto Rico">Hatillo, Puerto Rico</a> currently owned and managed by Curzon Puerto Rico. It is the largest <a href="/wiki/Shopping_center" title="Shopping center">shopping center</a> in northwestern <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, with over 130 stores and <a href="/wiki/Fast_food" title="Fast food">fast food</a> restaurants.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_del_Norte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="1990s-2000s">1990s-2000s</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_del_Norte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1990s-2000s"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>On May 14, 1991, the placement of the first stone was celebrated beginning construction on the Plaza del Norte shopping mall.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\nIn Spring 1992, being developed my HT Ventures, SE, Plaza del Norte inaugurated to much fanfare, featuring three main anchors, a 61,880 square-foot <a href="/wiki/JCPenney" title="JCPenney">JCPenney</a>, a 90,000 square-foot <a href="/wiki/Sears" title="Sears">Sears</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-dmm2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dmm2-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and a 110,580 square-foot <a href="/wiki/Walmart" title="Walmart">Walmart</a> store which was expected to create approximately 240 jobs for area residents. Due to success, it would later on receive renovations in 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In December 2002, Plaza del Norte attracted visitors from all over the Island with its unique architectural design, traditional fountain, and its mix of shops in its L-shaped design. Sears, JCPenney, Walmart, and <a href="/wiki/Pueblo_Supermarkets" title="Pueblo Supermarkets">Pueblo Xtra</a> were all the perfect combination to attract customers and were excellently complemented by over 120 other stores such as <a href="/wiki/Pitusa" title="Pitusa">Pitusa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edison_Brothers_Stores" title="Edison Brothers Stores">Bakers Shoes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blockbuster_(retailer)" title="Blockbuster (retailer)">Blockbuster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Contempo_Casuals" class="mw-redirect" title="Contempo Casuals">Contempo Casuals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Footaction" class="mw-redirect" title="Footaction">Footaction</a>, <a href="/wiki/GNC_(company)" title="GNC (company)">GNC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gordon%27s_Jewelers" class="mw-redirect" title="Gordon&#39;s Jewelers">Gordon\xe2\x80\x99s Jewelers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kay_Bee_Toys" class="mw-redirect" title="Kay Bee Toys">Kay Bee Toys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Journeys_(company)" title="Journeys (company)">Journeys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tiendas_Kress" class="mw-redirect" title="Tiendas Kress">Kress</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Sunwear" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Sunwear">Pacific Sunwear</a>, <a href="/wiki/Payless_Shoe_Source" class="mw-redirect" title="Payless Shoe Source">Payless Shoe Source</a>, <a href="/wiki/RadioShack" title="RadioShack">Radio Shack</a>, <a href="/wiki/Disney_Store" title="Disney Store">The Disney Store</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toys_%22R%22_Us" title="Toys &quot;R&quot; Us">Toys R Us</a>, and many others.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On August 5, 2004, it was reported that PMI Inc., owners of Plaza del Norte at the time, had pumped $250,000 into an institutional advertising campaign for the shopping mall. Designed by Sajo Garcia &amp; Partners, it had kicked off in July of that year and would run for two years. With the title Revista (magazine), the campaign was opened by a television spot whose concept would be tailored later on to each shopping season. It would include print and radio ads, billboards, and banners inside the mall.  A women\'s magazine appeared in the ads highlighting the fashionable products and clothes that were available at Plaza del Norte. To further promote its stores, the magazine gave tips on the latest trends in fashion and advertised where those products can be found. According to Raquel Matias, marketing director of Plaza del Norte, the campaign\'s target market was modern, professional, active, and cheerful women who were ever fashion-con-scious. "The participation of our retailers was vital [for the campaign\'s production]," said Matias, "since all clothes, footwear, and accessories used were provided by store owners and managers."  Plaza del Norte was managed by PMI. Its 670,000 square foot property was home to <a href="/wiki/JCPenney" title="JCPenney">J.C.Penney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sears" title="Sears">Sears</a>, <a href="/wiki/Walmart" title="Walmart">Wal-Mart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pueblo_Supermarkets" title="Pueblo Supermarkets">Pueblo Supermarkets</a>, <a href="/wiki/CineVista_Theatres" title="CineVista Theatres">Cine Vista Theaters</a>, and others. Sales per square foot averaged $420 a year while food court sales per square foot averaged $1,020 a year.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2005, the mall was acquired by <a href="/wiki/DDR_Corp." class="mw-redirect" title="DDR Corp.">DDR Corp.</a> from a $1.15 billion portfolio deal with Caribbean Property Group (CPG) which included the mall.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In May 2006, the mall anchored by <a href="/wiki/Walmart" title="Walmart">Walmart</a>, <a href="/wiki/JCPenney" title="JCPenney">JCPenney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pueblo_Supermarkets" title="Pueblo Supermarkets">Pueblo Supermarket</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sears" title="Sears">Sears</a>, and a  <a href="/wiki/CineVista_Theatres" title="CineVista Theatres">CineVista Theatres</a> was the dominant shopping center in the trade area due to its size and prominent location. The tenant mix within the balance of the shopping center featured a food court, restaurants, and a selection of specialty stores including <a href="/wiki/Gap_Inc." title="Gap Inc.">The Gap</a>, <a href="/wiki/5-7-9" title="5-7-9">5-7-9</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foot_Locker" title="Foot Locker">Foot Locker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foot_Locker" title="Foot Locker">Kids Foot Locker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foot_Locker" title="Foot Locker">Lady Foot Locker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Champs_Sports" title="Champs Sports">Champs Sports</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zale_Corporation" title="Zale Corporation">Zales</a>, Rave, and <a href="/wiki/Destination_Maternity" title="Destination Maternity">Motherhood Maternity</a>, which combined to generate very strong sales per square foot.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n</p><p>On January 11, 2007, it was reported that the clothing store <a href="/wiki/Gap_Inc." title="Gap Inc.">Gap</a> would be leaving Plaza del Norte on January 26 of that month after another disappointing year for the fashion retail giant, which was widely rumored to be on the verge of being sold at the time. This left seven remaining Gap stores in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> in addition to <a href="/wiki/Gap_Inc." title="Gap Inc.">Gap Inc.</a> stable mates <a href="/wiki/Banana_Republic" title="Banana Republic">Banana Republic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Old_Navy" title="Old Navy">Old Navy</a>, with two and five stores respectively. In addition, Gap Inc. would continue to operate three Gap and one Banana Republic outlets on the island.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n</p><p>On March 1, 2007, it was reported that CV Entertainment Group had shut down its 6-screen <a href="/wiki/CineVista_Theatres" title="CineVista Theatres">CineVista Theatres</a> multiplex in Hatillo\'s Plaza del Norte, one of several high-profile tenants that exited or were about to leave the shopping center owned by stateside <a href="/wiki/Real_estate_investment_trust" title="Real estate investment trust">Real Estate Investment Trust</a> (REIT) <a href="/wiki/Developers_Diversified_Realty" class="mw-redirect" title="Developers Diversified Realty">Developers Diversified Realty</a> (DDR) at the time. In addition to the previously reported closing of <a href="/wiki/Gap_Inc." title="Gap Inc.">Gap</a>, sources said 1-2-3 and Infinito, which had filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection along with sister stores Geraldine, Jackie\'s, Ranger and Fashion Bazaar, were also leaving one of Puerto Rico\'s largest malls. On the bright side, sources say <a href="/wiki/Marshalls" title="Marshalls">Marshalls</a> would soon replace the vacant <a href="/wiki/Pitusa" title="Pitusa">Pitusa</a>, another casualty at Plaza del Norte. According to <a href="/wiki/Developers_Diversified" class="mw-redirect" title="Developers Diversified">DDR\'s</a> website, 61,874 square feet, almost 10%, of the mall was now vacant. Sources said rent and common area maintenance charges soared at DDR-owned malls since the REIT purchased 15 Puerto Rico malls for US$1.15 billion in late 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In November 2008, <a href="/wiki/Circuit_City" title="Circuit City">Circuit City</a> would open a store at Plaza del Norte, this being right on the heels of its bankruptcy at the time. The store would last until 2009, when the remaining three Circuit City stores including this one would ultimately close.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="2010s,_and_on"><span id="2010s.2C_and_on"></span>2010s, and on</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_del_Norte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2010s, and on"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In February 2010, the mall was now additionally anchored by an <a href="/wiki/OfficeMax" title="OfficeMax">OfficeMax</a>. It also had other stores such as <a href="/wiki/A%C3%A9ropostale_(company)" title="A\xc3\xa9ropostale (company)">Aeropostale</a>, Aldo, <a href="/wiki/Zale_Corporation" title="Zale Corporation">Gordon\'s Jewelers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foot_Locker" title="Foot Locker">Foot Locker</a>, La Gran Via, Kress, Marianne/Marianne Plus, <a href="/wiki/PacSun" title="PacSun">Pacific Sunwear</a>, <a href="/wiki/Payless_(footwear_retailer)" title="Payless (footwear retailer)">Payless ShoeSource</a>, <a href="/wiki/RadioShack" title="RadioShack">RadioShack</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sunglass_Hut" title="Sunglass Hut">Sunglass Hut</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Children%27s_Place" title="The Children&#39;s Place">The Children\'s Place</a>, The Room, and Traffic Shoes, which all combined to generate very strong sales results.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2011, a <a href="/wiki/TJ_Maxx" title="TJ Maxx">TJ Maxx</a> store opened at the mall.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2011, <a href="/wiki/DDR_Corp." class="mw-redirect" title="DDR Corp.">DDR Corp.</a> announced a significant interior and exterior renovation of the mall, including a 10,000-square-foot expansion and renovation of the existing <a href="/wiki/JCPenney" title="JCPenney">JCPenney</a> store, which now occupies part of the <a href="/wiki/JCPenney" title="JCPenney">JCPenney</a> wing, including the former <a href="/wiki/Circuit_City" title="Circuit City">Circuit City</a>, which closed in 2009. The project included the addition of a <a href="/wiki/Rooms_To_Go" title="Rooms To Go">Rooms To Go</a>, the combination of three small retail-space units to accommodate a <a href="/wiki/PetSmart" title="PetSmart">PetSmart</a>, several other new stores and outparcels, a new <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Cinemas" title="Caribbean Cinemas">Caribbean Cinemas</a> movie theater and new carts and kiosks through the entire mall. The project was completed in mid-2013.<sup id="cite_ref-Puerto_Rico:_A_retailer\xe2\x80\x99s_paradise_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Puerto_Rico:_A_retailer\xe2\x80\x99s_paradise-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In early October 2015, <a href="/wiki/Walmart" title="Walmart">Walmart</a> left the mall for a Supercenter.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2017, the <a href="/wiki/DDR_Corp." class="mw-redirect" title="DDR Corp.">DDR Corp.</a> would spin off its Puerto Rican shopping centers to RVI (<a href="/wiki/SITE_Centers" title="SITE Centers">Retail Value Inc.</a>) due to struggles they had after the <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a>, making <a href="/wiki/SITE_Centers" title="SITE Centers">Retail Value Inc.</a> the new owner of the mall at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In November 2019, with an investment that exceeded $3 million, the first Econo supermarket in the municipality of Hatillo was opened at Plaza del Norte in the majority of the space of the former <a href="/wiki/Walmart" title="Walmart">Walmart</a> store.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On October 30, 2020, a new <a href="/wiki/Burlington_(department_store)" title="Burlington (department
