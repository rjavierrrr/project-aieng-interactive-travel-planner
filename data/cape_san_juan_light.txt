class="infobox-data" style="border: 0;">October 22, 1981</td></tr><tr style="display:none"><td colspan="2">\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Cape San Juan Light</b> (<i>Faro de Las Cabezas de San Juan</i>) is a historic <a href="/wiki/Lighthouse" title="Lighthouse">lighthouse</a> located on the northeastern part of the highest point of Cape San Juan in <a href="/wiki/Fajardo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Fajardo, Puerto Rico">Fajardo, Puerto Rico</a>. The lighthouse was constructed in 1880 and was officially lit on May 2, 1882. The original illuminating apparatus, not changed until after 1898, had an 18-mile (29&#160;km) range and displayed a fixed white light which every three minutes flashed red.\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Cape_San_Juan_Light.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Cape_San_Juan_Light.jpg/220px-Cape_San_Juan_Light.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="152" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/ea/Cape_San_Juan_Light.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="250" data-file-height="173" /></a><figcaption>Front of the lighthouse</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The lighthouse owned by the <a href="/wiki/Conservation_Trust_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Conservation Trust of Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico Conservation Trust</a> is part of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Las_Cabezas_de_San_Juan_(Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Las Cabezas de San Juan (Puerto Rico) (page does not exist)">Las Cabezas de San Juan Nature Reserve</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The  316-acre (128&#160;ha) reserve includes a <a href="/wiki/Bioluminescence" title="Bioluminescence">bioluminescence</a> bay, rare flora and fauna, various trails and boardwalks, and a scientific research center. Despite its small size, the reserve shelters seven different ecological systems, including beaches, lagoons, dry forest, coral reefs and mangroves.\n</p><p>In 1898, the lighthouse played a major role in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Fajardo" title="Battle of Fajardo">Battle of Fajardo</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_Campaign" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican Campaign">Puerto Rican Campaign</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a>. The lighthouse was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_government" class="mw-redirect" title="United States government">United States government</a> on October 22, 1981, and on the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Register_of_Historic_Sites_and_Zones" title="Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones">Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones</a> in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2001, under the <a href="/wiki/National_Historic_Lighthouse_Preservation_Act" title="National Historic Lighthouse Preservation Act">National Historic Lighthouse Preservation Act</a>, it became the first lighthouse to be transferred to a non-governmental organization in Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-Interior_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Interior-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Gallery">Gallery</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
