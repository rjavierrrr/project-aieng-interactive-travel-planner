shopping mall located in <a href="/wiki/Guaynabo" class="mw-redirect" title="Guaynabo">Guaynabo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The mall is located at the intersection of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_2" title="Puerto Rico Highway 2">PR-2</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_23" title="Puerto Rico Highway 23">PR-23</a>, and <a href="/wiki/PR-20" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-20">PR-20</a>, and has over 120 stores with its anchors being <a href="/wiki/T.J._Maxx" class="mw-redirect" title="T.J. Maxx">T.J. Maxx</a>, Capri, <a href="/wiki/Walgreens" title="Walgreens">Walgreens</a>, <a href="/wiki/PetSmart" title="PetSmart">PetSmart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Cinemas" title="Caribbean Cinemas">Caribbean Cinemas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Office_Depot" title="Office Depot">Office Depot</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Burlington_(department_store)" title="Burlington (department store)">Burlington</a>, plus an outdoor shopping area known as "Liberty Square".\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Patricio_Plaza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Origins">Origins</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Patricio_Plaza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>During the Spanish Colonial period, Tom\xc3\xa1s O\'Daly was granted land in the vicinity of Guaynabo and O\'Daly developed it into a thriving sugar hacienda. O\'Daly and fellow Irishman Miguel Kirwan became business partners in the "Hacienda San Patricio" which they named after the chief patron saint of Ireland, <a href="/wiki/Saint_Patrick" title="Saint Patrick">Saint Patrick</a>. The plantation no longer exists. The land on which it was located is now a suburb called San Patricio with a shopping mall, San Patricio Plaza.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Development_and_opening:_1960s">Development and opening: 1960s</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Patricio_Plaza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development and opening: 1960s"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>On December 14, 1963, while construction started in November 1963, it was officially announced by the president of San Patricio Shopping Center, Inc., James J. Wilson, that arrangements had been finalized for the $3,000,000 financing of the shopping center. Short-term financing for the project was provided by <a href="/wiki/Banco_Popular_(Puerto_Rico)" class="mw-redirect" title="Banco Popular (Puerto Rico)">Banco Popular</a> while the State Mutual of America Insurance Company of America was in charge of long-term financing. The shopping center, being located at the corner of San Patricio and F. D. Roosevelt avenues, would be planned to have several establishments. Among those establishments there would be: a <a href="/wiki/Pueblo_Supermarkets" title="Pueblo Supermarkets">Pueblo supermarket</a>, a <a href="/wiki/New_York_Department_Stores" title="New York Department Stores">New York Department Stores</a>; a <a href="/wiki/Kmart" title="Kmart">Kmart discount department store</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Banco_Popular_(Puerto_Rico)" class="mw-redirect" title="Banco Popular (Puerto Rico)">Banco Popular</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Thom_McAn" title="Thom McAn">Thom McAn shoe store</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Walgreens" title="Walgreens">Walgreens</a>, a <a href="/wiki/New_York_%26_Company" title="New York &amp; Company">Lerner Shops</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Chalet" title="Swiss Chalet">Swiss Chalet restaurant</a>. Pueblo, Kmart, and New York Department Stores, were planned to be the first to open.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On August 14, 1964, the eleventh Pueblo supermarket inaugurated at the shopping center. It was said to be the biggest location in the chain at the time, being 16,000 square feet.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On October 21, 1964, the Kmart inaugurated at the shopping center with a 69,000 square foot store. It was the chains first store out of the continental North America.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\nOn November 17, 1964, <a href="/wiki/New_York_%26_Company" title="New York &amp; Company">Lerner Shops</a> inaugurated at the shopping center, this store joined the 337 stores the chain had in Puerto Rico and the United States at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On November 27, 1964, the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Department_Stores" title="New York Department Stores">New York Department Stores</a> inaugurated at the shopping center.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:San_Patricio_Plaza_aerial.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/San_Patricio_Plaza_aerial.jpg/220px-San_Patricio_Plaza_aerial.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="184" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/San_Patricio_Plaza_aerial.jpg/330px-San_Patricio_Plaza_aerial.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/San_Patricio_Plaza_aerial.jpg/440px-San_Patricio_Plaza_aerial.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2828" data-file-height="2370" /></a><figcaption>Aerial of the shopping center in 1964</figcaption></figure>\n<p>On December 3, 1964, developed by Interstate General Corporation, with Theodore Berenson administrating the shopping center, San Patricio Plaza was officially inaugurated. It opened with 16 establishments, 250 square feet of retail space, and parking for 1,500 vehicles. It was anchored by <a href="/wiki/Kmart" title="Kmart">Kmart</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_Department_Stores" title="New York Department Stores">New York Department Stores</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pueblo_Supermarkets" title="Pueblo Supermarkets">Pueblo supermarket</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Walgreens" title="Walgreens">Walgreens</a>. Additionally out of those 16 establishments it had places such as <a href="/wiki/New_York_%26_Company" title="New York &amp; Company">Lerner Shops</a>, Giusti, <a href="/wiki/Thom_McAn" title="Thom McAn">Thom McAn</a>, Clubman, Rudes Decorators, <a href="/wiki/Zale_Corporation" title="Zale Corporation">Gordon\'s Jewelers</a>, Bithorn Travel Agency, Park Avenue Gift Shop, Metalfraft Furniture Store, San Patricio Sports, and others. The cost of the entire shopping center exceeded $8 million, it was designed by architect Summer Scheim. The 20-acre estate where the building was located belonged to the Gonzalez Giusti family, one of whose members, Mr. William Preston Giusti, was vice president of the San Patricio Plaza Association. <a 1970s-1980s"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In the 1970s, taking into account the economic conditions of the period in terms of high interest rates and oil costs, Interstate General Corporation decided to sell certain properties, including San Patricio Plaza, which was sold to the Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Giusti family.<sup id="cite_ref-SanPatricio.com_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SanPatricio.com-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On June 18, 1977, a <a href="/wiki/Baskin-Robbins" title="Baskin-Robbins">Baskin Robbins</a> opened a location on the mall premises next to the then Felicilandia amusement center.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In December 1978, the mall had establishments such as La Cobacha, Caribbean Music, Super Pet Center, Carrousel, Parklane Hosiery, Kress, Amado Beauty Sal\xc3\xb3n, Candyland, and others.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1982, the Felicilandia amusement center located on the mall premises would receive $1,000,000 renovations.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This same amusement center would later on close in the mid-1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In id="Galer.C3.ADa_San_Patricio_and_expansions:_1990s-2000s"></span>Galer\xc3\xada San Patricio and expansions: 1990s-2000s</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Patricio_Plaza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Galer\xc3\xada San Patricio and expansions: 1990s-2000s"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In early 1991, Galer\xc3\xada San Patricio shopping center located on Calle Tabonuco in Guaynabo was built at an investment of 1.1 million dollars. The first phase of this project, consisted of eight stores that had an established clientele, including Sterling House, Kitchen Craft, and Marisol Freyre. The first level was dedicated to shops and the second level provided office spaces. The construction of this shopping center was carried out by remodeling of an old warehouse located behind the famous Felicilandia amusement park and was carried out by the firm Carrer\xc3\xa1 Arquitectos.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1994, the first expansion of Galer\xc3\xada San Patricio was completed, this included the two floors of the wing where Uno Chicago Grill was located. With the creation of this new area, and in addition to Uno, came four additional stores, among them the then existing \xe2\x80\x9cBath, Dreams, And\xe2\x80\xa6\xe2\x80\x9d store.<sup id="cite_ref-sanpatricio.com_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sanpatricio.com-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1994, the mall changed its logo once again, the mall was given a $43 million dollar renovation and expansion, which included the addition of a food court which opened the same year. The mall\'s first <a href="/wiki/St._Patrick%27s_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Patrick&#39;s Day">St. Patrick\'s Day</a> celebrations would be held in March 1995, which would introduce the mall\xe2\x80\x99s "Patrick" mascot character. A <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Cinemas" title="Caribbean Cinemas">Caribbean Cinemas</a> location would open in 1997. \n</p><p>On June 21, 2001, it was reported that San Patricio Plaza had announced it would begin that January a $60 million third phase of its then ongoing expansion that included a 10.5-story, 150,000 square foot office building, a 25,000 square foot retail strip, and a multilevel parking area for 1,100 vehicles.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2003, another expansion phase of Galer\xc3\xada San Patricio began, which was completed later that next year which connected the Santander Tower to the San Patricio lobby. In addition to having become a much larger, beautiful and welcoming place, this new remodeling added space for the restaurants, shops, jewelry stores and personal care salons, which were located there.<sup id="cite_ref-sanpatricio.com_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sanpatricio.com-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On November 4, 2003, Bed Bath &amp; Beyond inaugurated their store at the mall, the space counting with 41,000 square feet with two levels. This construction had an investment of $7.5 million, of which was to be financed by Banco Popular.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It closed in June 2023 after the company declared bankruptcy alongside its other stores.\n</p><p>In 2008, a <a href="/wiki/Circuit_City" title="Circuit City">Circuit City</a> store opened at the mall, being the first of the chain on the island. The store, which opened with 28,000 square feet of space, was located in the area of the east facade, where the then new <a href="/wiki/Walgreens" title="Walgreens">Walgreens</a> pharmacy was located. It later closed in early 2009 after it declared bankruptcy.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="2010s,_and_on"><span id="2010s.2C_and_on"></span>2010s, and on</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Patricio_Plaza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010s, and on"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 2010, a <a href="/wiki/TJ_Maxx" title="TJ Maxx">TJ Maxx</a> would come to fill the 28,000 square feet of space left by <a href="/wiki/Circuit_City" title="Circuit City">Circuit City</a>. <a href="/wiki/PetSmart" title="PetSmart">PetSmart</a>  would also open a 24,000 square foot location at the mall this year.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On August 28, 2012, San Patricio Plaza announced a renovation of its Food Court, pedestrian bridge and multifloor parking. By January 2013, the remodelation was completed.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On October 22, 2014, the mall changed its logo again for its 50th anniversary. The entire San Patricio Village was also given a repainting that began in early 2014, replacing the old beige, pink and green color scheme that had been used since 1994, with a new green, yellow and white color scheme. The rest of the mall\'s 2nd floor, that had remained un remodeled in 2012, was remodeled as well.\n</p><p>In 2014, the mall celebrated its 50th anniversary with an exhibit about its history, and a time capsule which contains items from the mall\'s first 50 years that will open in 2064. A replica of the mall\'s original sign was created for the exhibition, which was later moved to the pedestrian bridge in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The exhibit partially closed in 2015. Visitors could still see the history showcase until 2018, when the remains of the exhibit were removed in favor of a larger <a href="/wiki/AT%26T" title="AT&amp;T">AT&amp;T</a> location.\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:San_Patricio_Guaynabo.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/San_Patricio_Guaynabo.jpg/220px-San_Patricio_Guaynabo.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="220" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/San_Patricio_Guaynabo.jpg/330px-San_Patricio_Guaynabo.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/San_Patricio_Guaynabo.jpg/440px-San_Patricio_Guaynabo.jpg 2x" data-file-width="960" data-file-height="960" /></a><figcaption>Area around San Patricio Mall in 2015</figcaption></figure>\n<p>On January 12, 2017, it was announced that the mall would undergo a $4 million renovation in order to look more modern. It included a new fountain, a new central atrium, new stairs, replacement of flooring and ceiling, exterior improvements, and more. The six ad banners on the fountain area were also removed; and the pink elevator in front of the fountain was demolished. The project officially began on January 17, 2017. The interior was originally expected to be completed in November 2017; however it was completed in March 2018 due delays caused by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In February 2017, the Santander Tower was converted into the <a href="/wiki/T-Mobile_US" title="T-Mobile US">T-Mobile</a> Center, following the closure of the former and T-Mobile moving their main headquarters in Puerto Rico from Hato Rey.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In February 2018, the mall started a campaign promoting their then-newly renovated image. A reinauguration ceremony was held in April 2018. The exterior improvements began in August 2017 and were completed in November 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 2018, the Caribbean Cinemas location closed for a renovation. The details about the upcoming location were announced in October. It was originally expected to reopen in December 2018, but it actually opened in February 2019. The project has been repeated with different editions in October of every year since then.\n</p><p>The mall\'s <a href="/wiki/Kmart_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kmart (United States)">Kmart</a> store closed in February 2019. On December 5, 2019,<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> it was announced that Kmart\'s former space would be remodeled into an outdoor shopping area called "The Square at San Patricio Plaza", with an investment of $12 million.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Construction was expected to begin in early 2020 and would have been completed in late 2020, but due to the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_Puerto_Rico" title="COVID-19 pandemic in Puerto Rico">COVID-19 pandemic</a>, this project was postponed for a year. Several enhancements would be made to the Village as well.\n</p><p>On October 20, 2020, it was announced that the mall\'s Village would undergo an enhancement project and would be made in collaboration with Guaynabo\'s government.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Construction on the \xe2\x80\x9cThe Square\xe2\x80\x9d area officially began in September 2021 after being delayed by a year due to the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>, following the former Kmart building\'s demolition which began in May.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In June 2023, it was announced that construction on the Square area was almost completed and the first tenants would open during the coming months. It was also announced that the area would be renamed "Liberty Square" as <a href="/wiki/Liberty_Puerto_Rico" title="Liberty Puerto Rico">Liberty Puerto Rico</a> would be sponsoring it.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \nIt was also announced on June 5, 2023, that a <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s" title="McDonald&#39;s">McDonald\'s</a> would open in the former site of <a href="/wiki/Pizza_Hut" title="Pizza Hut">Pizza Hut</a> by the end of 2023 or  Spring of 2024. Beyond">Bed Bath &amp; Beyond</a> location would be replaced by <a href="/wiki/Burlington_(department_store)" title="Burlington (department store)">Burlington</a> in 2024.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>42<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The store was inaugurated on June 21, 2024.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>44<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Felicilandia">Felicilandia</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Patricio_Plaza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Felicilandia"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Opened in 1972, Felicilandia, an amusement park, was located on the grounds of the San Patricio Plaza for about two decades.<sup id="cite_ref-PH_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PH-45"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>45<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Miami_News_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Miami_News-46"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>46<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1991, a storage building behind Felicilandia was bought by the owners of San Patricio Plaza and converted into another part of the mall, a two-story building right by the amusement park which added eight stores and offices to the mall.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>47<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Felicilandia was a year-round attraction which remained open for two decades. When it closed in the mid-1990s, some of the amusement rides were relocated for use elsewhere.<sup id="cite_ref-PH_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PH-45"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>45<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>An exhibit at the San Patricio Plaza created in 2014 and closed in 2018 included pictures and a little history of when Felicilandia was there.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>48<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Located in Guaynabo, a highly populated municipality, Felicilandia is a place steeped in <a href="/wiki/Nostalgia" title="Nostalgia">nostalgia</a> for some residents of Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_nostalgia_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_nostalgia-49"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>49<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-El_Nuevo_D\xc3\xada_2014_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-El_Nuevo_D\xc3\xada_2014-50"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>50<span
