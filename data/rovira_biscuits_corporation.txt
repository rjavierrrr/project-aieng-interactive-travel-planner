Corporation</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Rovira Biscuits Corporation</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican people">Puerto Rican</a> <a href="/wiki/Corporation" title="Corporation">corporation</a> that manufactures <a href="/wiki/Cracker_(food)" title="Cracker (food)">crackers</a> branded by the same name.  The company is well known for its production of <i>"export sodas"</i> crackers.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The company\'s headquarters are located in Urbanizacion La Ceiba, barrio <a href="/wiki/San_Anton" class="mw-redirect" title="San Anton">San Anton</a>, in the city of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>, and its main production facilities are located on <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_10" title="Puerto Rico Highway 10">PR-10</a> southbound, in barrio <a href="/wiki/Sabanetas_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sabanetas (Ponce)">Sabanetas</a>, just south of the intersection with <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_52" title="Puerto Rico Highway 52">PR-52</a> exit in Ponce, Puerto Rico.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 Rico</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The company began in Puerto Rico in 1929 when company founder and <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> immigrant<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Jos\xc3\xa9 Mar\xc3\xada Rovira Viza purchased a small bakery on calle Villa, in <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">downtown</a> Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  In 1937, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Miguel_Rovira" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Miguel Rovira">Jos\xc3\xa9 Miguel Rovira</a>, Jos\xc3\xa9 Mar\xc3\xada Rovira\xe2\x80\x99s son, joined the Company and started the transformation into a crackers manufacturer. During the next decade, and throughout the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, the company expanded its product offerings and facilities.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1947, the company became a legally registered corporation in Puerto Rico under the name of <i>Rovira Biscuit Corporation</i> and, in the same year,  it built new manufacturing facilities in Ponce.  In 1952 it moved from its original location in downtown Ponce to the <i>Cuatro Calles</i> sector, near Avenida Las Americas. The new facility provided 50,000 square feet of space.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In the early 1950s, further growth allowed the company to start marketing its products to the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">East Coast of the United States</a>, to satisfy the demand of the large number of Puerto Ricans who had started to migrate there in those years. This was followed by expanding its market to the Caribbean area. In the 1960s, the company added new technology, marketing plans, and management turning "Rovira" into a household name among consumers in Puerto Rico. Also in the 1960s, the company expanded its marketing into areas in the United States beyond the East Coast.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In the 2000s a company spinoff, Rovira Foods, Inc., was established to respond to the growing sales and distribution business in Puerto Rico. It represents the "Rovira" crackers and "Eric\xe2\x80\x99s" snacks brand names in addition to over 10 different product brands in the United States, Spain and <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. From this point forward, Rovira Biscuits Corporation, became the manufacturing arm of Rovira Foods, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2009, the company invested $25 million in a new 100,000 square feet corporate facility in the Mercedita sector of Barrio <a href="/wiki/Vayas" class="mw-redirect" title="Vayas">Vayas</a> in Ponce. The company had been operating from a facility one-third that size located in the Cuatro Calles sector of barrio <a href="/wiki/San_Ant%C3%B3n" title="San Ant\xc3\xb3n">San Ant\xc3\xb3n</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The new facility operates 24 hours a day.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Expansion">Expansion</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rovira_Biscuits_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Expansion"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In addition to its new facility near the intersection of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_52" title="Puerto Rico Highway 52">PR-52</a> and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_10" title="Puerto Rico Highway 10">PR-10</a>, Rovira Biscuit Corporation now also operates out of a new facility located on the outskirts of Ponce, close to <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_2" title="Puerto Rico Highway 2">PR-2</a>. This move is intended to "improve efficiency and production capacity to solidify [the company\'s] position as the most important cracker manufacturer in Puerto Rico."<sup id="cite_ref-19"
