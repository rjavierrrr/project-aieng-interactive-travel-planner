/></a><figcaption>Interior view of the institution\'s archaeological laboratory.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The <b>Center for Advanced Studies on Puerto Rico and the Caribbean</b> (Spanish: <i>Centro de Estudios Avanzados de Puerto Rico y El Caribe</i> or simply CEAPRC) is a private institute housed in the former <a href="/wiki/San_Ildefonso_Conciliar_Seminary_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="San Ildefonso Conciliar Seminary of Puerto Rico">San Ildefonso Conciliar Seminary</a> in <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">Old San Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> that offers <a href="/wiki/Graduate_school" class="mw-redirect" title="Graduate school">graduate studies</a> in <a href="/wiki/Arts" class="mw-redirect" title="Arts">arts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">philosophy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The center is currently <a href="/wiki/School_accreditation" class="mw-redirect" title="School accreditation">accredited</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Middle_States_Association_of_Colleges_and_Schools" title="Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools">Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools</a> and it publishes La <i>Revista del Centro de Estudios Avanzados de Puerto Rico y el Caribe</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Center_for_Advanced_Studies_on_Puerto_Rico_and_the_Caribbean&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Under_Ricardo_Alegr\xc3\xada"><span id="Under_Ricardo_Alegr.C3.ADa"></span>Under Ricardo Alegr\xc3\xada</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Center_for_Advanced_Studies_on_Puerto_Rico_and_the_Caribbean&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Under Ricardo Alegr\xc3\xada"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>It was incorporated on February 28, 1968, by <a href="/wiki/Pablo_Casals" title="Pablo Casals">Pablo Casals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Mar%C3%ADn" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn</a>, Roberto Bus\xc3\xb3 Carrasquillo, and <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Benitez" class="mw-redirect" title="Jaime Benitez">Jaime Ben\xc3\xadtez</a>. However, it remained inactive and without organization until 1976, when <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Alegr%C3%ADa" title="Ricardo Alegr\xc3\xada">Ricardo Alegr\xc3\xada</a> made a request before the <a href="/wiki/Council_on_Higher_Education_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Council on Higher Education of Puerto Rico">Council on Higher Education of Puerto Rico</a> to transform the center into an academic institution.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Seminario_Conciliar_de_San_Ildefonso_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Seminario_Conciliar_de_San_Ildefonso_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg/220px-Seminario_Conciliar_de_San_Ildefonso_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="154" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Seminario_Conciliar_de_San_Ildefonso_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg/330px-Seminario_Conciliar_de_San_Ildefonso_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Seminario_Conciliar_de_San_Ildefonso_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg/440px-Seminario_Conciliar_de_San_Ildefonso_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2670" data-file-height="1868" /></a><figcaption>The interior courtyard of the Center for Advanced Studies on Puerto Rico and the Caribbean.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>With the CEAPRC, Alegr\xc3\xada intended for a small institution that possessed an intimate environment, with intellectual growth and the promotion of a region-wide Caribbean emphasis as its focus.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez370_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez370-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In her 2002 book, Carmen Dolores Hern\xc3\xa1ndez credited the CEAPRC (which she labels an "experimental" institution "unparalleled in the island or the Antilles") with breaking away from the "cultural euro centrism" of higher education in Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez382_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez382-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The curriculum was intended to be different from that of the universities.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez371_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez371-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Puerto Rico and the Antilles received a central role with historic archeology, local literature and history in the spotlight. Besides numerous local sites, archeological digs were also held by students in the Dominican Republic and Cuba. Foreign professors such as Irving Rouse, Peter Roe, Mario Veloz Maggiolo, Sally Price and Lourdes Dom\xc3\xadnguez also collaborated with the CEAPRC. Students were allowed to take courses from other universities.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez372_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez372-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> On at least one occasion, a course was added due to student initiative.\n</p><p>The institution has had collaboration agreements with Universidad San Carlos (Guatemala), Universidad Aut\xc3\xb3noma (Dominican Republic, which included joint work with professors Jos\xc3\xa9 Alc\xc3\xa1ntara Alm\xc3\xa1nzar, Pedro Verg\xc3\xa9s, Carlos And\xc3\xbajar and Marcio Veloz Maggiolo) and Universidad de Veracruz (Mexico).<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez375_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez375-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The classes of Puerto Rican Literature and History, Puerto Rican Society, Puerto Rican the Caribbean during the XX Century and Puerto Rican Culture were mandatory, while several electives involving literature, cinema, racial studies, law, Arts and other practices were offered.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez406_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez406-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\nThe CEAPRC\'s faculty worked on a contractual basis, allowing a revolving door of professors to come and go.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez405_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez405-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> During its early years it grew to include figures like Arturo Morales Carri\xc3\xb3n, Jos\xc3\xa9 Arsenio Torres, Juan Rodr\xc3\xadguez Cruz, Eugenio Fern\xc3\xa1ndez M\xc3\xa9ndez, Luis D\xc3\xadaz Soler, Jos\xc3\xa9 Ram\xc3\xb3n de la Torre, Mar\xc3\xada Teresa Bab\xc3\xadn, Carmelo Rosario Natal, Carmen M. Ramos de Santiago, Francisco L\xc3\xb3pez Cruz, Lidio Cruz Monclova, Andr\xc3\xa9s S\xc3\xa1nchez Tarniella, Ernesto Jaime Ru\xc3\xadz de la Mata\xe2\x80\x94a member of the short-lived surrealist group <a href="/wiki/El_Mirador_Azul" title="El Mirador Azul">El Mirador Azul</a>, Edgar Mart\xc3\xadnez Masdeu, Gervasio Garc\xc3\xada, Arturo Santana, H\xc3\xa9ctor Campos Parsi,  Luis Nieves Falc\xc3\xb3n and Esther Mel\xc3\xb3n.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez405_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez405-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Local figures such as Mela Pons (Alegr\xc3\xada\'s wife) and Juan Antonio Corretjer, were involved in hosting conferences.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez406_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez406-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Alegr\xc3\xada himself taught the Culture of Puerto Rico course for decades. His work with the CEAPRC reached foreign academics such as \xc3\x81ngel Rama, who on August 10, 1983, noted in a letter that it had been a topic of discussion in a reunion held between him and Maldonado Denia at Paris.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez413_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez413-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> During Alegr\xc3\xada\'s tenure as director, the institution had an "open doors" policy.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez480_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez480-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Among those that the CEAPRC has hosted is a chief of the Soshone tribe.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez380_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez380-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Collaboration with foreign institutions brought the likes of Irving Rouse (Yale), Douglas Newton (Metropolitan Museum), Gordon Willey (Harvard), Jean-Batiste Romain (State University of Haiti), George Kubler (Yale), Nina S. de Friedman (Instituto de Antropolog\xc3\xada de Colombia), Eduardo Arcila Far\xc3\xadas (Colegio de M\xc3\xa9xico), William Bascom (University of California), Manuel Moreno Fraginals (Universidad de la Havana), Wande Abimbola (University of Ife), Carlos Rama (Universidad de Barcelona) and Manuel Garc\xc3\xada Ar\xc3\xa9valo (Universidad Nacional de Santo Domingo).<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez406_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez406-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez407_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez407-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Since then, \xc3\x81ngel L\xc3\xb3pez Cant\xc3\xb3s, Antonio Lorente, Pedro Verg\xc3\xa9s, Lourdes Dom\xc3\xadnguez, Lorenzo Rubio, Jes\xc3\xbas Urrea, Juan Tena Ibarra, Demetrio Ramos, Jorge Febles and Lucio Mijares P\xc3\xa9rez, among others have served as invited professors.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez406_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez406-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The CEAPRC created the slideshows <i>El arte de los abor\xc3\xadgenes de Puerto Rico</i>, <i>Las Artes populares de Puerto Rico</i>, <i>La catedral de San Juan</i>, <i>La Capilla del Santo Cristo de la Salud</i>, <i>La Iglesia de San Jos\xc3\xa9</i>, <i>El pintor Jos\xc3\xa9 Campeche</i> and <i>El pintor Francisco Oller Cestero</i> and the short films <i>La vida de Cristo seg\xc3\xban el santero Florencio Cab\xc3\xa1n</i> (directed by Am\xc3\xadlcar Tirado, winner of the 1979 Golden Eagle Award of the Non-Theatrical Event Council (Spanish: Consejo sobre Eventos Internacionales no Teatrales), <i>Don Tom\xc3\xa1s, el alfarero de Las Piedras</i>, <i>Juan Alindato y las m\xc3\xa1scaras de Ponce</i>, <i>Las tejedoras de sombreros de paja de Cabo Rojo</i>, <i>Cuando llegaron los indios de Boriquen</i>, <i>La cester\xc3\xada en Puerto Rico</i>, <i>El Gag\xc3\xa1 de Simil\xc3\xa1</i>, <i>Vud\xc3\xba en la Rep\xc3\xbablica Dominicana</i>, <i>Serie de Maestros Artesanos</i> (Jos\xc3\xa9 Arsenio Torres) are also part of its collection.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez408_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez408-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> All mentions of the Antillean natives by foreign historians were compiled, taking 25 years to complete in the <i>Cr\xc3\xb3nicas de los indios de las Antillas</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez376_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez376-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\nDuring the 1976\xe2\x80\x9380 years the ICP grew politicized, while the CEAPRC focused on Puerto Rican culture.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez370_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez370-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1978, the CEAC entered alliances with Temple University and the Universidad de Valladolid.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez374_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez374-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1981, Alegr\xc3\xada proposed the creation of a student magazine, <i>Caribe</i>, published on a biannual basis.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez375_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez375-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> That same year, the Catholic Church sponsored a course about its history at Puerto Rico, C\xc3\xa1tedra Juan Alejo Arizmendi.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez373_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez373-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1982, Alegr\xc3\xada established an exchange collaboration involving the Academia de Ciencias of Cuba.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez396_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez396-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  Since the CEAPRC had its own editorial, it published the work of figures like Arturo Morales Carri\xc3\xb3n, some of the master\'s thesis and historic documents.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez375_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez375-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Through it Alegr\xc3\xada  published <i>La vida de Jesucristo seg\xc3\xban el santero puertorrique\xc3\xb1o Florencio Cab\xc3\xa1n</i> and <i>Primeras representaciones gr\xc3\xa1ficas del indio americano (1493\xe2\x80\x931523)</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez407_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez407-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Other publications include Osiris Delgado\'s <i>El pintor Francisco Oller y Cestero</i>, F\xc3\xa9lix Ojeda Reyes\' <i>La manigua en Par\xc3\xads: correspondencia diplom\xc3\xa1tica de Ram\xc3\xb3n E. Betances</i> and Flor Pi\xc3\xb1ero de Rivera\'s <i>Arturo Schomburg: un puertorrique\xc3\xb1o descubre el legado hist\xc3\xb3rico del negro</i>.\n</p><p>The loan of Casa Blanca by the ICP was of only $1 per year.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez378_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez378-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> As the agreement was about to end, rumors circled that the Romero Barcel\xc3\xb3 administration intended to close down the CEAPRC, opposing the frequent presence of political opponents within the premises.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez377_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez377-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The institution had already face budge cuts when the new administration of the ICP objected to give it $6,000 from the Fundaci\xc3\xb3n Nacional de las Artes.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez378_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez378-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Alegr\xc3\xada, however, warned Rafael Rivera Garc\xc3\xada of the Office of Cultural Affairs that he would have to be ousted from Casa Blanca by the police, but ultimately decided to move  after new ICP director <a href="/wiki/Leticia_del_Rosario" title="Leticia del Rosario">Leticia del Rosario</a> intended to raise the loan to recover the $6,000. Having participated in the restoration of the San Jos\xc3\xa9 Church since 1977, Alegr\xc3\xada negotiated the abandoned Seminario Conciliar building for the CEAPRC from cardinal Luis Aponte Mart\xc3\xadnez, offering to restore it as well (in exchange, the institution didn\'t have to pay loan for some years, the specific quantity later being revised by a Church appraiser).<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez379_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez379-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The estimate of the project was $4,000,000 but Alegr\xc3\xada recycled materials from other sources and completed it for $600,000.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez380_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez380-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> After the process was completed in November 1986, the CEAPRC moved to the building. The CEAPRC also received donations from citizens for particular uses.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez404_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez404-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1982, it received recognition by the Middle States Association, which was suspicious about the $650 salary that the director (Alegr\xc3\xada) was receiving since it was improbable that his successor would agree to that sum.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez370_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez370-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Despite this, Alegr\xc3\xada considered the intervention of the federal entity counterintuitive given the topic of study, but allowed it so that the students could receive additional benefits.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez405_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez405-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The restoration of the Seminario Conciliar began in 1984.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez379_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez379-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> After moving to its new base, the Aula Magna was used for cultural activities.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez374_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez374-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> A program of public conferences on Puerto Rican and Caribbean topics that was opened also held its events there.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez373_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez373-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The old refectory was also converted into an exhibition room.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez377_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez377-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The classrooms were named after figures like <a href="/wiki/Eugenio_Mar%C3%ADa_de_Hostos" title="Eugenio Mar\xc3\xada de Hostos">Eugenio Mar\xc3\xada de Hostos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Baldorioty_de_Castro" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Baldorioty de Castro">Roman Baldorioty de Castro</a>, Jos\xc3\xa9 Campeche, Jos\xc3\xa9 Juli\xc3\xa1n Acosta and Alejandro Tapia.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez381_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez381-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1985, Alegr\xc3\xada began publishing <i>La Revista del Centro de Estudios Avanzados de Puerto Rico y el Caribe</i>, emphasizing an academic approach.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez375_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez375-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The CEAPRC also produced slideshows in collaboration with the Fundaci\xc3\xb3n Puertorrique\xc3\xb1a de las Humanidades for educational use.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez376_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez376-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> By the early 2000s, nine documentaries had been completed.\nA Middle States evaluation team reviewed the status in 1987, considering the CEAPRC an "institution with [...] a well-defined view of itself" when comparing it to other Universities and colleges.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez405_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez405-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In July 1987, the CEAC collaborated with the Puerto Rican Cultural Heritage House of New York in a course offered from teachers from the diaspora.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez407_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez407-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> When interviewed Alegr\xc3\xada considered his work at the CEAPRC a continuation of his previous work at the ICP, intending to create the professionals that were needed in the kind of projects that were undertaken there.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez381_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez381-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In May 1987, a restored chapel was inaugurated within the premises of the CEAPRC.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez410_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez410-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1989, Alegr\xc3\xada loaned several pieces of his collection for an Antillean masks exhibit.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez377_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez377-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1990 Arturo Schomburg and Casa de las Am\xc3\xa9ricas exhibits were organized.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez377_23-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez377-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1991, the institution began offering its first doctorate degree.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez370_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez370-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Exhibitions on the archeological work of Miguel Rodr\xc3\xadguez and Cuban slavery was also hosted.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez377_23-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez377-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1992, Puerto Rican engravings and several books from the Guillermo Esteves Volckers collection on the topic of Christopher Columbus were exhibited.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez377_23-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez377-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Museo de las Am\xc3\xa9ricas, inaugurated during this year, was viewed by Alegr\xc3\xada as the culmination of his life\'s work, bringing together several past initiatives.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez444_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez444-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1993, the XV International Archeology Congress was held at Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez371_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez371-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Exhibits on Caribbean archeology and local academic magazines were also held.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez377_23-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez377-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In June 1993, budget cuts by the Pedro Rossell\xc3\xb3 administration ended a yearly legislative donation of $100,000 when the House of Representatives eliminated it after Carlos Juan L\xc3\xb3pez Nieves criticized that Alegr\xc3\xada was personally involved with the CEAPRC and it had "become [his] business".<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez410_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez410-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The director didn\'t take the claims lightly. The following month, the Senate of Puerto Rico restored $63,000 of the total sum. In 1994, exhibits were held on the topics of Pablo de la Torriente Brau and Puerto Rican children\'s literature.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez408_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez408-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1995, the CEAPRC entered a collaboration with the <a href="/wiki/Universidad_de_Sevilla" class="mw-redirect" title="Universidad de Sevilla">Universidad de Sevilla</a> to offer a joint doctorate in American History.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez374_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez374-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1996, Alegr\xc3\xada and Pons loaned several pieces from their Talla de Santos woodwork collection to the Museo de las Am\xc3\xa9ricas, as part of the institutions second permanent exhibit.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez447_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez447-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Between 1997 and 1998, the municipalities of Caguas and San Juan offered grants to students.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez373_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez373-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> On the 100th anniversary of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a>, an exhibit on life before 1898 was held.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez377_23-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez377-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1997, the government eliminated the recurrent UPR donation to the CEAPRC.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez382_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez382-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The following year, the UPR made additional cuts in the collaboration for publications, investigations and visiting professors. In response, Alegr\xc3\xada auctioned pieces from his private collection to gather funds, gathering $40,000. In 1999, students from the CEAPRC participated in excavations at Havana.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez406_11-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez406-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Entering the new millennium, efforts were made to establish more links with the Dominican Republic and Cuba in the form of exchange programs.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez382_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez382-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> For Alegr\xc3\xada, this was a cultural response to the concept of an Antillean Confederation. By this time, the CEAPRC\'s library had grown to include 14,000 books and hosted the Guillermo Esteves Volckers, Rosa Estades and Monelisa P\xc3\xa9rez Marchand collections, as well as audiovisual content and documentation.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez404_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez404-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Some effort to modernize the institution\'s classic work took place with the digitalization of the he <i>Cr\xc3\xb3nicas de los indios de las Antillas</i> initiative.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez376_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez376-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Alegr\xc3\xada left his role in the CEAPRC in 2001, being replaced by Juan Manuel Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Lamela as director and Juan Rodr\xc3\xadguez Cancel as Dean of Academic Affairs.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez406_11-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez406-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The institution had an endowment  fund of $2,250,000 at the moment.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez466_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez466-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> By this time, figures like Juan Manuel Garc\xc3\xada Passalaqua, Enrique Laguerre, Luis De la Rosa, Guillermo Baralt, Blanca Silvestrini, Jos\xc3\xa9 Ferrer Canales, Ana Sagard\xc3\xada, Federico Acevedo, Ram\xc3\xb3n Luis Acevedo, Carmen Isabel Raffuci, Antonio J. Gonz\xc3\xa1lez and Silvia \xc3\x81lvarez Curbelo had served as professors in the CEAPRC.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez406_11-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez406-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In April 2001, the government promised an improvement of $100,000 in the assignment to the institution to commemorate Alegr\xc3\xada\'s 80th birthday.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez410_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez410-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The institution was one of 30 that joined in the celebrations surrounding this commemoration.<sup id="cite_ref-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez471_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hern\xc3\xa1ndez471-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Notable_members">Notable members</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Center_for_Advanced_Studies_on_Puerto_Rico_and_the_Caribbean&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Notable members"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Enrique_Laguerre" title="Enrique Laguerre">Enrique Laguerre</a> -
