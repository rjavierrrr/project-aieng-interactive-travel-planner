PUPR offers technical degrees with a special focus on <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a> professions.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Polytechnic_University_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Polytechnic University of Puerto Rico (PUPR) is a private non-profit institution specializing in Engineering, Architecture, Land Surveying and Geomatics, and Business Administration.<sup id="cite_ref-Welcome_to_PUPR_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Welcome_to_PUPR-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The main campus is located in the island\'s financial district which is the very core of metropolitan <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Welcome_to_PUPR_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Welcome_to_PUPR-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The university also has a virtual campus that offers online degrees as well as two satellite locations in Florida, a campus in Miami and another in Orlando.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Academics">Academics</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Polytechnic_University_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Academics"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Polytechnic University of Puerto Rico (PUPR) is a private non-profit institution granting degrees in engineering, architecture, landscape architecture, land surveying and geomatics, and business administration.<sup id="cite_ref-Welcome_to_PUPR_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Welcome_to_PUPR-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is located in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, Puerto Rico\'s largest city.<sup id="cite_ref-Welcome_to_PUPR_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Welcome_to_PUPR-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\nAmong institutions of higher learning in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and its territories, the Polytechnic University of Puerto Rico graduates the second largest number of Hispanic engineers.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Today, more than 40% of the graduating engineers in Puerto Rico are alumni of the Polytechnic University.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Currently, there are nearly 6,000 students at the San Juan campus.<sup id="cite_ref-Welcome_to_PUPR_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Welcome_to_PUPR-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (October 2019)">failed verification</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>PUPR is accredited by the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Higher_Education_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Council of Higher Education of Puerto Rico">Council of Higher Education of Puerto Rico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Middle_States_Association_of_Colleges_and_Schools" title="Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools">Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Architectural_Accrediting_Board" title="National Architectural Accrediting Board">National Architectural Accrediting Board</a> (NAAB), the Landscape Architectural Accreditation Board (LAAB) and the <a href="/wiki/International_Assembly_for_Collegiate_Business_Education" class="mw-redirect" title="International Assembly for Collegiate Business Education">International Assembly for Collegiate Business Education</a> (IACBE).  In addition, several of PUPR\'s engineering programs are accredited by the <a href="/wiki/Accreditation_Board_for_Engineering_and_Technology" class="mw-redirect" title="Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology">Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology</a> (ABET).\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Engineering_college_description_and_special_characteristics">Engineering college description and special characteristics</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Polytechnic_University_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Engineering college description and special characteristics"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>PUPR is the largest private Hispanic engineering school in all of the United States and Puerto Rico and the only engineering school in the metropolitan area of San Juan. Since its inception, PUPR has graduated approximately 4,500 Hispanic engineers and surveyors.\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Imago-pupr.svg" class="mw-file-description"><img alt="Imagotype of PUPR on blue." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Imago-pupr.svg/220px-Imago-pupr.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="49" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Imago-pupr.svg/330px-Imago-pupr.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Imago-pupr.svg/440px-Imago-pupr.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="243" data-file-height="54" /></a><figcaption>Imagotype of PUPR</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Currently, the College of Engineering is 80% of the total PUPR undergraduate enrollment with 4,566 students. This enrollment will rank PUPR on the <a href="/wiki/American_Society_for_Engineering_Education" title="American Society for Engineering Education">American Society for Engineering Education</a> (ASEE)  enrollment in engineering survey as the 20th largest engineering college in the U.S. and its territories.\n<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span
