class="external text" href="http://www.laconcharesort.com">La Concha Resort</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>La Concha Renaissance San Juan Resort</b>, also known as <b>La Concha Resort</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> is a historic luxury resort located at the <a href="/wiki/Condado_(Santurce)" title="Condado (Santurce)">Condado</a> oceanfront within the district of <a href="/wiki/Santurce,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="Santurce, San Juan, Puerto Rico">Santurce</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Concha_Renaissance_San_Juan_Resort&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <b>La Concha Hotel</b> opened in December 1958<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> during the <i>Tropical Modernism Movement</i>. The hotel was publicly owned, by the government of Puerto Rico, and managed by the US-based Associated Federal Hotels chain.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The main hotel building was designed by architects Osvaldo Toro and Miguel Ferrer, of the firm <a href="/wiki/Toro_Ferrer" title="Toro Ferrer">Toro Ferrer</a>. The two men had previously designed the first modern luxury resort in San Juan, the <a href="/wiki/Caribe_Hilton" class="mw-redirect" title="Caribe Hilton">Caribe Hilton</a>. Another part of the complex, the <a href="/wiki/Seashell" title="Seashell">seashell</a>-inspired building for the restaurant <i>La <a href="/wiki/Pearl" title="Pearl">Perla</a></i>, was designed by architect <a href="/wiki/Mario_Salvadori" title="Mario Salvadori">Mario Salvadori</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The hotel\'s design incorporated tropical climate features such as cross-ventilation, natural illumination, open lobbies and seamless transitions between inside and outside spaces, with details supposedly relevant to Island traditions: an interior patio, blinds, a mirador encompassing both the sea and the city and the use of water throughout as a leitmotif. The protective concrete element in the fa\xc3\xa7ade, called brise-soleil in French or \xe2\x80\x9cquiebrasol\xe2\x80\x9d in Spanish, is an architectural element used by famed architect Le Corbusier. The purpose of the \xe2\x80\x9cquiebrasol\xe2\x80\x9d is to filter the sun\'s rays towards the interior corridors.\n</p><p>In 1973, the La Concha Hotel was united with the adjacent <a href="/wiki/Condado_Vanderbilt_Hotel" title="Condado Vanderbilt Hotel">Condado Beach Hotel</a> into one hotel, known as the <b>Hyatt Puerto Rico</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1976,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> management of the complex was taken over by Hilton International<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and the resort was renamed the <b>Condado Beach La Concha Convention Center</b>, with a huge convention wing built between the two existing hotel structures to physically connect them. The complex was later taken over by <a href="/wiki/Carnival_Cruise_Line" title="Carnival Cruise Line">Carnival Cruise Line</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and renamed <b>The Condado Beach Trio</b>, before the La Concha wing was closed in 1995, followed by the closure of the Condado Beach wing in 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-ihepr.com_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ihepr.com-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The government-owned properties sat vacant for years, and were finally severed in 2004. The convention center that joined them was demolished to build a public park.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The La Concha reopened in December 2007 after a $220 million renovation,<sup id="cite_ref-ihepr.com_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ihepr.com-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> managed by <a href="/wiki/Renaissance_Hotels" title="Renaissance Hotels">Renaissance Hotels</a> as <b>La Concha Renaissance San Juan Resort</b>.\n</p><p>In the summer of 2010, the hotel opened a $100 million addition called The Suites Tower which features an atrium style building with one and two bedroom suites, kitchens, architectural lighting, and amenities. The hotel\'s <i>Casino del Mar</i> is located on the street level of the new tower.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="See_also">See also</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
