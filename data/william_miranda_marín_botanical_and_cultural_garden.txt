Garden</b> also known as <b>Botanical and Cultural Garden of Caguas</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Botanical_garden" title="Botanical garden">botanical garden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Archaeological_site" title="Archaeological site">archaeological site</a> and <a href="/wiki/Natural_reserve" class="mw-redirect" title="Natural reserve">natural reserve</a> of about 235,800 m\xc2\xb2 of extension, which is located in the <a href="/wiki/Ca%C3%B1ab%C3%B3n,_Caguas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ca\xc3\xb1ab\xc3\xb3n, Caguas, Puerto Rico">Ca\xc3\xb1ab\xc3\xb3n</a> neighborhood, of the autonomous municipality of <a href="/wiki/Caguas" class="mw-redirect" title="Caguas">Caguas</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=William_Miranda_Mar%C3%ADn_Botanical_and_Cultural_Garden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>This botanical garden is located near the main cities of the Island of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, just 30 minutes from <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>.\n</p><p><i>Botanical and Cultural Garden of Caguas </i>, Office of Sustainable Development, Autonomous Municipality of Caguas, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.\nThe visiting hours are from Thursday to Sunday from 10:00&#160;am to 4:00&#160;pm.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=William_Miranda_Mar%C3%ADn_Botanical_and_Cultural_Garden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The garden has several additional elements that define it as a multi-themed one since it is not only a botanical garden but also a zoo, historical, ecological and museum site.\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Jard%C3%ADn_Bot%C3%A1nico_Caguas.tiff" class="mw-file-description"><img class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Jard%C3%ADn_Bot%C3%A1nico_Caguas.tiff/lossy-page1-458px-Jard%C3%ADn_Bot%C3%A1nico_Caguas.tiff.jpg 1.5x, /></a><figcaption>Lotus flower in the Jard\xc3\xadn Bot\xc3\xa1nico y Cultural William Miranda Mar\xc3\xadn</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The Caguas Botanical and Cultural Garden works both as a nature reserve and as an archaeological area, as it houses historical ruins.\n</p><p>Among the archaeological ruins stand out the old sugar mill known as the "<a href="/wiki/Hacienda" title="Hacienda">Hacienda</a> San Jos\xc3\xa9", also the reconstruction of a <a href="/wiki/Barrack" class="mw-redirect" title="Barrack">barrack</a> of slaves, and important archaeological sites of the Indians <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">ta\xc3\xadno</a> s that inhabited in <a href="/wiki/Borik%C3%A9n" class="mw-redirect" title="Borik\xc3\xa9n">Borik\xc3\xa9n</a>, aboriginal name of Puerto Rico, with pieces dating from <a href="/wiki/Pre-Columbian_era" title="Pre-Columbian era">Pre-Columbian era</a> to the times of Spanish colonization.\n</p><p>This space contains part of all the Cag\xc3\xbce\xc3\xb1os and Puerto Ricans, as the essences of the Ta\xc3\xadnos, blacks and Spanish are added in its texture, the garden celebrates <a href="/wiki/Criollismo" title="Criollismo">criollismo</a>, the sum of the three races, in several of its educational areas.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Collections">Collections</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=William_Miranda_Mar%C3%ADn_Botanical_and_Cultural_Garden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Collections"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In the botanical garden it exhibits tropical flora in all its exuberance and variety. At the same time it houses the fauna of this vegetation. Vegetable exhibits are arranged in the following areas:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Arboretum" title="Arboretum">Arboretum</a> with sections of,\n<ul><li><i>The Heritage Forest</i> displays 36 species of native and <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> trees that were of great ecological, nutritional and practical importance to the island\'s ancestors.</li>\n<li><i>La Arboleda Ancestral Ta\xc3\xadna</i> celebrates the indigenous heritage of the Creole through a monumental public art project inspired by the indigenous <a href="/wiki/Batey_(game)" title="Batey (game)">bateyes</a>.</li>\n<li><i>The African Ancestral Grove</i> is a space of tribute, remembrance and respect for our African ancestors, which includes a bronze monument, representing the figure of <a href="/wiki/Osa%C3%ADn" class="mw-redirect" title="Osa\xc3\xadn">Osa\xc3\xadn</a>.</li>\n<li><i>The Grove of Consciousness</i> is a call to reflection on the importance of protecting our natural resources, presenting various species of trees threatened with extinction.</li>\n<li><i>La Arboleda Florida</i> is a colorful festival that allows us to enjoy 40 species of trees with showy flowers.</li>\n<li><i>The plantation of the "Palo de Pollo"</i>, the Pollales (forests of "Palo de Pollo" (<i> <a href="/wiki/Pterocarpus_officinalis" title="Pterocarpus officinalis">Pterocarpus officinalis</a></i>) in Puerto Rico are impressive groves that form in places very humid such as swamps. In the garden there is a planting of these trees that inhabit the banks of the <a href="/wiki/Caguitas_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Caguitas River">Caguitas River</a>.</li>\n<li><i>La Arboleda Criolla</i> explores the uses of 15 species of Cag\xc3\xbce\xc3\xb1o trees that produce wood of commercial importance during the 19th and 20th centuries.</li></ul></li>\n<li><i>La Huerta Frutal</i> exhibits 50 species of native and foreign fruit trees that have been part of the recipe book of Puerto Rican cuisine.</li>\n<li><i>The Casa J\xc3\xadbara y Siembra Agr\xc3\xadcola Familiar</i> honors the peasant grandparents of Puerto Rico. This area offers the recreation of a family plantation or "felling" accomplished with traditional techniques and an exhibition hall.</li>\n<li><i>The Planting of the Puerto Rican Artisan</i> celebrates the creativity of the Puerto Rican people in carving, along with 60 species of trees, herbs and vines that enhance them.</li>\n<li><i>El Palmar</i> exhibits dozens of species of palms from different parts of the world distributed by their geographical areas of origin.</li>\n<li><i>El Bambular</i>, which originates in the Plaza Agr\xc3\xadcola and borders the Caguitas River, is a collection of different species of <a href="/wiki/Bamboo" title="Bamboo">bamboos</a>.</li>\n<li><i>La <a href="/wiki/Manigua" class="mw-redirect" title="Manigua">Manigua</a></i> is a Taino expression to designate a tropical <a href="/wiki/Wetland" title="Wetland">wetland</a> ecosystem characterized by water-saturated soils and vegetation adapted to the lack of oxygen in the soil.</li>\n<li><i>The River Corridor</i> is a planting plan aimed at stimulating the natural process of regeneration of the vegetation adjacent to the Caguitas River that connects with the "Honor to the River" project.</li>\n<li><i>The Aquatic Plants and Tropical Fish Garden</i> occupies a space within the historic area of the old hacienda house, with a pond for growing aquatic plants and a hatchery for tropical fish.</li>\n<li><i>The Plaza Agr\xc3\xadcola Urbana</i> is made up of six warehouses for crop production <a href="/wiki/Hydroponics" title="Hydroponics">hydroponics</a>, and the propagation of practical plants.</li>\n<li><i>The <a href="/wiki/Butterfly" title="Butterfly">Butterfly</a></i> is a space in the historic area dedicated to the breeding and spread of butterflies.</li></ul>\n<p>Educational tours, trail hikes, publications and special activities are provided in all of these sections. It is a unique place to attend and have healthy fun and comprehensive learning.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span
