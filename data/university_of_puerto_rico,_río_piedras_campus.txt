It is the largest campus in the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico" title="University of Puerto Rico">University of Puerto Rico</a> system in terms of student population and it was Puerto Rico\'s first public university campus.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The university serves more than 18,000 students, 20% of whom are graduate students, and grants an average of over 3,000 degrees a year. Its academic offerings range from the bachelor to the doctoral level with 70 undergraduate programs and 19 graduate degrees including 71 specializations in the basic disciplines and professional fields.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> UPR\xe2\x80\x90RP has consistently granted the largest number of doctorate degrees to Hispanic students under the United States jurisdiction.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 href="/wiki/File:LaUniversidad.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/LaUniversidad.jpg/250px-LaUniversidad.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" iconic <a href="/wiki/Clock_tower" title="Clock tower">clock tower</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Roosevelt_Tower" title="Roosevelt Tower">Roosevelt Tower</a></figcaption></figure>\n<p>In the year 1900 the <i>Escuela Normal Industrial</i> (<a href="/wiki/Normal_school" title="Normal school">Normal</a> Industrial School) was established in <a href="/wiki/Fajardo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Fajardo, Puerto Rico">Fajardo, Puerto Rico</a>, as the first institution of higher education in Puerto Rico dedicated to train those who would become teachers and educators.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> At the time it only had 20 students and 5 professors.<sup id="cite_ref-Historia_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Historia-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>A year later (1901) the institution was moved to an area previously occupied by farmland immediately north of the town of <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Piedras,_Puerto_Rico" title="R\xc3\xado Piedras, Puerto Rico">R\xc3\xado Piedras</a>, today part of the municipality of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>. The Normal School was established in <i>La Convalecencia</i>, which was one of the residences of the former <a href="/wiki/List_of_governors_of_Puerto_Rico" title="List of governors of Puerto Rico">Spanish Governors</a>. The school enrolled 273 students on its first year, and its first bachelor\'s degrees were granted four years later in 1907.<sup id="cite_ref-Historia_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Historia-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On March 12, 1903, under the administration of the Public Instruction Commissioner, Samuel McCune Lindsay, the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_Puerto_Rico#History" title="Legislative Assembly of Puerto Rico">2nd Legislative Assembly</a> approved a law creating the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico" title="University of Puerto Rico">University of Puerto Rico</a> in R\xc3\xado Piedras, transferring all the funding of the Insular Normal School there. This school became the first department of the university, what is now the Faculty of Education, becoming the nucleus of the University of Puerto Rico.\n</p><p>Now legally established, the University of Puerto Rico started its first academic year (1903\xe2\x80\x931904) with an enrollment of 173 students. Due to the scarcity of teachers in the island, most of these students were appointed by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Education" title="Puerto Rico Department of Education">Department of Public Instruction</a> to teach at schools without having finished four years of college.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The first graduating class (June 1907) consisted of 13 students.\n</p><p>In 1907, the first class graduated from the normal course of four years after the university was legally established. Among the students in that class were <a href="/wiki/Carlota_Matienzo" title="Carlota Matienzo">Carlota Matienzo</a>, Isabel Andr\xc3\xa9u, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Loaiza_Cordero_del_Rosario&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Loaiza Cordero del Rosario (page does not exist)">Loa\xc3\xadza Cordero</a>, Marina Roviro, and Juan Herrero.\n</p><p>On September 22, 1913, the departments of Law and Pharmacology were established. The university at the time required only an eighth grade diploma, but with the expansion of its courses, this requirement changed. After 1917, the departments of Normal Education, Liberal Arts, Pharmacology, and Law required a <a href="/wiki/High_school#United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="High school">high school</a> diploma for admission.\n</p><p>On February 21, 1931, Dr. <a href="/wiki/Carlos_E._Chard%C3%B3n" title="Carlos E. Chard\xc3\xb3n">Carlos E. Chard\xc3\xb3n</a> was appointed as chancellor of the university. During his tenure the university experienced significant growth in endowments. These were used in the expansion of the physical facilities at the R\xc3\xado Piedras and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Mayaguez" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Puerto Rico at Mayaguez">Mayag\xc3\xbcez</a> Campuses. This helped turn the university into a respected educational center. Chard\xc3\xb3n resigned from the post of <a href="/wiki/Chancellor#Educational_usage" title="Chancellor">Chancellor</a> in 1936, being succeeded by Juan B. Soto. The most important part of this period was the expansion of the buildings of the university as part of a plan for the rehabilitation of Puerto Rico.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="History_of_student_protests">History of student protests</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Campus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History of student protests"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1236090951">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}@media print{body.ns-0 .mw-parser-output .hatnote{display:none!important}}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%932011_University_of_Puerto_Rico_strikes" title="2010\xe2\x80\x932011 University of Puerto Rico strikes">2010\xe2\x80\x932011 University of Puerto Rico strikes</a></div>\n<p>The university was the site of social upheaval during the 1960s and 1970s, when <a href="/wiki/FUPI" class="mw-redirect" title="FUPI">nationalist students</a> protested for <a href="/wiki/Civil_rights_movement" title="Civil rights movement">civil rights</a>, against the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">war in Vietnam</a>, for the <a href="/wiki/Independence_movement_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Independence movement in Puerto Rico">independence of Puerto Rico</a>, and to expel the <a href="/wiki/Reserve_Officers%27_Training_Corps" title="Reserve Officers&#39; Training Corps">ROTC</a> from the campus. In 1970 <a href="/wiki/Antonia_Mart%C3%ADnez" title="Antonia Mart\xc3\xadnez">Antonia Mart\xc3\xadnez</a> was murdered by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Police" title="Puerto Rico Police">police</a> during the anti-education reform and <a href="/wiki/List_of_protests_against_the_Vietnam_War" title="List of protests against the Vietnam War">Vietnam War protests</a> of that year. As the shouting started in the street, everyone in the building she was in, on <a href="/wiki/Avenida_Juan_Ponce_de_Le%C3%B3n" title="Avenida Juan Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n">Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n Avenue</a>, went to the balconies to see what was happening. She was, like many others, watching the events, saw <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Police" title="Puerto Rico Police">policemen</a> attacking at the students, and allegedly shouted at the police <i>\xc2\xa1Asesinos!</i> (Assassins!). A policeman turned and shot her in the head, killing her and injuring a roommate. A mural of her and her story existed on the College of Humanities, until administrative personnel covered it with paint. Other stories claim that she was shot by mistake.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The students of the university also had a strike in the Spring of 2017 during which the school was shut down for several months.\n</p>\n<div mw-heading3"><h3 id="Campus_architecture">Campus architecture</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Campus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Campus architecture"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Torre_Universidad_de-Puerto_Rico_Rio_Piedras.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Torre_Universidad_de-Puerto_Rico_Rio_Piedras.jpg/250px-Torre_Universidad_de-Puerto_Rico_Rio_Piedras.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="333" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Torre_Universidad_de-Puerto_Rico_Rio_Piedras.jpg/375px-Torre_Universidad_de-Puerto_Rico_Rio_Piedras.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Torre_Universidad_de-Puerto_Rico_Rio_Piedras.jpg/500px-Torre_Universidad_de-Puerto_Rico_Rio_Piedras.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1944" data-file-height="2592" /></a><figcaption>The iconic <a href="/wiki/Roosevelt_Tower" title="Roosevelt Tower">Roosevelt Tower</a> in the campus\'s main building</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The University of Puerto Rico was established in R\xc3\xado Piedras in 1903. The first buildings were the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120422131101/http://www.upr.edu/img/slideShow/la_upr_historia/images/h12.jpg">Normal School</a> (1902), the Model School (1903), and the Principal\'s Residence (1903), next to the <a href="/wiki/Carretera_Central_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Carretera Central (Puerto Rico)">Central Road</a> (today <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_25" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Highway 25">Ponce de Leon Avenue</a>). Over the years, the development of the university occurred somewhat spontaneously. In 1908 the architectural firm of <i>Clark, Howe and Homer</i> designed a new fa\xc3\xa7ade for the Model School in the California <a href="/wiki/Mission_Revival_Style_architecture" class="mw-redirect" title="Mission Revival Style architecture">Mission Style</a>. In 1909 a milk processing building was constructed in an agricultural area, east of the academic center. Arts and Crafts\' Workshop buildings, a Library and a Gymnasium were planned, as well as the grandstand and the fence (all demolished now). In 1912 construction began for the Memorial Hall (Baldorioty Building) and buildings for the Law School and the School of Pharmacy were in the works in 1918. In 1924 the Chicago firm of urbanists <i><a href="/wiki/Edward_H._Bennett" title="Edward H. Bennett">Bennett</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_E._Parsons" title="William E. Parsons">Parsons</a> and Frost</i> were contacted to design a master plan for the future development of the university.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was not until 1935, with the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Reconstruction_Administration" title="Puerto Rico Reconstruction Administration">Puerto Rico Reconstruction Administration</a> (PRRA) and the large sum of federal funds that it invested for public works in Puerto Rico, that the partial design and construction of the so-called <i>Parson Plan</i> began. However, from the 1940s onward a new architectural paradigm, which discarded historical vocabularies and incorporated attitudes learned from Germany and from the studio of <a href="/wiki/Frank_Lloyd_Wright" title="Frank Lloyd Wright">Frank Lloyd Wright</a>, stemmed from German \xc3\xa9migr\xc3\xa9 <a href="/wiki/Henry_Klumb" title="Henry Klumb">Henry Klumb</a>. For 20 years Klumb was sole architect for the University of Puerto Rico, designing buildings for the campuses at R\xc3\xado Piedras and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Mayag%C3%BCez" title="University of Puerto Rico at Mayag\xc3\xbcez">Mayag\xc3\xbcez</a> until in 1966. At that time chancellor <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Ben%C3%ADtez" class="mw-redirect" title="Jaime Ben\xc3\xadtez">Jaime Ben\xc3\xadtez</a> assigned the design of the General Studies Building to the architectural firm of Toro &amp; Ferrer. Other buildings for the university have been designed by architects Antonio Marqu\xc3\xa9s Carri\xc3\xb3n (sports facilities, 1971); Jos\xc3\xa9 Firpi (student residence <i>Torre Norte</i>, 1971); the firm of Reed, Torres, Beauchamp &amp; Marvel (new Education Faculty Building, 1974 and the first expansion to the Faculty of Natural Sciences Building, 1978); the firm of Garc\xc3\xada &amp; Landray (second expansion the Faculty of Natural Sciences Building, 1989); GDO Arquitectos (School of Architecture Building, 2001) and Toro Ferrer (new General Studies Building, 2007).\n</p><p>In addition to The Integration of the Information Competencies to the Curriculum Projects promotes the development of information competencies around the campus. The Library System offers facilities for the blind and physically handicapped. The graduate and special collections in the Business Administration, Planning, Public Administration, Social Work, and Library Science programs are integral parts of the Library System, but the libraries of the School of Law, Natural Sciences Faculty, and the School of Architecture, also located on the university campus, operate independently.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Information_system">Information system</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Campus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Information system"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Information Systems Office serves the educational, research and administrative efforts of the institution. As a member of Internet2 and the Internet, the university enjoys instant communication with institutions all over the world for the purposes of correspondence and information access. Improvements to existing telecommunications infrastructure include the installation of structured cabling in campus buildings and fiber optics as a means of interconnecting them. Currently, campus buildings are configured to ATM (155Mbits / s) and Gigabit Ethernet interfacing equipment, providing greater capacity to transfer video, voice and data. The campus is capable of offering video conferencing over ATM (Asynchronous Transfer Mode), ISDN (Integrated Services Digital Network), or via the Internet in H.323 format.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="University_museums">University museums</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Campus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: University museums"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1236090951"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Museum_of_Art,_History,_and_Anthropology_of_the_University_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Museum of Art, History, and Anthropology of the University of Puerto Rico">Museum of Art, History, and Anthropology of the University of Puerto Rico</a></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:MAHA_-_UPR.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/MAHA_-_UPR.jpg/220px-MAHA_-_UPR.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="123" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/MAHA_-_UPR.jpg/330px-MAHA_-_UPR.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/MAHA_-_UPR.jpg/440px-MAHA_-_UPR.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="898" /></a><figcaption><a href="/wiki/Museum_of_Art,_History,_and_Anthropology_of_the_University_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Museum of Art, History, and Anthropology of the University of Puerto Rico">MAHA</a></figcaption></figure>\n<p>The <i>Museum of Art, History, and Anthropology</i> (MAHA) was created in 1951 by legislative act to "complete and preserve our art, history, and anthropological heritage," as a means to educate and enrich our culture. The current building, designed by the architect Henry Klumb, was inaugurated in 1959. Since its establishment, the museum has actively contributed to the growth of Puerto Rican cultural life by holding art, historical, and anthropological exhibits and sponsoring lectures, seminars, publications, and workshops\xe2\x80\x93to the benefit of both the academic and the general communities.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Housing">Housing</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Campus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Housing"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The R\xc3\xado Piedras campus provides housing in three residence halls:\n</p>\n<ul><li><i>Torre del Norte (Currently Closed)</i>; a 22-story building located within a block of the campus. It has capacity to house 437 students in double and single units.</li>\n<li><i>ResiCampus</i>; located within the campus consists of a structure of nine floors and have a capacity to house 354 students.</li>\n<li><i>Plaza Universitaria Residence Hall</i>; administered by a private company in consortium to UPR; offers housing to undergraduate and graduate students.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li></ul>\n<p>There is also a Guest House within the <a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Student_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="University of Puerto Rico, R\xc3\xado Piedras Student Center (page does not exist)">Student Center</a>, with rooms at affordable rates for visitors to the university, faculty, or students.\n</p><p>10% of first-year and 4% of all undergraduates live in college housing. No married student housing is available. Also, private housing is available in surrounding areas of the university.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Organization">Organization</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Campus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Organization"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The R\xc3\xado Piedras Campus is a <a href="/wiki/Collegiate_university" title="Collegiate university">collegiate university</a>. The Chancellor of the Campus is the top academic and administrative officer and presides over its two deliberative bodies: the Administrative Board and the Academic Senate.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Administrative_Board">Administrative Board</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Campus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Administrative Board"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <i>Administrative Board</i>, composed of the deans, two senators representing the faculty, and one student senator, advises the Chancellor in matters pertaining to the university program. The Board makes recommendations on leaves and faculty aid applications, and grants promotions and tenures.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Academic_Senate">Academic Senate</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Campus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Academic Senate"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <i>Academic Senate</i>, in turn, is the official academic forum. It is composed of the deans, the director of the library system, elected faculty representatives from all the colleges and schools, student senators, and representatives from the staff of academic advisors. Its members participate on the institutional processes, establishing academic rules and collaborating with other organisms of the University of Puerto Rico system.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Student_Councils">Student Councils</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Campus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Student Councils"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <i>General Student Council</i> (<i>Consejo General de Estudiantes</i> or CGE in Spanish) of the <a href="/wiki/UPRRP" class="mw-redirect" title="UPRRP">UPRRP</a> is the elected student government of the campus. The council is composed of several representatives to the student council, elected by each School or College, the number of representatives varies depending on the number of students enrolled in the School or College, and the President and Senator of each School and College, for a total of around 50 members. Elections are held in April and representatives are elected for one year, from July 1 to June 30.\n</p><p>Having its origins in the 1920s, the Student Council was dissolved in 1948 while <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Benitez" class="mw-redirect" title="Jaime Benitez">Jaime Benitez</a> was chancellor, and under the council presidency of <a href="/wiki/Juan_Mari_Br%C3%A1s" title="Juan Mari Br\xc3\xa1s">Juan Mari Br\xc3\xa1s</a> and it was not until 1968 that the Student Council was re-installed under the name General Student Council and under the council presidency of <a href="/wiki/David_Noriega" class="mw-redirect" title="David Noriega">David Noriega</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The General Student Council is governed by the Board of Directors, which is elected from among the council\'s members and is composed of:\n</p>\n<ul><li>President</li>\n<li>Vice President</li>\n<li>Executive Secretary</li>\n<li>Press Secretary</li>\n<li>Public Relations Secretary</li>\n<li>Records Clerk</li></ul>\n<p>Additionally, they select Representatives to the Campus\' Administrative Board and the university\'s University Board, both of them with an Alternate Representative. They are members of the board of directors.\n</p><p>Every College and School has a student council of its own, composed by a President, Vice President, Executive Secretary, Treasurer, Records Clerk, Public Relations Chair, the college or School\'s Student Senator (and, if available for the college/school, an alternate senator), Representatives to the General Student Council (up to four), and representatives of each department or subdivision present in the School or College.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Colleges_organization">Colleges organization</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Campus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Colleges organization"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Colleges have a dean, an associate dean, assistant deans, department chairs, and other administrative directors. In August 2019, the president-elect of the <a href="/wiki/UPRRP_College_of_Business_Administration" class="mw-redirect" title="UPRRP College of Business Administration">College of Business Administration</a> Student Council, Alberto J. Lebr\xc3\xb3n, established the university\'s first independent college board, founding the UPRRP College of Business Administration Directive Board, composed of two representatives from each of the college\'s associations and student council directives, with Lebr\xc3\xb3n serving as the board\'s first chairman. Each college also has several committees established; examples being the school or college\'s advisors committee, the curriculum committee, and the accreditation committee.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Deanery">Deanery</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Campus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Deanery"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The campus (and each school/college) has three administrative divisions known as a \'Deanery\' or \'Dean\'s Office\'. These include:\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="Deanery_of_Academic_Affairs">Deanery of Academic Affairs</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Campus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Deanery of Academic Affairs"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Academic Affairs (DAA in Spanish, <i>Decanato de Asuntos Acad\xc3\xa9micos</i>) promotes academic excellence, giving guidance and orientation for successful planning with the Campus Strategic Plan. It implants academic policies, and looks for the effective improvement of all personnel. It promotes and designs institutional research and coordinates accreditation processes. It evaluates the programs offered at the university, while trying to improve institutional effectiveness.\n</p><p>It is responsible for the suitable development of the services in the Libraries, Registrar, Admissions, and Continuing Education. It advises the Rector and the adequate units, such as the Administrative Council and the Academic Senate on the development of new politics, and efficacy of processes, planification, and academic decisions.\n</p><p>It is organized with a dean heading it, with the help of the Associate Dean and 3 Auxiliary Deans, each assigned a certain area of concern such as Foreign Students, General Studies, Business Faculty, the Library System, etcetera.\n</p><p>Three schools are under the Dean of Academic Affairs. These include the School of Communication, the Graduate School of Planning and the Graduate School of Informatic Sciences and Technologies and they are represented on the Administrative Board by the Dean of Academic Affairs.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="Deanery_of_Student_Affairs">Deanery of Student Affairs</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Campus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Deanery of Student Affairs"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Deanery of Students is the leader organization, compromised with an ever-changing academic population. They try to bring the most advanced technological resources in order to be of help with respect to the services given to students.\n</p><p>Faculties included are:\n</p>\n<ul><li>College of General Studies</li>\n<li>School of Architecture</li>\n<li>School of Communication</li>\n<li>School of Law</li>\n<li>School of Business Administration</li>\n<li>Graduate School of Business Administration</li>\n<li>Graduate School of Informational Sciences and Technologies</li>\n<li>Graduate School of Planning</li>\n<li>College of Natural Sciences</li>\n<li>College of Social Sciences</li>\n<li>College of Education</li>\n<li>College of Humanities</li></ul>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="Deanery_of_Administration">Deanery of Administration</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Campus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Deanery of Administration"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Deanery of Administration is the unit that offers essential services to the university community: it implements and guards for the proper fulfillment of the regulations that rule over administrative processes of the Campus and the entire University System. It plans, coordinates, evaluates and supervises labors related with different administrative and operative processes, but over all, they ensure quality services that have a positive aspect on student life, teaching and learning scenarios, investigation areas, labor areas of conformities, according to the Campus mission and the Public Administration that commands the university.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Academics">Academics</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Campus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Academics"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Campus serves more than 18,000 students, 20% graduate, and grants an average of over 3,000 degrees a year. From 2005 up to 2010 doctorate degrees conferred have maintained an upward trend. UPR\xe2\x80\x90RP has consistently granted the largest number of doctorate degrees to Hispanics in the US.\n</p><p>As a public comprehensive doctoral institution, its\nacademic offerings range from the baccalaureate to the doctoral degree, through 70\nundergraduate programs and 19 graduate degrees with 71 specializations in the basic\ndisciplines and professional fields.\n</p><p>The graduate offer includes 12 PhDs, one Doctorate\nin Education, and international programs in Law, at both LLM and JD levels. The Master of Architecture professional degree is offered by the School, accredited by the <a href="/wiki/National_Architectural_Accrediting_Board" title="National Architectural Accrediting Board">National Architectural Accrediting Board</a> (NAAB).<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\nThe UPR School of Architecture was established thanks to legislation approved in Puerto Rico in 1958 for such purpose. That same year and to that effect, Puerto Rican architect Santiago Iglesias Jr. presented a resolution at the annual convention of the <a href="/wiki/American_Institute_of_Architects" title="American Institute of Architects">American Institute of Architects</a>, celebrated in Cleveland, Ohio. In 1965, Puerto Rican architect <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_Eduardo_Amaral" title="Jes\xc3\xbas Eduardo Amaral">Jes\xc3\xbas Eduardo Amaral</a>, was selected as an executive consultant and given the responsibility to establish the school. From its beginning, it has offered preparation of university level in many areas of Business Administration. It also offers a bachelor\'s degree in administration of office systems, which substituted the secretarial sciences bachelor\'s degree.\n</p><p>In 1958 the Center for Commercial Investigations and for Academical Initiatives with the purpose of promoting investigation and contribute in the creation of knowledge in the area of Business Administration. Eventually, Cooperative Education, <i>Link</i> (Enlace), International Commerce Development and Enterprise Development programs, in order to have a narrower collaboration with business world.\n</p><p>In the academic year of 1970\xe2\x80\x9371, the Graduated School of Business Administration began offering master\'s degree in Business Administration (MBA) making it the first of its kind in the Caribbean. In addition to master\'s degree the School offers altogether with Law School and the Doctoral Program of Business Management the <i>MBA/JD Program</i> which is built towards guidance on professional practice, whilst the Doctoral Program is orientated towards research.\n</p><p>Student enrollment in the College of Business Administration is approximately 2,800 undergraduates and 400 graduate students. This college enrollment is the third highest in the R\xc3\xado Piedras Campus. Nearly 530 Bachelor\'s degrees and 50 Graduate degrees are conferred annually. 63% of the students of the college are female, similar to the 67% of population of R\xc3\xado Piedras Campus being female.\n</p><p>In November 2013, the School of Business and the Graduate School of Business, became the first and only public study centers in business, accredited in Puerto Rico and Latin America by the prestigious Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business (AACSB).<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span The college headquarters are located in the New Building of Education in the south of the campus, and administrate nine buildings in all the campus, including a house in <a href="/wiki/Pueblo,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="Pueblo, San Juan, Puerto Rico">downtown R\xc3\xado Piedras</a> district in San Juan.\n</p><p>The college moved to R\xc3\xado Piedras once the university was founded on March 12, 1903.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="University_High_School">University High School</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Campus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: University High School"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1236090951"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/University_High_School_(San_Juan)" title="University High School (San Juan)">University High School (San Juan)</a></div>\n<p>The University High School was built in the year 1913. activities"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>One of the most enriching aspects of student life in R\xc3\xado Piedras is the vast number of cultural and social events organized by the university. In addition to the usual academic conferences and lectures, there are a host of activities to choose from on any given day. Cultural Activities, run by the dean of students, is the central agency at R\xc3\xado Piedras that plans and sponsors major events on and off campus. They run a busy schedule with a cultural activity virtually every day of the week and on many weekends, too. The <i>Serie de Cine al Aire Libre</i> runs every month throughout the school year, presenting classic and contemporary films in the Baldorioty de Castro Plaza in front of the Tower. Admission is always free. Additionally, there are indoor concerts by musical groups such as the <i>Orquesta Filarm\xc3\xb3nica de Puerto Rico</i>, the Tuna of UPR-R\xc3\xado Piedras, and the voice emsambles of the campus, Coro de la Universidad de Puerto Rico (founded in 1936 by Augusto Rodr\xc3\xadguez), and Coralia, the concert choir.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span
