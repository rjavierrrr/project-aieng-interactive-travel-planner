colspan="2">\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Santa Mar\xc3\xada Magdalena de Pazzis Cemetery</b> is a colonial-era <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic</a> cemetery located in <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Old San Juan, Puerto Rico">Old San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>. It is the final resting place of many of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>\'s most prominent natives and residents. Construction began in 1863 under the auspices of Ignacio Mascaro. The cemetery is located outside the walls of <a href="/wiki/Castillo_San_Felipe_del_Morro" title="Castillo San Felipe del Morro">Castillo San Felipe del Morro</a> fortress, one of the island\'s most famous landmarks. The average height of the wall is 40 feet and the width ranges from 15 to 20 feet.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was named in honor of <a href="/wiki/Magdalena_de_Pazzi" class="mw-redirect" title="Magdalena de Pazzi">Saint Maria Magdalena de Pazzi</a>.\n</p><p>According to Rafael Rodr\xc3\xadguez, Chaplain and director of pastoral services at the <a href="/wiki/Universidad_del_Sagrado_Coraz%C3%B3n" title="Universidad del Sagrado Coraz\xc3\xb3n">Universidad del Sagrado Coraz\xc3\xb3n</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Santurce,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Santurce, Puerto Rico">Santurce</a> district of the capital, the location of the cemetery is central to the Puerto Rican belief in the separation of death and life. The <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">colonial Spanish government</a> at the time construction of the cemetery commenced, viewed death with fear because it was a mystery. Therefore, they decided to build the cemetery to overlook the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> to symbolize the spirit\'s journey to cross over to the afterlife.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div style="clear:left;" class=""></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Notable_interments">Notable interments</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santa_Mar%C3%ADa_Magdalena_de_Pazzis_Cemetery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Notable interments"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Pedro_Albizu_Campos" title="Pedro Albizu Campos">Pedro Albizu Campos</a>, nationalist leader and politician</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Aurora_de_Albornoz" title="Aurora de Albornoz">Aurora de Albornoz</a>, scholar, poet</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Juli%C3%A1n_Acosta" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Juli\xc3\xa1n Acosta">Jos\xc3\xa9 Juli\xc3\xa1n Acosta</a>, abolitionist, journalist</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Alegr%C3%ADa" title="Ricardo Alegr\xc3\xada">Ricardo Alegr\xc3\xada</a>, father of modern Puerto Rican archaeology</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Miguel_%C3%81ngel_%C3%81lvarez" title="Miguel \xc3\x81ngel \xc3\x81lvarez">Miguel \xc3\x81ngel \xc3\x81lvarez</a>, journalist, comedian and actor</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Jose_Celso_Barbosa" class="mw-redirect" title="Jose Celso Barbosa">Jose Celso Barbosa</a>, founder of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_statehood_movement" title="Puerto Rico statehood movement">Puerto Rican statehood movement</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Antonio_R._Barcel%C3%B3" class="mw-redirect" title="Antonio R.
