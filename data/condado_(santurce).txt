href="/wiki/Shore" class="mw-redirect" title="Shore">oceanfront</a>, tree-lined, pedestrian-oriented upper middle to upper class community in <a href="/wiki/Santurce,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="Santurce, San Juan, Puerto Rico">Santurce</a>. It is one of the forty <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Subdivisions of San Juan, Puerto Rico">subbarrios</a> of Santurce in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Setting">Setting</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Condado_(Santurce)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Setting"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Condado is an upscale neighborhood located on the <a href="/wiki/Condado_Beach" title="Condado Beach">beach</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ_2018_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ_2018-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is located east of the historic colonial barrio of <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Old San Juan, Puerto Rico">Old San Juan</a>. It is one of 40 subbarrios of Santurce. The land area measures 203.81 <a href="/wiki/Acre" title="Acre">acres</a> (82.48&#160;<a href="/wiki/Hectare" title="Hectare">ha</a>) with a resident population of 6,170 according to the <a href="/wiki/2000_United_States_Census" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 United States Census">2000 United States Census</a>.\n</p><p>The eastern border is marked by De Diego Avenue and its straight extension towards the Atlantic coast. On the south, the subbarrio is bounded by Wilson Street, Aldea Street, Expreso Baldorioty de Castro, Piccioni Street and Delcasse Street, and by the <a href="/wiki/Condado_Lagoon" title="Condado Lagoon">Condado Lagoon</a> (from east to west). At the westernmost point is the <a href="/wiki/Dos_Hermanos_Bridge" title="Dos Hermanos Bridge">Dos Hermanos Bridge</a>, where Ashford Avenue ends and leads into Old San Juan. In the north are the beaches of the Atlantic Ocean next to hotels and other attractions such as nightclubs, casinos, shops and restaurants. Condado is one of the island\'s primary tourist destinations.\n</p><p>In recent years, El Condado neighborhood has become associated with wealth and status. Properties are scarce, hard to come by and extremely expensive. It is sometimes called the \xe2\x80\x9cManhattan of Puerto Rico\xe2\x80\x9d and properties tend to hover in the millions of dollars. It is one of the most desirable places to live on the island and as such commands a premium.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="20th_century">20th century</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Condado_(Santurce)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 20th century"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Cornelius_Vanderbilt_(1877).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Cornelius_Vanderbilt_%281877%29.jpg/220px-Cornelius_Vanderbilt_%281877%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="196" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Cornelius_Vanderbilt_%281877%29.jpg/330px-Cornelius_Vanderbilt_%281877%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Cornelius_Vanderbilt_%281877%29.jpg/440px-Cornelius_Vanderbilt_%281877%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="647" data-file-height="576" /></a><figcaption><a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Vanderbilt" title="Cornelius Vanderbilt">Cornelius Vanderbilt</a>, railroad tycoon in the U.S.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Condado began its process of urbanization in 1908 by two American industrialists, <a href="/wiki/Hernan_Behn" class="mw-redirect" title="Hernan Behn">Hernan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sosthenes_Behn" title="Sosthenes Behn">Sosthenes</a>, also known as the Behn Brothers. The quarter became a typical <a href="/wiki/Streetcar_suburb" title="Streetcar suburb">streetcar suburb</a> to the traditional urban center of Old San Juan. Its growth and development was mostly shaped by a <a href="/wiki/Streetcars_in_North_America" title="Streetcars in North America">transportation influenced</a> suburbanization developed on a <a href="/wiki/Grid_plan" title="Grid plan">grid plan</a>.\n</p><p>The neighborhood experienced an <a href="/wiki/Boom_and_bust" class="mw-redirect" title="Boom and bust">economic boom</a> in the first decades of the 20th century when some of the wealthiest families built their homes in the area. The <a href="/wiki/Vanderbilt_family" title="Vanderbilt family">Vanderbilts</a> built a summer home in 1919 which today has been converted into the luxurious <a href="/wiki/Condado_Vanderbilt_Hotel" title="Condado Vanderbilt Hotel">Condado Vanderbilt Hotel</a>. The Behn Brothers also built their home in Condado and to their memory, <a href="/wiki/Dos_Hermanos_Bridge" title="Dos Hermanos Bridge">Dos Hermanos Bridge</a> (or "Two Brothers Bridge" in English) is named in their honor as founders of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Telephone" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Telephone">Puerto Rico Telephone</a> and the new electric <a href="/wiki/Rail_transport_in_Puerto_Rico#San_Juan_Tramway" title="Rail transport in Puerto Rico">tramway line</a> that linked <i>the county</i> to Old San Juan.\n</p><p>The 1950s-60s era saw another boom in Condado, with the rapid industrialization of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, this flamboyant locale was becoming a popular tourist destination. Large hotels like the <a href="/wiki/Caribe_Hilton_Hotel" title="Caribe Hilton Hotel">Caribe Hilton Hotel</a> and <a href="/wiki/La_Concha_Resort" class="mw-redirect" title="La Concha Resort">La Concha Resort</a> were built as part of a government sponsorship program under the <a href="/wiki/Operation_Bootstrap" title="Operation Bootstrap">Operation Bootstrap</a> brand.\n</p><p>A convention center was built to host activities while many families began to move in during the development of high-end and high-rise apartment buildings. In fact, Ashford Avenue was named after another well-known <i>Condado</i> resident Dr. <a href="/wiki/Bailey_Ashford" title="Bailey Ashford">Bailey Ashford</a>. It runs through the subbarrio and leads toward the Puente Dos Hermanos.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Demographics">Demographics</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span Some of the luxurious homes which still remain nestled between the high-rise condominiums have been converted into small inns, shops, museums or restaurants.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Dr. <a href="/wiki/Bailey_Ashford" title="Bailey Ashford">Bailey Ashford</a>\'s home has been remodeled and there are plans to turn it into a museum. Condado\'s main public space is <a href="/wiki/La_Ventana_al_Mar" title="La Ventana al Mar">La Ventana al Mar</a>, designed by architect and urbanist <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Mignucci" title="Andr\xc3\xa9s Mignucci">Andr\xc3\xa9s Mignucci</a>. The park is flanked by two of Condado\'s landmark hotels: to the west by the <a href="/wiki/Condado_Vanderbilt_Hotel" title="Condado Vanderbilt Hotel">Condado Vanderbilt Hotel</a> (1919), designed by Warren and Whitmore, and to the east by the La Concha Hotel (1957), designed by <a href="/wiki/Toro_Ferrer" title="Toro Ferrer">Toro Ferrer</a>.\n</p><p>One of the first schools constructed by the United States is in Condado. Designed by <a href="/wiki/Antonin_Nechodoma" title="Antonin Nechodoma">Antonin Nechodoma</a>, it was designated as a historic site in November 2020 by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Planning_Board"
