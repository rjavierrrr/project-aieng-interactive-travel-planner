class="infobox-data">Open Daily, 8am to 4:30pm</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Parque de la Ceiba</b> (English: Ceiba Tree Park) is a <a href="/wiki/Passive_park" class="mw-redirect" title="Passive park">passive park</a> in sector Cuatro Calles of barrio <a href="/wiki/San_Ant%C3%B3n" title="San Ant\xc3\xb3n">San Ant\xc3\xb3n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>. Its centerpiece is the historic <i>Ceiba pentandra</i>, a tree associated with the founding of the city. Now surrounded by the park with the same name, the emblematic 500-year-old <i>Ceiba</i> tree stands on the edge of the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce Historic Zone</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-periodicolaperla.com_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-periodicolaperla.com-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The park opened in 1984, under the administration of Mayor <a href="/wiki/Jose_Dapena_Thompson" class="mw-redirect" title="Jose Dapena Thompson">Jose Dapena Thompson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> A sign on the fence that surrounds the tree identifies its species as <i><a href="/wiki/Ceiba_pentandra" title="Ceiba pentandra">Ceiba pentandra</a></i>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_de_la_Ceiba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The park is located on Comercio street, next to <a href="/wiki/Rio_Portugues" class="mw-redirect" title="Rio Portugues">Rio Portugues</a> in the Cuatro Calles sector of barrio San Ant\xc3\xb3n.<sup id="cite_ref-PH_Ceiba_de_Ponce_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PH_Ceiba_de_Ponce-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Today the area is a mixed residential/commercial area on urban route <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_133" title="Puerto Rico Highway 133">PR-133</a>.  The park is managed by the Ponce municipal government.<sup id="cite_ref-Ceiba_de_Ponce._TravelPonce_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ceiba_de_Ponce._TravelPonce-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The tree is about half a mile east of <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Delicias" title="Plaza Las Delicias">Plaza Las Delicias</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_de_la_Ceiba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The park sits on an area believed to have been the site of the first settlement of Europeans in the Ponce region.<sup id="cite_ref-visitaponce.com_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-visitaponce.com-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> "In the surroundings of the legendary Ceiba de Ponce, broken pieces of indigenous pottery, shells, and stones were found to confirm the presence of Taino Indians long before the Spaniards that latter settled in the area."<sup id="cite_ref-Ceiba_de_Ponce._TravelPonce_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ceiba_de_Ponce._TravelPonce-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>It has been said that this tree was already a large tree at the time of the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> to the <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a>. Reference is also made to a book that suggests the tree already existed in 1696, and an 1818 map of Ponce by <a href="/wiki/Alejandro_Ord%C3%B3%C3%B1ez" title="Alejandro Ord\xc3\xb3\xc3\xb1ez">Alejandro Ord\xc3\xb3\xc3\xb1ez</a> shows the location of the tree.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1916, the tree measured 118 feet in circumference, measured at 4 feet from the ground surface.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Historic_Ceiba_Tree,_Ponce.jpg" /></a><figcaption>Photograph of the ceiba tree taken in 1900.</figcaption></figure>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="The_tree">The tree</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_de_la_Ceiba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The tree"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Francisco_Oller_-_La_ceiba_de_Ponce.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/Francisco_Oller_-_La_ceiba_de_Ponce.jpg/300px-Francisco_Oller_-_La_ceiba_de_Ponce.jpg" decoding="async" Ponce">Museo de Arte de Ponce</a></figcaption></figure>\n<p>The feature of the park is the historic centuries-old tree. The tree is also known as <a href="/wiki/Kapok_fibre" title="Kapok fibre">\'kapok\'</a> and \'silk cotton\' tree.  The scientific name of the tree is <i><a href="/wiki/Ceiba_pentandra" title="Ceiba pentandra">Ceiba pentandra</a></i>. The legendary tree belongs to the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Ceiba" title="Ceiba">Ceiba</a></i>, of the species <i>pentandra</i>, and the family <i>Bombacaceae</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-visitaponce.com_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-visitaponce.com-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The word <i>Ceiba</i> comes from a <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno_language" title="Ta\xc3\xadno language">Taino</a> word pronounced <i>say-bah</i>. Ceiba is one of the largest and tallest trees in the tropics of the <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">Western Hemisphere</a>. They have been known to reach heights of over 180 feet.<sup id="cite_ref-elboricua.com_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elboricua.com-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The tree is closely related to the peculiar <a href="/wiki/Baobab_tree" class="mw-redirect" title="Baobab tree">baobab trees</a> of Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-Ceiba_Tree._El_Boricua_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ceiba_Tree._El_Boricua-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Ceiba tree is also Puerto Rico\'s official national tree.<sup id="cite_ref-elboricua.com_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elboricua.com-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Conservation">Conservation</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_de_la_Ceiba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Conservation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The tree has recently been decaying and, on 30 December 2006, it lost a large limb that accounted for some 30 percent of its foliage at that time.  The channeling of Rio Portugues and the development of a nearby recreational area have been mentioned as possible causes for the rapid deterioration of the tree in recent years.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  In June 2009, the environmentalist group ProOrnato Inc and the government of the municipality of Ponce joined efforts to preserve the old tree in a weekend-long event.<sup id="cite_ref-PH_Ceiba_de_Ponce_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PH_Ceiba_de_Ponce-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> On 18 July 2011, however, the tree lost another large limb. After this event, only 35 percent of the remaining tree was estimated to still be alive.<sup id="cite_ref-periodicolaperla.com_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-periodicolaperla.com-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Characteristics">Characteristics</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_de_la_Ceiba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Characteristics"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The fruit of the Ceiba tree contains a fiber that is eight time lighter than cotton and five times more <a href="/wiki/Buoyancy" title="Buoyancy">buoyant</a> than cork. As such it was used as the flotation for early life preservers. In addition to these attributes, the fiber fully repels water, has a low thermal conductivity and is resistant to rot. A little known fact is that before synthetics were used for insulation materials, mattresses and pillows, these were stuffed with the fiber of the Ceiba tree fruit. It was recently discovered that the absorption capacity of this fiber is also higher than that of the polypropylene material normally used in the cleanup of environmental <a href="/wiki/Oil_spill" title="Oil spill">oil spills</a>. "The wood of the Ceiba tree is exceedingly lightweight and easily worked. However, because it lacks durability and is susceptible to insects and decay, it was mostly used for the construction of large canoes by the indigenous inhabitants of the region."<sup id="cite_ref-elboricua.com_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elboricua.com-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Canoes made of Ceiba tree trunks were able to seat over 100 men.<sup id="cite_ref-Ceiba_Tree._El_Boricua_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ceiba_Tree._El_Boricua-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="In_the_arts">In the arts</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_de_la_Ceiba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: In the arts"><span>edit</span></a><span Arte de Ponce</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div
