(English: <i lang="en">Ca\xc3\xb1a Gorda Keys</i> or <i>Cays</i>) is a group of three uninhabited, <a href="/wiki/Mangrove" title="Mangrove">mangrove</a>-covered <a href="/wiki/Cay" title="Cay">keys</a>, located at <span class="geo-inline"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1156832818"><span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="https://geohack.toolforge.org/geohack.php?pagename=Cayos_de_Ca%C3%B1a_Gorda&amp;params=17_56_32_N_066_52_17_W_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">17\xc2\xb056\xe2\x80\xb232\xe2\x80\xb3N</span> <span class="longitude">066\xc2\xb052\xe2\x80\xb217\xe2\x80\xb3W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">17.94222\xc2\xb0N 66.87139\xc2\xb0W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">17.94222; -66.87139</span></span></span></a></span></span> off the southwestern coast of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. They belong to <a href="/wiki/Barrio" title="Barrio">barrio</a> <a href="/wiki/Carenero" title="Carenero">Carenero</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gu%C3%A1nica,_Puerto_Rico" title="Gu\xc3\xa1nica, Puerto Rico">Gu\xc3\xa1nica</a> <a href="/wiki/Municipio" title="Municipio">municipio</a>. The eastern key, <i>Isla Ballena</i> is separated from the Puerto Rican mainland close to <i>Punta Ballena</i> only by a 66 foot (20&#160;m) wide channel. The western key was given the name  <a href="/wiki/Gilligan%27s_Island" title="Gilligan&#39;s Island">Gilligan\'s Island</a> (after the <a href="/wiki/Gilligan%27s_Island" title="Gilligan&#39;s Island">TV series</a>) in the 1970s by the Keegan/Barnett family, who had recently moved to Guanica from <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia,_Pennsylvania" class="mw-redirect" title="Philadelphia, Pennsylvania">Philadelphia, Pennsylvania</a>. <i>Cayo Aurora</i> is served by boat (1,969&#160;ft (600&#160;m) from the mainland at Punta Jacinto) and has public beaches. Between the two is <i>Cayo Honda</i>, the central key. The total land area is 0.069&#160;sq&#160;mi (44 acres; 0.18&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) (<a href="/wiki/Census_block" title="Census block">Block</a> 1069, <a href="/wiki/Census_block_group" title="Census block group">Block Group</a> 1, <a href="/wiki/Census_tract" title="Census tract">Census tract</a> 9611, Gu\xc3\xa1nica Municipio, Puerto Rico).\n</p><p>The area is good for snorkeling and can be
