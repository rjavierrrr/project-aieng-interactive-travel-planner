It is bordered by the <a href="/wiki/Cag%C3%BCitas_River" title="Cag\xc3\xbcitas River">Cag\xc3\xbcitas River</a> to the north and located two miles southwest of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Lo%C3%ADza" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza">R\xc3\xado Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza</a>. Its population in 2020 was 19,020.<sup id="cite_ref-Pico_1969_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pico_1969-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Law2015_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Law2015-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Barrio-Pueblo_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Barrio-Pueblo-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>As was customary in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, in Puerto Rico, the municipality has a barrio called <i><a href="/wiki/Pueblos_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Pueblos in Puerto Rico">pueblo</a></i> (<i>barrio-pueblo</i> starting with the 1990 US Census) which contains a central plaza or main town square, the municipal buildings (such as the city hall), and a Catholic church. <a href="/wiki/Fiestas_patronales_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Fiestas patronales in Puerto Rico">Fiestas patronales</a> (patron saint festivals) are held in the main town square every year.<sup id="cite_ref-Santullano_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Santullano-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Pariser_5th_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pariser_5th-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The municipal and mayoral offices were located in the <a href="/wiki/Caguas_City_Hall" title="Caguas City Hall">City Hall building</a> at the western edge of the main town square until 2010 when a new City Hall building located in the northwestern side of the downtown area was inaugurated.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Historic_district" title="Historic district">historic downtown district</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Pueblos_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Pueblos in Puerto Rico">pueblo</a></i>) of Caguas was listed on the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Register_of_Historic_Sites_and_Zones" title="Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones">Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones</a> (<i>Registro Nacional de Sitios y Zonas Hist\xc3\xb3ricas</i>) on July 6, 1996, under the name <i><b>Zona Hist\xc3\xb3rica de Caguas</b></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:02_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:02-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caguas_barrio-pueblo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Caguas Pueblo (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Pueblo de Caguas</i>), officially designated as <b>Caguas Barrio-pueblo</b> and popularly referred to as <i>Downtown Caguas</i> in English, is located in the middle plains of the <a href="/wiki/Caguas_Valley" title="Caguas Valley">Caguas Valley</a>. It is bordered by the <a href="/wiki/Cag%C3%BCitas_River" title="Cag\xc3\xbcitas River">Cag\xc3\xbcitas River</a> to the north, the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Lo%C3%ADza" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza">Lo\xc3\xadza River</a> to the east and the <a href="/wiki/Turabo_River" title="Turabo River">Turabo River</a> to the south.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caguas_barrio-pueblo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The area where downtown Caguas is located today was originally inhabited by the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> people. This area of the <a href="/wiki/Caguas_Valley" title="Caguas Valley">valley</a> was ideal for the cultivation of crops, such as <a href="/wiki/Cassava" title="Cassava">cassava</a>, due to the fertile river-fed soil. At the time of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Spanish colonization of the Americas">Spanish colonization</a> the local tribes were led by the cacique <a href="/wiki/Caguax" title="Caguax">Caguax</a>, from whom the Spanish settlement of <b>San Sebasti\xc3\xa1n del Pi\xc3\xb1al de Caguax</b> got its name. This village was granted <a href="/wiki/Town_privileges" title="Town privileges">town privileges</a> in 1812 and its first city hall was built in 1820 when it received the title of <i>Villa y cabeza de distrito de Provincia de Espa\xc3\xb1a</i> ("<i>Villa</i> and District headquarters of a Spanish province"). The village was finally granted full city rights in 1894.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Caguas barrio-pueblo was in Spain\'s <a href="/wiki/Gazetteer" title="Gazetteer">gazetteers</a><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> until Puerto Rico was ceded by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_of_1898" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Paris of 1898">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became an <a href="/wiki/Insular_area" title="Insular area">unincorporated territory of the United States</a>. In 1899, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_War" title="United States Department of War">United States Department of War</a> conducted a <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of Puerto Rico finding that the population of Caguas Pueblo was 5,450.<sup id="cite_ref-OfficeSanger1900_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OfficeSanger1900-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The area was heavily damaged by San Felipe Segundo (also known as the <a href="/wiki/1928_Okeechobee_hurricane" title="1928 Okeechobee hurricane">1928 Okeechobee hurricane</a>) in 1928 and many historical buildings were demolished then, which is why many of the older buildings, such as the cathedral, date from the 1930s after many rebuilding projects in the downtown took place.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="The_central_plaza_and_its_church">The central plaza and its church</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caguas_barrio-pueblo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The central plaza and its church"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:PlazaPalmer1.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/PlazaPalmer1.jpg/220px-PlazaPalmer1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/PlazaPalmer1.jpg/330px-PlazaPalmer1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/PlazaPalmer1.jpg/440px-PlazaPalmer1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="960" data-file-height="640" /></a><figcaption>Plaza Palmer during <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> time.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The main town square of Caguas is named <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Plaza de Recreo Santiago R. Palmer</i></span>. This is one of the largest main town squares or <i>plazas</i> in Puerto Rico. The central plaza or square, is a public space that is often used for official and unofficial recreational events and is a place where people can gather and socialize from dusk to dawn. The <a href="/wiki/Laws_of_the_Indies" title="Laws of the Indies">Laws of the Indies</a>, Spanish law, which regulated life in Puerto Rico in the early 19th century, stated the plaza\'s purpose was for "the parties" (celebrations, festivities) (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">a prop\xc3\xb3sito para las fiestas</i>), and that the square should be proportionally large enough for the number of neighbors (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">grandeza proporcionada al\nn\xc3\xbamero de vecinos</i>). These Spanish regulations also stated that the streets nearby should be comfortable portals for passersby, protecting them from the elements: sun and rain.<sup id="cite_ref-Santullano_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Santullano-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Located across from the central plaza in Caguas barrio-pueblo is the <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es"><a href="/wiki/Catedral_Dulce_Nombre_de_Jes%C3%BAs_(Caguas,_Puerto_Rico)" title="Catedral Dulce Nombre de Jes\xc3\xbas (Caguas, Puerto Rico)">Catedral Dulce Nombre de Jes\xc3\xbas</a></i></span> which serves as the regional <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic</a> cathedral. A hermitage was built in 1626 and by 1775 it had been renamed to its current name. In 1830 the construction of a church made of masonry began. The church was used until 1928 when it was damaged by <a href="/wiki/1928_Okeechobee_hurricane" title="1928 Okeechobee hurricane">San Felipe Segundo hurricane</a>. Having sustained too much damage, it was demolished and reconstructed starting in 1936 based on an architectural design by Pedro M\xc3\xa9ndez and Joseph O\'Kelly. The church was restored in 1999. In 1964 the church became a cathedral.<sup id="cite_ref-Mari_Mut_2013_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mari_Mut_2013-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span
