It is the church\'s third temple in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> and the only one in Puerto Rico, serving the 23,000 members who live there.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Juan_Puerto_Rico_Temple&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The intent to construct the temple was announced by <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Church_(LDS_Church)" title="President of the Church (LDS Church)">church president</a> <a href="/wiki/Russell_M._Nelson" title="Russell M. Nelson">Russell M. Nelson</a> on October 7, 2018, during the church\'s <a href="/wiki/General_Conference_(LDS_Church)" title="General Conference (LDS Church)">general conference</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Oct2018temples_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oct2018temples-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Nelson announced 11 other temples at the same time, bringing the total number of temples operating, under construction, and announced at that time to 201.\n</p><p>Following release of an artist\'s rendering of the temple a few days earlier,<sup id="cite_ref-PRrendering_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PRrendering-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> on January 17, 2019, the church announced that a groundbreaking to signify the beginning of construction would be held on May 4, 2019, with Walter F. Gonz\xc3\xa1lez, the president of the church\'s <a href="/wiki/Area_(LDS_Church)#Caribbean" title="Area (LDS Church)">Caribbean</a> <a href="/wiki/Area_(LDS_Church)" title="Area (LDS Church)">Area</a>, presiding.<sup id="cite_ref-PuertoRicoGroundbreaking_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PuertoRicoGroundbreaking-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> At the groundbreaking, church leaders noted that construction of the temple was anticipated to take about two years.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The temple was originally planned to be completed in 2021,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> but construction was delayed due to the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> A public open house was held from December 1-17, 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-newsroom_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsroom-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The temple was dedicated by <a href="/wiki/D._Todd_Christofferson" title="D. Todd Christofferson">D. Todd Christofferson</a> on January 15, 2023,<sup id="cite_ref-churchnews_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-churchnews-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> where over 2,100 church members watched remotely during the three sessions held.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Architecture">Architecture</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Juan_Puerto_Rico_Temple&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Architecture"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The San Juan Puerto Rico Temple has a single story and one spire. It is 6,988 square feet and 74 feet tall with the spire (24 feet without), which was inspired by the towers on <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_City_Hall" title="San Juan City Hall">San Juan City Hall</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CJCT_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CJCT-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The exterior of the temple is concrete and inspired by local <a href="/wiki/Spanish_colonial_architecture" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish colonial architecture">Spanish colonial architecture</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-churchnews_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-churchnews-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The interior glasswork was inspired by <a href="/wiki/Quatrefoil" title="Quatrefoil">quatrefoil</a> motifs on local buildings.<sup id="cite_ref-newsroom_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsroom-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The walkways around the temple were made by locally-fabricated concrete<sup id="cite_ref-CJCT_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CJCT-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The crema vosscione tile was quarried in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> and the accent cement tiles were manufactured in <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>, while the carpet patterns Spanish Colonial styles and hosts colors evocative of Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-CJCT_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CJCT-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="See_also">See also</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
