1955, is an <a href="/wiki/National_archives" title="National archives">archive</a> documenting the history and culture of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The governmental <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Puerto_Rican_Culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Institute of Puerto Rican Culture">Institute of Puerto Rican Culture</a> began overseeing its operation in 1956. It is located in a building shared with the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_National_Library" title="Puerto Rico National Library">national library</a> on <a href="/wiki/Avenida_Juan_Ponce_de_Le%C3%B3n" title="Avenida Juan Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n">Avenida Juan Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>. Among its collections is the "Fondo de Obras Publicas" (records of public works), formerly housed in the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Campus" title="University of Puerto Rico, R\xc3\xado Piedras Campus">University of Puerto Rico</a>\'s archives.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESilvestrini-Pacheco1981_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESilvestrini-Pacheco1981-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The objectives of this institution, which is managed by volunteers, and spearheaded by Joseph Harrison Flores,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> is to make information accessible quickly
