Closed on legal holidays, except for scheduled events.</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Parque de la Abolici\xc3\xb3n</b> (English: Abolition Park) is a city park in <a href="/wiki/Barrio_Cuarto" class="mw-redirect" title="Barrio Cuarto">Barrio Cuarto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>.  It was the first park in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> to commemorate the abolition of <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slavery</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was built in 1874 and renovated in 1956.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Background">Background</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Slaves were brought to Puerto Rico from Africa, starting in the early 16th century and lasting through the 19th century, to replace the local Indian slaves who had been decimated.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The new slaves worked the <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sugar_cane" class="mw-redirect" title="Sugar cane">sugar cane</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gold_mining" title="Gold mining">gold mining</a> industries in Puerto Rico. During the 18th century, as gold mining ceased to be one of the major industries in Puerto Rico, slaves worked mostly in coffee plantations and sugar cane fields.  By a royal proclamation of <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> slavery was abolished on 22 March 1873.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Plaza_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n_de_la_Esclavitud.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Plaza_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n_de_la_Esclavitud.jpg/220px-Plaza_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n_de_la_Esclavitud.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="143" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Plaza_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n_de_la_Esclavitud.jpg/330px-Plaza_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n_de_la_Esclavitud.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Plaza_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n_de_la_Esclavitud.jpg/440px-Plaza_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n_de_la_Esclavitud.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1725" data-file-height="1119" /></a><figcaption>Parque de la Abolici\xc3\xb3n in 1908, looking north</figcaption></figure>\n<p>In 1874, a year after the abolition of slavery, a group of citizens built a small park in memory of the historic event.  In 1880 <a href="/wiki/Olimpio_Otero" class="mw-redirect" title="Olimpio Otero">Olimpio Otero</a>, Juan Mayoral Barn\xc3\xa9s, and <a href="/wiki/Rom%C3%A1n_Baldorioty_de_Castro" title="Rom\xc3\xa1n Baldorioty de Castro">Rom\xc3\xa1n Baldorioty de Castro</a> were instrumental in creating the concept for a park dedicated to the commemorating the abolition of slavery, the only such memorial in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Juan Mayoral Barn\xc3\xa9s brought the idea for the creation of the park to the Ponce Municipal Assembly on 14 March 1880. It was unanimously approved by the Assembly, ratified by the Central Government, and confirmed by Royal Decree on 1 March 1881.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The park was built and opened in the 1890s. It had swing sets for children, a fountain with colorful fish, and a <a href="/wiki/Roller_coaster" title="Roller coaster">roller coaster</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On 6 May 1956,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> under the administration of Ponce mayor <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Grillasca_Salas" title="Andr\xc3\xa9s Grillasca Salas">Andr\xc3\xa9s Grillasca Salas</a>, the roller coaster, swing sets and fish pond were removed, and replace with the current <a href="/wiki/Concha_Ac%C3%BAstica_de_Ponce" title="Concha Ac\xc3\xbastica de Ponce">Concha Ac\xc3\xbastica</a> amphitheater. The statue of the freed slave was added at the base of the obelisk.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The sculpture of the freed slave is a work of Victor Cott.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Also in 1956, the park\'s Concha Ac\xc3\xbastica opened.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location_and_features">Location and features</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Location and features"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Mapa_del_Parque_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Mapa_del_Parque_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n.jpg/220px-Mapa_del_Parque_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="300" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Mapa_del_Parque_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n.jpg/440px-Mapa_del_Parque_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n.jpg 2x" data-file-width="721" data-file-height="984" /></a><figcaption>Map of Parque de la Abolici\xc3\xb3n</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The park is located on Avenida Hostos at the fork of Salud Street and Marina Street, immediately north of the 100-foot high <a href="/wiki/Monumento_a_la_abolici%C3%B3n_de_la_esclavitud" title="Monumento a la abolici\xc3\xb3n de la esclavitud">Monumento a la abolici\xc3\xb3n de la esclavitud</a>. It sits across from the Darlington Building, Ponce\'s first multi-story building, which was completed in 1952.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The park features a grassy green area with trees and masonry benches as well as two monuments to commemorate the <a href="/wiki/Abolitionism" title="Abolitionism">abolition of slavery in Puerto Rico</a>: a <a href="/wiki/Monument" title="Monument">monument</a> depicting a black male slave with broken chains depicting he is a free man, and a 100-foot high <a href="/wiki/Obelisk" title="Obelisk">obelisk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monumento_a_la_abolici%C3%B3n_de_la_esclavitud" title="Monumento a la abolici\xc3\xb3n de la esclavitud">Monumento a la abolici\xc3\xb3n de la esclavitud</a>, which rises just behind the black iron <a href="/wiki/Sculpture" title="Sculpture">sculpture</a> of the freed slave to accentuates and give instance to the occasion. Between the green area of the park and the two monuments is the outdoors <a href="/wiki/Musical_acoustics" title="Musical acoustics">acoustic</a> <a href="/wiki/Amphitheater" class="mw-redirect" title="Amphitheater">amphitheater</a> known as <i><a href="/wiki/Concha_Ac%C3%BAstica_de_Ponce" title="Concha Ac\xc3\xbastica de Ponce">La Concha Acustica</a></i> (English: The Acoustic Shell) which completes the park in its triangular city block. \n</p><p>After almost 70 years of use with only minor maintenance, the park was renovated in 1956 by <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Porrata_Doria" class="mw-redirect" title="Francisco Porrata Doria">Francisco Porrata Doria</a>, an architect from Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span
