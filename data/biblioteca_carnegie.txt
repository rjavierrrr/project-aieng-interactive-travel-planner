href="/wiki/Avenida_Juan_Ponce_de_Le%C3%B3n" title="Avenida Juan Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n">Avenida Juan Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n</a> in <a href="/wiki/Puerta_de_Tierra,_San_Juan" title="Puerta de Tierra, San Juan">Puerta de Tierra</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a><sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">NRHP</a>-listed <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_library" title="Carnegie library">Carnegie library</a> funded through a $100,000 donation from the <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Corporation_of_New_York" title="Carnegie Corporation of New York">Carnegie Foundation</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpdoc_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpdoc-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> becoming Puerto Rico\'s first purpose-built library<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> when it was completed in 1915.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biblioteca_Carnegie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1901, then-<a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_Education_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Secretary of Education of Puerto Rico">Education Commissioner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Grove_Brumbaugh" title="Martin Grove Brumbaugh">Martin Grove Brumbaugh</a>, initiated talks with Andrew Carnegie with the purpose of building a public library in San Juan,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> as part of the United States\' <a href="/wiki/Education_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Education in Puerto Rico">Puerto Rican education program</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> For this, the San Juan municipal legislature had agreed to give $6,000 for maintenance expenses. Carnegie\'s private secretary, <a href="/wiki/James_Bertram_(Carnegie_secretary)" title="James Bertram (Carnegie secretary)">James Bertram</a>, indicated that the amount usually donated was 10 times the promised maintenance expenses. Nevertheless, Brumbaugh obtained the $100,000 amount.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This was in part due to then-<a href="/wiki/List_of_governors_of_Puerto_Rico#Post-Foraker_Act_of_1900" title="List of governors of Puerto Rico">Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Yager" title="Arthur Yager">Arthur Yager\'s</a> friendship with Carnegie himself.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Its origins stem from the Insular Library created in 1903, created by the <a href="/wiki/Foraker_Act" title="Foraker Act">then-new civil government</a>, which itself came from the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_campaign#&quot;Americanization&quot;_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico campaign">military-rule era</a> San Juan Free Library, which was a merger of five pre-existing libraries under Spanish-rule: the library of the <a href="/wiki/San_Jos%C3%A9_Church" title="San Jos\xc3\xa9 Church">Dominican</a> and Franciscan monasteries, the <a href="/wiki/Sociedad_Econ%C3%B3mica_de_Amigos_del_Pa%C3%ADs_en_Puerto_Rico" title="Sociedad Econ\xc3\xb3mica de Amigos del Pa\xc3\xads en Puerto Rico">Real Sociedad Econ\xc3\xb3mica de Amigos del Pa\xc3\xads</a>, the library of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Diputaci%C3%B3n_Provincial_de_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Diputaci\xc3\xb3n Provincial de Puerto Rico (page does not exist)">Provincial Council</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;&#91;<a href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diputaci%C3%B3n_Provincial_de_Puerto_Rico" class="extiw" title="es:Diputaci\xc3\xb3n Provincial de Puerto Rico">es</a>&#93;</span> and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Treasury" title="Puerto Rico Department of Treasury">Treasury Department of Puerto Rico</a>, the library of the <a href="/wiki/Bar_Association_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Bar Association of Puerto Rico">Royal College of Lawyers-General Secretary</a> and the library of the <a href="/wiki/Center_for_Advanced_Studies_on_Puerto_Rico_and_the_Caribbean" title="Center for Advanced Studies on Puerto Rico and the Caribbean">Institute of Secondary Education</a> and Normal School.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>It has a <a href="/wiki/Classical_Revival_architecture" class="mw-redirect" title="Classical Revival architecture">Classical Revival</a> stylings and was designed and built by the Puerto Rican engineer Ram\xc3\xb3n Carbia in 1914, when work on the project commenced on land donated by the Puerto Rico government.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In June 1916, the collection of the Insular Library, which had previously been known as the San Juan Public Library,<sup id="cite_ref-:2_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> was transferred to the building and the library, which was named Biblioteca Carnegie, itself was inaugurated on 27 July,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> when the Puerto Rican government formally accepted the donation.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> From 1917 till 2010, the library was administered by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Education" title="Puerto Rico Department of Education">Department of Education</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In its first forty years of service the library had nine reading rooms, membership, with an average of 200 daily visitors.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Under the 222 Law of 1946 a $6,000 amount was made available to the library\'s board of trustees, with the intent of establishing the <i>Biblioteca Rodante</i> ("Library on Wheels"). The then-<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Education" title="Puerto Rico Department of Education">Department of Public Instruction</a> was authorized to organize and equip the Biblioteca Rodante, which offered it services through the use of <i>cajas viajeras</i> ("travelling boxes"), which were shelves used to carry books to isolated schools and communities.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The project started in 1948 under the direction of Federico Porrata Dor\xc3\xada, <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Porrata-Dor%C3%ADa" title="Francisco Porrata-Dor\xc3\xada">Francisco Porrata-Dor\xc3\xada\'s</a> brother, with two vehicles carrying books for children and adults, as users ranged from elementary-level school children to adults aged eighty to ninety-years old.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>However, in 1965, the library was closed to due the deterioration,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> which in response <a href="/wiki/Nilita_Vient%C3%B3s_Gast%C3%B3n" title="Nilita Vient\xc3\xb3s Gast\xc3\xb3n">Nilita Vient\xc3\xb3s Gast\xc3\xb3n</a>, who had served as president of the neighboring <a href="/wiki/Ateneo_Puertorrique%C3%B1o" title="Ateneo Puertorrique\xc3\xb1o">Ateneo Puertorrique\xc3\xb1o</a>, penned <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150805224638/https://www.80grados.net/la-biblioteca-carnegie/">her essay on the library</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was remodelled with some alterations on the floors and reopened in 1969,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> albeit with a reduced number of visitors, registering 7,000 users that year.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> That same year it hosted its first Sal\xc3\xb3n de Humoristas de Puerto Rico ("Puerto Rico Comedian\'s Hall"), which would continue for 22 years, displaying the work of humourists such as <a href="/wiki/Carmelo_Filardi" title="Carmelo Filardi">Carmelo Filardi</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ram%C3%B3n_Arroyo_Cisneros&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ram\xc3\xb3n Arroyo Cisneros (page does not exist)">Ram\xc3\xb3n Arroyo Cisneros</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;&#91;<a href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ram%C3%B3n_Arroyo_Cisneros" class="extiw" title="es:Ram\xc3\xb3n Arroyo Cisneros">es</a>&#93;</span>, Arturo Y\xc3\xa9pez, Jos\xc3\xa9 Ram\xc3\xb3n Cepero, <a href="/wiki/Ismael_Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Ismael Rodr\xc3\xadguez">Ismael Rodr\xc3\xadguez</a>, D\xc3\xadaz de Villegas among others. The library received commendations from former-Governor Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn, who had recently started his vacation in Rome, writing "Humor is intelligent laughter."<sup id="cite_ref-:3_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In addition, the library was host to the first meetings of the <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> Cultural Center in 1973 and held thirty-six "UNESCO <a href="/wiki/Chamber_music" title="Chamber music">Chamber Music</a> Concerts" with the likes of <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_Mar%C3%ADa_Sanrom%C3%A1" title="Jes\xc3\xbas Mar\xc3\xada Sanrom\xc3\xa1">Jes\xc3\xbas Mar\xc3\xada Sanrom\xc3\xa1</a> and <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9ctor_Campos_Parsi" title="H\xc3\xa9ctor Campos Parsi">H\xc3\xa9ctor Campos Parsi</a>. Ricardo Alegr\xc3\xada had talented students from the <a href="/wiki/Escuela_de_Artes_Pl%C3%A1sticas_y_Dise%C3%B1o_de_Puerto_Rico" title="Escuela de Artes Pl\xc3\xa1sticas y Dise\xc3\xb1o de Puerto Rico">Escuela de Artes Pl\xc3\xa1sticas y Dise\xc3\xb1o de Puerto Rico</a> present their "masterpieces" at exhibitions held in the library\'s exhibition hall on the upper floor. It was also the meeting place for the first meetings and exhibitions of the Academy of Heraldic and Historical Studies and the Theatre Board, then directed by <a href="/wiki/Raulito_Carbonell" title="Raulito Carbonell">Raulito Carbonell\'s</a> namesake father.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>During the following decades, it closed by season<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and had its working hours reduced as a result of lack of funds.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Additionally, in 1989, as a consequence to the significant damages the building suffered during <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hugo" title="Hurricane Hugo">Hurricane Hugo</a>, the library was closed, leaving San Juan without a public library.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1991, the Department of Education, "supported by a group of citizens interested in the library, decided to restore the building."<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The next year, a law was passed which permitted the Department of Education integrate the community by subdelegating the administration of the library to a governmental agency or NGO. The organization selected was the Biblioteca Carnegie Board of Trustees,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> reopening in 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2010, the San Juan municipal legislature approved an ordinance to accept the library building, possessions and land from the Department of Education, per a Legislative Assembly resolution.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> After which, then-<a href="/wiki/List_of_mayors_of_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="List of mayors of San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan mayor</a> <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Santini" title="Jorge Santini">Jorge Santini</a> commenced the "total disinterest of the municipality in preserving it" however "community efforts have been made to reopen it without success."<sup id="cite_ref-:2_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> After the Jos\xc3\xa9 Juli\xc3\xa1n Acosta Theater Arts School, located a down the street, was declared "partially not apt for use" after the <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Puerto_Rico_earthquakes" title="2019\xe2\x80\x9320 Puerto Rico earthquakes">2019\xe2\x80\x9320 Puerto Rico earthquakes</a>, then-mayor <a href="/wiki/Carmen_Yul%C3%ADn_Cruz" title="Carmen Yul\xc3\xadn Cruz">Carmen Yul\xc3\xadn Cruz</a> announced an agreement with the Department of Education to rehabilitate the library and transfer students temporarily, using 11 classrooms and a recreative area at the library and supplying the use of the Ateneo Puertorrique\xc3\xb1o\'s theatre and the <a href="/wiki/Teatro_Tapia" title="Teatro Tapia">Teatro Tapia</a>, equipment and breakfasts supplied by the municipality, while lunches were the Department\'s responsibility, as well as Wi-Fi and a radio with direct communication to the municipality\'s Emergency Management Office and establishing an identification card system for the 182 students and 31 faculty, amounting to a sum of $85,000 investment.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Description">Description</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biblioteca_Carnegie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1983 the state architect of Puerto Rico wrote "This is a fine Neoclassical, 2-storied building with a rectangular plan." but noted that the building "has not been properly maintained and is in fair condition."<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpdoc_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpdoc-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 2\xe2\x80\x933">&#58;&#8202;2\xe2\x80\x933&#8202;</span></sup>\n</p><p>It was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">NRHP</a> in 1983 and on the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Register_of_Historic_Sites_and_Zones" title="Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones">Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones</a> in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-nris_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:02_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:02-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Services">Services</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biblioteca_Carnegie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Services"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Puerto Rico law permits members of the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Legislative Assembly of Puerto Rico">Legislative Assembly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Supreme Court of Puerto Rico">Supreme Court</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Court_of_First_Instance_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Court of First Instance of Puerto Rico (page does not exist)">Court of First Instance</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;&#91;<a href="https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/Special:EntityPage/Q6152392#sitelinks-wikipedia" class="extiw" title="d:Special:EntityPage/Q6152392"><span title="&quot;Court of First Instance of Puerto Rico&quot; in other languages">Wikidata</span></a>&#93;</span> and District court judges as well as prosecutors to check out most books for a maximum of thirty days.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Among the collection there were books from the collections from three <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Wales" title="Prince of Wales">Princes of Wales</a>, three Medieval abbots and <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_II_of_Bavaria" title="Ludwig II of Bavaria">Ludwig II</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> When the library closed after Hurricane Hugo it was open seven days a week, had rooms for references, circulation, magazines, teenagers, children, computer, movies and cultural events, as well as virtual references and night galleries.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The library held alliances with the <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Henr%C3%ADquez_Ure%C3%B1a_National_Library" title="Pedro Henr\xc3\xadquez Ure\xc3\xb1a National Library">Pedro Henr\xc3\xadquez Ure\xc3\xb1a National Library</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Juan_Bosch_(politician)" title="Juan Bosch (politician)">Juan Bosch</a> himself, it hosted a camp for children below the poverty line, with meals provided by the Department of Education\'s school cafeterias. Its <i>bebeteca</i> ("baby\'s library") hosted children\'s festival, while it held "la hora del cuento ("story hour") with older children. The teenagers\' room had book and computers, prepared for their homework or entertainment. It also hosted computer classes for the elderly, and invited the homeless to use their facilities as well.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="See_also">See also</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
