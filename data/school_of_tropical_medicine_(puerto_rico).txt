0;">February 3, 2000</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>School of Tropical Medicine</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es"><b>Escuela de Medicina Tropical</b></i>), was an educational institution created in 1926 by an act of the Puerto Rican Legislature, to further the research initiated by the Anemia Commissions and the Institute of Tropical Medicine on anemia and its causes. The institution existed as an independent entity until 1949, when it was integrated into the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico_School_of_Medicine" title="University of Puerto Rico School of Medicine">School of Medicine</a> of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico" title="University of Puerto Rico">University of Puerto Rico</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_of_Tropical_Medicine_(Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Puerto_Rico_Anemia_Commission">Puerto Rico Anemia Commission</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_of_Tropical_Medicine_(Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Puerto Rico Anemia Commission"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Captain <a href="/wiki/Bailey_Ashford" title="Bailey Ashford">Bailey K. Ashford</a>, a medical doctor, was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Army_Medical_Department_(United_States)" title="Army Medical Department (United States)">United States Army Medical Corps</a> which accompanied the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a> when Puerto Rico was invaded during the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_Campaign" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican Campaign">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> in 1898. As the medical officer in the general military hospital in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>, he was the first to describe and successfully treat <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North American</a> <a href="/wiki/Hookworm" title="Hookworm">hookworm</a> in 1899.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2024)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Medicina_Tropical.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Medicina_Tropical.jpg/220px-Medicina_Tropical.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="143" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Medicina_Tropical.jpg/330px-Medicina_Tropical.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Medicina_Tropical.jpg/440px-Medicina_Tropical.jpg 2x" data-file-width="498" data-file-height="324" /></a><figcaption>School of Tropical Medicine building</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Because of his clinical investigations into the <a href="/wiki/Anemia" title="Anemia">anemia</a> caused by hookworm infestation, Ashford was inspired to organize and conduct a parasite treatment campaign. It cured approximately 300,000 persons (one-third of the Puerto Rico population) and reduced the death rate from this anemia by 90 percent.<sup id="cite_ref-TSM_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TSM-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Ashford and Dr. Isaac Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Mart\xc3\xadnez encouraged the government to undertake a program to reduce hookworm and anemia. They were appointed as the founding members of the Puerto Rico Anemia Commission, established in 1904 by the Insular Government. Ashford had discovered that hookworm infestation was the principal cause of anemia on the island. He served on the Commission from 1904\xe2\x80\x931906. Medical research in Puerto Rico had its beginning with the "First Anemia Commission."<sup id="cite_ref-TSM_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TSM-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1911, Ashford together with doctors Isaac Gonzalez Martinez, Pedro Guti\xc3\xa9rrez Igaravides and Walter King, proposed that the local government create an Institute of Tropical Medicine to further the research initiated by the Anemia Commissions. In 1912, the local legislature approved the proposal and established the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Institute_of_Tropical_Medicine&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Institute of Tropical Medicine (page does not exist)">Institute of Tropical Medicine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-F_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="School_of_Tropical_Medicine">School of Tropical Medicine</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_of_Tropical_Medicine_(Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: School of Tropical Medicine"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The President of the Puerto Rico Senate, <a href="/wiki/Antonio_R._Barcel%C3%B3" class="mw-redirect" title="Antonio R. Barcel\xc3\xb3">Antonio R. Barcel\xc3\xb3</a>, was attending a conference in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> when he was approached by professors Jose Antonio Lopez Antongiorgi and Abraham L. Goodman from the Medical School of Columbia University. They spoke about the need for establishing a medical school in Puerto Rico where scientific investigations could be conducted into tropical diseases. Senator Barcel\xc3\xb3 became interested in the idea and, on June 23, 1924, he sponsored the legislation which provided Governor <a href="/wiki/Horace_Towner" class="mw-redirect" title="Horace Towner">Horace Towner</a>, with the prerequisite funding for the school.<sup id="cite_ref-PRET_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PRET-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The School of Tropical Medicine was formally dedicated in 1925. It was the second school in the United States and its territories (after Tulane\'s School of Tropical Medicine, 1913<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>), founded for the purpose of researching and training physicians in the cause and prevention of tropical diseases. Located in <a href="/wiki/Puerta_de_Tierra" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerta de Tierra">Puerta de Tierra</a>, San Juan, the school\'s building is one of the few examples of the <a href="/wiki/Plateresque" title="Plateresque">Neo-Plateresque</a> architectural style on the Island.<sup id="cite_ref-PRET_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PRET-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1926, new legislation expanded the Institute of Tropical Medicine into the School of Tropical Medicine of the University of Puerto Rico, which was operated under the sponsorship of Columbia University. An agreement was made between the University of Puerto Rico and Columbia University in regard to the finances of the institution.<sup id="cite_ref-F_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Having completed a 30-year Army career, Ashford assumed a full-time faculty position at the school and continued his interest in tropical medicine.<sup id="cite_ref-AST_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AST-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Together with doctors <a href="/wiki/Isaac_Gonz%C3%A1lez_Mart%C3%ADnez" title="Isaac Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Mart\xc3\xadnez">Isaac Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Mart\xc3\xadnez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ram%C3%B3n_M._Su%C3%A1rez_Calderon" title="Ram\xc3\xb3n M. Su\xc3\xa1rez Calderon">Ram\xc3\xb3n M. Su\xc3\xa1rez Calderon</a>, he continued his research and study of anemia.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Mart\xc3\xadnez conducted many investigations and experiments in <a href="/wiki/Parasitology" title="Parasitology">parasitology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bilharzia" class="mw-redirect" title="Bilharzia">bilharzia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leprosy" title="Leprosy">leprosy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Typhoid_fever" title="Typhoid fever">typhoid fever</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> During his years at the institution, Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Mart\xc3\xadnez founded <i>Anales de Medicina de Puerto Rico</i>, a scientific journal. He was elected president of the Medical Academy of Puerto Rico in 1917. In 1919, he published a chapter on his findings of the Intestinal Bilharzias in the book <i>La Pr\xc3\xa1ctica de la Medicina en el Tr\xc3\xb3pico</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-F_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Su\xc3\xa1rez Calderon identified the proper and effective treatment of a type of <a href="/wiki/Anemia" title="Anemia">anemia</a> known as <a href="/wiki/Tropical_sprue" title="Tropical sprue">tropical sprue</a>, the application of complex methods, such as <a href="/wiki/Electrocardiography" title="Electrocardiography">electrocardiography</a> and <a href="/wiki/Radioisotope" class="mw-redirect" title="Radioisotope">radioisotope</a>, to be used in clinics and the identification and treatment of the disease which causes <a href="/wiki/Rheumatic_fever" title="Rheumatic fever">heart rheumatism</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CPR_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CPR-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Su\xc3\xa1rez Calderon continued Ashford\'s work and investigations on anemia after the latter\'s death. In 1938, he published his scientific findings on tropical sprue.<sup id="cite_ref-G_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-G-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1927, the institution offered courses in tropical medicine and nutrition. Most of the students continued their graduate work at Columbia University. In May 1930, the University of Puerto Rico offered two degrees of Master of Arts for the students who continued their education and work at the School of Tropical Medicine.<sup id="cite_ref-TSM_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TSM-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1931, <a href="/wiki/William_B._Castle" title="William B. Castle">William B. Castle</a> and his assistant <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_P._Rhoads" title="Cornelius P. Rhoads">Cornelius P. Rhoads</a> studied hookworm and <a href="/wiki/Tropical_sprue" title="Tropical sprue">tropical sprue</a> in relation to anemia. They were able to treat some patients with liver extract, which efficacy was being studied. Dr. <a href="/wiki/George_C._Payne" title="George C. Payne">George C. Payne</a> continued to study anemia in 1936 and 1937.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="UPR_School_of_Medicine">UPR School of Medicine</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_of_Tropical_Medicine_(Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: UPR School of Medicine"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The agreement between the University of Puerto Rico and Columbia University in regard to the School of Tropical Medicine was terminated by mutual consent in 1948.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2024)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>In May 1949, the Puerto Rican Legislature authorized the creation of the School of Medicine of the University of Puerto Rico, also known as the <a href="/wiki/UPR_School_of_Medicine" class="mw-redirect" title="UPR School of Medicine">UPR School of Medicine</a>. The School of Tropical Medicine was merged into the new school, and admitted its first class in August 1950.<sup id="cite_ref-TSM_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TSM-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> On September 29, 1983, the building in which the School of Tropical Medicine was located was listed in the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was later added to the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Register_of_Historic_Sites_and_Zones" title="Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones">Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones</a> in 2000.<sup
