forests</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Carite State Forest</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Bosque Estatal de Carite</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/State_forest" title="State forest">state forest</a> and nature reserve located in the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_de_Cayey" title="Sierra de Cayey">Sierra de Cayey</a> mountain range in southeastern <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The forest extends over approximately 2,600 hectares (6,500 acres) and is located in the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Caguas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Caguas, Puerto Rico">Caguas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cayey,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cayey, Puerto Rico">Cayey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guayama,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guayama, Puerto Rico">Guayama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patillas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Patillas, Puerto Rico">Patillas</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Lorenzo,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Lorenzo, Puerto Rico">San Lorenzo</a>. The forest is located along the <i><a href="/wiki/Ruta_Panor%C3%A1mica" title="Ruta Panor\xc3\xa1mica">Ruta Panor\xc3\xa1mica</a></i> (officially the <i><a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Mar%C3%ADn" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn</a> <a href="/wiki/Scenic_route" title="Scenic route">Scenic Route</a></i>), which offers access to the forest.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carite_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Carite State Forest is one of 20 forestry units in the territory of Puerto Rico. This forest area was established by a proclamation in 1935 and was formerly listed as a state forest on July 1, 1975, through the Law of Puerto Rican Forests (Law Num. 133). The area was preserved with the intention of developing it for its <a href="/wiki/Wood_industry" title="Wood industry">timber industry</a>. The state forest was established with the goal of protecting the hydrological basins of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Lo%C3%ADza" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza">Lo\xc3\xadza</a>, <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Patillas" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Patillas">Patillas</a> and <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_de_la_Plata_(Puerto_Rico)" title="R\xc3\xado de la Plata (Puerto Rico)">La Plata</a> rivers which were being affected by erosion caused by deforestation throughout the 20th century. In more recent times the forest has become an important wildlife preserve, ecological research site and recreational area.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carite_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>This forest is located within the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_de_Cayey" title="Sierra de Cayey">Sierra de Cayey</a> mountain range. This range belongs to the larger <a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Cordillera Central (Puerto Rico)">Cordillera Central</a> which extends through Puerto Rico from west to east. The highest point of the Sierra at 2,962 feet (903 m) in elevation, <a href="/wiki/Cerro_La_Santa" title="Cerro La Santa">Cerro La Santa</a> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> for <i>peak of the saint</i> or <i>peak of the holy woman</i>), is located within the forest in the municipalities of Caguas, Cayey and San Lorenzo.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This is the highest point in those three municipalities.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Geology">Geology</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carite_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The mountains in the Carite Forest are of volcanic origin. The underlying rocks in the area located between the surface of the forest and depths of 3 kilometres (1.9&#160;mi) were created as a result of volcanic activity that took place during the <a href="/wiki/Early_Cretaceous" title="Early Cretaceous">Early Cretaceous</a> period between 79 and 144 million years ago. About 13 types of soil can be found within the forest region.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Climate">Climate</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carite_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Climate"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The climate of the forest is humid and cool; the most humid time of the year is between the months of May and October, analogous to the wet season of the island and the region\'s hurricane season. The average annual temperature is of 22&#160;\xc2\xb0C (72&#160;\xc2\xb0F) and the average annual precipitation measurement is 2,200 millimetres (85&#160;in).<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Ecology_and_conservation">Ecology and conservation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carite_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ecology and conservation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Fauna">Fauna</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carite_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Fauna"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Patagioenas_inornata_wetmorei.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Patagioenas_inornata_wetmorei.jpg/220px-Patagioenas_inornata_wetmorei.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="330" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Patagioenas_inornata_wetmorei.jpg/330px-Patagioenas_inornata_wetmorei.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/3f/Patagioenas_inornata_wetmorei.jpg 2x" data-file-width="384" data-file-height="576" /></a><figcaption>The <a href="/wiki/Plain_pigeon" title="Plain pigeon">Puerto Rican plain pigeon</a> or <i>paloma sabanera</i> is endemic to the island and populous in the region.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The forest is an important wildlife refuge and is home to 49 species of birds (9 of which are endemic), 15 species of amphibians, 12 species of reptiles and 7 species of mammals. About 9 species of animals that live in the forest are listed as endangered or threatened species, some of which are the Puerto Rican broad-winged hawk (<i><a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_broad-winged_hawk" title="Puerto Rican broad-winged hawk">Buteo platypterus brunnescens</a></i>), the Puerto Rican sharp-shinned hawk (<i><a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_sharp-shinned_hawk" title="Puerto Rican sharp-shinned hawk">Accipiter striatus venator</a></i>), the elfin woods warbler (<i><a href="/wiki/Elfin_woods_warbler" title="Elfin woods warbler">Setophaga angelae</a></i>), Eneida\'s coqu\xc3\xad (<i><a href="/wiki/Eneida%27s_coqu%C3%AD" title="Eneida&#39;s coqu\xc3\xad">Eleutherodactylus eneidae</a></i>), the web-footed coqui (<i><a href="/wiki/Web-footed_coqu%C3%AD" title="Web-footed coqu\xc3\xad">Eleutherodactylus karlschmidti</a></i>), the Puerto Rican rock frog or <i>coqu\xc3\xad guaj\xc3\xb3n</i> (<i><a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_rock_frog" title="Puerto Rican rock frog">Eleutherodactylus cooki</a></i>), and the Puerto Rican boa (<i><a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_boa" title="Puerto Rican boa">Chilabothrus inornatus</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The area is renowned for its population of Puerto Rican plain pigeons or <i>paloma sabanera</i> (<i><a href="/wiki/Plain_pigeon" title="Plain pigeon">Patagioenas inornata wetmorei</a></i>) which were formerly on the brink of extinction but were saved thanks to conservation efforts and is now considered stable. The golden coqu\xc3\xad (<i><a href="/wiki/Golden_coqu%C3%AD" title="Golden coqu\xc3\xad">Eleutherodactylus jasperi</a></i>) used to be found in this forest but no specimens have been found in recent years and the species are now considered extinct.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flora">Flora</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carite_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Flora"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Carite is home to more than 204 species of trees out of which 176 species are native. There are 43 endemic species of plants such as the Puerto Rican magnolia (<i><a href="/wiki/Magnolia_portoricensis" title="Magnolia portoricensis">Magnolia portoricensis</a></i>) and the Puerto Rican raintree (<i><a href="/wiki/Brunfelsia_portoricensis" title="Brunfelsia portoricensis">Brunfelsia portoricensis</a></i>). The forest is also home to the Luquillo Mountain stopper or <i>uvillo</i> (<i><a href="/wiki/Eugenia_haematocarpa" title="Eugenia haematocarpa">Eugenia haematocarpa</a></i>), an endangered <a href="/wiki/Myrtaceae" title="Myrtaceae">myrtle</a> which can only be found in <a href="/wiki/El_Yunque_National_Forest" title="El Yunque National Forest">El Yunque National Forest</a> and Carite State Forest.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The forest\'s vegetation can be classified into three ecological zones: the <a href="/wiki/Tropical_and_subtropical_moist_broadleaf_forests" title="Tropical and subtropical moist broadleaf forests">subtropical moist forest</a> of <a href="/wiki/Prestoea_montana" class="mw-redirect" title="Prestoea montana">Sierra palm trees</a> which comprises 9% of the forest, the very humid subtropical moist forest which comprises more than 98% of the forest area and is home to the elfin or <a href="/wiki/Dwarf_forest" title="Dwarf forest">dwarf forest</a>, and the low montane humid forest which only comprises about 0.5% of the forest area and is home to the <i>palo colorado</i> (<i><a href="/wiki/Cyrilla" title="Cyrilla">Cyrilla racemiflora</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Recreation">Recreation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carite_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Recreation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Charco_Azul.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Charco_Azul.jpg/220px-Charco_Azul.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="275" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Charco_Azul.jpg/330px-Charco_Azul.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Charco_Azul.jpg/440px-Charco_Azul.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1440" data-file-height="1799" /></a><figcaption>Charco Azul in 2015.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Recreation in the forest is managed by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Natural_and_Environmental_Resources" title="Puerto Rico Department of Natural and Environmental Resources">Puerto Rico Department of Natural and Environmental Resources</a> (DRNA). There are opportunities of camping, picnics and swimming. There used to be up to 25 hiking trails in the forest area; however, many of the trails have not been properly maintained, and some have never been reopened after being completely destroyed by hurricanes such as <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Georges" title="Hurricane Georges">Georges</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Maria</a>. The only currently maintained trail is the Charco Azul Trail which leads to the Charco Azul swimming area.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Camping_and_picnic_areas">Camping and picnic areas</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carite_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Camping and picnic areas"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The DRNA offices are open Monday to Friday from 7 a.m. to 3:30 p.m. The recreation areas are open from about 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Most of the areas designated for camping and picnics are located uphill from <a href="/wiki/Guavate,_Cayey,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guavate, Cayey, Puerto Rico">Guavate</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cayey,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cayey, Puerto Rico">Cayey</a> (in the past, the forest has also been referred to as the <i>Guavate Fores</i>t) close to the famous <i><a href="/wiki/Lechon" class="mw-redirect" title="Lechon">lechoneras</a></i>. The picnic area close to the Guavate lechoneras have not been opened after being destroyed by Hurricane Maria and the only maintained picnic area is located in the Charco Azul section of the forest. As of 2020, camping areas have not yet reopened after the destruction caused by the hurricane.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Charco_Azul">Charco Azul</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carite_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Charco Azul"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p><i>Charco Azul</i> (Spanish for <i>blue pool)</i>, located in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Patillas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Patillas, Puerto Rico">Patillas</a>, is one of the main highlights of the state forest. This is a clear, blue-colored natural pool formed by the Charco Azul river falls, which flow into the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Patillas" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Patillas">Patillas River</a>. The area, located along <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_184" title="Puerto Rico Highway 184">PR-184</a>, offers sites for picnics, camping and showers and restrooms for people who wish to swim in it.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading
