Morales</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Cathedral Dulce Nombre de Jes\xc3\xbas</b>, or in English, <b>Sweet Name of Jesus Cathedral</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Cathedral" title="Cathedral">cathedral</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a> in <a href="/wiki/Caguas" class="mw-redirect" title="Caguas">Caguas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. It is located on the Plaza Palmer in the center of town, and next to the <a href="/wiki/Colegio_Cat%C3%B3lico_Notre_Dame" title="Colegio Cat\xc3\xb3lico Notre Dame">Colegio Cat\xc3\xb3lico Notre Dame</a> Elementary. There are only five cathedrals in Puerto Rico and Cathedral Dulce Nombre de Jes\xc3\xbas is the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Caguas" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Caguas">Diocese of Caguas</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Patron_saint" title="Patron saint">patron saint</a> of the church, as well as that of the city, is the <i>Dulce Nombre de Jes\xc3\xbas</i> (<a href="/wiki/Holy_Name_of_Jesus" title="Holy Name of Jesus">Sweet Name of Jesus</a>).\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catedral_Dulce_Nombre_de_Jes%C3%BAs_(Caguas,_Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The cathedral was constructed where the old <i>ermita</i> (<a href="/wiki/Hermitage_(religious_retreat)" title="Hermitage (religious retreat)">hermitage</a> or rural <a href="/wiki/Chapel" title="Chapel">chapel</a>) of San Sebasti\xc3\xa1n del Barrero used to be located.<sup id="cite_ref-Urban_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Urban-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> San Sebasti\xc3\xa1n del Barrero was the first Spanish settlement in the area that now comprises the City of Caguas. It is known that the chapel was built prior to 1645.<sup id="cite_ref-Urban_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Urban-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Into the 18th century, the chapel was used for holding religious services. When the <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">Municipality</a> of Caguas was established by the <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Crown of Spain">Spanish Crown</a> in 1775, the chapel was designated as its <a href="/wiki/Parish_church" title="Parish church">parish church</a> and renamed.<sup id="cite_ref-Frogi_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Frogi-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The church was expanded at the end of the 19th century and remodeled in the 1920s. In 1930, part of the reconstruction of its current facade was started. This project was finished in the middle of the century.<sup id="cite_ref-Urban_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Urban-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1936, the church was improved and remodeled again to its present form.\n</p><p>The church was raised to the status of cathedral when the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Caguas" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Caguas">Roman Catholic Diocese of Caguas</a> was created by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Paul_VI" title="Pope Paul VI">Pope Paul VI</a> in 1964.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Tombs">Tombs</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catedral_Dulce_Nombre_de_Jes%C3%BAs_(Caguas,_Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tombs"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The remains of <a href="/wiki/Don_(honorific)" title="Don (honorific)">Don</a> Xim\xc3\xa9nez (died 1806), <a href="/wiki/Parish_priest" class="mw-redirect" title="Parish priest">parish priest</a> of the town, and of Rafael Grovas F\xc3\xa9lix (died 1991), the first <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Caguas" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Caguas">Bishop of Caguas</a>, are buried there. The remains of <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Manuel_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Santiago" title="Carlos Manuel Rodr\xc3\xadguez Santiago">Carlos Manuel Rodr\xc3\xadguez Santiago</a> are also located in the cathedral. Rodr\xc3\xadguez, better known locally as "el beato Charlie", spent his life in the surrounding neighborhood and is a candidate for <a href="/wiki/Canonization" title="Canonization">canonization</a>, the only <a href="/wiki/Laity" title="Laity">layman</a> from Puerto Rico to be so honored by the Catholic Church, and the first such American to <a href="/wiki/Beatification" title="Beatification">beatified</a>. His tomb reads: "We live for that night", referring to <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a>, which was one of his favorite sayings.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2
