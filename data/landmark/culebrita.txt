href="/wiki/Culebra_National_Wildlife_Refuge" title="Culebra National Wildlife Refuge">Culebra National Wildlife Refuge</a>)</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Culebrita Island</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Isla Culebrita,</i> meaning "little snake island") is a small, uninhabited island off the eastern coast of the island-<a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/Culebra,_Puerto_Rico" title="Culebra, Puerto Rico">Culebra</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Virgin_Islands" title="Spanish Virgin Islands">Spanish Virgin Islands</a>, administratively part of the <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Geography of Puerto Rico">archipelago of Puerto Rico</a> and geographically part of the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Islands" title="Virgin Islands">archipelago of the Virgin Islands</a>. Together with <i>Cayo Botella</i> off the northwestern point, and <i>Pel\xc3\xa1</i> and <i>Pelaita</i> to the west, Culebrita belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico">barrio</a> <a href="/wiki/Fraile,_Culebra,_Puerto_Rico" title="Fraile, Culebra, Puerto Rico">Fraile</a> of the main island of Culebra. It is a <a href="/wiki/Nature_reserve" title="Nature reserve">nature reserve</a> and is part of the <a href="/wiki/Culebra_National_Wildlife_Refuge" title="Culebra National Wildlife Refuge">Culebra National Wildlife Refuge</a>. The island is home to <a href="/wiki/Culebrita_Lighthouse" title="Culebrita Lighthouse">Culebrita Lighthouse</a>, one of the oldest <a href="/wiki/Lighthouse" title="Lighthouse">lighthouses</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>. Culebrita is only accessible by private boat from the main island of Culebra.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Culebrita_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Map_of_Culebra_highlighting_Fraile.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Map_of_Culebra_highlighting_Fraile.png/220px-Map_of_Culebra_highlighting_Fraile.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="128" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Map_of_Culebra_highlighting_Fraile.png/330px-Map_of_Culebra_highlighting_Fraile.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Map_of_Culebra_highlighting_Fraile.png/440px-Map_of_Culebra_highlighting_Fraile.png 2x" data-file-width="768" data-file-height="448" /></a><figcaption>Map of Culebra highlighting Fraile barrio which includes Culebrita</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Culebrita is a <a href="/wiki/Coral" title="Coral">coral</a> island approximately 1 mile in length and a tourist spot.<sup id="cite_ref-Williams_2015_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Williams_2015-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is roughly y-shaped with three branches extending from the island\'s center. <i>Punta del Este</i> is the easternmost landmass of the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Commonwealth of Puerto Rico">Commonwealth of Puerto Rico</a>.\n</p><p>There are six beaches on Culebrita, the chief one being <i>Playa Tortuga</i> (Turtle Beach) on the north side of the island. The beach is named for the many <a href="/wiki/Sea_turtle" title="Sea turtle">sea turtles</a> that use the beach for breeding grounds and the surrounding waters for grazing. Sea turtles Tina and Ike call Playa Tortuga home. The other two large beaches are Trash Beach and West Beach.  Because Trash Beach is located on the <a href="/wiki/Windward" class="mw-redirect" title="Windward">windward</a> side of the island, debris is often blown onto this beach. This is how the beach gets its name. West Beach is where the <a href="/wiki/Water_taxi" title="Water taxi">water taxis</a> from <a href="/wiki/Culebra_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="Culebra Island">Culebra Island</a> dock.<sup id="cite_ref-About.com_Culebrita_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-About.com_Culebrita-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Culebrita_chart.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Culebrita_chart.jpg/200px-Culebrita_chart.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="183" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Culebrita_chart.jpg/300px-Culebrita_chart.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Culebrita_chart.jpg/400px-Culebrita_chart.jpg 2x" data-file-width="650" data-file-height="596" /></a><figcaption>Chart of Culebrita, with Cayo Botella to the northwest</figcaption></figure>\n<p>There are also large <a href="/wiki/Tidal_pool" class="mw-redirect" title="Tidal pool">tidal pools</a> on the east side of the island where people lounge as if they were large baths. The pools trap small sea life at low tide.  The west side of the island contains two <a href="/wiki/Lagoon" title="Lagoon">lagoons</a>. The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Culebrita_Reef&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Culebrita Reef (page does not exist)">Culebrita Reef</a> lays off the southern coast of the island.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Wetlands">Wetlands</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Culebrita_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Wetlands"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Isla Culebrita has two shallow <a href="/wiki/Lagoon" title="Lagoon">lagoons</a>, the largest of which,  <i>Laguna de Molino</i> (Mill Lagoon), is located in northwest branch of the island. It covers approximately 5.5 acres (2.2&#160;ha) and is about 3 feet (0.91&#160;m) below sea level. The second lagoon, referred to as the eastern <a href="/wiki/Tidal_flat" class="mw-redirect" title="Tidal flat">tidal flat</a>, is located near the middle of the island\'s west coast and covers about 0.9 acres (0.36&#160;ha). Both lagoons are bordered on the seaward-side by a small mangrove fringe.<sup id="cite_ref-2006_Culebrita_Environmental_Survey_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2006_Culebrita_Environmental_Survey-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> These wetlands are an important habitat for some of the local wildlife.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Flora_and_fauna">Flora and fauna</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Culebrita_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Flora and fauna"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure 2x" data-file-width="3888" data-file-height="2592" /></a><figcaption>Cacti growing on Culebrita</figcaption></figure>\n<p>In 2006, an environmental and cultural resource survey of Culebrita  was plants and animals that may exist\xe2\x80\x9d on Isla Culebrita. During the survey, a total of 97 plant species were recorded, none of which are state or federally threatened or endangered species. In addition, 32 birds, four reptiles, and two mammals were recorded. Of these animals, one federally endangered species, the <a href="/wiki/Brown_pelican" title="Brown pelican">brown pelican</a> (<i>Pelecanus occidentalis</i>) and one threatened species, the <a href="/wiki/White-cheeked_pintail" title="White-cheeked pintail">white-cheeked pintail</a> (<i>Anas bahamensis</i>) were observed. Endangered <a href="/wiki/Green_sea_turtle" title="Green sea turtle">green sea turtles</a> (<i>Chelonia mydas</i>) were also observed on the island.<sup
