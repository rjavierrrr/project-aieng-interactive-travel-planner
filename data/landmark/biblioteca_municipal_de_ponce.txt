Marin">Ramon Marin</a>.</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Ponce Municipal Library</b>, formally, <i><b>Biblioteca Municipal Mariana Su\xc3\xa1rez de Longo</b></i> (English: Mariana Su\xc3\xa1rez de Longo Municipal Library), and also known as <b>Biblioteca Publica de Ponce</b> (English: Ponce Public Library), is the library system of the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>. Founded in 1870, it is the oldest public library in Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The system has its main library on <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Pou" title="Miguel Pou">Miguel Pou Boulevard</a>, in barrio <a href="/wiki/San_Ant%C3%B3n" title="San Ant\xc3\xb3n">San Ant\xc3\xb3n</a>, in the city of Ponce, and seven satellite library branches, three in the city\'s urban area and four spread out in the municipality\'s rural areas. The main library inaugurated a new building on Bulevar Miguel Pou in August 2007,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> where the former Puerto Rico District Court building was located.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The central library building on Bulevar Miguel Pou was designed by Ponce architect Juan Dalmau Sambol\xc3\xadn.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_Municipal_Library&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Gabinete_de_Lectura"><i>Gabinete de Lectura</i></h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_Municipal_Library&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Gabinete de Lectura"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The origins of the Ponce Municipal Library date to the establishment in Ponce of the "Gabinete de Lectura" in 1869\xe2\x80\x931870, founded by <a href="/wiki/Alejandro_Tapia_y_Rivera" title="Alejandro Tapia y Rivera">Alejandro Tapia y Rivera</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The books from the <i>Gabinete de Lectura</i>, the personal collection of Don Miguel Rosich and miscellaneous purchases were used to stock the Public Library.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Gabinete de Lectura was the first educational, cultural, and scientific center in Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Gabinete was established in 1870 by Federico Perez, Antonio Molina Jr., Diego Vicente Texeira, Rafael Rodriguez, Luis R. Velazquez, Angel Aguerrevera, and <a href="/wiki/Eduardo_Neumann_Gand%C3%ADa" title="Eduardo Neumann Gand\xc3\xada">Eduardo Neumann Gand\xc3\xada</a>, and was located on Calle Sol.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The central government in San Juan closed it down in 1874 out of fear that the common people would become educated and rebel against the government. However, it was reopened in 1876 by a group of people that included some of its original founders plus Dr. <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Pujals" title="Rafael Pujals">Rafael Pujals</a>, Oscar Schuck, Alfredo Casals, Antonio Molina, Ramon Rivera, Jacobo Tur, Sergio Cuevas Zequeira, Juan Cuevas Aboy, Oriol Pasarell, Francisco Oliver, Manuel Mayoral Barnes, and Manuel Yordan.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was re-established on 8 April 1877 with <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Pujals" title="Rafael Pujals">Rafael Pujals</a>, Jose A. Renta, Julio Chardon, <a href="/wiki/Ramon_Marin" class="mw-redirect" title="Ramon Marin">Ramon Marin</a>, Baldomero San Antonio, Luis Lassise, Antonio Perez Guerra, Eduardo Salichs, Juan J. Mayoral, Pedro P. Valdivieso, <a href="/wiki/Eduardo_Neumann_Gandia" class="mw-redirect" title="Eduardo Neumann Gandia">Eduardo Neumann Gandia</a>, Rafael Rodriguez, and Luis Velazquez as its directors.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="The_first_library">The first library</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_Municipal_Library&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The first library"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Ponce Municipal Library was formally founded in 1890<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and re-organized in 1894.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This first library was located on the first floor of <a href="/wiki/Ponce_City_Hall" title="Ponce City Hall">Casa Alcaldia</a> building,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> on what is now Calle <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Degetau" title="Plaza Degetau">Plaza Degetau</a>, but at the time was considered part of Calle Villa. It contained 1,500 volumes that came from the "Gabinete de Lectura", the private collection of Manuel Rosich, a prominent local educator, and donations from citizens at large.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The first librarian was Joaquin Figueroa.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The library started with 809 books and 669 pamphlets.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> By 1913, the total number of volumes was over 4,754,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> including 2,818 books and 1,936 periodicals.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1937, the library was restarted under the leadership of <a href="/wiki/Luis_A._Ferre" class="mw-redirect" title="Luis A. Ferre">Luis A. Ferre</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The library later relocated to Calle Isabel in the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce Historic Zone</a>. In 1940, mayor <a href="/wiki/Jose_Tormos_Diego" class="mw-redirect" title="Jose Tormos Diego">Jose Tormos Diego</a> reconditioned the northern annex of <a href="/wiki/Teatro_La_Perla" title="Teatro La Perla">Teatro La Perla</a> to be used as the headquarters of the Ponce municipal library.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span Ferre">Luis A. Ferre</a> was one of the sponsors of the Ponce Public Library and, in the 1950s, several of the works of art that would later be part of the collections at <a href="/wiki/Museo_de_Arte_de_Ponce" title="Museo de Arte de Ponce">Museo de Arte de Ponce</a>, first hung from the walls of the Public Library on the second floor of Teatro La Perla.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1971, construction on the north side of <a href="/wiki/Teatro_La_Perla" title="Teatro La Perla">Teatro La Perla</a> was completed<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and, during the administration of mayor <a href="/wiki/Juan_H._Cintr%C3%B3n_Garc%C3%ADa" title="Juan H. Cintr\xc3\xb3n Garc\xc3\xada">Juan H. Cintron</a> (1968\xe2\x80\x931972), the library moved to new facilities next to Teatro La Perla,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The building cost was $8.5M USD, with class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>40<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span href="/wiki/Rafael_Churumba_Cordero" class="mw-redirect" title="Rafael Churumba Cordero">Rafael Churumba Cordero</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>42<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Main_library_building">Main library building</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_Municipal_Library&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Main library building"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The main library is located in a new building on Miguel Pou Boulevard, barrio San Anton, Ponce, at 18\xc2\xb0 0\' 44" N, 66\xc2\xb0 36\' 12" W. The new structure opened in August 2007 under the administration of Mayor <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Zayas_Seijo" title="Francisco Zayas Seijo">Francisco Zayas Seijo</a><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and cost $14 million to build.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>44<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The new building is located at the site of the old <i>Tribunal de Ponce</i> (English: Ponce Judicial Center),<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>45<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> which was demolished to give way for the library.\n</p><p>Facilities also include an exhibition hall for art expositions and other similar public events of cultural and civic nature. The library\'s courtyard provides unrestricted wireless Internet access per the library\'s hotspot policy and provides an environment suitable for book presentations, storytelling activities, lectures and cocktails.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>46<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Mariana_Su\xc3\xa1rez_de_Longo"><span id="Mariana_Su.C3.A1rez_de_Longo"></span>Mariana Su\xc3\xa1rez de Longo</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_Municipal_Library&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mariana Su\xc3\xa1rez de Longo"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Main Library was named after Mariana Su\xc3\xa1rez de Longo, a much admired \xe2\x80\x9cTeacher of Teachers\xe2\x80\x9d. Suarez de Longo was born on 3 January 1906 in <a href="/wiki/Aibonito,_Puerto_Rico" title="Aibonito, Puerto Rico">Aibonito, Puerto Rico</a>, graduated from <a href="/wiki/Ponce_High_School" title="Ponce High School">Ponce High School</a> in 1923, and obtained a bachelor\'s degree in education from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Rio_Piedras" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Puerto Rico at Rio Piedras">University of Puerto Rico at Rio Piedras</a> in 1935.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>47<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Suarez de Longo then committed almost 40 years of her life to the education of the island\'s youth. In 1946, she became the first Puerto Rican woman to be designated School Superintendent, commanding the Ponce area from 1950 to 1962. In 1954 she obtained a master\'s degree in Management and Supervision from <a href="/wiki/New_York_University" title="New York University">New York University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>48<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> From 1963 to 1966 she directed the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Education" title="Puerto Rico Department of Education">Department of Education</a>\xe2\x80\x99s southern region, comprising fourteen <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipalities</a>. In 1966 she served as director of the Division of Curriculum of Puerto Rico\'s Department of Education. In 1969, Suarez de Longo directed the Staff Training Center of the Ponce Municipal <a href="/wiki/Head_Start_Program" class="mw-redirect" title="Head Start Program">Head Start</a> program.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>49<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The devoted educator retired in 1972.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>50<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> She died in Ponce on 13 January 1995.<sup class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Research_branch">Research branch</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_Municipal_Library&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Research branch"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Originally envisioned to be co-located with the public library was the <a href="/wiki/Archivo_Hist%C3%B3rico_de_Ponce" title="Archivo Hist\xc3\xb3rico de Ponce">Archivo Hist\xc3\xb3rico de Ponce</a> (Ponce Historical Archive). The Archivo Hist\xc3\xb3rico de Ponce is a library research tool about the municipality, and is currently (2011) located on Calle Marina and Plaza Degetau in the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce Historic Zone</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>71<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2016, it moved to the renovated <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Servicios" title="Ponce Servicios">Ponce Servicios</a> Building.\n</p><p>The Ponce Historical Archive boasts collections of newspapers published in the city from the 18th through the 21st century, including La Gaceta de Puerto Rico, El Aguila de Puerto Rico, El Universal, <a href="/wiki/El_Nuevo_Dia" class="mw-redirect" title="El Nuevo Dia">El Diario de Puerto Rico</a> among others.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>72<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Also archived there is <a href="/wiki/La_Perla_del_Sur" title="La Perla del Sur">La Perla del Sur</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Services">Services</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_Municipal_Library&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Services"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Ponce Municipal Library offers among its services a nearly half-million dollars virtual education center with access to over 30 digital services covering subjects from Auto Repair to Health and Medicine to Literature to encyclopedias and dictionaries with a fleet of some 150 computers. There is a <i>Mi Rinc\xc3\xb3n de Lectura</i> (English: Children\'s Reading Corner) providing a setting for parents who want to read to their kids. The two-story main library structure also has a youth library, sponsored by the international children\'s literature introductory program known as PIALI, <i>Programa Internacional de Acercamiento a la Literatura Infantil</i> (English: International Children\'s Literature Introductory Program).<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>73<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The library is also a center for the temporary exposition of visual arts.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>74<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Head_librarians">Head librarians</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_Municipal_Library&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Head librarians"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<ul><li>J.
