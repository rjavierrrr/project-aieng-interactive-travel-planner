2</a></figcaption></figure>\n<p><b>Bayam\xc3\xb3n City Hall</b> is a building in <a href="/wiki/Bayam%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico" title="Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico">Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico</a>. As the name suggests, it serves as city hall to that city. It is named after Don \xc3\x81ngel Rivera Rodr\xc3\xadguez, who was mayor of Bayam\xc3\xb3n from 1934 to 1944.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Bayam\xc3\xb3n City Hall was inaugurated in 1980 by Mayor <a href="/wiki/Ram%C3%B3n_Luis_Rivera" title="Ram\xc3\xb3n Luis Rivera">Ram\xc3\xb3n Luis Rivera</a> at the cost of $7.2 million. Rivera stepped down as mayor in 2000, giving way for his son <a href="/wiki/Ram%C3%B3n_Luis_Rivera,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Ram\xc3\xb3n Luis Rivera, Jr.">Ram\xc3\xb3n Luis Rivera, Jr.</a>, to become mayor. Since its inauguration, it\'s been occupied by mayors representing the <a href="/wiki/Partido_Nuevo_Progresista" class="mw-redirect" title="Partido Nuevo Progresista">Partido Nuevo Progresista</a> (PNP), a political party that advocates statehood for Puerto Rico.\n</p><p>Located next to <a href="/wiki/El_Parque_del_tren" class="mw-redirect" title="El Parque del tren">El Parque del tren</a>, the city hall is considered a tourist attraction, as it was built over an existing avenue to use its air rights. Visitors park next to the building, walk to the avenue, and board an elevator to reach the offices.\n</p><p>According to the building\'s architects, the reason for building it over the highway is because the city of Bayam\xc3\xb3n did not own property large enough within the city for a city hall building.<sup id="cite_ref-Architects_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Architects-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The second floor has a glass-window bridge, from where the visitors can cross from one side to the other, while looking at cars pass by. The upper levels are all for office activities, including citizen complaints, city planning, etc.\n</p><p>The building includes a theatre presenting live drama. This enables the building to be used and attract people in the evenings as well as during the day.\n</p><p>The Bayam\xc3\xb3n City Hall is painted gray. For a long time, it was the only building in <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a> that was built over an avenue.\n</p><p>In 2022, <a href="/wiki/Federal_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Federal Emergency Management Agency">FEMA</a> allocated close to $5 million for work on Bayam\xc3\xb3n City Hall.<sup
