class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cerro_de_Punta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountain</a> is part of the <a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Cordillera Central, Puerto Rico">Cordillera Central</a> and is located on the municipality of Jayuya, Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EPR_English_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EPR_English-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gnis_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gnis-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The access road to the point closest to the highest elevation is from the municipality of Jayuya.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Toro_Negro_State_Forest" title="Toro Negro State Forest">Toro Negro Forest Reserve</a>, and it has been described as "an alpine runt."<sup id="cite_ref-guide2cv_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guide2cv-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is located on the western end of the Toro Negro State Park. The mountain is just north of east-to-westbound <a href="/wiki/PR-143" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-143">Route 143</a>. It is located at coordinates 18.172458 and -66.591839W. The nearest populated place to Cerro de Punta is <i>Urbanizaci\xc3\xb3n Vega Linda</i>, located 3.3 miles (5.3&#160;km) away.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geology">Geology</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cerro_de_Punta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Unlike many <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> mountains, Cerro de Punta is not a <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcano</a> but simply the highest point in the <a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Cordillera Central, Puerto Rico">Cordillera Central</a>. Cordillera Central is the central mountain range that divides the island by running in an east\xe2\x80\x93west fashion.<sup id="cite_ref-puertorico_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-puertorico-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Best_view">Best view</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cerro_de_Punta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Best view"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The view from atop Cerro de Punta is said to be "the best view in all of Puerto Rico". On a clear day, it is possible to see virtually the entire island, including as far as <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, which is over 75 miles (120&#160;km) away.<sup id="cite_ref-puertorico_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-puertorico-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  There are a number of radio and television transmission and re-transmission towers just off the top of the mountain.<sup id="cite_ref-bkcave.com_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bkcave.com-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  There is an observation platform at the top of the mountain.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Wildlife_and_flora">Wildlife and flora</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cerro_de_Punta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Wildlife and flora"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The mountain is home to an abundant amount of wildlife, lush vegetation, flowering shrubs and trees, and numerous <a href="/wiki/Waterfall" title="Waterfall">waterfalls</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The mountain is covered by <a href="/wiki/Prestoea_montana" class="mw-redirect" title="Prestoea montana">Sierra palm</a> trees.<sup id="cite_ref-bkcave.com_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bkcave.com-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Various plants, including some endangered species are found here,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> such as the endangered <a href="/wiki/Fern" title="Fern">fern</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Elaphoglossum_serpens" title="Elaphoglossum serpens">Elaphoglossum serpens</a></i> which is found only on this mountain and nowhere else in the world,<sup id="cite_ref-rec_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rec-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and Cook\'s holly (<i><a href="/wiki/Ilex_cookii" title="Ilex cookii">Ilex cookii</a></i>) or <i>planta de te</i>, which is only found here and in neighboring <a href="/wiki/Monte_Jayuya" title="Monte Jayuya">Mount Jayuya</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Nearby_roads">Nearby roads</h2><span
