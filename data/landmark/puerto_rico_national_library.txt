Rico National Library</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Biblioteca Nacional de Puerto Rico</i>) is the <a href="/wiki/National_library" title="National library">national library</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. It was created in 1967 as the <i><b>Biblioteca General de Puerto Rico</b></i> (Puerto Rico General Library) by Joint Resolution No. 44 of the Puerto Rico Legislature.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It opened on April 11, 1973.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  In 2003 it was renamed <i><b>Biblioteca Nacional de Puerto Rico</b></i> by Act 188 of August 17 of that year.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The library is ascribed to the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Puerto_Rican_Culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Institute of Puerto Rican Culture">Institute of Puerto Rican Culture</a>.\n</p><p>The Library shares its nineteenth-century classical building, (at one time the <a href="/wiki/Bacardi_Rum" class="mw-redirect" title="Bacardi Rum">Bacardi Rum</a> flagship factory<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>), with the <a href="/wiki/Archivo_General_de_Puerto_Rico" title="Archivo General de Puerto Rico">General Archives of Puerto Rico</a>. The Library\'s <a href="/wiki/Special_collections" title="Special collections">specialized collections</a> include the Dominican Collection of leather-bound religious books dating from the 16th-19th centuries, the <a href="/wiki/Eugenio_Mar%C3%ADa_de_Hostos" title="Eugenio Mar\xc3\xada de Hostos">Eugenio Maria de Hostos</a> Collection, which includes 1,300 digitized manuscripts, and the private collection of <a href="/wiki/Concha_Mel%C3%A9ndez" title="Concha Mel\xc3\xa9ndez">Concha Mel\xc3\xa9ndez</a>, literary critic and former professor at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico" title="University of Puerto Rico">University of Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The building is located in the <a href="/wiki/Puerta_de_Tierra,_San_Juan" title="Puerta de Tierra, San Juan">Puerta de Tierra historic district</a> on the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Islet" title="San Juan Islet">Islet of San Juan</a>. It faces the <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Rivera_Park" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera Park">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera Park</a>. It is fully air\xe2\x80\x90conditioned and has free Internet access. The Library and Archives share a 119-seat amphitheater at street level where each one has its individual side desk, with an unobstructed view.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The building was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1976 under its former name, <b>Carcel de Puerta de Tierra</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-nris_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and on the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Register_of_Historic_Sites_and_Zones" title="Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones">Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones</a> in 2000 under the name <i><b>Archivo y Biblioteca General</b></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading
