class="category"><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Status</th><td class="infobox-data">Opened all year, 24 hours/day</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Plaza Degetau</b>, formally <b>Plaza Federico Degetau</b>, is the larger of two <a href="/wiki/Plaza" class="mw-redirect" title="Plaza">plazas</a> at <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Delicias" title="Plaza Las Delicias">Plaza Las Delicias</a>, the main city <a href="/wiki/Town_square" title="Town square">square</a> in the city of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The other plaza is named <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Mu%C3%B1oz_Rivera" title="Plaza Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera">Plaza Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera</a> and is located north of Plaza Degetau. The square is notable for its <a href="/wiki/Fountain" title="Fountain">fountains</a> and for the various monuments it contains. The historic <a href="/wiki/Parque_de_Bombas" title="Parque de Bombas">Parque de Bombas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Cathedral" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce Cathedral">Ponce Cathedral</a> buildings are located bordering the north side of this plaza. The square is the center of the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce Historic Zone</a>, and it is flanked by the historic <a href="/wiki/Ponce_City_Hall" title="Ponce City Hall">Ponce City Hall</a> to the south, the cathedral and historic firehouse to the north, the <a href="/wiki/NRHP" class="mw-redirect" title="NRHP">NRHP</a>-listed <a href="/wiki/Banco_Cr%C3%A9dito_y_Ahorro_Ponce%C3%B1o_(building)" title="Banco Cr\xc3\xa9dito y Ahorro Ponce\xc3\xb1o (building)">Banco Cr\xc3\xa9dito y Ahorro Ponce\xc3\xb1o</a> and <a href="/wiki/Banco_de_Ponce_(building)" title="Banco de Ponce (building)">Banco de Ponce</a> buildings to the east, and the <a href="/wiki/Armstrong-Poventud_Residence" class="mw-redirect" title="Armstrong-Poventud Residence">Armstrong-Poventud Residence</a> to the west. The square dates back to the early Spanish settlement in Ponce of 1670. It is the main tourist attraction of the city, receiving about a quarter of a million visitors per year.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span town\'s main square, or plaza, was the center of the town. In the case of Ponce, a <a href="/wiki/Catholic_church" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholic church">Catholic church</a> was built on the center of the plaza, thus splitting the plaza into two sections.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Plaza Degetau (the subject of this article) is the southern of the two sections (see "Diagram of Plaza Las Delicias" herein), with the other plaza, <i><a href="/wiki/Plaza_Mu%C3%B1oz_Rivera" title="Plaza Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera">Plaza Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera</a></i>, located to the north of Plaza <a href="/wiki/Federico_Degetau" title="Federico Degetau">Federico Degetau</a>. Plaza Degetau measures 6,400 square meters.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The history of Plaza Degetau dates back to as far as the creation of the first Catholic chapel in Ponce in 1670.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is also known that around 1840 Mayor <a href="/wiki/Salvador_de_Vives" title="Salvador de Vives">Salvador de Vives</a> planted trees as a renovation project for plaza.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was first lit in 1864.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The plaza, as it stands today, was designed by architect <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Porrata-Doria" class="mw-redirect" title="Francisco Porrata-Doria">Francisco Porrata-Doria</a> in 1914.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In addition to the cathedral and the firehouse, Plaza Degetau at one point also contained an open dining <a href="/wiki/Moorish_architecture" title="Moorish architecture">Moorish</a>-style kiosk that had been part of the 1882 Fair Exposition.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The kiosk was still present at the time of the American invasion of the island in 1898 as reported by American photo-journalist <a href="/wiki/William_Dinwiddie" title="William Dinwiddie">William Dinwiddie</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> but it was demolished in 1914.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Name">Name</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and later, <i>Plaza Mayor</i> (Main Plaza).<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In the early 20th century its name was officially changed to Plaza Federico Degetau, in honor of <a href="/wiki/Federico_Degetau" title="Federico Degetau">Federico Degetau</a>, the first <a href="/wiki/Resident_Commissioner_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Resident Commissioner of Puerto Rico">Resident Commissioner of Puerto Rico</a> to the United States House of Representatives.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Features">Features</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Degetau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Features"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Fuente_de_los_Leones_-_Ponce_Puerto_Rico.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Fuente_de_los_Leones_-_Ponce_Puerto_Rico.jpg/300px-Fuente_de_los_Leones_-_Ponce_Puerto_Rico.jpg" /></a><figcaption>The Lions Fountain on Plaza Degetau</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Plaza Degetau is perhaps the best known of the two plazas and the one most often seen in pictures.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Fuente_de_los_Leones">Fuente de los Leones</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Degetau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Fuente de los Leones"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In the center of this plaza lies the famous <i>Fuente de los Leones</i> (Lions Fountain). The large, round-shaped fountain is bounded by a low, marble and granite wall. The fountain\'s wall boundary is shaped in the form of a regular octagon and built so that one of the vertices of the octagon points towards the historic Ponce City Hall. The fountain also features four lion statues and water that flows under colored lighting effects. The four lion statues are located one statue on each alternating vertex of the fountain\'s octagonal boundary wall.\n</p><p>In 1878 the spot now occupied by Fuente de los Leones was occupied by a monument to the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Constitution_of_1812" title="Spanish Constitution of 1812">Spanish Constitution of 1812</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It had been erected under the direction of 1812 mayor of Ponce <a href="/wiki/Jose_Ortiz_de_la_Renta" class="mw-redirect" title="Jose Ortiz de la Renta">Jose Ortiz de la Renta</a>, ca. 1820, but was removed in the 1870s by <a href="/wiki/Carlism" title="Carlism">Carlist</a> mayor and Spanish military officer <a href="/wiki/Elicio_Berriz" title="Elicio Berriz">Elicio Berriz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>note 1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1882, the location was then occupied by "Arab kiosk" (a.k.a., "Kiosko La Alhambra"<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>) built in 1882 for the 1882 Ponce Fair. The kiosk was demolished in 1914.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The current (2022) fountain was purchased in 1939 at the <a href="/wiki/1939_New_York_World%27s_Fair" title="1939 New York World&#39;s Fair">New York World\'s Fair</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>note 2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> When originally installed, it was adorned with baby angel sculptures, but in the early 1940s the baby angels were replaced with the current (2022) lions. The lions were sculptured in 1940 by Victor Cott, a sculptor from <a href="/wiki/Juana_Diaz,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Juana Diaz, Puerto Rico">Juana Diaz</a>, during the mayoral administration of mayor <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Grillasca_Salas" title="Andr\xc3\xa9s Grillasca Salas">Andr\xc3\xa9s Grillasca Salas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The fountain, including a mechanical basement, was remodeled and restored in 1993. Its base was enlarged and a computerized lighting system was installed.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The fountain was the inspiration for a poem published in 2002,<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span mw-heading3"><h3 id="Juan_Morel_Campos">Juan Morel Campos</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Degetau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Juan Morel Campos"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1236090951">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}@media print{body.ns-0 .mw-parser-output .hatnote{display:none!important}}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Juan_Morel_Campos_(statue)" title="Juan Morel Campos (statue)">Juan Morel Campos (statue)</a></div>\n<p>This plaza also features a statue of native <a href="/wiki/Composer" title="Composer">composer</a> <a href="/wiki/Juan_Morel_Campos" title="Juan Morel Campos">Juan Morel Campos</a>. This statue was produced at the workshop of Italian sculptor Luiggi Tomassi.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Other_features">Other features</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Degetau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other features"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Also in this plaza is an <a href="/wiki/Obelisk" title="Obelisk">obelisk</a> in honor of the <a href="/wiki/Firefighters" class="mw-redirect" title="Firefighters">firefighters</a> who fought the "El Polvor\xc3\xadn" fire (see <a href="/wiki/Parque_de_Bombas" title="Parque de Bombas">Parque de Bombas</a>). The obelisk was unveiled in 1948, in time for the 50th anniversary of the frightful fire.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In the northwest side of the plaza and facing northwest, there is also a statue, called <i>Blind Justice</i>, of a woman in a long dress with her eyes covered by a cloth wrapped around the top of her head. The woman\'s left hand holds a sword that sits inside a shaft which rests on the ground, and there are two children sitting happily by her feet: one is embracing the lower part of the sword\'s shaft and the other child is playing with an orange tree branch. Blind Justice sits on the northwest area of the plaza and faces northwest.\n</p><p>A second statue, <i>Maternity,</i> sits on the plaza as well. This one consists of a woman sitting down and sitting two small children on her lap, one child sits on her left leg and the other one on her right leg, while the children lean against her chest. Maternity sits on the southwest side of the plaza and faces southwest. These two statues were designed by Victor Cott, a sculptor from <a href="/wiki/Juana_Diaz" class="mw-redirect" title="Juana Diaz">Juana Diaz</a> who also designed the four lions at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fuente_de_los_Leones&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fuente de los Leones (page does not exist)">Fuente de los Leones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Esclavo_libertado" class="mw-redirect" title="Esclavo libertado">Esclavo libertado</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Labradora&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="La Labradora (page does not exist)">La Labradora</a>.\n</p><p><i><a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Labradora&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="La Labradora (page does not exist)">La Labradora</a></i> is a third statue that used to be located on Plaza Degetau, on the southeast section of the plaza, facing southeast, but today (2018) adorns <a href="/wiki/Parque_Graciela_Rivera" title="Parque Graciela Rivera">Parque Graciela Rivera</a>. La Labradora, together with a fourth sculpture yet called <i>El Cuerno de la Abundancia</i> (<a href="/wiki/Horn_of_abundance" class="mw-redirect" title="Horn of abundance">The Horn of Abundance</a>), were moved out of the plaza in the late 1940s to make room for the <a href="/wiki/Monumento_a_los_heroes_de_El_Polvor%C3%ADn_(obelisk)" class="mw-redirect" title="Monumento a los heroes de El Polvor\xc3\xadn (obelisk)">Monumento a los heroes de El Polvor\xc3\xadn</a> (1948) and the <a href="/wiki/Juan_Morel_Campos_(statue)" title="Juan Morel Campos (statue)">statue of Juan Morel Campos</a> (~1950), respectively. El Cuerno de la Abundancia was located on the northeast section of the plaza and faced northeast but, in the late 1940s, was relocated to elsewhere in the city of Ponce; unfortunately, it was vandalized at the new location and was lost.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The placing of the statues as well as the relocation of the last two statues, occurred during the 16-year mayoral administration of <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Grillasca_Salas" title="Andr\xc3\xa9s Grillasca Salas">Andr\xc3\xa9s Grillasca Salas</a> in the 1940s-1950s.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Setting">Setting</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Degetau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Setting"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Plaza Degetau is bounded on the north by the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Cathedral" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce Cathedral">Our Lady of Guadalupe Cathedral</a> and the historic <a href="/wiki/Parque_de_Bombas" title="Parque de Bombas">Parque de Bombas</a> firehouse, on the south by Plaza Degetau street (also called Villa street and Comercio street), on the west by Union street, and on the east by Marina street. It is surrounded by two hotels, the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_City_Hall" title="Ponce City Hall">Ponce City Hall</a>, two historic banks (<a href="/wiki/Banco_de_Ponce_(building)" title="Banco de Ponce (building)">Banco de Ponce</a> and <a href="/wiki/Banco_Cr%C3%A9dito_y_Ahorro_Ponce%C3%B1o_(building)" title="Banco Cr\xc3\xa9dito y Ahorro Ponce\xc3\xb1o (building)">Banco Cr\xc3\xa9dito</a>), a long-standing ice cream parlor called "<a href="/wiki/King%27s_Ice_Cream" class="mw-redirect" title="King&#39;s Ice Cream">King\'s Ice Cream</a>", and various boutiques and cafes.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The plaza has wide mosaic-tile sidewalks, well-manicured flower gardens, well-trimmed bushes and <a href="/wiki/Ficus_microcarpa" title="Ficus microcarpa">Indian laurel</a> trees, late 1800s lamposts, and numerous marble benches. It is home to the Lions Fountain, "one of the most beautiful fountains in Puerto Rico."<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The fountain is made of marble and bronze. During the day, the plaza hustles with schoolchildren, shoppers, and tourists. After the sun sets, there are oftentimes live bands giving concerts to "multigenerational families."<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Gallery">Gallery</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span Furthermore, Harry S. Pariser states that Fuente de los Leones was purchased in 1939 (See Harry S. Pariser. <i>Explore Puerto Rico.</i> p.243. <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://books.google.com/books?id=KawuqbFxLS0C&amp;pg=PT239">here</a>), so it could not have been inaugurated in 1902, almost 40 years earlier. One explanation could be that after the Arab Kiosk was demolished in 1914 (and until 1939) there was a, perhaps, smaller and simpler fountain at this location which the fountain from the 1939 NYC World\'s Fair (the current one) then replaced. However, this would still not explain Oyoroa Santaliz\'s claim that <i>"in 1902, the "Fuente de los Leones", which we still enjoy today, was inaugurated"</i>, because it would be contradictory to Socorro Gir\xc3\xb3n\'s statement that the kiosk at that location was demolished in 1914, twelve years after Oyoroa Santaliz\'s alleged inauguration of the fountain there. Another explanation yet could be that the Arab kiosk, though located at the same plaza as the current Fuente de Los Leones, may have, in fact, been located further east of the current location of Fuente de Los Leones, permitting Fuente de Los Leones to co-exist next to the Arab kiosk from 1902 to 1914, when the kiosk was demolished.</span>\n</li>\n</ol></div></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="References">References</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Degetau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: References"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1239543626"><div class="reflist">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap mw-references-columns"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Socorro Giron. <i>Ponce, el teatro La Perla y La Campana de la Almudaina.</i> Gobierno Municipal de Ponce.
