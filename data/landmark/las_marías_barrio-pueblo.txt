class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The central plaza, or square, is a place for official and unofficial recreational events and a place where people can gather and socialize from dusk to dawn. The <a href="/wiki/Laws_of_the_Indies" title="Laws of the Indies">Laws of the Indies</a>, Spanish law, which regulated life in Puerto Rico in the early 19th century, stated the plaza\'s purpose was for "the parties" (celebrations, festivities) (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">a prop\xc3\xb3sito para las fiestas</i>), and that the square should be proportionally large enough for the number of neighbors (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">grandeza proporcionada al\nn\xc3\xbamero de vecinos</i>). These Spanish regulations also stated that the streets nearby should be comfortable portals for passersby, protecting them from the elements: sun and rain.<sup id="cite_ref-Santullano_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Santullano-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Located across the central plaza in Las Mar\xc3\xadas barrio-pueblo is the <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Parroquia Inmaculado Coraz\xc3\xb3n de Mar\xc3\xada</i></span>, a Roman Catholic church.<sup id="cite_ref-Mari_Mut_2013_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mari_Mut_2013-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Las_Mar%C3%ADas_barrio-pueblo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Las Mar\xc3\xadas barrio-pueblo was in Spain\'s <a href="/wiki/Gazetteer" title="Gazetteer">gazetteers</a><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> until Puerto Rico was ceded by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_of_1898" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Paris of 1898">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became an <a href="/wiki/Insular_area" title="Insular area">unincorporated territory of the United States</a>. In 1899, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_War" title="United States Department of War">United States Department of War</a> conducted a <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of Puerto Rico finding that the combined population of Las Mar\xc3\xadas Pueblo and Maravilla barrios was 1,235. (At the time, the census takers didn\'t distinguish between the current three separate <i>Maravilla</i> barrios- <a href="/wiki/Maravilla_Este" title="Maravilla Este">Maravilla Este</a>, <a
