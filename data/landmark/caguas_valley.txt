River">Turabo River</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Caguas Valley</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Valle de Caguas</i>), or the <b>Caguas-Juncos Valley</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and popularly referred to as the <b><a href="/wiki/Turabo_River" title="Turabo River">Turabo</a> Valley</b> (<i>Valle del Turabo</i>), is a large valley lying between two <a href="/wiki/Mountain_range" title="Mountain range">mountain subranges</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Cordillera Central (Puerto Rico)">Cordillera Central</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sierra_de_Cayey" title="Sierra de Cayey">Sierra de Cayey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Luquillo" class="mw-redirect" title="Sierra Luquillo">Sierra de Luquillo</a>, in the eastern region of the <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Geography of Puerto Rico">main island</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> From west to east, the valley is concentrated in the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Caguas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Caguas, Puerto Rico">Caguas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gurabo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Gurabo, Puerto Rico">Gurabo</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Lorenzo,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Lorenzo, Puerto Rico">San Lorenzo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juncos,_Puerto_Rico" title="Juncos, Puerto Rico">Juncos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Las_Piedras,_Puerto_Rico" title="Las Piedras, Puerto Rico">Las Piedras</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Humacao,_Puerto_Rico" title="Humacao, Puerto Rico">Humacao</a>. \n</p>\n<figure typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Topography_of_the_Commonwealth_of_Puerto_Rico_by_USDA.jpg" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caguas_Valley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:2021riogurabo.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/2021riogurabo.jpg/220px-2021riogurabo.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="151" class="mw-file-element" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/2021riogurabo.jpg/440px-2021riogurabo.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3823" data-file-height="2627" /></a><figcaption>The Gurabo River is an important tributary in the valley</figcaption></figure>\n<p>In a clockwise direction, the valley is bound by the <i>Altos de La Mesa</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Altos_de_San_Luis" title="Altos de San Luis">Altos de San Luis</a></i> mountain ridges to the north, the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_de_Luquillo" title="Sierra de Luquillo">Sierra de Luquillo</a> in the northeast, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Lorenzo_Batholith&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="San Lorenzo Batholith (page does not exist)">San Lorenzo Batholith</a> in the east and southeast, the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_de_Cayey" title="Sierra de Cayey">Sierra de Cayey</a> in the south, and the foothills of the <i><a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Cordillera Central (Puerto Rico)">Cordillera Central</a></i> in the west.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>This valley is fed mainly by the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Lo%C3%ADza" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza">R\xc3\xado Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza</a>, the largest hydrological basin in Puerto Rico, and numerous tributaries such as the <a href="/wiki/Cag%C3%BCitas_River" title="Cag\xc3\xbcitas River">Cag\xc3\xbcitas</a>, along which the contemporary settlement of <a href="/wiki/Caguas_barrio-pueblo" title="Caguas barrio-pueblo">Caguas</a> was built. The name <b>Valle del Turabo</b> ("Turabo Valley") comes from the <a href="/wiki/Turabo_River" title="Turabo River">Turabo River</a>, which is another tributary that flows from the south. The <a href="/wiki/Gurabo_River" title="Gurabo River">Gurabo River</a>, another major tributary, feeds into the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Lo%C3%ADza" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza">Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza</a> at a region where the valley narrows into a <a href="/wiki/Rift_valley" title="Rift valley">rift valley</a>, often called the <a href="/wiki/Gurabo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Gurabo, Puerto Rico">Gurabo</a> Valley, that runs from west to east and ends in <a href="/wiki/Las_Piedras,_Puerto_Rico" title="Las Piedras, Puerto Rico">Las Piedras</a> and <a href="/wiki/Humacao,_Puerto_Rico" title="Humacao, Puerto Rico">Humacao</a> in the southeastern coast of the island.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geology">Geology</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caguas_Valley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Caguas Valley, from <a href="/wiki/Caguas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Caguas, Puerto Rico">Caguas</a> to <a href="/wiki/Juncos,_Puerto_Rico" title="Juncos, Puerto Rico">Juncos</a>, is formed by <a href="/wiki/Holocene" title="Holocene">Holocene</a> <a href="/wiki/Alluvium" title="Alluvium">alluvial deposits</a> that lay on top of hardened rock of <a href="/wiki/Volcanic_rock" title="Volcanic rock">volcanic</a> origin, <a href="/wiki/Lava" title="Lava">lava</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Intrusive_rock" title="Intrusive rock">intrusive</a> and <a href="/wiki/Metamorphic_rock" title="Metamorphic rock">metamorphic rocks</a> that date from the <a href="/wiki/Late_Cretaceous" title="Late Cretaceous">Late Cretaceous</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Tertiary" title="Tertiary">Tertiary</a> period. There are also small deposits of limestone. Except for the alluvium zone, the rocks in the basin do not generate large <a href="/wiki/Aquifer" title="Aquifer">aquifers</a> due to their low <a href="/wiki/Permeability_(Earth_sciences)" class="mw-redirect" title="Permeability (Earth sciences)">permeability</a>. The alluvium in the valley includes non-uniform mixtures of <a href="/wiki/Sand" title="Sand">sand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gravel" title="Gravel">gravel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Silt" title="Silt">silt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mud" title="Mud">mud</a>, varying in thickness from 0 feet in with consolidated rocks to 160 feet in Caguas and Gurabo. In the area towards Gurabo and Juncos the alluvium contains more sand and gravel than in the area of Caguas; these alluvial deposits form the main aquifer in the area. The flows of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Lo%C3%ADza" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza">Lo\xc3\xadza River</a> and its tributaries, mainly the Gurabo River, have eroded most alluvial deposits within the fluvial flood zones.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Climate">Climate</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caguas_Valley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The climate of the Caguas-Juncos Valley is rainy, with areas receiving up to 100 inches of precipitation annually. Rain varies throughout the year with the dry period lasting from January to April, the rainiest period being from May to June, the driest period from July to August, and the period of heavy rains lasting until the end of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="La_India_Dormida"><i>La India Dormida</i></h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caguas_Valley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: La India Dormida"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Caguas_view_from_San_Luis_Alto.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Caguas_view_from_San_Luis_Alto.jpg/220px-Caguas_view_from_San_Luis_Alto.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Caguas_view_from_San_Luis_Alto.jpg/330px-Caguas_view_from_San_Luis_Alto.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Caguas_view_from_San_Luis_Alto.jpg/440px-Caguas_view_from_San_Luis_Alto.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1365" /></a><figcaption>View of <a href="/wiki/Caguas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Caguas, Puerto Rico">Caguas</a> and <i>La India Dormida</i> from <i>Altos de San Luis</i></figcaption></figure>\n<p><i><b>La India Dormida</b></i> (Spanish for "the sleeping <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Indian</a> [woman]") is a famous geographical landmark for locals in the Valley of Caguas. Although often called a mountain, this is actually a group of mountains that through <a href="/wiki/Forced_perspective" title="Forced perspective">forced perspective</a> (when seen from the north) form the shape of a sleeping woman. According to legend, the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> cacique <a href="/wiki/Caguax" title="Caguax">Caguax</a> had a niece called <i>Taina</i> (or <i>Tayana</i>) who sacrificed herself and was turned into the mountain during the Spanish conquest of <i><a href="/wiki/Borinquen" class="mw-redirect" title="Borinquen">Borinquen</a></i> (or <i>Boriken</i>, the native name for Puerto Rico). The best way to observe this optical illusion is from Altos de San Luis, Altos de La Mesa and on expressway <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_52" title="Puerto Rico Highway 52">PR-52</a> and highway <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_1" title="Puerto Rico Highway 1">PR-1</a> when driving from north to south through the valley.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span
