River">Can\xc3\xb3vanas River</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Rio Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza</b> (<a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>: <i>Great River of Lo\xc3\xadza</i> or <i>Lo\xc3\xadza River</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">river</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Geography of Puerto Rico">main island</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. It is the largest river in Puerto Rico by discharge volume. Situated on the north coast of the island, it originates in the northeastern <a href="/wiki/Sierra_de_Cayey" title="Sierra de Cayey">Sierra de Cayey</a>, flowing from south to north, and draining into the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>, a few miles east of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DRNA_rivers_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DRNA_rivers-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p><i>Rio Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza</i> runs for approximately 40 miles (64 kilometers). It has its origin in the <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/San_Lorenzo,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Lorenzo, Puerto Rico">San Lorenzo</a> at an altitude of approximately 3,500 feet (1,100 meters) above sea level. It runs through the municipalities of San Lorenzo, <a href="/wiki/Caguas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Caguas, Puerto Rico">Caguas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gurabo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Gurabo, Puerto Rico">Gurabo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trujillo_Alto,_Puerto_Rico" title="Trujillo Alto, Puerto Rico">Trujillo Alto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carolina,_Puerto_Rico" title="Carolina, Puerto Rico">Carolina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Can\xc3\xb3vanas, Puerto Rico">Can\xc3\xb3vanas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lo%C3%ADza,_Puerto_Rico" title="Lo\xc3\xadza, Puerto Rico">Lo\xc3\xadza</a>, forming the <a href="/wiki/Lo%C3%ADza_Lake" title="Lo\xc3\xadza Lake">Lo\xc3\xadza Lake</a> along its route, making it one of the longest rivers on the island.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Lo%C3%ADza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Rio Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza <a href="/wiki/Drainage_basin" title="Drainage basin">basin</a> is the largest in Puerto Rico with an area of 751 square kilometres (289.9&#160;sq&#160;mi).<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/River_source" title="River source">source</a> of the river is located in the <a href="/wiki/Espino,_San_Lorenzo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Espino, San Lorenzo, Puerto Rico">Espino</a> barrio of <a href="/wiki/San_Lorenzo,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Lorenzo, Puerto Rico">San Lorenzo, Puerto Rico</a> on the eastern slopes of the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_de_Cayey" title="Sierra de Cayey">Sierra de Cayey</a> mountain range, close to <a href="/wiki/Carite_State_Forest" title="Carite State Forest">Carite State Forest</a>. The river flows northeastwardly through the San Lorenzo <a href="/wiki/Batholith" title="Batholith">batholith</a>, a hilly region of <a href="/wiki/Intrusive_rock" title="Intrusive rock">intrusive</a> <a href="/wiki/Igneous_rock" title="Igneous rock">igneous rock</a>, where it meets with the tributaries of <a href="/wiki/Emajagua_River" title="Emajagua River">Emajagua</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cayaguas_River" title="Cayaguas River">Cayaguas</a>. From here, the river turns northwest towards the <a href="/wiki/Caguas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Caguas, Puerto Rico">Caguas Valley</a> where it meets numerous other rivers and creeks including the <a href="/wiki/Turabo_River" title="Turabo River">Turabo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gurabo_River" title="Gurabo River">Gurabo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bairoa_River" title="Bairoa River">Bairoa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cag%C3%BCitas_River" title="Cag\xc3\xbcitas River">Cag\xc3\xbcitas</a> rivers. North of this, the river is <a href="/wiki/Carra%C3%ADzo_Dam" title="Carra\xc3\xadzo Dam">dammed</a> and flows through the reservoir <a href="/wiki/Lo%C3%ADza_Lake" title="Lo\xc3\xadza Lake">Lo\xc3\xadza Lake</a> (also known as Carra\xc3\xadzo Lake, after the <a href="/wiki/Carra%C3%ADzo" title="Carra\xc3\xadzo">barrio</a> of <a href="/wiki/Trujillo_Alto,_Puerto_Rico" title="Trujillo Alto, Puerto Rico">Trujillo Alto</a> it is located in). The river finally flows into <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_metropolitan_area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan metropolitan area">San Juan\'s urban area</a> and the Northern Plain of Puerto Rico where it discharges into the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Lo%C3%ADza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The river is of historical importance due to the number of settlements, cities and towns that were founded along it such as <a href="/wiki/San_Lorenzo_barrio-pueblo" title="San Lorenzo barrio-pueblo">San Lorenzo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caguas_barrio-pueblo" title="Caguas barrio-pueblo">Caguas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trujillo_Alto,_Puerto_Rico" title="Trujillo Alto, Puerto Rico">Trujillo Alto</a>.\n</p><p>In the 1898 <i>Military Notes on Puerto Rico</i> by the U.S. it is written that the "<i>limits of the Loisa River are: On the east, the sierra of Luquillo (situated near the northeast corner of the island); on the south, the sierra of Cayey, and on the west, ramifications of the latter. It rises in the northern slopes of the sierra of Cayey, and, running in a northwest direction for the first half of its course and turning to northeast in the second half, it arrives at Loisa, a port on the northern coast, where it discharges its waters into the Atlantic. During the first part of its course, it is known by the name of Cayagua</i>."<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The river was commemorated in a <a href="/wiki/Poem" class="mw-redirect" title="Poem">poem</a> by Puerto Rican poet <a href="/wiki/Julia_de_Burgos" title="Julia de Burgos">Julia de Burgos</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Burgos_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Burgos-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span
