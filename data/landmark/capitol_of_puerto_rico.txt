Puerto Rico</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Capitolio de Puerto Rico</i>), also known as <i><b>Casa de las Leyes</b></i> <i>(House of Laws)</i>, and most commonly referred to as <i><b>El Capitolio</b></i> <i>(The Capitol)</i>, is the <a href="/wiki/Seat_of_government" title="Seat of government">seat</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Legislative Assembly of Puerto Rico">Legislative Assembly</a>, or the bicameral <a href="/wiki/Legislature" title="Legislature">legislature</a>, composed of the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Senate of Puerto Rico">Senate</a> and <a href="/wiki/House_of_Representatives_of_Puerto_Rico" title="House of Representatives of Puerto Rico">House of Representatives</a>, responsible for the legislative branch of the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Government of Puerto Rico">government of Puerto Rico</a>. Located  on <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Islet" title="San Juan Islet">San Juan Islet</a> immediately outside the <a href="/wiki/Walls_of_Old_San_Juan" title="Walls of Old San Juan">Walls of Old San Juan</a>, the  <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">oceanfront</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">neoclassical</a> <a href="/wiki/Beaux-Arts_architecture" title="Beaux-Arts architecture">Beaux-Arts</a>-style, entirely white <a href="/wiki/Marble" title="Marble">marble</a>-covered edifice was constructed between 1921 and 1929 to resemble the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">ancient</a> Roman <a href="/wiki/Pantheon,_Rome" title="Pantheon, Rome"> Pantheon</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, using as inspiration the <a href="/wiki/Low_Memorial_Library" title="Low Memorial Library">Low Memorial Library</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. It was added to the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1977.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capitol_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Puerto_Rico_State_Department.JPG" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Puerto_Rico_State_Department.JPG/220px-Puerto_Rico_State_Department.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Puerto_Rico_State_Department.JPG/330px-Puerto_Rico_State_Department.JPG 1.5x, href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Palace_of_the_Royal_Intendency&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Old Palace of the Royal Intendency (page does not exist)">Old Palace of the Royal Intendency</a> (Real Intendencia), now the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_State" title="Puerto Rico Department of State">Puerto Rico Department of State</a>.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Prior to the establishment of the Capitol, the Legislative Assembly used the Provincial Delegation Building, which now houses the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_State" title="Puerto Rico Department of State">Department of State</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The idea of constructing a capitol was first introduced by former <a href="/wiki/Resident_Commissioner_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Resident Commissioner of Puerto Rico">Resident Commissioner</a> <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Rivera" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera</a> in 1907, and was partly meant to demonstrate some degree of civilian rule in the island, which at the time was already a colony of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nps-2006_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nps-2006-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The project proposed by Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera assigned $3 million for the construction and established rules for a contest among architectures to decide which one would do the work.\n</p><p>In September 1907, Commissioner of the Interior Laurence Grahame sent the official request for architects through the press and magazines of <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The specifications for the building were that it was supposed to have three principal installations: the Executive Council, the House of Delegates, and the Supreme Court. It was supposed to be simple, conservative, and appropriate to the island\'s weather conditions. 134 architectural designs were submitted from the US, <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, and Puerto Rico.\n</p><p>The evaluation of the proposals was in charge of <a href="/wiki/William_F._Willoughby" title="William F. Willoughby">William F. Willoughby</a> (president of the Executive Council), <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_de_Diego" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 de Diego">Jos\xc3\xa9 de Diego</a> (<a href="/wiki/House_of_Representatives_of_Puerto_Rico" title="House of Representatives of Puerto Rico">Speaker of the House</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Rivera" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera</a>), Jos\xc3\xa9 S. Qui\xc3\xb1ones (President of the Supreme Court), and Laurence Grahame. Three American architects were also asked to join the commission: E.B. Homerde, John E. Howe, and Bowen Bancroft Smith. In the end, three projects were selected, belonging to Frank E. Perkins (<a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>), James H. Ritchie and Lewis B. Abbot (<a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>), and Henry L. Beadle (New York).\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Capitol-pr-perkins-1907.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/38/Capitol-pr-perkins-1907.png" decoding="async" width="200" height="145" class="mw-file-element" data-file-width="200" data-file-height="145" /></a><figcaption>Perkins design</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Finally, the project chosen was the one from Frank E. Perkins, which presented a <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">neoclassical</a> style with little ornamentation. The main halls were organized around a central patio, with the south facade leading to the lobby. The lobby had the dome above, which bore a style similar to the <a href="/wiki/Pantheon,_Rome" title="Pantheon, Rome">Pantheon</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, including the oculus. To the right, there would be a session hall for the Executive Council, and to the left one for the House of Delegates. The Supreme Court would meet in a hall facing north.\n</p>\n<figure typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Capitol-pr-valle-zeno-1907.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Capitol-pr-valle-zeno-1907.png/250px-Capitol-pr-valle-zeno-1907.png" decoding="async" width="250" height="68" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Capitol-pr-valle-zeno-1907.png/375px-Capitol-pr-valle-zeno-1907.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/30/Capitol-pr-valle-zeno-1907.png 2x" data-file-width="390" data-file-height="106" /></a><figcaption>Carlos del Valle Zeno design</figcaption></figure>\n<p>However, <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Rivera" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera</a> and the local press preferred another project that was disqualified by the evaluating committee, because the architect had included a <a href="/wiki/Slogan" title="Slogan">slogan</a> in the blueprints, which was forbidden. The project belonged to a <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican people">Puerto Rican</a> engineer called Carlos del Valle Zeno. Del Valle\'s project presented a <a href="/wiki/Renaissance_architecture" title="Renaissance architecture">Renaissance</a> style, with neoclassic orders.\n</p><p>Despite public opinion, Perkins was hired to make the construction blueprints for the Capitol, which were then auctioned. It wasn\'t until 1920 that the project was retaken when the Commissioner of the Interior, Guillermo Esteves, published a new design. The change came after a revision from the Department of Interior, which found Perkins\' design to be inappropriate. With the supervision of architects Adrian C. Finlayson, Francisco Rold\xc3\xa1n, and Pedro de Castro, new blueprints were made using the Renaissance style.\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Puerto_Rico_-_Capitolio.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Puerto_Rico_-_Capitolio.jpg/220px-Puerto_Rico_-_Capitolio.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="138" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Puerto_Rico_-_Capitolio.jpg/330px-Puerto_Rico_-_Capitolio.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Puerto_Rico_-_Capitolio.jpg/440px-Puerto_Rico_-_Capitolio.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3215" data-file-height="2010" /></a><figcaption>The Capitol, postcard of 1936.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Construction of the building eventually began in 1921 under the leadership of <a href="/wiki/Antonio_R._Barcel%C3%B3" class="mw-redirect" title="Antonio R. Barcel\xc3\xb3">Antonio R. Barcel\xc3\xb3</a>. However, construction was halted after completion of the first level. When Finlayson got ill, he was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Carmoega" title="Rafael Carmoega">Rafael Carmoega</a> who, along with architects Joseph O\'Kelly, William Schimmelpfennig, Harry Pembleton, Albert Nichols, Luis F. Pina, and Gonzalo Fern\xc3\xb3s Maldonado, made another design to finish the structure.\n</p><p>Their design incorporated some of the elements from Perkins\'s original design, including the dome. The model for the central structure and the dome was that of the <a href="/wiki/Low_Memorial_Library" title="Low Memorial Library">Low Memorial Library</a> at <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The building was inaugurated on February 11, 1929, with the first meeting of the Legislative Assembly taking place three days later.\n</p><p>It was listed on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1977 as <b>El Capitolio de Puerto Rico</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-nris_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nris-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Capitol_Building_complex">Capitol Building complex</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capitol_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Capitol Building complex"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Capitol,_puerto_rico.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Capitol%2C_puerto_rico.jpg/250px-Capitol%2C_puerto_rico.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="167" class="mw-file-element" dome</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Designed in the 1950s by the Toro-Ferrer architectural firm, the Rafael Mart\xc3\xadnez Nadal building serves as the Senate Annex on the Senate side with offices for senators and two hearing rooms, and the <a href="/wiki/Ernesto_Ramos_Antonini" title="Ernesto Ramos Antonini">Ernesto Ramos Antonini</a> building serves as the House Annex on the House side with offices for representatives as well as hearing rooms. The <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Mar%C3%ADn" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn</a> Office Building houses the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Legislative_Services_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Office of Legislative Services of Puerto Rico">Office of Legislative Services</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Luis_A._Ferr%C3%A9" title="Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9">Luis A. Ferr\xc3\xa9</a> Building houses offices for both chambers, as well as a 700 space parking garage, while the <a href="/wiki/Antonio_R._Barcel%C3%B3" class="mw-redirect" title="Antonio R. Barcel\xc3\xb3">Antonio R. Barcel\xc3\xb3</a> Building, former School of Tropical Medicine building houses offices for both chambers, the Legislative Library, and offices for the Superintendent of the Capitol and the <a href="/wiki/Resident_Commissioner_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Resident Commissioner of Puerto Rico">Resident Commissioner of Puerto Rico</a>. The Ram\xc3\xb3n Mellado Parsons Building houses Senate offices. The buildings that formerly housed the <a href="/wiki/State_Commission_of_Elections_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="State Commission of Elections of Puerto Rico">State Commission of Elections</a> in Puerta de Tierra are currently used to house House archives, as well as administrative offices.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Frieze" title="Frieze">frieze</a> on the north <a href="/wiki/Fa%C3%A7ade" title="Fa\xc3\xa7ade">fa\xc3\xa7ade</a> of the building is inscribed with the following text in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> by the first <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Governor of Puerto Rico">Governor</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Commonwealth of Puerto Rico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Mar%C3%ADn" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn</a>:\n</p>\n<table class="wikitable">\n<caption>\n</caption>\n<tbody><tr>\n<th>Spanish\n</th>\n<th>English\n</th></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>El derecho, la liberta y la dignidad por encima de todo.\n</td>\n<td>Law, freedom, and dignity above all.\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Frieze" title="Frieze">frieze</a> on the south <a href="/wiki/Fa%C3%A7ade" title="Fa\xc3\xa7ade">fa\xc3\xa7ade</a> of the building is inscribed with the following <a href="/wiki/Gettysburg_Address" title="Gettysburg Address">Gettysburg Address</a> text in <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> by the 16th <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">president</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States of America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a>:\n</p>\n<table class="wikitable">\n<caption>\n</caption>\n<tbody><tr>\n<th>English\n</th></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Government of the people, by the people, for the people, shall not perish from the Earth.\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Architectural_drawings">Architectural drawings</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capitol_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Architectural drawings"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Architecture and Construction Archives at the University of Puerto Rico (AACUPR) is the custodian of the Capitol of Puerto Rico Collection (1924-1926). Approximately eight cubic feet in size, the collection contains architectural drawings, photographs and textual documents. The original drawings, executed in ink over drawing cloth, were produced by the Department of the Interior under the supervision of Puerto Rican architect <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Carmoega" title="Rafael Carmoega">Rafael Carmoega</a>. The 28 originals and 38 blue prints show plans, elevations, sections as well as electrical and plumbing distribution. There is also a proposal for the surrounding gardens by the architectural firm of Bennet, Parsons and Frost and several plates showing entries for the building competition. The collection was transferred to the AACUPR by the Capitol Superintendency in 1986.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Gallery">Gallery</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
