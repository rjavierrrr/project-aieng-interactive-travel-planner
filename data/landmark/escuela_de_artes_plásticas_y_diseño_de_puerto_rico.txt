class="infobox-data" style="border: 0;">October 10, 1972</td></tr><tr style="display:none"><th colspan="2">\n</th></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Escuela de Artes Pl\xc3\xa1sticas y Dise\xc3\xb1o de Puerto Rico</b> (English: <span lang="en">School of <a href="/wiki/Plastic_Arts" class="mw-redirect" title="Plastic Arts">Plastic Arts</a> and Design of Puerto Rico</span>) is an institution of <a href="/wiki/Higher_education" class="mw-redirect" title="Higher education">higher learning</a> engaged in the training of students in the <a href="/wiki/Visual_arts" title="Visual arts">visual arts</a>.  It is located in <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">Old San Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.  The school was founded in 1965 as part of the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Puerto_Rican_Culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Institute of Puerto Rican Culture">Institute of Puerto Rican Culture</a>. Painter <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Antonio_Torres_Martin%C3%B3" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Antonio Torres Martin\xc3\xb3">Jos\xc3\xa9 Antonio Torres Martin\xc3\xb3</a> was one of the school\'s co-founders.<sup id="cite_ref-AP_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The school\'s first director was <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Pou" title="Miguel Pou">Miguel Pou</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>As an autonomous school it was created by an amendment of legislation by the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Legislative Assembly of Puerto Rico">Legislative Assembly of Puerto Rico</a> in 1971, and achieved its definitive form and <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_entity" class="mw-redirect" title="Autonomous entity">autonomy</a> under Public Law 54 of August 22, 1990.\n</p><p>The school offers <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_degrees" class="mw-redirect" title="Bachelor degrees">bachelor degrees</a> in seven concentrations: <a href="/wiki/Graphic_arts" title="Graphic arts">graphic arts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Photography" title="Photography">photography</a> and design (with specialties in digital graphic design and photography and motion), <a href="/wiki/Art_education" class="mw-redirect" title="Art education">art education</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sculpture" title="Sculpture">sculpture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Painting" title="Painting">painting</a>, <a href="/wiki/Industrial_design" title="Industrial design">industrial design</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fashion_design" title="Fashion design">fashion design</a>. Today, the school is Puerto Rico\'s foremost institution of <a href="/wiki/Higher_education" class="mw-redirect" title="Higher education">higher education</a> in the visual arts.
