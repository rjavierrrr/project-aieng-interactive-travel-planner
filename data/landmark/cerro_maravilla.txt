Rico</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Cerro Maravilla</b> is <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>\'s fourth highest peak<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> at 1,205 meters (3,953&#160;ft). It is located on the northern edge <a href="/wiki/An%C3%B3n" title="An\xc3\xb3n">Barrio An\xc3\xb3n</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> close to the border with the municipality <a href="/wiki/Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico" title="Jayuya, Puerto Rico">Jayuya</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and is part of the <a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Cordillera Central (Puerto Rico)">Cordillera Central</a> (Central Mountain Range). It is known as <i>El Cerro de los M\xc3\xa1rtires</i> ("Mountain of the Martyrs") and characterized as the most infamous peak in Puerto Rico, due to the 1978 <a href="/wiki/Cerro_Maravilla_murders" title="Cerro Maravilla murders">Cerro Maravilla murders</a> which took place here.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location_and_access">Location and access</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cerro_Maravilla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location and access"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The peak is located at the end of the 0.5&#160;km-long <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_577" title="Puerto Rico Highway 577">Puerto Rico Highway 577</a>, which is accessible via <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_143" title="Puerto Rico Highway 143">Puerto Rico Highway 143</a>, traveling either westbound or eastbound. PR-143 is a secondary two-way, two-lane mountainous road that is relatively well traveled. The peak\'s coordinates are: <span class="geo-inline"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1156832818"><span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="https://geohack.toolforge.org/geohack.php?pagename=Cerro_Maravilla&amp;params=18_09_11_N_66_33_15_W_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">18\xc2\xb009\xe2\x80\xb211\xe2\x80\xb3N</span> <span class="longitude">66\xc2\xb033\xe2\x80\xb215\xe2\x80\xb3W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">18.15306\xc2\xb0N 66.55417\xc2\xb0W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">18.15306; -66.55417</span></span></span></a></span></span>.\n</p><p>The height of the mountain makes for some interesting sights. For example, on clear days both the northern and southern coasts of the island can be appreciated simultaneously. In the winter season, the air is crisp and the temperature at night will usually fluctuate between the 40s and 60s degrees Fahrenheit. class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cerro_Maravilla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cerro Maravilla incident"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The peak might be best known for being the place where two <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_independence_movement" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican independence movement">Puerto Rico independence</a> <a href="/wiki/Activists" class="mw-redirect" title="Activists">activists</a> were slain on 25 July 1978 in a police <a href="/wiki/Ambush" (December 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Although the temperature remains mild year round, night temperatures in winter may reach the mid 40s at times.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
