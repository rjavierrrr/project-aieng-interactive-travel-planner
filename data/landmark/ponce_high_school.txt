The school\'s main building is a historic structure located on Cristina Street, in the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce Historic Zone</a>.  From its beginning the school has secured a unique place in Puerto Rico\'s educational history. Of over 3,000 schools erected in Puerto Rico in the first quarter of the twentieth century, Ponce High was the largest, "at a time enrolling more students than all the other Puerto Rico high schools combined", and for many years enrolling more students than any other high school in Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The cost of the building in 1915 dollars was $150,000 <a href="/wiki/USD" class="mw-redirect" title="USD">USD</a> ($4.52&#160;million in 2023 dollars<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-US_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-US-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The building was listed on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> on 4 August 1987.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The school has the only diamond-level <a href="/wiki/DECA_(organization)" title="DECA (organization)">DECA</a> chapter in Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Ponce High School building is "among the most important public buildings ever built in Puerto Rico."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The school is the oldest continuously-operating high school in Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-Neysa_Rodriguez_Deynes_2016_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Neysa_Rodriguez_Deynes_2016-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The history of the institution dates back to January 1899. On 25 January 1899 a historic fire took place on the lot currently occupied by the High School.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The fire is known as the "Fuego del Polvor\xc3\xadn" (the Fire of the Ammunition Depot). At that time the lot was used as a U.S. Army ammunition depot. Today a register marks the location of the fire.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In November 1899, the new American government authorized the establishment of a public secondary school in Ponce modeled after the American school system. In 1903 the Roosevelt Industrial School was built on land where the US military had its munitions depot that caused the fire.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  Five years after <i>El Polvorin</i>, in the 1904-1905 timeframe, a two-story building with 12 classrooms was built to accommodate the Ruiz Gandia Grammar School in the same spacious <i>El Polvorin</i> lot. The first class of Ponce High School students formed in 1902.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> As new facilities were completed, the students were moved to them. For the 1907-1908 school year another building was built called the McKinley building, and the Ponce High students occupied said facilities as well. Construction of the now-historic Ponce High School building on Cristina Street started in 1915 and the building was inaugurated in 1920.<sup id="cite_ref-hip.upr.edu_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hip.upr.edu-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The school is located in barrio <a href="/wiki/Tercero_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Tercero (Ponce)">Tercero</a>. It faces Cristina street to the north and is bounded on the south by Comercio street (<a href="/wiki/PR-133" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-133">PR-133</a>), on the west by Salud street, and on the east by Trujillo street. Its location is only two blocks from  <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Delicias" title="Plaza Las Delicias">Plaza Las Delicias</a>. It is also diagonally across from the <a href="/wiki/Instituto_de_M%C3%BAsica_Juan_Morel_Campos" title="Instituto de M\xc3\xbasica Juan Morel Campos">Ponce Free School of Music</a>, the old location of Liceo de Ponce (<a href="/wiki/Liceo_Ponce%C3%B1o" class="mw-redirect" title="Liceo Ponce\xc3\xb1o">Liceo Ponce\xc3\xb1o</a>), an early 20th-century girls-only school. It is also one block from <a href="/wiki/Teatro_La_Perla" title="Teatro La Perla">Teatro La Perla</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Construction_and_appearance">Construction and appearance</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Construction and appearance"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The main and most historical of the buildings faces north. In the back of this building is a large yard paved with concrete.  This backyard contains several other large school buildings.  The main building follows the <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">neoclassical</a> architectural style. The front entry consists of a raised <a href="/wiki/Portico" title="Portico">portico</a> and a lobby area. \nThe school library is located on the first floor and the auditorium is on the second floor.\n</p><p>At both Eastern and Western sides of the lobby, and at both levels, L-shaped arcaded hallways lead to the classrooms. Two semi-enclosed patios are thus defined between the library-auditorium wing (much higher and wider) and the identical classroom wings facing East and West. Three sets of stairs are symmetrically laid out in the scheme: one of secondary importance at either side of the lobby; the most public one next to side entrances at the L-shaped circulation wings. A third set of stairs faces the patios and is the only one not to connect to the building\'s basement where more classrooms are found.<sup id="cite_ref-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_2_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_2-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The most continuous side of the E-shaped structure constitutes the front <a href="/wiki/Facade" class="mw-redirect" title="Facade">facade</a>; it is articulated to best reflect and express the internal organization in plan. Sophisticated neoclassical details, although integral to the building\'s public "face", are not used with such insistence anywhere else on the building, except for the lobby, which includes some mouldings, cornices and relatively simple pilaster inlays. Set back from the street, the front facade is enhanced by two small green <a href="/wiki/Parterre" title="Parterre">parterres</a> adjoining the concrete-iron fence and sidewalk. The elevation includes <a href="/wiki/Doric_order" title="Doric order">Doric</a> columns and pilasters running two stories high, identical <a href="/wiki/Pediment" title="Pediment">pediments</a> at each end and an <a href="/wiki/Escutcheon_(heraldry)" title="Escutcheon (heraldry)">escutcheon</a>-like crowning piece with a clock. The latter is dead center over the entrance <a href="/wiki/Portico" title="Portico">portico</a>. Concrete flat roofs, an extended <a href="/wiki/Parapet" title="Parapet">parapet</a> and a wide horizontal cornice tie together all the elements, therefore underlining the horizontal continuity of the structure. <a href="/wiki/Fenestration_(architecture)" class="mw-redirect" title="Fenestration (architecture)">Fenestration</a> consists mainly of wood <a href="/wiki/Louver" title="Louver">louvered</a> windows with glass. Some have been substituted by contemporary metal louvered windows, which are found all over the rest of the building.<sup id="cite_ref-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_2_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_2-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>A most significant feature of Ponce High School is its two-story high, elongated auditorium, which also contains a generous <a href="/wiki/Stage_(theatre)" title="Stage (theatre)">stage</a> area and a U-shaped wood <a href="/wiki/Mezzanine" title="Mezzanine">mezzanine</a> supported from the concrete ceiling. Its original lighting fixtures were removed and replaced by fluorescent ones. Classrooms are well lit and ventilated; there are many openings. Walls are plain, having been painted over and over throughout the years. The original wood floor finish is still retained in the library, the auditorium, and many of the classrooms; some have been substituted or changed to concrete. Hallways and stairs are paved in concrete. Vinyl flooring is used at offices. Glazed concrete tile is found at the lobby, where the original iron grilles, of geometrical design, are still in use.\n</p><p>Two small lean-to wood and zinc structures were added a few years ago. The auditorium was adapted to air conditioning features, with acoustic ceiling and related fixtures having been added. In spite of it all, and probably, because of the school\'s imposing scale, well balanced proportions and well-kept classical detailing, the integrity has not yet been lost.<sup id="cite_ref-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_2_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_2-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Significance">Significance</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Significance"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Ponce High School is one of the five best school buildings built in Puerto Rico at the beginning of the 20th century and one of the most impressive. Between 1900 and 1925 over 3,000 schools were erected on the Island; only four included fully equipped auditoriums (not just assembly rooms) in their design: San Juan\'s Central High, Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera School in Salinas, Arecibo\'s own Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera School and Ponce High School. In that sense, these are the most representative examples of school building ideas being developed at the time in the United States by architects of renown, such as Haussander and <a href="/wiki/Perkins_and_Will" class="mw-redirect" title="Perkins and Will">Perkins</a> of Chicago, <a href="/wiki/Charles_B._J._Snyder" class="mw-redirect" title="Charles B. J. Snyder">Snyder</a> of New York, Cooper of Boston and, especially, <a href="/wiki/William_B._Ittner" title="William B. Ittner">William B. Ittner</a> of St. Louis.<sup id="cite_ref-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_3_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_3-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Given the date of erection (earlier than for most other public schools built on the Island) Ponce High School emerges, as one of the earlier American government-sponsored construction efforts at such a large scale on the Island. Strikingly significant is the fact that with the choice of building vocabulary (strongly reminiscent of Mc Kim, Mead &amp; White\'s <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_Station_(New_York_City)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pennsylvania Station (New York City)">Pennsylvania Station</a> in New York) the use of the neoclassical style for educational structures was introduced in Puerto Rico. Ponce High School is today one of the most dignified and imposing structures in the city, a fact underlined by its architectural style, scale and overall architectural merits.<sup id="cite_ref-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_3_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_3-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Architect">Architect</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Architect"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>So far, the name of the architect for Ponce High School remains a mystery, although many facts point to <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adrian_C._Finlayson&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Adrian C. Finlayson (page does not exist)">Adrian C. Finlayson</a> as its designer.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Mr. Finlayson was, at the time, architect of the <a href="/wiki/Insular_area" title="Insular area">Insular</a> Government\'s Interior Department, and was responsible for many other structures of similar use, size and style. As such, he is a key figure on Puerto Rico\'s architectural development.<sup id="cite_ref-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_3_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_3-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Most historians, however, coincide that Finlayson was in fact its architect.<sup id="cite_ref-Neysa_Rodriguez_Deynes_2016_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Neysa_Rodriguez_Deynes_2016-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Setting">Setting</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Setting"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Its setting \xe2\x80\x94 on a block surrounded by other schools also of architectural merit \xe2\x80\x94 adds to the school\'s important <a href="/wiki/Urban_area" title="Urban area">urban</a> role. The site itself is a historic one, where the U.S. Army barracks were once located, before a fire ravaged the premises. The event is widely remembered in Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_3_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_3-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Partial_list_of_principals">Partial list of principals</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Partial list of principals"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<ul><li>Horace O.
