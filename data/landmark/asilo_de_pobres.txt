0;">January 24, 2003</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Asilo de Ancianos de Mayag\xc3\xbcez</b>, also known as <b>Asilo De Pobres</b> or <b>Asilo Municipal</b>, is the name of a historic <a href="/wiki/Classical_Revival" class="mw-redirect" title="Classical Revival">Classical Revival</a> style building located in <a href="/wiki/Mayag%C3%BCez_barrio-pueblo" title="Mayag\xc3\xbcez barrio-pueblo">downtown Mayag\xc3\xbcez</a>, originally as a shelter for the homeless, poor and disabled of the city. \n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asilo_De_Pobres&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Mayaguez_-_Asilo_de_Pobres.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Mayaguez_-_Asilo_de_Pobres.jpg/220px-Mayaguez_-_Asilo_de_Pobres.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="141" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Mayaguez_-_Asilo_de_Pobres.jpg/330px-Mayaguez_-_Asilo_de_Pobres.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Mayaguez_-_Asilo_de_Pobres.jpg/440px-Mayaguez_-_Asilo_de_Pobres.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3189" data-file-height="2045" /></a><figcaption>The building after 1918 restorations.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The idea for the asylum for the poor began with a campaign to build it by Salvador Suau y Mulet when he was <a href="/wiki/List_of_mayors_of_Mayag%C3%BCez,_Puerto_Rico" title="List of mayors of Mayag\xc3\xbcez, Puerto Rico">Mayor of Mayag\xc3\xbcez</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This building is highly representative of the 19th century institutional architecture of Puerto Rico. The building was designed and built by commissioned state architects <a href="/wiki/Manuel_V._Domenech" title="Manuel V. Domenech">Manuel V. Domenech</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Luis_Perocier&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Luis Perocier (page does not exist)">Luis Perocier</a> in 1895. The building as it stands today however was not fully completed until 1920 due to complications caused by the 1918 earthquake, after which it was restored and expanded. The institution was operated by the <a href="/wiki/Sisters_of_Charity" title="Sisters of Charity">Sisters of Charity</a> during its first years.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>For some time, <a href="/wiki/Salvador_Agron" title="Salvador Agron">Salvador Agr\xc3\xb3n</a>, the inspiration for the Broadway musical <a href="/wiki/The_Capeman" title="The Capeman">The Capeman</a>, lived in the building with his mother. In 1962 it became asylum for the elderly. The number of elderly patients increased, and, in 1967, occupational therapy and arts programs were added by the <a href="/wiki/Daughters_of_Charity_of_Saint_Vincent_de_Paul" title="Daughters of Charity of Saint Vincent de Paul">Daughters of Charity of Saint Vincent de Paul</a>, they also run the laundry, cafeteria, pharmacy and others.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The building lost some of its original elements after restorations carried between 1972 and 1979,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> but it was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1985 and on the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Register_of_Historic_Sites_and_Zones" title="Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones">Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones</a> in 2003 for its architectural value.<sup id="cite_ref-nris_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  In 2016 the elderly residents were relocated to a larger and newer facility located near the <a href="/wiki/Eugenio_Mar%C3%ADa_de_Hostos_Airport" title="Eugenio Mar\xc3\xada de Hostos Airport">Eugenio Mar\xc3\xada de Hostos Airport</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8"
