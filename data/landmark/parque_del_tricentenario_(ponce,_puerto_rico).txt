Entrance free.</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Parque del Tricentenario</b> is a passive urban park in the city of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>. The park was built to commemorate the 300th anniversary of the founding of the city. It was inaugurated during the mayoral administration of mayor <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Cordero_Santiago" title="Rafael Cordero Santiago">Rafael Cordero Santiago</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_del_Tricentenario_(Ponce,_Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The park is a passive urban family park. It is located in Barrio <a href="/wiki/Tercero_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Tercero (Ponce)">Tercero</a> at the entrance to the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce Historic Zone</a> on Miguel Pou Boulevard, at the southern terminus of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_1" title="Puerto Rico Highway 1">PR-1</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Features">Features</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_del_Tricentenario_(Ponce,_Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Features"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The park is a three-<a href="/wiki/Plaza" class="mw-redirect" title="Plaza">plaza</a> park. All three plazas have gardens and marble benches. Setting the stage for the park is <i>El Puente de los Leones</i> (The Bridge of the Lions). It spans <a href="/wiki/Rio_Portugues" class="mw-redirect" title="Rio Portugues">Rio Portugues</a> and is the gateway to, not just Parque del Tricentenario, but to the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce historical district</a> as well. It features two brass lions guarding the entrance: the older lion represents wisdom and experience, while the younger one stands for the glorious future.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="The_Illustrious_Ponce_Citizens_Plaza">The Illustrious Ponce Citizens Plaza</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_del_Tricentenario_(Ponce,_Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Illustrious Ponce Citizens Plaza"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Its centerpiece is a plaza containing a fountain dedicated to Ponce\'s most illustrious citizens. This plaza is known as <i>Parque de los Ponce\xc3\xb1os Ilustres</i> (the Illustrious Ponce Citizens Plaza). At either side of this plaza, and tucked in somewhat but facing side streets that lead to and return from the center of the city, are two separate areas dedicated to two of the most honored citizens of Ponce. The one on the right is dedicated to governor <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Hernandez_Colon" class="mw-redirect" title="Rafael Hernandez Colon">Rafael Hernandez Colon</a>; the one on the left is dedicated to governor <a href="/wiki/Luis_A._Ferre" class="mw-redirect" title="Luis A. Ferre">Luis A. Ferre</a>.  A large wall near Hernandez Colon\'s right plazalette has the encryption, "<i>Ponce es del que nos visita. Ponce es nuestro</i>" (Ponce belongs to those who visit us. Ponce is ours).  \n</p><p>The middle area is the largest area of this plaza. It contains a very large fountain and behind the fountain are 24 "<a href="/wiki/Kiosk" title="Kiosk">kiosks</a>" with fountainettes. Sixteen of these fountainette kiosks are dedicated to illustrious Ponce citizens in 16 different areas of endeavor. Moving from left to right, these twelve areas are: History, Politics, Journalism, Writing, Architecture, Citizenship, Medicine, Music, Craftsmanship, Education, Sports, Plastic Arts, Law, Business, Ponce\xc3\xb1istas, and Theatrical Arts. Each fountainette kiosk then has one or more <a href="/wiki/Commemorative_plaque" title="Commemorative plaque">plaques</a> with the names or those being honored engraved on them.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Every few years the municipal government announces that new names are to be added to the list and petitions its citizens for suggestions.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="The_Francisco_Porrata_Doria_Plaza">The Francisco Porrata Doria Plaza</h3><span 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="768" /></a><figcaption>Plaza Francisco Porrata-Doria, looking southeast</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The second major plaza, called the <i>Plaza de la Arquitectura <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Porrata_Doria" class="mw-redirect" title="Francisco Porrata Doria">Francisco Porrata Doria</a></i> (Francisco Porrata Doria Architecture Plaza), features a <a href="/wiki/Rotunda_(architecture)" title="Rotunda (architecture)">rotunda</a> and honors the city\'s architecture. On the rotunda\'s outer crown is the following encryption: "<i>Ponce es Tradicion, Cultura, Espiritu, Civismo, Ilusion. Ponce es Virtud.</i>" ("Ponce is Tradition, Culture, Spirit, Civism, Ilusion. Ponce is Virtue.") The same rotunda has another encryption on its floor that reads: "<i>Estabilidad, Durabilidad, Conveniencia, Belleza, Firmeza.</i>" ("Estability, Durability, Convenience, Beauty, Resolution.") To the right of this plaza is the bust of governor <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Sanchez_Vilella" class="mw-redirect" title="Roberto Sanchez Vilella">Roberto Sanchez Vilella</a> who, though not born in Ponce, did lived there during his formative school years, and is considered an adopted son of the city.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="The_Latin_American_Statesmen_Plaza">The Latin American Statesmen Plaza</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_del_Tricentenario_(Ponce,_Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Latin American Statesmen Plaza"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The third major plaza honors <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin American</a> statesmen and it is called <i>Plaza de los Pr\xc3\xb3ceres Latinoamericanos</i> (Latin-American Statesmen Plaza). This third plaza is dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Sim%C3%B3n_Bol%C3%ADvar" title="Sim\xc3\xb3n Bol\xc3\xadvar">Sim\xc3\xb3n Bol\xc3\xadvar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Duarte" title="Juan Pablo Duarte">Juan Pablo Duarte</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mart%C3%AD" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Mart\xc3\xad">Jos\xc3\xa9 Mart\xc3\xad</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Mar%C3%ADn" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn</a>, and there are statues of these prominent people there.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_del_Tricentenario_(Ponce,_Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The park was inaugurated in 1992 under the administration of mayor <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Cordero_Santiago" title="Rafael Cordero Santiago">Rafael Cordero Santiago</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Symbolisms">Symbolisms</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_del_Tricentenario_(Ponce,_Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Symbolisms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The entrance of the park, via <a href="/wiki/Puente_de_Los_Leones" class="mw-redirect" title="Puente de Los Leones">Puente de Los Leones</a>, is guarded by two massive pillars each topped by a lion - one the symbol of knowledge, the other the symbol of power.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="References">References</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
