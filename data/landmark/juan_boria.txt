class="infobox-data" style="line-height:1.4em;">Negroid Poems</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Juan Boria</b> (February 17, 1905 &#8211; May 29, 1995) also known as the <b>Negro Verse Pharaoh</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rican</a> poet known for his <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Afro-Caribbean" class="extiw" title="wikt:Afro-Caribbean">Afro-Caribbean</a> poetry.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Biography">Biography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Boria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Early_years">Early years</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Boria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Juan Boria Romero was born on February 17, 1906, in <a href="/wiki/Dorado,_Puerto_Rico" title="Dorado, Puerto Rico">Dorado, Puerto Rico</a> to Juan Boria Nev\xc3\xa1rez, a sugar factory engineer, and Anselma Romero Sempri, a housewife. He was of predominant African ancestry and of partial Italian ancestry through his mother. He took his elementary studies in Dorado. After that, he followed studies in the nearby town of <a href="/wiki/Toa_Baja" class="mw-redirect" title="Toa Baja">Toa Baja</a>. During weekends, he worked as a <a href="/wiki/Masonry" title="Masonry">mason</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carpenter" class="mw-redirect" title="Carpenter">carpenter</a> to pay for his studies. He finished his studies at <a href="/wiki/Central_High_School_(San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico)" title="Central High School (San Juan, Puerto Rico)">Central High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Santurce,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="Santurce, San Juan, Puerto Rico">Santurce</a> in 1923.<sup id="cite_ref-prpop_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prpop-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>At this time, his family moved to the town of <a href="/wiki/Cata%C3%B1o,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cata\xc3\xb1o, Puerto Rico">Cata\xc3\xb1o</a> and he started studies in the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico,_Rio_Piedras_Campus" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Puerto Rico, Rio Piedras Campus">University of Puerto Rico</a> in <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Piedras" class="mw-redirect" title="R\xc3\xado Piedras">R\xc3\xado Piedras</a>. He received his <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor&#39;s degree">bachelor\'s degree</a> as a teacher of <a href="/wiki/Industrial_arts" title="Industrial arts">industrial arts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drawing" title="Drawing">drawing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Carpentry" title="Carpentry">carpentry</a>. After several years working in several places, he started working as a teacher in 1937 at a school in <a href="/wiki/San_Germ%C3%A1n" class="mw-redirect" title="San Germ\xc3\xa1n">San Germ\xc3\xa1n</a>. From there, he was transferred to another school in <a href="/wiki/Can%C3%B3vanas" class="mw-redirect" title="Can\xc3\xb3vanas">Can\xc3\xb3vanas</a>.\n</p><p>In 1941, Juan Boria married Em\xc3\xa9rita Rodr\xc3\xadguez with whom he had a daughter named: Zayda. From his first marriage, he already had a daughter named: Cruz Amanda.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Starting_his_career">Starting his career</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Boria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Starting his career"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1938, during a show at the school in Can\xc3\xb3vanas, he heard a colleague &#8212; Pablito Rivera &#8212; recite the <a href="/wiki/Negro" title="Negro">Negro</a> poem "La negra curandera." He liked it so much that he asked him for a copy to learn and recite at his friends meetings. After some time, his friends prompted him to participate in a program at the <a href="/wiki/WKAQ_(AM)" title="WKAQ (AM)">WKAQ</a> radio station. The poet Fortunato Vizcarrondo, considered the most important Negro poet in the island at the time, heard him and visited him. Vizcarrondo then offered him other of his poems for him to recite at the program.\n</p><p>In 1941, he participated in a show at <a href="/wiki/Lo%C3%ADza" class="mw-redirect" title="Lo\xc3\xadza">Lo\xc3\xadza</a>, where he met the most important personality in Puerto Rican show-business, <a href="/wiki/Ram%C3%B3n_Rivero_(Diplo)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ram\xc3\xb3n Rivero (Diplo)">Ram\xc3\xb3n Rivero</a> (known as "Diplo"), who was so impressed with Boria\'s performance that he made Boria part of his <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/troupe" class="extiw" title="wikt:troupe">troupe</a> called <i>La Far\xc3\xa1ndula Bohemia</i>, traveling the island and performing as a poet and as an actor. In 1944, Boria once more joined the famous troupe and accompanied <a href="/wiki/Ram%C3%B3n_Rivero_(Diplo)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ram\xc3\xb3n Rivero (Diplo)">Ram\xc3\xb3n Rivero</a> (Diplo) to the <a href="/wiki/Panama_Canal" title="Panama Canal">Panama Canal</a> to entertain Puerto Rican soldiers stationed there.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="International_fame">International fame</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Boria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International fame"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1950, he traveled to <a href="/wiki/Havana,_Cuba" class="mw-redirect" title="Havana, Cuba">Havana, Cuba</a> under the recommendation of "Diplo" and <a href="/wiki/Luis_Pal%C3%A9s_Matos" title="Luis Pal\xc3\xa9s Matos">Luis Pal\xc3\xa9s Matos</a>. He planned to stay there for 15 days but was so sought after that he ended up staying two months. He followed with travels to the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>, Spain, and Portugal, as well as New York, where he headlined at the <a href="/wiki/Teatro_Puerto_Rico" title="Teatro Puerto Rico">Teatro Puerto Rico</a>, and other cities in the United States. Afterward, he alternated his artistic activities with his job as a teacher in <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Piedras,_Puerto_Rico" title="R\xc3\xado Piedras, Puerto Rico">R\xc3\xado Piedras</a>.\n</p><p>In 1954, Boria debuted in television on the show <i>Tribuna del Arte</i> from <a href="/wiki/Telemundo" title="Telemundo">Telemundo</a>. He also performed in <i>La Taberna India</i> with "Diplo." He followed with his debut in film in 1964 in the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Mexican people">Mexican</a>/<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rican</a> film <i>Mientras Puerto Rico duerme</i>, directed by Juli\xc3\xa1n Soler. He also appeared in several <a href="/wiki/Short_film" title="Short film">short films</a> produced by the Division of Community Education.\n</p><p>From 1965 he traveled several cultural centers, schools, and public squares in Puerto Rico reciting at events sponsored by the Puerto Rican Culture Institute. He also participated at the Cultural Olympics in 1979.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Recognitions">Recognitions</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Boria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Recognitions"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In October, 1980, the 7th Festival of <a href="/wiki/Bomba_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Bomba (Puerto Rico)">Bomba</a> y <a href="/wiki/Plena" title="Plena">Plena</a> celebrated in his hometown of Dorado was dedicated to him. Also, the show <i>Nuestra Noche</i> that same year was held in his honor. The show was celebrated in the <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Clemente_Coliseum" title="Roberto Clemente Coliseum">Roberto Clemente Coliseum</a> and featured singers like <a href="/wiki/Wilkins_V%C3%A9lez" class="mw-redirect" title="Wilkins V\xc3\xa9lez">Wilkins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haciendo_Punto_en_Otro_Son" title="Haciendo Punto en Otro Son">Haciendo Punto en Otro Son</a>, and others.\n</p><p>In 1983, the mayor of Dorado, Alfonso L\xc3\xb3pez Chaar, acquired the Juana de Arco Theater at the entrance of the town and renamed it as Juan Boria Theater. On November 1, 1984, Boria himself presented his first show at the theater.\n</p><p>Boria retired from teaching in 1974. Afterward, he started his own shop in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">Cupey</a>.\n</p><p>Boria died May 29, 1995, in <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Piedras,_Puerto_Rico" title="R\xc3\xado Piedras, Puerto Rico">R\xc3\xado Piedras, Puerto Rico</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Influences">Influences</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Boria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Influences"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Although he identified with the works of Vizcarrondo, Pal\xc3\xa9s Matos and <a href="/wiki/Nicol%C3%A1s_Guill%C3%A9n" title="Nicol\xc3\xa1s Guill\xc3\xa9n">Nicol\xc3\xa1s Guill\xc3\xa9n</a>, his repertoire included poems from:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Emilio Ballagas</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9lix_B._Caignet" title="F\xc3\xa9lix B. Caignet">F\xc3\xa9lix B. Caignet</a></li>\n<li>Gonzalo Casta\xc3\xb1\xc3\xb3n</li>\n<li>Marcelino Arozamena</li>\n<li>Lorenzo Coball\xc3\xa9s</li>\n<li>Alfonso Cam\xc3\xadn</li>\n<li>Pablo Motito</li>\n<li>Gilberto Hern\xc3\xa1ndez Santana</li>\n<li>Enrique Montijo</li>\n<li>Mar\xc3\xada Teresa Vall\xc3\xa9s</li>\n<li>Luis Manuel Ruiz</li>\n<li>Rub\xc3\xa9n Suro</li></ul>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Discography">Discography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Boria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Discography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<ul><li><i><a href="/w/index.php?title=%C2%A1Qu%C3%A9_negrota!&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="\xc2\xa1Qu\xc3\xa9 negrota!
