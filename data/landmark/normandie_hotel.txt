2000</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Normandie Hotel</b> is a historic building located in the <a href="/wiki/Isleta_de_San_Juan" class="mw-redirect" title="Isleta de San Juan">Isleta de San Juan</a>, in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a> which opened on October 10, 1942 as a hotel. Its design was inspired by the French transatlantic passenger ship <a href="/wiki/SS_Normandie" title="SS Normandie">SS <i>Normandie</i></a> in addition to featuring the same <a href="/wiki/Art_deco" class="mw-redirect" title="Art deco">art deco</a> design as the ocean liner that inspired it, and the hotel\'s original roof sign was one of the two signs that adorned the top deck of the SS <i>Normandie</i> but were removed from it during an early refitting.  It is an example of what came to be known as the <a href="/wiki/Streamline_Moderne" title="Streamline Moderne">Streamline Moderne</a> architecture style.\n</p><p>As of 2021<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Normandie_Hotel&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> the building was vacant and not in operation and in 2022, was sold to private owners who said they plan to renovate it.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Normandie_Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:F%C3%A9lix_B%C3%A9nitez_Rexach_et_La_m%C3%B4me_Moineau_Lucienne_Dhotelle.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img alt="A black and white photograph of husband and wife F\xc3\xa9lix B\xc3\xa9nitez Rexach and Lucienne D&#39;Hotelle, seated on a bench inside their private airplane; font-style: normal;">&#160;&#91;<a href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_m%C3%B4me_Moineau" class="extiw" title="fr:La m\xc3\xb4me Moineau">fr</a>&#93;</span> in 1927 or 1928;<sup id="cite_ref-USvRexach_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USvRexach-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USvDHotelle_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USvDHotelle-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> reportedly, he built the hotel as a wedding present for his bride.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT-77_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-77-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> He served as the president of the Escambron Development Company, which was incorporated in approximately 1943 to issue bonds to finance the completion of the hotel. Escambron owned and operated the hotel.<sup id="cite_ref-USvRexach_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USvRexach-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The hotel was designed by architect <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Reichard&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ra\xc3\xbal Reichard (page does not exist)">Ra\xc3\xbal Reichard</a> (1908\xe2\x80\x931996) and construction began in 1938. The hotel\'s exterior was designed to resemble the famed luxury ocean liner,<sup id="cite_ref-NYT-77_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-77-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> elongated and curved in front and back, with porthole-shaped lights on the facade of the 6th floor, as well as on the front on all floors except the front entrance and 2nd floor. Inside, the hotel features art deco design, complete with <a href="/wiki/Roman_architecture" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman architecture">Roman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egyptian</a>, and <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> details, high ceilings, corridors looking down into a central skylighted <a href="/wiki/Atrium_(architecture)" title="Atrium (architecture)">atrium</a>, banisters that resemble those on ships on the edges of all floors except the lobby, a pool outside in the back shaped similarly to the hotel\'s, and had blue or orange awnings above the windows on the upper floors until the hotel closed in the 1960s.\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:San_Juan_normandie_hotel_Calle_Normandie_IMG_1631.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/San_Juan_normandie_hotel_Calle_Normandie_IMG_1631.jpg/220px-San_Juan_normandie_hotel_Calle_Normandie_IMG_1631.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="103" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/San_Juan_normandie_hotel_Calle_Normandie_IMG_1631.jpg/330px-San_Juan_normandie_hotel_Calle_Normandie_IMG_1631.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/San_Juan_normandie_hotel_Calle_Normandie_IMG_1631.jpg/440px-San_Juan_normandie_hotel_Calle_Normandie_IMG_1631.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4023" data-file-height="1891" /></a><figcaption>The hotel in 2016</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Designers and artists from Puerto Rico, Dominican Republic, France, and Spain all contributed to the hotel\'s overall construction. When it opened on October 10, 1942,<sup id="cite_ref-SJDS-2021-03-17_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SJDS-2021-03-17-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> it became an instant sensation among the island\'s social elite. Completed at a cost of more than US$2,000,000, the hotel catered to many major <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_the_United_States" title="Cinema of the United States">Hollywood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Latin_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Latin American">Latin American</a> film stars such as <a href="/wiki/Cantinflas" title="Cantinflas">Cantinflas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Libertad_Lamarque" title="Libertad Lamarque">Libertad Lamarque</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Negrete" title="Jorge Negrete">Jorge Negrete</a>. The hotel also served as a performing arts venue for many of Puerto Rico\'s top entertainers, such as <a href="/wiki/Ruth_Fern%C3%A1ndez" title="Ruth Fern\xc3\xa1ndez">Ruth Fern\xc3\xa1ndez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Myrta_Silva" title="Myrta Silva">Myrta Silva</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sylvia_Rexach" title="Sylvia Rexach">Sylvia Rexach</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Carmen_Delia_Dipini" title="Carmen Delia Dipini">Carmen Delia Dipini</a>.\n</p><p>The coastline of Puerto Rico was expropriated by the federal government for defense during World War II, including the hotel, and Ben\xc3\xadtez Rexach, who had moved to the Dominican Republic in 1944, reportedly protested by refusing to pay his property taxes.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT-77_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-77-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> However, court records indicate Ben\xc3\xadtez Rexach had argued unsuccessfully that as an American citizen without congressional representation, he was exempt from federal taxes.<sup id="cite_ref-USvRexach_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USvRexach-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> After being closed and abandoned in the 1970s,<sup id="cite_ref-NYT-77_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-77-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> the hotel was restored in the early 1990s. Afterwards, it underwent massive renovations due to heavy damage caused by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Georges" title="Hurricane Georges">Hurricane Georges</a> in 1998. After storm-related damages were repaired, the hotel remained in business until 2004, when another renovation project commenced. In early 2005, the 173-room hotel reopened after its refurbishing and would operate as a <a href="/wiki/Boutique_Hotel" class="mw-redirect" title="Boutique Hotel">Boutique Hotel</a> for 4 years, before closing again by late 2010, since then the hotel has remained unused and vacant for over a decade.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The Normandie Hotel was purchased on August 8, 2013, by Ben Medetsky and Jack Polatsek of Interra Capital Group for nearly US$4,000,000 but after initial plans for redevelopment of the hotel, the group decided to sell it.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2015, the owners removed the hotel\'s historic roof sign in order to protect and preserve it, and only the structure that held up the letters of the sign remains.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Although various groups of investors attempted to purchase the property in 2014,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> then in the spring of 2017<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and again in the spring of 2018,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> it wasn\'t actually sold until January 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-SJDS-2022-01-21_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SJDS-2022-01-21-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/List_of_Majority_Leaders_of_the_House_of_Representatives_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Majority Leaders of the House of Representatives of Puerto Rico">Majority Leader</a> <a href="/wiki/%C3%81ngel_Matos_Garc%C3%ADa" class="mw-redirect" title="\xc3\x81ngel Matos Garc\xc3\xada">\xc3\x81ngel Matos Garc\xc3\xada</a> (<a href="/wiki/Popular_Democratic_Party_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Popular Democratic Party (Puerto Rico)">PPD</a>) filed Joint House Resolution 0089 in March 2021; if passed, the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Economic_Development_and_Commerce" title="Puerto Rico Department of Economic Development and Commerce">Puerto Rico Department of Economic Development and Commerce</a> would seize the property and demolish the hotel.<sup id="cite_ref-SJDS-2021-03-17_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SJDS-2021-03-17-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Puerto_Rican_Culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Institute of Puerto Rican Culture">Institute of Puerto Rican Culture</a> made a statement opposing the resolution two days later.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Later that year, despite rumors to the contrary, <a href="/wiki/Hard_Rock_International" class="mw-redirect" title="Hard Rock International">Hard Rock International</a> denied that it was interested in the hotel.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The hotel was purchased by the Ishay Group for US$8.6&#160;million, who plan to restore the hotel and reopen it, possibly converting some rooms into residences.<sup id="cite_ref-SJDS-2022-01-21_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SJDS-2022-01-21-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Recognition">Recognition</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Normandie_Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Recognition"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Normandie Hotel was added to the U.S. <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1980.<sup href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and to the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Register_of_Historic_Sites_and_Zones" title="Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones">Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones</a> in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Popular_culture">Popular culture</h2><span
