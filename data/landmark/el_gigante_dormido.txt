href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Cordillera Central (Puerto Rico)">Cordillera Central</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>El Gigante Dormido</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> for the <i>sleeping giant</i>) is a mountain located in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Adjuntas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Adjuntas, Puerto Rico">Adjuntas, Puerto Rico</a>. Its highest peak, <b>Cerro El Gigante</b>, rises to 3,175 feet (968 m) in elevation above sea level.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The mountain is famous for its distinctive shape that resembles the silhouette of a reclining man\'s head. This shape gives the mountain and its summit their names which mean \'<i>the sleeping giant</i>\' and \'<i>hill of the giant</i>\' respectively in Spanish. Due to its location south of the downtown district of <a href="/wiki/Adjuntas_barrio-pueblo" title="Adjuntas barrio-pueblo">Adjuntas Pueblo</a>, the mountain can easily be seen from many parts of town. Many local folk tales are associated with the mountain and the origin of its peculiar shape.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span
