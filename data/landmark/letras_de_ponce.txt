Rivera Villafa\xc3\xb1e</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Letras de Ponce</b> (English: Ponce letters) is a famous landmark in Barrio <a href="/wiki/Sabanetas_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sabanetas (Ponce)">Sabanetas</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>, located southbound on <a href="/wiki/PR-52" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-52">PR-52</a> at the intersection with <a href="/wiki/PR-10" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-10">PR-10</a>. The iconic monument consists of five letters that spell the name of the city P-O-N-C-E in 20-foot  high (6.1&#160;m) by 18-foot  wide (5.5&#160;m) by 12-foot  deep (3.7&#160;m) red and black letters. The letters, and its resulting sign, were designed by Carlos Rivera Villafa\xc3\xb1e, a sculptor from Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The landmark is said to be "known to every Puerto Rican."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Letras_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 2002 the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Transportation_and_Public_Works" title="Puerto Rico Department of Transportation and Public Works">Puerto Rico Department of Transportation</a> presented a contest for the design of a monument to be placed on State Route 52 (PR-52), at the entrance of the city of Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The contest was part of what would become 97 projects of <a href="/wiki/Public_art" title="Public art">public art</a> spread throughout Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In selecting the work of art, the Government of Puerto Rico considered 318<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> submissions from hundreds of artists around the world, including <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ada_Bobonis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ada Bobonis (page does not exist)">Ada Bobonis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ann_Hamilton_(artist)" title="Ann Hamilton (artist)">Ann Hamilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antoni_Muntadas" title="Antoni Muntadas">Antoni Muntadas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liliana_Porter" title="Liliana Porter">Liliana Porter</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Devorah_Sperber" title="Devorah Sperber">Devorah Sperber</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Rivera Villafa\xc3\xb1e\'s proposal was one of the winners, and the winner for the Ponce entrance monument.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Construction_and_design">Construction and design</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Letras_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Construction and design"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The letters are built of "marine steel", also known as <i>corteen</i>, the same material used in the construction of ships.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Each letter measures 20 feet high by 18 feet wide by 12 feet deep.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Together they span an area of half a kilometer. The breadth of the letters is intended to symbolize the extension of the city of Ponce, "heightening Ponce\'s urban identity and raising it to its territorial extension."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The letters cost US$430,000.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Its design, manufacture and suiting all were done in Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Carlos_A._Rivera_Villafa\xc3\xb1e"><span id="Carlos_A._Rivera_Villafa.C3.B1e"></span>Carlos A. Rivera Villafa\xc3\xb1e</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Letras_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Carlos A. Rivera Villafa\xc3\xb1e"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Carlos A. Rivera Villafa\xc3\xb1e was born in Ponce in 1966. He graduated from the Escuela de Artes Graficas de Puerto Rico in San Juan, Puerto Rico with a bachelor\'s degree in 1989. In addition to the <i>Letras de Ponce</i>, Rivera Villafa\xc3\xb1e has works in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, Madrid, <a href="/wiki/Lima" title="Lima">Lima</a>, <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">Havana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cuenca,_Ecuador" title="Cuenca, Ecuador">Cuenca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>, New York City, and Miami.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Maintenance">Maintenance</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Letras_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Maintenance"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The monument is regularly maintained with fresh paint as recommended by its designer.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> They require special care as well as a special paint.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> They are painted in black and red, the traditional colors of the city of Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The monument is also painted after acts of vandalism, including graffiti.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Letras_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The monument is located on smooth, flat terrain, and each letter is surrounded by flowers. Its site is just outside the northeastern edge of the urban area of Ponce, on PR-52, the main road into Ponce from San Juan. While the monument is visible as motorists approach the city on the highway, it has been noted that motorists are not able to stop to admire it, as they risk being fined.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The monument\'s unique location made it an ideal site to hold a protest. In October 2009, a group of some 20 Government of Puerto Rico public employees used the site to launch a protest against their recent layoff.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li><span class="geo-inline"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1156832818"><span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="https://geohack.toolforge.org/geohack.php?pagename=Letras_de_Ponce&amp;params=18_01_31.51_N_66_34_12.86_W_type:landmark"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">18\xc2\xb001\xe2\x80\xb231.51\xe2\x80\xb3N</span> <span class="longitude">66\xc2\xb034\xe2\x80\xb212.86\xe2\x80\xb3W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">18.0254194\xc2\xb0N 66.5702389\xc2\xb0W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">18.0254194; -66.5702389</span></span></span></a></span></span></li></ul>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Alterations_and_vandalism">Alterations and vandalism</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Letras_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Alterations and vandalism"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>It is illegal to make unauthorized physical alterations to the sign. The letters have been vandalised a few times. In November 2011, the letter "O" was vandalised with graffiti.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Controversy">Controversy</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Letras_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Controversy"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Due to its location at the center and both sides of a major expressway, it is one of Puerto Rico\'s most controversial works of public art.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In April 2009, the Puerto Rico Department of Transportation and Public Works (DTOP) was fined almost US$80&#160;million for placing the letters there.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Subsequently, a bill was introduced into the Legislature of Puerto Rico to eliminate public art from Puerto Rico roads. The basis was that, allegedly, they were in violation of the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Highway_Administration" title="Federal Highway Administration">Federal Highway Administration</a> regulations.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In February 2010, it was agreed that the letters would stay where they were.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div
