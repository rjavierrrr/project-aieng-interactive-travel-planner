class="infobox-label">Created</th><td class="infobox-data">12 September 1969</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>San Juan Botanical Garden</b>, officially known as the <b><a href="/wiki/Botanical_Garden" class="mw-redirect" title="Botanical Garden">Botanical Garden</a> of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico" title="University of Puerto Rico">University of Puerto Rico</a></b>, is located in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> city of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Capital_(political)" class="mw-redirect" title="Capital (political)">capital</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  This lush 300-acre (1.2&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) \xe2\x80\x9curban garden\xe2\x80\x9d of <a href="/wiki/Native_plants" class="mw-redirect" title="Native plants">native</a> and exotic <a href="/wiki/Flora" title="Flora">flora</a> serves as a laboratory for the study, conservation and enrichment of plants, trees, flowers, grasses and many other plants.  Seventy-five acres are landscaped and open to the general public as well as researchers.\n</p><p>The entrance is located off the south side of the intersection of Highway 1 and Road 847 in <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Piedras,_Puerto_Rico" title="R\xc3\xado Piedras, Puerto Rico">R\xc3\xado Piedras</a>.  The \xe2\x80\x9cBosque Urbano del Nuevo Milenio\xe2\x80\x9d (<a href="/wiki/Nuevo_Milenio_State_Forest" title="Nuevo Milenio State Forest">Nuevo Milenio State Forest</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> a project for the conservation and growth of the <a href="/wiki/Urban_forest" title="Urban forest">urban forest</a>, is on the eastern side of the Botanical Garden.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Background">Background</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Juan_Botanical_Garden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The botanical garden, proposed in 1959, was endorsed by the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Higher_Education_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Council of Higher Education of Puerto Rico">Council of Higher Education of Puerto Rico</a> on 12 September 1969. On the initiative of Arturo Roque and supported by the president of the university, Dr. <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Ben%C3%ADtez" class="mw-redirect" title="Jaime Ben\xc3\xadtez">Jaime Ben\xc3\xadtez</a>, the Botanical Garden was inaugurated on March 10, 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Administratively, it functions as a part of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Mayag%C3%BCez" title="University of Puerto Rico at Mayag\xc3\xbcez">University of Puerto Rico, Mayag\xc3\xbcez campus</a>. Its international identification code is <b>UPR</b>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  Fred Schaffner is the director.\n</p><p>The garden was partially opened to the public from 1971 until 1989. However, in the wake of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hugo" title="Hurricane Hugo">Hurricane Hugo</a>, the garden was closed for reconstruction until it could reopen for visitors in 1991. The garden\'s botanical collections, lakes, falls and shelters are well connected by paths, roads and walkways, allowing the visitor to know and better appreciate the value of native and tropical flora.\n</p><p>A Master Plan designated as the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Ecological_Corridor" title="San Juan Ecological Corridor">San Juan Ecological Corridor</a> is underway which includes the development of public spaces, display exhibits, linkages for the new <a href="/wiki/Tren_Urbano" title="Tren Urbano">Tren Urbano</a> with the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico" title="University of Puerto Rico">University of Puerto Rico</a>, the Nuevo Milenio State Forest, the R\xc3\xado Piedras River riparian zones, and its floodplain restoration.\n</p>\n<div data-file-height="1332" /></a><figcaption>Nature trails at the Botanical Gardens.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The Botanic Garden exhibits several <i>thematic collections</i><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> comprising tropical and subtropical plants. It also exhibits a collection of <i>modern sculptures</i> <sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> by artists from Puerto Rico and Latin America.    \n</p>\n<ul><li><i>Garden of Heliconias</i>. Brightly colored <a href="/wiki/Heliconia" title="Heliconia">Heliconias</a> native to tropical <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>. There is also a small group of Heliconias from Asia.</li>\n<li><i>Bamboo Chapel</i>. A small chapel in a feathery <a href="/wiki/Bamboo" title="Bamboo">bamboo</a> forest.</li>\n<li><i>Orchid Garden</i>. A grand selection of <a href="/wiki/Orchidaceae" class="mw-redirect" title="Orchidaceae">orchid</a> species with a special collection of <a href="/wiki/Cattleya" title="Cattleya">Cattleya</a>.</li>\n<li><i>Monet\'s Garden</i>. An adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Claude_Monet" title="Claude Monet">Monet\'s</a> own aquatic garden in <a href="/wiki/Giverny" title="Giverny">Giverny</a> with its <a href="/wiki/Nymphaeaceae" title="Nymphaeaceae">water lilies</a> to the tropical milieu.</li>\n<li><i>Palmetum</i>. Gathers over 125 species of <a href="/wiki/Arecaceae" title="Arecaceae">palms</a>.</li>\n<li><i>Aquatic Garden</i>. Collection includes <a href="/wiki/Cyperus_papyrus" title="Cyperus papyrus">papyrus</a> and red sealing wax palms.</li>\n<li><i>Ta\xc3\xadno Garden</i>. An <a href="/wiki/Arboretum" title="Arboretum">arboretum</a> with a collection of species of trees found locally that were useful in the daily life of the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Ta\xc3\xadno Indians</a>.</li>\n<li><i>The Herbarium</i>. A collection of over 36,000 specimens available for study and research by University of Puerto Rico students and researchers from other institutions in Puerto Rico, the Caribbean and other parts of the world. The new herbarium\'s facilities are being finished.</li></ul>\n<p>The Garden comprises a living laboratory for informal instruction of the community in all aspects of the native flora and the other plants which are cultivated there.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Endangered_native_species">Endangered native species</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Juan_Botanical_Garden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Endangered native species"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/List_of_endemic_flora_of_Puerto_Rico" title="List of endemic flora of Puerto Rico">endemic plants of Puerto Rico</a>, including some which are threatened in the wild or in danger of extinction, are found in the northern part of the Garden. Irwin &amp; Barneby * <i>Eugenia stahlii</i> (<a href="/wiki/Kiaersk." class="mw-redirect" title="Kiaersk.">Kiaersk.</a>) Krug &amp; (Urb.) * <i>Lyonia stahlii</i> (Urb.) * <i>Ternstroemia stahlii</i> (Krug &amp; Urb.)  The genus <i>Stahlia</i> is represented by a single species, <i>Stahlia monosperma</i> (Tul.) (Urb.), found in the wild only in Puerto Rico and the eastern <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>. This species, listed as endangered, is known in Puerto Rico under the name of <i><a href="/wiki/C%C3%B3bana_Negra" class="mw-redirect" title="C\xc3\xb3bana Negra">c\xc3\xb3bana
