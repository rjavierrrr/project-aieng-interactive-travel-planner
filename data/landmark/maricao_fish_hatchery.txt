class="infobox-label" style="font-weight:bold; border: 0;">Added to NRHP</th><td class="infobox-data" style="border: 0;">February 21, 2017</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Maricao Fish Hatchery</b> (<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_Spanish" title="Puerto Rican Spanish">Spanish</a>: <i>Vivero de Peces de Maricao</i>), also known as the <b>Insular Fish Hatchery</b> (<i>Criadero Insular de Peces</i>), is a historic 3.32 acre <a href="/wiki/Fish_hatchery" title="Fish hatchery">fish hatchery</a> located along the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Rosario" title="R\xc3\xado Rosario">R\xc3\xado Rosario</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Maricao_Afuera" title="Maricao Afuera">Maricao Afuera</a> district (<i>barrio</i>) of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rican</a> municipality of <a href="/wiki/Maricao,_Puerto_Rico" title="Maricao, Puerto Rico">Maricao</a>, within the <a href="/wiki/Maricao_State_Forest" title="Maricao State Forest">Maricao State Forest</a> jurisdiction.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The hatchery is located at approximately 1,500 feet (460 meters) above sea level, 1.2 kilometers from <a href="/wiki/Maricao_barrio-pueblo" title="Maricao barrio-pueblo">downtown Maricao</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maricao_Fish_Hatchery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Maricao Fish Hatchery was established in 1937 on three segments of land originally measuring approximately 4 acres along the <a href="/wiki/Maricao_River" title="Maricao River">Maricao River</a> on the northern edge of the Maricao State Forest Reserve by the <a href="/wiki/Civilian_Conservation_Corps" title="Civilian Conservation Corps">Civilian Conservation Corps</a> (CCC) for the Insular Forest Service, and under the direction of <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Frederick_Hildebrand" title="Samuel Frederick Hildebrand">Dr. Samuel F. Hildebrand</a> of the Departments of <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Agriculture" title="United States Department of Agriculture">Agriculture</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Commerce" title="United States Department of Commerce">Commerce</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The original district consisted of six buildings and additional infrastructure built using stone and concrete as construction materials. The hatchery facilities officially opened in January 1938, and it represents the introduction of recreation fishing or sport fishing in Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Government of Puerto Rico">Government of Puerto Rico</a> added an additional 2.75 acres for the hatchery in 1940, and by 1942, the expanded fish hatchery facilities included large grow-out ponds for breeding fish (particularly <a href="/wiki/Brown_trout" title="Brown trout">brown</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rainbow_trout" title="Rainbow trout">rainbow trout</a>) and sixteen smaller ponds for breeding of <a href="/wiki/Crustacean" title="Crustacean">crustaceans</a>. At a later date, another grow-out pond was added in the northern segment, west of the original ponds. Reconstruction of these facilities were undertaken between the years 1998 and 2000 after considerable damages caused by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Georges" title="Hurricane Georges">Hurricane Georges</a>, which devastated the island in September 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> At a later date, a visitor parking was built on a lot across the Maricao River. This visitors parking is not included within the district\'s boundaries.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Today the property is administered by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Natural_and_Environmental_Resources" title="Puerto Rico Department of Natural and Environmental Resources">Puerto Rico Department of Natural and Environmental Resources</a>. Although the fish hatchery is no longer operational, this district retains integrity of location, setting, workmanship, materials, feeling and association. For this reason, it was added to the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">United States National Register of Historic Places</a> in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Buildings_and_structures">Buildings and structures</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maricao_Fish_Hatchery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Buildings and structures"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Maricao Fish Hatchery historic district has been included in the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> since February 21, 2017, as it is a prime example of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Deal_Era_Constructions_in_the_Forest_Reserves_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="New Deal Era Constructions in the Forest Reserves of Puerto Rico (page does not exist)">New Deal Era Constructions in the Forest Reserves of Puerto Rico</a>, a collection of historic sites built by the <a href="/wiki/Civilian_Conservation_Corps" title="Civilian Conservation Corps">Civilian Conservation Corps</a> on multiple <a href="/wiki/List_of_protected_areas_of_Puerto_Rico" title="List of protected areas of Puerto Rico">protected natural areas of Puerto Rico</a> between 1933 and 1942. The fish hatchery historic district includes 28 buildings, structures and resources, out of which 18 are inscribed as <a href="/wiki/Contributing_property" title="Contributing property">contributing properties</a>. In addition to the hatchery ponds, tanks and water channels, the <i>Vivero de Peces</i> historic district consists of an entrance bridge built by the CCC in 1938, a historic entrance stone marker, a series of gazebos and the main administration office buildings. The buildings and structures built after the year 1966 are not included in the historic district designation.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="See_also">See also</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
