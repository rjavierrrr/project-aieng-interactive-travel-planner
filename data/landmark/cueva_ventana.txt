lang="en">Window Cave</i>) is a large cave situated atop a <a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">limestone</a> cliff in <a href="/wiki/Arecibo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Arecibo, Puerto Rico">Arecibo, Puerto Rico</a>, overlooking the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Arecibo" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Arecibo">R\xc3\xado Grande de Arecibo</a> valley. It is visible from the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_123" title="Puerto Rico Highway 123">PR-123</a> but is accessible from a trail that begins adjacent to a <a href="/wiki/Puma_Energy_International" class="mw-redirect" title="Puma Energy International">Puma</a> gas station located along <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_10" title="Puerto Rico Highway 10">PR-10</a> on kilometer 75. The cave and surrounding land are privately owned.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Tourism">Tourism</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cueva_Ventana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Tourism"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div><p>\nThe cave is a popular tourist attraction for Puerto Ricans as well as tourists visiting from outside Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> An initial admittance fee for Saturday and Sunday visits was established by the owner of a nearby gas station, due to the location\'s popularity. The number of visitors is at its largest on both Saturdays and Sundays from early morning to an hour before nightfall. In January 2013, a daily admission fee was established, and later raised the following month. A boardwalk project was constructed from the gas station, leading up to around 200 feet into the trail, as well as a small parking lot atop a plateau behind the gas station. After a rise in popularity and visits, different admission fees were established for certain days of the week, providing necessary safety equipment for the 45-minute guided tour. The number of visitors is greatest on both Saturdays and Sundays from early morning to an hour before nightfall. Nightly tours are also offered on Wednesdays and Thursdays.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span
