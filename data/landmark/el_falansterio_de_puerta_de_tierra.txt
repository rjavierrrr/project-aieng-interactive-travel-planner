2000</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Puerta de Tierra Phalanstery</b> (<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_Spanish" title="Puerto Rican Spanish">Spanish</a>: <i>Falansterio de Puerta de Tierra</i>), simply known as <i><b>El Falansterio</b></i> and originally known as the <b>Puerta de Tierra Tenement Group Project A</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Historic_districts_in_the_United_States" title="Historic districts in the United States">historic district</a> and <a href="/wiki/Art_Deco" title="Art Deco">Art Deco</a> <a href="/wiki/Public_housing" title="Public housing">public housing</a> building complex from 1937 located in the <a href="/wiki/Puerta_de_Tierra,_San_Juan" title="Puerta de Tierra, San Juan">Puerta de Tierra</a> sub-district of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>. \n</p><p>The district is located in the eastern section of the <a href="/wiki/Isleta_de_San_Juan" class="mw-redirect" title="Isleta de San Juan">Islet of San Juan</a>, close to the <a href="/wiki/Capitol_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Capitol of Puerto Rico">Capitol of Puerto Rico</a>, and it is bounded by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_35" title="Puerto Rico Highway 35">Manuel Fern\xc3\xa1ndez Juncos Avenue</a> to the south, San Juan Bautista Street to the east, and the del Tren and Mat\xc3\xadas Ledesma Streets to the north and west, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The construction was a <a href="/wiki/New_Deal" title="New Deal">New Deal</a> investment from the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Reconstruction_Administration" title="Puerto Rico Reconstruction Administration">Puerto Rico Reconstruction Administration</a> with funding from the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Emergency_Relief_Administration" title="Federal Emergency Relief Administration">Federal Emergency Relief Administration</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n</p><p>The building complex today serves as a public condominium owned by <i>Condominio del Falansterio, Inc.</i> and has been listed in the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">United States National Register of Historic Places</a> since April 4, 1984,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and on the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Register_of_Historic_Sites_and_Zones" title="Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones">Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones</a> since 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-:02_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:02-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span
