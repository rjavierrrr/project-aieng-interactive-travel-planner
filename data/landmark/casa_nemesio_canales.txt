Canales</i>) is a museum dedicated to founder and first mayor of Jayuya  <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosario_Canales&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rosario Canales (page does not exist)">Rosario Canales</a> in <a href="/wiki/Coabey" title="Coabey">Coabey</a>, in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico" title="Jayuya, Puerto Rico">Jayuya, Puerto Rico</a>. The museum grounds also contain the <b>El Cem\xc3\xad Museum</b> (Spanish: <i>Museo del Cem\xc3\xad</i>) and views of <a href="/wiki/Los_Tres_Picachos" title="Los Tres Picachos">Los Tres Picachos</a> mountain on the <a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Cordillera Central (Puerto Rico)">Cordillera Central</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Nemesio_Canales_Museum">Nemesio Canales Museum</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Casa_Nemesio_Canales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Nemesio Canales Museum"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The museum (Spanish: <i>Museo Nemesio Canales</i>) is dedicated to the life and work of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Ricans" title="Puerto Ricans">Puerto Rican</a> writer and politician <a href="/wiki/Nemesio_Canales" title="Nemesio Canales">Nemesio Canales</a>, who also participated in the <a href="/wiki/Grito_de_Lares" title="Grito de Lares">Grito de Lares</a> revolt in 1868. The museum replicates the original structure and its interiors, and in addition of being dedicated to Nemesio Canales it also has several displays to different members of the <i>Canales</i> family, including Rosario Canales, founder and first mayor of <a href="/wiki/Jayuya_barrio-pueblo" title="Jayuya barrio-pueblo">Jayuya</a>. The original house, built in the late 1800s, was deteriorated and destroyed by hurricanes.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Cemi_Museum_in_Barrio_Coabey,_Jayuya.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Cemi_Museum_in_Barrio_Coabey%2C_Jayuya.jpg/220px-Cemi_Museum_in_Barrio_Coabey%2C_Jayuya.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Cemi_Museum_in_Barrio_Coabey%2C_Jayuya.jpg/330px-Cemi_Museum_in_Barrio_Coabey%2C_Jayuya.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Cemi_Museum_in_Barrio_Coabey%2C_Jayuya.jpg/440px-Cemi_Museum_in_Barrio_Coabey%2C_Jayuya.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="664" /></a><figcaption>El Cemi Museum\'s <a href="/wiki/Zemi" title="Zemi">zemi</a>-shaped building.</figcaption></figure>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="El_Cem\xc3\xad_Museum"><span id="El_Cem.C3.AD_Museum"></span>El Cem\xc3\xad Museum</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Casa_Nemesio_Canales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: El Cem\xc3\xad Museum"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p><b>El Cem\xc3\xad museum</b> (Spanish: <i>Museo del Cem\xc3\xad</i>) is dedicated to the native <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Taino</a> culture, particularly the <a href="/wiki/Zemi" title="Zemi">zemi</a> and other artifacts found in the area. This museum is housed in a zemi-shaped building located in the grounds of the Canales house.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Other displayed items include a carved wooden tongue depressor that was used in ritual vomiting ceremonies. There is also a mural depicting a series of petroglyphs that the Ta\xc3\xadno are believed to have created.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="References">References</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
