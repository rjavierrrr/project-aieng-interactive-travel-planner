<b>Cayo Ratones</b> or <b>Isla Ratones</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/Mice" class="mw-redirect" title="Mice">mice</a> island</i>) is a small island located near the <a href="/wiki/Joyuda_Lagoon" title="Joyuda Lagoon">Joyuda Lagoon</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cabo_Rojo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cabo Rojo, Puerto Rico">Cabo Rojo, Puerto Rico</a>. The island is a popular spot for <a href="/wiki/Snorkeling" title="Snorkeling">snorkeling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bathing" title="Bathing">bathing</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isla_de_Ratones_(Cabo_Rojo,_Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The island was originally known as <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Pi\xc3\xb1as</i></span>, <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Pi\xc3\xb1ero</i></span> Island, or <b>Pineapple Island</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-Vargas_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vargas-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  In the past, the island was used to cultivate <a href="/wiki/Pineapple" title="Pineapple">pineapple</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sugarcane" title="Sugarcane">sugar cane</a> crops.<sup id="cite_ref-Vargas_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vargas-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  Later on for years the island was abandoned and was practically a dumpster, because of which the residents of <a href="/wiki/Joyuda,_Puerto_Rico" title="Joyuda, Puerto Rico">Joyuda</a> began referring to it as <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Isla de los Ratones</i></span>, (<abbr title="translation">transl.</abbr><span>&#8201;Island of Mice</span>) because of the amount of rodents that inhabited the island.<sup id="cite_ref-Vargas_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vargas-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>During half of the twentieth century, the then owner of the <a href="/wiki/Cerveceria_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Cerveceria India">Cerveceria India</a>, Don <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_Vald%C3%A9s_Cobi%C3%A1n" title="Alfonso Vald\xc3\xa9s Cobi\xc3\xa1n">Alfonso Vald\xc3\xa9s Cobi\xc3\xa1n</a> was one of the people in charge of the Island.  During this time he began cleaning up the small isle.<sup id="cite_ref-Vargas_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vargas-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1988, the island was sold to <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>, who organized a fantasy island <a href="/wiki/Sweepstakes" class="mw-redirect" title="Sweepstakes">sweepstakes</a> promoted by <a href="/wiki/Cyndi_Lauper" title="Cyndi Lauper">Cyndi Lauper</a>. After several protests, nothing more was heard from MTV.<sup id="cite_ref-book_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-book-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Now, the custodians of the island are the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Natural_and_Environmental_Resources" title="Puerto Rico Department of Natural and Environmental Resources">Puerto Rico Department of Natural and Environmental Resources</a> (DRNA) and Tourmarine, a private tourist company.<sup id="cite_ref-Vargas_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vargas-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  They have a cooperative agreement to help in the cleaning and trash collecting because of the DRNA lack of personnel.<sup id="cite_ref-Vargas_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vargas-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>As part of the annual <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Tr\xc3\xadalo de Joyuda en Cabo Rojo</i></span> or Joyuda Triathlon in Cabo Rojo, one leg of the event consist of reaching the island and returning.<sup id="cite_ref-Vargas_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vargas-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Restoration">Restoration</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isla_de_Ratones_(Cabo_Rojo,_Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Restoration"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 2005, <a href="/wiki/Caborroje%C3%B1os_Pro_Salud_y_Ambiente" title="Caborroje\xc3\xb1os Pro Salud y Ambiente">Caborroje\xc3\xb1os Pro Salud y Ambiente</a>, an environmentalist organization undertook a restoration project on the island\'s north shore to try to stop erosion and sedimentation that is threatening the coral reef and mangrove forest that used to surround the island.<sup id="cite_ref-NOAA_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NOAA-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The island has lost approximately 45 percent of its area over the past 60 years due to <a href="/wiki/Coral_bleaching" title="Coral bleaching">coral bleaching</a>, disease, and destruction of reefs by wave force and currents.<sup id="cite_ref-Star_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Star-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2006, the <a href="/wiki/Inter_American_University_of_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Inter American University of Puerto Rico">Inter American University of Puerto Rico</a> received grant from <a href="/wiki/NOAA" class="mw-redirect" title="NOAA">NOAA</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico_Foundation" title="Gulf of Mexico Foundation">Gulf of Mexico Foundation</a> to implement a second phase of the restoration project.<sup id="cite_ref-Ciencia_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ciencia-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Students, professors and members of Caborroje\xc3\xb1os Pro Salud y Ambiente, under the direction of the DRNA, planted some 400 floating seeds of <a href="/wiki/Red_mangrove" title="Red mangrove">red mangrove</a> that forms part of the ecological inventory that surrounds the island.<sup id="cite_ref-Vargas_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vargas-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Approximately 0.25 acres (0.10&#160;ha) of mangrove habitat was restored and 0.0091 acres (0.0037&#160;ha) of artificial reef was created.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Gallery">Gallery</h2><span
