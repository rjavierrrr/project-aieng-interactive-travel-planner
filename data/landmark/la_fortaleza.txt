(<a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>: <i>The Fortress</i>), also known as <i><b>Palacio de Santa Catalina</b><sup id="cite_ref-Wikisource_2019_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wikisource_2019-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> (Saint Catherine\'s Palace)</i>, is the <a href="/wiki/Official_residence" title="Official residence">official residence</a> and workplace of the <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Governor of Puerto Rico">governor of Puerto Rico</a>. Located in the historical district of <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">Old San Juan</a> in the capital city of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, it has served as the residence of the governor since the 16th century, making it the oldest executive mansion in continuous use in the <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Built between 1533 and 1540 by orders of <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles I of Spain</a>, the structure was the first <a href="/wiki/Medieval_fortification" title="Medieval fortification">fortification</a> constructed by the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish</a> on <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Islet" title="San Juan Islet">San Juan Islet</a> to defend <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Bay" title="San Juan Bay">San Juan Bay</a>, the harbor of <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">Old San Juan</a>. <i>La fortaleza</i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Castillo_San_Felipe_del_Morro" title="Castillo San Felipe del Morro">El Morro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Castillo_San_Crist%C3%B3bal_(San_Juan)" title="Castillo San Crist\xc3\xb3bal (San Juan)">San Crist\xc3\xb3bal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fort%C3%ADn_San_Juan_de_la_Cruz" title="Fort\xc3\xadn San Juan de la Cruz">El Ca\xc3\xb1uelo</a>, and other forts part of the <a href="/wiki/Walls_of_Old_San_Juan" title="Walls of Old San Juan">Walls of Old San Juan</a>, protected <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_Puerto_Rico#Strategic_geography" title="Geography of Puerto Rico">strategically and militarily</a> important <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, or <i>La Llave de las Indias (The Key to the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_the_Indies" title="Council of the Indies">Indies</a>)</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> from invasion by competing <a href="/wiki/List_of_modern_great_powers#Early_modern_powers" title="List of modern great powers">world powers</a> and harassment by <a href="/wiki/Privateers" class="mw-redirect" title="Privateers">privateers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Golden_Age_of_Piracy" title="Golden Age of Piracy">pirates</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Age_of_Sail" title="Age of Sail">Age of Sail</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Fort_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fort-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was designated a <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a> by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> in 1983 as part of <i><a href="/wiki/La_Fortaleza_and_San_Juan_National_Historic_Site_in_Puerto_Rico" title="La Fortaleza and San Juan National Historic Site in Puerto Rico">La Fortaleza and San Juan National Historic Site</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-UNESCO_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNESCO-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<link class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Fortaleza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The structure"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:AMH-6724-KB_View_of_Puerto_Rico.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/AMH-6724-KB_View_of_Puerto_Rico.jpg/200px-AMH-6724-KB_View_of_Puerto_Rico.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="166" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/AMH-6724-KB_View_of_Puerto_Rico.jpg/300px-AMH-6724-KB_View_of_Puerto_Rico.jpg 1.5x, /></a><figcaption><i>La Fortaleza</i> from the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Bay" title="San Juan Bay">Bay of San Juan</a> as recorded in the 1671 edition of <a href="/wiki/John_Ogilby" title="John Ogilby">John Ogilby</a>\'s <i>America</i>, one of the most influential works of the 17th Century</figcaption></figure>\n<p><i>La Fortaleza</i> was the first defensive fortification built in the historic city of Old San Juan, originally known as <i>Ciudad de Puerto Rico (rich port city)</i>, and the first of a series of military structures built to protect the city, which included the <a href="/wiki/Castillo_San_Felipe_del_Morro" title="Castillo San Felipe del Morro">Castillo San Felipe del Morro</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Castillo_San_Crist%C3%B3bal_(San_Juan)" title="Castillo San Crist\xc3\xb3bal (San Juan)">Fort San Crist\xc3\xb3bal</a>. The construction was authorized by <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor</a> as a defense against attacks from <a href="/wiki/Island_Caribs" class="mw-redirect" title="Island Caribs">Island Caribs</a> and the European powers of the time.\n</p><p>Initially, the structure consisted of four walls enclosing an interior <a href="/wiki/Patio" title="Patio">patio</a> with a circular tower known as the <i>Homage Tower</i>. From the top of the tower, following military tradition, the governor would take fidelity oaths at critical moments to the King and Queen of Spain. Later, a second tower named the <i>Austral Tower</i> was constructed.\n</p><p>During the 1640 reconstruction, a chapel named after <a href="/wiki/Catherine_of_Alexandria" title="Catherine of Alexandria">Santa Catalina Alejandr\xc3\xada</a>  originally standing outside the fortification\'s walls was integrated into the structure\'s walls, resulting in the alternate name of <i>Palacio de Santa Catalina (Saint Catherine\'s Palace).</i>\n</p><p>The complex currently consists of a few attached buildings with formal living quarters on the second floor and private quarters on the third. It overlooks the high city walls that front the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Bay" title="San Juan Bay">bay</a>, and within the north perimeter of the house are sheltered gardens and a swimming pool.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Fortaleza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Fortaleza---1899.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Fortaleza---1899.jpg/300px-Fortaleza---1899.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="148" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Fortaleza---1899.jpg/450px-Fortaleza---1899.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Fortaleza---1899.jpg/600px-Fortaleza---1899.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2107" data-file-height="1039" /></a><figcaption><i>La Fortaleza</i> in 1899</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Starting in 1529, <a href="/wiki/List_of_governors_of_Puerto_Rico" title="List of governors of Puerto Rico">Governor La Gama</a> petitioned the emperor on the need to build defensive fortifications "because the island\'s defenseless condition caused the people to emigrate."  Construction started in 1533, using stone, and concluded by 1540.  Yet the fort had no guns, and <a href="/wiki/Gonzalo_Fern%C3%A1ndez_de_Oviedo_y_Vald%C3%A9s" title="Gonzalo Fern\xc3\xa1ndez de Oviedo y Vald\xc3\xa9s">Gonzalo Fern\xc3\xa1ndez de Oviedo y Vald\xc3\xa9s</a> commented, "if it had been constructed by blind men could not have been located in a worse location."  Yet the structure has served as the governor\'s residence since 1544.<sup id="cite_ref-RA_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RA-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NPS_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NPS-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Since the 16th century, <i>La Fortaleza</i> has acted as the residence of the Governor of Puerto Rico, making it the oldest executive mansion in continuous use in the Americas.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The mansion was remodeled in 1846 by the Spanish authorities to adapt its military origin to its new purely administrative function.\n</p><p><i>La Fortaleza</i> has been captured three times by foreign powers:\n</p>\n<ul><li>1598, <a href="/wiki/George_Clifford,_3rd_Earl_of_Cumberland" title="George Clifford, 3rd Earl of Cumberland">George Clifford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Cumberland" title="Earl of Cumberland">Earl of Cumberland</a>, attacked San Juan.</li>\n<li>In 1625, General <a href="/wiki/Boudewijn_Hendricksz" title="Boudewijn Hendricksz">Boudewijn Hendricksz</a> (Balduino Enrico) of the Netherlands invaded the city and established himself at <i>La Fortaleza</i>. During the Dutch retreat, the fortress and the city were set ablaze.</li>\n<li>1898, the U.S. Navy invaded and occupied Puerto Rico, establishing a provisional government.</li></ul>\n<p>In 1834, Colonel <a href="/wiki/George_Dawson_Flinter" title="George Dawson Flinter">George Dawson Flinter</a> described the fortress of Santa Catalina as having a chapel, stables, cistern, and an east wing with spacious apartments.<sup id="cite_ref-Flinter_2016_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Flinter_2016-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>According to tradition, in 1898, just before the United States <a href="/wiki/History_of_Puerto_Rico#Invasion_of_1898" title="History of Puerto Rico">invaded</a> Puerto Rico during the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a>, the last Spanish governor of the island, <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_de_Ortega_y_Diez" title="Ricardo de Ortega y Diez">Ricardo De Ortega</a>, struck a <a href="/wiki/Longcase_clock" class="mw-redirect" title="Longcase clock">longcase clock</a> in <i>La Fortaleza</i> with his sword, stopping the clock and marking the time at which Spain lost control over Puerto Rico.\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Nationalist_revolt" title="San Juan Nationalist revolt">San Juan Nationalist revolt</a>, intending to attack then-governor <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Mar%C3%ADn" Three of the guards of the building, among them Lorenzo Ramos, were seriously injured.\n</p><p>On October 9, 1960, <i>La Fortaleza</i> was designated a United States <a href="/wiki/National_Historic_Landmark" title="National Historic Landmark">National Historic Landmark</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-lfnhl_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lfnhl-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Teodoro_Vidal" title="Teodoro Vidal">Teodoro Vidal</a>, a cultural affairs official under <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Mar%C3%ADn" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn</a>, organized renovation work on the building around this time.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1983, <i>La Fortaleza</i>, along with the <a Site">World Heritage Site</a> by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-UNESCO_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UNESCO-7"><span towers: the Astral Tower and the Homenaje Tower (Homage Tower), which show that it was originally a military structure.\n</p><p>The <i>Homage Tower</i> houses the <i>Santa Catalina Chapel</i>, where a colorful mosaic of 95,000 pieces in honor of the Holy Trinity stands out.\n</p><p>In 1956, when an area covered with a panel was unsealed, what could be the old kitch<i>en of La Fortaleza</i> was discovered in the <i>Austral Tower</i>.\n</p><p>The paintings exhibited at <i>La Fortaleza</i> are on loan from the <i>Institute of Puerto Rican Historical Culture</i> and are changed according to the taste of the ruler of the day.\n</p><p>Originally, <i>La Fortaleza</i> was painted salmon red, the characteristic color of the island\'s military installations of the time of Spanish colony. Its wooden doors were green and its columns were painted white with gold details.\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:P061411PS-0274_(5937467540).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/P061411PS-0274_%285937467540%29.jpg/220px-P061411PS-0274_%285937467540%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/P061411PS-0274_%285937467540%29.jpg/330px-P061411PS-0274_%285937467540%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/P061411PS-0274_%285937467540%29.jpg/440px-P061411PS-0274_%285937467540%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4096" data-file-height="2731" /></a><figcaption>U.S. President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> tours <i>La Fortaleza</i> in 2011</figcaption></figure>\n<p>U.S. Presidents who have visited <i>La Fortaleza</i>, include <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Hoover" title="Herbert Hoover">Herbert Hoover</a> in 1931, <a href="/wiki/Harry_S._Truman" title="Harry S. Kennedy</a> in 1961, <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> in 2011, and <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a> in 2013.\n</p><p><i>La Fortaleza</i> has had 156 governors: 124 under the Spanish regime, 19 under the U.S. regime and 13 Puerto Ricans, 11 elected, one appointed by the President of the United States <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_T._Pi%C3%B1ero" title="Jes\xc3\xbas T. Pi\xc3\xb1ero">Jes\xc3\xbas T. Pi\xc3\xb1ero</a> and one sworn in by the Constitution of Puerto Rico <a href="/wiki/Wanda_V%C3%A1zquez_Garced" title="Wanda V\xc3\xa1zquez Garced">Wanda V\xc3\xa1zquez Garced</a>.\n</p><p>Three flags fly over <i>La Fortaleza</i>: the Puerto Rican flag, the American flag, and the Governor\'s white flag. When the white flag is not raised, it is because the governor is outside the <i>Santa Catalina Palace</i>.\n</p><p>The <i>Hundido Garden</i> was named Do\xc3\xb1a In\xc3\xa9s Garden in honor of Do\xc3\xb1a <a href="/wiki/In%C3%A9s_Mendoza" title="In\xc3\xa9s Mendoza">In\xc3\xa9s Mendoza</a>, wife of former governor <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Mar%C3%ADn" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn</a>, because that was her favorite spot at <i>La Fortaleza</i>, where she lived for 16 years.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="In_literature">In literature</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Fortaleza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In literature"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 2011, Puerto Rican author <a href="/wiki/Giannina_Braschi" title="Giannina Braschi">Giannina Braschi</a> wrote the dramatic novel <i><a href="/wiki/United_States_of_Banana" title="United States of Banana">United States of Banana</a></i>, featuring climactic scenes of revolution at La Fortaleza.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Gallery">Gallery</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Fortaleza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Gallery"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<ul class="gallery mw-gallery-traditional">\n\t\t<li class="gallerybox" style="width: 155px">\n\t\t\t<div class="thumb" style="width: 150px;
