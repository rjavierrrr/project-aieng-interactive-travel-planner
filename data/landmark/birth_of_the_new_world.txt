2016</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <i><b>Birth of the New World</b></i>  (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es"><b>Nacimiento del Nuevo Mundo</b></i>, colloquially known as <i><b>La Estatua de Col\xc3\xb3n</b></i>, <abbr style="font-size:85%" title="literal translation">lit.</abbr> <i><b>Columbus\' Statue</b></i>) is a 360 foot (110&#160;m) bronze sculpture located on the Atlantic coastline of <a href="/wiki/Arecibo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Arecibo, Puerto Rico">Arecibo, Puerto Rico</a>. When completed in 2016, it became the tallest sculpture in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> (as well as the tallest not only in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, but in the <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">Western Hemisphere</a> in general) surpassing Mexico\'s <i><a href="/wiki/Guerrero_Chimalli" title="Guerrero Chimalli">Guerrero Chimalli</a></i> (which measures 200 feet or 61 meters in height including its base), and the fourth tallest worldwide, after the <a href="/wiki/Statue_of_Unity" title="Statue of Unity">Statue of Unity</a> in India (597&#160;ft; 182&#160;m), the <a href="/wiki/Spring_Temple_Buddha" title="Spring Temple Buddha">Spring Temple Buddha</a> in China (420&#160;ft; 130&#160;m), and the <a href="/wiki/Laykyun_Sekkya" title="Laykyun Sekkya">Laykyun Sekkya</a> in Myanmar (380&#160;ft; 120&#160;m). After being imported to Puerto Rico, there were plans for it to be erected in <a href="/wiki/Catano,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Catano, Puerto Rico">Cata\xc3\xb1o</a>. After being moved to <a href="/wiki/Mayag%C3%BCez,_Puerto_Rico" title="Mayag\xc3\xbcez, Puerto Rico">Mayag\xc3\xbcez</a> and facing further delays, <i>Birth of the New World</i> was assembled at its current location.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Birth_of_the_New_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Originally designed by <a href="/wiki/Georgians" title="Georgians">Georgian</a> sculptor <a href="/wiki/Zurab_Tsereteli" title="Zurab Tsereteli">Zurab Tsereteli</a> as a monument to commemorate the 500th anniversary of <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Voyages_of_Christopher_Columbus" title="Voyages of Christopher Columbus">first voyage</a>, <i>Birth of the New World</i> was constructed in 1991. The statue prominently depicts Columbus controlling an <a href="/wiki/Anachronism" title="Anachronism">anachronistic</a> depiction of a steering wheel, with a backdrop featuring the <i><a href="/wiki/Ni%C3%B1a_(ship)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ni\xc3\xb1a (ship)">Ni\xc3\xb1a</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Pinta_(ship)" title="Pinta (ship)">Pinta</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Santa_Mar%C3%ADa_(ship)" title="Santa Mar\xc3\xada (ship)">Santa Mar\xc3\xada</a></i> traversing the Atlantic Ocean.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Made of 2,750 bronze and steel pieces and weighing more than 1,300,000 pounds (650 short tons; 590 tonnes), the monument\'s 360&#160;ft (110&#160;m) height made it the tallest in the <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">Western Hemisphere</a> during the last decade of the 20th century, dwarfing the <a href="/wiki/Statue_of_Liberty" title="Statue of Liberty">Statue of Liberty</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Monumento_a_la_Virgen_de_la_Paz" title="Monumento a la Virgen de la Paz">Monumento a la Virgen de la Paz</a>.\n</p><p>Tsereteli offered <i>Birth of the New World</i> to the cities of <a href="/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fort_Lauderdale" class="mw-redirect" title="Fort Lauderdale">Fort Lauderdale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miami_Beach,_Florida" title="Miami Beach, Florida">Miami Beach</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">Columbus, Ohio</a>, but none of those cities accepted it, considering it an eyesore due to its disproportionate features or because its size would affect their skylines.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Amid its long search for a home, the statue gathered the derisive nicknames of "Chris Kong", "Robo-Columbus", and "From Russia with Ugh" while searching for a base.<sup id="cite_ref-govan2010_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-govan2010-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Despite the hindrances, the monument was featured in a stamp.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> A smaller and modified version, named <i><a href="/wiki/Birth_of_a_New_Man" title="Birth of a New Man">The Birth of a New Man</a></i> and meant to be the European link of a two-part composition along with <i>Birth of the New World</i>, was adopted by <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a>, Spain, and dedicated there in 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Search_for_a_location">Search for a location</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Birth_of_the_New_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Search for a location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="New_York_City">New York City</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Birth_of_the_New_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: New York City"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1991, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> was the first to be offered the statue. Tseretali proposed erecting the statue on <a href="/wiki/Roosevelt_Island" title="Roosevelt Island">Roosevelt Island</a>. However, this plan was met with concerns of its size, as it would visually dwarf the Statue of Liberty, yet be dwarfed itself by skyscrapers of the city\'s skyline that sat near Roosevelt Island.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Plans for it to be located in New York City were briefly resurrected in 1997 when then-real estate developer <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a> proposed that the statue be at <a href="/wiki/Riverside_South,_Manhattan" title="Riverside South, Manhattan">his development of rail yards</a>, but this was nixed by city leadership including mayor <a href="/wiki/Rudy_Giuliani" title="Rudy Giuliani">Rudy Giuliani</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="Miami_Beach">Miami Beach</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Birth_of_the_New_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Miami Beach"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1992, the <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a> area was offered the statue, after financier and investor <a href="/wiki/Bennett_S._LeBow" title="Bennett S. LeBow">Bennett S. LeBow</a> sought to see it erected at <a href="/wiki/South_Pointe_Park" title="South Pointe Park">South Pointe</a> in Miami Beach, near the <a href="/wiki/Government_Cut" title="Government Cut">Government Cut</a> entrance of the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Miami" class="mw-redirect" title="Port of Miami">Port of Miami</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-UPI1_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UPI1-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-return1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-return1-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Supporters argued that it had the potential to become an icon for the City of Miami.<sup id="cite_ref-return1_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-return1-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The sculpture attracted local criticism, including for the fact that the design included a depiction of a <a href="/wiki/Ship%27s_wheel" title="Ship&#39;s wheel">ship\'s wheel</a>, despite the fact that this device would not supplant the use of <a href="/wiki/Tiller" title="Tiller">tillers</a> for over 250 years after Columbus\'s first voyage to the Americas.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a> activist groups also expressed concern over a statue that celebrated a man whom they regarded as a <a href="/wiki/Rapist" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapist">rapist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Murder" title="Murder">murderer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Plans to erect it in Miami ultimately failed due to the large costs associated with erecting the statue.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="Fort_Lauderdale">Fort Lauderdale</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Birth_of_the_New_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Fort Lauderdale"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1992, as plans to locate it in Miami were fizzling out, <a href="/wiki/Fort_Lauderdale,_Florida" title="Fort Lauderdale, Florida">Fort Lauderdale, Florida</a> City Commissioner Gary Keno expressed interest in locating it in his city.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-8" class="reference"><a support for erecting the statue there.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The head would ultimately remain in a warehouse there for six years.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Native American activist groups again expressed concern.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="Columbus,_Ohio"><span id="Columbus.2C_Ohio"></span>Columbus, Ohio</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1993, the city of <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">Columbus, Ohio</a> was offered the statue.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Governor <a href="/wiki/George_V._Voinovich" class="mw-redirect" title="George V. Voinovich">George V. Voinovich</a> expressed interest, sending a <a href="/wiki/Letter_of_intent" title="Letter of intent">letter of intent</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Proponents argued that it could become an icon for the city, and would be a tourist generator.<sup id="cite_ref-divides_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-divides-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The costs associated with erecting the statue, again, posed an issue.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Mayor <a href="/wiki/Greg_Lashutka" title="Greg Lashutka">Greg Lashutka</a> refused to spend any city funds on erecting the statue.<sup id="cite_ref-divides_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-divides-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Despite his support for the statue, Governor Voinovich stated that no state money was available to erect the statue.<sup id="cite_ref-divides_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-divides-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This would mean that citizens groups would be required to raise as much as $25 million for the statue to be erected in Columbus.<sup id="cite_ref-divides_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-divides-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Additionally, Native American activist groups again raised concern.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-divides_11-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-divides-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The statue also met criticism for its massive size and criticisms that it would appear gaudy.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-divides_11-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-divides-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="St._Petersburg,_Florida"><span id="St._Petersburg.2C_Florida"></span>St. Petersburg, Florida</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Birth_of_the_New_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: St. Petersburg, Florida"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1995, Tsereteli sought to convince the city of <a href="/wiki/St._Petersburg,_Florida" title="St. Petersburg, Florida">St. Petersburg, Florida</a> to become the home of his statue.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> However, his overtures were met with large disinterest by the city.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="Baltimore">Baltimore</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Birth_of_the_New_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Baltimore"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1997, there was an effort to locate the statue in <a href="/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Inner_Harbor" title="Inner Harbor">Inner Harbor</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-jensengermani_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jensengermani-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Locations floated for the statue to be located in the Inner Harbor included <a href="/wiki/Fort_Carroll" title="Fort Carroll">Fort Carroll</a> and the site of a former Allied Chemical plant on the east side of the Inner Harbor.<sup id="cite_ref-jensengermani_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jensengermani-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Joseph DiCara, whose construction management firm would be responsible for assembling the statue if it were built in Baltimore, advocated for it to be erected in the city.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-jensengermani_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jensengermani-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Some other local businessmen, local <a href="/wiki/Italian_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Italian American">Italian American</a> community leaders, and politicians also lent their support.<sup id="cite_ref-jensengermani_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jensengermani-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Supporters touted its potential as a tourist attraction.<sup id="cite_ref-jensengermani_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jensengermani-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Mayor <a href="/wiki/Kurt_Schmoke" title="Kurt Schmoke">Kurt Schmoke</a> was initially interested in the prospect of erecting the statue.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> However, his office also expressed concern over the city lacking resources to help finance its erection.<sup id="cite_ref-jensengermani_12-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jensengermani-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Ivan Kazansky, the head of Moscow\'s Union of Sculptures, advised Baltimore against letting Tsereteli "ruin" their harbor.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Additionally, concerns again arose over its size.<sup id="cite_ref-maher1_4-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maher1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="Cata\xc3\xb1o,_Puerto_Rico"><span id="Cata.C3.B1o.2C_Puerto_Rico"></span>Cata\xc3\xb1o, Puerto Rico</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Birth_of_the_New_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Cata\xc3\xb1o, Puerto Rico"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1998, Edwin "Amolao" Rivera Sierra, mayor of the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Cata%C3%B1o,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cata\xc3\xb1o, Puerto Rico">Cata\xc3\xb1o</a> in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, decided to acquire the monument. Despite being donated by Tsereteli, the cost of importing the statue was 2.4&#160;million dollars, all of which was taken from the public treasury with the approval of governor <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Rossell%C3%B3" title="Pedro Rossell\xc3\xb3">Pedro Rossell\xc3\xb3</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-coto2011_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coto2011-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Rivera Sierra insisted that the municipal treasury would not lose a single cent on the project, and planned to erect the monument near the entrance of Cata\xc3\xb1o Bay. The unassembled pieces of the statue were relocated to an adjacent recreational park upon arriving on 12 November 1998. In its original plan, construction would have begun in 1999, with the dedication taking place in October 2000. However, the project caused immediate controversy; several homes would have to be destroyed to make way for it and the cost of its assembly was deemed excessive. The <i>Oficina del Contralor</i> (Office of the Comptroller) opened an investigation into the transaction and found that the 1.6&#160;million dollars in taxes that were due when the statue entered the port were not paid. The auditor also investigated the use of public funds to cover the travel expenses of Rivera Sierra when personally meeting Tseretelli in Russia.\n</p><p>The height of the monument raised concerns that it would interfere with air traffic to nearby <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Mar%C3%ADn_International_Airport" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn International Airport">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn International Airport</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-coto2011_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coto2011-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Ultimately, the Puerto Rico Port Authority denied Cata\xc3\xb1o the permits required to advance, citing "security concerns". The central government decided to stop providing any funding for the project. The unassembled pieces remained stored near Cata\xc3\xb1o Bay for several years. Rivera Sierra\'s successor, <a href="/wiki/Wilson_Soto" title="Wilson Soto">Wilson Soto</a>, declared that the municipality would not invest any public funds in the project. By this time, some of the pieces were beginning to show signs of rusting, and storing the pieces was costing $1.6 million in taxes per year, not including the salaries of several security guards.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The municipality also lost an estimated income of $200,000 that the recreational park would have provided were it open to the public. The total cost of the project was re-estimated due to the necessity of replacing several parts and some of the supporting steel structures, fixing exposure damage and the additional need for a sandblasting procedure. In early 2005, Tseretelli began searching for developers that would take over the project. However, these efforts were interrupted by Soto, who considered that simply assembling the statue was not enough to attract tourism. He suggested that the entire area would need to be developed.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The size of the project caused potential developers to lose interest.\n</p><p>On 16 August 2008, the municipality confirmed that <i>Birth of a New World</i> had been transferred to Holland Group Port Investment (HGPI), a private group that was involved in the development of the <a href="/wiki/Mayag%C3%BCez,_Puerto_Rico" title="Mayag\xc3\xbcez, Puerto Rico">Mayag\xc3\xbcez</a> port. Soto justified the action by claiming that simply storing the pieces was costing the public treasury 4 million dollars, and that Cata\xc3\xb1o could not afford a project that would exceed $100 million.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The private entity transferred the pieces to its facilities in Mayag\xc3\xbcez, announcing its intention to assemble the statue near the coastline in time for the <a href="/wiki/2010_Central_American_and_Caribbean_Games" title="2010 Central American and Caribbean Games">2010 Central American and Caribbean Games</a> that were being held in that municipality. HGPI devised a plan to attract an estimated 500,000 tourists to with <i>Birth of a New World</i> as its cornerstone. A regional plan would include the establishment of a museum of history near the statue. The possibility of establishing a cruise route between Mayag\xc3\xbcez and Seville was also explored. Once there, a Russian team examined the condition of the pieces and ensured that it could still be assembled.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="Finding_a_home_in_Arecibo,_Puerto_Rico"><span id="Finding_a_home_in_Arecibo.2C_Puerto_Rico"></span>Finding a home in Arecibo, Puerto Rico</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Birth_of_the_New_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Finding a home in Arecibo, Puerto Rico"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>HGPI was unable to fulfill its plan in time, citing disinterest by the municipal government, and the statue was reassigned for a different project. <a href="/wiki/Arecibo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Arecibo, Puerto Rico">Arecibo</a> became a frontrunner, and Mayor <a href="/wiki/Lemuel_Soto" title="Lemuel Soto">Lemuel Soto</a> began the permit process for a project that would combine the statue with other attractions, such as the <a href="/wiki/Arecibo_Observatory" title="Arecibo Observatory">Arecibo Observatory</a>. However, the municipality faced competition; Representative <a href="/wiki/David_Bonilla_Cort%C3%A9s" title="David Bonilla Cort\xc3\xa9s">David Bonilla Cort\xc3\xa9s</a> requested that the statue be assembled on <a href="/wiki/Desecheo_Island" title="Desecheo Island">Desecheo Island</a><sup id="cite_ref-coto2011_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coto2011-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and the newly elected mayor of Cata\xc3\xb1o, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Rosario" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Rosario">Jos\xc3\xa9 Rosario</a>, proposed using the statue as part of an eco park that would be constructed in the swampy region of Ci\xc3\xa9naga las Cucharillas.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a> also tentatively considered the project, but lost interest due to the condition of the pieces.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Ultimately, Tseretelli decided that it was "too late" to consider other venues and settled for Arecibo. However, once there the monument faced more controversy, this time by activists that condemned Columbus\' role in opening the door to the European <a href="/wiki/Genocide_of_indigenous_peoples" title="Genocide of indigenous peoples">genocide of indigenous peoples</a> and by groups that opposed its impact in the maritime-terrestrial zone.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Eventually, the <a href="/wiki/Luis_Fortu%C3%B1o" title="Luis Fortu\xc3\xb1o">Luis Fortu\xc3\xb1o</a> administration began processing the respective permits.\n</p><p>In 2012, Rosario denounced the Puerto Rico Department of Treasury for imposing nearly $3 million in retroactive taxes for the original transaction and unfinished projects related to it, resulting in an embargo of the municipality\'s income.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In June 2012, the first pieces began arriving at Barrio <a href="/wiki/Islote,_Arecibo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Islote, Arecibo, Puerto Rico">Islote</a> in Arecibo.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <i>Birth of the New World</i> became the main attraction of a tourist project called Columbus Theme Park (later renamed Terravista ParkLand) developed by Pan American Grain, which the administration expected would produce 4.6&#160;million dollars in income per year and recruit 900 new employees. The assembly investment was now estimated at $98 million, with only the reclassification of the zone preventing the project\'s advance. This process was completed by January 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> On 11 February 2014, a contemporary-<a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> movement, named Movimiento Ind\xc3\xadgena J\xc3\xadbaro Boricua, joined other anti-Columbus factions in protest the impending assembly of the monument.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The monument survived the passing of Cat. 5 <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">hurricane Maria</a> in September 2017 without structural damage.<sup id="cite_ref-Maria_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maria-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> However, the adjacent infrastructure was heavily affected.<sup id="cite_ref-Maria_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maria-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The remodeling of the beach house (which is expected to host the visitor center), as well as the construction of new roads, parking and a plaza were all halted.<sup id="cite_ref-Maria_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maria-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Reforestation efforts in the area were completely reverted after the winds decimated the flora.<sup id="cite_ref-Maria_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maria-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> These developments forced architect Roberto Alsina to delay the timetable and reconsider strategies, this while working to undo the damage to the area that would host the Terravista ParkLand.<sup id="cite_ref-Maria_24-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maria-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Tsereteli_et_al._v._Gonz\xc3\xa1lez_Freyre_et_al."><span id="Tsereteli_et_al._v._Gonz.C3.A1lez_Freyre_et_al."></span><i>Tsereteli et al. v. Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Freyre et al.</i></h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Birth_of_the_New_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Tsereteli et al. v. Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Freyre et al."><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Terravista_ParkLand_under_construction.jpeg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Terravista_ParkLand_under_construction.jpeg/220px-Terravista_ParkLand_under_construction.jpeg" decoding="async" width="220" height="113" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Terravista_ParkLand_under_construction.jpeg/330px-Terravista_ParkLand_under_construction.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Terravista_ParkLand_under_construction.jpeg/440px-Terravista_ParkLand_under_construction.jpeg 2x" data-file-width="3367" data-file-height="1724" /></a><figcaption>Status of land development as of December 2019.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>On 14 March 2019, Tsereteli sued Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Freyre, Pan American Grain and Columbus Park Corporation, claiming that previous agreements had not been fulfilled as the sculptor\'s corporation \xe2\x80\x93 Birth of New World LLC \xe2\x80\x93 had not been refunded an investment of $17 million (for transferring it between Mayag\xc3\xbcez and Arecibo) or received ownership of the terrains.<sup id="cite_ref-demanda_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-demanda-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This conflict prevented plans from fully opening to the public and prompted the municipality to change dates for the formal inauguration on multiple occasions.<sup id="cite_ref-tribunal_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tribunal-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Tsereteli was stated to be content with the visibility gained by the monument and unconcerned by the possibility of it remaining closed off for the time being.<sup id="cite_ref-tribunal_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tribunal-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>During a year, beginning when a contract was signed in November 2013, both sides discussed the transfer of between 766 and 866 yards (700 and 792&#160;m) of the plot to an entity of the author\'s choosing.<sup id="cite_ref-lasagacontinua_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lasagacontinua-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> However, Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Freyre claims that Tsereteli did not discuss the matter again until he issued a warning in late 2018, after more than a year of no communication, and that the Russian artist did not heed attempts to mediate.<sup id="cite_ref-lasagacontinua_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lasagacontinua-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The monument is assembled but the development of the Terravista ParkLand had yet to begin following the hurricane delays and is expected to take up to five years.<sup id="cite_ref-lasagacontinua_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lasagacontinua-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2021, both sides worked on a settlement to continue the project as a joint venture.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Economic_impact">Economic impact</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Birth_of_the_New_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Economic impact"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Prior to the inauguration, an economic study by consulting firm Estudios T\xc3\xa9cnicos, estimated that the statue would attract at least 300,000 tourists per year to the municipality, with additional income from taxes related to the project.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Shortly after its assembly was completed, the Puerto Rico Tourism Company labelled Birth of the New World a potential anchor attraction for the north region.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2019, then-mayor <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Molina_(politician)" title="Carlos Molina (politician)">Carlos Molina</a> stated that the increased traffic of people interested in seeing the monument had been the main motivator behind the opening of several new businesses in the municipality.<sup id="cite_ref-Molina2019_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Molina2019-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> He considered that Birth of the New World became \xe2\x80\x9ca tourist attraction for the local and foreign visitor\xe2\x80\x9d.<sup id="cite_ref-Molina2019_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Molina2019-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> He quantified the economic impact as of 2020 at 1,000 new jobs, 100 businesses and 500 property acquisitions.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The volume of people stopping along <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_22" title="Puerto Rico Highway 22">Puerto Rico Highway 22</a> (unsigned Interstate PR2) to see the monument motivated a legislative bill to build a lookout and rest area.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2021, Ernesto Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Rodr\xc3\xadguez, town hall secretary of Carlos Ram\xc3\xadrez (who succeeded Molina), acknowledged that Columbus is a controversial topic but noted that the tourism that the monument attracts is beneficial to the town\'s coffers. Despite logistic issues involving the traffic to the area and an international trend of removing statues depicting Columbus, Birth of the New World was considered the main attraction in the area following the collapse of the <a href="/wiki/Arecibo_Observatory" title="Arecibo Observatory">Arecibo Observatory</a> and the municipal administration opposed the demolition demanded by activists.<sup id="cite_ref-cuantificable_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cuantificable-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Restaurants and bars have been the most benefited from the increased clientele.<sup id="cite_ref-cuantificable_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cuantificable-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div
