colspan="2">\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera Park</b> (or <b>Parque Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera</b> in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>) is a 27.2 acre (110,000 m<sup>2</sup>) <a href="/wiki/Recreational" class="mw-redirect" title="Recreational">recreational</a> public space located in <a href="/wiki/Puerta_de_Tierra,_San_Juan" title="Puerta de Tierra, San Juan">Puerta de Tierra</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The park was named in honor of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rican</a> statesman <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Rivera" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera</a>. It is the largest <a href="/wiki/Town_square" title="Town square">public square</a> in the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan%E2%80%93Caguas%E2%80%93Guaynabo_metropolitan_area" title="San Juan\xe2\x80\x93Caguas\xe2\x80\x93Guaynabo metropolitan area">San Juan metropolitan area</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Rivera_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Luis_Munoz_Rivera_statue.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Luis_Munoz_Rivera_statue.jpg/140px-Luis_Munoz_Rivera_statue.jpg" decoding="async" width="140" height="217" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Luis_Munoz_Rivera_statue.jpg/210px-Luis_Munoz_Rivera_statue.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Luis_Munoz_Rivera_statue.jpg/280px-Luis_Munoz_Rivera_statue.jpg 2x" data-file-width="314" data-file-height="486" /></a><figcaption>Statue of Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera, located at the park which bears his name.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>In 1917 Law 43 was passed stating a park named Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera Park should be built in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a> and land was set aside for its creation.<sup id="cite_ref-US_Congress_1918_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-US_Congress_1918-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The land once formed part of the city\'s "<a href="/wiki/L%C3%ADnea_Avanzada" title="L\xc3\xadnea Avanzada">Third line of defense</a>" built in the 18th century. The powder magazine built in 1769, \xe2\x80\x9c<a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Ger%C3%B3nimo_Powderhouse&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="San Ger\xc3\xb3nimo Powderhouse (page does not exist)">El Polvor\xc3\xadn de San Ger\xc3\xb3nimo</a>\xe2\x80\x9d, designed by military engineer Thomas O\'Daly, is still located on the grounds of the park.\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Polvorin_San_Geronimo_SW_view_-_San_Juan_Puerto_Rico.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Polvorin_San_Geronimo_SW_view_-_San_Juan_Puerto_Rico.jpg/170px-Polvorin_San_Geronimo_SW_view_-_San_Juan_Puerto_Rico.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="128" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Polvorin_San_Geronimo_SW_view_-_San_Juan_Puerto_Rico.jpg/255px-Polvorin_San_Geronimo_SW_view_-_San_Juan_Puerto_Rico.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Polvorin_San_Geronimo_SW_view_-_San_Juan_Puerto_Rico.jpg/340px-Polvorin_San_Geronimo_SW_view_-_San_Juan_Puerto_Rico.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4000" data-file-height="3000" /></a><figcaption><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Ger%C3%B3nimo_Powderhouse&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="San Ger\xc3\xb3nimo Powderhouse (page does not exist)">San Ger\xc3\xb3nimo Powderhouse</a></figcaption></figure>\n<p>The east side of the park is bordered by the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_Building_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Supreme Court Building (Puerto Rico)">Puerto Rican Supreme Court building</a> designed in 1952-56 by architects <a href="/wiki/Toro_Ferrer" title="Toro Ferrer">Toro Ferrer</a>. To the north lies the public beach called <a href="/wiki/El_Escambr%C3%B3n_Beach" title="El Escambr\xc3\xb3n Beach">El Escambr\xc3\xb3n Beach</a>, the Parque del Tercer Milenio and the <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Sixto_Escobar" title="Estadio Sixto Escobar">Estadio Sixto Escobar</a>, former home of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Senators" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan Senators">San Juan Senators</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cangrejeros_de_Santurce_(baseball)" title="Cangrejeros de Santurce (baseball)">Santurce Crabbers</a> baseball clubs.\n</p><p>The park was designed by <a href="/wiki/Edward_H._Bennett" title="Edward H. Bennett">Bennett, Parsons &amp; Frost</a> of Chicago in 1925. Its construction from 1926 to 1934 was directed by <a href="/wiki/William_E._Parsons" title="William E. Parsons">William Parsons</a> and <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Valines_Cofres%C3%AD" class="mw-redirect" title="Francisco Valines Cofres\xc3\xad">Francisco Valines Cofres\xc3\xad</a>. The distinctive <i>faux bois</i> park elements and furniture were designed by sculptor Victor M. Cott in the 1930s. Subsequent major restorations have been directed by architects Orval Sifontes in the 1970s, Otto Reyes Casanova in 1990\xe2\x80\x9393, and by <a href="/wiki/Andres_Mignucci" class="mw-redirect" title="Andres Mignucci">Andres Mignucci</a>, in 2000\xe2\x80\x9304, who also wrote about the park\'s history in his book <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">[Con]textos: el Parque Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera y el Tribunal Supremo de Puerto Rico</i></span>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2015, then <a href="/wiki/List_of_mayors_of_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="List of mayors of San Juan, Puerto Rico">mayor of San Juan</a> <a href="/wiki/Carmen_Yul%C3%ADn_Cruz" title="Carmen Yul\xc3\xadn Cruz">Carmen Yul\xc3\xadn Cruz</a> announced the park would be used for educational purposes. In late December 2018, the park was the venue for a large fashion show raising awareness for the <a href="/wiki/Epidemiology_of_HIV/AIDS" title="Epidemiology of HIV/AIDS">HIV pandemic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2
