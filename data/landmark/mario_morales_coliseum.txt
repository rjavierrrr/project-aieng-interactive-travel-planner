title="LVSF">LVSF</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Mario Morales Coliseum</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Coliseo Mario Morales</i>) is an <a href="/wiki/Indoor_arena" class="mw-redirect" title="Indoor arena">indoor sporting arena</a> that is located in <a href="/wiki/Guaynabo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guaynabo, Puerto Rico">Guaynabo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. It used mainly for <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> but it can also be used for table tennis<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> or <a href="/wiki/Volleyball" title="Volleyball">volleyball</a>. The coliseum\'s seating capacity is 5,500 seats.<sup id="cite_ref-Discover_Puerto_Rico_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Discover_Puerto_Rico-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Apart from the Guaynabo Mets\' home games, the coliseum is also used for <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">boxing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roller_derby" title="Roller derby">roller derby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Music" title="Music">musical</a> acts. It features a large painting of Mario Morales on the entrance.\n</p><p>The coliseum opened in 1983, originally named <b>Mets Pavilion</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> but is currently named after Puerto Rican basketball player <a href="/wiki/Mario_Morales" title="Mario Morales">Mario Morales</a>. It is the home arena of the basketball team the <a href="/wiki/Mets_de_Guaynabo_(basketball)" title="Mets de Guaynabo (basketball)">Mets de Guaynabo</a>. It also serves as the home court of the <a href="/wiki/Mets_de_Guaynabo_(women%27s_volleyball)" title="Mets de Guaynabo (women&#39;s volleyball)">Mets de Guaynabo</a> women\'s volleyball team in the <a href="/wiki/Liga_de_Voleibol_Superior_Femenino" title="Liga de Voleibol Superior Femenino">LVSF</a>.\n</p><p>In August 2020, it also became the venue where COVID-19 testing was done during the <a
