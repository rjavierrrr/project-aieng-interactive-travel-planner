Both are dedicated to the same group of men.</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Monumento a los heroes de El Polvor\xc3\xadn</b> (Monument to the "El Polvor\xc3\xadn" fire heroes) is a <a href="/wiki/Mausoleum" title="Mausoleum">mausoleum</a> <a href="/wiki/Monument" title="Monument">monument</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>, dedicated to the seven <a href="/wiki/Firefighter" title="Firefighter">firefighters</a> and one civilian who subdued the flames of the "El Polvor\xc3\xadn" fire that took place on the night of <a href="/wiki/Calle_25_de_Enero" title="Calle 25 de Enero">25 January 1899</a> in that city.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monumento_a_los_h%C3%A9roes_de_El_Polvor%C3%ADn_(mausoleum)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The monument is located in the <a href="/wiki/Cementerio_Civil_de_Ponce" title="Cementerio Civil de Ponce">Cementerio Civil de Ponce</a> (Ponce Civil Cemetery), in barrio <a href="/wiki/Portugu%C3%A9s_Urbano" title="Portugu\xc3\xa9s Urbano">Portugu\xc3\xa9s Urbano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>.  Its coordinates are N 18.01327 W 66.63286 (18\xc2\xb0 0\' 47.7714" N, 66\xc2\xb0 37\' 58.2954" W).\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Background">Background</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monumento_a_los_h%C3%A9roes_de_El_Polvor%C3%ADn_(mausoleum)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>On 25 January 1899, a large fire (later dubbed "El Polvor\xc3\xadn") threatened the lives of Ponce\xc3\xb1os, as well as the economy of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> as a whole, given Ponce\'s <i>de facto</i> role as Puerto Rico\'s banking and agricultural capital. A painting inside <a href="/wiki/Parque_de_Bombas" title="Parque de Bombas">Parque de Bombas</a> commemorates the heroic acts of seven valiant "<a href="/wiki/Firefighter" title="Firefighter">bomberos</a>" and one civilian who fought bravely against the voracious fire that threatened the region. Disobeying orders from the American troops that had recently taken control of Puerto Rico, the group was able to appease the flames that had started inside the U.S. Army\xe2\x80\x99s gunpowder reserves. Due to their courageous efforts, disaster was narrowly averted.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> For their heroic valor and success, the group was honored many times both in Ponce and the rest of Puerto Rico. The <a href="/wiki/Mausoleum" title="Mausoleum">mausoleum</a> was built to keep their remains in the after life and honor their memory. Buried in the tomb are the seven firefighters and one civilian who fought the fire. Also buried are 60 other Ponce firemen that have since qualified to be buried there.<sup id="cite_ref-pride_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pride-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="The_structure">The structure</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monumento_a_los_h%C3%A9roes_de_El_Polvor%C3%ADn_(mausoleum)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The structure"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The mausoleum was built in 1911. This monument is different from the <a href="/wiki/Monumento_a_los_heroes_de_El_Polvor%C3%ADn_(obelisk)" class="mw-redirect" title="Monumento a los heroes de El Polvor\xc3\xadn (obelisk)">obelisk unveiled in 1948</a> and erected at <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Delicias" title="Plaza Las Delicias">Plaza Las Delicias</a>, which is also dedicated to the same group of men.<sup id="cite_ref-hacen_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hacen-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pride_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pride-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The obelisk and statue portions of this mausoleum were rebuilt in 2011, at the 100th anniversary of the original obelisk, statue and mausoleum (1911), and uncovered in time for its 101st anniversary, on 25 January 2012.  The monument had been rebuilt in the 1950s and was rebuilt again in 2011-2012. The 2011-2012 restoration was performed by <a href="/wiki/Plastic_arts" title="Plastic arts">plastic arts</a> artist Ram\xc3\xb3n Rivera C\xc3\xa1liz, director of the <i>Secretar\xc3\xada de Arte y Cultura</i> (Office of Arts and Culture) of the Municipality of Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-hacen_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hacen-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  For this and many other contributions in  the city of Ponce, on 12 December 2013, Rivera C\xc3\xa1liz was honored with a ceremony and added to the list of illustrious Ponce citizens at the <a href="/wiki/Tricentennial_Park_(Ponce,_Puerto_Rico)" class="mw-redirect" title="Tricentennial Park (Ponce, Puerto Rico)">Park of the Illustrious Ponce Citizens</a> in Ponce\'s <a href="/wiki/Tricentennial_Park_(Ponce,_Puerto_Rico)" class="mw-redirect" title="Tricentennial Park (Ponce, Puerto Rico)">Tricentennial Park</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The monument is flanked by a 4-foot high foundation and surrounded by 19 3-foot high columns connected by chain segments. The chain segments are adorned with metal stamps depicting Ponce firefighting equipment and the stamps have dates on them. These surrounding columns and chain segments have a total length of some 40 feet on each side and form a square that measures 40-feet x 40-feet. There is also an interior chain located closer to the obelisk itself, this one supported by four 3-foot high columns. Many of the dates are repeated. The dates mark significant events in the history of the Ponce Fire Corps.  The dates include, 2 February 1883, date on which the Ponce Fire Corps moved to what is today <a href="/wiki/Parque_de_Bombas" title="Parque de Bombas">Parque de Bombas Museum</a>.
