class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Antiguo Cuartel Militar Espa\xc3\xb1ol de Ponce</b> or <b>"El Castillo"</b> (English: <i>Old Spanish Military Headquarters in Ponce</i> or <i>"The Castle"</i>) is the only structure directly related to the events of the land defense of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_Campaign#The_land_campaign_in_Puerto_Rico_(July_25_-_August_13)" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican Campaign">1898 American invasion</a> of the Island.<sup id="cite_ref-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_2-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The historic building dates from 1894<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>a<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and is located on Calle Castillo in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">the city\'s historic district</a>. The structure was listed on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> on 14 May 1987.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antiguo_Cuartel_Militar_Espa%C3%B1ol_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>"<i>El Castillo</i>", as this building is commonly known, was built in 1894 by the Spanish Royal Corps of Engineers as general headquarters for the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Armed_Forces" title="Spanish Armed Forces">Spanish military</a> garrison stationed in Ponce. Since 1898, when Puerto Rico became a possession of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, this structure has served various purposes, first as <a href="/wiki/Courthouse" title="Courthouse">courthouse</a> and later as district <a href="/wiki/Jailhouse" class="mw-redirect" title="Jailhouse">jailhouse</a>. It was being used as a district jail house in 1987.<sup id="cite_ref-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_3_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_3-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  In the late 1980s, it ceased to be used as a district jail, was reconditioned, and in 1992 it was inaugurated as the headquarters of the Ponce School of Fine Arts, which is its role.\n</p><p>The building was designed by the Spanish Royal Corps of Engineers. The architecture consists of 19th Century Civil Architecture. It is one of the largest <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">Neoclassic</a> <a href="/wiki/Isabelline_Gothic" class="mw-redirect" title="Isabelline Gothic">Isabelino</a> civil architecture structures built in Ponce during the 19th century. Its sober exterior walls help express its intended original use. The building played a significant role in law enforcement during the last years of the <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> colonial rule for southern Puerto Rico and has played a significant role for both law enforcement and judicial purposes since then.\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a class="mw-redirect" title="Quinto (Ponce)">Quinto</a> in Ponce, Puerto Rico, the new tenant at "El Castillo".</figcaption></figure>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Significance">Significance</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antiguo_Cuartel_Militar_Espa%C3%B1ol_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Significance"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Spanish garrison stationed in Ponce at the time of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_Campaign" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican Campaign">arrival of the American troops in 1898</a> played a significant role in the defense of the island. While the Spanish commander in charge of the Ponce garrison had determined it was not possible to defend the city of Ponce from the American advance at the time, the Ponce troops, together with the aid of other smaller garrisons, held the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_Campaign#Battle_of_Asomante" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican Campaign">Asomante</a> area preventing the U.S. Army from invading <a href="/wiki/Aibonito" class="mw-redirect" title="Aibonito">Aibonito</a> in their advance to the conquest of fortified areas of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>. The Ponce garrison also helped the <a href="/wiki/Guayama" class="mw-redirect" title="Guayama">Guayama</a> garrison in its attempt to detain American troops from taking that city. "This is the only structure directly related to these [land] events that has been identified to date".<sup id="cite_ref-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_3_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_3-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Description">Description</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antiguo_Cuartel_Militar_Espa%C3%B1ol_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The building consists of a two level square structure with an interior <a href="/wiki/Courtyard" title="Courtyard">courtyard</a>. It is built in brick and stone masonry; timber beams and brick layers were used for the roof and floors. The interior courtyard is surrounded by open-arched galleries in both levels. During the time when the building was being used as a district jail in 1987, the galleries provided the foundation of the jail barracks.<sup id="cite_ref-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_2_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_2-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>A repetition of windows over the facade\'s plane without any ornament gives the building an austere appearance, typical of military architecture developed during the Spanish period in Puerto Rico. The building still preserves its original lead gutters in the form of <a href="/wiki/Cannon" title="Cannon">cannons</a>. The building has been altered little since its original construction.  The most significant alteration has been the replacement of the original wooden <a href="/wiki/Louver" title="Louver">louvers</a> windows with metal louvered windows. Also, iron bars have been added to its interior openings. The building has also been upgraded in two rather minimal ways: the interior <a href="/wiki/Courtyard" title="Courtyard">courtyard</a> was repaved and modern sanitary facilities were installed.<sup id="cite_ref-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_2_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mariano_G._Coronas_Castro_1987._Page_2-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="See_also">See also</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
