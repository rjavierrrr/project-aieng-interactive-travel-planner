scope="row" class="infobox-label" style="font-weight:bold; border: 0;">Designated&#160;RNSZH</th><td class="infobox-data" style="border: 0;">March 15, 2001</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Church of Saint Michael the Archangel of Utuado</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Iglesia San Miguel Arc\xc3\xa1ngel de Utuado</i>) is a historic <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Roman Catholic</a> parish church located in <a href="/wiki/Utuado_barrio-pueblo" title="Utuado barrio-pueblo">Utuado Pueblo</a>, the administrative and historic center of the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Utuado,_Puerto_Rico" title="Utuado, Puerto Rico">Utuado, Puerto Rico</a>. The church is listed in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Inventory_of_the_Historic_Churches_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Inventory of the Historic Churches of Puerto Rico (page does not exist)">Inventory of the Historic Churches of Puerto Rico</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Register_of_Historic_Sites_and_Zones" title="Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones">Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones</a> and the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">United States National Register of Historic Places</a> due to its architectural and historical significance.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:02_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:02-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The church was designed in 1866 by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Onofre_Llorapart&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Onofre Llorapart (page does not exist)">Onofre Llorapart</a> and built in stages between 1872 and 1878 in the main town square (<i>plaza p\xc3\xbablica</i>) of Utuado, on its east-facing block that it currently shares with the parish house (<i>casa parroquial</i>) and its adjacent Catholic school. This urban setting is highly traditional of the historic downtowns (<a href="/wiki/Pueblos_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Pueblos in Puerto Rico">pueblos</a>) of Puerto Rico. The exterior and fa\xc3\xa7ade of the building showcase the traditional <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Colonial_architecture" title="Spanish Colonial architecture">Spanish Colonial architecture</a> of the 19th-century contemporary churches of Puerto Rico with elements inspired in <a href="/wiki/Renaissance_architecture" title="Renaissance architecture">Renaissance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">Neoclassical architecture</a>. With the exception of routine renovations and the removal of the Renaissance-style side pillars and arcades of the church in 1965, the building is well preserved and has never suffered from integrity-endangering modifications.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span
