municipality of Caguas dates to 1856-1860, although it did not officially become the municipal headquarters until 1887. The building is well-maintained and still preserves its original <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">Neoclassical</a> fa\xc3\xa7ade.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The building was added to the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> as <b>Alcaldia de Caguas</b> in March 22, 1989.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This building hosted municipal offices until 2010 when the new city hall building was inaugurated. The building now houses several cultural institutions including a <a href="/wiki/Caguas_Museum_of_History" title="Caguas Museum of History">museum</a> dedicated to the <a href="/wiki/History_of_Caguas,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="History of Caguas, Puerto Rico">history of Caguas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpdoc_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpdoc-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="The_new_city_hall">The new city hall</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caguas_City_Hall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The new city hall"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:171016-F-FH950-0052_(37544309370).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/171016-F-FH950-0052_%2837544309370%29.jpg/220px-171016-F-FH950-0052_%2837544309370%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/171016-F-FH950-0052_%2837544309370%29.jpg/330px-171016-F-FH950-0052_%2837544309370%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/171016-F-FH950-0052_%2837544309370%29.jpg/440px-171016-F-FH950-0052_%2837544309370%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3000" data-file-height="1997" /></a><figcaption>The new city hall building in 2017.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The building of a new city hall was part of a project to develop and build more modern office and administrative spaces in the city. Some people, however, have criticized the continuous use of <a href="/wiki/Brutalist_architecture" title="Brutalist architecture">Brutalist architecture</a> in new government buildings. The new city hall building, nicknamed "El Cacique Mayor" (Spanish for \'<i>the major <a href="/wiki/Cacique" title="Cacique">cacique</a></i>\'), was inaugurated in September 2010 by Caguas mayor <a
