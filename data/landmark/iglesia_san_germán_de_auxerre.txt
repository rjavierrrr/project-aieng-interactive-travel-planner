0;">December 10, 1984</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Iglesia San Germ\xc3\xa1n de Auxerre</b> (English: <span lang="en">Church of Saint Germanus of Auxerre</span>) is a historic <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> parish church located in <a href="/wiki/San_Germ%C3%A1n,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Germ\xc3\xa1n, Puerto Rico">San Germ\xc3\xa1n, Puerto Rico</a>, overlooking the main <a href="/wiki/Plaza" class="mw-redirect" title="Plaza">plaza</a> of the town. <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish</a> settlers founded San Germ\xc3\xa1n <a href="/wiki/Parish" title="Parish">parish</a> in 1510 and built the first permanent church in 1688. The church was repaired and reconstructed between 1717 and 1739 after damages caused by numerous <a href="/wiki/Earthquake" title="Earthquake">earthquakes</a> during the 18th century. Between 1834 and 1897, new repairs were made to the building, and in 1920, the tower was rebuilt after the <a href="/wiki/1918_Puerto_Rico_earthquake" class="mw-redirect" title="1918 Puerto Rico earthquake">1918 earthquake</a>. With <i><a href="/wiki/Trompe-l%27%C5%93il" title="Trompe-l&#39;\xc5\x93il">trompe-l\'\xc5\x93il</a></i> painting that imitates wood coffers on the ceiling, the Church of San Germ\xc3\xa1n\'s interior is one of the most lavishly decorated on the island.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The vault and arches are painted in the <i>trompe-l\'\xc5\x93il</i> manner. The church conserves the 1869 marble altar as well as ten other 19th century smaller secondary marble altars. A collection of 17th century metalwork, 18th century wooden carvings and a painting by <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Campeche" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Campeche">Jos\xc3\xa9 Campeche</a> are kept in the choir loft. It was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1984.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading
