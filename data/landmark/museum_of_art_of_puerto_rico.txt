href="http://www.mapr.org">mapr.org</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Museum of Art of Puerto Rico</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Museo de Arte de Puerto Rico</i>, abbreviated <b>MAPR</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>) is an art museum in <a href="/wiki/Santurce,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="Santurce, San Juan, Puerto Rico">Santurce</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico">barrio</a> of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>, with 18 exhibition halls.<sup id="cite_ref-Peffer_Lonely_Planet_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Peffer_Lonely_Planet-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The museum is located in a historic building, formerly occupied by the San Juan Municipal Hospital.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museum_of_Art_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The museum building, designed by architect William H. Shimmelphening and built in 1920, is the remaining building in the former ruined complex of the San Juan Municipal Hospital. The building served as a hospital until 1966 when most of its body was moved to the newly inaugurated <i>Centro M\xc3\xa9dico de R\xc3\xado Piedras</i> (R\xc3\xado Piedras Medical Center). Afterwards it served as office space for the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Transportation_and_Public_Works" title="Puerto Rico Department of Transportation and Public Works">Puerto Rico Department of Transportation and Public Works</a> until 1975.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The idea behind the Puerto Rico Museum of Art dates to 1995, when the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Tourism_Company" title="Puerto Rico Tourism Company">Puerto Rico Tourism Company</a> (Spanish: <i>Compa\xc3\xb1\xc3\xada de Turismo de Puerto Rico</i>) with funding by the Government Development Bank for Puerto Rico (<i>Banco Gubernamental de Fomento</i>, BGF). Instead of demolishing the building, the old structure was incorporated into the new museum building.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Permanent_collection">Permanent collection</h2><span collection"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div><p>\nThe permanent collection of the Museum of Art of Puerto Rico is divided into 24 galleries located on the
