(2019\xe2\x80\x93)</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Juan Ram\xc3\xb3n Loubriel Stadium</b> (Spanish: <i>Estadio Juan Ram\xc3\xb3n Loubriel</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Soccer-specific_stadium" title="Soccer-specific stadium">soccer-specific stadium</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Bayam%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico" title="Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico">Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico</a>. It is best known as the former home of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Islanders" title="Puerto Rico Islanders">Puerto Rico Islanders</a> of the <a href="/wiki/North_American_Soccer_League_(2011%E2%80%932017)" title="North American Soccer League (2011\xe2\x80\x932017)">North American Soccer League</a> and current home of the <a href="/wiki/Bayam%C3%B3n_FC" class="mw-redirect" title="Bayam\xc3\xb3n FC">Bayam\xc3\xb3n FC</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Liga_Puerto_Rico" title="Liga Puerto Rico">Liga Puerto Rico</a>. The stadium can seat up to 12,500 people. It has easy access the metro station known as "Deportivo Station."\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Ram%C3%B3n_Loubriel_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Built in 1973 as a <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> stadium with a capacity of 12,500, it was home to the <a href="/wiki/Vaqueros_de_Bayam%C3%B3n_(baseball)" title="Vaqueros de Bayam\xc3\xb3n (baseball)">Vaqueros de Bayam\xc3\xb3n</a> until 2003 when the team became defunct.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="La_Meca_of_Puerto_Rican_Football">La Meca of Puerto Rican Football</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Ram%C3%B3n_Loubriel_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: La Meca of Puerto Rican Football"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 2003 with the Vaqueros folding, the stadium seemed doomed to be relegated to a youth stadium or to demolition, but late that same year the stadium became home of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Islanders" title="Puerto Rico Islanders">Puerto Rico Islanders</a>. Its "<i>L</i>" shaped stands were not suited for the sport and it originally had an awkward feel to it since the stand behind the goal angles away from the pitch.\n</p><p>Another name that has been used for the stadium is "La Islandera" since it was the home of the Islanders.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>It was home to <a href="/wiki/Bayamon_FC" class="mw-redirect" title="Bayamon FC">Bayamon FC</a>, a professional football team in the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Soccer_League" title="Puerto Rico Soccer League">Puerto Rico Soccer League</a> now playing in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayamon_Soccer_Complex&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bayamon Soccer Complex (page does not exist)">Bayamon Soccer Complex</a>, and Sevilla Bayamon FC, now called <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC_Puerto_Rico" title="Sevilla FC Puerto Rico">Sevilla-FC Juncos</a>; they moved to <a href="/wiki/Juncos,_Puerto_Rico" title="Juncos, Puerto Rico">Juncos</a>.\n</p><p>It has also played host to the <a href="/wiki/CFU_Club_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="CFU Club Championship">CFU Club Championship</a> Group C in 2006, where the Islanders played but lost to <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Trinidad and Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</a>, <a href="/wiki/W_Connection" class="mw-redirect" title="W Connection">W Connection</a> 0-1 and the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_MLS-USL_Challenge" title="Puerto Rico MLS-USL Challenge">Puerto Rico MLS-USL Challenge</a> in 2007, and two <a href="/wiki/2010_World_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 World Cup">2010 World Cup</a> qualifying matches against the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic_national_football_team" title="Dominican Republic national football team">Dominican Republic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Honduras_national_football_team" title="Honduras national football team">Honduras</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="Renovation_in_2012">Renovation in 2012</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Ram%C3%B3n_Loubriel_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Renovation in 2012"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The renovations to the stadium have been made to upgrade a stadium created for baseball to be soccer-specific. The stadium will also have new dressing rooms and bathrooms and the replacement of some seating. There will also be some temporary bleachers until the addition for new seating is completed later. Lighting has also been adjusted to be more uniform across the field.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The remodeling of the stadium will be done in several stages, with a total cost of $7 million.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a (2017)</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Ram%C3%B3n_Loubriel_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: be using the stadium for its home matches of the <a href="/wiki/Liga_Puerto_Rico" title="Liga Puerto Rico">Liga Puerto Rico</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2
