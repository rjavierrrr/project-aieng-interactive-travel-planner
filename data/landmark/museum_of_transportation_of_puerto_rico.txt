class="infobox-label">Website</th><td class="infobox-data"><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://motpr.com/">http://motpr.com/</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Museum Of Transportation (MOT) of Puerto Rico</b> is a museum located in the town of <a href="/wiki/Guaynabo" class="mw-redirect" title="Guaynabo">Guaynabo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> which documents the history of transportation in Puerto Rico, from the <a href="/wiki/Canoes" class="mw-redirect" title="Canoes">canoes</a> used by the natives, to the actual <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">subway</a> system located in the metro area known as the "<a href="/wiki/Tren_Urbano" title="Tren Urbano">Tren Urbano</a>" (Urban Train).\n</p><p>Founded in 2013, the museum began as an initiative by a group of local automotive enthusiasts. Consisting of an architecturally rich design inspired by other great and renowned museums from around the globe. The building consists on three floors and over 35,000 s/f housing exhibitions from original <a href="/wiki/Horse-drawn_carriage" class="mw-redirect" title="Horse-drawn carriage">horse-drawn carriages</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ox_wagon" class="mw-redirect" title="Ox wagon">ox wagon</a>, to cars from the late 1950s to the present. The MOT includes historic motoring photographs, decorative walls, vintage <a href="/wiki/Neon_sign" title="Neon sign">neon signs</a>, antique cars, <a href="/wiki/Bicycle" title="Bicycle">bicycles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle" title="Motorcycle">motorcycles</a>.\n</p><p>In April 2019, the mayor announced the Museum would be closing its doors.<sup id="cite_ref-El_Nuevo_Dia_2019_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-El_Nuevo_Dia_2019-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Exhibits">Exhibits</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museum_of_Transportation_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Exhibits"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The first floor of the MOT hosts a permanent exhibition documenting the history of transportation in Puerto Rico including a horse carriage and 1900s ox wagon, a miniature community mock-up with a functional railroad, maritime and aviation memorabilia, and a <a href="/wiki/Flight_simulator" title="Flight simulator">flight simulator</a>.\n</p><p>The second floor hosts an automotive exhibition consisting on various themes for a total of four exhibitions per year. Some of the past themes have been: <a href="/wiki/Chevrolet_Corvette" title="Chevrolet Corvette">Corvette</a> 60th Anniversary, a <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen" title="Volkswagen">Volkswagen</a> exhibition, British cars exhibition, <a href="/wiki/Ford_Mustang" title="Ford Mustang">Ford Mustang</a> exhibition and more. The second floor is also home to the MOT\'s race car simulator as well as racing memorabilia from renowned drivers such as <a href="/w/index.php?title=Diego_Febles&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Diego Febles (page does not exist)">Diego Febles</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chiqui_Soldevilla&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Chiqui Soldevilla (page does not exist)">Chiqui Soldevilla</a>. Among the racing memorabilia is Febles\' 1978 1st-place trophy from the <a href="/wiki/1978_24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="1978 24 Hours of Le Mans">24 Hours of Daytona</a>, his 1982 1st-place trophy for the <a href="/wiki/12_Hours_of_Sebring" title="12 Hours of Sebring">12 Hours of Sebring</a> as well as other trophies, original race suits, pictures, helmets and other memorabilia.\n</p><p>The third floor consists of a lounge cafe and a conference room available for rent and fully equipped for seminars and various other activities.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2
