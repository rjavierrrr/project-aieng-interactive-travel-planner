2000</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>General Norzagaray Bridge</b> is a brick and masonry <a href="/wiki/Barrel_vault" title="Barrel vault">barrel vault</a> bridge built in 1855 in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>.  Also known as <b>Puente de los Frailes</b>, it brings what is now <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_873" title="Puerto Rico Highway 873">Puerto Rico Highway 873</a> across <a href="/w/index.php?title=Frailes_Creek&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Frailes Creek (page does not exist)">Frailes Creek</a>, a tributary to the <a href="/wiki/Guaynabo_River" title="Guaynabo River">Guaynabo River</a>.  It has eight 9.8-metre (32&#160;ft) barrel vault spans.  Its total length is 120.7 metres (396&#160;ft) and its roadway width is 7.00 metres (22.97&#160;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpnom_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpnom-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> (NRHP) in 1995,<sup id="cite_ref-nris_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and on the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Register_of_Historic_Sites_and_Zones" title="Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones">Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones</a> in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1855, it was important for carrying the <a href="/wiki/Carretera_Central_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Carretera Central (Puerto Rico)">Carretara Central</a> across Quebrada Frailes.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpnom_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpnom-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>It is named for <a href="/wiki/Fern%C3%A1ndo_de_Norzagaray_y_Escudero" class="mw-redirect" title="Fern\xc3\xa1ndo de Norzagaray y Escudero">Fern\xc3\xa1ndo de Norzagaray y Escudero</a>, <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Governor of Puerto Rico">governor of Puerto Rico</a> from 1852 to 1855.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpnom_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpnom-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>It was designed by chief engineer <a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Sanchez-Nunez_y_Layne&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Manuel Sanchez-Nunez y Layne (page does not exist)">Manuel Sanchez-Nunez y Layne</a> and built by engineer <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gustavo_Steinacher&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gustavo Steinacher (page does not exist)">Gustavo Steinacher</a> (who also designed and built the first suspension bridge in the Caribbean, over the <a href="/wiki/Cag%C3%BCitas_River" title="Cag\xc3\xbcitas River">Cag\xc3\xbcitas River</a> just north of <a href="/wiki/Caguas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Caguas, Puerto Rico">Caguas, Puerto Rico</a><sup id="cite_ref-mps_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mps-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>) and cost 45,346 <a href="/wiki/Pesos" class="mw-redirect" title="Pesos">pesos</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpnom_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpnom-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>According to its NRHP registration, it "is one of the most impressive bridges from the Spanish Colonial period", serving as a "prime example of Spanish 19th century masonry arch technology".<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpnom_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpnom-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is the only such example in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpnom_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpnom-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The superstructure of the bridge was modified in 1927 by removal of earth fill and addition of steel girders installed on the arch piers to support a concrete deck.  This does not interfere significantly with the historic integrity of the bridge.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpnom_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpnom-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>It spans between what are now the <a href="/wiki/Barrio" title="Barrio">barrios</a> of <a href="/wiki/Caimito,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="Caimito, San Juan, Puerto Rico">Caimito</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tortugo,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="Tortugo, San Juan, Puerto Rico">Tortugo</a> in San Juan.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Gallery">Gallery</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
