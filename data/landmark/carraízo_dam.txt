href="http://www.salonhogar.com/ciencias/eltiempo/carraizo.html">Carra\xc3\xadzo</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Carra\xc3\xadzo Dam</b> is a concrete <a href="/wiki/Gravity_dam" title="Gravity dam">gravity dam</a> located in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Trujillo_Alto,_Puerto_Rico" title="Trujillo Alto, Puerto Rico">Trujillo Alto, Puerto Rico</a> within the flow of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Lo%C3%ADza" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza">R\xc3\xado Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza</a>. Its construction was completed in 1953. Carra\xc3\xadzo Dam impounds <a href="/wiki/Lo%C3%ADza_Lake" title="Lo\xc3\xadza Lake">Lo\xc3\xadza Lake</a> which serves as Puerto Rico\'s main <a href="/wiki/Water_reservoir" class="mw-redirect" title="Water reservoir">water reservoir</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Background_and_construction">Background and construction</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carra%C3%ADzo_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and construction"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>At the end of the 1940s, the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Aqueducts_and_Sewers_Authority" title="Puerto Rico Aqueducts and Sewers Authority">Puerto Rico Aqueducts and Sewers Authority</a> (PRASA) proposed the construction of a dam at the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Lo%C3%ADza" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza">R\xc3\xado Grande de Lo\xc3\xadza</a> in the Barrio <a href="/wiki/Carra%C3%ADzo" title="Carra\xc3\xadzo">Carra\xc3\xadzo</a> of <a href="/wiki/Trujillo_Alto,_Puerto_Rico" title="Trujillo Alto, Puerto Rico">Trujillo Alto</a> with the intention of providing both water and electricity to the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Metropolitan_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan Metropolitan Area">San Juan Metropolitan Area</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Construction of the dam began in 1950 and finished in 1954. As part of the construction, the dam included three <a href="/wiki/Hydroelectricity" title="Hydroelectricity">hydroelectric turbines</a> with a total capacity of 3 <a href="/wiki/Megawatt" class="mw-redirect" title="Megawatt">Megawatts</a> for the provision of electricity. However, during the 1960s and 1970s, the turbines were shut down because their operation and maintenance wasn\'t considered cost-effective versus the cost of buying energy at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-osl_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-osl-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1989 <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hugo" title="Hurricane Hugo">Hurricane Hugo</a> caused overtopping of the dam, thereby flooding the hydroelectric facility and rendering it inoperable. It stayed closed until 2013, when PRASA elected to renovate the facility.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:FEMA_-_PR_DR4339_1405_-_Community_flooded.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/FEMA_-_PR_DR4339_1405_-_Community_flooded.jpg/220px-FEMA_-_PR_DR4339_1405_-_Community_flooded.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="140" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/FEMA_-_PR_DR4339_1405_-_Community_flooded.jpg/330px-FEMA_-_PR_DR4339_1405_-_Community_flooded.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/FEMA_-_PR_DR4339_1405_-_Community_flooded.jpg/440px-FEMA_-_PR_DR4339_1405_-_Community_flooded.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3300" data-file-height="2100" /></a><figcaption>The Mi\xc3\xb1i Mi\xc3\xb1i Community was flooded when the Carra\xc3\xadzo Dam spilled its water during Hurricane Mar\xc3\xada in September, 2017.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>In 2017 <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> caused overtopping and flooding to nearby areas.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Operation">Operation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carra%C3%ADzo_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Carra\xc3\xadzo dam is a concrete <a href="/wiki/Dam#Gravity_dams" title="Dam">gravity dam</a>. It features eight <a href="/wiki/Floodgate" title="Floodgate">floodgates</a> along its 210-meter body. The floodgates open at a height of 1 to 10 meters, but they are usually opened at 4 meters. The reservoir created by the Carra\xc3\xadzo Dam is the <a href="/wiki/Lo%C3%ADza_Lake" title="Lo\xc3\xadza Lake">Lo\xc3\xadza Lake</a>. Carra\xc3\xadzo\'s water storage capacity is of 4,650,000 gallons, and 41.15 meters is its peak level.<sup id="cite_ref-osl_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-osl-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="See_also">See also</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
