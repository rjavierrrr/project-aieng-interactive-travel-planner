It opened in 1982 and is anchored by <a href="/wiki/Marshalls" title="Marshalls">Marshalls Megastore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Cinemas" title="Caribbean Cinemas">Caribbean Cinemas</a>, <a href="/wiki/TJ_Maxx" title="TJ Maxx">TJ Maxx</a>, <a href="/wiki/Best_Buy" title="Best Buy">Best Buy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Golden_Corral" title="Golden Corral">Golden Corral</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pueblo_Supermarkets" title="Pueblo Supermarkets">Pueblo Supermarkets</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chili%27s_Grill_%26_Bar" class="mw-redirect" title="Chili&#39;s Grill &amp; Bar">Chili\'s Grill &amp; Bar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Walgreens" title="Walgreens">Walgreens</a>, and <a href="/wiki/PetSmart" title="PetSmart">PetSmart</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Rio_Hondo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Opening_and_success:_1980s">Opening and success: 1980s</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Rio_Hondo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Opening and success: 1980s"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The 350,000 square foot Plaza R\xc3\xado Hondo at the time was first owned and developed by the firm Wilder-Manley Associates, and represented an investment of $25 million. The mall was also managed by the firm Wilder-Manley of Puerto Rico, Inc., a subsidiary of WMA.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On March 31, 1982, the first group of stores at the mall first opened, of which included <a href="/wiki/Pueblo_Supermarkets" title="Pueblo Supermarkets">Pueblo Supermarkets</a>.  The second few group of stores opened at the end of July of that year. This included <a href="/wiki/Barker%27s_Discount_Department_Stores" title="Barker&#39;s Discount Department Stores">Barker\'s</a>, <a href="/wiki/F._W._Woolworth_Company" title="F. W. Woolworth Company">Woolworth</a>, La Giralda, and many more.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 1982, <a href="/wiki/Barker%27s_Discount_Department_Stores" title="Barker&#39;s Discount Department Stores">Barker\'s</a> one of the stores anchoring the mall inaugurated being the chains 16th store on the island.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Barker%E2%80%99s_store_at_Plaza_R%C3%ADo_Hondo.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Barker%E2%80%99s_store_at_Plaza_R%C3%ADo_Hondo.jpg/220px-Barker%E2%80%99s_store_at_Plaza_R%C3%ADo_Hondo.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="117" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Barker%E2%80%99s_store_at_Plaza_R%C3%ADo_Hondo.jpg/330px-Barker%E2%80%99s_store_at_Plaza_R%C3%ADo_Hondo.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Barker%E2%80%99s_store_at_Plaza_R%C3%ADo_Hondo.jpg/440px-Barker%E2%80%99s_store_at_Plaza_R%C3%ADo_Hondo.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1897" data-file-height="1011" /></a><figcaption>Barker\'s store during inauguration</figcaption></figure>\n<p>In November 1982, the mall had inauguration events including things such as fashion shows, exhibitions, and music. These events took place starting on the 5th of that month and ending in December of that year.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The mall opened with 70 stores of which included <a href="/wiki/Barker%27s_Discount_Department_Stores" title="Barker&#39;s Discount Department Stores">Barker\'s</a>, <a href="/wiki/F._W._Woolworth_Company" title="F. W. Woolworth Company">Woolworth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pueblo_Supermarkets" title="Pueblo Supermarkets">Pueblo Supermarket</a>, <a href="/wiki/Walgreens" title="Walgreens">Walgreens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kinney_Shoes" title="Kinney Shoes">Kinney Shoes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gordon%27s_Jewelers" class="mw-redirect" title="Gordon&#39;s Jewelers">Gordon\'s</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marianne" title="Marianne">Marianne</a>, La Favorita, Centro Disco, <a href="/wiki/Thom_McAn" title="Thom McAn">Thom McAn</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Hallmark_Cards" title="Hallmark Cards">Hallmark</a>, Almacenes Rodr\xc3\xadguez, Flagg Shoes, Madison, Preciosa, Butler Shoes, Lerner\'s, Almacenes Karlo, <a href="/wiki/RadioShack" title="RadioShack">RadioShack</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edison_Brothers_Stores" title="Edison Brothers Stores">Baker Shoes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zale_Corporation" title="Zale Corporation">Zales</a>, Rahola, Tiffany Bakery, Land of Oz, Lee Borinquen, Taco Paco, <a href="/wiki/Burger_King" title="Burger King">Burger King</a>, <a href="/wiki/Golden_Skillet" title="Golden Skillet">Golden Skillet</a>, Almacenes Gonz\xc3\xa1lez, La Giralda, and many more. It also featured on opening free parking for over 2,000 cars.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On October 20, 1983, after the acquiring of 5 <a href="/wiki/Barker%27s_Discount_Department_Stores" title="Barker&#39;s Discount Department Stores">Barker\xe2\x80\x99s</a> stores on the island by the <a href="/wiki/Kmart" title="Kmart">Kmart Corporation</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> a new Kmart store would inaugurate at Plaza R\xc3\xado Hondo in the former Barker\xe2\x80\x99s space.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On March 26, 1985, a <a href="/wiki/Ponderosa_and_Bonanza_Steakhouses" title="Ponderosa and Bonanza Steakhouses">Ponderosa Steakhouse</a> opened at the mall.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On July 3, 1986, the Rio Hondo Cinemas a 6-screen cinema opened owned by the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Cinemas" title="Caribbean Cinemas">Caribbean Cinemas</a> chain. It opened with premieres such as <a href="/wiki/Cobra_(1986_film)" title="Cobra (1986 film)">Stallone Cobra</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Karate_Kid_Part_II" title="The Karate Kid Part II">The Karate Kid Part II</a>, <a href="/wiki/Off_Beat_(1986_film)" title="Off Beat (1986 film)">Off Beat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poltergeist_II:_The_Other_Side" title="Poltergeist II: The Other Side">Poltergeist II: The Other Side</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Psycho_III" title="Psycho III">Psycho III</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Renovations,_expansions,_and_success:_1990s-2000s"><span id="Renovations.2C_expansions.2C_and_success:_1990s-2000s"></span>Renovations, expansions, and success: 1990s-2000s</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Rio_Hondo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Renovations, expansions, and success: 1990s-2000s"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In November 1994, it was reported that the then 394,400 square foot mall would remodel and expand under the ownership of Manley Berenson.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Originally, Plaza R\xc3\xado Hondo had a "large box" look. The owner characterized the mall as looking like a factory. The goal of this renovation was, to quote the owner, "to break out of the box." The new look which brought forth the remodeling of both the exterior and interior of the mall, consisted of a river theme throughout the whole mall featuring bright, tropical colors, suggesting the tropical landscape and seascape of Puerto Rico. This encouraged the tenants to renovate their own spaces. The center was re-merchandised to meet the needs of all kinds of consumer budgets. Among the new stores were Bostonian, <a href="/wiki/Payless_Shoe_Source" class="mw-redirect" title="Payless Shoe Source">Payless Shoe Source</a>, a bookstore, a <a href="/wiki/GNC_(company)" title="GNC (company)">GNC</a> Nutrition store, an upscale men\'s clothing store, and kiosks for cellular telephones, candy and jewelry. Plaza R\xc3\xado Hondo remained open for business during its fast-track construction schedule, which allowed the renovation to be substantially complete for the 1994 Christmas season. These renovations would be fully complete by February 1995. By then the 425,000 square foot mall, would be anchored by a 92,000 square foot <a href="/wiki/Kmart" title="Kmart">Kmart</a>, a 36,000 square foot <a href="/wiki/F._W._Woolworth_Company" title="F. W. Woolworth Company">Woolworth</a>, a 19,700 square foot La Giralda, and a 9,000 square foot <a href="/wiki/Walgreens" title="Walgreens">Walgreens</a> as a junior anchor.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1996, the International Council of Shopping Centers awarded Plaza R\xc3\xado Hondo with the "International Design and Development Award" for its layout and style, this was given to the mall after the major renovations it had in 1994 into 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1997, the <a href="/wiki/F._W._Woolworth_Company" title="F. W. Woolworth Company">Woolworth</a> store at the mall closed after the chain went under that year.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In late 1998, management Yebba Realty Ventures at the time purchased the lease from <a href="/wiki/F._W._Woolworth_Company" title="F. W. Woolworth Company">F.W. Woolworth Company</a> of their 36,680 square foot store at the mall for $800,000 with the intention of expanding the premises to accommodate the addition of two new junior anchor stores <a href="/wiki/Marshalls" title="Marshalls">Marshalls</a> and Tiendas Capri.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In April 1998, management at the time Yebba Realty Ventures believed that Plaza R\xc3\xado Hondo required additional food services within the mall. Shortly after the acquisition a long-time tenant, with considerable lease term remaining and a store occupying some 20,000 square feet at the center of the mall began to experience financial difficulties, management immediately entered into negotiations to purchase the lease, which they were able to do for $600,000. A 12-unit food court plus 2 outside facing retail bays were complete at a total cost of approximately $3,645,000. The first year net income generated from the food court was approximately $1,112,280. The total cost of the lease buy-out and food court construction was approximately $4,245,000 thereby equating to a yield on investment of 26.2%. Again applying a 10% cap rate to the net operating income stream, Management created additional net value of $6,877,800. Beyond that, the food court increased the business at the mall.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1999, after the redevelopment of the former Woolworth store, both Marshalls and Capri would inaugurate that year. Marshalls, having the highest grossing opening day in the history of the chain on a sales per square foot basis at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2001, Yebba Realty Ventures sold the mall to Caribbean Property Group (CPG) for a purchase price of $16,683,080 million dollars.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>\nOn June 17, 2004, it was reported that Plaza R\xc3\xado Hondo would soon become home to a <a href="/wiki/Marshalls" title="Marshalls">Marshalls MegaStore</a> (featuring <a href="/wiki/HomeGoods" title="HomeGoods">HomeGoods</a> inside Marshalls store) and <a href="/wiki/CompUSA" title="CompUSA">CompUSA\'s</a> flagship store in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, said Martha Hermilla, the mall\'s marketing manager. "This year, we are adding a 55,000 square foot space for a Marshalls Mega Store. The space currently occupied by a regular Marshalls will be occupied by CompUSA, which intends this to be its flagship store on the island,\xe2\x80\x9d said Hermilla. Apparel &amp; domestics retailer Marshalls\' megastore would be a freestanding building on an outparcel beside the <a href="/wiki/Pueblo_Supermarkets" title="Pueblo Supermarkets">Pueblo supermarket</a>. Occupying 33,000 square feet, the regular Marshalls at Plaza Rio Hondo would close. Computer retailer CompUSA\'s store at Plaza Rio Hondo would be the company\'s second on the island. The first, occupying 22,000 square feet, was in <a href="/wiki/Guaynabo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guaynabo, Puerto Rico">Guaynabo\'s</a> Plaza Caparra. Also planned to kick off at Plaza Rio Hondo that year was an expansion of the mall by some 20,000 square feet and the refurbishment of its interiors. "It is going to be a major renovation," said Hermilla. Although she said all construction was to begin in those following few months, she couldn\'t say when the projects would be completed. Plaza Rio Hondo, at the time occupied 461,989 square feet and had more than 90 stores. The mall\'s sales per square foot averaged $450; the food court generated per square foot sales averaging of $770. Including Plaza Rio Hondo, PMI Inc. managed 17 properties in Puerto Rico with more than 28,000 parking spaces, more than 5.3 million square feet of gross leasable space, and more than 800 merchants.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2017, <a href="/wiki/DDR_Corp." had after the <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a>, making <a href="/wiki/SITE_Centers" title="SITE Centers">Retail Value Inc.</a> the new owner of the mall at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On November 6, 2019, it was the owner of the mall once again.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On May 10, 2023, it was reported that Curzon Puerto Rico, an affiliate of Curzon Advisers, was now the owner of one of the largest shopping center portfolios in Puerto Rico, formerly owned by Developers Diversified (<a href="/wiki/DDR_Corp." class="mw-redirect" title="DDR Corp.">DDR Corp.</a>) which had acquired it again in 2021. In this portfolio of 9 shopping centers, Plaza R\xc3\xado Hondo was included, making Curzon Puerto Rico the new owners of the mall.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On January 29, 2024, it was reported that the Aliss department store, located at the mall had begun the process of closing its operations, which would end on that January 31.
