This training facility was named posthumously after <a href="/wiki/Medal_of_Honor" title="Medal of Honor">Medal of Honor</a> recipient and native of Salinas, Puerto Rico, <a href="/wiki/Specialist_(rank)" title="Specialist (rank)">Specialist</a> Four <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9ctor_Santiago-Col%C3%B3n" title="H\xc3\xa9ctor Santiago-Col\xc3\xb3n">H\xc3\xa9ctor Santiago-Col\xc3\xb3n</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Campamento_Santiago&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a 2x" data-file-width="2786" data-file-height="2266" /></a><figcaption>Soldiers of the 65th Infantry chow during a day of training in Salinas, Puerto Rico. August 1941.</figcaption></figure><p> In 1940, the <a href="/wiki/Federal_government_of_the_United_States" title="Federal government of the United States">Federal government of the United States</a> leased this military camp, then known as Camp Salinas, from the Government of Puerto Rico. In 1967, the U.S. Government licensed the camp to the Puerto Rico National Guard. Since that time the camp has grown from a tent city with very few permanent facilities to an installation of more than 300 buildings of approximately 66,489 square metres (715,680&#160;sq&#160;ft).\n</p><p>In 1941 the <a href="/wiki/65th_Infantry_Regiment" class="mw-redirect" title="65th Infantry Regiment">65th Infantry Regiment</a> 3rd Battalion was the first combat unit to train at this Southern Puerto Rico military camp before being sent to <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a> to protect the <a href="/wiki/Panama_Canal" title="Panama Canal">Panama Canal</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.\n</p><p>In 1966 Col. <a href="/wiki/Alberto_A._Nido" title="Alberto A. Nido">Alberto A. Nido</a> requested the construction of a range, the <a href="/wiki/National_Guard_Bureau" title="National Guard Bureau">National Guard Bureau</a> approved the construction at the cost of $10,000. Captain Gabriel I. Pe\xc3\xb1agar\xc3\xadcano was assigned project officer and the U.S. Army Antilles Command caretakers of the camp, provided earth-moving equipment and personnel. In 1974, Campamento Santiago\'s air strip was renamed after administrative official Manuel Collazo.<sup id="cite_ref-Norat429_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Norat429-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The camp was named after Fourth Class Specialist H\xc3\xa9ctor Santiago Col\xc3\xb3n from Salinas, Puerto Rico, a posthumous Medal of Honor recipient.<sup id="cite_ref-Norat415_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Norat415-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The soldier died at the <a href="/wiki/Quang_Tri_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Quang Tri Province">Quang Tri Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Vietnam" title="South Vietnam">South Vietnam</a>  on June 28, 1968, while serving for <a href="/wiki/1st_Cavalry_Division_(United_States)" title="1st Cavalry Division (United States)">1st Cavalry Division</a>, <a href="/wiki/7th_Cavalry_Regiment" title="7th Cavalry Regiment">7th Cavalry</a>, Company B, 5th Battalion, and became famous for trying to shield his comrades from a grenade with his own body.<sup id="cite_ref-Norat415_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Norat415-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The installation used for training of the Puerto Rico National Guard was formally named "Campamento Santiago" pursuant to an order issued by the General Quarters of the United States Army on July 1, 1975.<sup id="cite_ref-Norat415_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Norat415-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This marked the second instance that a military installation in Puerto Rico was renamed after a local soldier after Campamento Garc\xc3\xada in Vieques was named after Fernando Garc\xc3\xada.<sup id="cite_ref-Norat415_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Norat415-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Major General <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Fernando_Chard%C3%B3n" title="Carlos Fernando Chard\xc3\xb3n">Carlos Chard\xc3\xb3n</a> was actively involved in the process to propose the recognition.<sup id="cite_ref-Norat416_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Norat416-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Cal. 45 shooting range at Campanento Santiago was named after Col. Eduardo Andino, a distinguished pistol and rifle shooter that won a U.S. Army National Pistol Championship in 1922.<sup id="cite_ref-Norat364_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Norat364-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>From 1990 to 1991, military units in Puerto Rico such as the <a href="/wiki/92nd_Maneuver_Enhancement_Brigade" class="mw-redirect" title="92nd Maneuver Enhancement Brigade">92nd Maneuver Enhancement Brigade</a> reported to Camp Santiago for initial training in preparation before deployment to <a href="/wiki/Operation_Desert_Storm" class="mw-redirect" title="Operation Desert Storm">Operation Desert Storm</a> in Kuwait. Camp Santiago also had a role in training National Guard and Reserve soldiers in Puerto Rico for <a href="/wiki/Operation_Enduring_Freedom" title="Operation Enduring Freedom">Operation Enduring Freedom</a> during the <a href="/wiki/War_in_Afghanistan_(2001%E2%80%932021)" title="War in Afghanistan (2001\xe2\x80\x932021)">War in Afghanistan (2001\xe2\x80\x932021)</a>. In 2006 more soldiers went back to Camp Santiago to prepare for the <a href="/wiki/Iraq_War" title="Iraq War">Iraq War</a> during <a href="/wiki/Operation_Desert_Shield_(Gulf_War)" class="mw-redirect" title="Operation Desert Shield (Gulf War)">Operation Desert Shield</a>.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/U.S._Navy" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Navy">U.S. Navy</a> had a radio station receiver site at Camp Santiago.\n</p><p>Camp Santiago also served as an Athletes\' Villages for the <a href="/wiki/1993_Central_American_and_Caribbean_Games" title="1993 Central American and Caribbean Games">1993 Central American and Caribbean Games</a> in Ponce and the <a href="/wiki/2010_Central_American_and_Caribbean_Games" title="2010 Central American and Caribbean Games">2010 Central American and Caribbean Games</a> in Mayag\xc3\xbcez.\n</p><p>Following the events of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> in 2017, Camp Santiago JTC suffered damages, the Puerto Rico National Guard received an injection of $500 million to improve and repair post facilities the due to the force of the hurricane.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Installation">Installation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Campamento_Santiago&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Installation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Camp Santiago Joint Training Center (CSJTC) is located 82 kilometres (51&#160;mi)  south of San Juan, the capital of Puerto Rico. The main gate is located right off <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_52" title="Puerto Rico Highway 52">Puerto Rico Highway 52</a> on exit 65, the camp is easily accessible from almost everywhere in Puerto Rico. Though it has no permanent residents, Camp Santiago can house thousands of troops on a temporarily basis. Some old barracks were demolished and replaced with new barracks; Other facilities include the Sustainment Automation Support Management Office (SASMO), a Puerto Rico National Guard Museum, Theater, Dining Facilities, Base Chapel, Fire Station, a pool for recreation and water training exercises, a cyber caf\xc3\xa9 (computer classroom/laboratory), Troop Medical Clinic, The Brig Gen. Victor J. Torres Fitness Center, ID Card office, MWR Community Center and <a href="/wiki/AAFES" class="mw-redirect" title="AAFES">AAFES</a> <a href="/wiki/Post_exchange" class="mw-redirect" title="Post exchange">post exchange</a> and gas station on base.\n</p><p>Training facilities at Camp Santiago include Virtual Convoy Operations Trainer (OCOT), Call For Fire Trainer (CFFT), Engage Similation Training (EST2000), Maneuver Area Training Equipment Site (MATES), 2HMMWV Engress Assistance Trainer (HEAT), FOB Compound Area, EID Path Zoo, a new Rappelling Tower, AWTs, Pre-mob Training Area, Multi-Purpose Machine Gun Range and an Urban Assault Range (UAR). Future projects for the Camp Santiago Joint Maneuver Training Center (CSJMTC) includes a land navigation course, a live shooting house, and a military pistol qualification range.\n</p><p>Warriors Plaza was inaugurated on September 11, 2011, to commemorate the 10th anniversary of the September 11, 2001 <a href="/wiki/9/11" class="mw-redirect" title="9/11">9/11</a> tragedy and honor all the service members of the Puerto Rico National Guard who were mobilized to the New York area to respond to the search and rescue mission.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Current_use">Current use</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Campamento_Santiago&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Current use"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Puerto_Rico_Army_National_Guard.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Puerto_Rico_Army_National_Guard.jpg/250px-Puerto_Rico_Army_National_Guard.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="188" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Puerto_Rico_Army_National_Guard.jpg/375px-Puerto_Rico_Army_National_Guard.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/bf/Puerto_Rico_Army_National_Guard.jpg 2x" data-file-width="400" data-file-height="300" /></a><figcaption>Puerto Rico Army National Guard soldiers present the colors at the 2012 annual training closing ceremonies at Camp Santiago, PR.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Camp Santiago is the primary training area for the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a>, <a href="/wiki/Army_National_Guard" title="Army National Guard">Army National Guard</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Reserve" title="United States Army Reserve">United States Army Reserve</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps_Reserve" title="United States Marine Corps Reserve">United States Marine Corps Reserve</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Air_National_Guard" title="Air National Guard">Air National Guard</a> in Puerto Rico. The camp is used for <a href="/wiki/Annual_training" title="Annual training">Annual Field Training Exercises</a> (FTX) each summer and for monthly drills throughout the year. Some Army National Guard and Army Reserve units from the Continental United States (CONUS) also come to Camp Santiago for their annual training camp. The only permanent units at Camp Santiago are the 1600th Ordnance Company and the 770th Transportation Company. The <a href="/wiki/Army_ROTC" class="mw-redirect" title="Army ROTC">Army ROTC</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Air_Force_ROTC" class="mw-redirect" title="Air Force ROTC">Air Force ROTC</a> hold their semester training exercises at Camp Santiago.\n</p><p>Camp Santiago is open to the general public for the annual Puerto Rico National Guard Family Day held each February.\n</p><p>The Puerto Rico Rifle and Pistol Association hold their state championship every year at Camp Santiago, with participants from the military, law enforcement agencies, and civilian organizations. Federal and state law enforcement agencies use Camp Santiago as a support facility for operations in southern Puerto Rico. Military youth groups such as the <a href="/wiki/Civil_Air_Patrol" title="Civil Air Patrol">Civil Air Patrol</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Naval_Sea_Cadet_Corps" title="United States Naval Sea Cadet Corps">United States Naval Sea Cadet Corps</a> conduct Summer Encampment at Camp Santiago.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="See_also">See also</h2><span
