style="border: 0;">December 21, 2000</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Bas\xc3\xadlica of the Black Virgin of Monserrat</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>:<i> El Santuario de la Monserrate de Hormigueros</i> and <i>Casa de los Peregrinos</i>) is a historic <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic</a> shrine built in the town of <a href="/wiki/Hormigueros,_Puerto_Rico" title="Hormigueros, Puerto Rico">Hormigueros</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, dedicated to the <a href="/wiki/Titles_of_Mary" class="mw-redirect" title="Titles of Mary">Marian title</a> of \xe2\x80\x9c<a href="/wiki/Virgin_of_Montserrat" title="Virgin of Montserrat">Virgin of Montserrat</a>\xe2\x80\x9d.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Pope_John_Paul_II" title="Pope John Paul II">Pope John Paul II</a> granted a pontifical decree of <a href="/wiki/Canonical_coronation" title="Canonical coronation">canonical coronation</a> titled <i>Omnibus Manifestum</i> towards its venerated Marian image on 18 July 1994. The <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_San_Juan" class="mw-redirect" title="Archbishop of San Juan">Archbishop of San Juan</a>, Cardinal <a href="/wiki/Luis_Aponte_Mart%C3%ADnez" title="Luis Aponte Mart\xc3\xadnez">Luis Aponte Mart\xc3\xadnez</a>, executed the rite of coronation on 12 February 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The same Pontiff issued a decree titled <i>Celeberrimum in Civitate</i> that raised the sanctuary to the status of a <a href="/wiki/Minor_basilica" class="mw-redirect" title="Minor basilica">minor basilica</a> on 19 May 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bas%C3%ADlica_of_the_Virgin_of_Monserrat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: of Basilica Menor</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The foundation of the basilica is on the site of a rural <a href="/wiki/Chapel" title="Chapel">chapel</a> built by the <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalan</a> estate owner <a href="/wiki/Don_(honorific)" title="Don (honorific)">Don</a> <a href="/wiki/Giraldo_Gonz%C3%A1lez_de_la_Renta" title="Giraldo Gonz\xc3\xa1lez de la Renta">Gerardo Gonz\xc3\xa1lez</a>, who was the owner of the valleys of the region.<sup id="cite_ref-Hormigueros_Leyend_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hormigueros_Leyend-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Although the specific date when the original chapel was constructed is not known, some records date it as far as 1590. Also, <a href="/wiki/Radiocarbon_dating" title="Radiocarbon dating">radiocarbon dating</a> tests done to pieces of wood from a coffin found in the basilica point to the existence of the chapel as far back as 1570.\n</p><p>The structure combines romantic elements and styles in its architecture, including a <a href="/wiki/Bell_tower" title="Bell tower">bell tower</a> with <a href="/wiki/Mozarabic_art_and_architecture" title="Mozarabic art and architecture">Mozarabic</a> elements.\n</p><p>In 1696, the chapel acquired a guest house called <i>Casa de Peligrinos</i> (Pilgrims House). In 1814, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Alejo_de_Arizmendi" title="Juan Alejo de Arizmendi">Juan Alejo de Arizmendi</a>, the first native Puerto Rican bishop, fell ill in this house after traveling around the island in his second pastoral visit.<sup id="cite_ref-review_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> He desired to be buried in his beloved chapel. He died in <a href="/wiki/Arecibo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Arecibo, Puerto Rico">Arecibo</a> the following October 12, but his wishes were not followed, burial instead taking place in his <a href="/wiki/Cathedral" title="Cathedral">cathedral</a>. The house is now the <a href="/wiki/Rectory" class="mw-redirect" title="Rectory">rectory</a> of the basilica.\n</p><p>The basilica and its rectory were listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1975 as the <i>Santuario de la Monserate de Hormigueros and Casa de Peregrinos</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-nris_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nris-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nrhpdoc_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpdoc-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was also added to the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Register_of_Historic_Sites_and_Zones" title="Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones">Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones</a> in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On 19 May 1998, <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_Paul_II" title="Pope John Paul II">Pope John Paul II</a> issued a pontifical decree that raised the status of the structure to a <a href="/wiki/Basilicas_in_the_Catholic_Church" title="Basilicas in the Catholic Church">minor basilica</a>, a distinction held previously on the island only by the <a href="/wiki/Catedral_Metropolitana_Bas%C3%ADlica_de_San_Juan_Bautista_(San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico)" class="mw-redirect" title="Catedral Metropolitana Bas\xc3\xadlica de San Juan Bautista (San Juan, Puerto Rico)">Cathedral of San Juan Bautista</a> in the capital.\n</p><p>On 24 October 1999, the <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_San_Juan" class="mw-redirect" title="Archbishop of San Juan">Archbishop of San Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Gonz%C3%A1lez_Nieves" title="Roberto Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Nieves">Roberto Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Nieves</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Gonz%C3%A1lez_Nieves" title="Roberto Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Nieves">Roberto Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Nieves</a> read the papal decree before the public in commemoration of the shrines 400th centennial anniversary.<sup id="cite_ref-minor_basilica_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-minor_basilica-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Folklore">Folklore</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bas%C3%ADlica_of_the_Virgin_of_Monserrat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Folklore"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:BasilicaMenor-HormiguerosPR-06.JPG" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/BasilicaMenor-HormiguerosPR-06.JPG/220px-BasilicaMenor-HormiguerosPR-06.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/BasilicaMenor-HormiguerosPR-06.JPG/440px-BasilicaMenor-HormiguerosPR-06.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1075" data-file-height="806" /></a><figcaption>Central nave of the basilica</figcaption></figure>\n<p>There is <a href="/wiki/Folklore" title="Folklore">folklore</a> about the town\'s founder, an explanation of why the church in the town, Bas\xc3\xadlica Menor de la Virgen de Monserrate, became so important. The legend has to do with the townspeople aiding Geraldo in finding his eight-year-old daughter, "with eyes as blue as the turquoise skies", when she disappeared. When his daughter was found 15 days later she was unharmed, not hungry, and unafraid and happily explained that a beautiful, black woman had helped her, caressed her face and given her fruit. Geraldo reported it was the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_of_Montserrat" title="Virgin of Montserrat">Virgin of Montserrat</a> and Geraldo said that for having helped his daughter she would be venerated for all ages. Many people heard of the tale but it had not been the first tale from Geraldo.<sup id="cite_ref-Hormigueros_Leyend_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hormigueros_Leyend-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The first and more popular tale from Geraldo had been that a savage bull was going to attack him and when he prayed, the bull bent its knees, bowed its head to the ground, and didn\'t attack.<sup id="cite_ref-Ram\xc3\xadrez-Rivera_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ram\xc3\xadrez-Rivera-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Some versions of the story state the bull\'s legs actually broke rendering the bull immobile and unable to charge at
