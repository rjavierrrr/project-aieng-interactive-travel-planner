class="infobox-label">Profession</th><td class="infobox-data"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1129693374"><div class="hlist">\n<ul><li>Medical <a href="/wiki/Physician" title="Physician">physician</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Sociologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Sociologist">sociologist</a></li>\n<li>political leader in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a></li></ul>\n</div></td></tr><tr><td colspan="2" class="infobox-below" style="border-top: 1px solid right;"><div></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Jos\xc3\xa9 Celso Barbosa Alcala</b> (July 27, 1857 \xe2\x80\x93 September 21, 1921) was a <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Ricans" title="Puerto Ricans">Puerto Rican</a> <a href="/wiki/Physician" title="Physician">physician</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sociologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Sociologist">sociologist</a> and political leader. Known as the father of the <a href="/wiki/Statehood_movement_in_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Statehood movement in Puerto Rico">statehood movement in Puerto Rico</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-doak_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-doak-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Barbosa was the first Puerto Rican, and one of the first persons of African descent to earn a medical degree in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MJCB_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MJCB-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>After his return to the island in 1880, Barbosa made many contributions to medicine and public health. He initiated an early form of health insurance, encouraging employers to pay a fee to cover future needs of their employees. In 1900 Barbosa was among the first five Puerto Rican leaders appointed to the Executive Cabinet under Governor <a href="/wiki/Charles_H._Allen" title="Charles H. Allen">Charles H. Allen</a>, in the first civilian government organized by the United States. He served in the Cabinet until 1917. From 1917 to 1921, Barbosa served in the <a href="/wiki/1st_Puerto_Rican_Senate" class="mw-redirect" title="1st Puerto Rican Senate">first elected Puerto Rican Senate</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Early_years">Early years</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_Celso_Barbosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Barbosa was born in 1857 in the city of <a href="/wiki/Bayam%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico" title="Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico">Bayam\xc3\xb3n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Captaincy_General_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Captaincy General of Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> to parents of African and European ancestry. He received both his primary and secondary education in Puerto Rico. He was the first person of African ancestry to attend Puerto Rico\'s prestigious Jesuit Seminary. After graduating from the seminary, Barbosa tutored private students to save money to attend college. In 1875, he moved to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> to attend prep school, where he learned <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> in a year.<sup id="cite_ref-Barbosa_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Barbosa-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Education">Education</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_Celso_Barbosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:House_of_Jos%C3%A9_Celso_Barbosa.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/House_of_Jos%C3%A9_Celso_Barbosa.jpg/250px-House_of_Jos%C3%A9_Celso_Barbosa.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="171" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/House_of_Jos%C3%A9_Celso_Barbosa.jpg/375px-House_of_Jos%C3%A9_Celso_Barbosa.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/House_of_Jos%C3%A9_Celso_Barbosa.jpg/500px-House_of_Jos%C3%A9_Celso_Barbosa.jpg 2x" data-file-width="888" data-file-height="609" /></a><figcaption>Museo Jos\xc3\xa9 Celso Barbosa, the doctor\'s birthplace and childhood home</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Originally Barbosa wished to become a lawyer, but after he suffered a bout of <a href="/wiki/Pneumonia" title="Pneumonia">pneumonia</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, his doctor recommended that he study medicine. In 1877 he applied but was denied admission to the <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University_Vagelos_College_of_Physicians_and_Surgeons" class="mw-redirect" title="Columbia University Vagelos College of Physicians and Surgeons">College of Physicians and Surgeons</a> at Columbia University, due to his race.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> He then applied and was admitted to the medical school of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a>, where he graduated as <a href="/wiki/Valedictorian" title="Valedictorian">valedictorian</a> of the class of 1880. Barbosa was the first person from Puerto Rico to earn a medical degree in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-MJCB_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MJCB-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> He returned to Puerto Rico, where he set up a practice in his hometown of Bayam\xc3\xb3n.<sup id="cite_ref-rivera_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rivera-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The Spanish colonial government initially did not recognize Barbosa\'s medical degree, as it was not from a known European university. The American consul to the island intervened on behalf of Barbosa to have his United States degree recognized, so that he could practice.<sup id="cite_ref-MJCB_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MJCB-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Barbosa provided medical care all over the island. He introduced the novel idea of employers paying a fee for the future healthcare needs of their employees (a very early <a href="/wiki/Health_insurance" title="Health insurance">health insurance</a> system). In 1893, Barbosa founded the first Puerto Rican cooperative and named it <i>El Ahorro Colectivo.</i><sup id="cite_ref-Barbosa_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Barbosa-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Political_career">Political career</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_Celso_Barbosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Political career"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>During the Spanish colonial period, Barbosa was a member of the Partido Autonomista Puro (Pure Autonomous Party), also known as "Partido Hist\xc3\xb3rico" and "Partido Ortodoxo", led by <a href="/wiki/Rom%C3%A1n_Baldorioty_de_Castro" title="Rom\xc3\xa1n Baldorioty de Castro">Rom\xc3\xa1n Baldorioty de Castro</a>, but left because of ideological differences.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1898, when the United States bombarded and blockaded San Juan during the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a>, Barbosa and other doctors who lived in Bayam\xc3\xb3n traveled to the town of <a href="/wiki/Cata%C3%B1o,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cata\xc3\xb1o, Puerto Rico">Cata\xc3\xb1o</a> and took a ferry to the capital. Barbosa, as a member of the <a href="/wiki/International_Red_Cross_and_Red_Crescent_Movement" title="International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement">Red Cross</a>, went to the aid of the wounded Puerto Rican and Spanish soldiers. He and his party on the ferry had to travel across <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Bay" title="San Juan Bay">San Juan Bay</a> at risk, as they were under cannon fire. Barbosa and those with him were recommended by the Spanish government for the <i>Cruz de la Orden del M\xc3\xa9rito Naval</i> (The Cross of the Order of Naval Merit) for their bravery.<sup id="cite_ref-iriarte_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iriarte-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>As a result of the war, the United States made Puerto Rico one of its territories. On July 4, 1899, Barbosa formed the pro-statehood <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_of_Puerto_Rico_(1899)" class="mw-redirect" title="Republican Party of Puerto Rico (1899)">Partido Republicano de Puerto Rico</a>. He became known as the father of the statehood for Puerto Rico movement.<sup id="cite_ref-doak_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-doak-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div style="clear:both;" class=""></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Later_years">Later years</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_Celso_Barbosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Later years"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Bust_of_Jose_Celso_Barbosa.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Bust_of_Jose_Celso_Barbosa.jpg/225px-Bust_of_Jose_Celso_Barbosa.jpg" decoding="async" width="225" height="282" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Bust_of_Jose_Celso_Barbosa.jpg/338px-Bust_of_Jose_Celso_Barbosa.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Bust_of_Jose_Celso_Barbosa.jpg/450px-Bust_of_Jose_Celso_Barbosa.jpg 2x" data-file-width="478" data-file-height="599" /></a><figcaption>Bust of Dr. Barbosa</figcaption></figure>\n<p>On June 5, 1900, President <a href="/wiki/William_McKinley" title="William McKinley">William McKinley</a> named Barbosa, together with <a href="/wiki/Rosendo_Matienzo_Cintr%C3%B3n" title="Rosendo Matienzo Cintr\xc3\xb3n">Rosendo Matienzo Cintr\xc3\xb3n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_de_Diego" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 de Diego">Jos\xc3\xa9 de Diego</a>, Manuel Camu\xc3\xb1as and Andr\xc3\xa9s Crosas, as part of an Executive Cabinet under U.S.-appointed Governor <a href="/wiki/Charles_H._Allen" title="Charles H. Allen">Charles H. Allen</a>, the first civilian governor of the island. The Executive Cabinet also included six American members.<sup id="cite_ref-PRSAW_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PRSAW-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Barbosa served on the Executive Cabinet until 1917, dealing with a variety of governors appointed by the US during that period, and providing continuity in administration.<sup id="cite_ref-Gonz\xc3\xa1lez_Vales_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gonz\xc3\xa1lez_Vales-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>During this period, in 1907 he founded the newspaper <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Tiempo_(Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="El Tiempo (Puerto Rico) (page does not exist)">El Tiempo</a>,</i> the first bilingual newspaper published on the island.<sup id="cite_ref-Gonz\xc3\xa1lez_Vales_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gonz\xc3\xa1lez_Vales-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>With representative elections authorized in 1917, Barbosa ran for an <a href="/wiki/At-large" title="At-large">at-large</a> seat. He was elected, serving as a member of the <a href="/wiki/1st_Puerto_Rican_Senate" class="mw-redirect" title="1st Puerto Rican Senate">first Puerto Rican Senate</a>, from 1917 to 1921.<sup id="cite_ref-Gonz\xc3\xa1lez_Vales_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gonz\xc3\xa1lez_Vales-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Jos\xc3\xa9 Celso Barbosa died in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a> on September 21, 1921. He was buried in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Maria_Magdalena_de_Pazzis_Cemetery" class="mw-redirect" title="Santa Maria Magdalena de Pazzis Cemetery">Santa Maria Magdalena de Pazzis Cemetery</a> in <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">Old San Juan</a>. One of his daughters, <a href="/wiki/Pilar_Barbosa" title="Pilar Barbosa">Pilar Barbosa</a>, became a historian, serving as the <a href="/wiki/Official_Historian_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Official Historian of Puerto Rico">Official Historian of Puerto Rico</a> from 1993 to 1997. She was also a political activist who carried on her father\'s work.<sup id="cite_ref-vargas-rosario_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vargas-rosario-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Gonz\xc3\xa1lez_Vales_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gonz\xc3\xa1lez_Vales-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Legacy_and_honors">Legacy and honors</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jos%C3%A9_Celso_Barbosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Legacy and honors"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<ul><li>Puerto Rico has declared his birthday, July 27, an official holiday,<sup id="cite_ref-doak_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-doak-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> known as <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Celso_Barbosa_Day_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Celso Barbosa Day (Puerto Rico)">Jos\xc3\xa9 Celso Barbosa Day</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Celso_Barbosa_House_Museum" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Celso Barbosa House Museum">Barbosa\'s house in Bayam\xc3\xb3n</a> has been preserved and is operated as a <a href="/wiki/Historic_house_museum" title="Historic house museum">historic house museum</a>. Many of his awards, certificates, books and other artifacts of interest are on exhibit.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>On August 1, 2006, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Postal_Service" title="United States Postal Service">United States Postal Service</a> Post Office at 100 Avenida RL Rodriguez in Bayam\xc3\xb3n was named as the "Dr. Jose Celso Barbosa Post Office Building".  The act for this was signed by President <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>NB 1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pl-109-253_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pl-109-253-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>In <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>, there is a school named after him.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>Avenida Jos\xc3\xa9 C. Barbosa, in <a href="/wiki/Yauco" class="mw-redirect" title="Yauco">Yauco</a>, is named for him.</li></ul>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Descendants">Descendants</h2><span
