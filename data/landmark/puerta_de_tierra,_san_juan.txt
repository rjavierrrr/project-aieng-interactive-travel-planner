class="infobox-data" style="border: 0;">October 15, 2019</td></tr><tr style="display:none"><td colspan="2">\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Puerta de Tierra</b> is a <i>subbarrio</i> (subdistrict) occupying the eastern portion of the <a href="/wiki/Isleta_de_San_Juan" class="mw-redirect" title="Isleta de San Juan">Islet of San Juan</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico"><i>barrio</i></a> of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Antiguo" title="San Juan Antiguo">San Juan Antiguo</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The name Puerta de Tierra (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> for <i>land gate</i>) derives from the former eastern gated entrance to the <a href="/wiki/City_Wall_of_San_Juan" class="mw-redirect" title="City Wall of San Juan">walled city of San Juan</a> where <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Col%C3%B3n_(San_Juan)" title="Plaza Col\xc3\xb3n (San Juan)">Plaza Col\xc3\xb3n</a> (formerly Santiago Square, named after the <i>Puerta de Santiago</i>) is today. With a population of 2,924 as of 2010, this is the most populated area of San Juan Antiguo. On October 15, 2019, it was added to the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Puerta_de_Tierra,_San_Juan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Indigenous_history">Indigenous history</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Puerta_de_Tierra,_San_Juan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Indigenous history"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Before the arrival of the Europeans to Puerto Rico, Puerta de Tierra (along with Old San Juan) formed part of a smaller islet which was populated by the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Ta\xc3\xadno</a>. Remains of a small indigenous fishing village have been found where the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_National_Guard_Museum" title="Puerto Rico National Guard Museum">Puerto Rico National Guard Museum</a> stands today, however most archaeological sites in the area have been destroyed and lost during the construction of the Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera Avenue and surrounding buildings.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Spanish_colonization">Spanish colonization</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Puerta_de_Tierra,_San_Juan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Spanish colonization"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Spanish began the colonization of Puerto Rico in 1508 when Juan Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n founded <a href="/wiki/Caparra_Archaeological_Site" title="Caparra Archaeological Site">Caparra</a>, the first permanent settlement in the island, located in the southern part of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Bay" title="San Juan Bay">Bay of San Juan</a> (today part of <a href="/wiki/Guaynabo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guaynabo, Puerto Rico">Guaynabo</a>). This Spanish village was abandoned in 1519 when the Spanish founded the settlement of <i>Puerto Rico de San Juan Bautista</i> (modern-day Old San Juan) across the bay. The area where Puerta de Tierra is located became critical for the settlement and the defense of the Islet of San Juan as it represented the closest part of the islet to the main island of Puerto Rico. This area, later referred to as <a href="/wiki/El_Boquer%C3%B3n_(San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico)" title="El Boquer\xc3\xb3n (San Juan, Puerto Rico)">El Boquer\xc3\xb3n</a>, was separated from the main island by the San Antonio strait (today known as the San Antonio Channel). The first bridge across the San Antonio Channel was built by enslaved Ta\xc3\xadnos between 1520 and 1521 as entrusted by the <a href="/wiki/Hieronymites" title="Hieronymites">Order of Saint Jerome</a> (or Hieronymites).<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>One of the first <a href="/wiki/Witch-hunt" title="Witch-hunt">witch-hunts</a> in the New World occurred in Puerta de Tierra in 1594 when bishop and <a href="/wiki/Inquisitor" title="Inquisitor">inquisitor</a> Don Nicol\xc3\xa1s Ramos accused a group of people of African descent of celebrating pagan rituals just outside of the settlement of San Juan. The accused were tortured and forced to reject their beliefs, although three women who did not comply were burned at the stake by orders of the <a href="/wiki/Inquisition" title="Inquisition">Holy Inquisition</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="San_Juan\'s_eastern_expansion"><span id="San_Juan.27s_eastern_expansion"></span>San Juan\'s eastern expansion</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Puerta_de_Tierra,_San_Juan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: San Juan&#039;s eastern expansion"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Puerta_santiago.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Puerta_santiago.jpg/220px-Puerta_santiago.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="141" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Puerta_santiago.jpg/330px-Puerta_santiago.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/b4/Puerta_santiago.jpg 2x" data-file-width="400" data-file-height="257" /></a><figcaption><i>Puerta de Santiago</i>, also known as <i>Puerta de Tierra</i>, today <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Col%C3%B3n_(San_Juan)" title="Plaza Col\xc3\xb3n (San Juan)">Plaza Col\xc3\xb3n</a>, is the namesake of this subdistrict.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Construction of the fortified wall system of San Juan began in 1634. Although the area outside the city walls occupying the east side of Old San Juan was almost uninhabited prior to the 19th century, this area represents the first urban expansion of San Juan during the colonial period. Some of the settlers of the area around this time were freed families originating from the British, Dutch and Danish colonies in the Caribbean. While most residences were wooden huts and <i>boh\xc3\xados</i>, this represents the first eastward demographic expansion of the colonial city of San Juan. Most of the huts were located along the eastern walls and the <i>Puerta de Santiago</i> (Santiago Gate). Due to the fact that this was the main entrance by land to the fortified city, the gate was referred to as <i>Puerta de Tierra</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The area was further settled after the eastward expansion of the batteries of <a href="/wiki/Castillo_San_Crist%C3%B3bal_(San_Juan)" title="Castillo San Crist\xc3\xb3bal (San Juan)">Fort San Crist\xc3\xb3bal</a> in 1771 and the construction of the <i>Camino Real</i> (today the <a href="/wiki/Avenida_Juan_Ponce_de_Le%C3%B3n" title="Avenida Juan Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n">Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n Avenue</a>) later in 1810. The area was attacked by the British during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_San_Juan_(1797)" title="Battle of San Juan (1797)">1797 Battle of San Juan</a> under the command of Sir <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Abercromby" title="Ralph Abercromby">Ralph Abercromby</a> and Sir <a href="/wiki/Henry_Harvey" title="Henry Harvey">Henry Harvey</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolutionary_and_Napoleonic_Wars" title="French Revolutionary and Napoleonic Wars">Coalition</a> <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Spanish_War_(1796%E2%80%931808)" title="Anglo-Spanish War (1796\xe2\x80\x931808)">Anglo-Spanish War</a>. The British invasion was thwarted by the local Spanish militia and regiments of Afro-Puerto Ricans after an unsuccessful siege of San Juan. This event led to the re-fortification of El Boquer\xc3\xb3n in the eastern end of Puerta de Tierra. Remnants of these military installations can still be seen today in the <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Rivera_Park" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera Park">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera Park</a>. By 1838, Puerta de Tierra had a population of 168 residents, mainly of <a href="/wiki/Afro%E2%80%93Puerto_Ricans" title="Afro\xe2\x80\x93Puerto Ricans">African descent</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Historia_de_Puerta_de_Tierra_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Historia_de_Puerta_de_Tierra-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="1867_earthquake_and_wall_demolitions">1867 earthquake and wall demolitions</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Puerta_de_Tierra,_San_Juan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1867 earthquake and wall demolitions"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Although the military installations in San Juan were modernized in 1865, the eastern portion of the walls were no longer deemed necessary due to the eastward expansion of the city and were consequently partially demolished. The <a href="/wiki/1867_Virgin_Islands_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="1867 Virgin Islands earthquake and tsunami">1867 Virgin Islands earthquake</a> destroyed the remaining eastern military installations in the area, and a new road and railway station were built with the name of <i>Paseo Virgen de Nuestra de Se\xc3\xb1ora de Covadonga</i> and the Covadonga Terminal, respectively. The Paseo Covadonga exists today under the name of Covadonga Avenue.<sup id="cite_ref-Historia_de_Puerta_de_Tierra_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Historia_de_Puerta_de_Tierra-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The San Juan <a href="/wiki/Tram" title="Tram">streetcar</a> system was developed along the Camino Real in 1880, during a time when the southern coast of Puerta de Tierra began to be industrially developed as piers and docks were installed along the San Antonio Channel. During this time, Puerta de Tierra became the most populated area in the Islet of San Juan, which is reflected through the construction of a new parish church (<a href="/wiki/Church,_School,_Convent_and_Parish_House_of_San_Agust%C3%ADn" title="Church, School, Convent and Parish House of San Agust\xc3\xadn">Church of San Agust\xc3\xadn</a>), bullfight arenas, schools and new streets. Although the area was quickly developing, most of the residential structures were unplanned, something that also happened along the northern walls of the city in <a href="/wiki/La_Perla,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="La Perla, San Juan, Puerto Rico">La Perla</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American_War"><span id="Spanish.E2.80.93American_War"></span>Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Puerta_de_Tierra,_San_Juan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1236090951">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}@media print{body.ns-0 .mw-parser-output .hatnote{display:none!important}}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_campaign" title="Puerto Rico campaign">Puerto Rico campaign</a></div>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> took control of Puerto Rico from <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1898)" title="Treaty of Paris (1898)">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a>. In 1899, the United States conducted its first census of Puerto Rico, finding that the population of Puerta de Tierra was 5,453. Puerta de Tierra was badly damaged during the path of the <i>San Ciriaco hurricane</i> (also known as the <a href="/wiki/1899_San_Ciriaco_hurricane" title="1899 San Ciriaco hurricane">1899 Puerto Rico Hurricane</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-OfficeSanger1900_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OfficeSanger1900-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="20th_century">20th century</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Puerta_de_Tierra,_San_Juan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 20th century"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:The_Street_railway_journal_(1901)_(14758136892).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/The_Street_railway_journal_%281901%29_%2814758136892%29.jpg/330px-The_Street_railway_journal_%281901%29_%2814758136892%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/The_Street_railway_journal_%281901%29_%2814758136892%29.jpg/440px-The_Street_railway_journal_%281901%29_%2814758136892%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1230" data-file-height="704" /></a><figcaption>San Juan streetcar in 1901.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Puerta de Tierra went through large changes during the beginning of the 20th century. <i>Barriada Miranda</i> was the first formally established public residential area in the zone in 1903 along the <i>Camino Real</i>, now called the <a href="/wiki/Carretera_Central_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Carretera Central (Puerto Rico)">Carretera Central</a>. Politician Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera suggested in 1907 that the <a href="/wiki/Capitol_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Capitol of Puerto Rico">territorial capitol of Puerto Rico</a> should be built in the largest of the empty parcels of land where the eastern city walls used to be, next to the <a href="/wiki/Teatro_Tapia" title="Teatro Tapia">Tapia Theater</a>. The first public elementary school, Jos\xc3\xa9 Juli\xc3\xa1n Acosta School, was also built here in 1907. The portion of this road that passed through Puerta de Tierra was popularly known as the <i>Broadway of Puerto Rico</i> by the media due to the large number of theaters and cultural performing institutions being built at the time. Tres Banderas Theater, for example, was the first cinema to be established in the area in 1910. Some of the first non-Catholic Christian institutions were also established here in the 1910s such as the first <a href="/wiki/Methodism" title="Methodism">Methodist</a> church and the first <a href="/wiki/Lutheranism" title="Lutheranism">Lutheran</a> church in San Juan.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/1928_Okeechobee_hurricane" title="1928 Okeechobee hurricane">1928 Okeechobee hurricane</a> (known locally as the <i>San Felipe Segundo hurricane</i>) caused approximately 300 deaths in Puerta de Tierra. This and other disasters such as disease outbreaks negatively affected the development of the area in the first half of the 20th century. Puerta de Tierra and its docks were also the site of large and important workers\' protests during this time. The <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">Second World War</a>, however, brought an increase in the industrial activity in the area. The first use of condominiums as public housing in the United States also occurred during this time in Puerta de Tierra, the first of which was the <i>Condominio San Luis</i>, and multi-level housing soon replaced wooden single-family houses.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span of the <a href="/wiki/Isleta_de_San_Juan" class="mw-redirect" title="Isleta de San Juan">Islet of San Juan</a> and it is connected to the mainland by bridges (<a href="/wiki/Dos_Hermanos_Bridge" title="Dos Hermanos Bridge">Dos Hermanos</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Antonio_Bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="San Antonio Bridge (page does not exist)">San Antonio</a>) and a causeway. It is bordered by the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> to the north, <a href="/wiki/El_Boquer%C3%B3n_(San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico)" title="El Boquer\xc3\xb3n (San Juan, Puerto Rico)">El Boquer\xc3\xb3n</a> and <a href="/wiki/Condado_(Santurce)" title="Condado (Santurce)">El Condado</a> to the east, the San Antonio Channel to the south and <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">Old San Juan</a> immediately to the west. Puerta de Tierra is the site of many of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>\'s government buildings, including the <a href="/wiki/Capitol_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Capitol of Puerto Rico">Capitol of Puerto Rico</a>. With a <a href="/wiki/2000_United_States_Census" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 United States Census">2000 census</a> population of 4,135 and a land area of 0.60 sq. miles (1.55&#160;km\xc2\xb2), Puerta de Tierra is the largest and most populous <i>subbarrio</i> of San Juan Antiguo <i>barrio</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2010_Census_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2010_Census-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In October 2017, Puerta de Tierra won an award from the <a href="/wiki/International_Union_of_Architects" title="International Union of Architects">International Union of Architects</a>, a non-governmental body based in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> which represents the world\'s architects. Puerta de Tierra was awarded "best public spaces".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-halign-center" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:The_Skyline_of_San_Juan_with_the_Capitol_of_Puerto_Rico.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/The_Skyline_of_San_Juan_with_the_Capitol_of_Puerto_Rico.jpg/600px-The_Skyline_of_San_Juan_with_the_Capitol_of_Puerto_Rico.jpg" decoding="async" width="600" height="150" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/The_Skyline_of_San_Juan_with_the_Capitol_of_Puerto_Rico.jpg/900px-The_Skyline_of_San_Juan_with_the_Capitol_of_Puerto_Rico.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/The_Skyline_of_San_Juan_with_the_Capitol_of_Puerto_Rico.jpg/1200px-The_Skyline_of_San_Juan_with_the_Capitol_of_Puerto_Rico.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="319" /></a><figcaption>Skyline of San Juan with the <a href="/wiki/Capitol_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Capitol of Puerto Rico">Capitol of Puerto Rico</a> and Puerta de Tierra in the foreground.</figcaption></figure>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="National_Register_of_Historic_Places">National Register of Historic Places</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Puerta_de_Tierra,_San_Juan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: National Register of Historic Places"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>On October 15, 2019, Puerta de Tierra was officially added to the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>. According to Puerto Rican newspaper <a href="/wiki/El_Nuevo_D%C3%ADa" title="El Nuevo D\xc3\xada">El Nuevo Dia</a>, Bibiana Hern\xc3\xa1ndez had worked for decades to have Puerta de Tierra added to the US National Register of Historic Places (NRHP) and was overjoyed at the news. Many people such as artist and community spokesman, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jes%C3%BAs_%E2%80%9CBubu%E2%80%9D_Negr%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jes\xc3\xbas \xe2\x80\x9cBubu\xe2\x80\x9d Negr\xc3\xb3n (page does not exist)">Jes\xc3\xbas \xe2\x80\x9cBubu\xe2\x80\x9d Negr\xc3\xb3n</a>, have worked along with Arleen Pab\xc3\xb3n-Charneco, a Puerto Rican architect and author of the NRHP nomination forms, to bring more visibility to the area.\n</p><p>The community expressed concerns saying the area was starting to experience gentrification. This designation now makes the entire <a href="/wiki/Isleta_de_San_Juan" class="mw-redirect" title="Isleta de San Juan">Isleta de San Juan</a> (San Juan Islet) a historic district, along with the <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan_Historic_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Old San Juan Historic District">Old San Juan Historic District</a> (which is also on the small isle) and the designation should make it easier to prioritize its care with federal funds for these purposes. There were already thirteen structures in Puerta de Tierra on the NRHP, including <a href="/wiki/El_Falansterio_de_Puerta_de_Tierra" title="El Falansterio de Puerta de Tierra">El Falansterio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brambaugh_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Brambaugh School">Brambaugh School</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Celso_Barbosa_Graded_School" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Celso Barbosa Graded School">Jos\xc3\xa9 Celso Barbosa Graded School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>There is a phrase seen on murals around Puerta de Tierra that says <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Aqu\xc3\xad vive gente</i></span> ("People live here") and the community has been planning to create a museum about Puerta de Tierra.<sup id="cite_ref-museum_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-museum-9"><span
