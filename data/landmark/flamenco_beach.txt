template">e</abbr></a></li></ul></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Flamenco Beach</b> is a public <a href="/wiki/Beach" title="Beach">beach</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> <a href="/wiki/Culebra,_Puerto_Rico" title="Culebra, Puerto Rico">island of Culebra</a> (<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1177148991">.mw-parser-output .IPA-label-small{font-size:85%}.mw-parser-output .references .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;ku\xcb\x88le\xce\xb2\xc9\xbea&#93;</a></span>). It is known for its shallow turquoise waters, white sand, swimming areas, and diving sites. It stretches for a mile around a sheltered, horseshoe-shaped bay. Playa Flamenco is a popular beach destination for both Culebra and Puerto Rico. In March 2014, Flamenco Beach was ranked 3rd best beach in the world with a <a href="/wiki/TripAdvisor" class="mw-redirect" title="TripAdvisor">TripAdvisor Travelers\' Choice Award</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In August 2016, it was announced that Flamenco Beach had regained its <a href="/wiki/Blue_Flag_Beach" class="mw-redirect" title="Blue Flag Beach">Blue Flag Beach</a> international distinction.<sup id="cite_ref-endi_8_2018_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-endi_8_2018-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flamenco_Beach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Flamenco is located on the northern shore of Culebra. Its name in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> (<i>flamingo beach</i>) comes from the numerous Caribbean flamingos (<i><a href="/wiki/American_flamingo" title="American flamingo">Phoenicopterus ruber</a></i>) that were once common in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Flamenco_Lagoon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Flamenco Lagoon (page does not exist)">Flamenco Lagoon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The beach lies on a half-mile-wide, horseshoe strip of coast. The beach is bordered by the <a href="/wiki/Culebra_National_Wildlife_Refuge" title="Culebra National Wildlife Refuge">Culebra National Wildlife Refuge</a>, which is of itself one of the oldest wildlife preserves in the United States. Off the coast of Flamenco, approximately a quarter mile, is a reef where the surf breaks. Characteristic of Flamenco is the beach\'s white sand and shallow clear waters.<sup id="cite_ref-FB_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FB-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flora_and_fauna">Flora and fauna</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flamenco_Beach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Flora and fauna"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The waters off Flamenco beach are home to species of <a href="/wiki/Parrot_fish" class="mw-redirect" title="Parrot fish">parrot fish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blue_tang" title="Blue tang">blue tang</a>, multiple species of <a href="/wiki/Wrasse" title="Wrasse">wrasse</a>, and other <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> fish species. Crustacean species such as <a href="/wiki/Ghost_Crab" class="mw-redirect" title="Ghost Crab">Ghost Crab</a> are also observed. Some 50,000 seabirds visit Culebra\'s Flamenco Peninsula each summer to nest\xe2\x80\x94mostly <a href="/wiki/Sooty_tern" title="Sooty tern">sooty terns</a> and other migratory species. Summer visitors to Flamenco Beach are familiar with them as they often feed in the area in large numbers. By September, the birds have gathered up their broods and flown out to sea only to return home the following summer. Occasionally, <a href="/wiki/Leatherback_sea_turtle" title="Leatherback sea turtle">leatherback</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hawksbill_sea_turtle" title="Hawksbill sea turtle">hawksbill</a> <a href="/wiki/Sea_turtle" title="Sea turtle">sea turtles</a> may be seen as the beaches of the Culebra archipelago are also a major breeding ground for them and the adjacent sea grass beds provide shelter and food for green sea turtles.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Rusting_tanks">Rusting tanks</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flamenco_Beach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rusting tanks"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>One of the most distinctive views of the beach include two rusting carcasses of old <a href="/wiki/M4_Sherman" title="M4 Sherman">M4 Sherman tanks</a> that were left behind<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes.com_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes.com-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-epicbeaches.com_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-epicbeaches.com-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> after their departure from Flamenco Beach in 1975, which was used as a weapons-testing ground by them for more than 30 years. While the salty winds from the sea worked on the metal, causing it to rapidly rust and crumble apart, the locals and visitors often decorate them, covering the badly corroded hulks in layers of colorful graffiti. Today, the tanks still remain and have become a unique feature of the otherwise pristine and beautiful beach.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span href="/w/index.php?title=Flamenco_Beach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tourism"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Flamenco Beach is the top ranked beach in Puerto Rico and one of the top beaches in the world. The beach has available lifeguard towers, kiosks selling food, beach accessories, and easy access from the town of <a href="/wiki/Culebra_barrio-pueblo" title="Culebra barrio-pueblo">Dewey</a> (where the <a href="/wiki/Ferry" title="Ferry">ferry boat</a> arrives).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-endi_8_2018_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-endi_8_2018-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="World_ranking_through_the_years">World ranking through the years</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flamenco_Beach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: World ranking through the years"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<ul><li>3rd in 2014 by <a href="/wiki/TripAdvisor" class="mw-redirect" title="TripAdvisor">TripAdvisor</a> Travelers\' Choice Awards<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>8th in 2015 by Tripadvisor Travelers\' Choice Awards<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>6th in 2016 by TripAdvisor Travelers\' Choice Awards<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>13 in 2017 by TripAdvisor Travelers\' Choice Awards<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li></ul>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Facilities">Facilities</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flamenco_Beach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Facilities"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The beach has bathroom facilities and showers. There are about a half-dozen kiosks selling local food. This include <a href="/wiki/Pi%C3%B1a_colada" title="Pi\xc3\xb1a colada">pi\xc3\xb1a colada</a>, mango smoothies, rice-and-beans burritos and all manner of seafood, from conch salad to fish skewers.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes.com_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes.com-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Flamenco Beach has a designated camping area for a fee which provides potable water, bathrooms and shower facilities.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Recreation">Recreation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flamenco_Beach&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Recreation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<ul><li><b>Swimming</b>: Areas designated with buoys. Shallow waters allow a safe environment for swimmers and non-swimmers.</li>\n<li><b>Sunbathing</b>: Excellent conditions year-round but beware of the tropical sun as exposure without proper sunblock may create uncomfortable situations such as sunburns and moderate to extreme itching when healing.</li>\n<li><b>Scuba and snorkeling</b>: There are reefs, urchins, a variety of fish species and other marine wildlife.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li><b>Sand</b>: white sand receives the shallow turquoise waters.</li>\n<li><b>Walking and hiking</b>: The horseshoe bay provides a walk path and surrounding trails are another way to look for bird-watching opportunities or photograph surrounding nature.<sup id="cite_ref-epicbeaches.com_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-epicbeaches.com-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span
