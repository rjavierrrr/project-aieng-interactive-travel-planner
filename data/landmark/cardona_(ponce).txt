Resources</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Isla Cardona</b>, also known as <b>Sor Isolina Ferr\xc3\xa9 Island</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> is a small, uninhabited island located 1.30 nautical miles south of the mainland <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rican</a> shore across from Barrio <a href="/wiki/Playa_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Playa (Ponce)">Playa</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> on the west side of the entrance to the harbor of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>. The small island is considered part of barrio Playa.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is home to the 1889 <a href="/wiki/Cardona_Island_Light" title="Cardona Island Light">Cardona Island Light</a>, which is listed in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._National_Register_of_Historic_Places" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. National Register of Historic Places">U.S. National Register of Historic Places</a>. Together with <a href="/wiki/Caja_de_Muertos" class="mw-redirect" title="Caja de Muertos">Caja de Muertos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isla_de_Gatas" class="mw-redirect" title="Isla de Gatas">Gatas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morrillito" title="Morrillito">Morrillito</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isla_de_Ratones_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Isla de Ratones (Ponce)">Ratones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isla_del_Frio" class="mw-redirect" title="Isla del Frio">Isla del Frio</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Isla_de_Jueyes" title="Isla de Jueyes">Isla de Jueyes</a>, Cardona is one of seven islands ascribed to the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The island gained notoriety in 2010 when the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Puerto_Rican_Bird_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Puerto Rican Bird Society (page does not exist)">Puerto Rican Bird Society</a> made it a target for the eradication of the <a href="/wiki/Black_rat" title="Black rat">black rat</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isla_Cardona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Located on the <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%ADa_de_Ponce" title="Bah\xc3\xada de Ponce">Bah\xc3\xada de Ponce</a>, the island has an area of 8.71 acres (3.52&#160;ha).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The island, sometimes erroneously called a key (or <a href="/wiki/Cay" title="Cay">cay</a>), is located at latitude 17\xc2\xb057"24.3\' and longitude -66\xc2\xb038\'5.9\' (latitude 17.95672N, longitude: -66.634982W<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>). The short distance from the mainland shore makes Cardona a popular landing point for aquatic events such as <a href="/wiki/Kayaking" title="Kayaking">kayaking</a> and summing, and every year the island is visited by dozens of swimmers in the annual <i><a href="/wiki/Cruce_a_Nado_Internacional" title="Cruce a Nado Internacional">Cruce a Nado</a></i>, an international swimming competition sponsored by the Ponce Municipal Government.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Name">Name</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isla_Cardona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Name"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The name "Cardona" is believed to come from the last name of a family of lighthouse keepers stationed at the island for many decades. Whatever its origin, the island is now also called "<a href="/wiki/Isolina_Ferr%C3%A9" title="Isolina Ferr\xc3\xa9">Sor Isolina Ferr\xc3\xa9</a>" in appreciation and to the memory of the religious woman that dedicated her life to the betterment of the residents of <a href="/wiki/Barrio_Playa" class="mw-redirect" title="Barrio Playa">Barrio Playa</a>, of which Cardona Island is a part.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Tourism">Tourism</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isla_Cardona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tourism"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The island can be seen from the observation tower on the <a href="/wiki/Paseo_Tablado_La_Guancha" title="Paseo Tablado La Guancha">La Guancha boardwalk</a> and is accessible by private boat. Starting in the summer of 2018, tourists can also board a ferry for a trip to the island.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The island offers "a beautiful, nice snorkeling, some trails to explore, and a lighthouse."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The ferry trips ceased in January 2020 after <a href="/wiki/2020_Puerto_Rico_earthquakes" class="mw-redirect" title="2020 Puerto Rico earthquakes">a major earthquake</a> struck the area, but restarted six months later.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The island is administered by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Natural_and_Environmental_Resources" title="Puerto Rico Department of Natural and Environmental Resources">Puerto Rico Department of Natural and Environmental Resources</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography_and_climate">Geography and climate</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isla_Cardona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geography and climate"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The island is mostly a low island covered by brushwood.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The climate is dry and the island supports <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_dry_forests" title="Puerto Rican dry forests">dry forest</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Black_rat">Black rat</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isla_Cardona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Black rat"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 2010, the Sociedad Ornitol\xc3\xb3gica Puertorrique\xc3\xb1a (Puerto Rican Bird Society) and the <a href="/wiki/Club_N%C3%A1utico_de_Ponce" title="Club N\xc3\xa1utico de Ponce">Ponce Yacht and Fishing Club</a> launched a campaign to eradicate the <a href="/wiki/Black_rat" title="Black rat">black rat</a> from the island.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span
