Cristo</b></i> (Chapel of Christ), also called <b><span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Capilla del Santo Cristo de la Salud</i></span></b> is a small chapel / museum located in the Old <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Van_Atten_2015_p._60_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Van_Atten_2015_p._60-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Built in the 18th century and saved from demolition in the 20th century and preserved, the structure has become a <a href="/wiki/Cultural_icon" title="Cultural icon">cultural icon</a> of Puerto Rico. Most of the articles located at its altar are from 1753.<sup id="cite_ref-icono_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icono-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NPS_Pabon_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NPS_Pabon-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Travel guides list <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Capilla del Cristo</i></span> as one of the must-see places of Old San Juan.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a Its altar is made of silver and gold.\n</p><p>The St. Juan and St. Peter races, (<span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Las carreras de S. Juan y S. Pedro</i></span>) is an annual festival that\'s taken place on Cristo Street near Capilla del Cristo since before the mid 19th century.<sup id="cite_ref-El_Gibaro_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-El_Gibaro-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-El_Cancionero_de_Borinquen_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-El_Cancionero_de_Borinquen-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capilla_del_Cristo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Capilla del Cristo is located at the end of Calle del Cristo in the <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan_Historic_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Old San Juan Historic District">Historic District of Old San Juan</a> near <a href="/wiki/La_Fortaleza" title="La Fortaleza">La Fortaleza</a>, the official residence of the governor of Puerto Rico.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Architecture">Architecture</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capilla_del_Cristo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Architecture"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The basic structure of the small temple is <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es"><a href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mamposter%C3%ADa#Mamposter\xc3\xada_ordinaria" class="extiw" title="es:Mamposter\xc3\xada">mamposter\xc3\xada ordinaria</a></i></span> or stones held together by mortar or cement. It\'s a one-story-high, brick and stone structure with a curved belfry atop. Its gate was added in the 1940s for the protection of its interior. It has three oversized arches which open up to the <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Parque de Palomas</i></span>, Tetuan Street, and Calle de Cristo de la Salud (street). Built in the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Baroque_architecture" title="Spanish Baroque architecture">Spanish Baroque</a> style, <a href="/wiki/Extremadura" title="Extremadura">Extremaduran</a> Juan Francisco Mestre was the author of its design.<sup id="cite_ref-NPS_Pabon_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NPS_Pabon-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The building has been kept in good condition by the volunteer group Cristo de la Salud Brotherhood.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Folklore">Folklore</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capilla_del_Cristo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Folklore"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Religious folklore, particularly among Catholics, said that during a horse race on <i>La Calle de Cristo</i>, a young rider and his horse took a bad fall. The rider went over the precipice but was miraculously saved.<sup id="cite_ref-NPS_Pabon_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NPS_Pabon-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The chapel was built where the fateful race was said to have occurred in honor of the young rider named Baltazar Monta\xc3\xb1ez.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It has become both a tourist attraction as well as a stop for religious pilgrims, who occasionally leave a <a href="/wiki/Votive" class="mw-redirect" title="Votive">religious votive</a> at the chapel. It is only open on Tuesdays.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n</p><p>Different versions of the legend mention that either the rider or Tomas Mateo Pratts, an observer yelled for divine intervention.<sup id="cite_ref-icono_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icono-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Santos_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Santos-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In a book about Puerto Rican legends, Jos\xc3\xa9 Ramirez-Rivera writes that the horse was killed but Baltazar lived. Afterwards, permission was granted to build the Catholic chapel and festivals were held for years afterward to celebrate the miracle.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In his writings about Baltazar, Puerto Rican historian <a href="/wiki/Cayetano_Coll_y_Toste" title="Cayetano Coll y Toste">Cayetano Coll y Toste</a> described him as a slave who worked in the sugar cane fields of Puerto Rico but made no mention of the legendary accident.<sup id="cite_ref-Halloran_American_Literatures_Initiative_2009_p._25_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Halloran_American_Literatures_Initiative_2009_p._25-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Deane_2011_p._228_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Deane_2011_p._228-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Gallery">Gallery</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Capilla_del_Cristo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Gallery"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<ul class="gallery mw-gallery-packed">\n\t\t<li class="gallerybox" style="width: 98.666666666667px">\n\t\t\t<div class="thumb" style="width: 96.666666666667px;"><span typeof="mw:File"><a href="/wiki/File:San_Juan_-_Capilla_del_Cristo.jpg" class="mw-file-description" title="Capilla del Cristo in 1915.
