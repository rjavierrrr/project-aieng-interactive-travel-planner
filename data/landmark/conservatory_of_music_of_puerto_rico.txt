colspan="2">\n</th></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Puerto Rico Conservatory of Music</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Conservatorio de M\xc3\xbasica de Puerto Rico</i>) is a public <a href="/wiki/Music_school" title="Music school">conservatory</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  It has hosted a number of international musicians as students as well as faculty, and has a longstanding relationship with the classical music movement in Puerto Rico, including the annual <a href="/wiki/Casals_Festival" title="Casals Festival">Casals Festival</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Symphony_Orchestra" title="Puerto Rico Symphony Orchestra">Puerto Rico Symphony Orchestra</a> (PRSO).\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conservatory_of_Music_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Following the success of the Casals Festival held in San Juan in 1957, state legislator <a href="/wiki/Ernesto_Ramos_Antonini" title="Ernesto Ramos Antonini">Ernesto Ramos Antonini</a> proposed several laws which would create the Puerto Rico Symphony Orchestra that same year, and the Conservatory of Music in June 1959. The conservatory was originally envisioned as a school for preparing musicians for the PRSO and for preparing music teachers for the state public education system. Throughout the years, however, the conservatory has become a musical landmark in the Caribbean, providing advanced academic studies in various music areas to local students as well as international visitors.\n</p><p>Since its inception, the conservatory has been under the administration of state government agencies, including the Puerto Rico Industrial Development Company (<i>Compa\xc3\xb1\xc3\xada de Fomento Econ\xc3\xb3mico</i>), the Administration for the Encouragement of Arts and Culture (<i>Administraci\xc3\xb3n para el Fomento de las Artes y Cultura</i>), and the Musical Arts Corporation (<i>Corporaci\xc3\xb3n de las Artes Musicales</i>, or CAM). It remained a part of the CAM until 1995 when a state law granted it fiscal and administrative autonomy. Since then, the conservatory has been autonomously run by a <a href="/wiki/Board_of_trustees" class="mw-redirect" title="Board of trustees">board of trustees</a> appointed by the Governor and confirmed by the Senate.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Academics">Academics</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conservatory_of_Music_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Academics"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The conservatory offers a variety of post-secondary degrees in music, the post-graduate program offers <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor&#39;s degree">bachelor\'s degrees</a> in:<a href="/wiki/Musical_composition" title="Musical composition">musical composition</a>, <a href="/wiki/Music_education" title="Music education">music education</a>, <a href="/wiki/Voice" class="mw-redirect" title="Voice">voice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Classical_guitar" title="Classical guitar">classical guitar</a>, symphony instruments, <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Caribbean music">Caribbean music</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Piano" title="Piano">piano</a>. The graduate program consists of: <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master&#39;s degree">master\'s degrees</a> in <a href="/wiki/Music_education" title="Music education">music education</a>, <a href="/wiki/Voice" class="mw-redirect" title="Voice">voice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Classical_guitar" title="Classical guitar">classical guitar</a>, symphony instruments and <a href="/wiki/Piano" title="Piano">piano</a>, as well as artists <a href="/wiki/Diploma" title="Diploma">diplomas</a> in <a href="/wiki/Voice" class="mw-redirect" title="Voice">voice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Classical_guitar" title="Classical guitar">classical guitar</a>, symphony instruments, <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Caribbean music">Caribbean music</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Piano" title="Piano">piano</a>. It also offers <a href="/wiki/Continuing_education" title="Continuing education">continuing education</a> programs, and teacher certifications. In addition to its regular academic programs, it organizes various international student exchange programs with music schools all around the world.\n</p><p>The Conservatory\'s Preparatory School offers programs for children, teenagers and adults with the purpose of encouraging music appreciation within the local community. These programs do not offer degrees or certifications, but provide basic to near expert level teaching to regular citizens who are not in its formal academic program.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading
