Refuge</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Vieques National Wildlife Refuge</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Refugio nacional de vida silvestre de Vieques</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/National_Wildlife_Refuge" title="National Wildlife Refuge">National Wildlife Refuge</a> on the island of <a href="/wiki/Vieques,_Puerto_Rico" title="Vieques, Puerto Rico">Vieques</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> archipelago.  It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Islands_National_Wildlife_Refuge_Complex" title="Caribbean Islands National Wildlife Refuge Complex">Caribbean Islands National Wildlife Refuge Complex</a>.\n</p><p>The refuge contains several ecologically distinct habitats including; beaches, coastal <a href="/wiki/Lagoon" title="Lagoon">lagoons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mangrove" title="Mangrove">mangrove</a> <a href="/wiki/Wetland" title="Wetland">wetlands</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Highland_(geography)" class="mw-redirect" title="Highland (geography)">upland</a> forested areas. Some of the best examples of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_dry_forests" title="Puerto Rican dry forests">sub-tropical dry forest</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> can be found on refuge lands.\n</p><p>The marine environment surrounding the refuge contains <a href="/wiki/Coral_reef" title="Coral reef">coral reefs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sea_grass" class="mw-redirect" title="Sea grass">sea grass</a> beds. The refuge and its surrounding waters are home to at least two plants and eight animals on the <a href="/wiki/Endangered_Species_Act" class="mw-redirect" title="Endangered Species Act">Federal endangered species list</a> including; the <a href="/wiki/West_Indian_manatee" title="West Indian manatee">Antillean manatee</a> (<i>Trichechus manatus manatus</i>), the <a href="/wiki/Brown_pelican" title="Brown pelican">brown pelican</a> (<i>Pelecanus occidentalis</i>), and four species of <a href="/wiki/Sea_turtle" title="Sea turtle">sea turtles</a>.\n</p><p>In addition to its ecological value, the refuge contains important resources of archeological and historic significance, a legacy of the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Ta\xc3\xadno people">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> culture and <a href="/wiki/Sugarcane" title="Sugarcane">sugarcane</a> era.\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-halign-none" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Coral_Reef_Ecosystem_of_PR.JPG"
