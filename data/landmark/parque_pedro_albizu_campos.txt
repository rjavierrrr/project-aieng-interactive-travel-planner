Entrance free.</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Parque Pedro Albizu Campos</b> (English: <i>Pedro Albizu Campos Park</i>) is a passive <a href="/wiki/Recreational" class="mw-redirect" title="Recreational">recreational</a> <a href="/wiki/Park" title="Park">park</a> in the city of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>.  It was dedicated on 12 September 1991, to the memory of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican people">Puerto Rican</a> <a href="/wiki/Nationalist" class="mw-redirect" title="Nationalist">Nationalist</a> leader <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Albizu_Campos" title="Pedro Albizu Campos">Pedro Albizu Campos</a> by the Government of the Autonomous Municipality of Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  Dr. Pedro Albizu Campos was born in Ponce on 12 September 1891.  He was raised in Ponce and lived most of his life there as well.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_Pedro_Albizu_Campos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The park is located in sector Tenerias of Barrio <a href="/wiki/Machuelo_Abajo" title="Machuelo Abajo">Machuelo Abajo</a>, at the location where the house stood where Albizu Campos spent much of his youth. It sits on a 5-<a href="/wiki/Acre" title="Acre">acre</a> lot bordered by <a href="/wiki/Rio_Bucana" class="mw-redirect" title="Rio Bucana">Rio Bucana</a> on the east end, Puerto Rico route <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_1" title="Puerto Rico Highway 1">PR-1</a> on the south, Pedregal Street on the north, and Dr. Pedro Albizu Campos street on the west.  It is near the intersection of Ponce By-pass Road (<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_2" title="Puerto Rico Highway 2">PR-2</a>), Miguel Pou Boulevard (<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_1" title="Puerto Rico Highway 1">PR-1</a>), and Comercio Street (<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_133" title="Puerto Rico Highway 133">PR-133</a>).  The park is found at N 18.00999 W 66.59377 (18.00999, -66.59377). \n</p><p>There is a <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Albizu_Campos_(statue)" title="Pedro Albizu Campos (statue)">full-body statue of Dr. Albizu Campos</a> as the centerpiece of the park.<sup id="cite_ref-auto1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The statue is the work of Ponce sculptor Rafael Lopez del Campo.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The park was built in response to the initiative of the group <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce%C3%B1os_de_Verdad&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ponce\xc3\xb1os de Verdad (page does not exist)">Ponce\xc3\xb1os de Verdad</a> and the support of the general public.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Use">Use</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_Pedro_Albizu_Campos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Use"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Though a general use passive park, the park is also home to various civic and political activities relating to the ideals of autonomy, self-government and independence for Puerto Rico.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Stones">Stones</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_Pedro_Albizu_Campos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Stones"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Among the features of the park is a set of twelve stones set on the ground in a two-by-six vertical formation. The stones contain the names of the last twelve known survivors of the 1937 <a href="/wiki/Ponce_massacre" title="Ponce massacre">Ponce massacre</a>. In addition to the name the survivor, the person\'s date of death is also engraved on each stone. Also engraved are a <a href="/wiki/Christian_cross" title="Christian cross">Christian cross</a> and the letters "<a href="/wiki/Rest_in_peace" title="Rest in peace">RIP</a>" (rest in peace). \n</p><p>The names and dates on some of the stones have faded away due to exposure to the weather elements. The names and dates on the twelve stones are as follows:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Rafael Soto Moreno,\t\t21-6-74</li>\n<li>Samuel Aponte Morales,\t23-10-(77?)</li>\n<li>Pedro L. del Valle Torres,\tDic-2-79</li>\n<li>Nelson Cede\xc3\xb1o Ramos,\t\t25-10-82</li></ul>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="See_also">See also</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
