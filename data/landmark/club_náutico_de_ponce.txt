2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="768" /></a><figcaption>Club\'s emblem.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The <b>Club N\xc3\xa1utico de Ponce</b> (<i>Ponce Yacht and Fishing Club</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Yacht_club" title="Yacht club">yacht club</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>. The club is located contiguously on <a href="/wiki/Gatas_(Ponce)" title="Gatas (Ponce)">Isla de Gatas</a>, near the <a href="/wiki/Paseo_Tablado_La_Guancha" title="Paseo Tablado La Guancha">La Guancha</a> recreational and cultural area, and next to the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Ponce" class="mw-redirect" title="Port of Ponce">Port of Ponce</a>.\n</p><p>The complex consists of two restaurants, a private beach, a gym, a running track, two pools, various basketball, tennis, and volleyball courts, and a <a href="/wiki/Marina" title="Marina">marina</a>.  The club also runs a sailing school for children.  Founded in 1941 by a group of nautical enthusiasts from Ponce to promote water and fishing sports, the marina is one of Puerto Rico\'s oldest.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Club_N%C3%A1utico_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Ponce_Marina.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Ponce_Marina.jpg/260px-Ponce_Marina.jpg" decoding="async" width="260" height="208" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Ponce_Marina.jpg/390px-Ponce_Marina.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Ponce_Marina.jpg/520px-Ponce_Marina.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2300" data-file-height="1840" /></a><figcaption>Club N\xc3\xa1utico de Ponce looking southwest</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The idea of building a Club in Isla de Gatas began in 1935 by seven men, among them <a href="/wiki/Juan_Serrall%C3%A9s_Col%C3%B3n" title="Juan Serrall\xc3\xa9s Col\xc3\xb3n">Pedro Juan Serralles Galiano</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-allatsea_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allatsea-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  At that time the island had an area of approximately 1-acre (0.40&#160;ha) and people had to take a small boat to reach it.  The clubhouse was finished in 1938, and over the years the original 1 acre was expanded to 13 acres (5.3&#160;ha), in part due to club member Tito Castro.  Mr. Castro owned a construction company and the surplus rocks that were removed from various road and construction projects that he contracted for were deposited around Isla de Gatas and eventually filled the shallow surrounding it.<sup id="cite_ref-allatsea_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allatsea-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Isla de Gatas is connected to the mainland by a dike at Punta Carenero.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In later years the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers built a <a href="/wiki/Breakwater_(structure)" title="Breakwater (structure)">breakwater</a> protecting the area.<sup id="cite_ref-allatsea_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allatsea-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  This, and the Tito Castro land reclamations, made it possible to build a road that joined the island to the mainland.<sup id="cite_ref-allatsea_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allatsea-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>During World War II the Club was used by the U.S. Navy who had five PT Boats stationed there.<sup id="cite_ref-allatsea_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allatsea-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  Since 1946 membership has grown from 100 people to over 900 families in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-allatsea_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allatsea-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The original two-story clubhouse is now a restaurant called "<i>El Comodoro</i>" (<a href="/wiki/Commodore_(United_States)" title="Commodore (United States)">The Commodore</a>). Club N\xc3\xa1utico has room for 250 boat slips.<sup id="cite_ref-allatsea_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allatsea-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span
