Resort</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>San Juan Marriott Resort &amp; Stellaris Casino</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Hotel" title="Hotel">hotel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Casino" title="Casino">casino</a> located on the beach in <a href="/wiki/Condado_(Santurce)" title="Condado (Santurce)">Condado</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. It is operated by <a href="/wiki/Marriott_International" title="Marriott International">Marriott International</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Juan_Marriott_Resort_%26_Stellaris_Casino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:San_Juan_-_Puerto_Rico_Sheraton.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/San_Juan_-_Puerto_Rico_Sheraton.jpg/220px-San_Juan_-_Puerto_Rico_Sheraton.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/San_Juan_-_Puerto_Rico_Sheraton.jpg/330px-San_Juan_-_Puerto_Rico_Sheraton.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/San_Juan_-_Puerto_Rico_Sheraton.jpg/440px-San_Juan_-_Puerto_Rico_Sheraton.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3517" data-file-height="2320" /></a><figcaption>Puerto Rico-Sheraton Hotel, 1960s</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The hotel was designed by architects <a href="/wiki/Toro_Ferrer" title="Toro Ferrer">Osvaldo L. Toro and Miguel Ferrer</a> and opened on October 4, 1963 as the <b>Puerto Rico-Sheraton Hotel</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Directly on the beach in <a href="/wiki/Condado_(Santurce)" title="Condado (Santurce)">Condado</a>, it boasted an Olympic-sized <a href="/wiki/Swimming_pool" title="Swimming pool">swimming pool</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  Sheraton sold the property to the San Juan Dupont Plaza Corp. of Delaware<sup id="cite_ref-auto_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> in 1979<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and it was renamed the <b>Dupont Plaza Hotel</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On December 31, 1986, the Dupont Plaza was the site of <a href="/wiki/Dupont_Plaza_Hotel_fire" class="mw-redirect" title="Dupont Plaza Hotel fire">the second deadliest hotel fire in U.S. history</a>, started by disgruntled employees in the middle of a labor/salary dispute. The fire claimed 97 lives and left 140 people injured. The San Juan Dupont Plaza Corp renovated the hotel in 1988, at a cost of $9 million, and renamed it the <b>Palm Hotel and Casino</b>. A reopening was announced for early 1989, but it was cancelled on December 19, 1988, when the Puerto Rican government refused to grant the hotel an operating permit, because the owners had only installed sprinklers in the hotel\'s first three floors, and not throughout the entire building.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n</p><p>A massive federal trial over the fire began in San Juan on March 15, 1989. It was one of the largest personal damages trials in US history up to that point, with $1.7 billion sought by 2,300 plaintiffs from 250 defendants, including a maze of corporations and subsidiaries involved in the property\'s ownership.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/American_International_Group" title="American International Group">AIG</a>, a lead insurance underwriter supplying coverage for the blaze, ended up acquiring title to the shuttered hotel in June 1989, as part of the settlement of claims arising from the fire.<sup id="cite_ref-auto1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In October 1992, AIG announced plans to completely renovate the hotel at a cost of $130 million<sup id="cite_ref-auto1_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and rebrand it as a Marriott.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The hotel reopened on February 16, 1995, as the <b>San Juan Marriott Resort &amp; Casino</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was later renamed slightly, becoming the <b>San Juan Marriott Resort &amp; Stellaris Casino</b>. AIG<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> sold the hotel to Rockwood Capital in August 2011 for $133 million.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Rockwood Capital resold it in May 2017 to an unnamed Chinese buyer<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> for $184 million.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Facilities">Facilities</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Juan_Marriott_Resort_%26_Stellaris_Casino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Facilities"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The San Juan Marriott has 525 hotel rooms and a casino. In addition, the hotel has 8 meeting rooms with a total of 10,900&#160;sq&#160;ft (1,010&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) of meeting space.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="See_also">See
