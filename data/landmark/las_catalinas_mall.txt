located in <a href="/wiki/Caguas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Caguas, Puerto Rico">Caguas, Puerto Rico</a>. The mall opened in 1997, and is currently anchored by a Sector Sixty6 entertainment center which takes up the space of a previous <a href="/wiki/Big_Kmart" class="mw-redirect" title="Big Kmart">Big Kmart</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Forever_21" title="Forever 21">Forever 21</a> junior anchor store, and a <a href="/wiki/Ross_Stores" title="Ross Stores">Ross Dress for Less</a> which is scheduled to open in 2025 in the former Sears store at the mall which closed their doors in February 2021,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The Shops at Caguas are home to the first "large premium format" <a href="/wiki/Movie_theater" title="Movie theater">movie theater</a> in Puerto Rico (and second in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> region, at the time of its opening), currently owned and managed by <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Cinemas" title="Caribbean Cinemas">Caribbean Cinemas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shops_at_Caguas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Origins">Origins</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shops_at_Caguas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The mall\'s previous name of <i>Las Catalinas</i> is an homage to the 19th century, when the current shopping center property was the site of a <a href="/wiki/Sugarcane" title="Sugarcane">sugarcane</a> plantation, <i>Hacienda Santa Catalina</i>. In addition to sugar production, and left with copious amounts of leftover post-processing sugarcane, Hacienda Santa Catalina also produced <a href="/wiki/Rum" title="Rum">rum</a>, as well as cultivated many fruits.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="1990s-2000s">1990s-2000s</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shops_at_Caguas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1990s-2000s"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In April 1997, <a href="/wiki/Vornado_Realty_Trust" title="Vornado Realty Trust">Vornado Realty Trust</a> announced that it would acquire Kmart\'s 50% interest in the then-Caguas Centrum, as the center was previously known, which was currently under construction. The name of the mall would then be changed to Las Catalinas.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On December 4, 1997, after development by Manley Berenson and Yebba Realty Ventures, Las Catalinas Mall officially opened with 80 stores and 3,000 parking spaces. It was anchored at the time by a <a href="/wiki/Kmart" title="Kmart">Kmart Super Center</a> and a Sears department store. It also featured a <a href="/wiki/Food_court" title="Food court">food court</a>, \xe2\x80\x9cEl Trapiche.\xe2\x80\x9d The mall was built with the purpose to serve the surrounding communities, but its variety of businesses lead it to become a regionally popular destination.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On November 4, 1998, <a href="/wiki/Vornado_Realty_Trust" title="Vornado Realty Trust">Vornado Realty Trust</a> bought Kmart\'s 50% interest in the mall, forming a joint ownership between Vornado and Yebba Realty Ventures. In addition, Vornado acquired 75% and Yebba Realty Ventures acquired 25% of Kmart\'s anchor store. Vornado\'s total purchase price was about $38 million.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>By the year 2000, the successful mall had over 100<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> tenants, including many popular businesses of American origin, such as <a href="/wiki/5-7-9" title="5-7-9">5-7-9</a>, <a href="/wiki/Afterthoughts_(retailer)" title="Afterthoughts (retailer)">Afterthoughts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Claire%27s" title="Claire&#39;s">Claire\'s Boutique</a>, <a href="/wiki/EB_Games" title="EB Games">Electronics Boutique</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foot_Locker" title="Foot Locker">Footaction</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hallmark_Cards" title="Hallmark Cards">Hallmark</a>, <a href="/wiki/KB_Toys" title="KB Toys">KB Toys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Payless_(footwear_retailer)" title="Payless (footwear retailer)">Payless ShoeSource</a>, <a href="/wiki/RadioShack" title="RadioShack">RadioShack</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zale_Corporation" title="Zale Corporation">Zales</a>, and many others. The food court and restaurant options included <a href="/wiki/El_Mes%C3%B3n_S%C3%A1ndwiches" title="El Mes\xc3\xb3n S\xc3\xa1ndwiches">El Mes\xc3\xb3n</a>, Hot Potato, <a href="/wiki/Howard_Johnson%27s" title="Howard Johnson&#39;s">Howard Johnson\'s</a>, <a href="/wiki/KFC" title="KFC">KFC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quiznos" title="Quiznos">Quiznos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taco_Bell" title="Taco Bell">Taco Bell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wetzel%27s_Pretzels" title="Wetzel&#39;s Pretzels">Wetzel\'s Pretzels</a>, and more.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Also in the early 2000s, additional land was developed to open <a href="/wiki/The_Home_Depot" class="mw-redirect" title="The Home Depot">The Home Depot</a> home improvement store and a <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Cinemas" title="Caribbean Cinemas">Caribbean Cinemas</a> theater. These additions complimented the mall\'s variety, and would help to enhance the status of Las Catalinas as a top regional shopping destination.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In December 2001, <a href="/wiki/Amigo_Supermarkets" title="Amigo Supermarkets">Amigo Supermarkets</a> announced that they would be opening a location at the mall by summer 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On September 23, 2002, <a href="/wiki/Vornado_Realty_Trust" title="Vornado Realty Trust">Vornado Realty Trust</a> increased its interest in Las Catalinas to 100% by acquiring the other 50% of the mall from Yebba Realty Ventures and the 25% from the Kmart anchor store. The purchase price was approximately $48 million.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="2010s-present">2010s-present</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shops_at_Caguas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010s-present"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 2010, an <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a> store and an iShop opened stores in the mall.\n</p><p>In April 2012, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Best_Buy" title="Best Buy">Best Buy</a>, which was located on the premises of the mall, would be closing by mid-May 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In January 2015, <a href="/wiki/Vornado_Realty_Trust" title="Vornado Realty Trust">Vornado Realty Trust</a> announced they would subsume some of their properties, including Las Catalinas, into a new company, <i>Urban Edge Properties</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 2015, <a href="/wiki/Sears_Holdings" title="Sears Holdings">Sears Holdings</a> reconfigured 235 of their properties, including the store at Las Catalinas, into <i>Seritage Growth Properties</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 2015, a 13,773\xc2\xb2-foot <a href="/wiki/Forever_21" title="Forever 21">Forever 21</a> clothing store opened at the mall, the chain\'s fifth store on the island, at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On November 8, 2018, Sears Holdings announced that Kmart would be closing as part of a company-wide plan to shutter around 40 stores in the U.S. and P.R. The store officially closed on January 31, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On December 28, 2020, it was announced that Sears would also be closing in February 2021 as part of a company-wide plan to close 23 stores nationwide, leaving the mall temporarily with no anchor tenants.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In February 2021, it was announced that a 22,000\xc2\xb2-foot Coast Guard Exchange (CGX) would be opening in the former <a href="/wiki/The_ODP_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="The ODP Corporation">Office Depot</a> space at the mall, scheduled to open by summer of that year.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In August 2021, it was announced that a Sector Sixty6 Entertainment Center and a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Corral" title="Golden Corral">Golden Corral</a> restaurant would replace the former Kmart store. They were expected to open by 2022, but were delayed until summer 2023.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In August 2022, a 1,916\xc2\xb2-foot <a href="/wiki/Tous_(company)" title="Tous (company)">Tous</a> store opened at the mall.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In June 2023, it was announced that both a 2,109\xc2\xb2-foot <a href="/wiki/Hot_Topic" title="Hot Topic">Hot Topic</a> store, and a 5,520\xc2\xb2-foot <a href="/wiki/Puma_(brand)" title="Puma (brand)">Puma</a> store would be coming to the mall. <a href="/wiki/Hot_Topic" title="Hot Topic">Hot Topic</a> would open that month, being the chains 7th store on the island.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On August 21, 2023, the Golden Corral restaurant would open to much fanfare.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On October 25, 2023, Sector Sixty6, an entertainment center, taking up the space of the former Kmart at the mall, would officially debut at a cost of $22 million.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On November 9, 2023, it was announced by Urban Edge Properties (owners of Las Catalinas Mall) that, after 26 years, the center would be changing its
