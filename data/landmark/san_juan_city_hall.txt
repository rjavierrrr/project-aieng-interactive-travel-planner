colspan="2">\n</th></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>San Juan City Hall</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Casa de la Alcald\xc3\xada de San Juan</i>, or <i>Alcald\xc3\xada de San Juan</i>) is the seat of government for the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">Autonomous Municipality of San Juan</a> in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The city hall is located in Old San Juan, in front of the <a href="/wiki/Plaza_de_Armas,_San_Juan" title="Plaza de Armas, San Juan">Plaza de Armas</a> and next to the <i>Antiguo Palacio de la Real Intendencia (Palace of the Royal Intendancy)</i>, which today hosts the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_State" title="Puerto Rico Department of State">Puerto Rico Department of State</a>. \n</p><p>The San Juan City Hall was built in stages from 1604 to 1789. It went through numerous additions and expansions throughout its history. Its current structure and fa\xc3\xa7ade by the square, inspired by the <a href="/wiki/Casa_de_la_Panader%C3%ADa" title="Casa de la Panader\xc3\xada">Casa de la Panader\xc3\xada</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Mayor,_Madrid" title="Plaza Mayor, Madrid">Plaza Mayor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, completed in 1840 and was designed by architect Pedro Garc\xc3\xada.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Features">Features</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Juan_City_Hall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Features"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:San_Juan-_City_Hall_(1906).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/San_Juan-_City_Hall_%281906%29.jpg/220px-San_Juan-_City_Hall_%281906%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="137" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/San_Juan-_City_Hall_%281906%29.jpg/330px-San_Juan-_City_Hall_%281906%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/San_Juan-_City_Hall_%281906%29.jpg/440px-San_Juan-_City_Hall_%281906%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1421" data-file-height="883" /></a><figcaption>San Juan City Hall (1906)</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The city hall building has a large staircase and an inner courtyard which is open to visitors. The Plaza de Armas main entrance has an inscription in Latin which translates to "Esteem the light of justice whoever is at the service of their people". The first floor of the main building hosts tourism offices while the second floor houses the office of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_mayors_of_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="List of mayors of San Juan, Puerto Rico">Mayor of San Juan</a> and the seat of municipal government.\n</p><p>One of its most famous rooms is the <i>Sala Capitular</i> (Spanish for <a href="/wiki/Chapter_house" title="Chapter house">chapter hall</a>), a meeting hall that has hosted numerous historical events, such as the proclamation of Admiral <a href="/wiki/Ram%C3%B3n_Power_y_Giralt" title="Ram\xc3\xb3n Power y Giralt">Ram\xc3\xb3n Power y Giralt</a> as the first representative for Puerto Rico at the <a href="/wiki/Cortes_of_C%C3%A1diz" title="Cortes of C\xc3\xa1diz">Cortes de C\xc3\xa1diz</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spain</a> in 1812 and the signing of the abolition of <a href="/wiki/Slavery_in_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Slavery in Puerto Rico">slavery in Puerto Rico</a> in 1873.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The city hall also hosts the San Juan Bautista Gallery, which often showcases works of art made by local artists.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Gallery">Gallery</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
