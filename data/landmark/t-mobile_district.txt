website</a></span></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Distrito T-Mobile</b> (initially developed as <b>District Live!</b>) is an entertainment and retail district complex located adjacent to the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Convention_Center" title="Puerto Rico Convention Center">Puerto Rico Convention Center</a>, part of a redevelopment of the Convention Center area that initially had opened in 2005. Distrito T-Mobile is 476,000 square feet complex of entertainment, dining, and shopping. It has 10 eateries, an 8-screen movie theater featuring IMAX, a 177 room Hotel, 2 Live Music venues, an outdoor performance plaza and an urban park featuring a zip line and a maze.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Distrito_T-Mobile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Initially known as District Live!, Distrito T-Mobile is part of the planned <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1238216509">.mw-parser-output .vanchor>:target~.vanchor-text{background-color:#b1d2ff}@media screen{html.skin-theme-clientpref-night .mw-parser-output .vanchor>:target~.vanchor-text{background-color:#0f4dc9}}@media screen and (prefers-color-scheme:dark){html.skin-theme-clientpref-os .mw-parser-output .vanchor>:target~.vanchor-text{background-color:#0f4dc9}}</style><span class="vanchor"><span id="Puerto_Rico_Convention_Center_District"></span><span class="vanchor-text">Puerto Rico Convention Center District</span></span> (master planned by <a href="/wiki/Sasaki_Associates" class="mw-redirect" title="Sasaki Associates">Sasaki Associates</a>). The main project was the <a href="/wiki/Aloft_Hotels" title="Aloft Hotels">Aloft hotel</a> with 177 rooms, and a music hall which can seat 6,000 visitors, as well as a <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Cinemas" title="Caribbean Cinemas">Caribbean Cinemas</a> movie theater.<sup id="cite_ref-for_business_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-for_business-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Puerto Rican government selected <a href="/wiki/Starwood_Hotels_and_Resorts" title="Starwood Hotels and Resorts">Starwood</a>, under its <a href="/wiki/Sheraton_Hotels_and_Resorts" title="Sheraton Hotels and Resorts">Sheraton</a> flag, to operate the reported $209 million facility, known as the <i>Sheraton Puerto Rico Hotel &amp; Casino</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> With these two hotels and others in the area, the district now has 1,762 hotel rooms available for visitors.<sup id="cite_ref-hotel_business_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hotel_business-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The <i>District</i> came as part of a plan by the Puerto Rican Government to develop the areas surrounding the Puerto Rico Convention Center and add more tourist attractions that can bring visitors regularly to the area. The original plan was announced in 2016 by then governor <a href="/wiki/Alejandro_Garcia_Padilla" class="mw-redirect" title="Alejandro Garcia Padilla">Alejandro Garcia Padilla</a> who hailed it as a <a href="/wiki/Public%E2%80%93private_partnership" title="Public\xe2\x80\x93private partnership">public-private partnership</a> where the government would give access to the land but the developing of the facilities would fall completely on private hands, while the Puerto Rican Government kept a 30% share on the project. Construction began that very same year with an expected opening date of 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In March 2020, wireless carrier <a href="/wiki/T-Mobile_US" title="T-Mobile US">T-Mobile US</a> reached a 10-year <a href="/wiki/Naming_rights" title="Naming rights">naming rights</a> deal, naming the complex Distrito T-Mobile. The agreement includes the provision of phone charging stations, and special benefits for T-Mobile subscribers (such as reserved parking spaces and other offers). It is the company\'s first naming rights agreement outside of the <a href="/wiki/Contiguous_United_States" title="Contiguous United States">continental United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The district held its grand opening in August 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Distrito T-Mobile hosted a <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Eve" title="New Year&#39;s Eve">New Year\'s Eve</a> event on December 31, 2021, which was expected to attract at least 10,000 spectators, and feature a concert by <a href="/wiki/Daddy_Yankee" title="Daddy Yankee">Daddy Yankee</a>. The event was covered by <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>\'s New Year\'s special <i><a href="/wiki/Dick_Clark%27s_New_Year%27s_Rockin%27_Eve" title="Dick Clark&#39;s New Year&#39;s Rockin&#39; Eve">Dick Clark\'s New Year\'s Rockin\' Eve</a></i>, marking the special\'s first-ever broadcast from the territory.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Facilities">Facilities</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Distrito_T-Mobile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Facilities"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p><i>Popular Plaza</i> (whose naming rights are owned by <a href="/wiki/Popular,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Popular, Inc.">Banco Popular</a>) serves as one of the main plazas of the district, which features an outdoor stage and multiple restaurants, including the <a href="/wiki/Sports_bar" class="mw-redirect" title="Sports bar">sports bar</a> <i>Arena <a href="/wiki/Medalla_Light" class="mw-redirect" title="Medalla Light">Medalla</a></i>, <i>Barullo Taberna Espa\xc3\xb1ola</i>, <i>La Burges\xc3\xada</i>, <i>La Central by Mario Pag\xc3\xa1n</i>, <i>Denko Asian Eatery</i>, <i>Dulcinea Ice Cream &amp; Churros</i>, <i>Lupe Reyes</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Iv%C3%A1n_Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Iv\xc3\xa1n Rodr\xc3\xadguez">Pudge\'s</a> Pizza</i>, and <i>Saz\xc3\xb3n Comida Criolla</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola_Music_Hall" title="Coca-Cola Music Hall">Coca-Cola Music Hall</a> is an indoor events venue, designed primarily as a concert hall with a capacity of 5,000. The hall is also capable of hosting other types of indoor events.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The district features a <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Cinemas" title="Caribbean Cinemas">Caribbean Cinemas</a> <i>VIP theater</i> with a restaurant and bar, food service, seven <a href="/wiki/IMAX" title="IMAX">IMAX</a> screens with <a href="/wiki/ScreenX" title="ScreenX">ScreenX</a>, and one <i>Caribbean Cinemas Extreme (CXC)</i> screen with a 4K projector and <a href="/wiki/Dolby_Atmos" title="Dolby Atmos">Dolby Atmos</a> sound system.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <i>ToroVerde Urban Park</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Family_entertainment_center" title="Family entertainment center">family entertainment center</a> which features a <a href="/wiki/Food_court" title="Food court">food court</a>, arcade, <a href="/wiki/Virtual_reality" title="Virtual reality">virtual reality</a> experiences, a <a href="/wiki/Climbing_wall" title="Climbing wall">climbing wall</a> (promoted as being the tallest in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Zipline" class="mw-redirect" title="Zipline">ziplines</a> across the district.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>It also features a 177-room <a href="/wiki/Aloft_Hotels" title="Aloft Hotels">Aloft Hotels</a> location, the chain\'s first in the Caribbean.<sup
