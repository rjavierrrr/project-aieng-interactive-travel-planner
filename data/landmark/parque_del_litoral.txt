href="http://www.paseolitoral.com/">http://www.paseolitoral.com/</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Parque Litoral Israel "Shorty" Castro</b> is a park located in the city of <a href="/wiki/Mayag%C3%BCez,_Puerto_Rico" title="Mayag\xc3\xbcez, Puerto Rico">Mayag\xc3\xbcez, Puerto Rico</a>. It is commonly named "Parque <i>del</i> Litoral".\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Features">Features</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_del_Litoral&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Features"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Litoral_Park.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Litoral_Park.jpg/220px-Litoral_Park.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="110" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Litoral_Park.jpg/330px-Litoral_Park.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Litoral_Park.jpg/440px-Litoral_Park.jpg 2x" data-file-width="951" data-file-height="476" /></a><figcaption>Litoral Park</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The park was created after the city was selected to host the <a href="/wiki/2010_Central_American_and_Caribbean_Games" title="2010 Central American and Caribbean Games">2010 Central American and Caribbean Games</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bonnin_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bonnin-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  Of all the construction projects built to prepare the city for the games the park was created with the intention of becoming the main legacy for the city.\n</p><p>The park runs for 2.54&#160;km with the <a href="/wiki/Mayag%C3%BCez_Bay" title="Mayag\xc3\xbcez Bay">Mayag\xc3\xbcez Bay</a> on one side and the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_102" title="Puerto Rico Highway 102">Jos\xc3\xa9 Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Clemente Avenue</a> on the other.  The entire park covers an area of about forty two acres.<sup id="cite_ref-Bonnin_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bonnin-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The park consist of different sections.  In its northern end beginning in the <a href="/wiki/Yag%C3%BCez_River" title="Yag\xc3\xbcez River">Yag\xc3\xbcez River</a> is the "Central American Plaza" and an open-air amphitheater.<sup id="cite_ref-Bonnin_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bonnin-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AFI_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AFI-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  In its southern end the parks meets up with the Parque Infantil del Milenio, and the new <a href="/wiki/Mayag%C3%BCez_Athletics_Stadium" title="Mayag\xc3\xbcez Athletics Stadium">Jos\xc3\xa9 Antonio Figueroa Athletics Stadium</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Isidoro_Garc%C3%ADa_Stadium" title="Isidoro Garc\xc3\xada Stadium">Isidoro Garc\xc3\xada Stadium</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Planning_and_construction">Planning and construction</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_del_Litoral&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Planning and construction"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The park was designed by Bonnin Orozco Architectural Firm and Local Office Landscape Architecture over a year and a half period from January 2006 to July 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-Bonnin_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bonnin-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  Later after winning the public bids for the project, construction began in December 2007 and ended in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-Bonnin_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bonnin-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The project cost approximately $19,429,550 and was financed by the Government of Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-AFI_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AFI-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The project was constructed by <a href="/wiki/Ferrovial" title="Ferrovial">Ferrovial Agroman, S.A.</a> While the structural design was in charge of the engineers Nelson Rodr\xc3\xadguez and Jaime R. Roberts.<sup id="cite_ref-Gill_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gill-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Dedication">Dedication</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_del_Litoral&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Dedication"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Puerto Rican Public Law 186 (2010) honored beloved local comedian and musician <a href="/wiki/Shorty_Castro" title="Shorty Castro">Shorty Castro</a> by naming the newly constructed shoreline park after him.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Awards">Awards</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_del_Litoral&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The park won three awards in 2010 of the XIX Edition of the "Obras Cemex" Award in the categories of Infrastructure, sustainability and coherency with accessibility.<sup id="cite_ref-Gill_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gill-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  It is the first time of this competition that the same project wins both sustainability and coherency with accessibility awards.<sup id="cite_ref-Gill_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gill-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The judges considered many factors including: the water canals that used to be contaminated are now positive element to the area.  Also light contamination was eliminated in consideration to sea turtles and local trees were planned.<sup id="cite_ref-Gill_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gill-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="See_also">See also</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
