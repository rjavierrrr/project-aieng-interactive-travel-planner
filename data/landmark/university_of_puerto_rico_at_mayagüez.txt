Puerto Rico, Mayag\xc3\xbcez Campus</b> (<b>UPRM</b>) or <b>Recinto Universitario de Mayag\xc3\xbcez</b> (<b>RUM</b>) in Spanish (also referred to as <b>Colegio</b> and <b>CAAM</b> in allusion to its former name), is a <a href="/wiki/Public_university" title="Public university">public</a> <a href="/wiki/Land-grant_university" title="Land-grant university">land-grant university</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mayag%C3%BCez,_Puerto_Rico" title="Mayag\xc3\xbcez, Puerto Rico">Mayag\xc3\xbcez, Puerto Rico</a>. UPRM is the second-largest university campus of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico" title="University of Puerto Rico">University of Puerto Rico</a> system. In addition to its status as a land-grant university, it is also a member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Sea_Grant_College_Program" title="National Sea Grant College Program">sea-grant</a> and <a href="/wiki/National_Space_Grant_College_and_Fellowship_Program" title="National Space Grant College and Fellowship Program">space-grant</a> research consortia. In 2009, the <a href="/wiki/Campus" title="Campus">campus</a> population was composed of 12,108 students, 1,924 regular staff members, and 1,037 members of the education staff.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2013, the student population remained relatively steady at 11,838, but the instructional faculty dropped to 684.<sup id="cite_ref-FSSD_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FSSD-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In the second semester of 2019 around 12,166 students were enrolled.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> By the end of the academic year 2022-2023 there were 10,071 students enrolled.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> UPRM has been accredited by the <a href="/wiki/Middle_States_Commission_on_Higher_Education" title="Middle States Commission on Higher Education">Middle States Commission on Higher Education</a> (MSCHE) since 1946.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Mayag%C3%BCez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico" title="University of Puerto Rico">University of Puerto Rico</a> was created by an act of the Legislative Assembly on March 12, 1903, emerging as an outgrowth of the Normal School, which had been established three years earlier to train teachers for the Puerto Rican school system. In 1908, the benefits of the Morill-Nelson Act declared applicable to the island, fostered the rapid growth of the university. Evidence of that growth was the establishment of the College of Liberal Arts at <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Piedras" class="mw-redirect" title="R\xc3\xado Piedras">R\xc3\xado Piedras</a> in 1910 and the College of Agriculture at Mayag\xc3\xbcez in 1911 as a <a href="/wiki/Land-grant_university" title="Land-grant university">land-grant university</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Huellas_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Huellas-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The founding of the institution is credited to D.W. May (Director of the Federal Experiment Station), <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_de_Diego" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 de Diego">Jos\xc3\xa9 de Diego</a> and Carmelo Alemar.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1912 the name of the institution was changed to the College of Agriculture and Mechanic Arts or <i>Colegio de Agricultura y Artes Mec\xc3\xa1nicas</i> (CAAM).<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1918, an earthquake and a fire caused significant damage to the institution. The ruins of the entrance of one of the buildings, <a href="/wiki/Federico_Degetau" title="Federico Degetau">Degetau</a> Hall \xe2\x80\x94 which not only withstood the earthquake, but proved to be almost indestructible after the rebuilt hall was torn down in the late 1950s \xe2\x80\x94 would later become the emblem of the institution. In 1988, the archway (the <a href="/wiki/Portico" title="Portico">portico</a>) was rebuilt, landscaped and turned into a monument.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The campus rectory was located at the <a href="/wiki/Edificio_Jose_de_Diego" class="mw-redirect" title="Edificio Jose de Diego">Jos\xc3\xa9 de Diego building</a>, the oldest standing edification within the campus, built in 1911. The building was inscribed in the National Historic Building Registry as an architectural patrimony. In 1940, a clock tower was built into the main building. In 1993, the building underwent a restoration.<sup id="cite_ref-Huellas_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Huellas-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1929, the <a href="/wiki/Liga_Atl%C3%A9tica_Interuniversitaria" class="mw-redirect" title="Liga Atl\xc3\xa9tica Interuniversitaria">Liga Atl\xc3\xa9tica Interuniversitaria</a> was created at the Mayag\xc3\xbcez campus. The LAI is an athletic organization, similar to the <a href="/wiki/National_Collegiate_Athletic_Association" title="National Collegiate Athletic Association">NCAA</a>, created with the purpose of promoting and regulating sports activities. It originally consisted of a three event competition: basketball, baseball and track and field. Currently the competition consists of more than 25 sports.\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Portico1.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Portico1.jpg/220px-Portico1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Portico1.jpg/330px-Portico1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Portico1.jpg/440px-Portico1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="680" /></a><figcaption>UPRM\'s <a href="/wiki/Portico" title="Portico">portico</a>.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>In 1942 as a result of university reform, the campus was organized with a considerable degree of autonomy into the Colleges of <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">Agriculture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">Engineering</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">Science</a> under the direction of a <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_(education)" title="Chancellor (education)">vice-chancellor</a>. In the 1950s the institution saw more programs added when the College of Arts and Sciences, and the Nuclear Center were established.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_Legislative_Assembly" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican Legislative Assembly">Puerto Rican Legislative Assembly</a> reorganized the University of Puerto Rico\'s system in 1966. This reorganization of the system lead to the controversial change of its name to University of Puerto Rico \xe2\x80\x93 Mayag\xc3\xbcez Campus, which is used in the present day as the official name of the institution.<sup id="cite_ref-Huellas_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Huellas-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2007, 35.4% of all <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">Engineering</a> degrees were granted to women at UPRM, one of the largest percentages in US universities.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The University of Puerto Rico at Mayag\xc3\xbcez is the second largest <a href="/wiki/Hispanic-serving_institution" title="Hispanic-serving institution">Hispanic-serving institution</a> in the United States.\n</p><p>In 2010 the campus went on strike as part of the  <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%932011_University_of_Puerto_Rico_strikes" title="2010\xe2\x80\x932011 University of Puerto Rico strikes">2010\xe2\x80\x932011 University of Puerto Rico strikes</a>. In 2017 in response to budget cuts to the university system by the <a href="/wiki/Financial_Oversight_and_Management_Board_for_Puerto_Rico" title="Financial Oversight and Management Board for Puerto Rico">Financial Oversight and Management Board for Puerto Rico</a> the campus students voted to join the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico_strikes,_2017" title="University of Puerto Rico strikes, 2017">University of Puerto Rico strikes, 2017</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Organization">Organization</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Mayag%C3%BCez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Organization"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>UPRM consists of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=UPRM_College_of_Agricultural_Sciences&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="UPRM College of Agricultural Sciences (page does not exist)">Colleges of Agricultural Sciences</a>, Arts and Sciences, Business Administration, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico#College_of_Engineering" title="University of Puerto Rico">Engineering</a>, and the Division of Continuing Education and Professional Studies. The College of Agricultural Sciences includes the Agricultural Experiment Station.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Administrative_board">Administrative board</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Mayag%C3%BCez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Administrative board"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Administrative board of the Mayag\xc3\xbcez Campus consists of the <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_(education)" title="Chancellor (education)">chancellor</a> as presiding officer, the <a href="/wiki/Dean_(education)" title="Dean (education)">deans</a>, two academic senators elected among those faculty members of the senate who are not <i>ex officio</i>, senators, and an elected student representative. The president of the university serves as an <i>ex officio</i> member. The board acts as an advisory body to the chancellor, prepares the development plan of the campus, approves the proposed budget prepared by the chancellor, and grants tenure, promotions and leaves of absence.\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Administracion.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Administracion.jpg/220px-Administracion.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Administracion.jpg/330px-Administracion.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Administracion.jpg/440px-Administracion.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="1067" /></a><figcaption>UPRM <a href="/wiki/Edificio_Jos%C3%A9_de_Diego" title="Edificio Jos\xc3\xa9 de Diego">Central Administration Building</a>.</figcaption></figure>\n<table class="wikitable">\n\n<tbody><tr style="background:#eee;">\n<th>\n</th>\n<th>Dean\n</th>\n<th>Years\n</th></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>1\n</td>\n<td>Frank Lincoln Stevens\n</td>\n<td>1911 -\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>2\n</td>\n<td>Ralph Stillman Garwood\n</td>\n<td>\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>3\n</td>\n<td>Charles E. The academic senate is the official forum of the academic community. As of spring 2023, the campus had 634 instructional faculty (585 full-time and 49 part-time), which includes those who are tenured, on tenure-track, and not on tenure-track. In terms of student enrollment, a total of 10,071 students were enrolled (9,251 undergraduates and 820 graduates).<sup id="cite_ref-FSSD_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FSSD-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The Mayag\xc3\xbcez campus is one of two land-grant universities in the tropics and the only one where Spanish is the native language (although English is also used extensively). The academic calendar has two semesters: fall (early August to mid-December) and spring (early January to mid-May). While textbooks and research materials used at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico" title="University of Puerto Rico">University of Puerto Rico</a> are often in English, Spanish is the language of instruction in most courses at UPRM, and students are required to have a working knowledge of the English language. The individual professor decides the language used in class lectures and in student evaluation activities.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Research">Research</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Mayag%C3%BCez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Research"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>UPRM is one of the important centers in the Atlantic region for the study of tropical marine science due to its location, facilities, and researchers. The research facilities includes the Puerto Rico Water Resources and Environmental Research Institute, the Caribbean Coral Reef Institute (CCRI), the Research and Development Center, the Agricultural Research Station and the Caribbean Atmospheric Research Center (ATMOSCarib).<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The College of Engineering offers computerized laboratories for teaching, measuring fatigue and fractures in different materials, designing manufactured products and the processes to build them, and <a href="/wiki/Computer_design" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer design">computer design</a>. Some of the most important fields include <a href="/wiki/Digital_humanities" title="Digital humanities">digital humanities</a>, Caribbean cultural studies, and transnational topics.\n</p><p>In addition to the numerous research laboratories under direct faculty supervision, the Mayag\xc3\xbcez campus has several research and development institutes that provide valuable support for research activities. Arts and Sciences is the college with the largest portfolio of funded research at the university and the highest number of patents, mostly from the Physics Department. Most of the science education funded efforts are in the following areas: <a href="/wiki/Science_education" title="Science education">science</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mathematics_education" physical-biological process">oceanic processes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nanotechnology" title="Nanotechnology">nanotechnology</a> and advanced materials, <a href="/wiki/Human_behavior" title="Human behavior">human behavior</a> and <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-acweb.upr.edu_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acweb.upr.edu-41"><span
