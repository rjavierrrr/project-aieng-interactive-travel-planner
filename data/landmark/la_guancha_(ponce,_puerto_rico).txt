Guancha</b> (English: <i>La Guancha Recreational and Cultural Complex</i>) is a recreational complex in barrio <a href="/wiki/Playa_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Playa (Ponce)">Playa</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>, with family recreational and cultural facilities that opened on 23 June 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The highlight of the complex is the <a href="/wiki/Paseo_Tablado_La_Guancha" title="Paseo Tablado La Guancha">Paseo Tablado La Guancha</a>, which is flanked by a beach, an observation tower, an amphitheater, and 24 open-air kiosks.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was developed during the administration of Mayor <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Cordero_Santiago" title="Rafael Cordero Santiago">Rafael Cordero Santiago</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In October 2017, after <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a>, it closed to the public to undergo repairs but, as of January 2020, when the area suffered severe damage due to the <a href="/wiki/2020_Puerto_Rico_earthquake" class="mw-redirect" title="2020 Puerto Rico earthquake">2020 Puerto Rico earthquakes</a>, no repairs had been made and, as of 17 June 2020, it remained closed.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> By late 2020 arrangements were made for the merchants that operated from waterfront kiosks to reopen their businesses out of provisional facilities at a temporary location away from the waterfront while reconstruction of the waterfront area takes place.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Complejo_Recreativo_y_Cultural_La_Guancha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Originally La Guancha consisted of a road and sidewalk alongside a <a href="/wiki/Levee" title="Levee">dike</a> built close to the easternmost shore of barrio Playa across from <a href="/wiki/Club_N%C3%A1utico_de_Ponce" title="Club N\xc3\xa1utico de Ponce">Ponce Yacht Club</a>.  The <a href="/wiki/Romance_(love)" title="Romance (love)">romantic</a> area was frequented by families and <a href="/wiki/Term_of_endearment" title="Term of endearment">sweethearts</a> alike, especially on weekends, for relaxation and <a href="/wiki/Human_bonding" title="Human bonding">bonding</a>.  During the administration of Mayor <a href="/wiki/Churumba" class="mw-redirect" title="Churumba">Cordero</a>, however, a number of facilities were added to the La Guancha area at a cost of over $18 million, developing it into a new recreational complex.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The new facilities followed the design of engineer Axel Bonilla Cortes.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Facilities">Facilities</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Complejo_Recreativo_y_Cultural_La_Guancha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Facilities"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Seven new facilities were added to the existing walkway area:<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Enrique_A._Vic%C3%A9ns" class="mw-redirect" title="Enrique A. Vic\xc3\xa9ns">Dr. Enrique "Coco" Vicens</a> Recreational and Cultural Center (Spanish: Centro Recreativo y Cultural Enrique "Coco" Vic\xc3\xa9ns), which consists of an open-air amphitheater for concert-type facilities and seating some 30,000 spectators.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Its stage is known as "Tarima Hector Lavoe".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>A rides and amusement park area, intended for the celebration of <a href="/wiki/Patron_saint" title="Patron saint">Patron Saint\'s Day</a> Festivals, fairs, and similar festivities</li>\n<li>The Jose Angel Zayas Colon Children\'s Park, with over 40 games for the youngsters, plus the (Julio and Geraldo) Chamorro-Franceschini Brothers Park, with facilities for the handicapped</li>\n<li>Several parking areas with parking for some 900 vehicles, and distributed throughout the complex</li>\n<li>A majestic fountain, as its main axis, displaying the city\'s <a href="/wiki/Coat-of-arms" class="mw-redirect" title="Coat-of-arms">Coat-of-arms</a> and its iconic <a href="/wiki/Lion" title="Lion">lion</a></li>\n<li>A multi-use building providing facilities for the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Municipal_Police" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce Municipal Police">Ponce Municipal Police</a>, medical emergencies and ambulance, and other related agencies</li>\n<li>A <a href="/wiki/Beach_nourishment" title="Beach nourishment">nourished beach area</a> with over 10,000 cubic meters of new sand that replenished and built up the previous beach area, and including five <a href="/wiki/Beach_volleyball" title="Beach volleyball">beach volleyball</a> courts, <a href="/wiki/Gazebo" title="Gazebo">gazebos</a>, access ramps to the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>, and associated illumination.</li></ul>\n<p>This project complemented the recently inaugurated tourist pier in the nearby <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Ponce" class="mw-redirect" title="Port of Ponce">Port of Ponce</a>, and which saw an investment of $4 million.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Funding">Funding</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Complejo_Recreativo_y_Cultural_La_Guancha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Funding"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Complex was developed by the Department of Community and Economic Development of the Autonomous Municipality of Ponce at a cost of $18.6 million.  The breakdown of the cost included $3.6 million in the acquisition of the <a href="/wiki/Paseo_Tablado_La_Guancha" title="Paseo Tablado La Guancha">Paseo Tablado La Guancha</a>, 6.5 million in the purchase of over 27 acres (110,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) of land, $8.0 million in the construction of the project, $0.2 million for the bidding process, and $0.3 million for the upgrade of <a href="/wiki/PR-12" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-12">PR-12</a> (Avenida Santiago de los Caballeros) as <a href="/wiki/Infrastructure" title="Infrastructure">infrastructure</a> leading to the new complex.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<link
