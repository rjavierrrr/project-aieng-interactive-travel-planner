November 1986</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Ponce City Hall</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Casa Alcald\xc3\xada de Ponce</i>) is a historic <a href="/wiki/City_hall" class="mw-redirect" title="City hall">city hall</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>. It is located it the center of the city, on <a href="/wiki/Federico_Degetau" title="Federico Degetau">Calle Degetau</a>, across from <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Degetau" title="Plaza Degetau">Plaza Degetau</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce Historic Zone</a>. The building serves as the seat of the executive branch of government of the Autonomous Municipality of Ponce, including the office of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_mayors_of_Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="List of mayors of Ponce, Puerto Rico">mayor of Ponce</a>. Built in 1840, it is the oldest <a href="/wiki/Colonial_architecture" title="Colonial architecture">colonial building</a> in the city.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The building was listed on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1986 as Casa Alcald\xc3\xada de Ponce-City Hall.<sup id="cite_ref-nris_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_City_Hall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Ponce City Hall has one of the most unusual histories of any city hall throughout the world, because it was a jail until the end of the 19th century.<sup id="cite_ref-planetware.com_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-planetware.com-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Rooms currently used as offices were jail cells before, and the <a href="/wiki/Courtyard" title="Courtyard">courtyard</a> of the City Hall was the place where <a href="/wiki/Execution" class="mw-redirect" title="Execution">executions</a> took place.<sup id="cite_ref-planetware.com_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-planetware.com-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The last public hanging on the island happened in its courtyard.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It has been visited by four <a href="/wiki/U.S._president" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. president">U.S. presidents</a>, and three of them actually gave a speech from the City Hall\'s front balcony: <a href="/wiki/Theodore_Roosevelt" title="Theodore Roosevelt">Theodore Roosevelt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Hoover" title="Herbert Hoover">Herbert Hoover</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Franklin_Roosevelt" class="mw-redirect" title="Franklin Roosevelt">Franklin Roosevelt</a>. <a href="/wiki/George_H._W._Bush" title="George H. W. Bush">George H. W. Bush</a> visited, as well.<sup id="cite_ref-planetware.com_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-planetware.com-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-frommer_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-frommer-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Design_and_construction">Design and construction</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_City_Hall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design and construction"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Construction of the building started in 1844 and was completed in 1847. Engineering and architectural design for the building was directed by Francisco Gil Cap\xc3\xb3 under the oversight of Mayor <a href="/wiki/Salvador_de_Vives" title="Salvador de Vives">Salvador de Vives</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ponceweb_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ponceweb-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The facade is said to be constructed of "simple but elegant lines."<sup id="cite_ref-ponceweb_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ponceweb-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Its large front facade clock was acquired in England in 1877 at the time the city was given its city charter by <a href="/wiki/Queen_Isabel_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Queen Isabel II">Queen Isabel II</a> of Spain.<sup id="cite_ref-ponceweb_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ponceweb-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The interior has two <a href="/wiki/Courtyard" title="Courtyard">courtyards</a> designed in the typical architecture of the time and a spacious stairway leads to the main hall of the Municipal Legislature and the office of the mayor.<sup id="cite_ref-ponceweb_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ponceweb-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Significance">Significance</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_City_Hall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Significance"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Ponce_-_City_Hall_01.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Ponce_-_City_Hall_01.jpg/220px-Ponce_-_City_Hall_01.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="136" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Ponce_-_City_Hall_01.jpg/330px-Ponce_-_City_Hall_01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Ponce_-_City_Hall_01.jpg/440px-Ponce_-_City_Hall_01.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3261" data-file-height="2019" /></a><figcaption>Photomechanical print (1954)</figcaption></figure>\n<p>By 1848, the population of the Spanish settlement at Ponce had grown sufficiently to merit recognition as a city by Queen Isabel II. The construction of the City Hall coincides with this decree, thus qualifying the building as a landmark to the founding of Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-castro_page3_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-castro_page3-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>As the first governmental structure in the city, the building incorporates elements designed to display its authoritative role. In fact, the design is contemporary to the 1848 addition to the <i>Royal Palace</i> (<a href="/wiki/La_Fortaleza" title="La Fortaleza">La Fortaleza</a>) in San Juan and displays defensive, military characteristics, such as the buttressed street wall, similar to those of the Fortaleza. Most importantly, the vocabulary utilized is purely the Neoclassical Isabelino of the Spanish 19th century, representative of the works performed by the Royal Corps of Engineers of Spain and satisfying the demands of the growing aristocratic population of Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-castro_page3_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-castro_page3-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>According to the Laws of the Indies, established in the 16th century to regulate the colonization of towns in the Americas, a settlement was to be planned around a central plaza, with the Holy Catholic church facing westward and the town hall or "Cabildo" (seat of government power) either opposite or adjacent, emphasizing the major and joint role of the church and state in Hispanic society. Located at the southern fringe of the plaza, at the center of the block between Calle Marina and Calle Cristina, the City Hall dominates the street wall between buildings of similar character and demonstrates faithfully the Spanish concept of the urban plaza. Although the interior has been altered during the 20th century in response to the growing needs of the municipal government, the exterior has maintained its individual and contextual integrity. Along with the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Cathedral" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce Cathedral">Catedral de Nuestra Senora de la Guadalupe</a> in the town <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Delicias" title="Plaza Las Delicias">plaza</a>, the Ponce City Hall forms an essential element of the urban concept of plaza in Ponce, a Spanish colonial town flourishing during the 19th century.<sup id="cite_ref-castro_page3_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-castro_page3-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_City_Hall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Ayuntamiento_de_Ponce.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Ayuntamiento_de_Ponce.jpg/220px-Ayuntamiento_de_Ponce.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Ayuntamiento_de_Ponce.jpg/330px-Ayuntamiento_de_Ponce.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Ayuntamiento_de_Ponce.jpg/440px-Ayuntamiento_de_Ponce.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a><figcaption>Main courtyard</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The building is an attached, plastered <a href="/wiki/Masonry" title="Masonry">masonry</a> structure at the south side of the Plaza Degetau, occupying a lot at the center of the block between Calle Marina and Calle Cristina. The building\'s neoclassical austerity and strong symmetrical character are typical of 19th-century Spanish civil architecture.<sup id="cite_ref-castro_page2_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-castro_page2-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The main facade consists of two stories, divided into five bays: a three bay central section flanked by and only slightly projecting single-bay section on either side. At the ground level, a very distinctive feature of the City Hall is the continuous, projecting water table which creates the appearance of a <a href="/wiki/Fortification" title="Fortification">fortification</a> wall. The central entrance consists of a wide rectangular void, framed by an elliptical archivolt molding. This composition is emphasized by a frontispiece consisting of flanking pairs of banded pilasters supporting decorative brackets which, in turn, support a balustered balcony accessed through the second level. The single bay sections have smooth rustication only at the ground floor, terminating at a continuous string course which divides this level from the upper.<sup id="cite_ref-castro_page2_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-castro_page2-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>All openings other than the main entrance and the second and fourth bays at ground level are articulated with full-height, <a href="/wiki/Wooden" class="mw-redirect" title="Wooden">wooden</a>, double doors with louvre panels. The central entrance houses decorative, <a href="/wiki/Wrought-iron" class="mw-redirect" title="Wrought-iron">wrought-iron</a> railings and its flanking windows begin at a height of approximately four feet. At the upper level bays, baluster rails create <a href="/wiki/Balconet" title="Balconet">balconettes</a>. Planar surrounds frame the window openings, extending above to incorporate separate architrave moldings.<sup id="cite_ref-castro_page2_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-castro_page2-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>A continuous cornice and <a href="/wiki/Parapet" title="Parapet">parapet</a> extend across the facade. A pedimented frontispiece composed of a <a href="/wiki/Clock" title="Clock">clock</a>, flanked by pairs of small pairs of Tuscan <a href="/wiki/Pilaster" title="Pilaster">pilasters</a>, capped by an <a href="/wiki/Entablature" title="Entablature">entablature</a> and supported at either side by a scroll element emphasizes the central bay above the running cornice. In addition, a <a href="/wiki/Weather_vane" title="Weather vane">weathervane</a> rises up above the pediment, further strengthening the symmetrical quality of the facade.<sup id="cite_ref-castro_page2_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-castro_page2-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div
