Rico">Autonomous Municipality of Ponce</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Pante\xc3\xb3n Nacional Rom\xc3\xa1n Baldorioty de Castro</b> (English: Rom\xc3\xa1n Baldorioty de Castro National Pantheon) is a tract of land in <a href="/wiki/Segundo_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Segundo (Ponce)">Barrio Segundo</a> of the city of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>, originally designed as the city\'s <a href="/wiki/Cemetery" title="Cemetery">cemetery</a>, but later converted into what has come to be a famous burial place. Established in 1842, it is <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>\'s first (and only) national pantheon.<sup id="cite_ref-loc.gov_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-loc.gov-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is the only cemetery dedicated as a museum in Puerto Rico and the Caribbean.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Prior to being dedicated as a Pante\xc3\xb3n Nacional, it was known as <b>Cementerio Viejo</b> or as <b>Cementerio Antiguo de Ponce</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>a<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and is listed under that name on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>. The Pantheon is named after <a href="/wiki/Rom%C3%A1n_Baldorioty_de_Castro" title="Rom\xc3\xa1n Baldorioty de Castro">Rom\xc3\xa1n Baldorioty de Castro</a>, a prolific Puerto Rican politician, and firm believer of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rican</a> <a href="/wiki/Autonomy" title="Autonomy">autonomy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a>. His remains are located here.<sup id="cite_ref-loc.gov_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-loc.gov-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Pantheon also houses a small <a href="/wiki/Museum" title="Museum">museum</a> about the history of <a href="/wiki/Autonomism" title="Autonomism">autonomism</a> in the Island, and it is currently used both as a park and a venue for the expression of culture and the arts.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is called the <a href="/wiki/Museo_del_Autonomismo_Puertorrique%C3%B1o" title="Museo del Autonomismo Puertorrique\xc3\xb1o">Museo del Autonomismo Puertorrique\xc3\xb1o</a>.\n</p><p>Prior to being turned into a National Pantheon in 1991, it was known as Antiguo Cementerio de Ponce (Ponce\'s Old Cemetery), to differentiate it from the newer (though now also over 100 years old) <a href="/wiki/Cementerio_Civil_de_Ponce" title="Cementerio Civil de Ponce">Cementerio Civil de Ponce</a> (Ponce Civil Cemetery).<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Built in 1842, after the design of Antonio Torruella,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> the cemetery was enlarged in 1864, following the design of Nieto Blajol Iglesia. It closed in 1918.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pante%C3%B3n_Nacional_Rom%C3%A1n_Baldorioty_de_Castro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:View_of_Ponce.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/View_of_Ponce.jpg/350px-View_of_Ponce.jpg" decoding="async" width="350" height="249" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/View_of_Ponce.jpg/525px-View_of_Ponce.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/View_of_Ponce.jpg/700px-View_of_Ponce.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2019" data-file-height="1439" /></a><figcaption>View of the Panteon, circa 1890s (looking south)</figcaption></figure>\n<p>On 13 February 1838, the secretary of the <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico#Government" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Municipality of Ponce</a> met with <a href="/wiki/Civic_engagement" title="Civic engagement">civic</a> leaders of the "Villa of Ponce" to discuss plans for building a new cemetery. The existing cemetery was in deplorable and bad condition, and it posed a danger to public health.  Because the "Villa of Ponce" did not have the money to build the cemetery, a voluntary proportional contribution was established in which all neighbors were to contribute. A commission was named composed of one member of the Municipal Council and four neighbors of influence to open up a voluntary subscription, name a depository and to proceed with the construction of the cemetery until its completion. The project was to be carried out in a lot adjacent to the existing cemetery extending one hundred square "varas" (a measure of length equal to 0.84m. approximately). Named by vote to form the commission were the "Sor Sindico" \'(secretary to the mayor of the "Villa") and the neighbor leaders Don Geronimo Ortiz, Don Tomas Souffront, Don Luis Font, and Don Mateo Maenamaxe. On 26 February 1838, the project was approved by the <a href="/wiki/List_of_mayors_of_Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="List of mayors of Ponce, Puerto Rico">mayor of Ponce</a>. However, it was not until 1842 that the project was started out with the contributions of the neighbors of the "Villa". In 1843, the cemetery was inaugurated under the auspices of the mayor of Ponce, Don <a href="/wiki/Juan_Rond%C3%B3n" title="Juan Rond\xc3\xb3n">Juan Rondon Martinez</a>, also the first person to be buried there. In 1864 the cemetery was enlarged and repaired. The new work consisted of repairs and construction of the enclosing walls and pathways, construction of new niches and construction of a chapel and mortuary. In 1915 the cemetery was closed down.<sup id="cite_ref-State_Historic_Preservation_Officer_1983._Page_3_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-State_Historic_Preservation_Officer_1983._Page_3-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Architecture_and_construction">Architecture and construction</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pante%C3%B3n_Nacional_Rom%C3%A1n_Baldorioty_de_Castro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Architecture and construction"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The cemetery consisted of well-designed small <a href="/wiki/Mausoleum" title="Mausoleum">mausoleums</a> that were lined up following a central pathway followed by lateral ones, starting from the entrance of the cemetery to a <a href="/wiki/Chapel" title="Chapel">chapel</a> located at the end. Most structures were designed and constructed following the <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">neoclassical</a> style that prevailed at that time. This style consisted of the use of columns and <a href="/wiki/Pilaster" title="Pilaster">pilasters</a> following the <a href="/wiki/Doric_order" title="Doric order">Doric</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ionic_order" title="Ionic order">Ionic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Corinthian_order" title="Corinthian order">Corinthian</a> order, barrel vaults, Greek <a href="/wiki/Pediments" class="mw-redirect" title="Pediments">pediments</a>, Roman <a href="/wiki/Arch" title="Arch">arches</a> and other details characteristic of said style. The thick walls and piers were built using brick, <a href="/wiki/Mortar_(masonry)" title="Mortar (masonry)">mortar</a>, and "argamasa" (a mixture of crushed brick, sand, stone aggregate and lime). The enclosing walls were built of brick and mortar, and the part of the walls built in 1864 were built using stone and mortar. It was built in 1842 and enlarged in 1864.<sup id="cite_ref-State_Historic_Preservation_Officer_1983._Page_3_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-State_Historic_Preservation_Officer_1983._Page_3-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Significance">Significance</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pante%C3%B3n_Nacional_Rom%C3%A1n_Baldorioty_de_Castro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Significance"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Various illustrious Puerto Ricans of transcendental importance are buried here. The most important person buried in the cemetery is Don <a href="/wiki/Rom%C3%A1n_Baldorioty_de_Castro" title="Rom\xc3\xa1n Baldorioty de Castro">Rom\xc3\xa1n Baldorioty de Castro</a>, distinguished patriot, journalist, educator, writer, orator, and <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">abolitionist</a>. In 1870, he was elected delegate to the Courts of Cadiz, where he opposed the slavery system of the time. He was responsible for most of the liberty amendments. On 23 March 1873, the abolition of slavery was proclaimed by Baldorioty de Castro.<sup id="cite_ref-State_Historic_Preservation_Officer_1983._Page_3_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-State_Historic_Preservation_Officer_1983._Page_3-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Also buried there are <a href="/wiki/Parque_de_Bombas" title="Parque de Bombas">Don Juan Seix</a>, founder of the <a href="/wiki/Parque_de_Bombas" title="Parque de Bombas">Ponce Fire Department</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Rondon_Martinez" class="mw-redirect" title="Juan Rondon Martinez">Don Juan Rondon Martinez</a>, mayor of Ponce (founder of the cemetery and first person buried in it).<sup id="cite_ref-State_Historic_Preservation_Officer_1983._Page_3_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-State_Historic_Preservation_Officer_1983._Page_3-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Another prominent mayor buried there is <a href="/wiki/Salvador_de_Vives" title="Salvador de Vives">Salvador de Vives</a>, who founded <a href="/wiki/Hacienda_Buena_Vista" title="Hacienda Buena Vista">Hacienda Buena Vista</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  Other people buried in the cemetery include <a href="/wiki/Juan_Morel_Campos" title="Juan Morel Campos">Juan Morel Campos</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manuel_G._Tavarez" class="mw-redirect" title="Manuel G. Tavarez">Manuel G. Tavarez</a>, distinguished composers and musicians, most famous for their <a href="/wiki/Danza" title="Danza">danzas</a> (folkloric music typical of Puerto Rico) compositions,<sup id="cite_ref-State_Historic_Preservation_Officer_1983._Page_3_13-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-State_Historic_Preservation_Officer_1983._Page_3-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and tenor of kings <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Paoli" title="Antonio Paoli">Antonio Paoli</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  It houses the remains of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_Nationalist_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican Nationalist Party">nationalist</a> hero <a href="/wiki/Casimiro_Berenguer" title="Casimiro Berenguer">Casimiro Berenguer</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and members of the prominent <a href="/wiki/Juan_Serralles" class="mw-redirect" title="Juan Serralles">Seralles</a> family,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> founders of the <a href="/wiki/Destileria_Serralles" class="mw-redirect" title="Destileria Serralles">Destileria Serralles</a>, and after whom the <a href="/wiki/Castillo_Serralles" class="mw-redirect" title="Castillo Serralles">Castillo Serralles</a> took its name. Ex-governor <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Sanchez_Vilella" class="mw-redirect" title="Roberto Sanchez Vilella">Roberto Sanchez Vilella</a>\'s tomb is also buried there and the late mayor of Ponce, <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Cordero_Santiago" title="Rafael Cordero Santiago">Rafael Cordero Santiago</a>, has a mausoleum there.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pante%C3%B3n_Nacional_Rom%C3%A1n_Baldorioty_de_Castro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Tomb_of_Casimiro_Berenguer_in_Barrio_Segundo,_Ponce,_PR_(DSC00228).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Tomb_of_Casimiro_Berenguer_in_Barrio_Segundo%2C_Ponce%2C_PR_%28DSC00228%29.jpg/200px-Tomb_of_Casimiro_Berenguer_in_Barrio_Segundo%2C_Ponce%2C_PR_%28DSC00228%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="150" class="mw-file-element" Pante\xc3\xb3n Nacional</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The cemetery plan is shaped as a rectangle measuring approximately 157.71 meters in length by 84.31 meters wide. It is attached to a rectangular-shaped lot at the north-east corner measuring about 32.80 meters in length by 25.03 meters wide, and a small rectangular lot where the <a href="/wiki/Mortuary" class="mw-redirect" title="Mortuary">mortuary</a> used to be located, at the center of the south side of cemetery measuring about 14.13 meters wide by 8.97 meters in length.<sup id="cite_ref-State_Historic_Preservation_Officer_1983._Page_2_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-State_Historic_Preservation_Officer_1983._Page_2-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>A small mortuary structure building used to stand at the entrance of the cemetery. Following this structure, small <a href="/wiki/Mausoleum" title="Mausoleum">mausoleums</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crypt" title="Crypt">crypts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Niche_(architecture)" title="Niche (architecture)">niches</a> were lined up on a central pathway that led to a small chapel located at the center of the cemetery. The cemetery was totally enclosed by very high walls that still remain. Most mausoleums and crypts were constructed following the <a href="/wiki/Neo-classical_architecture" class="mw-redirect" title="Neo-classical architecture">neo-classical</a> style trend that prevailed at the time. Thick walls and <a href="/wiki/Pier" title="Pier">piers</a> were used as the main structural system. These were constructed using brick/"argarnasa", stone and mortar.<sup id="cite_ref-State_Historic_Preservation_Officer_1983._Page_2_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-State_Historic_Preservation_Officer_1983._Page_2-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The cemetery was closed in 1915 and it deteriorated since then. By 1984, it became subjected to vandalism; crypts and mausoleums were <a href="/wiki/Plunder" class="mw-redirect" title="Plunder">plundered</a>. On that year, it contained a variety of mausoleums, crypts and niches partially destroyed, and most of the cemetery was covered by heavy vegetation. The enclosing wall plaster had fallen down exposing the brick and stone to weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-State_Historic_Preservation_Officer_1983._Page_2_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-State_Historic_Preservation_Officer_1983._Page_2-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Around 1990, the city of Ponce managed to salvage it. It repaired and reconstructed it, turning it into a National Pantheon. The architect in charge if its 1991 design was Virgilio Monsanto.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The cemetery was at that time called <i>Cementerio Viejo</i> (English: Old Cemetery), but it was renamed <i>Pante\xc3\xb3n Nacional Rom\xc3\xa1n Baldorioty de Castro</i> (English: Rom\xc3\xa1n Baldorioty de Castro National Pantheon). The city of Ponce also established a small museum about <a href="/wiki/Autonomism" title="Autonomism">autonomism</a>. The city has also commissioned the <a href="/wiki/Pontifical_Catholic_University_of_Puerto_Rico_School_of_Architecture" title="Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Architecture">Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Architecture</a> to perform a study to determine the viability and cost for the total reconstruction of the Pantheon.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Current_use">Current use</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pante%C3%B3n_Nacional_Rom%C3%A1n_Baldorioty_de_Castro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Current use"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The cemetery is not active for the burial of just-deceased people as it does not have an order by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Health" title="Puerto Rico Department of Health">Puerto Rico Department of Health</a> to operate in that way due to the propensity of the terrain for landslides.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Instead, it is used to transfer remains after a period of five years has lapsed.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> As of April 2014, the most-recently transferred remains were those of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Madeleine_Velasco_Alvarado&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Madeleine Velasco Alvarado (page does not exist)">Madeleine Velasco Alvarado</a> in 2008, <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Cordero_Santiago" title="Rafael Cordero Santiago">Rafael Cordero Santiago</a> in 2004, and <a href="/wiki/Simon_Moret_Gallart" class="mw-redirect" title="Simon Moret Gallart">Simon Moret Gallart</a> in 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In April 2014 the possibility of interment of the remains of <a href="/wiki/Cheo_Feliciano" title="Cheo Feliciano">Cheo Feliciano</a> was being considered. Ponce Mayor Maria Melendez stated she would issue a municipal order to transfer Feliciano\'s remains to the Panteon,<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> if possible after one year.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="See_also">See also</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
