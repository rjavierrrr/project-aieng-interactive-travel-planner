The construction of this home set the stage for the construction of other homes of similar architectural elements, character and opulence in turn-of-the-twentieth-century Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-Campo_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Campo-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 3">&#58;&#8202;3&#8202;</span></sup> The architectural style is collectively known as <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Creole" title="Ponce Creole">Ponce Creole</a>. The home was designed and built by <a href="/wiki/Manuel_V._Domenech" title="Manuel V. Domenech">Manuel V\xc3\xadctor Domenech</a> for the Armstrong-Poventud family.  It is listed on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> as the <b>Armstrong-Toro House</b>, and is also known as the <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Casa de las Cariatides</i></span>. In 1991, the <a href="/wiki/Instituto_de_Cultura_Puertorrique%C3%B1a" title="Instituto de Cultura Puertorrique\xc3\xb1a">Instituto de Cultura Puertorrique\xc3\xb1a</a> turned the house into a museum, which it manages.<sup id="cite_ref-icp.gobierno.pr_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icp.gobierno.pr-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-periodicolaperla.com_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-periodicolaperla.com-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Construction_date">Construction date</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Residencia_Armstrong-Poventud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Construction date"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Puerto_Rican_Culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Institute of Puerto Rican Culture">Institute of Puerto Rican Culture</a> (<a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Puerto_Rican_Culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Institute of Puerto Rican Culture">ICP</a>) and the Puerto Rico Office of Historical Preservation have determined that the house was built in 1899. The <i>American Architect and Building News</i> magazine, on the front cover of its 25 January 1899, issue published the design of the Residencia Armstrong-Poventud.  The Residencia was inaugurated fully as a museum by the ICP in October 2008. Prior to 2008, and starting in 1991, visitors were allowed to tour the house while it concurrently served as the Southern headquarters of the institute.<sup id="cite_ref-icp.gobierno.pr_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icp.gobierno.pr-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The institute operates a small but diverse store in a wing of the museum.<sup id="cite_ref-periodicolaperla.com_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-periodicolaperla.com-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Significance">Significance</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Residencia_Armstrong-Poventud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Significance"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Casa_Carlos_Armstrong,_9_Plaza_Munoz_Rivera,_Ponce_(Ponce_County,_Puerto_Rico).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img 1.5x, 2x" data-file-width="597" data-file-height="413" /></a><figcaption>The Armstrong-Poventud Residence in 1977.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The Carlos Armstrong-Toro Residence is one of Ponce\'s most famous houses. This structure was designed and built by Manuel Domenech, one of the island\'s most distinguished architects of the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Some of Domenech\'s designs include: the <a href="/wiki/Asilo_De_Pobres" title="Asilo De Pobres">Asilo De Pobres</a> de Mayaguez and the <a href="/wiki/Rosaly-Batiz_House" class="mw-redirect" title="Rosaly-Batiz House">Residencia Batiz</a>. Domenech, a public figure in Puerto Rico during the 20th century, built many noteworthy private residences and public buildings in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, Ponce, and <a href="/wiki/Mayaguez,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayaguez, Puerto Rico">Mayaguez</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Campo_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Campo-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 3">&#58;&#8202;3&#8202;</span></sup>\n</p><p>Mr. Armstrong-Toro was one of Ponce\'s most distinguished citizens as well. His father, Pedro Lothario Armstrong y Creagh,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> was a Ponce merchant from <a href="/wiki/St._Croix,_Virgin_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Croix, Virgin Islands">St. Croix</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Danish_West_Indies" title="Danish West Indies">Danish West Indies</a>. At the time a great deal of Puerto Rico\'s imports came through Danish <a href="/wiki/St._Thomas,_Virgin_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Thomas, Virgin Islands">St. Thomas</a>. Armstrong-Toro was one of the first bankers in Puerto Rico, founder of the <a href="/wiki/Banco_de_Ponce" title="Banco de Ponce">Banco de Ponce</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Banco_Cr%C3%A9dito_y_Ahorro_Ponce%C3%B1o_(building)" title="Banco Cr\xc3\xa9dito y Ahorro Ponce\xc3\xb1o (building)">Banco Cr\xc3\xa9dito y Ahorro Ponce\xc3\xb1o</a>. Mr. Armstrong-Toro developed an international banking network with offices in Puerto Rico, <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="The United States">the United States</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>. As a result of his extensive financial relations with the Danish government, Mr. Armstrong was conferred the high honor of the "<a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Dannebrog" title="Order of the Dannebrog">Order of the Dannebrog</a>" of Denmark and was named Danish Consul to Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-Campo_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Campo-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 3">&#58;&#8202;3&#8202;</span></sup>\n</p><p>Architecturally speaking, the Armstrong-Toro Residence is significant in the history of Ponce because it is one of Domenech\'s most distinguished eclectic designs and probably the best known. Its unique caryatid-framed main entrance, its intricate ornamental details and its location upon the most prominent lot in the city of Ponce (across from the Cathedral and <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Delicias" title="Plaza Las Delicias">public plaza</a>) have made this house a true architectural landmark in the city\'s traditional urban core. As a result, many houses were built during the early 20th century with similar elements in a similar vocabulary, contributing to the unique <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">historic district of Ponce</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Campo_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Campo-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 3">&#58;&#8202;3&#8202;</span></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Residencia_Armstrong-Poventud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Residencia Armstrong-Poventud is a two-story masonry building on the westside of Calle Union (No. 9), directly in front of the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Cathedral" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce Cathedral">Cathedral</a> and <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Delicias" title="Plaza Las Delicias">Plaza</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">historic urban center</a> of Ponce. The almost-cubic proportions of the facade are articulated in three bays: 2 wider bays flanking a central, narrower entrance bay.<sup id="cite_ref-Campo_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Campo-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 2">&#58;&#8202;2&#8202;</span></sup>\n</p><p>At the ground level, a pink-stone <a href="/wiki/Plinth" class="mw-redirect" title="Plinth">plinth</a> serves as a <a href="/wiki/Podium" title="Podium">podium</a> for the one story eclectic pilasters at the north and south extremes of the facade and for the two <a href="/wiki/Caryatid" title="Caryatid">caryatids</a>, which flank the main entrance and define the three bays of the facade. The central entrance arch cuts the podium and houses intricately carved hardwood doors and a stained-glass fanlight protected by decorative <a href="/wiki/Wrought_iron" title="Wrought iron">wrought iron</a> railings. Bays 1 and 3 both consist of paired arches, located above the podium and articulated in an ionic pilaster order. Each arch-window houses folding <a href="/wiki/Jalousie" class="mw-redirect" title="Jalousie">jalousie</a> shutters.<sup id="cite_ref-Campo_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Campo-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 3">&#58;&#8202;3&#8202;</span></sup>\n</p><p>At the upper level there are four <a href="/wiki/Ionic_order" title="Ionic order">Ionic</a> pilasters with pedestals resting on a continuous horizontal string-course. The three openings are symmetrically arranged and equally proportioned and articulated: a simple surround with a keystone frames a wide arch, opening out to a balcony through wooden jalousie doors. Decoratively etched clear glass lights occupy the tympanum over each opening. The first and third bay balconies contain waist-high decorative cast-iron railings and the wider central balcony consists of stone balusters and rails supported by the caryatid figures of the ground floor. A full-entablature <a href="/wiki/Cornice" title="Cornice">cornice</a> and solid <a href="/wiki/Parapet" title="Parapet">parapet</a> cap the facade composition. Roman-style <a href="/wiki/Amphorae" class="mw-redirect" title="Amphorae">amphorae</a> on the parapet, above each pilaster, serve as <a href="/wiki/Finials" class="mw-redirect" title="Finials">finials</a> for each bay division.<sup id="cite_ref-Campo_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Campo-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 3">&#58;&#8202;3&#8202;</span></sup><sup id="cite_ref-npsgallery_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-npsgallery-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>At the northern end of the structure, a concrete <a href="/wiki/Garage_(house)" class="mw-redirect" title="Garage (house)">port-cochere</a> enclosed by decorative wrought-iron railings projects from the upper-level floor line, creating a side terrace for the upper floor.<sup id="cite_ref-Campo_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Campo-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 2">&#58;&#8202;2&#8202;</span></sup>\n</p><p>The house is very simple in plan and elegant in execution. The main entrance accesses a <a href="/wiki/Foyer" class="mw-redirect" title="Foyer">foyer</a> with steps leading up to the major circulation hall through wooden and stained-glass double-doors. A sweeping stairway, toward the back of the house on the left side of the hall accesses the second floor, which is similar in plan to the first. All floors within the house are <a href="/wiki/Parquet" title="Parquet">parquet</a> and ceilings are of hand-painted decorative pressed metal. The floors were installed during the last restoration and the pressed-metal ceilings are all original.<sup id="cite_ref-Campo_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Campo-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 2">&#58;&#8202;2&#8202;</span></sup>\n</p><p>As a result of the disastrous <a href="/wiki/1918_San_Ferm%C3%ADn_earthquake" title="1918 San Ferm\xc3\xadn earthquake">earthquake of 1918</a>, the structure\'s northern wall had fallen and was subsequently reconstructed according to the original plans. The concrete-vaulted port-cochere was added at that time. Aside from this latter addition, the Armstrong-Toro house maintains the exact architecture of the original design.<sup id="cite_ref-Campo_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Campo-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 2">&#58;&#8202;2&#8202;</span></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Acquisition_and_restoration">Acquisition and restoration</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Residencia_Armstrong-Poventud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Acquisition and restoration"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Puerto_Rican_Culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Institute of Puerto Rican Culture">Institute of Puerto Rican Culture</a> (ICP) acquired the building from the descendants of the original owners.<sup id="cite_ref-icp.gobierno.pr_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icp.gobierno.pr-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Some of the original furniture and personal items of the home were included in the acquisition, as the Armstrong family lived the house uninterrupted for eight decades.  The ICP restored the building on two occasions: in the 1980s and then again from 2006 to 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-icp.gobierno.pr_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icp.gobierno.pr-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Description">Description</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Residencia_Armstrong-Poventud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Description"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Almost all the furniture and utensils from that era are still in place, as well as paintings and pictures belonging the original owners of this grand old home located directly in front of <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Cathedral" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce Cathedral">Our Lady of Guadalupe Cathedral</a>, in the town square. The house is easy to spot thanks to the two <a href="/wiki/Caryatid" title="Caryatid">caryatids</a> in the front of the house and its overall <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">neoclassic</a> architectural style.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> With its gorgeous, neoclassical facade, the Casa Armstrong-Poventud is among the most stately and elegant houses in Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Built in 1900 as the home for this Scottish banker, the house served as the regional office of the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Puerto_Rican_Culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Institute of Puerto Rican Culture">ICP</a> for 15 years.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Casa Armstrong-Poventud building is a magnificent example of the neoclassical architectural heritage of the island.  The Residence was listed in the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places_listings_in_southern_Puerto_Rico#Ponce" title="National Register of Historic Places listings in southern Puerto Rico">National Register of Historic Places</a> on 29 October 1987.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Historical_background">Historical background</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Residencia_Armstrong-Poventud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Historical background"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Casa Armstrong-Poventud Museum is an example of the main <a href="/wiki/Belle-%C3%89poque" class="mw-redirect" title="Belle-\xc3\x89poque">Belle-\xc3\x89poque</a> (English: "The Beautiful Epoch"), a French term that is used to describe a long period in European history noted for peace, political stability, and industrial progress. Started around the middle of the 19th century and ran up to <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. This period of great prosperity coincided with England\'s Queen Victoria\'s kingdom (1837\xe2\x80\x931901) during which industrial and artistic greatness were promoted with such a success that it also came to be known as the <a href="/wiki/Victorian_Era" class="mw-redirect" title="Victorian Era">Victorian Era</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-icp.gobierno.pr_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icp.gobierno.pr-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="European_fairs">European fairs</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Residencia_Armstrong-Poventud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: European fairs"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Unlike previous eras, the level of artistic and industrial prosperity reached during the Belle \xc3\x89poque was such that it reached the masses, in Europe as well as in <a href="/wiki/The_Americas" class="mw-redirect" title="The Americas">the Americas</a>.  Important exhibitions marked this progress.  There was one in England in 1851, and various others followed in various other major cities. There was for example another one in Paris in 1855 that brought about an even larger number of attendees, unprecedented in the history of mankind: 5,100,000 people coming from virtually every existing country.  In 1878, Paris celebrated another Fair yet where the telephone and the light bulb were shown for the first time. The city was first lit during this 1878 Paris Fair.<sup id="cite_ref-icp.gobierno.pr_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icp.gobierno.pr-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Ponce_1882_fair">Ponce 1882 fair</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Residencia_Armstrong-Poventud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Ponce 1882 fair"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Ponce had its own fair in 1882. It was dedicated to agriculture, commerce, industry and the arts. Just like Paris four years earlier, Ponce was also illuminated on the first day of the Ponce Fair: <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Delicias" title="Plaza Las Delicias">Plaza Las Delicias</a>, the Uni\xc3\xb3n Mercantil building, the <a href="/wiki/Old_Ponce_Casino" class="mw-redirect" title="Old Ponce Casino">Ponce Casino</a> and some of the most majestic homes in the Majestic City, such as the Armstrong Residence.<sup id="cite_ref-icp.gobierno.pr_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icp.gobierno.pr-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Ponce\'s_majestic_residences"><span id="Ponce.27s_majestic_residences"></span>Ponce\'s majestic residences</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Residencia_Armstrong-Poventud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Ponce&#039;s majestic residences"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The economic affluence in Ponce during the second half of the 19th century was due to great wealth from <a href="/wiki/Sugarcane" title="Sugarcane">sugarcane</a> <a href="/wiki/Sugar_plantations_in_the_Caribbean" title="Sugar plantations in the Caribbean">plantations</a>. This made possible the construction of various public works such as the <a href="/wiki/Teatro_La_Perla" title="Teatro La Perla">Teatro La Perla</a>, the Ponce aqueduct, and several majestic homes, like Armstrong Puventud Home.  The home of Se\xc3\xb1or Carlos W. Armstrong Toro and lady Eulalia Pou, now known as the Armstrong-Poventud Residence, represents this wealth despite the restrictive colonial Spanish government of the Island at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-icp.gobierno.pr_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icp.gobierno.pr-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Personal_life_of_Se\xc3\xb1or_Armstrong"><span id="Personal_life_of_Se.C3.B1or_Armstrong"></span>Personal life of Se\xc3\xb1or Armstrong</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Residencia_Armstrong-Poventud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life of Se\xc3\xb1or Armstrong"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Se\xc3\xb1or Carlos Walter Armstrong Toro established his wealth on the agricultural prosperity the Island experienced: first as businessman, later as a banker, and finally as a politician. Mr. Carlos Armstrong Toro had an import/export business that exposed him to the great cities of the world and put him in touch with the architectural styles of the day.  He was, for example, a partner in a New York City business. In addition, he was also the <a href="/wiki/Danish_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Danish people">Danish</a> consul in Ponce. It was with this background that he ordered th construction of his home in Ponce.  Se\xc3\xb1or Armstrong Toro married lady Eulalia Pou Carreras, on 11 January 1868. Do\xc3\xb1a Eulalia Pou was herself from a wealthy Ponce family.<sup id="cite_ref-icp.gobierno.pr_3-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icp.gobierno.pr-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Architecture_of_the_residence">Architecture of the residence</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Residencia_Armstrong-Poventud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Architecture of the residence"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Museum at Casa Armstrong Poventud presents the latest architectural and artistic taste in vogue at the end of the 19th century in Europe. For example, heavy use of glass on doors and windows. The glass decor used in the front door of the residence, for instance, is almost identical to those used in the <a href="/wiki/The_Crystal_Palace" title="The Crystal Palace">Crystal Palace</a> at the Great Exhibition of the Works of Industry of all Nations in England years earlier. The floral and natural motifs in the residence, are also characteristic of those comprising the new artistic expression of the Victorian Era evident in Europe and the United States in the first decades of the 20th century.<sup id="cite_ref-icp.gobierno.pr_3-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icp.gobierno.pr-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Front_door_entrance">Front door entrance</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Residencia_Armstrong-Poventud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Front door entrance"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The front door is an excellent example of doors characteristic of the <a href="/wiki/Victorian_architecture" title="Victorian architecture">Victorian era.</a> It achieved two purposes: decorative but also to allow the passage of natural daylight. Almost all the doors in this Museum have unique majestic-style characteristics not found in doors in the capital city of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, where doors are considerably simpler.  The residence displays a harmonious appreciation for the style, as much by the architects as by the owners, of the Belle \xc3\x89poque.<sup id="cite_ref-icp.gobierno.pr_3-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icp.gobierno.pr-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Cariatides">Cariatides</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Residencia_Armstrong-Poventud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Cariatides"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The "cari\xc3\xa1tides" (<a href="/wiki/Caryatid" title="Caryatid">caryatids</a>) statues used by architect <a href="/wiki/Manuel_V._Domenech" title="Manuel V. Domenech">Manuel V. Domenech</a> for the front of the house were not the classical ones, but modernized adaptations of the classical ones. They do not display the solemn expression of the traditional ones, but smiling young people with a modern hairdo and displaying body motion.  Even the pedestal on which the status lay has an <a href="/wiki/Art-Nouveau" class="mw-redirect" title="Art-Nouveau">Art-Nouveau</a> style.<sup id="cite_ref-icp.gobierno.pr_3-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icp.gobierno.pr-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Interior_details">Interior details</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Residencia_Armstrong-Poventud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Interior details"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The interior furniture and other items are also Victorian.  The Residence emphasizes a multiplicity of styles in its interior, not just different epochs, but also different countries: Chinese jar, <a href="/wiki/Victorian_era" title="Victorian era">Victorian</a> lamps, <a href="/wiki/Art-Nouveau" class="mw-redirect" title="Art-Nouveau">Art-Nouveau</a>, and Austrian glass chandeliers.<sup id="cite_ref-icp.gobierno.pr_3-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icp.gobierno.pr-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Electrical_and_plumbing_design">Electrical and plumbing design</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Residencia_Armstrong-Poventud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Electrical and plumbing design"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Mr. Manuel Victor Domenech used a variety of architectural elements from various epochs in a unique creative manner. He used, for example the most advanced objects of residential utility of the time such as electricity, bathroom and kitchens that functioned with an automatic plumbing system, to create a structure characteristics of the end of the Victorian era, known as <a href="/wiki/Modernism" title="Modernism">Modernism</a>.  There is heavy use of electric light bulb lamps at a time when over 90% of the population of Puerto Rico used no electricity or plumbing.<sup id="cite_ref-icp.gobierno.pr_3-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icp.gobierno.pr-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Overall">Overall</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Residencia_Armstrong-Poventud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Overall"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The overall impression of the residence is one displaying a new appearance of modernism. It has both Victorian and modernistic elements in it, approaching details seen in <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, New York City, Chicago, and <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> at the time. The resulting style is one of modernistic Victorian architecture.<sup id="cite_ref-icp.gobierno.pr_3-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icp.gobierno.pr-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Effects_of_the_2020_Puerto_Rico_earthquake">Effects of the 2020 Puerto Rico earthquake</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Residencia_Armstrong-Poventud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Effects of the 2020 Puerto Rico earthquake"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The building sustained damage due to the <a href="/wiki/2020_Puerto_Rico_earthquake" class="mw-redirect" title="2020 Puerto Rico earthquake">2020 Puerto Rico earthquake</a>. There are plans to restore the house from the damages it sustained.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span
