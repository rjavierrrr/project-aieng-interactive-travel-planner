border: 0;">Added to NRHP</th><td class="infobox-data" style="border: 0;">22 January 2014</td></tr><tr style="display:none"><th colspan="2">\n</th></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Hiram Bithorn Stadium</b> (Spanish: <b>Estadio Hiram Bithorn</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Baseball_park" class="mw-redirect" title="Baseball park">baseball park</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>, built in 1962 and designed by Puerto Rican architect Pedro Miranda.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The stadium is home to the <a href="/wiki/Cangrejeros_de_Santurce_(baseball)" title="Cangrejeros de Santurce (baseball)">Cangrejeros de Santurce</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Liga_de_B%C3%A9isbol_Profesional_Roberto_Clemente" title="Liga de B\xc3\xa9isbol Profesional Roberto Clemente">Puerto Rican Baseball League</a> (LBPRC), and briefly was home to <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Expos" title="Montreal Expos">Montreal Expos</a> during their final years.\n</p><p>It is operated by the municipal government of the city of San Juan. Its name honors the first Puerto Rican to play in the major leagues, <a href="/wiki/Hiram_Bithorn" title="Hiram Bithorn">Hiram Bithorn</a>, who first played with the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a> in 1942. Built in 1962, under the mayoral administration of <a href="/wiki/Felisa_Rinc%C3%B3n_de_Gautier" title="Felisa Rinc\xc3\xb3n de Gautier">Felisa Rinc\xc3\xb3n de Gautier</a> as a replacement for <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Sixto_Escobar" title="Estadio Sixto Escobar">Estadio Sixto Escobar</a>, it was added to the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Dimensions">Dimensions</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hiram_Bithorn_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Dimensions"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The stadium has approximately 18,000 seats. The stadium is 325 feet (99&#160;m) down the left-field line, 325 feet (99&#160;m) down the right-field line and 404 feet (123&#160;m) to center field. The fences are 8 feet (2.5 m) high. When the Expos played home games at Hiram Bithorn, the field dimensions were set to match Olympic Stadium in Montreal.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hiram_Bithorn_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Hiram Bithorn Stadium was opened in 1962.\n</p><p>The first <a href="/wiki/NBA_outdoor_games" title="NBA outdoor games">outdoor</a> <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a> game was played between the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Suns" title="Phoenix Suns">Phoenix Suns</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Bucks" title="Milwaukee Bucks">Milwaukee Bucks</a> on September 24, 1972, during that year\'s preseason. The Suns defeated the Bucks, 116\xe2\x80\x93103.\n</p><p>In the mid-1990s Hiram Bithorn Stadium was planned to be the home of the yet-to-be-named Puerto Rico team, a charter franchise of the <a href="/wiki/United_Baseball_League_(proposed)" title="United Baseball League (proposed)">United League</a> (UL) which was a planned third league of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB).\n</p><p>The stadium hosted <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>\'s Opening Day Game in 2001, in which the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Blue_Jays" title="Toronto Blue Jays">Toronto Blue Jays</a> faced the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Rangers_(baseball)" title="Texas Rangers (baseball)">Texas Rangers</a> in an <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a> match-up.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> However, 4,000 who bought tickets were turned away when the police determined the safe capacity of the park had been vastly exceeded.\n</p><p>It was the object of a major overhaul under the mayoral administration of <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Santini" title="Jorge Santini">Jorge Santini</a>, before becoming the part-time home of the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Expos" title="Montreal Expos">Montreal Expos</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_League_(baseball)" title="National League (baseball)">National League</a> in <a href="/wiki/2003_Montreal_Expos_season" title="2003 Montreal Expos season">2003</a> and <a href="/wiki/2004_Montreal_Expos_season" title="2004 Montreal Expos season">2004</a> before their move to <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Nationals" title="Washington Nationals">Washington Nationals</a>. The Expos played 20 "home" games across the two seasons as a result of poor attendance at their home <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Stadium_(Montreal)" title="Olympic Stadium (Montreal)">Olympic Stadium</a> in Montreal. Before Major League Baseball\'s announcement of the Montreal Expos\' move to Washington, Puerto Rico and San Juan made an effort to lure the Expos franchise to the island territory permanently.\n</p><p>Hiram Bithorn Stadium hosted parts of the first two rounds of the <a href="/wiki/2006_World_Baseball_Classic" title="2006 World Baseball Classic">2006 World Baseball Classic</a>. <a href="/wiki/2006_World_Baseball_Classic_Pool_C" title="2006 World Baseball Classic Pool C">Pool C</a>, which included the teams of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_national_baseball_team" title="Puerto Rico national baseball team">Puerto Rico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cuba_national_baseball_team" title="Cuba national baseball team">Cuba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Panama_national_baseball_team" title="Panama national baseball team">Panama</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_baseball_team" title="Netherlands national baseball team">the Netherlands</a>, was played there. It also hosted <a href="/wiki/2006_World_Baseball_Classic_Pool_2" title="2006 World Baseball Classic Pool 2">Pool 2</a> of the second round of the Classic which featured Cuba, Puerto Rico, <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic_national_baseball_team" title="Dominican Republic national baseball team">the Dominican Republic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Venezuela_national_baseball_team" title="Venezuela national baseball team">Venezuela</a>, the top two finishers from <a href="/wiki/2006_World_Baseball_Classic_Pool_C" title="2006 World Baseball Classic Pool C">Pool C</a> and <a href="/wiki/2006_World_Baseball_Classic_Pool_D" title="2006 World Baseball Classic Pool D">Pool D</a>. <a href="/wiki/2009_World_Baseball_Classic_Pool_D" title="2009 World Baseball Classic Pool D">Pool D games</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2009_World_Baseball_Classic" title="2009 World Baseball Classic">2009 World Baseball Classic</a> were played there between March 7 and March 11, 2009. The Hiram Bithorn Stadium hosted the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Baseball_Classic" title="2013 World Baseball Classic">2013 World Baseball Classic</a> with <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_national_baseball_team" title="Puerto Rico national baseball team">Puerto Rico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venezuela_national_baseball_team" title="Venezuela national baseball team">Venezuela</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic_national_baseball_team" title="Dominican Republic national baseball team">Dominican Republic</a> and newcomers <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_baseball_team" title="Spain national baseball team">Spain</a> in Pool C.\n</p><p>In 2008, it served as the stadium for <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9ticos_de_San_Juan" class="mw-redirect" title="Atl\xc3\xa9ticos de San Juan">Atl\xc3\xa9ticos de San Juan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Academia_Quintana" title="Academia Quintana">Academia Quintana</a>, two soccer teams in the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Soccer_League" title="Puerto Rico Soccer League">Puerto Rico Soccer League</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>\'s first-ever professional soccer league.\n</p><p>In 2010, Major League Baseball returned to the stadium, as the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Marlins" class="mw-redirect" title="Florida Marlins">Florida Marlins</a> faced the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a> in a three-game series during the regular season.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The Marlins were to play the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a> on May 30 and 31, 2016 in honor of Roberto Clemente Day. However, on May 6, 2016, it was announced that the Puerto Rico games would be postponed due to the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%932016_Zika_virus_epidemic" class="mw-redirect" title="2015\xe2\x80\x932016 Zika virus epidemic">Zika virus outbreak</a>, and moved to <a href="/wiki/Marlins_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Marlins Park">Marlins Park</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ESPN-Puerto_Rico_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESPN-Puerto_Rico-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" class="mw-redirect" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Twins" title="Minnesota Twins">Minnesota Twins</a> played a two-game series at Hiram Bithorn Stadium on April 17 and 18, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is the first time since 2010 that a Major League Baseball regular-season game was played in Puerto Rico. Furthermore, the league announced in August 2019 that they would be returning for a three-game series in April 2020 between the <a href="/wiki/Miami_Marlins" title="Miami Marlins">Miami Marlins</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This series was later cancelled as a result of the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_Puerto_Rico" title="COVID-19 pandemic in Puerto Rico">COVID-19 pandemic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure
