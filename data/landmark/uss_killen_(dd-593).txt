href="/wiki/Fletcher-class_destroyer" title="Fletcher-class destroyer"><i>Fletcher</i>-class</a> <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyer</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Namesake">Namesake</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=USS_Killen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Namesake"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Edward Killen joined the Navy on 5 May 1801 as a seaman aboard the <a href="/wiki/Schooner" title="Schooner">schooner</a> <a href="/wiki/USS_Enterprise_(1799)" title="USS Enterprise (1799)">USS&#160;<i>Enterprise</i></a>. Accompanying her to the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean</a> he was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_Mate" class="mw-redirect" title="Master&#39;s Mate">Master\'s Mate</a> on 9 November 1803. He volunteered for <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant" title="Lieutenant">Lieutenant</a> <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Decatur" title="Stephen Decatur">Stephen Decatur</a>\'s expedition into <a href="/wiki/Tripoli,_Libya" title="Tripoli, Libya">Tripoli</a> Harbor on 16 February 1804 to destroy <a href="/wiki/USS_Philadelphia_(1799)" title="USS Philadelphia (1799)">USS&#160;<i>Philadelphia</i></a>, a United States <a href="/wiki/Frigate" title="Frigate">frigate</a> captured by Tripolitan <a href="/wiki/Pirate" class="mw-redirect" title="Pirate">pirates</a> in the <a href="/wiki/First_Barbary_War" title="First Barbary War">First Barbary War</a>. After successfully completing this mission on <a href="/wiki/USS_Intrepid_(1798)" title="USS Intrepid (1798)">USS&#160;<i>Intrepid</i></a>, Killen served on board <i>Enterprise</i> until his death on 24 July 1806.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Construction_and_commissioning">Construction and commissioning</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=USS_Killen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Construction and commissioning"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p><i>Killen</i> was <a href="/wiki/Ship_naming_and_launching" class="mw-redirect" title="Ship naming and launching">launched</a> on 10 January 1943, by the <a href="/wiki/Puget_Sound_Navy_Yard" class="mw-redirect" title="Puget Sound Navy Yard">Puget Sound Navy Yard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bremerton,_Washington" title="Bremerton, Washington">Bremerton, Washington</a>; sponsored by Mrs. Inez Cowdrey; and <a href="/wiki/Ship_commissioning" title="Ship commissioning">commissioned</a> on 4 May 1944.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="World_War_II">World War II</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=USS_Killen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: World War II"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>After shakedown <i>Killen</i> cleared <a href="/wiki/Port_Angeles,_Washington" title="Port Angeles, Washington">Port Angeles, Wash.</a> on 19 August 1944, escorted a <a href="/wiki/Convoy" title="Convoy">convoy</a> from <a href="/wiki/Pearl_Harbor" title="Pearl Harbor">Pearl Harbor</a> and arrived at <a href="/wiki/Manus_Island" title="Manus Island">Manus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Admiralty_Islands" title="Admiralty Islands">Admiralty Islands</a> on 14 September. Following training exercises the destroyer departed <a href="/wiki/Jayapura" title="Jayapura">Hollandia</a> on 12 October with the Central Philippine Attack Force that arrived off <a href="/wiki/San_Pedro_Bay_(Philippines)" title="San Pedro Bay (Philippines)">San Pedro Bay</a> on the 20th. For the next 5&#160;days she gave day and night fire support to troops ashore <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Leyte" title="Battle of Leyte">on Leyte</a>, and during one 30-minute period on the 21st silenced three enemy <a href="/wiki/Artillery" title="Artillery">artillery</a> positions. When the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Japanese Navy</a> decided to contest the landings in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Leyte_Gulf" title="Battle of Leyte Gulf">Battle of Leyte Gulf</a>, <i>Killen</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;s</span> squadron engaged the enemy <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Surigao_Strait" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Surigao Strait">at Surigao Strait</a>. On the morning of 25 October, at 03:25, she launched five <a href="/wiki/Torpedo" title="Torpedo">torpedoes</a> toward <a href="/wiki/Battleship" title="Battleship">battleship</a> <a href="/wiki/Japanese_battleship_Yamashiro" title="Japanese battleship Yamashiro"><i>Yamashiro</i></a>. One hit, slowing her to 5 knots (9.3&#160;km/h), enabled other American destroyers to maneuver for the kill. In the widespread fleet actions for <a href="/wiki/Leyte_(island)" class="mw-redirect" title="Leyte (island)">Leyte</a>, covering hundreds of thousands of nautical miles, the U.S. Fleet reduced the Japanese Fleet to an ineffective force thus greatly speeding up the advance toward Japan and end of the war.\n</p><p><i>Killen</i> resumed antiaircraft screen. While on patrol off Leyte on 1 November she was attacked by seven enemy aircraft. The destroyer splashed four raiders before a bomb from one of the attackers found its mark in <i>Killen</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;s</span> port side, killing 15. After temporary repairs at San Pedro Bay and Manus, she steamed into <a href="/wiki/Hunters_Point_Naval_Shipyard" title="Hunters Point Naval Shipyard">Hunter\'s Point, Calif</a>., on 15 January 1945, for overhaul.\n</p><p>Returning to Manus on 9 May, the destroyer sailed the next day for convoy escort and patrol duty in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>. Killen formed part of the screen for the <a href="/wiki/Cruiser" title="Cruiser">cruiser</a> USS Nashville carrying General <a href="/wiki/Douglas_MacArthur" title="Douglas MacArthur">Douglas MacArthur</a> to nearly every island in the chain to give his "I have returned" speech.\n</p><p>Lacking the propulsion system necessary to operate with the fast carriers around <a href="/wiki/Okinawa_Island" title="Okinawa Island">Okinawa</a>, <i>Killen</i> was kept in the south with the cruiser fleet. She steamed into <a href="/wiki/Brunei_Bay" title="Brunei Bay">Brunei Bay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Borneo" title="Borneo">Borneo</a>, on 10 June with <a href="/wiki/Operation_Oboe_Six" class="mw-redirect" title="Operation Oboe Six">the assault forces</a>, and supported the troops with prelanding bombardment. She resumed exercises on 15 June before arriving <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Balikpapan_(1945)" title="Battle of Balikpapan (1945)">off Balikpapan</a>, Borneo, on 27 June for fire support missions. After silencing enemy shore batteries on Borneo, <i>Killen</i> prepared for the final phase of the Pacific war as she arrived <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">Manila</a> on 14 July. She cleared that port 2&#160;weeks later, and joined the <a href="/wiki/North_Pacific_Force" class="mw-redirect" title="North Pacific Force">North Pacific Force</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Aleutian_Islands" title="Aleutian Islands">Aleutian Islands</a>.\n</p><p>Upon the cessation of hostilities the destroyer was assigned to the occupation forces in the Japanese islands. Departing <a href="/wiki/Adak_Island" title="Adak Island">Adak</a> on 31 August, <i>Killen</i> took up station at <a href="/wiki/Ominato" class="mw-redirect" title="Ominato">Ominato</a> in northern <a href="/wiki/Honsh%C5%AB" class="mw-redirect" title="Honsh\xc5\xab">Honsh\xc5\xab</a>, and supported the occupation forces until 14 November, when she sailed to <a href="/wiki/Puget_Sound" title="Puget Sound">Puget Sound</a>. From there she proceeded to <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a>, arriving on 2 April, and decommissioning on 9 July 1946.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 2x" data-file-width="738" data-file-height="490" /></a><figcaption>Hulk of <i>Killen</i> tied up at Roosevelt Roads, 1975.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The veteran destroyer served as a target ship during the atom bomb tests in 1958 (<a href="/wiki/Operation_Hardtack_I" title="Operation Hardtack I">Operation Hardtack I</a>, shots WAHOO and UMBRELLA),<sup id="cite_ref-DNA_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DNA-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and in 1962 engaged in high explosive tests in the <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay" title="Chesapeake Bay">Chesapeake Bay</a> to assess the structural effects of the ship\'s nuclear exposures. <i>Killen</i> was struck from the <a href="/wiki/Naval_Vessel_Register" title="Naval Vessel Register">Navy List</a> and sent to the US Naval Station at <a href="/wiki/Roosevelt_Roads" class="mw-redirect" title="Roosevelt Roads">Roosevelt Roads</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">P.R.</a> in January 1963 to be used as a target ship for missile and gunnery practice off the nearby Puerto Rican island of <a href="/wiki/Vieques" class="mw-redirect" title="Vieques">Vieques</a> where she was eventually sunk/scuttled in a shallow bay in 1975 and still lies today.\n</p><p>Studies and site visits made in 1999 by a Puerto Rican marine archaeologist and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Georgia" title="University of Georgia">University of Georgia</a> discovered nearly two hundred steel barrels of unknown origin and contents among the wreckage of the <i>Killen</i>. Based on government descriptions of the nuclear tests in the Pacific,<sup id="cite_ref-DNA_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DNA-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> some scientists and Vieques environmental activists have been concerned that nuclear-fallout cleaning materials were likely stored inside those barrels and improperly disposed, possibly entering the local environment prior to sinking or exposing contaminants to the animals and habitat of Bahia Salina del Sur in Vieques after sinking.\n</p><p>In 2003\xe2\x80\x932004, a Virginia-based company called Underwater Ordnance Recovery (UOR), in cooperation with the University of Georgia, was contracted by the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico\'s Justice Department to again study the ex-Killen wreck.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Nothing unexpected was found. In conclusion, the barrels were probably just used for ballast &amp; buoyancy during the ex-Killen\'s career as a target, and were never a danger at all. UOR\'s final conclusion, issued in March 2004, was that the ex-Killen presented minimal to no environmental danger and should be left alone as it is now serving as an artificial reef for sea life.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Awards">Awards</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a received two <a href="/wiki/Battle_star" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle star">battle stars</a> for <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> service.\n</p>\n<div
