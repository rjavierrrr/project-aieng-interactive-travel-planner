forests</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Sus\xc3\xbaa State Forest</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Bosque Estatal de Sus\xc3\xbaa</i>) is one of the 20 forest units that make up the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Puerto_Rico_state_forests" title="List of Puerto Rico state forests">public forest system</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. This forest is located in the southeastern foothills of the <a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Cordillera Central (Puerto Rico)">Central Mountain Range</a> or <i>Cordillera Central</i>, in the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Sabana_Grande,_Puerto_Rico" title="Sabana Grande, Puerto Rico">Sabana Grande</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yauco,_Puerto_Rico" title="Yauco, Puerto Rico">Yauco</a>. Most of the forest, particularly the lower parts, are of <a href="/wiki/Secondary_forest" title="Secondary forest">secondary growth</a> as the land had originally been <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforested</a> and used for <a href="/wiki/Pastoral_farming" class="mw-redirect" title="Pastoral farming">cattle grazing</a>, timber production and agriculture. The forest is an important habitat for the endangered <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_nightjar" title="Puerto Rican nightjar">Puerto Rican nightjar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sus%C3%BAa_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The forest reserve was proclaimed in 1935 by the forestry division of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Reconstruction_Administration" title="Puerto Rico Reconstruction Administration">Puerto Rico Reconstruction Administration</a> (PRRA) and subject to the 1917 Puerto Rico Forestry Law. The forest is now managed by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Natural_and_Environmental_Resources" title="Puerto Rico Department of Natural and Environmental Resources">Puerto Rico Department of Natural and Environmental Resources</a> (DRNA). The forest is also important for its <a href="/wiki/Archaeological_excavation" title="Archaeological excavation">archaeological</a> value as many <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> sites have been uncovered in the forest.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Ecology">Ecology</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sus%C3%BAa_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ecology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Endangered_Puerto_Rican_nightjar_face_%287840009282%29.jpg/440px-Endangered_Puerto_Rican_nightjar_face_%287840009282%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1518" data-file-height="1012" /></a><figcaption>The forest is important to the endangered <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_nightjar" title="Puerto Rican nightjar">Puerto Rican nightjar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></figcaption></figure>\n<p>The forest is an important transition zone from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_and_subtropical_moist_broadleaf_forests" title="Tropical and subtropical moist broadleaf forests">subtropical moist broadleaf</a> biome to a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_and_subtropical_dry_broadleaf_forests" title="Tropical and subtropical dry broadleaf forests">subtropical dry broadleaf</a> biome which is important to several endangered species such as the Puerto Rican nightjar (<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_nightjar" title="Puerto Rican nightjar"><i>Antrostomus noctitherus</i></a>). The forest is also considered an important ecological corridor between the <a href="/wiki/Gu%C3%A1nica_State_Forest" title="Gu\xc3\xa1nica State Forest">Gu\xc3\xa1nica dry forest</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Maricao_State_Forest" title="Maricao State Forest">Maricao State Forest</a>. There is a high rate of endemism in the forest and species such as the <i><a href="/wiki/Zamia_portoricensis" title="Zamia portoricensis">Zamia portoricensis</a></i> and the <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Calliandra_locoensis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Calliandra locoensis (page does not exist)">Calliandra locoensis</a></i> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Fabaceae" title="Fabaceae">Fabaceae</a></i> family is only found in the area and nowhere else in Puerto Rico or the world. \n</p><p>Some endangered or threatened species found in the forest are the pincho rose tree (<i><a href="/wiki/Ottoschulzia_rhodoxylon" title="Ottoschulzia rhodoxylon">Ottoschulzia rhodoxylon</a></i>), the black cobana (<i><a href="/wiki/Libidibia_monosperma" title="Libidibia monosperma">Libidibia monosperma</a></i>), the Sierra higuero (<i><a href="/wiki/Crescentia_portoricensis" title="Crescentia portoricensis">Crescentia portoricensis</a></i>) and the threatened red fruit bat (<i><a href="/wiki/Red_fruit_bat" title="Red fruit bat">Stenoderma rufum</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Recreation">Recreation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sus%C3%BAa_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Recreation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Hiking is permitted in the forest on weekdays from 7:30 a.m. to 4:00 p.m. although visitors must contact the forest offices (787-999-2200 Ext. 5156) before arriving. Camping is allowed ($6 per tent) and cabins are also available for rent ($40 per night).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The forest office also offers group tours and guided hikes, and support for researchers interested in conducting scientific investigation in the forest.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span
