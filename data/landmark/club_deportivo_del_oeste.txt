is a private club in Cabo Rojo, Puerto Rico which has the largest marina and one of only two golf courses in the  <a href="/wiki/Porta_del_Sol" title="Porta del Sol">Porta del Sol</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Club_Deportivo_del_Oeste&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Club Deportivo del Oeste was founded in 1965 by prominent professionals from <a href="/wiki/Mayag%C3%BCez" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayag\xc3\xbcez">Mayag\xc3\xbcez</a>, Cabo Rojo and other Municipalities from western Puerto Rico in Joyuda Barrio of Cabo Rojo.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Among its facilities are an eighteen hole golf course in approximately 120 acres (0.49&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) of land, its first hole is considered the hardest in all of Puerto Rico<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Rodriguez_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rodriguez-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The course was designed by Jack Bender in 1965.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The golf course is one of two in the Porta del Sol region the other being in Aguadilla.\n<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>It also has a marina, four tennis courts, a club house for meetings and parties, a members only restaurant, pool, gym and a recreational park for children.\n</p><p>It annually holds the "Torneo Internacional de Aguja Azul en L\xc3\xadnea Liviana" or "International <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_blue_marlin" title="Atlantic blue marlin">Atlantic blue marlin</a> Light Line <a href="/wiki/Fishing_tournament" title="Fishing tournament">Fishing tournament</a>" which is the biggest of its kind in Puerto Rico and in 2010 celebrates its twenty second edition.<sup id="cite_ref-Rodriguez_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rodriguez-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Two fishing records of released fish have been established at the tournament; in 2003 a (178 blue marlins released) and in 2005 a total of 205 blue marlins released at the tournament.\n<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The club annually serves non for profit organizations such as the <a href="/wiki/Scouting_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Scouting in Puerto Rico">Boy Scouts</a>, the "Hogar para ni\xc3\xb1os con C\xc3\xa1ncer" and "Centro Espibi".  The club also holds navigational courses offered by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a> and free golf clinics for children of low income families.<sup id="cite_ref-Rodriguez_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rodriguez-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The "Deportivo" also offers scholarships for summer camps to students, it serves as a dock for the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Natural_and_Environmental_Resources" title="Puerto Rico Department of Natural and Environmental Resources">Puerto Rico Department of Natural and Environmental Resources</a> and it allows the Navy to use its vicinity to place an antenna used to measure the tide.  Furthermore the club will serve for the installation of a Tsunami Ready alarm for Cabo Rojo.<sup id="cite_ref-Rodriguez_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rodriguez-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Presidents">Presidents</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Club_Deportivo_del_Oeste&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Presidents"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<table class="wikitable">\n\n<tbody><tr style="background:#eee;">\n<th>\n</th>\n<th>President\n</th>\n<th>Years\n</th></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>1\n</td>\n<td>Salvador Suau, Noel Pi\xc3\xb1eiro\n</td>\n<td>1965\xe2\x80\x931966\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>2\n</td>\n<td>Ing.
