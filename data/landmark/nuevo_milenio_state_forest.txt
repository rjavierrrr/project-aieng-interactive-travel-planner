title="Puerto Rican moist forests">Puerto Rican moist forests</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Nuevo Milenio State Forest</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Bosque Estatal del Nuevo Milenio</i> or <i>Bosque Urbano del Nuevo Milenio</i>) is one of the 20 forests that make up the public forest system of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The forest is located east of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Botanical_Garden" title="San Juan Botanical Garden">University of Puerto Rico Botanical Garden</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Sabana_Llana_Sur,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="Sabana Llana Sur, San Juan, Puerto Rico">Sabana Llana Sur</a> district of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> making it one of the two state forests located within the capital\'s municipal boundaries (the other being <a href="/wiki/San_Patricio_State_Forest" title="San Patricio State Forest">San Patricio State Forest</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuevo_Milenio_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The area where the forest now stands had originally been deforested and was the site of several quarries. The forest reserve was first proclaimed in 1998 with the intention of preserving one of the last green areas in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Secondary_forest" title="Secondary forest">secondary forest</a>. The city is very densely populated and has lost most of its forests due to <a href="/wiki/Urban_sprawl" title="Urban sprawl">urban sprawl</a>. This was done thanks to the effort of local residents, academics and environmentalists. In 2003 the forest was added to the <i>San Juan Ecological Corridor</i> management zone, also managed by the municipality of San Juan which designates the area as ecologically important as it forms part of the hydrological basin of the <a href="/wiki/Estuary" title="Estuary">estuary</a> of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Bay" title="San Juan Bay">San Juan Bay</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Between the years 1998 and 2005 the forest was used for research purposes for studying the impact of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Georges" title="Hurricane Georges">Hurricane Georges</a> in the destruction and regrowth of the forest.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Ecology">Ecology</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuevo_Milenio_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ecology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flora">Flora</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuevo_Milenio_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Flora"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The forest is very characteristic of the <a href="/wiki/Secondary_forest" title="Secondary forest">secondary forest</a> ecosystem found throughout Puerto Rico. Some native tree species found in the forest are the yarumo (<i><a href="/wiki/Cecropia" title="Cecropia">Cecropia schreberiana</a></i>), the West Indian locust tree (<i><a href="/wiki/Hymenaea_courbaril" title="Hymenaea courbaril">Hymenaea courbaril</a></i>), the Puerto Rican royal palm tree (<i><a href="/wiki/Roystonea_borinquena" title="Roystonea borinquena">Roystonea borinquena</a></i>), and the pink trumpet tree (<i><a href="/wiki/Tabebuia_heterophylla" title="Tabebuia heterophylla">Tabebuia heterophylla</a></i>) or Puerto Rican oak as it is colloquially known. Common introduced trees found in the forest are the flea tree (<i><a href="/wiki/Albizia_lebbeck" title="Albizia lebbeck">Albizia lebbeck</a></i>), the karoi tree (<i><a href="/wiki/Albizia_procera" title="Albizia procera">Albizia procera</a></i>) and the Malay pterocarpus (<i><a href="/wiki/Pterocarpus_indicus" title="Pterocarpus indicus">Pterocarpus indicus</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Fauna">Fauna</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuevo_Milenio_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Fauna"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The forest is home to native species such as the zenaida dove (<a href="/wiki/Zenaida_dove" title="Zenaida dove"><i>Zenaida aurita</i></a>), the scaly-naped pigeon (<i><a href="/wiki/Scaly-naped_pigeon" title="Scaly-naped pigeon">Patagioenas squamosa</a></i>), the gray kingbird (<a href="/wiki/Gray_kingbird" title="Gray kingbird"><i>Tyrannus dominicensis</i></a>), the northern mockingbird (<i><a href="/wiki/Northern_mockingbird" title="Northern mockingbird">Mimus polyglottos</a></i>), bananaquits (<i><a href="/wiki/Bananaquit" title="Bananaquit">Coereba flaveola</a></i>), red-tailed hawks (<a href="/wiki/Red-tailed_hawk" title="Red-tailed hawk"><i>Buteo jamaicensis</i></a>) and the Puerto Rican mango (<i><a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_mango" title="Puerto Rican mango">Anthracothorax aurulentus</a></i>); endemic species such as the Puerto Rican woodpecker (<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_woodpecker" title="Puerto Rican woodpecker"><i>Melanerpes portoricensis</i></a>), the Puerto Rican spindalis (<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_spindalis" title="Puerto Rican spindalis"><i>Spindalis portoricensis</i></a>), the Puerto Rican tanager (<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_tanager" title="Puerto Rican tanager"><i>Nesospingus speculiferus</i></a>) and the green mango hummingbird (<a href="/wiki/Green_mango" title="Green mango"><i>Anthracothorax viridis</i></a>); and migratory species such as the black-and-white warbler (<a href="/wiki/Black-and-white_warbler" title="Black-and-white warbler"><i>Mniotilta varia</i></a>), the Caribbean martin (<a href="/wiki/Caribbean_martin" title="Caribbean martin"><i>Progne dominicensis</i></a>) and the black-whiskered vireo (<a href="/wiki/Black-whiskered_vireo" title="Black-whiskered vireo"><i>Vireo altiloquues</i></a>). The forest is also home to several reptile and amphibian species such as the Puerto Rican crested anole (<i><a href="/wiki/Anolis_cristatellus" title="Anolis cristatellus">Anolis cristatellus</a></i>) and the common coqui (<i><a href="/wiki/Leptodactylus_albilabris" title="Leptodactylus albilabris">Leptodactylus albilabris</a></i>).\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Recreation">Recreation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nuevo_Milenio_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Recreation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Although being located next to the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Botanical_Garden" title="San Juan Botanical Garden">San Juan Botanical Garden</a>, the urban forest is not usually open to visitors and those interested in visiting it should contact the forest offices to arrange a visit (787-772-2009). The forest offers great opportunities for birdwatching and nature photography for those who do visit.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="See_also">See also</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
