print{.mw-parser-output .navbar{display:none!important}}</style><div class="navbar plainlinks hlist navbar-mini"><ul><li class="nv-view"><a href="/wiki/Template:Sidebar_Tourism_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Template:Sidebar Tourism in Puerto Rico"><abbr title="View this template">v</abbr></a></li><li class="nv-talk"><a href="/wiki/Template_talk:Sidebar_Tourism_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Template title="Special:EditPage/Template:Sidebar Tourism in Puerto Rico"><abbr title="Edit this template">e</abbr></a></li></ul></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>El Tuque</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Beach" title="Beach">beach</a> and family <a href="/wiki/Recreation" title="Recreation">recreational</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tourist" class="mw-redirect" title="Tourist">tourist</a> complex in the Punta Cucharas sector of <a href="/wiki/Barrio_Canas" class="mw-redirect" title="Barrio Canas">Barrio Canas</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>.  It was designed in the early 1960s by <a href="/wiki/List_of_Puerto_Rican_architects" title="List of Puerto Rican architects">Luis Flores</a>, an architect from <a href="/wiki/Cayey,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cayey, Puerto Rico">Cayey, Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  It is located on <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_2" title="Puerto Rico Highway 2">PR-2</a>, Km 220.1, in the El Tuque sector of Barrio <a href="/wiki/Canas_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Canas (Ponce)">Canas</a> in Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The sector of El Tuque is considered Puerto Rico\'s largest populated sector.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The beach opened on 17 July 1965.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Tuque&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The name <i>El Tuque</i> comes from the name of a farm of measuring 267.11 <a href="/wiki/Cuerda" title="Cuerda">cuerdas</a> that was located in that area of Barrio Canas in the early 20th century.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The once deserted beach was developed into a <i>balneario</i> (balneary or bathhouse) by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Government of Puerto Rico">government of the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico</a> in the mid-1960s, as part of the central government\'s attempt to provide a network of such recreational facilities throughout the island.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It included gazebos and fire pits near the beach, a restaurant, changing rooms with showers and lockers, a children\'s pool, and two adult Olympic-size swimming pools with snack bars. It was inaugurated on 17 July 1965.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Due to <a href="/wiki/Coastal_erosion" title="Coastal erosion">beach erosion</a> that occurred over the years, a <a href="/wiki/Beach_nourishment" title="Beach nourishment">beach replenishment</a> project then took place in 1979,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> but the sand added was darker than the original sand there and locals now consider the beach "dirty".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>By the early-1980s, however, the area had fallen into disrepair, starting with the swimming pools and parking areas, for lack of proper maintenance by the Commonwealth Government. As a result, the beach opened only sporadically.  Eventually the complex was permanently shut down in the late 1980s. The Municipality of Ponce, then obtained ownership of the property from the Commonwealth government via a transfer.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> About a decade later, in 1996, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Corps_of_Engineers" title="United States Army Corps of Engineers">United States Army Corps of Engineers</a> picked up the El Tuque project again for purposes of re-furbishing the beach with new sand.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="El_Tuque_Recreational_Complex">El Tuque Recreational Complex</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Tuque&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: El Tuque Recreational Complex"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In May 2002, a group of private investors became interested in the area and entered into a contract with the Puerto Rico central government to develop the area into a family and tourist recreational center. The investors spent $16 million to develop the area into the El Tuque Beach Entertainment Center.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> El Tuque Recreational Complex opened in the summer of 2002 and included an aquatic complex / splash water park with three pools with waves, high speed water slides, and water sports. Some of activities of the park were hiking, swimming, boating, fishing, whitewater paddling, snorkeling, and scuba diving.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The aquatic park closed in 2007, the same year that an accident at the water park injured a park patron leaving him quadriplegic.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The complex had a pub and a <a href="/wiki/Quality_Inn" title="Quality Inn">Quality Inn</a> hotel located on the beach, within the recreational complex facilities.  A nearby <a href="/wiki/Holiday_Inn" title="Holiday Inn">Holiday Inn</a> hotel that had opened a few years earlier, provides additional lodging facilities for beach-goers.\n</p><p>A second addition to the project, which opened in 2003, includes a new <a href="/wiki/Oval_track_racing" title="Oval track racing">speedway</a> called the <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_de_Ponce" title="Grand Prix de Ponce">Ponce International Speedway Park</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It includes a drag-racing track and circuit track. The speedway drag-racing sections has both 1/4-mile and 1/8th-mile sections, and the full circuit is 1.52 miles long and incorporates 12 turns.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> A small <a href="/wiki/Marina" title="Marina">marina</a> has also been added. Boating and <a href="/wiki/Kayak" title="Kayak">kayaking</a> is promoted as part of the center\'s eco-tourism activities.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Solace_by_the_Sea">Solace by the Sea</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=El_Tuque&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Solace by the Sea"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 2017, following <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria_(2017)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hurricane Maria (2017)">Hurricane Mar\xc3\xada</a>, Grupo Misla Villalba, a group of local investors, remodeled the former Quality Inn Hotel, which had closed 2 years before, and opened a new hotel, named "Solace by the Sea" on 30 November 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div
