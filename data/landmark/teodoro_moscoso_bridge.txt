height: 200px;" data-mw-kartographer="mapframe" data-style="osm-intl" data-width="250" data-height="200" data-zoom="10" data-lat="18.424444444444" data-lon="-66.024722222222" data-overlays="[&quot;_d3a8006c788d0ce1c485cdfa4172a6473bfc45c3&quot;]" href="/wiki/Special:Map/10/18.424444444444/-66.024722222222/en"><img src="https://maps.wikimedia.org/img/osm-intl,10,18.424444444444,-66.024722222222,250x200.png?lang=en&amp;domain=en.wikipedia.org&amp;title=Teodoro_Moscoso_Bridge&amp;revid=1246960561&amp;groups=_d3a8006c788d0ce1c485cdfa4172a6473bfc45c3" width="250" height="200" decoding="async" srcset="https://maps.wikimedia.org/img/osm-intl,10,18.424444444444,-66.024722222222,250x200@2x.png?lang=en&amp;domain=en.wikipedia.org&amp;title=Teodoro_Moscoso_Bridge&amp;revid=1246960561&amp;groups=_d3a8006c788d0ce1c485cdfa4172a6473bfc45c3 2x" alt="Map" /></a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Teodoro Moscoso Bridge</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Puente Teodoro Moscoso</i>) is a bridge in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. It works as an extension of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_17" title="Puerto Rico Highway 17">PR-17</a>, also known as the <a href="/wiki/Jesus_T._Pi%C3%B1ero" class="mw-redirect" title="Jesus T. Pi\xc3\xb1ero">Jesus T. Pi\xc3\xb1ero</a> Expressway, connecting this  thoroughfare with the <a href="/wiki/Rom%C3%A1n_Baldorioty_de_Castro" title="Rom\xc3\xa1n Baldorioty de Castro">Rom\xc3\xa1n Baldorioty de Castro</a> Expressway (<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_26" title="Puerto Rico Highway 26">PR-26</a>). It crosses the <a href="/wiki/Laguna_San_Jos%C3%A9_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Laguna San Jos\xc3\xa9 (Puerto Rico)">San Jos\xc3\xa9 Lagoon</a> thereby linking <a href="/wiki/Hato_Rey" title="Hato Rey">Hato Rey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rio_Piedras,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Rio Piedras, Puerto Rico">Rio Piedras</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a> to <a href="/wiki/Isla_Verde,_Puerto_Rico" title="Isla Verde, Puerto Rico">Isla Verde</a> in <a href="/wiki/Carolina,_Puerto_Rico" title="Carolina, Puerto Rico">Carolina</a>. The bridge has become the new entrance to the <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Mar%C3%ADn_International_Airport" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn International Airport">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn International Airport</a>. It was opened on February 28, 1994 under the administration of Governor <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Rossell%C3%B3" title="Pedro Rossell\xc3\xb3">Pedro Rossell\xc3\xb3</a>, this construction being the first to be held under a Public Private Partnership in Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The bridge consists of four lanes, <a href="/wiki/Variable-message_sign" title="Variable-message sign">electronic signs</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Toll_plaza" class="mw-redirect" title="Toll plaza">toll plaza</a> with a fee of $3.65 in each direction.<sup id="cite_ref-Sube_el_peaje_del_puente_Teodoro_Moscoso_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sube_el_peaje_del_puente_Teodoro_Moscoso-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The bridge has flagpoles on both sides with alternating <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_United_States" title="Flag of the United States">American</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Flag of Puerto Rico">Puerto Rican</a> flags. The bridge accepts <i><a href="/wiki/AutoExpreso" title="AutoExpreso">AutoExpreso</a></i>, Puerto Rico\'s toll transponder system.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span mw-heading2"><h2 id="Etymology">Etymology</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teodoro_Moscoso_Bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The bridge is named after <a href="/wiki/Teodoro_Moscoso" title="Teodoro Moscoso">Teodoro Moscoso</a>, known as "the architect of <a href="/wiki/Operation_Bootstrap" title="Operation Bootstrap">Operation Bootstrap</a>", a plan of <a href="/wiki/Economic_development" title="Economic development">economic expansion</a> after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Overview">Overview</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teodoro_Moscoso_Bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Overview"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>As one of the longest bridges in Puerto Rico, it measures 1.4 miles (2.3&#160;km) in length. From <a href="/wiki/Hato_Rey" title="Hato Rey">Hato Rey</a> and <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Piedras,_Puerto_Rico" title="R\xc3\xado Piedras, Puerto Rico">Rio Piedras</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, the bridge serves as a direct entrance to <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Mar%C3%ADn_International_Airport" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn International Airport">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn International Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Isla_Verde,_Puerto_Rico" title="Isla Verde, Puerto Rico">Isla Verde</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carolina,_Puerto_Rico" title="Carolina, Puerto Rico">Carolina</a>. The bridge goes over <i><span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Laguna San Jos\xc3\xa9</i></span> (San Jose Lagoon)</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n</p><p>The bridge was built with an investment of $126.8 million. As of April 2016, the standard toll is $3.65 collected on the South Shore for southbound and northbound traffic, making it the most expensive toll fare in Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-Sube_el_peaje_del_puente_Teodoro_Moscoso_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sube_el_peaje_del_puente_Teodoro_Moscoso-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is the first project of privatization of road on the island and across the United States.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (September 2022)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> \n</p><p>The bridge holds one of the largest <a href="/wiki/10K_run" title="10K run">10K races</a> in the world, the <a href="/wiki/World%27s_Best_10K" title="World&#39;s Best 10K">World\'s Best 10K</a>, which attracts thousands of local and international competitors each year. \n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading
