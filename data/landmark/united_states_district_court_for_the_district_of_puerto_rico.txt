The court is based in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>.  The main building is the <a href="/wiki/Clemente_Ruiz_Nazario" title="Clemente Ruiz Nazario">Clemente Ruiz Nazario</a> United States Courthouse located in the <a href="/wiki/Hato_Rey" title="Hato Rey">Hato Rey</a> district of San Juan. The <a href="/wiki/United_States_magistrate_judge" title="United States magistrate judge">magistrate judges</a> are located in the adjacent Federico Degetau Federal Building, and several <a href="/wiki/Senior_status" title="Senior status">senior district judges</a> hold court at the <a href="/wiki/Jose_V._Toledo_Federal_Building_and_U.S._Courthouse" class="mw-redirect" title="Jose V. Toledo Federal Building and U.S. Courthouse">Jose V. Toledo Federal Building and U.S. Courthouse</a> in <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">Old San Juan</a>. The old courthouse also houses the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Bankruptcy_Court" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Bankruptcy Court">U.S. Bankruptcy Court</a>. Most appeals from this court are heard by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Court_of_Appeals_for_the_First_Circuit" title="United States Court of Appeals for the First Circuit">United States Court of Appeals for the First Circuit</a>, which is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a> but hears appeals at the Old San Juan courthouse for two sessions each year. <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">Patent</a> claims as well as claims against the U.S. government under the <a href="/wiki/Tucker_Act" title="Tucker Act">Tucker Act</a> are appealed to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Court_of_Appeals_for_the_Federal_Circuit" title="United States Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit">Federal Circuit</a>.\n</p><p>The current <b><a href="/wiki/United_States_attorney" class="mw-redirect" title="United States attorney">United States attorney</a></b> is <a href="/wiki/W._Stephen_Muldrow" title="W. Stephen Muldrow">W. Stephen Muldrow</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Scope_and_relevance">Scope and relevance</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_District_Court_for_the_District_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Scope and relevance"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The United States first established a federal court in Puerto Rico under the <a href="/wiki/Foraker_Act" title="Foraker Act">Foraker Act</a> of 1900. This court was a <a href="/wiki/United_States_territorial_court" title="United States territorial court">territorial court</a>, operating within what the Supreme Court would soon define in the <a href="/wiki/Insular_Cases" title="Insular Cases">Insular Cases</a> as an <a href="/wiki/Unincorporated_territories_of_the_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="Unincorporated territories of the United States">unincorporated territory of the United States</a>. As such, the court was established under <a href="/wiki/Article_Four_of_the_United_States_Constitution" title="Article Four of the United States Constitution">Article IV</a> rather than <a href="/wiki/Article_Three_of_the_United_States_Constitution" title="Article Three of the United States Constitution">Article III</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Constitution" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Constitution">United States Constitution</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_States" title="Supreme Court of the United States">Supreme Court of the United States</a> discussed the nature of the court in <i><a href="/wiki/Balzac_v._Porto_Rico" title="Balzac v. Porto Rico">Balzac v. Porto Rico</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Case_citation" title="Case citation">258 U.S. 298</a> (1922). Because the court was a territorial court rather than a full-fledged District Court, its judges did not enjoy Article III protections such as <a href="/wiki/Life_tenure" title="Life tenure">life tenure</a>.\n</p><p>The District Court in Puerto Rico continued to be an Article IV court even after Puerto Rico attained its <a href="/wiki/Political_status_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Political status of Puerto Rico">commonwealth status</a>. However, in 1966, the U.S. Congress conferred life tenure on the federal judges of Puerto Rico, transforming the court into a full-fledged Article III district court with the same status as the other <a href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court" class="mw-redirect" title="United States District Court">United States District Courts</a> throughout the country.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The congressional report on the bill making this change described the change of status as being "appropriate in light of the court\'s caseload and the conferral of Commonwealth status on Puerto Rico," and also explained:\n</p>\n<dl><dd>There is no reason why the U.S. District Judges for the District of Puerto Rico should not be placed in a position of parity as to tenure with all other Federal Judges throughout our judicial system. Moreover, federal litigants in Puerto Rico should not be denied the benefit of judges made independent by life tenure from the pressures of those who might influence his chances of reappointment, which benefits the Constitution guarantees to the litigants in all other Federal Courts. These judges in Puerto Rico have and will have the exacting same heavy responsibilities as all other Federal district judges and, therefore, they should have the same independence, security, and retirement benefits to which all other Federal district judges are entitled.</dd></dl>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Federico_Degetau_Federal_Building.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Federico_Degetau_Federal_Building.jpg/220px-Federico_Degetau_Federal_Building.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="154" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Federico_Degetau_Federal_Building.jpg/330px-Federico_Degetau_Federal_Building.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Federico_Degetau_Federal_Building.jpg/440px-Federico_Degetau_Federal_Building.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="449" /></a><figcaption>Federico Degetau Federal Building</figcaption></figure>\n<p>See 1966 U.S.C.C.A.N. 2786\xe2\x80\x9390; see also <i>Examining Bd. of Engineers Architects and Surveyors v. Flores de Otero</i>, 426 U.S. 572, 595 n.26 (1976) ("The reason given for this [law] was that the Federal District Court in Puerto Rico \'is in its jurisdiction, powers, and responsibilities the same as the U.S. district courts in the (several) states\'."). This important change in the federal judicial structure of the island was implemented not as a request of the Commonwealth government, but rather at the repeated request of the Judicial Conference of the United States. See Senate Report No. 1504, 1966 U.S.C.C.A.N. 2786\xe2\x80\x9390.\n</p><p>No similar law has been passed for the three insular territories that still have Article IV status, though there have been calls from time to time that these judges also deserve the protection of life tenure.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. Unlike the Supreme Court, where one justice is specifically nominated to be chief, the office of chief judge rotates among the district court judges. To be chief, a judge must have been in active service on the court for at least one year, be under the age of 65, and have not previously served as chief judge. \n</p><p>A vacancy is filled by the judge highest in seniority among the group of qualified judges. The chief judge serves for a term of seven years, or until age 70, whichever occurs first. The age restrictions are waived if no members of the court would otherwise be qualified for the position.\n</p><p>When the office was created in 1948, the chief judge was the longest-serving judge who had not elected to retire, on what has since 1958 been known as <a href="/wiki/Senior_status" title="Senior status">senior status</a>, or declined to serve as chief judge. After August 6, 1959, judges could not become or remain chief after turning 70 years old. The current rules have been in operation since October 1, 1982.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Succession_of_seats">Succession of seats</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_District_Court_for_the_District_of_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Succession of seats"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1216972533">.mw-parser-output .col-begin{border-collapse:collapse;padding:0;color:inherit;width:100%;border:0;margin:0}.mw-parser-output .col-begin-small{font-size:90%}.mw-parser-output .col-break{vertical-align:top;text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .col-break-2{width:50%}.mw-parser-output .col-break-3{width:33.3%}.mw-parser-output .col-break-4{width:25%}.mw-parser-output .col-break-5{width:20%}@media(max-width:720px){.mw-parser-output .col-begin,.mw-parser-output .col-begin>tbody,.mw-parser-output .col-begin>tbody>tr,.mw-parser-output .col-begin>tbody>tr>td{display:block!important;width:100%!important}.mw-parser-output .col-break{padding-left:0!important}}</style><div>\n<table class="col-begin" role="presentation">\n\n\n<tbody><tr>\n<td class="col-break">\n<table class="wikitable" style="">\n<tbody><tr>\n<th colspan="4" align="center" style="background:#ffdead;" width="200">Seat 1\n</th></tr>\n<tr>\n<td colspan="4" style="text-align:center;background:#ffdead;">Seat established on September 12, 1966 by 80&#160;Stat.&#160;764\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Hiram_Rafael_Cancio" title="Hiram Rafael Cancio">Cancio</a></td>\n<td>1967\xe2\x80\x931974\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Juan_R._Torruella" title="Juan R.
