class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Museo de la Arquitectura Ponce\xc3\xb1a</b> (Museum of Ponce Architecture) is an architecture museum housed at the <a href="/wiki/Casa_Wiechers-Villaronga" title="Casa Wiechers-Villaronga">Casa Wiechers-Villaronga</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. It is preserves the history of the architectural styles of Ponce and Puerto Rico. The Casa Wiechers-Villaronga was acquired and restored by the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Puerto_Rican_Culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Institute of Puerto Rican Culture">Institute of Puerto Rican Culture</a>. The museum is located in the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce Historic Zone</a>. The historic house was designed and built in 1912 by <a href="/wiki/Alfredo_Wiechers_Pieretti" title="Alfredo Wiechers Pieretti">Alfredo B. Wiechers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The house that is home to this architecture museum is itself an example of the architectural history of the city. The museum is housed at the historic <a href="/wiki/Casa_Wiechers-Villaronga" title="Casa Wiechers-Villaronga">Casa Wiechers-Villaronga</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Arquitectura_Ponce%C3%B1a&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1912, architect <a href="/wiki/Alfredo_B._Wiechers" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfredo B. Wiechers">Alfredo B. Wiechers</a> built the <a href="/wiki/Wiechers-Villaronga_Residence" class="mw-redirect" title="Wiechers-Villaronga Residence">Wiechers-Villaronga Residence</a> for his own residence and studio.\n</p><p>From 1911 to 1918, Wiechers was commissioned with various important buildings in Ponce, such as the Logia Aurora, Club Deportivo de Damas, the <a href="/wiki/Juan_Emilio_Vigui%C3%A9" title="Juan Emilio Vigui\xc3\xa9">Teatro Habana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Banco_de_Ponce_(building)" title="Banco de Ponce (building)">Banco of Ponce building</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hospital_de_Damas" class="mw-redirect" title="Hospital de Damas">Santo Asilo de Damas Hospital</a> among others, where he fully expressed the European <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">Neo-Classical</a> style which he had learned from European training.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1919, feeling political persecution from the American invaders, Mr. Wiechers sold the house to Mr. Gabriel Villaronga and moved to his native France. Many generations of Villarongas lived the house through the twentieth century.\n</p><p>In the early 1990s, and in order to establish the Museo de la Arquitectura Ponce\xc3\xb1a, the house was acquired and restored by the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Puerto_Rican_Culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Institute of Puerto Rican Culture">Institute of Puerto Rican Culture</a> (ICP).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1996, the museum opened as the home of the <i>Museo de la Arquitectura Ponce\xc3\xb1a y Planeacion Urbana</i> (Ponce Architecture and Urban Planning Museum).  The goal was to showcase Ponce\'s rich architectural heritage.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Arquitectura_Ponce%C3%B1a&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The museum is a U-shaped, one-storied structure, measuring 68\'-10" in width by 95\'-2" in length, located at the northeast corner of Calle Reina and Calle Mendez Vigo. It is one block west of <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Delicias" title="Plaza Las Delicias">Plaza Las Delicias</a>, the center of the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce Historic Zone</a>. Calle Reina has several other historic houses on it, including <a href="/wiki/Residencia_Subir%C3%A1" title="Residencia Subir\xc3\xa1">Residencia Subir\xc3\xa1</a> and <a href="/wiki/Casa_Miguel_C._Godreau" title="Casa Miguel C. Godreau">Casa Miguel C. Godreau</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Architecture">Architecture</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_de_la_Arquitectura_Ponce%C3%B1a&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Architecture"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The structure that is home to the museum is considered a "treasure trove of culture",<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> a jewel amongst the many antique Ponce mansions that have been preserved and converted into museums. The museum possesses elaborate neoclassical details, a majestic roof-top <a href="/wiki/Gazebo" title="Gazebo">gazebo</a>, and a full set of original and custom-made Catalan modernist furniture. There are also well-preserved shower and bathroom fixtures.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Behind all its highly elaborated decoration, with European <a href="/wiki/Baroque_architecture" title="Baroque architecture">Baroque</a> influence, essentially the structure is <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">neo-classic</a> in style, making a unique and yet elegant combination of both styles. The museum rests on a rusticated stone <a href="/wiki/Podium" title="Podium">podium</a>; its facades and main walls are of brick <a href="/wiki/Masonry" title="Masonry">masonry</a> with some interior partitions and walls of the gallery and kitchen in wood. Wooden beams supports a <a href="/wiki/Galvanized" class="mw-redirect" title="Galvanized">galvanized</a> zinc roof. The windows and doors of the museum are wooden with movable <a href="/wiki/Louver" title="Louver">louvers</a> and fixed colored glass inlets. A variety of flooring material is used throughout the house: from native cement-colored tiles in the dining area and <a href="/wiki/Vestibule_(architecture)" title="Vestibule (architecture)">vestibule</a>, 1" by 6" <a href="/wiki/Tongue-and-groove" class="mw-redirect" title="Tongue-and-groove">tongue-and-groove</a> wood slats in the living area and bedrooms, and ceramic tiles in the bathroom, to marble tiles at the entry-way.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The Baroque\'s influence is manifested immediately on the museum\'s rounded corners, typical of these residence types in Ponce. The corner is framed by two rusticated <a href="/wiki/Pilaster" title="Pilaster">pilasters</a> and divided into three bays, by two pilaster strips or <a href="/wiki/Lesene" title="Lesene">lesenes</a>. Each bay contains a wooden movable louvreed window with glass inlets at its top, and a floral relief <a href="/wiki/Motif_(visual_arts)" title="Motif (visual arts)">motif</a> over the <a href="/wiki/Window" title="Window">fenestration</a>. The rest of the wall is decorated with floral <a href="/wiki/Garland" title="Garland">garlands</a>. The corner is accentuated with a round sitting nook or "glorieta", that is detailed with <a href="/wiki/Ionic_order" title="Ionic order">Ionic</a> columns. Another interesting feature of the house are the <a href="/wiki/Balcony" title="Balcony">balconies</a> which are divided into three sections with Ionic columns and framed with Baroque <a href="/wiki/Molding_(decorative)" title="Molding (decorative)">moldings</a> and sculptured faces on the central top part of the openings. The forged-iron railings of the balconies are elaborated after the <a href="/wiki/Art_Nouveau" title="Art Nouveau">Art Nouveau</a> style. The facades are crowned with a continuous masonry <a href="/wiki/Cornice" title="Cornice">cornice</a>. On top of this cornice, a <a href="/wiki/Battlement" title="Battlement">battlement</a>-type <a href="/wiki/Parapet" title="Parapet">parapet</a> with sculptured lion faces and "<a href="/wiki/Candelabra" title="Candelabra">candelabra</a>" decorates the roof line of the structure.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Some of the outstanding architectural features present in this museum are highly decorative and detailed <a href="/wiki/Pilaster" title="Pilaster">pilasters</a>, rusticated <a href="/wiki/Podium" title="Podium">podium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cornice" title="Cornice">cornices</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Candelabra" title="Candelabra">candelabra</a>", <a href="/wiki/Relief" title="Relief">relief</a> and <a href="/wiki/Motif_(visual_arts)" title="Motif (visual arts)">motifs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ionic_order" title="Ionic order">Ionic</a> capitols, etc. They readily depict the Neo-Classical trend of the epoch. The Villaronga Residence is an outstanding example of this style and is one of two residences still standing of a series of houses designed and built by Wiechers, so important to the architectural and cultural heritage of the city of Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Of special architectural importance is the fact that all the furniture is original, including the bathroom appliances. Most of the furniture belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Modernisme" title="Modernisme">Modernisme</a> style (Catalan <a href="/wiki/Art_Nouveau" title="Art Nouveau">Art Nouveau</a>) and were imported from <a href="/wiki/Barcelona,_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Barcelona, Spain">Barcelona, Spain</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The hanging tapestry were painted by <a href="/wiki/Librado_Net" title="Librado Net">Librado Net</a>, a famed local artist.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The main entrance is off-centered and located on Reina Street. The entrance hall is decorated with sculptured tiles and the door is located at the end of a marbled stairway. The interior is well kept and unaltered. The interior areas are painted in different colors and the walls have a decorative plastered Art-Nouveau <a href="/wiki/Frieze" title="Frieze">frieze</a>. The ceiling in most of the areas is decorated embossed <a href="/wiki/Tin" title="Tin">tin</a> with a continuous decorative molding at its corners. The <a href="/wiki/Balcony" title="Balcony">gallery</a> and part of the kitchen walls are wooden with fixed wood louvered windows used for better ventilation and light. Other interesting details on the house are: the bathroom fixtures, such as the <a href="/wiki/Shower" title="Shower">shower</a> stall and the ceramic wall tiles which were imported from <a href="/wiki/Barcelona,_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Barcelona, Spain">Barcelona, Spain</a>, the light fixtures\xe2\x80\x94such as the ones at the dining-room, the master bedroom and living room\xe2\x80\x94which were also imported from Spain, and a "medio punto" at the dining area, typical of the architecture of this Southern area of Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2
