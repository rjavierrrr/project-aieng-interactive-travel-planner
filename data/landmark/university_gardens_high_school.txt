dir="ltr"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Magnet secondary school located in Hato Rey Sur, San Juan, Puerto Rico.</div>\n<p class="mw-empty-elt">\n</p><p><b>University Gardens High School</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i><b>Escuela Superior University Gardens</b></i>, generally abbreviated as <b>UGHS</b>), formally <b>University Gardens Community School Specialized in Science and Mathematics</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Secondary_school" title="Secondary school">secondary</a> <a href="/wiki/Magnet_school" title="Magnet school">magnet school</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Hato_Rey_Sur" title="Hato Rey Sur">Hato Rey Sur</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>. University Gardens is run by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Education" title="Puerto Rico Department of Education">Puerto Rico Department of Education</a> and is overseen by its Specialized Schools Unit (UnEE, for its initials in Spanish).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>University Gardens is known for its high standards, its "hard work" culture coming into the national culture.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Recognized for featuring some of the highest standardized test scores in Puerto Rico, it has been criticized for having 27.8% of its student population come from living below the <a href="/wiki/Poverty_threshold" title="Poverty threshold">poverty line</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_Gardens_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Since at least 1972 a high and middle school already existed in the gated eponymous community,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> located in the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Piedras,_Puerto_Rico" title="R\xc3\xado Piedras, Puerto Rico">R\xc3\xado Piedras</a> district.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The University Gardens neighborhood is located right along <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_52" title="Puerto Rico Highway 52">PR-52</a>, and close to the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico,_R%C3%ADo_Piedras_Campus" title="University of Puerto Rico, R\xc3\xado Piedras Campus">University of Puerto Rico, R\xc3\xado Piedras campus</a>. It has historically been composed mostly of middle class and upper lower class families, and serves as "serves as a buffering zone between the rich and lower class areas of San Juan". Currently, there has been an influx of upper class individuals, who have transformed some residence into mansions.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1976, it was converted into a magnet school.\n</p><p>Rox\xc3\xa1nna I. Duntley-Matos described the school\'s appearance as it was in 1981 as:\n</p>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1244412712">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 32px}.mw-parser-output .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;margin-top:0}@media(min-width:500px){.mw-parser-output .templatequotecite{padding-left:1.6em}}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>[having] a one level concrete building with modern aluminum-lined see-through glass doors. It gleamed of science\xe2\x80\x94air conditioned science\xe2\x80\x94surrounded by tropical concrete benches in the front patio, embraced by what seemed to be a square wired fence. Youth, dressed in yellow polos and dark blue pants, strolled around the yard discussing each other\'s romantic adventures, the latest movies, the upcoming parties or school special events, the homework they had failed to complete or tests they would soon have to face.</p><div class="templatequotecite">\xe2\x80\x94&#8202;<cite>Roxanna I. Duntley-Matos, <i>Transformative Accomplices: Multicultural Community Organizing in a Transnational Educational Context</i>, <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></cite></div></blockquote>\n<p>It used to offer grades 10th-12th,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> however this was changed by a Department of Education reorganization.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Hurricane_Mar\xc3\xada,_earthquakes_and_COVID-19"><span id="Hurricane_Mar.C3.ADa.2C_earthquakes_and_COVID-19"></span>Hurricane Mar\xc3\xada, earthquakes and COVID-19</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_Gardens_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Hurricane Mar\xc3\xada, earthquakes and COVID-19"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>After the <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Puerto_Rico_earthquakes" title="2019\xe2\x80\x9320 Puerto Rico earthquakes">2019\xe2\x80\x9320 Puerto Rico earthquakes</a> UGHS was evaluated and, while the field inspector declared the school "suitable for immediate operation and occupancy," they did note that there were fissures on the walls, as well as the fa\xc3\xa7ade of a wall that was close to breakings off.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>During February 2021, it was one of 172 public schools identified as "suitable to open" that formed part of an initial phase proposed by the Department to resume in-person teaching starting in March.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Nevertheless, this plan was abandoned, and in-person classes did not occur until 13 May.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On the first day of class seniors came in a caravan on the first day of school, as part of their traditional celebration for the beginning of their final school year. Due to the <a href="/wiki/Impact_of_the_COVID-19_pandemic_on_education" title="Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on education">COVID-19 pandemic</a>, from August 2021 onwards, classes will start at 8:00&#160;a.m. and end at 1:00&#160;p.m., after students receive their take-out only lunch.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="School_community">School community</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_Gardens_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: School community"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Accreditation">Accreditation</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_Gardens_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Accreditation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Puerto_Rico_Specialized_School_Unit_Logo.png" class="mw-file-description"><img alt="Capital &#39;&#39;U&#39;&#39; with red and color sections, denoting a magnet, followed by a minuscule &#39;&#39;n&#39;&#39; and two capital &#39;&#39;E&#39;&#39;s in black. However, in 2014, it was approved, but not accredited, by the Council.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>University Gardens is part of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Education" title="Puerto Rico Department of Education">Puerto Rico Department of Education\'s</a> Specialized Schools Unit (UnEE, for its initials in Spanish),<sup id="cite_ref-:2_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-15"><span class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Educational Transformation Projects,<sup id="cite_ref-:2_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Curriculum and Pedagogical Innovation Division,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and the Undersecretariat for Academic and Program Affairs.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1990, University Gardens was classified as an "Effective School", as instruction was based on <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Edmonds#Effective_schools_research" title="Ronald Edmonds">Ronald Edmonds\' model</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Being what is considered a five star school by the Department of Education, it receives less funds than those given to underperforming school, which receive more money for teachers\' professional development.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Recognized for featuring some of the highest standardized test scores in Puerto Rico, it has been criticized for having 27.8% of its student population come from living below the <a href="/wiki/Poverty_threshold" title="Poverty threshold">poverty line</a>, as of 2018,<sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></sup> down from 32.3% in the 1997-1998 school year, when it counted with a 758 total student population.<sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span
