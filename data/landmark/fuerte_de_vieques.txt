<b>Fort\xc3\xadn Conde de Mirasol</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-pr.gov_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr.gov-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> also known as <b>Fuerte de Vieques, is</b> a fort built in 1845 located in the town of Isabel Segunda in <a href="/wiki/Vieques" class="mw-redirect" title="Vieques">Vieques</a>, an island municipality of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. In 1991, the fort was restored by the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Puerto_Rican_Culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Institute of Puerto Rican Culture">Institute of Puerto Rican Culture</a>. The structure houses the <b>Vieques Museum of Art and History and the Vieques Historic Archives</b>, an extensive collection of documents related to the history of Vieques.<sup id="cite_ref-Welcome_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Welcome-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was listed on the <a href="#cite_note-nris-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and on the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Register_of_Historic_Sites_and_Zones" title="Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones">Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones</a> in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4"><span
