class="infobox-data">Hike</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Tres Picachos</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> for <i>Three Little Peaks</i>) is one of the highest peaks in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> at 968 meters (3,176&#160;ft). It is located on the border between  the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Ciales,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ciales, Puerto Rico">Ciales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico" title="Jayuya, Puerto Rico">Jayuya</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> in the central part of the island, and is part of the <a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Cordillera Central, Puerto Rico">Cordillera Central</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USDA_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USDA-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The mountain has three joint peaks from which the name is derived. It is believed that <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Ta\xc3\xadno people">Ta\xc3\xadno</a> Indians thought the mountain to be sacred.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading
