(<i>Plaza de San Jos\xc3\xa9</i> in Spanish) is a 19th century square located in the <a href="/wiki/Ensanche_de_Pontevedra" title="Ensanche de Pontevedra">centre of the city</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pontevedra" title="Pontevedra">Pontevedra</a> (<a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>), in the first urban expansion area, near the <a href="/wiki/Campolongo_(Pontevedra)" title="Campolongo (Pontevedra)">Campolongo</a> neighbourhood.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Origin_of_the_name">Origin of the name</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_de_San_Jos%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin of the name"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The square is named after the demolished St. Joseph\'s Chapel on the southeast side of the square.<sup id="cite_ref-capela_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-capela-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_de_San_Jos%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The square was formed at the end of the 19th century at the junction of the <a href="/wiki/Calle_Marqu%C3%A9s_de_Riestra" title="Calle Marqu\xc3\xa9s de Riestra">streets Marquis of Riestra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calle_de_la_Oliva" title="Calle de la Oliva">Oliva</a> and Augusto Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Besada. It is located in the former St. Joseph\'s Field, which owes its name to the chapel, now disappeared,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> which was located on the site currently occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Caixa_de_Pontevedra" title="Caixa de Pontevedra">Pontevedra Savings Bank building</a>. In the first half of the 19th century, in the St. Joseph\'s Field there was an oak grove that formed alleys under the shade of which a livestock fair was held (cattle, pigs, horses and sheep) on the first and fifteenth of each month.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n</p><p>The Baroque Chapel of St. Joseph was built in 1712. It was a medium-sized building whose fa\xc3\xa7ade featured the image of St. Joseph in a <a href="/wiki/Niche_(architecture)" title="Niche (architecture)">niche</a> in the upper part of a circular pediment above which were the coats of arms of the Gago and Montenegro families, among others. Its construction is attributed to Pablo Payo Gago de Mendoza y Sotomayor. Important weddings and funerals were celebrated here. It was demolished in the early 1940s because it interfered with the urban development of the city centre.<sup id="cite_ref-capela_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-capela-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The square was also the site of the Baroque pazo of the Gago de Mendoza and Montenegro families, which dates from the 15th century.<sup id="cite_ref-capela_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-capela-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The pazo was on the north side of the square, and its centre was located on the site now occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Caf%C3%A9_Moderno_(Pontevedra)" title="Caf\xc3\xa9 Moderno (Pontevedra)">Caf\xc3\xa9 Moderno</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Part of the structure remains to the left of the pazo house at the beginning of <a href="/wiki/Calle_de_la_Oliva" title="Calle de la Oliva">Oliva Street</a>. In the 1980s, a new floor was added and the original battlements were moved to the top. It had a central tower of three storeys and two lateral towers of two floors with pointed battlements in the centre of which were six-pointed stars inscribed in a circle. The area that is now the square was used centuries ago as the pazo\'s parade ground. It is the birthplace of the three Gago de Mendoza brothers, distinguished sailors.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On 12 April 1888, the M\xc3\xa9ndez N\xc3\xba\xc3\xb1ez Hotel (demolished in the 1980s) was inaugurated on the west side of the square. It had a restaurant and a meeting room. When it closed, it became a bus stop.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The hotel was born during the first hotel boom caused by the arrival of the railway in the nearby <a href="/wiki/Plaza_de_Galicia" title="Plaza de Galicia">Station Square</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1932, the editorial office of the literary magazine Cristal was located in the attic of the hotel and was visited by <a href="/wiki/Federico_Garc%C3%ADa_Lorca" title="Federico Garc\xc3\xada Lorca">Federico Garc\xc3\xada Lorca</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1902, the building of the <a href="/wiki/Caf%C3%A9_Moderno_(Pontevedra)" title="Caf\xc3\xa9 Moderno (Pontevedra)">Caf\xc3\xa9 Moderno</a> was finished, commissioned by Bernardo Mart\xc3\xadnez Bautista, an emigrant who became rich in Cuba, on the site of the former <a href="/wiki/Pazo" title="Pazo">pazo</a> of the Gago de Mendoza and Montenegro families. In 1903, the Caf\xc3\xa9 Moderno was inaugurated on the ground floor with a distinguished atmosphere, a meeting point for meetings and film screenings, and the paving of the square was carried out by the master builder Mr. Miranda.<sup id="cite_ref-capela_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-capela-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1904, the busy square was the setting for a film screening of Ali Baba.<sup id="cite_ref-capela_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-capela-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In the first decades of the 20th century, the square had a newstand in its centre and became a cultural epicentre and meeting place for Galician intellectuals.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In December 1943, the renovation of the paving and pavements of the square was approved, as well as the sewage system.<sup id="cite_ref-capela_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-capela-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1944, the Pontevedra Savings Bank acquired a building under construction on the site of the former St. Joseph\'s Chapel for its provincial headquarters and inaugurated the new building in 1948.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The square was called Plaza de San Jos\xc3\xa9 until 1931 when it was called Plaza de Pablo Iglesias. On 28th December 1936, the square was renamed Plaza de San Jos\xc3\xa9<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and in democracy it was called Plaza de Calvo Sotelo before it reverted to its traditional name of St. Joseph\'s Square on 25 April 1996.\n</p><p>The square was completely redeveloped in 2001 and transformed into a semi-pedestrian area.<sup id="cite_ref-fuente_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fuente-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-obras_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obras-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2006, the <a href="/wiki/Tertulia_Monument_(Literary_Circle_in_Modern_Coffee)" title="Tertulia Monument (Literary Circle in Modern Coffee)">Tertulia Monument</a> was installed in the centre of the square,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> as a tribute to the Galician intellectuals linked to the Caf\xc3\xa9 Moderno in the first decades of the 20th century.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Description">Description</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_de_San_Jos%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The square has an irregular triangular shape and the five <a href="/wiki/Calle_Marqu%C3%A9s_de_Riestra" title="Calle Marqu\xc3\xa9s de Riestra">streets of Marquis de Riestra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calle_de_la_Oliva" title="Calle de la Oliva">Oliva</a>, Rosal\xc3\xada de Castro, San Jos\xc3\xa9 and Augusto Garc\xc3\xada Besada converge here. It is one of the focal points of the <a href="/wiki/Ensanche_de_Pontevedra" title="Ensanche de Pontevedra">city\'s first urban expansion</a>.\n</p><p>The square is paved with alternating grey and sepia-coloured tiles<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and is semi-pedestrian, with a single lane of traffic in the northern part that channels traffic from Marquis de Riestra Street to Augusto Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Besada Street.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fuente_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fuente-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-obras_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obras-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The square is dominated to the north by the <a href="/wiki/Caf%C3%A9_Moderno_(Pontevedra)" title="Caf\xc3\xa9 Moderno (Pontevedra)">Caf\xc3\xa9 Moderno building</a>, and to the southeast by the <a href="/wiki/Caixa_de_Pontevedra" title="Caixa de Pontevedra">Pontevedra Savings Bank building</a>. In the middle of the square, on a base of polished black granite, stands the <a href="/wiki/Tertulia_Monument_(Literary_Circle_in_Modern_Coffee)" title="Tertulia Monument (Literary Circle in Modern Coffee)">Tertulia monument</a>, created in 2006 by the sculptor C\xc3\xa9sar Lombera to commemorate the meetings held here by Galician intellectuals.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Above the building of the Pontevedra Savings Bank overlooking the square is the <a href="/wiki/Teucer_statue" title="Teucer statue">statue of Teucer</a>, the mythical founder of the city, also created in 2006 by the sculptor C\xc3\xa1ndido Pazos.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The square has a total of sixteen trees: five Chinese <a href="/wiki/Magnolia" title="Magnolia">magnolias</a>, three <a href="/wiki/Malus_baccata" title="Malus baccata">Siberian crab apple</a> trees and three red apple trees, both grafted onto native apple varieties, as well as five <a href="/wiki/Malus_domestica" class="mw-redirect" title="Malus domestica">apple trees</a> grafted onto Asian apple trees.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBlanco_DiosCastro_Gonz\xc3\xa1lez2010137,139,140,141_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBlanco_DiosCastro_Gonz\xc3\xa1lez2010137,139,140,141-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Outstanding_buildings">Outstanding buildings</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_de_San_Jos%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Outstanding buildings"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>On the northwest side of the square are the remains of the Baroque <a href="/wiki/Pazo" title="Pazo">pazo</a> of the Gago de Mendoza and Montenegro families, which currently has two floors. Its pointed <a href="/wiki/Battlement" title="Battlement">battlements</a> stand out in the upper part, decorated in the centre with six-pointed stars inscribed in a circle.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On the north side of the square and next to the old pazo of the Gago de Mendoza family is the <a href="/wiki/Caf%C3%A9_Moderno_(Pontevedra)" title="Caf\xc3\xa9 Moderno (Pontevedra)">Caf\xc3\xa9 Moderno building</a>, with an <a href="/wiki/Eclecticism_in_architecture" title="Eclecticism in architecture">eclectic</a> stone fa\xc3\xa7ade and the most important <a href="/wiki/Art_Nouveau" title="Art Nouveau">art nouveau</a> interior in the city. It has three floors, housing the Caf\xc3\xa9 Moderno on the ground floor. The fa\xc3\xa7ade has wrought iron galleries and geometrically shaped ornamental elements.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On the southeast side of the square is the central <a href="/wiki/Caixa_de_Pontevedra" title="Caixa de Pontevedra">building of the Pontevedra Savings Bank</a>. It is a majestic stone building with four floors and a ground floor. The large hall, which can be accessed from the main entrance, connects the different areas of the building.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span
