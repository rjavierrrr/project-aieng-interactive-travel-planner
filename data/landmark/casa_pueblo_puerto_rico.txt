Pueblo</b> is an environmental community organization in <a href="/wiki/Adjuntas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Adjuntas, Puerto Rico">Adjuntas, Puerto Rico</a>, headed by <a href="/wiki/Alexis_Massol-Gonz%C3%A1lez" class="mw-redirect" title="Alexis Massol-Gonz\xc3\xa1lez">Alexis Massol-Gonz\xc3\xa1lez</a>, a civil engineer and winner of the 2002 <a href="/wiki/Goldman_Environmental_Prize" title="Goldman Environmental Prize">Goldman Environmental Prize</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span title="University of Puerto Rico at Mayag\xc3\xbcez">University of Puerto Rico at Mayag\xc3\xbcez</a>, is the assistant executive director of Casa Pueblo.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Through voluntary participation of individuals and groups,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> its mission is to explore, enjoy, and protect the wild places in Puerto Rico; to practice and promote the responsible use of the land\'s ecosystems and resources; to educate and enlist others to protect and restore the quality of the natural and human environment; and to use all lawful means to carry out these objectives, particularly in line with principles of <a href="/wiki/Radical_democracy" title="Radical democracy">radical democracy</a>, community <a href="/wiki/Self-managed_economy" class="mw-redirect" title="Self-managed economy">self-management</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Anti-Colonialism" class="mw-redirect" class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Casa_Pueblo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The organization was established in 1980 as a cultural center named <i>Taller de Arte y Cultura</i> (Art and Culture Workshop). In 1985, the organization acquired a house that was converted into a headquarters for the group and as a <a href="/wiki/NGO" class="mw-redirect" title="NGO">non-governmental</a>, independent, self-supporting, community cultural center.  The house was used as a cultural center, with a meeting and expositions hall, library, carts shop, butterfly garden and museum hall, running on solar energy. The cultural center was named Casa Pueblo, a name eventually adopted by the environmental organization itself.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Independent_sources" title="Wikipedia:Independent sources"><span title="This claim needs a reference to a independent, third-party source. (June 2020)">third-party source needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The organization has a radio station, with environmental and cultural programming; it opened an environmental school in August 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Ideology">Ideology</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Casa_Pueblo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ideology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Casa Pueblo has official policies on many conservation issues. They group these into 17 categories: agriculture, biotechnology, energy, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_justice" title="Environmental justice">environmental justice</a>, forest and wilderness management, global issues, government and political issues, land management, military issues, nuclear issues, oceans, pollution and waste management, precautionary principle, transportation, urban and land use policies, water resources, and wildlife conservation.\n</p><p>Casa Pueblo advocates for public investment in wind, solar, and other <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a>; for the creation of <a href="/wiki/Green_job" title="Green job">green jobs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">efficient energy use</a>; and against the development of new destructive mining projects.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>They believe in principles of <a href="/wiki/Radical_democracy" title="Radical democracy">radical democracy</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-:5_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> community <a href="/wiki/Self-managed_economy" class="mw-redirect" title="Self-managed economy">self-management</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and indigenous-centered <a href="/wiki/Anti-Colonialism" class="mw-redirect" title="Anti-Colonialism">anti-colonialism</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:6_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Some have compared the goals of Casa Pueblo to those of the <a href="/wiki/Zapatista_Army_of_National_Liberation" title="Zapatista Army of National Liberation">neo-Zapatistas</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Rebel_Zapatista_Autonomous_Municipalities" title="Rebel Zapatista Autonomous Municipalities">Rebel Zapatista Autonomous Municipalities</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:6_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Achievements">Achievements</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Casa_Pueblo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Achievements"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Some of the achievements of Casa Pueblo are:\n</p>\n<ul><li>In the early 1990s, successfully stopped gold mining operations in the Adjuntas area after a decade long opposition battle against the 2020 Plan, which activists accused of supporting <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_cleansing" title="Ethnic cleansing">ethnic cleansing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Casa_Pueblo._26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Casa_Pueblo.-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>In 1989, it began production of its own <a href="/wiki/Agricultural_cooperative" title="Agricultural cooperative">coffee cooperative</a>, <i>Madre Isla Coffee</i>, via a self-sufficient coffee farm maintained by community members and outside volunteers.<sup id="cite_ref-Casa_Pueblo._26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Casa_Pueblo.-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>Lobbied in the Government of Puerto Rico for the creation of the community-sponsored forest in the <a href="/wiki/Porta_Cordillera" title="Porta Cordillera">Puerto Rican mountain region</a> called <a href="/wiki/Pueblo_State_Forest" class="mw-redirect" title="Pueblo State Forest">Bosque del Pueblo</a>, the only <a href="/wiki/List_of_Puerto_Rico_state_forests" title="List of Puerto Rico state forests">forest in Puerto Rico</a> managed under an agreement between the government and a <a href="/wiki/NGO" class="mw-redirect" title="NGO">community-based organization</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>In 1995, the group also purchased <a href="/wiki/List_of_Puerto_Rico_state_forests" title="List of Puerto Rico state forests">Bosque del Pueblo</a>, a 737-acre nature conservation area.<sup id="cite_ref-Casa_Pueblo._26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Casa_Pueblo.-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>In 2003, Casa Pueblo opened the Communitarian Institute of Biodiversity and Culture, which offers high school level courses, undergraduate courses during summer, environmental workshops and alternative programs with a couple <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">stateside</a> universities.<sup id="cite_ref-Casa_Pueblo._26-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Casa_Pueblo.-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>In 2012, the organization successfully lobbied against the <a href="/wiki/V%C3%ADa_Verde_project" title="V\xc3\xada Verde project">V\xc3\xada Verde project</a>., ending the government\'s plan to run a gas pipeline through sensitive areas of Puerto Rico\'s interior.<sup id="cite_ref-saj.usace.army.mil_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-saj.usace.army.mil-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>In 2013, it inaugurated the "Escuela Bosque", a school for training in environmental issues, problems, and solutions.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>On 31 March 2013, Casa Pueblo successfully lobbied the Government of Puerto Rico for the establishment of the <i>"Corredor Ecol\xc3\xb3gico del Bosque Modelo"</i>, a conglomerate of model virgin forests in central Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>\n<li>Since 1999, Casa Pueblo has installed more than 1,000 solar panels in <a href="/wiki/Adjuntas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Adjuntas, Puerto Rico">Adjuntas, Puerto Rico</a> to establish energy independence via democratic <a href="/wiki/Community_ownership" title="Community ownership">community-owned</a> <a href="/wiki/Cooperative" title="Cooperative">cooperative</a> management, to weather the effects of <a href="/wiki/Privatization" title="Privatization">energy privatization</a> and <a href="/wiki/Natural_disaster" title="Natural disaster">natural disasters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Microgrid" title="Microgrid">microgrids</a> under this project support more than 350 homes and local businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n<ul><li>After <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> hit the area on 20 September 2017, the island was left without power. Casa Pueblo became an "energy oasis" as the sole energy provider of Adjuntas where people came to connect their life-saving equipment, and they distributed 10,000 solar lamps to members of the community.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Many mainstream politicians praised Casa Pueblo\'s role in supporting the community in lieu of a functioning energy grid, although Casa Pueblo had been a strong critic of <a href="/wiki/LUMA_Energy#Backlash,_controversy_and_protests" title="LUMA Energy">energy privatization</a>.</li>\n<li>After <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ian" title="Hurricane Ian">Hurricane Ian</a> in 2022, Casa Pueblo and its radio station, Radio Casa Pueblo, operational since 1999, continued to advocate on the importance of solar energy for Puerto Rican communities, especially those in the <a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Cordillera Central (Puerto Rico)">Cordillera Central</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Claridad_2022_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Claridad_2022-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li></ul></li>\n<li>Casa Pueblo has planted over 30,000 trees in line with indigenous practices, as part of reforestation efforts.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_23-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li></ul>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2
