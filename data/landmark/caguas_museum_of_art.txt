/></a><figcaption>View of the art museum from the Caguas Center for Fine Arts.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The <b>Caguas Museum of Art</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: Museo de Arte de Caguas) is an <a href="/wiki/Art_museum" title="Art museum">art museum</a> in <a href="/wiki/Caguas_barrio-pueblo" title="Caguas barrio-pueblo">Caguas Pueblo</a>, the downtown and administrative district of the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Caguas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Caguas, Puerto Rico">Caguas, Puerto Rico</a>. The first level of the museum is dedicated to Caguas native painter <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlos_Osorio_(Puerto_Rican_painter)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Carlos Osorio (Puerto Rican painter) (page does not exist)">Carlos Osorio</a> and the second level offers temporary exhibitions. The entrance is free and guided tours are available.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The museum also host cultural events.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Casa_Amarilla"><i>Casa Amarilla</i></h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caguas_Museum_of_Art&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Casa Amarilla"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The museum is located in a historic two-story, <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">neoclassical</a> building called <b>Casa Amarilla</b> (Spanish for \'yellow house) which dates to the early 20th century. It is located on the corner of Padial and Ruiz Belvis streets. This building used to be the residence of the administrator of the <a href="/wiki/American_Tobacco_Company" title="American Tobacco Company">American Tobacco Company</a> in 1911.<sup id="cite_ref-2"
