class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Ciudad Deportiva Millito Navarro</b> (Millito Navarro Sports City) is a multi-sport complex currently under construction in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>.  The complex is to consist of a <a href="/wiki/Soccer_stadium" class="mw-redirect" title="Soccer stadium">soccer stadium</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Baseball_field" title="Baseball field">baseball field</a>, three <a href="/wiki/Tennis_courts" class="mw-redirect" title="Tennis courts">tennis courts</a>, a track for <a href="/wiki/Track_and_field" title="Track and field">track and field</a>, a covered <a href="/wiki/Basketball_court" title="Basketball court">basketball court</a>, and a covered <a href="/wiki/Gym" title="Gym">gymnasium</a>. It is also planned to have schooling, physical training, and medical facilities on the premises.<sup id="cite_ref-elsuralavista_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elsuralavista-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Its projected cost is 32.3 million <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-periodicolaperla.com_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-periodicolaperla.com-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The complex is named after Ponce\'s <a href="/wiki/Emilio_Navarro" class="mw-redirect" title="Emilio Navarro">Millito Navarro</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> the first <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican people">Puerto Rican</a> to play baseball in the <a href="/wiki/Negro_leagues" class="mw-redirect" title="Negro leagues">Negro leagues</a> and who, living until age 105, was also the oldest living professional baseball player to have played in the Negro leagues.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Description_and_location">Description and location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ciudad_Deportiva_Millito_Navarro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description and location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Ciudad Deportiva is being built in an area of over 1 million square feet of land,<sup id="cite_ref-elsuralavista_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elsuralavista-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> between Urbanizacion Los Caobos and  Caminos del Sur.<sup id="cite_ref-periodicolaperla.com_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-periodicolaperla.com-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is to consist of a <a href="/wiki/Soccer_stadium" class="mw-redirect" title="Soccer stadium">soccer stadium</a>, a baseball field, three <a href="/wiki/Tennis_courts" class="mw-redirect" title="Tennis courts">tennis courts</a>, a track for <a href="/wiki/Track_and_field" title="Track and field">track and field</a> sports, a covered gymnasium, and covered <a href="/wiki/American_handball" title="American handball">handball</a> courts. In addition it is planned to have a sports academy, the first one of its kind in Puerto Rico. Other facilities are to include a central <a href="/wiki/Plaza" class="mw-redirect" title="Plaza">plaza</a>, a medical sports clinic, and facilities for <a href="/wiki/Martial_arts" title="Martial arts">martial arts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-elsuralavista_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elsuralavista-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Ciudad Deportiva will be located in Ponce\'s Barrio <a href="/wiki/Bucana" class="mw-redirect" title="Bucana">Bucana</a>, next to Polideportivo Frankie Col\xc3\xb3n at Urbanizacion Los Caobos.<sup id="cite_ref-elsuralavista_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elsuralavista-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ciudad_Deportiva_Millito_Navarro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The idea for Ciudad Deportiva was one of five ideas that Mayor <a href="/wiki/Mar%C3%ADa_Mel%C3%A9ndez" title="Mar\xc3\xada Mel\xc3\xa9ndez">Maria Melendez Altieri</a> had for permanent structures for Ponce during her administration. She called the five ideas the "emblematic" projects of her administration and dubbed the overall plan "Ponce Avanza" (Ponce Moves Forward). The original plan judged the initial cost of the <i>Ciudad Deportiva</i> at $4.5 million initially.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  In 2010, the mayor\'s plan for Ciudad Deportiva was presented before the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Senate" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Senate">Puerto Rico Senate</a>. In early 2012 the first payment of $1,050,000 was made to CGC Builders to start construction work.<sup id="cite_ref-elsuralavista_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elsuralavista-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Purpose">Purpose</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ciudad_Deportiva_Millito_Navarro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Purpose"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The purpose of the complex is to "provide ... sports facilities in the city of Ponce for the celebration of sporting events lasting more than one day".<sup id="cite_ref-senadopr_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-senadopr-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Funding">Funding</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ciudad_Deportiva_Millito_Navarro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Funding"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In February 2012, the sports campus has an initial projected total cost of $20 million <a href="/wiki/USD" class="mw-redirect" title="USD">USD</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-senadopr_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-senadopr-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The municipality has secured US$6.3 million to start construction.<sup id="cite_ref-elsuralavista_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elsuralavista-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In January 2012, the municipality of Ponce adjudicated US$1.05 million to GCG Builders to level the ground to commence the first phase of construction.<sup id="cite_ref-elsuralavista_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elsuralavista-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This was revised in May 2012 to $32.3 million <a href="/wiki/USD" class="mw-redirect" title="USD">USD</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-periodicolaperla.com_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-periodicolaperla.com-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Construction_phases">Construction phases</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ciudad_Deportiva_Millito_Navarro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Construction phases"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Sports City will be developed in phases. Phase I will include construction of the three tennis courts and an area for <a href="/wiki/Extreme_sports" class="mw-redirect" title="Extreme sports">extreme sports</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Skateboarding" title="Skateboarding">skateboarding</a>. Phase II will encompass the baseball field, the track and field area, soccer field, and the central plaza. A future phase yet would include the soccer stadium.<sup id="cite_ref-elsuralavista_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elsuralavista-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The skateboarding facility opened on 23 March 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> By October 2013, building of the soccer field (Spanish: cancha de f\xc3\xbatbol) had already started.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2
