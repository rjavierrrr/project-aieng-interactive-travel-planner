Tortuguero Natural Reserve</b> is a natural <a href="/wiki/Reservoir" title="Reservoir">reservoir</a> located between the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Vega_Baja,_Puerto_Rico" title="Vega Baja, Puerto Rico">Vega Baja</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manat%C3%AD,_Puerto_Rico" title="Manat\xc3\xad, Puerto Rico">Manat\xc3\xad</a> in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.\n</p><p>The lagoon is one of only two natural reservoirs in the island of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> (the other being <a href="/wiki/Joyuda_Lagoon" title="Joyuda Lagoon">Joyuda Lagoon</a>), and is home to an ample variety of species.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="General_information">General information</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tortuguero_Lagoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: General information"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Tortuguero Lagoon Nature Reserve is the only freshwater lagoon in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. It contains about 708 million gallons of water. The reserve was designated in 1979 through a program of Coastal Zone Management PR. It is located between the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Vega_Baja" class="mw-redirect" title="Vega Baja">Vega Baja</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manat%C3%AD" class="mw-redirect" title="Manat\xc3\xad">Manat\xc3\xad</a>. It covers approximately 2.43&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (0.94&#160;sq&#160;mi).<sup id="cite_ref-Map_of_Vega_Baja_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Map_of_Vega_Baja-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>It is divided into two main parts: in the east and northeast, the Laguna Grande ("Big Lagoon" in English), and in the south, the Laguna Rica. A swamp located northeast of the pond, El Cabo Caribe ("Cape Caribbean" in English), includes one of the most important areas of the reserve as a source of nesting and feeding waterflow ecosystem.\n</p><p>Generally, Tortuguero is made up of swamps, marshes, soils of silica sand and hills. Although not a forest, its variety of rare endemic plants and flowers has earned it a place amongst the most important reserves in Puerto Rico, just after <a href="/wiki/El_Yunque_National_Forest" title="El Yunque National Forest">El Yunque</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toro_Negro_State_Forest" title="Toro Negro State Forest">Toro Negro State Forest</a>  and the <a href="/wiki/Maricao_State_Forest" title="Maricao State Forest">Forest of Maricao</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Native,_exotic_and_endemic_species_in_the_reserve"><span id="Native.2C_exotic_and_endemic_species_in_the_reserve"></span>Native, exotic and endemic species in the reserve</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tortuguero_Lagoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Native, exotic and endemic species in the reserve"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Native species:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Eels of the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Anguillidae" title="Anguillidae">Anguila</a></i></li>\n<li>Chad (<i><a href="/wiki/Elops_saurus" title="Elops saurus">Elops saurus</a></i>)</li>\n<li>Common snook (<i><a href="/wiki/Common_snook" title="Common snook">Centropomus undecimalis</a></i>)</li>\n<li>Big-eye scad mackerel or horse-eye jack (<i><a href="/wiki/Horse-eye_jack" title="Horse-eye jack">Caranx latus</a></i>)</li></ul>\n<p>Endemic species:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Puerto Rican spindalis (<i><a href="/wiki/Spindalis_portoricensis" class="mw-redirect" title="Spindalis portoricensis">Spindalis portoricensis</a></i>)</li>\n<li>Puerto Rican oriole (<i><a href="/wiki/Icterus_portoricensis" class="mw-redirect" title="Icterus portoricensis">Icterus portoricensis</a></i>)</li>\n<li>Adelaide\'s warbler (<i><a href="/wiki/Setophaga_adelaidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Setophaga adelaidae">Setophaga adelaidae</a></i>)</li></ul>\n<p>Exotic species:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Caiman (<i><a href="/wiki/Caiman_crocodilus" class="mw-redirect" title="Caiman crocodilus">Caiman crocodilus</a></i>)</li>\n<li>Black seabream or chopa (<i><a href="/wiki/Spondyliosoma_cantharus" class="mw-redirect" title="Spondyliosoma cantharus">Spondyliosoma cantharus</a></i>)</li>\n<li>Tilapia (<i><a href="/wiki/Oreochromis_urolepis" title="Oreochromis urolepis">Oreochromis urolepis</a></i>)</li></ul>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Flora">Flora</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tortuguero_Lagoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Flora"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The flora of this reserve is the fourth most important in Puerto Rico. There are 717 species of plants.\n- 144 are rare and endangered\n- 56 live in fine white sands around the lagoon\n- 110 live in organic soils of swamps\n- 37 species are native to America\n</p><p>All of the above are found exclusively on the Tortuguero Lagoon. There are seven species of insectivorous plants in the area of the lagoon. Two of these are in danger of extinction, which are <i><a href="/wiki/Drosera_capillaris" title="Drosera capillaris">Drosera capillaris</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Utricularia_subulata" title="Utricularia subulata">Utricularia subulata clesitogama</a></i>. The rest is divided into:\n- 265 are indicators of wetlands\n- 132 tree species\n- 79 species of sedges\n- 78 herbs\n- 38 species of ferns\n</p><p>The extremely rare tree <i><a href="/wiki/Schoepfia_arenaria" title="Schoepfia arenaria">Schoepfia arenaria</a></i> was found to grow in the reserve in 1990. Less than 200 of these Puerto Rican <a href="/wiki/Endemic" class="mw-redirect" title="Endemic">endemic</a> trees are known to exist.<sup id="cite_ref-Rivera1991_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rivera1991-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Fauna">Fauna</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tortuguero_Lagoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Fauna"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The reserve provides an environment conducive to bird diversity. There have been 83 species of birds identified across the reserve. Of these, 30 are migratory and the rest are resident in Puerto Rico. Among the residents are the <a href="/wiki/Spindalis_portoricensis" class="mw-redirect" title="Spindalis portoricensis">Puerto Rican spindalis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Icterus_portoricensis" class="mw-redirect" title="Icterus portoricensis">Puerto Rican oriole</a> and <a href="/wiki/Setophaga_adelaidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Setophaga adelaidae">Adelaide\'s warbler</a>. In aquatic ecosystems, birds such as <a href="/wiki/Oxyura_jamaicensis" class="mw-redirect" title="Oxyura jamaicensis">ruddy duck</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fulica_caribaea" class="mw-redirect" title="Fulica caribaea">Caribbean coot</a> and are generally abundant. As for fish, they are generally abundant but small. The lagoon is a shelter and breeding ground for females fish. There are 23 species of fish in the lagoon, among the native are <a href="/wiki/Eel" title="Eel">eels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tarpon" title="Tarpon">tarpon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Common_snook" title="Common snook">snook</a> and mackerel bigeye. Among those introduced are the black seabream, bass and tilapia. It also emphasizes the presence of <a href="/wiki/Oviparity" title="Oviparity">oviparous</a> fish such as the "guppy" of the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Poecilia" title="Poecilia">Poecilia</a></i>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="References">References</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
