Quinto Centenario (2011)</figcaption></figure>\n<p><b>Plaza del Quinto Centenario</b> (<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_Spanish" title="Puerto Rican Spanish">Spanish</a> for \'Square of the Fifth Centenary\'), also <b>Plaza del V Centenario</b>, popularly referred to as <b>Plaza del T\xc3\xb3tem</b> (\'Totem Square\'), is a modern square in <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">Old San Juan</a>, Puerto Rico, inaugurated in 1992 to commemorate the 500th anniversary of the <a href="/wiki/Age_of_Discovery" title="Age of Discovery">European discovery</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Spanish colonization of the Americas">Spanish conquest of Puerto Rico and the Americas</a> and the first European settlement of San Juan. The square is located between the <a href="/wiki/Ballaj%C3%A1,_Old_San_Juan" title="Ballaj\xc3\xa1, Old San Juan">Ballaj\xc3\xa1</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mercado,_Old_San_Juan" title="Mercado, Old San Juan">Mercado</a> neighborhoods of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Antiguo" title="San Juan Antiguo">San Juan Antiguo</a> <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico">barrio</a> of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>. It is located next to the <a href="/wiki/Ballaj%C3%A1_Barracks" title="Ballaj\xc3\xa1 Barracks">Ballaj\xc3\xa1 Barracks</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Galer%C3%ADa_Nacional" title="Galer\xc3\xada Nacional">Puerto Rican National Gallery</a> and the <a href="/wiki/San_Jos%C3%A9_Church" title="San Jos\xc3\xa9 Church">San Jos\xc3\xa9 Church</a>, close to <a href="/wiki/Castillo_San_Felipe_del_Morro" title="Castillo San Felipe del Morro">El Morro</a> and the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_National_Historic_Site" title="San Juan National Historic Site">San Juan National Historic Site</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_del_Quinto_Centenario&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>At the time of the Spanish settlement of San Juan, the site of the Plaza del Quinto Centenario originally belonged to the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Order" title="Dominican Order">Dominican Order</a>, which established a monastery during the 16th century. The main structure of the monastery still stands today, and it serves as the headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/Galer%C3%ADa_Nacional" title="Galer\xc3\xada Nacional">National Gallery</a> operated by the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Puerto_Rican_Culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Institute of Puerto Rican Culture">Institute of Puerto Rican Culture</a>. Throughout the 19th century, the lot was owned by the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Army" title="Spanish Army">Spanish Army</a>, which established the <a href="/wiki/Ballaj%C3%A1_Barracks" title="Ballaj\xc3\xa1 Barracks">Ballaj\xc3\xa1 Barracks</a>, and later during the first half of the 20th century it was home to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">US Army</a>\'s Fort Brooke. The site and its surrounding buildings were abandoned during the second half of the 20th century, and by the late 1960s the entire area was in a rapid state of <a href="/wiki/Urban_decay" title="Urban decay">urban decay</a>. After the institution of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_National_Historic_Site" title="San Juan National Historic Site">San Juan National Historic Site</a> as a <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">UNESCO World Heritage Site</a> in 1983, and in an attempt to revitalize the area in 1986, the Puerto Rico State Office of Historic Preservation began a series of projects aimed at restoring the historic heritage of the neighborhood and reviving the economic, cultural and tourism industries in the area.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>As its current name describes, the modern square was built and finished in 1992 in time for the celebration of the 5th centenary (500th anniversary) of the European discovery of Puerto Rico and the Americas, during a period of extensive renovation and redevelopment of the Ballaj\xc3\xa1 neighborhood along other important historic sites in Old San Juan. The square hosted a series of cultural programs including traditional arts and crafts, cultural performances and a carnival. It also hosted the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Symphony_Orchestra" title="Puerto Rico Symphony Orchestra">Puerto Rico Symphony Orchestra</a>, and a celebratory concert honoring Puerto Rican composer <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Hern%C3%A1ndez_Mar%C3%ADn" title="Rafael Hern\xc3\xa1ndez Mar\xc3\xadn">Rafael Hern\xc3\xa1ndez Mar\xc3\xadn</a>, and other important figures of the cultural history of Puerto Rico. Some of the musical performers included <a href="/wiki/Roc%C3%ADo_Jurado" title="Roc\xc3\xado Jurado">Roc\xc3\xado Jurado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Feliciano" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Feliciano">Jos\xc3\xa9 Feliciano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Celia_Cruz" title="Celia Cruz">Celia Cruz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ruth_Fern%C3%A1ndez" title="Ruth Fern\xc3\xa1ndez">Ruth Fernandez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ednita_Nazario" title="Ednita Nazario">Ednita Nazario</a> and <a href="/wiki/Danny_Rivera" title="Danny Rivera">Danny Rivera</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The Plaza del Quinto Centenario today is one of the main staging locations for the annual <a href="/wiki/San_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Street_Festival" title="San Sebasti\xc3\xa1n Street Festival">San Sebasti\xc3\xa1n Street Festival</a> during the month of January.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The square was also the site for the celebration of the 500th anniversary of the official founding of the city of San Juan on September 12 of 2022, officiated by Mayor <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Romero" title="Miguel Romero">Miguel Romero Lugo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Description">Description</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_del_Quinto_Centenario&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:El_Totem_Tel%C3%BArico.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/El_Totem_Tel%C3%BArico.jpg/220px-El_Totem_Tel%C3%BArico.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="331" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/El_Totem_Tel%C3%BArico.jpg/330px-El_Totem_Tel%C3%BArico.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/El_Totem_Tel%C3%BArico.jpg/440px-El_Totem_Tel%C3%BArico.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2848" data-file-height="4288" /></a><figcaption>Close-up of the <i>T\xc3\xb3tem tel\xc3\xbarico</i> by ceramist Jaime Su\xc3\xa1rez.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></figcaption></figure>\n<p>The main feature of the square is the \'Teluric Totem\' (<i>T\xc3\xb3tem Tel\xc3\xbarico</i>), which lends its name to the Plaza del T\xc3\xb3tem (\'Totem Square\'), the name popularly and locally used for the Plaza del Quinto Centenario. The "totem" is actually an art sculpture created by Puerto Rican ceramist Jaime Su\xc3\xa1rez, and it is made from broken fragments of ceramic vases and other types of clay pottery from the Americas,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> which allude to the pottery fragments of the indigenous <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno" title="Ta\xc3\xadno">Taino</a> found in archaeological sites throughout Puerto Rico. As such, this sculpture represents the historic but violent first meeting between the original native inhabitants of Puerto Rico and the European colonizers.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The totem has a base or pedestal built from black granite, and the entire sculpture rises to 40 feet (12 m) in height. The square at the base of the totem portrays a <a href="/wiki/Wind_rose" title="Wind rose">wind rose</a> pointing at the cardinal directions. A similar sculpture by the same artist can be found in front of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Federico_Degetau_Federal_Building&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Federico Degetau Federal Building (page does not exist)">Federico Degetau Federal Building</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hato_Rey" title="Hato Rey">Hato Rey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The square is bound by sculptures representing the <a href="/wiki/Agnus_Dei" title="Agnus Dei">Agnus Dei</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Lamb_of_God" title="Lamb of God">Lamb of God</a>, one of the symbols of San Juan and the main feature of the escutcheon of the <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Coat of arms of Puerto Rico">coat of arms of Puerto Rico</a>. The art designs and steps setup, and their arrangement in the square, represent the 500-year history period of the history of Puerto Rico since the European settlement in 1492, and each level of the square represents a centennial or 100 years in the history of the island. The southern end of the square is also marked by needle-shaped columns which point towards the <a href="/wiki/Polaris" title="Polaris">North Star</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Plaza del Quinto Centenario is bound by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Norzagaray_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Norzagaray Street (page does not exist)">Norzagaray Street</a> to the north, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Calle_del_Cristo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Calle del Cristo (page does not exist)">del Cristo Street</a> to the east, Dr. Francisco Rufino de Goenaga Street to the south, and Morovis Street to the west, and it hosts an extensive parking garage underground.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span
