title="Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico">Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico</a>. The mall is anchored by a <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Cinemas" title="Caribbean Cinemas">Caribbean Cinemas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Walmart" title="Walmart">Walmart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burlington_Stores" class="mw-redirect" title="Burlington Stores">Burlington</a>, <a href="/wiki/H%26M" title="H&amp;M">H&amp;M</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dave_%26_Buster%27s" title="Dave &amp; Buster&#39;s">Dave &amp; Buster\'s</a> and <a href="/wiki/Home_Depot" title="Home Depot">The Home Depot</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The mall was opened in 1998; it was later acquired in 2005 by <a href="/wiki/DDR_Corp." class="mw-redirect" title="DDR Corp.">DDR Corp.</a> out of a $1.15 billion portfolio deal with Caribbean Property Group (CPG), which included the mall.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> And is now owned by Curzon Puerto Rico. \n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_del_Sol_(Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Opening_and_success:_1990s-2000s">Opening and success: 1990s-2000s</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_del_Sol_(Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Opening and success: 1990s-2000s"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1998, developed as a joint venture between BY Ventures, SE, and <a href="/wiki/Walmart" title="Walmart">Walmart</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-dmm_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dmm-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Plaza del Sol would open with more than 50 stores to much fanfare including a 16,000 square foot Group Usa, a 16,000 square foot <a href="/wiki/Old_Navy" title="Old Navy">Old Navy</a>, a 20,000 square foot <a href="/wiki/Pep_Boys" title="Pep Boys">Pep Boys</a>, and a 9,000 square foot Mikasa store. Its main anchors being <a href="/wiki/Walmart" title="Walmart">Walmart</a>, a 112,000 square foot <a href="/wiki/The_Home_Depot" class="mw-redirect" title="The Home Depot">Home Depot</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Bed_Bath_%26_Beyond_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Bed Bath &amp; Beyond Inc.">Bed Bath &amp; Beyond</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dmm_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dmm-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It would also feature a food court, the Caf\xc3\xa9 del Sol, restaurants, a 14-screen <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Cinemas" title="Caribbean Cinemas">Caribbean Cinemas</a>, and a carousel.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In January 2001, the at the time 612,000 square foot Plaza del Sol would feature 109 stores such as <a href="/wiki/Payless_ShoeSource" class="mw-redirect" title="Payless ShoeSource">Payless ShoeSource</a>, <a href="/wiki/Journeys_(company)" title="Journeys (company)">Journeys</a>, <a href="/wiki/5-7-9" title="5-7-9">5-7-9</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Russe_(retailer)" title="Charlotte Russe (retailer)">Charlotte Russe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tiendas_Kress" class="mw-redirect" title="Tiendas Kress">Kress</a>, Rave, <a href="/wiki/Motherhood_Maternity" class="mw-redirect" title="Motherhood Maternity">Motherhood Maternity</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gap_Inc." title="Gap Inc.">The Gap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zales_Jewelers" class="mw-redirect" title="Zales Jewelers">Zales Jewelers</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Sally_Beauty_Holdings" title="Sally Beauty Holdings">Sally Beauty</a>, <a href="/wiki/Afterthoughts_(retailer)" title="Afterthoughts (retailer)">Afterthoughts</a>, <a href="/wiki/GNC_(company)" title="GNC (company)">GNC</a>, <a href="/wiki/KB_Toys" title="KB Toys">KB Toys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electronics_Boutique" class="mw-redirect" title="Electronics Boutique">Electronics Boutique</a>, and others.<sup id="cite_ref-dmm_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dmm-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In September 2003, it was reported that Plaza del Sol had reported higher sales August that year than the past year; among the best performers were the food court, the movie theater, and <a href="/wiki/Walmart" title="Walmart">Walmart</a>, said at the time Marketing Manager Valerie Castro. "Food court sales were spectacular. One thing that helped was the opening of <a href="/wiki/El_Mes%C3%B3n_S%C3%A1ndwiches" title="El Mes\xc3\xb3n S\xc3\xa1ndwiches">El Meson Express</a>. During its first month, the eatery registered sales of $100,000; that\'s a record-breaker," said Castro. "Overall, food court sales in August were 5.7% higher than in the same month in 2002." She noted that average sales per square foot at the food court were $1,074. "These sales are a good indicator of how much traffic we receive," said Castro. She estimated that an average of some one million consumers visited the mall each month. "It gets even better at Christmas," she said. The mall\'s movie theater had reported 20.2% higher sales over August 2002. "In June they increased 23.8%," said Castro. She attributed the sales gain to the blockbuster movies launched during summer. Castro said <a href="/wiki/Walmart" title="Walmart">Walmart</a>, among the first to open at Plaza del Sol in 1998, reported that sales had increased 7% or $4 million from 2001 to 2002. The shopping center\'s overall sales in August had jumped 1% over the same month that past year. "Average sales per square foot are $508," said Castro.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In April 2004, it was reported that Johnny Rockets of Puerto Rico Inc, the at the time new local franchisee of retro-style restaurant chain <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Rockets" title="Johnny Rockets">Johnny Rockets</a>, was opening an eatery in <a href="/wiki/Bayam%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico" title="Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico">Bayamon\'s</a> Plaza del Sol that week, the first of three it planned for the island in 10 years.  "We will have a soft opening April 23 from 3:00 p.m. until 10:00 p.m., said Craig Doyle, then operations manager of Johnny Rockets of Puerto Rico. The restaurant, which would be available for private activities, would occupy 2,056 square feet in a corner on the mall\'s second floor and would have about 70 employees, including management. Doyle said the soft opening would also help management pick which employees will be shift leaders and certified training managers.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In August 2004, it was reported that Plaza del Sol would begin construction on a multistory parking lot, it would be adding 400 parking spaces to the 3,400 the mall already had. Two hundred of the 400 new spaces were expected to be ready by Christmas of that year.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In November 2004, it was reported that <a href="/wiki/Developers_Diversified_Realty" class="mw-redirect" title="Developers Diversified Realty">Developers Diversified Realty</a> (DDR) had announced it had entered into an agreement to purchase 15 Puerto Rican retail real estate assets, totaling nearly 5.0 million square feet from Caribbean Property Group, LLC ("CPG"). The transaction had a total value of approximately $1.15 billion, which represented a 7.4% cap rate on a pro-forma basis without the benefit of straight-line rents. The transaction was expected to close during the first quarter of 2005, subject to the company\'s due diligence and standard closing conditions. This transaction included Plaza del Sol, which was one of the many shopping centers acquired by DDR through the deal, making DDR owners of the shopping mall. The deal would officially be completed on January 27, 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In January 2005, it was reported that after years of eyeing the local market, electronics mega retailer <a href="/wiki/Best_Buy" title="Best Buy">Best Buy</a> had finally decided to open shop on the island. The first store would be located at <a href="/wiki/Bayam%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico" title="Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico">Bayamon\'s</a> Plaza del Sol. About nine more stores were planned to open later.  Best Buy\'s store in Plaza del Sol would be located right above the mall\'s 30,000 square foot <a href="/wiki/Bed_Bath_%26_Beyond_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Bed Bath &amp; Beyond Inc.">Bed Bath &amp; Beyond</a> (BBB) box and would have more than 65 employees. This is in line with the plans the shopping mall\'s new owner at the time, Ohio-based <a href="/wiki/Developers_Diversified_Realty" class="mw-redirect" title="Developers Diversified Realty">Developers Diversified Realty</a> (DDR), had reported. DDR said it could expand the mall vertically instead of horizontally to increase its value, as space to grow was limited.  Besides adding a second story to BBB to house Best Buy, sources close to the mall said that up to three additional stories could be added to Plaza del Sol to provide more retail space. "Ever since it was first built, Plaza del Sol was designed with aggressive expansion in mind," said the source. In fact, before the sale to DDR, Plaza del Sol had already said it planned to add a multistory parking lot. The construction of Best Buy in Plaza del Sol was part of the retailer\'s 2005 plans to invest $700 million in property and equipment, and to open some 73 stores nationwide and in Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="2010s,_and_on"><span id="2010s.2C_and_on"></span>2010s, and on</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_del_Sol_(Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2010s, and on"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 2017, <a href="/wiki/DDR_Corp." class="mw-redirect" title="DDR Corp.">DDR Corp.</a> spun off its Puerto Rican shopping centers to RVI (<a href="/wiki/SITE_Centers" title="SITE Centers">Retail Value Inc.</a>) due to struggles they had after the <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a>, making <a href="/wiki/SITE_Centers" title="SITE Centers">Retail Value Inc.</a> the new owner of the mall at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In August 2021, Developers Diversified (<a href="/wiki/DDR_Corp." class="mw-redirect" title="DDR Corp.">DDR Corp.</a>) came back to the PR retail landscape with a $550 million deal with <a href="/wiki/SITE_Centers" title="SITE Centers">RVI</a> which Plaza del Sol was included in, making DDR the owner of the mall once again.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In September 2022, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Bed_Bath_%26_Beyond_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Bed Bath &amp; Beyond Inc.">Bed Bath &amp; Beyond</a> would be closing as part of a plan to close 150 stores nationwide.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On May 10, 2023, it was reported that Curzon Puerto Rico, an affiliate of Curzon Advisers, was now the owner of one of the largest shopping center portfolios in Puerto Rico, formerly owned by Developers Diversified (<a href="/wiki/DDR_Corp." class="mw-redirect" title="DDR Corp.">DDR Corp.</a>) which had acquired it again in 2021. In this portfolio of 9 shopping centers, Plaza del Sol was included, making Curzon Puerto Rico the new owners of the mall.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On February 6, 2024, it was reported that <a href="/wiki/Burlington_Stores" class="mw-redirect" title="Burlington Stores">Burlington</a> would be replacing the space left by Bed Bath &amp; Beyond at the mall, a store opening for the Burlington was scheduled for May 17.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Anchor_Stores">Anchor Stores</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_del_Sol_(Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4"
