The house was acquired and restored by the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Puerto_Rican_Culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Institute of Puerto Rican Culture">Institute of Puerto Rican Culture</a> and now operates as the <b><a href="/wiki/Museo_de_la_Arquitectura_Ponce%C3%B1a" title="Museo de la Arquitectura Ponce\xc3\xb1a">Museo de la Arquitectura Ponce\xc3\xb1a</a></b> (Museum of Ponce Architecture). The house sits in the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce Historic Zone</a>. The Villaronga Residence is an outstanding example of the <a href="/wiki/Classical_Revival" class="mw-redirect" title="Classical Revival">Classical Revival</a> style in used in Ponce designs in the early part of the 20th century and is one of two residences still standing of a series of houses designed and built by <a href="/wiki/Alfredo_Wiechers_Pieretti" title="Alfredo Wiechers Pieretti">Alfredo B. Wiechers</a>, so important to the architectural and cultural heritage of the city of Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Significance">Significance</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Casa_Wiechers-Villaronga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Significance"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Wiechers-Villaronga Residence was built in 1912 by Alfredo B. Wiechers, architect, for his own residence and studio, which he sold later to Mr. Julio Mercado, who in turn give it to his daughter, Elena Mercado and her husband Mr. Gabriel Villaronga as a wedding present. Wiechers was born in Ponce and studied at the <a href="/wiki/%C3%89cole_Sp%C3%A9ciale_d%27Architecture" title="\xc3\x89cole Sp\xc3\xa9ciale d&#39;Architecture">School of Architecture</a> in Paris, France, in 1901, where he was bestowed a <a href="/wiki/Gold_medal" title="Gold medal">gold medal</a> for outstanding achievement and excellence during his professional studies. He graduated in 1905 and worked at the office of <a href="/wiki/Enric_Sagnier" title="Enric Sagnier">Enric Sagnier</a>, a famous Spanish architect, in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona,_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Barcelona, Spain">Barcelona, Spain</a>. In 1911, he decided to work and live in Ponce; thus he designed and built his house and studio which he opened in 1912. In a short period of time, from 1911 to 1918, Wiechers was commissioned with various important buildings such as: the Loggia Aurora, Club Deportivo de Damas, the Havana Theatre, <a href="/wiki/Banco_de_Ponce_(building)" title="Banco de Ponce (building)">Banco of Ponce Building</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hospital_de_Damas" class="mw-redirect" title="Hospital de Damas">Santo Asilo de Damas Hospital</a> among others, where he fully expressed the European <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">Neo-Classical</a> style which he learned from, and was influenced by, Enric Sagnier.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The Neo-Classical influence is manifested in most of his buildings and particularly in the Villaronga residence for its richness and highly decorative details (<a href="/wiki/Pilaster" title="Pilaster">pilasters</a>, rusticated <a href="/wiki/Podium" title="Podium">podium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cornice" title="Cornice">cornices</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Candelabra" title="Candelabra">candelabra</a>", <a href="/wiki/Relief" title="Relief">relief</a> and <a href="/wiki/Motif_(visual_arts)" title="Motif (visual arts)">motifs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ionic_order" title="Ionic order">Ionic</a> capitols, etc. are some of the decorative details of the Neo-Classical trend). The Villaronga Residence is an outstanding example of this style and is one of two residences still standing of a series of houses designed and built by Wiechers, so important to the architectural and cultural heritage of the city of Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Of special importance is the fact that all the furniture is original, including the bathroom appliances. Most of the furniture belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Modernisme" title="Modernisme">Modernisme</a> style (Catalan <a href="/wiki/Art_Nouveau" title="Art Nouveau">Art Nouveau</a>) and were imported from <a href="/wiki/Barcelona,_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Barcelona, Spain">Barcelona, Spain</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The hanging tapestry were painted by <a href="/wiki/Librado_Net" title="Librado Net">Librado Net</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Casa_Wiechers-Villaronga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The structure was designed in 1911 by its original owner, the local Ponce architect Alfredo Wiechers. It was built in the same year by El\xc3\xadas Concepci\xc3\xb3n Albizu. In 1918, the house was acquired by the Villaronga Mercado family.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The house was listed on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1984 as <b>Villaronga House</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-nris_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Architecture">Architecture</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Casa_Wiechers-Villaronga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Architecture"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The structure is considered "treasure trove of culture",<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> a jewel amongst the many antique Ponce mansions that have been preserved and converted into museums. The building possesses elaborate neoclassical details, a majestic roof-top gazebo, and a full set of original and custom-made Catalan modernist furniture. There are also well-preserved shower and bathroom fixtures.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Casa_Wiechers-Villaronga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Wiechers-Villaronga Residence is a U-shaped, one-storied structure, measuring 68\'-10" in width by 95\'-2" in length, located at the northeast corner of Reina and Mendez Vigo Streets. Behind all its highly elaborated decoration, with European <a href="/wiki/Baroque_architecture" title="Baroque architecture">Baroque</a> influence, essentially the structure is <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">Neo-Classic</a> in style, making a unique and yet elegant combination of both styles. The building rests on a rusticated stone <a href="/wiki/Podium" title="Podium">podium</a>; its facades and main walls are of brick <a href="/wiki/Masonry" title="Masonry">masonry</a> with some interior partitions and walls of the gallery and kitchen in wood. Wooden beams supports a <a href="/wiki/Galvanized" class="mw-redirect" title="Galvanized">galvanized</a> zinc roof. The windows and doors of the building are wooden with movable <a href="/wiki/Louver" title="Louver">louvers</a> and fixed colored glass inlets. A variety of flooring material is used throughout the house: from native cement-colored tiles in the dining area and <a href="/wiki/Vestibule_(architecture)" title="Vestibule (architecture)">vestibule</a>, 1" by 6" <a href="/wiki/Tongue-and-groove" class="mw-redirect" title="Tongue-and-groove">tongue-and-groove</a> wood slats in the living area and bedrooms, and ceramic tiles in the bathroom, to marble tiles at the entry-way.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The Baroque\'s influence is manifested immediately on the building\'s rounded corners, typical of these residence types in Ponce. The corner is framed by two rusticated <a href="/wiki/Pilaster" title="Pilaster">pilasters</a> and divided into three bays, by two pilaster strips or <a href="/wiki/Lesene" title="Lesene">lesenes</a>. Each bay contains a wooden movable louvreed window with glass inlets at its top, and a floral relief <a href="/wiki/Motif_(visual_arts)" title="Motif (visual arts)">motif</a> over the <a href="/wiki/Window" title="Window">fenestration</a>. The rest of the wall is decorated with floral <a href="/wiki/Garland" title="Garland">garlands</a>. The corner is accentuated with a round sitting nook or "glorieta", that is detailed with <a href="/wiki/Ionic_order" title="Ionic order">Ionic</a> columns. Another interesting feature of the house are the <a href="/wiki/Balcony" title="Balcony">balconies</a> which are divided into three sections with Ionic columns and framed with Baroque <a href="/wiki/Molding_(decorative)" title="Molding (decorative)">moldings</a> and sculptured faces on the central top part of the openings. The forged-iron railings of the balconies are elaborated after the <a href="/wiki/Art_Nouveau" title="Art Nouveau">Art Nouveau</a> style. The facades are crowned with a continuous masonry <a href="/wiki/Cornice" title="Cornice">cornice</a>. On top of this cornice, a <a href="/wiki/Battlement" title="Battlement">battlement</a>-type <a href="/wiki/Parapet" title="Parapet">parapet</a> with sculptured lion faces and "<a href="/wiki/Candelabra" title="Candelabra">candelabra</a>" decorates the roof line of the structure.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The main entrance is off-centred and located on Reina Street. The entrance hall is decorated with sculptured tiles and the door is located at the end of a marbled stairway. The interior is well kept and unaltered. The interior areas are painted in different colors and the walls have a decorative plastered Art-Nouveau <a href="/wiki/Frieze" title="Frieze">frieze</a>. The ceiling in most of the areas is decorated embossed <a href="/wiki/Tin" title="Tin">tin</a> with a continuous decorative molding at its corners. The <a href="/wiki/Balcony" title="Balcony">gallery</a> and part of the kitchen walls are wooden with fixed wood louvered windows used for better ventilation and light. Other interesting details on the house are: the bathroom fixtures, such as the <a href="/wiki/Shower" title="Shower">shower</a> stall and the ceramic wall tiles which were imported from <a href="/wiki/Barcelona,_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Barcelona, Spain">Barcelona, Spain</a>, the light fixtures\xe2\x80\x94such as the ones at the dining-room, the master bedroom and living room\xe2\x80\x94which were also imported from Spain, and a "medio punto" at the dining area, typical of the architecture of this Southern area of Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Owner">Owner</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Casa_Wiechers-Villaronga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Owner"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Alfredo Wiechers Pieretti himself designed the house. He was the son of a German <a href="/wiki/Colonist" class="mw-redirect" title="Colonist">colonist</a> and his Corsican wife. He was born in Ponce and educated at the Paris\' L\'Ecole Sp\xc3\xa9ciale d\'Architecture. He lived for six years in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> while working at Enric Sagnier i Villavecchia\'s distinguished studio.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> After leaving his architectural mark on the City of Ponce (<a href="/wiki/Casa_Serralles" class="mw-redirect" title="Casa Serralles">Casa Serralles</a> 1911, <a href="/wiki/Casa_Oppenheimer" title="Casa Oppenheimer">Casa Oppenheimer</a> 1913) he sold the house to the Villaronga family and fled in 1919 to Barcelona, under political pressure. Although his prolific works (1911\xe2\x80\x931918) include hotels, stores, mausoleums, and even factories, the majority of his work was done in Ponce\xe2\x80\x94he ventured out of Ponce only to design two structures for wealthy <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalan</a> families in the neighboring mountain towns of <a href="/wiki/Adjuntas" class="mw-redirect" title="Adjuntas">Adjuntas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aibonito" class="mw-redirect" title="Aibonito">Aibonito</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The house was purchased by Don Julio Mercado, father of Helena Mercado, and given to Helena Mercado as a wedding present upon marriage to Mr. Villaronga.  Fact by Luis R. Mercado, Great Grandchild of Julio Mercado.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Restoration">Restoration</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Casa_Wiechers-Villaronga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Restoration"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The house was restored by the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Puerto_Rican_Culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Institute of Puerto Rican Culture">Institute of Puerto Rican Culture</a> (ICP) in the early 1990s and is now opened as the <b>Museum of Puerto Rican Architecture</b>.  Its furnishings - all original - speak to the social history in the city during the early twentieth century. The various rooms of the residence display aspects of Ponce\'s architecture.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Museum">Museum</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Casa_Wiechers-Villaronga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Museum"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The museum opened in 1996 as the home of the <i>Ponce Architecture and Urban Planning Museum</i>.  The goal was to showcase Ponce\'s rich architectural heritage.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The structure is considered a "gem of fine Art Nouveau from a bygone era".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  It has an extensive collection of displays and photos of master works from <a href="/wiki/Blas_Silva" class="mw-redirect" title="Blas Silva">Blas Silva Boucher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Porrata_Doria" class="mw-redirect" title="Francisco Porrata Doria">Francisco Porrata Doria</a>, Alfredo Wiechers Pieretti and other prominent local architects from the early 20th century.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The city of Ponce, considered by some as the "irrefutable guardian of Puerto Rican <i><a href="/wiki/Criollo_people" title="Criollo people">criollismo</a></i>",<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> was selected as a member of the prestigious Art Nouveau Route of the European Union for its "world preservation of modernist heritage".<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span
