/></a><figcaption>Interior of a general store in <a href="/wiki/Moundville,_Alabama" title="Moundville, Alabama">Moundville</a>, Alabama, US, 1936</figcaption></figure>\n<p>A <b>general merchant store</b> (also known as <b>general merchandise store</b>, <b>general dealer</b>, <b>village shop</b>, or <b>country store</b>) is a rural or small-town <a href="/wiki/Retailing" class="mw-redirect" title="Retailing">store</a> that carries a <a href="/wiki/General_line_of_merchandise" title="General line of merchandise">general line of merchandise</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It carries a broad selection of merchandise, sometimes in a small space, where people from the town and surrounding rural areas come to purchase all their general goods. The store carries routine stock and obtains special orders from <a href="/wiki/Wholesale" class="mw-redirect" title="Wholesale">warehouses</a>. It differs from a <a href="/wiki/Convenience_store" title="Convenience store">convenience store</a> or corner shop in that it will be the main shop for the community rather than a convenient supplement.\n</p><p>General stores often sell staple food items such as milk and bread, and various household goods such as hardware and electrical supplies. The concept of the general store is very old, and although some still exist, there are far fewer than there once were, due to <a href="/wiki/Urbanization" title="Urbanization">urbanization</a>, <a href="/wiki/Urban_sprawl" title="Urban sprawl">urban sprawl</a>, and the relatively recent phenomenon of <a href="/wiki/Big-box_stores" class="mw-redirect" title="Big-box stores">big-box stores</a>. The term "general merchandise store" is also used to describe a hybrid of a <a href="/wiki/Department_store" title="Department store">department store</a>, with a wide selection of goods, and a <a href="/wiki/Discount_store" title="Discount store">discount store</a>, with low prices. Examples include <a href="/wiki/J._C._Penney" class="mw-redirect" title="J. C. Penney">J. C. Penney</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sears" title="Sears">Sears</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=General_store&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>General dealers were established in the 18th and 19th centuries in many remote populated places where mobility was limited and a single shop was sufficient to service the entire community. Due to its close connection and confinement to its customers, general dealers often adjusted their sales offerings to the specific preferences of their community.<sup id="cite_ref-NSW_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NSW-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>General dealers existed, apart from <a href="/wiki/Continental_Europe" title="Continental Europe">mainland</a> <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, in all European colonies and generally in areas where <a href="/wiki/Settler" title="Settler">colonists</a> encroached upon communities that previously did not trade with money.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In the colonies, trading or bartering in local produce had existed long before official shops were opened. The growing need for imported goods, both from European settlers and <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples" title="Indigenous peoples">native</a> populations, led to the establishment of a network of <a href="/wiki/Merchant" title="Merchant">merchants</a>, and subsequently to the creation of a money economy.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="By_country">By country</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=General_store&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By country"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Australia">Australia</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a of supermarkets.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Canada">Canada</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=General_store&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Canada"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The oldest continually run general store in Canada is Trousdale\'s, located in <a href="/wiki/Sydenham,_Frontenac_County,_Ontario" title="Sydenham, Frontenac County, Ontario">Sydenham, Ontario</a>, which has been operated by the Trousdale family since 1836. Sociability has always been a feature, as locals come to chat as well as buy.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Gray Creek Store in Gray Creek, <a href="/wiki/Kootenay_Bay" title="Kootenay Bay">Kootenay Bay</a>, Canada is the largest and oldest general dealer in the <a href="/wiki/Kootenay_Lake" title="Kootenay Lake">Kootenay Lake</a> region<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Enniskillen General Store in Clarington, Ontario has been in operation since 1840 and still continues today. Robinson\'s General Store in Dorset, Ontario, voted "Canada\'s Best Country Store", has been owned and operated by the same family since 1921.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Dominican_Republic">Dominican Republic</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=General_store&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Dominican Republic"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Colmado.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Colmado.png/220px-Colmado.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="213" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Colmado.png/330px-Colmado.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Colmado.png/440px-Colmado.png 2x" data-file-width="1297" data-file-height="1254" /></a><figcaption>Woman buying from a colmado in the Dominican Republic</figcaption></figure>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>, a colmado is the country\'s equivalent of a general store. Colmado literal translation is \'full to the brim\'<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> implying its great density of goods in a small space. The colmado is much more than just a general store, for it offers a social gathering point for the residents of the town or neighborhood. The colmado is an important institution in the Dominican Republic serving as an economic, social and political center for every small community. It is common for colmados to have loud Dominican music such as merengue, bachata, or salsa playing.  A common pastime for Dominican men is to play dominoes and drink a beer at their local colmado on Sundays. Another particularity of the colmado is that they provide delivery service of their products directly to the customer\'s dwelling. Products range from beer, snacks, toilet paper to flashlights and canned food.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Egypt">Egypt</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span As in many other countries, their numbers were greatly reduced with the advent of supermarkets, from over 3400 such shops in 1980 to 241 in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="India">India</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=General_store&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: India"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Tappri.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Tappri.jpg/220px-Tappri.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Tappri.jpg/330px-Tappri.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Tappri.jpg/440px-Tappri.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="375" /></a><figcaption>Tapri on Mumbai streets</figcaption></figure>\n<p>In India, a tapri is a regional version of a general store. Small Kirana stores, which are generally located on the corner of streets and generally known as katta or tapri, sell cigarettes, tobacco, and tea.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Men_in_shalwar_kameez_on_the_road_to_Kalash,_Pakistan.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Men_in_shalwar_kameez_on_the_road_to_Kalash%2C_Pakistan.jpg/220px-Men_in_shalwar_kameez_on_the_road_to_Kalash%2C_Pakistan.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Men_in_shalwar_kameez_on_the_road_to_Kalash%2C_Pakistan.jpg/330px-Men_in_shalwar_kameez_on_the_road_to_Kalash%2C_Pakistan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Men_in_shalwar_kameez_on_the_road_to_Kalash%2C_Pakistan.jpg/440px-Men_in_shalwar_kameez_on_the_road_to_Kalash%2C_Pakistan.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4272" data-file-height="2848" /></a><figcaption>General Store on the road to <a href="/wiki/Kalasha_Desh" class="mw-redirect" title="Kalasha Desh">Kalash</a> valleys, <a href="/wiki/Chitral" title="Chitral">Chitral</a>, Pakistan</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Recently, there has been a notable integration of Aadhaar-enabled Payment System (AePS) services in Kirana or general stores across India. AePS allows customers to conduct basic banking transactions such as cash withdrawal, balance inquiry, and fund transfer using their Aadhaar number and biometric authentication. This integration has enabled Kirana stores to serve as financial service points, offering convenient banking services to local communities. \n</p><p>On 30 July 2024, <a href="/wiki/Open_Network_for_Digital_Commerce" title="Open Network for Digital Commerce">Open Network for Digital Commerce</a> (ONDC) introduced an interoperable QR code to link neighborhood store owners with consumers. title="Rural">rural</a> areas.\n</p><p>Their rarity in England is due to several factors, such as the rise in car ownership, competition from large chain <a href="/wiki/Supermarket" title="Supermarket">supermarkets</a>, the rising cost of village properties, and the increasing trend of the wealthy to own <a href="/wiki/Holiday_homes" class="mw-redirect" title="Holiday homes">holiday homes</a> in picturesque villages, consequently these houses which used to be occupied full-time by potential customers are often vacant for long periods.\n</p><p>Of those villages in England who still have shops, these days they are often a combination of services under one roof to increase the likelihood of profit and survival. Extra services may include a <a href="/wiki/Post_office" title="Post office">post office</a>, private business services such as <a href="/wiki/Tearoom" class="mw-redirect" title="Tearoom">tearooms</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_cafe" class="mw-redirect" title="British cafe">cafes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bed_and_breakfast" title="Bed and breakfast">bed and breakfast</a> accommodation; or state services such as <a href="/wiki/Public_library" title="Public library">libraries</a> and <a href="/wiki/General_Practitioner" class="mw-redirect" title="General Practitioner">General Practitioner</a> (GP) or Dental clinics; and charity partners such as <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Institute" title="Women&#39;s Institute">Women\'s Institute</a> (WI) coffee mornings held on the day most elderly villagers might collect their weekly pensions.\n</p><p>Community shops have become popular in some villages, often jointly owned and run by many villagers as a <a href="/wiki/Co-operative" class="mw-redirect" title="Co-operative">co-operative</a>. The Village Retail Services Association promotes the role and function of the village shop in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Many modern village shops choose to stock items which draw in customers from neighbouring areas who are seeking <a href="/wiki/Local_food" title="Local food">locally sourced</a>, <a href="/wiki/Organic_food" title="Organic food">organic</a> and specialist produce such as local cuts of meat, local cheeses, wines etc.\n</p><p>Corner shops are usually so-called because they are located on the corner plot or street end of a row of <a href="/wiki/Terraced_house" title="Terraced house">terraced housing</a>, often <a href="/wiki/Victorian_era" title="Victorian era">Victorian</a> or <a href="/wiki/Edwardian_era" title="Edwardian era">Edwardian</a> factory workers\' houses. The doorway into the shop was usually on the corner of the plot to maximise shop floor space within, this also offered two display windows onto two opposing streets. Many have now altered the original shop front layout in favour of a mini-supermarket style. Although it is common that corner shops found in the <a href="/wiki/UK" class="mw-redirect" title="UK">UK</a> were former grocers\' shops, other specialist retailers also occupied such plots and have suffered the same fate of being largely replaced by supermarkets and hypermarkets, such retailers as <a href="/wiki/Greengrocer" title="Greengrocer">greengrocers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baker" title="Baker">bakers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Butcher" title="Butcher">butchers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fishmonger" title="Fishmonger">fishmongers</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=General_store&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: In popular culture"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Many British television and radio series, especially <a href="/wiki/Soap_operas" class="mw-redirect" title="Soap operas">soap operas</a>, feature corner shops or village shops as cornerstones for community gatherings and happenings. The dying days and changing culture of the traditional British grocer were explored to great effect in the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> TV comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Open_All_Hours" title="Open All Hours">Open All Hours</a></i> (1976\xe2\x80\x931985), set in the real suburb of <a href="/wiki/Balby" title="Balby">Balby</a> in <a href="/wiki/Doncaster" title="Doncaster">Doncaster</a>; the shop front used for the street scenes in the series does actually exist in the area and is now a hairdressing salon. The <a href="/wiki/BBC_Scotland" title="BBC Scotland">BBC Scotland</a> comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Still_Game" title="Still Game">Still Game</a></i> has a corner shop as a recurring location where characters can meet and gossip; the actor who plays its owner, Navid Harrid (<a href="/wiki/Sanjeev_Kohli" title="Sanjeev Kohli">Sanjeev Kohli</a>), plays a similar role as Bangaram in the Radio 4 comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Fags,_Mags_and_Bags" title="Fags, Mags and Bags">Fags, Mags and Bags</a></i> which is set entirely in Ramesh\'s shop.\n</p><p>The band <a href="/wiki/Cornershop_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cornershop (band)">Cornershop</a> in part base their image on the perception that many convenience shops are now owned by British Asian people. In terms of British popular culture, these media representations give some idea of the importance attached to local shops in the national psyche and as a mainstay of community life.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="United_States">United States</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=General_store&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: United States"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Group_of_men_standing_in_front_of_Karch_%26_Heberer_store_at_Fairplay_in_the_late_1800s_-_DPLA_-_450f67cd5cf1861d1c246320ca2ee32a_(cropped).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img alt="Karch &amp; Heberer General Store in <a href="/wiki/Fairplay,_Colorado" title="Fairplay, Colorado">Fairplay, Colorado</a>, late 1800s</figcaption></figure>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Drawing_of_a_country_store_by_Marguerite_Martyn.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Drawing_of_a_country_store_by_Marguerite_Martyn.jpg/350px-Drawing_of_a_country_store_by_Marguerite_Martyn.jpg" decoding="async" width="350" height="238" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Drawing_of_a_country_store_by_Marguerite_Martyn.jpg/525px-Drawing_of_a_country_store_by_Marguerite_Martyn.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Drawing_of_a_country_store_by_Marguerite_Martyn.jpg/700px-Drawing_of_a_country_store_by_Marguerite_Martyn.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3000" data-file-height="2042" /></a><figcaption>Fanciful drawing by <a href="/wiki/Marguerite_Martyn" title="Marguerite Martyn">Marguerite Martyn</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Post-Dispatch" title="St. Louis Post-Dispatch">St. Louis Post-Dispatch</a></i> of October 21, 1906, headed "Passing of the Country Store in the <a href="/wiki/Southwestern_United_States" title="Southwestern United States">Southwest</a>"</figcaption></figure>\n<p>General stores and itinerant peddlers dominated in rural America until the coming of the automobile after 1910.  Farmers and ranchers depended on general stores that had a limited stock and slow turnover; they made enough profit to stay in operation by selling at high prices. Often farmers would barter butter, cheese, eggs, vegetables or other foods which the merchant would resell. Prices were not marked on each item; instead the customer negotiated a price. Men did most of the shopping, since the main criterion was credit rather than quality of goods. Indeed, most customers shopped on credit, paying later when crops or cattle were sold; the owner\'s ability to judge credit worthiness was vital to his success. The store was often a gathering point for local men to chat, pass around the weekly newspaper, and talk politics.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (April 2017)">page&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>In the South, the general store was especially important after the Civil War, as the merchant was one of the few sources of credit available until the cash crops (usually cotton or tobacco) came in.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (April 2017)">page&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  There were few towns and very few cities, so rural general stores and itinerant peddlers were the main sources of supply.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>During the first half of the 20th century, general stores were displaced in many areas of the United States by many different types of specialized retailers in trading towns and small cities. However, from the 1960s to the present, many small specialized retailers have in turn been crushed by the so-called "<a href="/wiki/Category_killer" title="Category killer">category killers</a>", which are "big-box" <a href="/wiki/Wholesale" class="mw-redirect" title="Wholesale">wholesale</a>-type retailers large enough to carry the majority of best-selling goods in a specific category, like sporting goods or office supplies. <a href="/wiki/Gray%27s_General_Store" title="Gray&#39;s General Store">Gray\'s General Store</a> of Adamsville, Rhode Island is reputed to be the oldest continually operating general store in the United States until its temporary closure in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It subsequently reopened in the summer of 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>However, the convenience inherent in the general store has been revived in the form of the modern <a href="/wiki/Convenience_store" title="Convenience store">convenience store</a>. A few <a href="/wiki/Variety_store" title="Variety store">variety stores</a> draw upon the concept of having a broad variety of goods servicing small communities where big-box retailers are absent; <a href="/wiki/Dollar_General" title="Dollar General">Dollar General</a> stores, in particular, draws its name from both the "dollar store" concept of having items at a fixed price point and the general store.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="List_of_general_stores">List of general stores</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=General_store&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: List of general stores"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>This is a select list of historical general stores in the United States, listed in alphabetical order.\n</p>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1184024115">.mw-parser-output .div-col{margin-top:0.3em;column-width:30em}.mw-parser-output .div-col-small{font-size:90%}.mw-parser-output .div-col-rules{column-rule:1px solid #aaa}.mw-parser-output .div-col dl,.mw-parser-output .div-col ol,.mw-parser-output .div-col ul{margin-top:0}.mw-parser-output .div-col li,.mw-parser-output .div-col dd{page-break-inside:avoid;break-inside:avoid-column}</style><div class="div-col" style="column-width: 30em;">\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/A._D._Strickland_Store" title="A.
