(1973\xe2\x80\x93present)</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Roberto Clemente Coliseum</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Coliseo Roberto Clemente</i>) is a sports and concert <a href="/wiki/Arena" title="Arena">arena</a> located in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>. It was, for many years, Puerto Rico\'s largest indoor event facility, and remains one of the largest.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="General_information">General information</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roberto_Clemente_Coliseum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: General information"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Coliseum is named after <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> hall of famer <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Clemente" title="Roberto Clemente">Roberto Clemente</a>, <a href="/wiki/1972_Puerto_Rico_DC-7_crash" title="1972 Puerto Rico DC-7 crash">who died in a plane crash</a> off <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Mar%C3%ADn_International_Airport" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn International Airport">Isla Verde International Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Carolina,_Puerto_Rico" title="Carolina, Puerto Rico">Carolina</a> in 1972, while flying with relief articles to be given to victims of the <a href="/wiki/1972_Nicaragua_earthquake" title="1972 Nicaragua earthquake">Nicaragua earthquake</a>. Construction of the facility began in the early 1970s, it was finished in January 1973, and inaugurated in February of that year by the <a href="/wiki/Fania_All-Stars" title="Fania All-Stars">Fania All-Stars</a>.\n</p><p>The coliseum has been host to a variety of events, including world championship <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">boxing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a>, business expos, circus, concerts and other things.\n</p><p>Many <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rican</a> and international performers have been at the arena, among others.\n</p><p>Concerts that have been held there include:\n</p>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1184024115">.mw-parser-output .div-col{margin-top:0.3em;column-width:30em}.mw-parser-output .div-col-small{font-size:90%}.mw-parser-output .div-col-rules{column-rule:1px solid #aaa}.mw-parser-output .div-col dl,.mw-parser-output .div-col ol,.mw-parser-output .div-col ul{margin-top:0}.mw-parser-output .div-col li,.mw-parser-output .div-col dd{page-break-inside:avoid;break-inside:avoid-column}</style><div class="div-col">\n<ul><li>Saga/FM: Silent Knight/City Of Fear Tour\n<ul><li>January 10, 1981 (The photo that appears on the Worlds Apart Album was shot there)</li></ul></li>\n<li>Santana: Zebop! religious services.\n</p><p>Most of the world championship boxing bouts fought in Puerto Rico during the late 1970s and early 1980s were fought at the Roberto Clemente coliseum, including <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Dur%C3%A1n" title="Roberto Dur\xc3\xa1n">Roberto Dur\xc3\xa1n</a>\'s world title defense versus Mexican <a href="/wiki/Leoncio_Ortiz" title="Leoncio Ortiz">Leoncio Ortiz</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" title="Jean Pierre Coopman">Jean Pierre Coopman</a>, the only time a world Heavyweight championship fight has been held in Puerto Rico. <a href="/wiki/George_Foreman" title="George Ch\xc3\xa1vez">Julio C\xc3\xa9sar Ch\xc3\xa1vez</a> had one of his first important fights there, on the undercard of <a href="/wiki/Edwin_Rosario" title="Edwin Rosario">Edwin Rosario</a>\'s world Lightweight championship win against <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Luis_Ram%C3%ADrez" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Luis Ram\xc3\xadrez">Jos\xc3\xa9 Luis Ram\xc3\xadrez</a>. Because of all the boxing action going on during that era, many observers call the period from the middle 1970s to the middle 1980s as the <i>golden age of <a href="/wiki/Boxing_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Boxing in Puerto Rico">boxing in Puerto Rico</a></i>.\n</p><p>The arena also hosted the final phase of the <a href="/wiki/1974_FIBA_World_Championship" title="1974 FIBA World Championship">1974</a> <a href="/wiki/FIBA_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="FIBA World Championship">World Basketball championship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The Coliseum has also been the place of large political rallies, and Puerto Rico\'s <i>electoral commission</i> uses its facilities to count votes after each election, and also hosted <a href="/wiki/Miss_Universe_2002" title="Miss Universe 2002">Miss Universe 2002</a>, won by <a href="/wiki/Oxana_Fedorova" title="Oxana Fedorova">Oxana Fedorova</a> of <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>.
