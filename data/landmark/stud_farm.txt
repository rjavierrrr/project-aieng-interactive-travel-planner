Wales</a></figcaption></figure>\n<p>A <b>stud farm</b> or <b>stud</b> in <a href="/wiki/Animal_husbandry" title="Animal husbandry">animal husbandry</a> is an establishment for <a href="/wiki/Selective_breeding" title="Selective breeding">selective breeding</a> of <a href="/wiki/Livestock" title="Livestock">livestock</a>. The word "<a href="/wiki/Stud_(animal)" title="Stud (animal)">stud</a>" comes from the Old English <i>stod</i> meaning "herd of horses, place where horses are kept for breeding".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Historically, documentation of the breedings that occur on a stud farm leads to the development of a <a href="/wiki/Stud_book" class="mw-redirect" title="Stud book">stud book</a>.  Male animals made available for breeding to outside female animals are said to be "standing at stud", or at "stud service", referencing the relatively high probability that they are kept at a stud farm.\n</p><p>The word stud is often restricted to larger domesticated (especially farm) animals, such as <a href="/wiki/Cattle" title="Cattle">cattle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horses</a>.  A specialized vocabulary exists for the studs of other animals, such as <a href="/wiki/Kennel" title="Kennel">kennel</a> (dog), <a href="/wiki/Cattery" title="Cattery">cattery</a> (cat) and <a href="/wiki/Aviary" title="Aviary">aviary</a> (birds).\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Horse_stud_farms">Horse stud farms</h2><span various sizes that are dedicated to animal breeding, usually of <a href="/wiki/Purebred" title="Purebred">purebred</a> livestock.  Some may have originally been founded with government assistance or sponsorship, or owned by political leaders, while others are solely the <a href="/wiki/Private_enterprise" class="mw-redirect" title="Private enterprise">private enterprise</a> of those who own them.  Some of the largest and most impressive are those dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Thoroughbred" title="Thoroughbred">Thoroughbred</a> horse breeding, such as the historic <a href="/wiki/Claiborne_Farm" title="Claiborne Farm">Claiborne Farm</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kentucky" title="Kentucky">Kentucky</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Darley_Stud" title="Darley Stud">Darley Stud</a>, owned by Dubai <a href="/wiki/Sheikh_Mohammed_bin_Rashid_Al_Maktoum" class="mw-redirect" title="Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum">Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum</a>, which stands over fifty stallions at stud in facilities located in seven countries.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Stud_service">Stud service</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stud_farm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Stud service"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Many stud farms make male animals available for breeding to outside female animals that are not owned by the stud farm.  Doing so provides an outside source of revenue to a stud farm via the <a href="/wiki/Stud_fee" class="mw-redirect" title="Stud fee">stud fees</a> paid to obtain the services of the stud animal as well as contributing to the overall <a href="/wiki/Genetic_diversity" title="Genetic diversity">genetic diversity</a> of the animal\'s offspring.  At state stud farms, stud service is not only a source of income, but due to the high standards set for breeding animals, has an overall effect of improving the quality of animals throughout an area.\n</p><p>In most cases, the owner of the female brings the animal to the stud farm for breeding, sometimes leaving her there for over a month to be sure that pregnancy has occurred.  However, with the invention of <a href="/wiki/Artificial_insemination" title="Artificial insemination">artificial insemination</a> and the ability to ship semen, combined with the availability of <a href="/wiki/DNA" title="DNA">DNA</a> testing for parentage verification, many <a href="/wiki/Breed_registry" title="Breed registry">breed registries</a> allow semen to be shipped from the stud farm to the location of the female animal, thus reducing or eliminating the need for animals to travel.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Stud_manager">Stud manager</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stud_farm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Stud manager"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>A stud manager or "stud master" is an individual responsible for an employer\'s <a href="/wiki/Selective_breeding" title="Selective breeding">breeding</a> stock.  The term is usually used for individuals working with <a href="/wiki/Dog" title="Dog">dogs</a> or <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horses</a>.  It is usually applied regardless of gender.\n</p><p>The stud manager typically suggests desired <a href="/wiki/Mating" title="Mating">matings</a> to the owner, and arranges for the same, whether in-house or by contract with animals standing at stud, approves and arranges matings to the owner\'s animals at stud requested by outsiders, and keeps all records, including notifying the appropriate animal <a href="/wiki/Breed_registry" title="Breed registry">registries</a>.\n</p><p>In a European <a href="/wiki/Great_house" title="Great house">large or formal household</a> the "Stud Master" or manager may be a permanent title and position; in this case this individual is often responsible for overseeing the maintenance of the <a href="/wiki/Stable" title="Stable">stables</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kennel" title="Kennel">kennels</a> as well.  Most large stud farms have a full-time individual assigned to stallion management, but many stud managers have either a part-time or contractual arrangement or their duties as stud manager may be incorporated with those of the overall farm or stable manager.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="See_also">See also</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
