neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Isla_Grande_(Santurce)" title="Isla Grande (Santurce)">Isla Grande</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>, managed by <a href="/wiki/ASM_Global" class="mw-redirect" title="ASM Global">ASM Global</a>. The Coca-Cola Music Hall is part of the <a href="/wiki/Distrito_T-Mobile" title="Distrito T-Mobile">T-Mobile District</a> development at the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Convention_Center" title="Puerto Rico Convention Center">Puerto Rico Convention District</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coca-Cola_Music_Hall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Coca-Cola Music Hall was developed as part of a major development plan around the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Convention_Center" title="Puerto Rico Convention Center">Puerto Rico Convention Center</a>. Initially developed as District Live!, it was developed as a major entertainment hub, which included two live music venues, an outdoor plaza with a performing stage, retail and eating spaces and a <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Cinemas" title="Caribbean Cinemas">Caribbean Cinemas</a> movie theater.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The area was officially opened as the <a href="/wiki/Distrito_T-Mobile" title="Distrito T-Mobile">T-Mobile District</a>.\n</p><p>Construction for the T-Mobile District, along with the Coca-Cola Music Hall, began in 2016 as part of a major redevelopment that would complement the Puerto Rico Convention Center that had opened in 2005. The Coca-Cola Music Hall was described as a top class live event venue that would feature a prestigious sound system, a 75x30 LED screen and its own in-house lighting equipment that would facilitate staging for future events. At a $40 Million investment, the Coca-Cola Music Hall\'s construction was completed in 2020 but the opening of the venue, originally scheduled for March 2020, was held back until 2021 due to the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>. Naming rights were sold to <a href="/wiki/The_Coca-Cola_Company" title="The Coca-Cola Company">The Coca-Cola Company</a> in 2019 for an undisclosed amount, thus making it the first venue in Puerto Rico to sell its naming right.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Puerto Rican singer, <a href="/wiki/Ednita_Nazario" title="Ednita Nazario">Ednita Nazario</a> held the inaugural event at the venue to a sold-out performance.\n</p><p>On March 16, 2022, the Coca-Cola Music Hall hosted the <a href="/wiki/Miss_World_2021" title="Miss World 2021">Miss World 2021</a> pageant,<sup id="cite_ref-A_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-A-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>a<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> which was originally scheduled on December 16, 2021 at the <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Miguel_Agrelot_Coliseum" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Miguel Agrelot Coliseum">Jos\xc3\xa9 Miguel Agrelot Coliseum</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-B_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-B-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>b<span
