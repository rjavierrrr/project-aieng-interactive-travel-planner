Cayey</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Cerro Las Tetas</b>, nicknamed <b>Las Tetas de Cayey</b> but <a href="/wiki/Official" title="Official">officially</a> <b>Las Piedras del Collado</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pueblodesalinas_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pueblodesalinas-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> are two <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountain</a> peaks located in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Salinas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Salinas, Puerto Rico">Salinas, Puerto Rico</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-gnis_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gnis-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> north of the city of Salinas proper. Since September 1, 2000, the peaks have become part of the <b>Las Piedras del Collado Nature Reserve</b> and are protected by law.<sup id="cite_ref-pueblodesalinas_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pueblodesalinas-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Their height is 2,762 feet (842&#160;m) above sea level.<sup id="cite_ref-gnis_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gnis-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Etymology">Etymology</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cerro_Las_Tetas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Cerro Las Tetas are so-named for their resemblance to a female\xe2\x80\x99s <a href="/wiki/Breasts" class="mw-redirect" title="Breasts">breasts</a>; <i>tetas</i> is colloquial for "breasts" in Spanish, much like the English slang "tits".<sup id="cite_ref-sierra-club_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sierra-club-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cerro_Las_Tetas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>A common misconception is that <i>Las Tetas</i> are located in the municipality of Cayey.  <i>Las Tetas</i> are actually located the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Salinas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Salinas, Puerto Rico">Salinas</a>, in Barrio Palmas, a <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico">barrio</a> on the northwestern part of the municipality of Salinas.<sup id="cite_ref-maptest_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maptest-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> They are located close to\xe2\x80\x94but not in\xe2\x80\x94the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Cayey" class="mw-redirect" title="Cayey">Cayey</a>, thus the "de Cayey" (English "of Cayey") in its name. They are located about 500 metres (1,600&#160;ft) southwest of the intersection of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_1" title="Puerto Rico Highway 1">PR-1</a> and <a href="/wiki/PR-162" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-162">PR-162</a>. Topological maps readily show this.<sup id="cite_ref-maptest_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maptest-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-mtnzone_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mtnzone-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Confusion_regarding_its_location">Confusion regarding its location</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cerro_Las_Tetas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Confusion regarding its location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <b>Las Tetas</b> peaks are part of the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_de_Cayey" title="Sierra de Cayey">Sierra de Cayey</a> (or Cayey Range).<sup id="cite_ref-pr_enc_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr_enc-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The <b>Sierra de Cayey</b>, like the <a href="/wiki/Cordillera_Central,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Cordillera Central, Puerto Rico">Cordillera Central</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Luquillo" class="mw-redirect" title="Sierra Luquillo">Sierra de Luquillo</a> ranges, extends over several municipalities beyond Cayey.<sup id="cite_ref-solboricua_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-solboricua-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The name of the mountain range where Las Tetas is located (Sierra de <b>Cayey</b>) may be a possible source of confusion regarding the location of the peaks themselves:<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> a little-known fact is that the town of Cayey derives its name from Sierra de Cayey, and not the other-way-around.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Another possible source of confusion may lie in the fact that the road to reach the peaks begins from within the Municipality of Cayey\xe2\x80\x94albeit just meters from its border with the Municipality of Salinas.  A third possible source of confusion, about the municipality where the mountains are located, may be the fact that the peaks are located closer to downtown Cayey than to downtown Salinas.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Cayey is also a <a href="/wiki/Land-locked" class="mw-redirect" title="Land-locked">land-locked</a>, mostly mountainous municipality; Salinas, by comparison and as a municipality on the southern coastal plain, consists mostly of flatlands. This may be a fourth reason yet why many people believe <i>Las Tetas</i> mountain peaks are located in Cayey, and not in Salinas. Yet another possible reason for the confusion may be that, in order to locate <i>Las Tetas</i> on the official <a href="/wiki/USGS" class="mw-redirect" title="USGS">USGS</a> map, the map for the Cayey Quadrangle is used.<sup id="cite_ref-mtnzone_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mtnzone-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In the mid-2000s, the town of <a href="/wiki/Salinas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Salinas, Puerto Rico">Salinas</a> took the town of <a href="/wiki/Cayey,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cayey, Puerto Rico">Cayey</a> to court over the location of "Las Tetas" and won the case. The matter involved land development and the Puerto Rico Planning Board. The result was a vindication of the claim that the "Las Tetas" location as being in the municipality of Cayey was incorrect. Las Tetas, the Court agreed, are actually "Las Tetas de Salinas" for they are actually located within the municipality of Salinas, not in Cayey.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> They have also been called <i>Las Tetas de Salinas.</i><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  Even so, the name of the mountains are so embedded in Puerto Rican tradition that they continue to be called "Las Tetas de Cayey".\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Viewing_location">Viewing location</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cerro_Las_Tetas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Viewing location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <i>Las Tetas</i> peaks can be easily viewed from the <a href="/wiki/Rest_area" title="Rest area">rest area</a> on <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_52" title="Puerto Rico Highway 52">Puerto Rico Highway 52</a>, located at kilometer post 49.0 of that highway. The rest area is colocated with the <a href="/wiki/Monumento_al_J%C3%ADbaro_Puertorrique%C3%B1o" title="Monumento al J\xc3\xadbaro Puertorrique\xc3\xb1o">Monumento al J\xc3\xadbaro Puertorrique\xc3\xb1o</a> statue and monument, in the municipality of Salinas.  The two peaks can be seen in the northwestern horizon when viewing from the rest area.<sup id="cite_ref-elnuevodia_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elnuevodia-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Hiking, mountain climbing, and camping at Cerro Las Tetas can be arranged by local tour companies.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Height">Height</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cerro_Las_Tetas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Height"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The peaks are contoured at 841 metres (2,759&#160;ft) high.
