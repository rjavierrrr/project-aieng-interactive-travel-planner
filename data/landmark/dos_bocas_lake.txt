scope="row" class="infobox-label">Surface elevation</th><td class="infobox-data">292&#160;ft (89&#160;m)</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Dos Bocas Lake</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Lake" title="Lake">lake</a> located in the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Arecibo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Arecibo, Puerto Rico">Arecibo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Utuado,_Puerto_Rico" title="Utuado, Puerto Rico">Utuado</a> on the island of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The lake was created from 1937 until it was completed in 1942 with the construction of the Dos Bocas Dam, by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Electric_Power_Authority" title="Puerto Rico Electric Power Authority">Puerto Rico Electric Power Authority</a> for a hydroelectric power plant. The building of the dam was a <a href="/wiki/New_Deal" title="New Deal">New Deal</a> project.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It also serves as one of the island\'s potable water reservoirs. In recent years, silt has accumulated in the lake causing capacity to shrink. Silt has also made some portions of the land inaccessible by boat.\n</p><p>Ferries operated by the Department of Transportation of Puerto Rico provide transportation for residents on Lago Dos Bocas as well as for tourists. Several restaurants are located on the shore of the lake. Other nearby attractions include the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Abajo_State_Forest" title="R\xc3\xado Abajo State Forest">R\xc3\xado Abajo State Forest</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc"
