colspan="2">\n</th></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Teatro Alejandro Tapia y Rivera</b> (commonly referred as "Teatro Tapia"), is the oldest free-standing <a href="/wiki/Theater_(structure)" title="Theater (structure)">drama stage building</a> located in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>. It is named after <a href="/wiki/Alejandro_Tapia_y_Rivera" title="Alejandro Tapia y Rivera">Alejandro Tapia y Rivera</a> (1826\xe2\x80\x931882), a <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican people">Puerto Rican</a> poet and dramatist.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teatro_Tapia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The theater faces east, across from Plaza de Col\xc3\xb3n, on Fortaleza Street, and was constructed in 1824. It was designed in the <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> style as a horseshoe shaped <a href="/wiki/Opera_house" title="Opera house">opera house</a> with three tiers of boxes. A block south lies a brick-shaped former <i>Casa de camineros</i>, house of road-maintainers. To the north-east lies a string of cultural institutions: the restored <i>Casino de Puerto Rico</i>, the <a href="/wiki/YMCA" title="YMCA">YMCA</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_library" title="Carnegie library">Carnegie library</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ateneo_Puertorrique%C3%B1o" title="Ateneo Puertorrique\xc3\xb1o">Ateneo Puertorrique\xc3\xb1o</a>, and Moorish-inspired <a href="/wiki/Casa_de_Espa%C3%B1a" title="Casa de Espa\xc3\xb1a">Casa de Espa\xc3\xb1a</a>. In the northern seaward cliff sprawls the historic and labyrinthine fort of <a href="/wiki/Fort_San_Crist%C3%B3bal_(Puerto_Rico)" class="mw-redirect" title="Fort San Crist\xc3\xb3bal (Puerto Rico)">San Crist\xc3\xb3bal</a>. The original landward gate of old San Juan lays just east of this site on Calle Fortaleza.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Significance">Significance</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teatro_Tapia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Significance"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>For over one hundred years since it was built it was the center of cultural life in the city. Famous soprano <a href="/wiki/Adelina_Patti" title="Adelina Patti">Adelina Patti</a> sang there in one of her earlier tours of <a href="/wiki/The_Americas" class="mw-redirect" title="The Americas">the Americas</a> during the mid-nineteenth century. Other notable performers to have graced its stage include the Russian ballerina <a href="/wiki/Anna_Pavlova" title="Anna Pavlova">Anna Pavlova</a>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Emilio_J._Pasarell" title="Emilio J. Pasarell">Emilio Pasarell</a> and Antonia Saez have said that "the most significant moments of Puerto Rico\'s musical history are associated with...Teatro Tapia."<sup id="cite_ref-Thompson1990_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Thompson1990-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teatro_Tapia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a data-file-height="1368" /></a><figcaption>View of the Tapia Theater and the San Juan Bay, in Old San Juan, Puerto Rico, in 1908.</figcaption></figure><p>Initially named the San Juan Municipal Theater, it was renamed the <i>Antonio Paoli Theater</i> in 1935.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was subsequently officially renamed the <i>Teatro Alejandro Tapia y Rivera</i> in 1937.<sup id="cite_ref-Thompson1990_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Thompson1990-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n</p><p>After years of neglect during the 1940s, the theater was saved from demolition by the former long-time Mayor of San Juan <a href="/wiki/Felisa_Rinc%C3%B3n_de_Gautier" title="Felisa Rinc\xc3\xb3n de Gautier">Felisa Rinc\xc3\xb3n de Gautier</a>. It was restored in the late 1940s and has subsequently been used to provide a venue for <a href="/wiki/Musical_theater" class="mw-redirect" title="Musical theater">musical theater</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drama" title="Drama">drama</a> and other cultural events. With a maximum capacity of 647 people, this 19th-century theater serves as an arena for cultural events and enchanting ballet performances. The interior audience hall is remarkable for the period. Subsequent restorations (in 1976 and 1997) returned it to its old charm.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="References">References</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
