Terry</b> (English: <i>Charles H. Terry Athletic Field</i>) or, simply, <b>El Charles H. Terry</b>, is the oldest continuously functioning athletic field and baseball park in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, and the second oldest in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The field is located in the city of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a> and has a capacity of several thousand spectators. It is located next to the <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Pancho_Coimbre_Museum" class="mw-redirect" title="Francisco Pancho Coimbre Museum">Francisco Pancho Coimbre Sports Museum</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Campo_Atl%C3%A9tico_Charles_H._Terry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Campo Atl\xc3\xa9tico Charles H. Terry was built in 1909 by initiative of then <a href="/wiki/Ponce_High_School" title="Ponce High School">Ponce High School</a> students Cayetano Pou, Rafael Matienzo and Francisco Perez, and the financial backing of Lucas P. Valdivieso and Fernando Luis Toro. The field was initially called <i>La Liga del Castillo</i> for its proximity to the <i>Carcel del Castillo</i> (Castillo Jail) then located just north of it, where <a href="/wiki/Antiguo_Cuartel_Militar_Espa%C3%B1ol_de_Ponce" title="Antiguo Cuartel Militar Espa\xc3\xb1ol de Ponce">Escuela de Bellas Artes de Ponce</a> is located today (2019).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The Charles H. Terry Athletic Field opened in November 1910, in barrio <a href="/wiki/Quinto_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Quinto (Ponce)">Quinto</a>, in Ponce, Puerto Rico. Its original intended use was to serve as an athletics, sports, and baseball field for students of the new <a href="/wiki/Ponce_High_School" title="Ponce High School">Ponce High School</a>. It was named after one of the first principals of the school, the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">continental</a> Charles H. Terry, for his effort and dedication in securing the field for use by the students of his school.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Prior to being used as an athletic field for school activities, the area was used for military exercises by the Spanish Military Headquarters adjacent to it.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The first official baseball game in Ponce took place in this field in 1899.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The first baseball championship in Ponce took place here in 1904 between the local Ponce, Pabst, and Atenas teams.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> teacher Charles H. Terry, who in 1909 was named superintendent of Ponce public schools, made the arrangements with the United States Army stationed at the old Spanish Military headquarters adjacent to the field to acquire the area where the athletic field would be built. The building of the physical facilities started the same year. Engineers from the Central Guanica sugarcane mill lend technical assistance for the development of the facility. Adrian J. Grief, an officer at <a href="/wiki/Central_Gu%C3%A1nica" title="Central Gu\xc3\xa1nica">Central Gu\xc3\xa1nica</a>, donated the materials for the construction of the field. Volleyball, <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a>, and basketball facilities were operational by 1913.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1913-1914 the American "All Stars" baseball team arrived in Ponce and played at <i>Campo Atletico</i>. The teams included players from the <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_(baseball)" title="Triple-A (baseball)">AAA baseball</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The 1920s saw the field being used mostly for track and field, basketball and baseball activities.\n</p><p>On 27 February 1930 the daily <i>Aguila de Puerto Rico</i> announces a ceremony to name the field, which so far was called the local playground and Liga del Castillo, as <i>Campo Atletico Charles H. Terry</i>. In 1938 the semi-professional league is created in Puerto Rico and it starts sharing use of Campo Atletico with the professional league.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n3 December 1944, registered the first\xe2\x80\x94and only\xe2\x80\x94no hits-no-runs games in Puerto Rican professional baseball.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The last game in professional baseball played in Campo Atletico took place on 11 February 1949. After this date games were played at <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Francisco_Montaner" title="Estadio Francisco Montaner">Estadio Francisco Montaner</a>. Likewise, in that year basketball games were moved from the courts at <a href="/wiki/Colegio_Ponce%C3%B1o" title="Colegio Ponce\xc3\xb1o">Colegio Ponce\xc3\xb1o de Varones</a> to the court at Charles H. Terry.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In the 1950s, Campo Atletico is rebuilt considerably. In 1983 the field is converted into Ciudad Deportiva Charles H. Terry. On 21 May 1992, the area beneath the grand stand is converted into a city sports museum, <a href="/wiki/Museo_Francisco_%22Pancho%22_Coimbre" title="Museo Francisco &quot;Pancho&quot; Coimbre">Museo Francisco "Pancho" Coimbre</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="100th_anniversary">100th anniversary</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Campo_Atl%C3%A9tico_Charles_H._Terry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 100th anniversary"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The celebrations commemorating the 100th anniversary of the park took place on 20 November 2010 at the <a href="/wiki/Museo_Francisco_%22Pancho%22_Coimbre" title="Museo Francisco &quot;Pancho&quot; Coimbre">Francisco "Pancho" Coimbre Sports Museum</a>.  The museum is adjacent to the park. As part of the celebrations there was an exposition of photos and sports <a href="/wiki/Paraphernalia" title="Paraphernalia">paraphernalia</a> related to the historic park. There was also a <a href="/wiki/Three-dimensional_space" title="Three-dimensional space">3-D</a> model of the park produced by the <a href="/wiki/Pontifical_Catholic_University_of_Puerto_Rico_School_of_Architecture" title="Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Architecture">Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Architecture</a>. A documentary film was also incorporated. Participants included players, sports personalities and historians who have witnessed to the historical moments of the park.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Uses">Uses</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Campo_Atl%C3%A9tico_Charles_H._Terry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Uses"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The field is used for the <a href="/wiki/Physical_Education" class="mw-redirect" title="Physical Education">Physical Education</a> activities of several schools in the area, including Ponce High School and the <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Pujals" title="Rafael Pujals">Rafael Pujals</a> Middle School, located immediately across the street from the park.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
