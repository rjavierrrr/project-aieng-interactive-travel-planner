href="/wiki/Porta_del_Sol" title="Porta del Sol">western Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It sits by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_2" title="Puerto Rico Highway 2">Puerto Rico Highway 2</a> which is the main road in the west. Close to major shopping centers like <a href="/wiki/Mayag%C3%BCez_Mall" title="Mayag\xc3\xbcez Mall">Mayag\xc3\xbcez Mall</a> and Western Plaza. Less than 5 minutes away from the world-renowned <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Mayag%C3%BCez" title="University of Puerto Rico at Mayag\xc3\xbcez">University Of Puerto Rico at Mayag\xc3\xbcez </a>. \n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Facilities">Facilities</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayag%C3%BCez_Resort_%26_Casino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Facilities"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The hotel has two restaurants, a large conference center, a casino, and a newly constructed river pool.  It was the main hotel for the <a href="/wiki/2010_Central_American_and_Caribbean_Games" title="2010 Central American and Caribbean Games">2010 Central American and Caribbean Games</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayag%C3%BCez_Resort_%26_Casino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The hotel is located on the land where the Valdes Castle once stood. Valdes Castle was the home of a prominent Puerto Rican figure <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_Vald%C3%A9s_Cobi%C3%A1n" title="Alfonso Vald\xc3\xa9s Cobi\xc3\xa1n">Alfonso Vald\xc3\xa9s Cobi\xc3\xa1n</a>, he was the co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Compa%C3%B1%C3%ADa_Cervecera_de_Puerto_Rico" title="Compa\xc3\xb1\xc3\xada Cervecera de Puerto Rico">Cervecer\xc3\xada India</a> which is the largest brewery on the island and creator of Medalla Light and Malta India. \n</p><p>The hotel was built in 1963 by <a href="/wiki/Hilton_Hotels_%26_Resorts" title="Hilton Hotels &amp; Resorts">Hilton</a> and was named Mayag\xc3\xbcez Hilton. The hotel had a very modern design mimicking that of the <a href="/wiki/Caribe_Hilton_Hotel" title="Caribe Hilton Hotel">Caribe Hilton </a> hotel in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>. It had 140 rooms with air-conditioning and an Olympic-sized pool for its guests. The entrance still has the main gates of the Valdes Castle, showcasing its history. It also opened a brand new Casino which was one of the first in western Puerto Rico and was very popular amongst guests. \n</p><p>It boasted the tourism to the west but its location wasn\'t as stellar as they had hoped. Although Mayag\xc3\xbcez is one of the biggest cities on the west side, it was being overthrown by <a href="/wiki/Rinc%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico" title="Rinc\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico">Rincon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aguadilla,_Puerto_Rico" title="Aguadilla, Puerto Rico">Aguadilla</a> which were more inclined to get visited by tourists due to the proximity to the airport and its beach offerings that Mayag\xc3\xbcez did not have. \n</p><p>Because of this, Hilton decided to sell the hotel. The hotel was then purchased by a private group and it was renamed The Mayag\xc3\xbcez Resort and Casino. As with its rebranding, it expanded its recreational activities to open a new pool area with a zero-entry pool, a lazy river, and a water slide. And also expanding the room count by opening a brand-new tower adding more than double their rooms. \n</p><p><br />\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="See_also">See also</h2><span
