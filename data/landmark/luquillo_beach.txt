.IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .infobox .IPA-label-small,.mw-parser-output .navbox .IPA-label-small{font-size:100%}</style><span class="IPA-label IPA-label-small">Spanish pronunciation:</span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="es-Latn-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">&#91;lu\xcb\x88ki\xca\x8eo&#93;</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Luquillo_barrio-pueblo" title="Luquillo barrio-pueblo">town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Municipalities of Puerto Rico">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> located in the northeast coast, northwest of <a href="/wiki/Fajardo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Fajardo, Puerto Rico">Fajardo</a>; and east of <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande,_Puerto_Rico" title="R\xc3\xado Grande, Puerto Rico">Rio Grande</a>. Luquillo is spread over 5 barrios and <a href="/wiki/Luquillo_barrio-pueblo" title="Luquillo barrio-pueblo">Luquillo Pueblo</a> (the downtown area and the administrative center of the city). It lends its name to the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_de_Luquillo" title="Sierra de Luquillo">Sierra de Luquillo</a>, where <a href="/wiki/El_Yunque_National_Forest" title="El Yunque National Forest">El Yunque National Forest</a> is located. It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Fajardo_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Fajardo Metropolitan Statistical Area">Fajardo Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>.\n</p><p>The city of Luquillo is 26 square miles and it sits on 12 miles of <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a> coastline. It is nestled between the blue waters of the Atlantic and the <a href="/wiki/El_Yunque_National_Forest" title="El Yunque National Forest">El Yunque National Forest</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Rainforest" title="Rainforest">rainforest</a>, giving it a diverse and unique ecology. Luquillo marks the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Northeast_Ecological_Corridor" title="Northeast Ecological Corridor">Northeast Ecological Corridor</a> Nature Reserve which runs down the coast from downtown Luquillo all the way down to the Seven Seas Beach in <a href="/wiki/Fajardo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Fajardo, Puerto Rico">Fajardo</a>. During certain times of the year, it is not unusual to encounter rare or <a href="/wiki/Endangered_species" title="Endangered species">endangered species</a> of fauna (like the <a href="/wiki/Leatherback_turtle" class="mw-redirect" title="Leatherback turtle">leatherback turtle</a>) while visiting in Luquillo.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luquillo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Luquillo was founded in 1797 and is known as "<i>La Capital del Sol</i>" (Capital of the Sun), "<i>La Riviera de Puerto Rico</i>" (the Puerto Rican Riviera), and "<i>Los Come Cocos</i>" (the <a href="/wiki/Coconut" title="Coconut">coconut</a> eaters). Legend says that the town was named after the Indian cacique <i>Loquillo</i>, who died a few years after the last Indian rebellion in 1513. However, the word Luquillo most likely originates from <i>Yukiyu</i>, the Taino name for <a href="/wiki/El_Yunque_(Puerto_Rico)" title="El Yunque (Puerto Rico)">El Yunque</a>, most likely meaning "white mountain" (i.e. foggy mountain).In 1989, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hugo" title="Hurricane Hugo">Hurricane Hugo</a> damaged the town.\n</p><p>On September 20, 2017 <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> struck the island of Puerto Rico.  In Luquillo, rivers were breached, there were landslides, the electrical power collapsed, and 1100 homes were destroyed or damaged. Incidents of looting and assaults were reported in the aftermath of the storm.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USGS_Maria_Landslides_map-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luquillo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Luquillo is located on the northeast coast.<sup id="cite_ref-PR_Ency_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_Ency-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Barrios">Barrios</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luquillo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Barrios"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Like all municipalities of Puerto Rico, Luquillo is subdivided into <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico">barrios</a>. id="Government">Government</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luquillo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Government"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1236090951"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mayoralty_in_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayoralty in Puerto Rico">Mayoralty in Puerto Rico</a></div>\n<p>Like all municipalities in Puerto Rico, Luquillo is administered by a mayor. The current mayor is <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_M%C3%A1rquez_Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Jes\xc3\xbas M\xc3\xa1rquez Rodr\xc3\xadguez">Jes\xc3\xbas M\xc3\xa1rquez Rodr\xc3\xadguez</a>, who was elected at the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_general_election,_2012" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican general election, 2012">2012 general election</a>.\n</p><p>The city belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Senatorial_district_VIII" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rico Senatorial district VIII">Puerto Rico Senatorial district VIII</a>, which is represented by two senators. In 2012, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_A._Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Pedro A. Rodr\xc3\xadguez">Pedro A. Rodr\xc3\xadguez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luis_Daniel_Rivera" title="Luis Daniel Rivera">Luis Daniel Rivera</a> were elected as district senators.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Transportation">Transportation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luquillo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Transportation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Entrada_a_Barrio_Sabana,_Luquillo,_Puerto_Rico.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Entrada_a_Barrio_Sabana%2C_Luquillo%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg/220px-Entrada_a_Barrio_Sabana%2C_Luquillo%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="126" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Entrada_a_Barrio_Sabana%2C_Luquillo%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg/330px-Entrada_a_Barrio_Sabana%2C_Luquillo%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Entrada_a_Barrio_Sabana%2C_Luquillo%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg/440px-Entrada_a_Barrio_Sabana%2C_Luquillo%2C_Puerto_Rico.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3169" data-file-height="1820" /></a><figcaption>Entrance sign for Sabana barrio and PR-983</figcaption></figure>\n<p>There is no public transportation in Luquillo, and residents and visitors rely on <a href="/wiki/Uber" title="Uber">Uber</a> or Luquillo Taxi\xe2\x84\xa2\xef\xb8\x8f &amp; Tours Puerto Rico LLC <a href="/w/index.php?title=Luquillo_Taxi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Luquillo Taxi (page does not exist)">Luquillo Taxi</a> for service.\n</p><p>There are 20 bridges in Luquillo.<sup id="cite_ref-bridge_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bridge-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_3" title="Puerto Rico Highway 3">PR-3</a> is the main road through Luquillo. Other municipality roads include PR-983, PR-988, PR-991 and PR-940.<sup id="cite_ref-bridge_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bridge-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Symbols">Symbols</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luquillo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Symbols"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">municipio</i></span> has an official flag and coat of arms.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flag">Flag</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luquillo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Flag"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>It consists of three horizontal stripes, the blue top and green bottom are double of width of the yellow central stripe. Blue makes reference to the sky and the sea; yellow represents the sand of its beaches and green represents the vegetation of the <a href="/wiki/El_Yunque_(Puerto_Rico)" title="El Yunque (Puerto Rico)">mountains</a>. In the center stripe resides the coat of arms of the municipality superimposed and surrounded by two palm tree leaves crossed at the bottom.<sup id="cite_ref-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LexJuris_(Leyes_y_Jurisprudencia)_de_Puerto_Rico_2020-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luquillo,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Coat of arms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In a gold background a centered mountain range with three green mountains is accompanied at the bottom by a bay with blue and silver waves; the top portion of the shield in blue, has three iris branches. Above the shield resides a three tower gold crown.
