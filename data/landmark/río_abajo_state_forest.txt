class="infobox-data">1980</td></tr><tr style="display:none"><th colspan="2">\n</th></tr><tr style="display:none"><td colspan="2">\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Rio Abajo State Forest</b> is a forest preserve in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> owned by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Natural_and_Environmental_Resources" title="Puerto Rico Department of Natural and Environmental Resources">Department of Natural Resources</a> and one of the 20 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Puerto_Rico_state_forests" title="List of Puerto Rico state forests">state forests in the island</a>. It was designated a <a href="/wiki/National_Natural_Landmark" title="National Natural Landmark">National Natural Landmark</a> in 1980 and constitutes an area of 3,590 acres. It consists mostly of <a href="/wiki/Subtropical" class="mw-redirect" title="Subtropical">subtropical</a> wet and moist <a href="/wiki/Karst_forest" class="mw-redirect" title="Karst forest">karst forest</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mogote" title="Mogote">Mogotes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sinkhole" title="Sinkhole">sinkholes</a> fill the landscape.<sup id="cite_ref-NNL_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NNL-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Smithsonian_2007_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Smithsonian_2007-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The forest preserve is located in the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Arecibo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Arecibo, Puerto Rico">Arecibo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Utuado,_Puerto_Rico" title="Utuado, Puerto Rico">Utuado</a>. In addition to its ecological value the forest also contains a number of archaeological sites.<sup id="cite_ref-DRNA_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DRNA-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%ADo_Abajo_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Much of the development within the forest area was made by the <a href="/wiki/Civilian_Conservation_Corps" title="Civilian Conservation Corps">Civilian Conservation Corps</a> (CCC) during the 20th century. The forest preserve was one of the five designated forests designated in 1943 by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Reconstruction_Administration" title="Puerto Rico Reconstruction Administration">Puerto Rico Reconstruction Administration</a> (Spanish: <i>Administraci\xc3\xb3n de Reconstrucci\xc3\xb3n de Puerto Rico</i>) to preserve the remaining forested areas in the island. This forest also protects a large portion of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Grande_de_Arecibo" title="R\xc3\xado Grande de Arecibo">Arecibo River</a> hydrological basin.<sup id="cite_ref-DRNA_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DRNA-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The remains of the lumber roads cut by the loggers and CCC workers have now become trails.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Due to its value as an ideal representation of the karstic ecosystems in Puerto Rico and the Caribbean, the state forest was designated a <a href="/wiki/National_Natural_Landmark" title="National Natural Landmark">National Natural Landmark</a> by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_the_Interior" title="United States Department of the Interior">United States Department of Interior</a> in 1980.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%ADo_Abajo_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>R\xc3\xado Abajo State Forest is located on rugged <a href="/wiki/Karst" title="Karst">karstic</a> terrain in north-central Puerto Rico, between the municipalities of Arecibo (<a href="/wiki/Hato_Viejo,_Arecibo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Hato Viejo, Arecibo, Puerto Rico">Hato Viejo</a> and <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Arriba,_Arecibo,_Puerto_Rico" title="R\xc3\xado Arriba, Arecibo, Puerto Rico">R\xc3\xado Arriba</a>) and Utuado (<a href="/wiki/Caguana,_Utuado,_Puerto_Rico" title="Caguana, Utuado, Puerto Rico">Caguana</a> and <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Abajo,_Utuado,_Puerto_Rico" title="R\xc3\xado Abajo, Utuado, Puerto Rico">R\xc3\xado Abajo</a>).\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geology">Geology</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%ADo_Abajo_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The forest preserve is part of the Montebello karst, part of the <a href="/wiki/Cibao_Marl" title="Cibao Marl">Cibao formation</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Karst_(Puerto_Rico)" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern Karst (Puerto Rico)">Northern karst</a> zone of Puerto Rico and its most prominent landforms are <a href="/wiki/Mogote" title="Mogote">mogotes</a>, distinctive steep eroded hills composed of <a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">limestone</a>. Caves, canyons, mesas, sinkholes and ravines are also very common in this type of geology and can be encountered within the forest limits. This is the only place in Puerto Rico where the great <a href="/wiki/Eocene%E2%80%93Oligocene_extinction_event" title="Eocene\xe2\x80\x93Oligocene extinction event">Eocene-Oligocene</a> unconformity can be clearly seen.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Ecology_and_conservation">Ecology and conservation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%ADo_Abajo_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ecology and conservation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Image_large1.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Image_large1.jpg/220px-Image_large1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="123" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Image_large1.jpg/330px-Image_large1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Image_large1.jpg/440px-Image_large1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="768" data-file-height="431" /></a><figcaption>Puerto Rican parrots at the forest aviary. The forest is the site of a revitalized parrot population since 1983.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The forest is home to wildlife that include <a href="/wiki/Endangered_species" title="Endangered species">endangered species</a> such as the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_broad-winged_hawk" title="Puerto Rican broad-winged hawk">Puerto Rican broad-winged hawk</a> (<i>Buteo platypterus brunnescens</i>), the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_boa" title="Puerto Rican boa">Puerto Rican boa</a> (<i>Chilabothrus inornatus</i>), and a captive population of critically endangered <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_parrot" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican parrot">Puerto Rican parrots</a> (<i>Amazona vittata</i>). Captive parrots are slowly being released into Rio Abajo in an attempt to form a second population.<sup id="cite_ref-DRNA_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DRNA-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-drna_parrots_released_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-drna_parrots_released-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Flora">Flora</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%ADo_Abajo_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Flora"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The forest is home to more than 175 species of trees, out of which about 47 species are considered endangered or threatened. Some of the most common plant species are the ceiba or kapok (<i><a href="/wiki/Ceiba_pentandra" title="Ceiba pentandra">Ceiba pentandra</a></i>), algarrobo (<i><a href="/wiki/Samanea_saman" title="Samanea saman">Samanea saman</a></i>), jobo (<i><a href="/wiki/Spondias_mombin" title="Spondias mombin">Spondias mombin</a></i>), maga (<i><a href="/wiki/Thespesia_grandiflora" title="Thespesia grandiflora">Thespesia grandiflora</a></i>), tabaiba (<i><a href="/wiki/Sapium" title="Sapium">Sapium laurocerasus</a></i>), cohoba (<i><a href="/wiki/Cojoba_arborea" title="Cojoba arborea">Cojoba arborea</a></i>), and white fiddlewood (<i><a href="/wiki/Vitex_divaricata" title="Vitex divaricata">Vitex divaricata</a></i>). The forest is also home to one of the four remaining populations of <i><a href="/wiki/Daphnopsis_hellerana" class="mw-redirect" title="Daphnopsis hellerana">Daphnopsis hellerana</a></i>, which only grows on limestone, erubia (<i><a href="/wiki/Solanum_ensifolium" title="Solanum ensifolium">Solanum drymophilum</a></i>), palo de rosa (<i><a href="/wiki/Ottoschulzia_rhodoxylon" title="Ottoschulzia rhodoxylon">Ottoschulzia rhodoxylon</a></i>), and the critically endangered nigua (<i><a href="/wiki/Cornutia_obovata" title="Cornutia obovata">Cornutia obovata</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-DRNA_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DRNA-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Fauna">Fauna</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%ADo_Abajo_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Fauna"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>More than 34 species of birds have been documented in the forest. It is also a key protected habitat to the endangered Puerto Rican broad-winged hawk (<i><a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_broad-winged_hawk" title="Puerto Rican broad-winged hawk">Buteo platypterus brunnescens</a></i>) and a number of 60 individuals is estimated to live in the area. Puerto Rican parrot (<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_amazon" title="Puerto Rican amazon"><i>Amazona vittata</i></a>) populations have been re-introduced into the forest since 1983 as part of a conservation program by the Puerto Rico Department of Natural and Environmental Resources and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Fish_and_Wildlife_Service" title="United States Fish and Wildlife Service">United States Fish and Wildlife Service</a>. The forest is also home to the Jos\xc3\xa9 Luis Vivaldi Lugo Aviary, dedicated to the breeding of the endangered Puerto Rican parrot for the purpose of developing a healthy population in the area; 43 individuals have been released into the wild as of 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-DRNA_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DRNA-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Recreation">Recreation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%ADo_Abajo_State_Forest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Recreation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The reserve has a reputation for being a great camping spot (reservations required) and it offers visitors as many as 70 different walking trails that will better enable visitors to observe as much of the plant and bird life in the area as possible.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Some of the trails are the <a href="/wiki/Frank_H._Wadsworth" title="Frank H. Wadsworth">Frank H. Wadsworth</a> trail,<sup id="cite_ref-Atabey_2016_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Atabey_2016-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Visitors Center trail, the Cueva del Agua trail, and the Tanam\xc3\xa1 River trail.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span
