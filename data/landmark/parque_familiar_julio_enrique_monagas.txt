closed</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Parque Familiar Julio Enrique Monagas</b> (English: <i>Julio Enrique Monagas Family Park</i>) is Puerto Rico\'s largest passive park.<sup id="cite_ref-LGP_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LGP-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-periodicolaperla.com_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-periodicolaperla.com-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is located in barrio <a href="/wiki/Bucan%C3%A1" class="mw-redirect" title="Bucan\xc3\xa1">Bucan\xc3\xa1</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>, on the banks of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Bucan%C3%A1" class="mw-redirect" title="R\xc3\xado Bucan\xc3\xa1">Bucan\xc3\xa1</a> and <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Portugu%C3%A9s" class="mw-redirect" title="R\xc3\xado Portugu\xc3\xa9s">Portugu\xc3\xa9s</a> rivers. The park was named after <a href="/wiki/Julio_Enrique_Monagas" title="Julio Enrique Monagas">Julio Enrique Monagas</a>, "the father of Puerto Rican Olympic sports".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In November 2017, the park was severely damaged by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> and, as of today (January 2021), remains closed.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading href="/wiki/Barrio" title="Barrio">barrio</a> <a href="/wiki/Bucana" class="mw-redirect" title="Bucana">Bucana</a>, at the merging of <a href="/wiki/Rio_Portugues" class="mw-redirect" title="Rio Portugues">Rio Portugues</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rio_Bucana" class="mw-redirect" title="Rio Bucana">Rio Bucana</a>, off <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_2" title="Puerto Rico Highway 2">PR-2</a> (Ponce By-pass). The park was built at a cost of over $3 million.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_Familiar_Julio_Enrique_Monagas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The park was created under the administration of Ponce mayor <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Cordero_Santiago" title="Rafael Cordero Santiago">Rafael Cordero Santiago</a>. It initially consisted of 80 acres (320,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) of land and lakes. It was subsequently expanded to 125 acres (0.51&#160;km<sup>2</sup>). It opened in 1994 under the administration of mayor <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Cordero_Santiago" title="Rafael Cordero Santiago">Rafael Cordero Santiago</a><sup id="cite_ref-Parque_Julio_Enrique_Monagas._2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parque_Julio_Enrique_Monagas.-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and was considerably enlarged in October 2008 under the administration of mayor <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Zayas_Seijo" title="Francisco Zayas Seijo">Francisco Zayas Seijo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LGP_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LGP-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  It was built during the administration of Mayor <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Cordero_Santiago" title="Rafael Cordero Santiago">Rafael Cordero Santiago</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-periodicolaperla.com_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-periodicolaperla.com-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> At the moment of its inauguration in 1994, it was the largest passive park in the Caribbean.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The park was severely damaged by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a> in September 2017 and, as of November 2019, remained closed. In April 2019 the <a href="/wiki/USACE" class="mw-redirect" title="USACE">USACE</a> removed debris and, upon completion, the Government of the Municipality of Ponce proceeded to perform cleanup activities.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In late September 2019, mayor <a href="/wiki/Mayita" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayita">Mayita</a> announced the allocation of $3.9 USD million for the reconstruction of the park. The funds, however, were to be shared with reconstruction of <a href="/wiki/Teatro_La_Perla" title="Teatro La Perla">Teatro La Perla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Complejo_Recreativo_de_La_Guancha" class="mw-redirect" title="Complejo Recreativo de La Guancha">Complejo Recreativo de La Guancha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Centro_Ceremonial_Ind%C3%ADgena_de_Tibes" class="mw-redirect" title="Centro Ceremonial Ind\xc3\xadgena de Tibes">Centro Ceremonial Ind\xc3\xadgena de Tibes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Plaza_del_Mercado_Isabel_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Plaza del Mercado Isabel II">Plaza del Mercado Isabel II</a>, and <i>Complejo Acu\xc3\xa1tico V\xc3\xadctor Vasallo</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Amenities">Amenities</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_Familiar_Julio_Enrique_Monagas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Amenities"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The family-oriented park had paddle boats, picnic areas with gazebos, exotic birds, skate park and various playgrounds for children and a <a href="/wiki/Ridable_miniature_railway" title="Ridable miniature railway">Ridable miniature railway</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span
