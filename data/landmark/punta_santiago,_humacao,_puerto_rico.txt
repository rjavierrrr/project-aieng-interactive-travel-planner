Its population in 2020 was 3,716.<sup id="cite_ref-Pico_1969_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pico_1969-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Located in the estuary of Humacao, Punta Santiago is famous for its fishing heritage, its iconic pier and <a href="/wiki/Cayo_Santiago" title="Cayo Santiago">Cayo Santiago</a>, popularly known as Monkey Island (<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_Spanish" title="Puerto Rican Spanish">Spanish</a>: <i>Isla de de los Monos</i>) after its <a href="/wiki/Rhesus_macaque" title="Rhesus macaque">Rhesus macaque</a> population. The barrio is also the home of the <a href="/wiki/Humacao_Nature_Reserve" title="Humacao Nature Reserve">Humacao Nature Reserve</a> and the Punta Santiago Beach and Vacation Area.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Punta Santiago is one of the most vulnerable barrios to <a href="/wiki/Flood" title="Flood">flooding</a> and complete destruction whenever <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">hurricanes</a> enter Puerto Rico through the area of Humacao.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a 2x" data-file-width="750" data-file-height="611" /></a><figcaption>Fishing boats (1899)</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The fishing village of Punta Santiago was originally called <b>Playa de Humacao</b> as it was the closest beach to <a href="/wiki/Humacao_barrio-pueblo" title="Humacao barrio-pueblo">Humacao Pueblo</a>, the historic and administrative downtown area of Humacao.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The community of Punta Santiago used to be part of two former barrios of Humacao, one called Humacao Playa and the other called Mab\xc3\xba. By the end of the 19th century barrio Mab\xc3\xba had been eliminated and incorporated into barrio Playa, now renamed Punta Santiago after the geographical <a href="/wiki/Headland" title="Headland">headland</a> or <a href="/wiki/Peninsula" title="Peninsula">point</a> (<i>punta</i>) where the fishing village is located.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Since December 1938, <a href="/wiki/Cayo_Santiago" title="Cayo Santiago">Cayo Santiago</a>, located immediately off Punta Santiago, has been the home to a population of free roaming <a href="/wiki/Rhesus_macaque" title="Rhesus macaque">Rhesus macaques</a> imported from <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> by the <a href="/wiki/School_of_Tropical_Medicine_(Puerto_Rico)" title="School of Tropical Medicine (Puerto Rico)">School of Tropical Medicine</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University_Vagelos_College_of_Physicians_and_Surgeons" class="mw-redirect" title="Columbia University Vagelos College of Physicians and Surgeons">Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico" title="University of Puerto Rico">University of Puerto Rico</a>.\n</p><p>The area where the Humacao nature reserve is located today saw a number of bunkers and defensive infrastructure built during the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">Second World War</a>, including a lookout post at the top of <i>El Morrillo</i> hill.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This area which contains a number of <a href="/wiki/Mangrove" title="Mangrove">mangrove</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pterocarpus" title="Pterocarpus">Pterocarpus</a> forests was later proclaimed a wildlife refuge in 1986.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Hurricane_Maria">Hurricane Maria</h3><span 2017. The <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">hurricane eye</a> entered the island close to <a href="/wiki/Punta_Yeguas" title="Punta Yeguas">Punta Yeguas</a> in <a href="/wiki/Yabucoa,_Puerto_Rico" title="Yabucoa, Puerto Rico">Yabucoa</a>, not far from Punta Santiago.
