rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.diplo.org/">Official site</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Ram\xc3\xb3n Rivero</b> (May 29, 1909 \xe2\x80\x93 August 24, 1956) &#8212; known as <b>Diplo</b> &#8212; was a Puerto Rican <a href="/wiki/Comedian" title="Comedian">comedian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Composer" title="Composer">composer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FPR_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FPR-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Rivero made his television debut in Puerto Rico in "La Taberna India" (The India Tavern), the first comedy/variety show on Puerto Rican TV. He starred in the movies, Los Peloteros ("The Baseball Players"), and Una Gallega en La Habana ("A Gallega in Havana"). Rivero also organized the world\'s first known Walk-A-Thon, to raise money for the Liga Puertorrique\xc3\xb1a Contra el C\xc3\xa1ncer (the Puerto Rican League Against Cancer).<sup id="cite_ref-GoPR_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoPR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Early_years">Early years</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ram%C3%B3n_Rivero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Rivero (birth name: <b>Arturo Ram\xc3\xb3n M\xc3\xa1ximo Ortiz del Rivero</b><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>note 1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>) was born in the town of <a href="/wiki/Naguabo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Naguabo, Puerto Rico">Naguabo, Puerto Rico</a>, to Jos\xc3\xa9 Ortiz Alibr\xc3\xa1n and Providencia del Rivero. In 1917, his family moved to the <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">Old San Juan</a> section of San Juan, the capital of Puerto Rico. There he received his primary and secondary education. As a young man he learned to play various musical instruments, among them the piano, the guitar and the mandolin, under the tutelage of Amelia Maest\xc3\xba y Jorge Rubiano. His father, a lawyer, sent him to Ontario, Canada to study law, however while he was in Canada, he learned to play baseball and received an offer to try out for the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Giants" title="New York Giants">New York Giants</a> major league team. He did not accept the offer and when he returned to Puerto Rico he joined the "Senadores de San Juan" (San Juan Senators) baseball club. Rivero married and found it difficult to sustain his family economically as a professional baseball player, therefore he moved with his family to the town of <a href="/wiki/Cayey,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cayey, Puerto Rico">Cayey</a> and worked as a physical education instructor at a local school.<sup id="cite_ref-FPR_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FPR-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FD_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FD-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DRRD_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DRRD-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Career_as_an_entertainer">Career as an entertainer</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ram%C3%B3n_Rivero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career as an entertainer"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-redirect" title="YouTube video (identifier)">YouTube</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>In Cayey, he met and befriended Jose Luis Torregrosa, and joined Torregrosa\'s traveling theatrical company. Torregrosa became his best friend and later served as his comedy sidekick. Together they acted in the comedy "El proceso de Armando L\xc3\xados"; this experience convinced Rivero to pursue a career in comedy. After witnessing a comedy by Cuban comedian Leopoldo Fern\xc3\xa1ndez also known as "Tres Patines", Rivero adopted the idea of creating a comical "Afro-Puerto Rican" character.<sup id="cite_ref-FD_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FD-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> His inspiration for the character was someone he met during his youth years in San Juan called "Diplomacia", hence he named his character "Diplo".<sup id="cite_ref-FPR_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FPR-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Together with Torregrosa, Rivero founded "La Far\xc3\xa1ndula Bohemia", a theatrical company in which he was able to develop the character "Diplo" and make him known to the public. Rivero\'s father asked him to change his name because another son was a priest, and having a comedian in the family ran against propriety. Rivero respected his father\'s request and changed his name from <i>Arturo Ram\xc3\xb3n M\xc3\xa1ximo Ortiz del Rivero</i> to Ram\xc3\xb3n Rivero, and he added the nickname "Diplo". Among the local talents that Rivero and Torregrosa discovered was <a href="/wiki/Juan_Boria" title="Juan Boria">Juan Boria</a>, a Puerto Rican poet who would become known for his <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Afro-Caribbean" class="extiw" title="wikt:Afro-Caribbean">Afro-Caribbean</a> poetry. In 1941, Boria was participating in a show in the town of <a href="/wiki/Lo%C3%ADza" class="mw-redirect" title="Lo\xc3\xadza">Lo\xc3\xadza</a>. Rivero and Torregrosa, who were present, were so impressed with Boria\'s performance that they incorporated him into their <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/troupe" class="extiw" title="wikt:troupe">troupe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GoPR_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoPR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Rivero\'s comedy act became well known, not only on the traveling theatrical scene, but also in the local radio shows such as "Los embajadores del buen humor" (Ambassadors of Good Humor), "La vida en broma" (Life as a Joke) and "El Tremendo Hotel" (The tremendous hotel), where he also actively participated as a script writer. During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, Rivero and his acting troupe, the "La Far\xc3\xa1ndula Bohemia" (Bohemian Show-business), traveled to the United States military bases in which Puerto Rican troops were stationed and not only did he entertain the men, but he also accepted and delivered mail to the families of those whom he met overseas.<sup id="cite_ref-FD_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FD-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Puerto Rican industrialist <a href="/wiki/Angel_Ramos_(industrialist)" class="mw-redirect" title="Angel Ramos (industrialist)">Angel Ramos</a>, owner of WKAQ Radio, signed an exclusive deal with Rivero to broadcast "El Tremendo Hotel" via the airwaves. His show became the most popular radio show in Puerto Rico. "El Tremendo Hotel", together with his other production <i>La Far\xc3\xa1ndula Corona</i>, catapulted WKAQ to the top of the ratings.<sup id="cite_ref-Telemundo_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telemundo-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Among the many Puerto Rican artists who began their careers in "El Tremendo Hotel" were <a href="/wiki/Luis_Vigoreaux" title="Luis Vigoreaux">Luis Vigoreaux</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miguel_%C3%81ngel_%C3%81lvarez" title="Miguel \xc3\x81ngel \xc3\x81lvarez">Miguel \xc3\x81ngel \xc3\x81lvarez</a><sup id="cite_ref-PRPC_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PRPC-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Sylvia_Rexach" title="Sylvia Rexach">Sylvia Rexach</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> On one occasion, Rivero denounced and lambasted a foreign newspaperwoman who had published an article in the U.S. stating that "Puerto Rican men sold their women to the American servicemen", exhorting his countrymen to expel her from Puerto Rico (which they did), during a radio broadcast of <i>El Tremendo Hotel</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-FPR_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FPR-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FD_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FD-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GoPR_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoPR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DRRD_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DRRD-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Rivero produced and acted in various theatrical productions in Puerto Rico among them <i>Mosquilandia</i> (Mosquito Land), <i>El Pr\xc3\xadncipe Wele-Wele</i> (Prince Wele-Wele), <i>A Mi Me Matan Pero Yo Gozo</i> (They can kill me, but I\'ll enjoy it), <i>Ese Ni\xc3\xb1o Es Mio</i> (The child is mine), <i>Hay Que Defenderse</i> (One must defend himself), <i>La Familia del L\xc3\xado</i> (The family of problems) and <i>El Tremendo Hotel</i>, however his success was not limited to the theater and radio.<sup id="cite_ref-FPR_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FPR-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GoPR_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoPR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1946, <i>El Mundo</i>, a local newspaper, began a comic strip about Rivero\'s character Diplo, making this the first time that a Puerto Rican from the local entertainment industry received such a distinction.<sup id="cite_ref-FPR_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FPR-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  Rivero was a pioneer of Puerto Rican television who produced the first comedy show <i>La Taberna India</i> (The India Saloon) for Angel Ramos\'s <a href="/wiki/Telemundo" title="Telemundo">Telemundo</a>, a local television station.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> He became known to his fans as the "Rey de la Far\xc3\xa1ndula" (King of Show-business) and "Se\xc3\xb1or Televisi\xc3\xb3n" (Mr. Television). In winter 1953, Rivero and his troupe traveled to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> to perform before the Puerto Rican community which had migrated and settled there during the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_migration_to_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican migration to New York">Great Puerto Rican migration of the 1940s</a>. For three weeks Rivero and his show "El Tremendo Hotel" held sold-out performances at the Teatro San Juan, Teatro Hispano and the <a href="/wiki/Teatro_Puerto_Rico" title="Teatro Puerto Rico">Teatro Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DRRD_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DRRD-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 possibly the best motion picture ever made in the island, <i>Los Peloteros</i> ("The Baseball Players") (with Puerto Rican actress <a href="/wiki/M%C3%ADriam_Col%C3%B3n" title="M\xc3\xadriam class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The movie, which is based on a real-life story, is about a man (Pepe) who, despite the fact that no one takes him seriously, is willing to coach a group of very poor children and help them raise funds for their baseball team.<sup id="cite_ref-imdb_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-imdb-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GoPR_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoPR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Rivero wrote more than 3000 scripts for radio, theater, television and motion pictures and was the author of <i>\xc2\xbfPor qu\xc3\xa9 se r\xc3\xade la gente?</i> (What Makes People Laugh), and <i>El Album de la Radio</i>, (1940\xe2\x80\x931948), a significant study and the first historical record of the radio industry in Puerto Rico. Rivero also composed more than ten songs, among them "\xc2\xbfPor qu\xc3\xa9 ser\xc3\xa1?" (Why is it?), "Ya me olvidaste" (You already forgot me), "Comienza el fin" (The beginning of the end), "As\xc3\xad es" (That\'s how it is), "Me gust\xc3\xb3" (I liked it), "Hola, don Pepito" (Hello, Mister Pepito) and "Donde quiera que t\xc3\xba vayas" (Wherever you go).<sup id="cite_ref-GoPR_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoPR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DRRD_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DRRD-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Activist">Activist</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ram%C3%B3n_Rivero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Activist"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389"><table class="infobox" style="clear: center; float:none;margin:auto"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="font-size:115%">External audio</th></tr><tr><td colspan="2" class="infobox-full-data" style="text-align: left"><span typeof="mw:File"><span><img alt="audio icon" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Nuvola_apps_arts.svg/16px-Nuvola_apps_arts.svg.png" decoding="async" width="16" height="16" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Nuvola_apps_arts.svg/24px-Nuvola_apps_arts.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Nuvola_apps_arts.svg/32px-Nuvola_apps_arts.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="128" data-file-height="128" /></span></span> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Juy3_KoV6Io"><span class="plainlinks">Newsreel of Rivero in the world\'s first "Walk-A-Thon"</span></a> on <a href="/wiki/YouTube_video_(identifier)" class="mw-redirect" title="YouTube video (identifier)">YouTube</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>In 1953, Rivero organized the world\'s first known <a href="/wiki/Walkathon" title="Walkathon">Walk-A-Thon</a>. He walked 80 miles from the capital city of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, crossing Puerto Rico up and down the treacherous mountain roads of <a href="/wiki/Cayey" class="mw-redirect" title="Cayey">Cayey</a>, known as "La Piqui\xc3\xb1a", to <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>, on the other side of the island, to raise money for the Liga Puertorrique\xc3\xb1a Contra el C\xc3\xa1ncer (the Puerto Rican League Against Cancer). In that walkathon, Rivero raised the equivalent of $85,000 in 4 days. The Walk-A-Thon turned into a historical event that became part of the collective consciousness of Puerto Ricans all over the world, and has been copied several times since.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Rivero also defended the victims of injustice during student revolts at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico" title="University of Puerto Rico">University of Puerto Rico</a>, led the first actors\' strike on the island, and was the first producer/personality to actively combat racial prejudice on Puerto Rican television, incorporating blacks in his television shows; first in "La Taberna India" (Juan Boria), followed by Rita Delgado and Dixon as cast members of his "La Far\xc3\xa1ndula Corona".<sup id="cite_ref-FD_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FD-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GoPR_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoPR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Legacy">Legacy</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ram%C3%B3n_Rivero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Legacy"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1955, Rivero\'s doctors recommended that he lessen his work activities for the good of his health. <a href="/wiki/Cervecer%C3%ADa_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Cervecer\xc3\xada India">Cervecer\xc3\xada India</a>, Inc., Puerto Rico\'s largest beer brewery and the sponsor of "La Taberna India", did not agree with his doctors request and required that Rivero continue producing 3 weekly television shows for them, resulting in Rivero\'s refusal and resignation. He then approached Cervecer\xc3\xada Corona (Cervecer\xc3\xada India\'s competitor) with the idea of producing a weekly television comedy show. Cervecer\xc3\xada Corona agreed to sponsor the show which he named "La Far\xc3\xa1ndula Corona".  Rivero\'s new show became very popular among the Puerto Rican audiences.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Rivero was in the process of organizing his performing troupe, which was to travel to the town of <a href="/wiki/Yabucoa" class="mw-redirect" title="Yabucoa">Yabucoa</a> in an attempt to help the town after it was devastated by the <a href="/wiki/1956_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1956 Atlantic hurricane season">Santa Clara hurricane</a>, when on August 24, 1956, he suddenly died of a congenital aneurysm. Rivero was the father of five children, three from his first two marriages and two which he had in his third marriage with Puerto Rican actress Alicia Bibiloni.<sup id="cite_ref-FPR_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FPR-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Rivero was buried at Cementerio Santa Mar\xc3\xada Magdalena de Pazzi, <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>, and a state of national mourning for two weeks was declared on the island.<sup id="cite_ref-FD_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FD-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was estimated by "El Imparcial", a local newspaper, that a total of 50,000 of his countrymen attended his burial.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Due to his untimely sudden death, Rivero was unable to participate in an anticipated film with Hollywood actress <a href="/wiki/Rita_Hayworth" title="Rita Hayworth">Rita Hayworth</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GoPR_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoPR-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The memory of Rivero has been honored by both by the Government of Puerto Rico, who declared a national Ramon Rivero "Diplo" day in 2009, and by Naguabo his hometown who named an urbanization after him. In 1965, a 15-foot bronze statue/monument to Ramon Rivero "Diplo" was unveiled in the town square. Puerto Rican poet <a href="/wiki/Juan_Antonio_Corretjer" title="Juan Antonio Corretjer">Juan Antonio Corretjer</a> wrote "Morir y Re\xc3\xadr (A Ram\xc3\xb3n Rivero)" (Laugh and Die (To Ram\xc3\xb3n Rivero)) a poem dedicated to Rivero and his character "Diplo". On June 16, 2006, the Government of Puerto Rico approved "Ley Num. 115" (Law Number 115) which declares every May 29 the "Day of Ram\xc3\xb3n Ortiz del Rivero (Diplo)"<sup id="cite_ref-DRRD_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DRRD-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2009, a "Diplo Returns to San Juan" walkathon, in memory of the first event, raised almost $200,000.
