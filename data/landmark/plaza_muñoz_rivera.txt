year, 24 hours/day</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Plaza Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera</b>, formally <b>Plaza Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera</b>, is the smaller of the two <a href="/wiki/Plazas" class="mw-redirect" title="Plazas">plazas</a> at <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Delicias" title="Plaza Las Delicias">Plaza Las Delicias</a> in the city of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. It is located on the north side of Plaza Las Delicias, north of the larger southern <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Degetau" title="Plaza Degetau">Plaza Degetau</a>. The square is notable for its <a href="/wiki/Fountain" title="Fountain">fountains</a> and for the <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Rivera_(Ponce_statue)" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera (Ponce statue)">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera statue</a>. The historic <a href="/wiki/Parque_de_Bombas" title="Parque de Bombas">Parque de Bombas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Cathedral" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce Cathedral">Ponce Cathedral</a> buildings are located immediately to the south of Plaza Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera. The square is at the center of the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce Historic Zone</a>, and is flanked by the <a href="/wiki/Armstrong-Poventud_Residence" class="mw-redirect" title="Armstrong-Poventud Residence">Armstrong-Poventud Residence</a> to the west, the <a href="/wiki/Teatro_Fox_Delicias" title="Teatro Fox Delicias">Teatro Fox Delicias</a> to the north, and <a href="/wiki/Catholic_University_of_Puerto_Rico_School_of_Architecture" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Architecture">Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Architecture</a> to the east. The square dates back to the early Spanish settlement in Ponce of 1670. It is the main tourist attraction of the city, receiving about a quarter of a million visitors per year.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span Large Fountains</figcaption></figure>\n<p>According to the traditional Spanish colonial custom, a town\'s main square, or plaza, was the center of the town. In the case of Ponce, a <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism">Catholic church</a> was built on the center of the plaza, thus splitting the plaza into two sections: Plaza Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera to the north (the subject of this article) and Plaza Degetau to the south.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Plaza Las Delicias square is, thus, actually made up of two plazas. The north section of the square is named <i>Plaza Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera</i> (Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera square), while the south section is called <i><a href="/wiki/Plaza_Degetau" title="Plaza Degetau">Plaza Federico Degetau</a></i> (Federico Degetau square). Plaza Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera measures 2,400 square meters.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The history of the plaza dates back to as far as the creation of the first Catholic chapel in Ponce in 1670.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is also known that around 1840 Mayor <a href="/wiki/Salvador_de_Vives" title="Salvador de Vives">Salvador de Vives</a> planted trees as a renovation project for the plaza.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was first lit in 1864.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> An 1874 map of downtown Ponce shows that this northern plaza (see map/diagram "Diagram of Plaza Las Delicias") was already called "Las Delicias" while the southern plaza (today\'s <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Degetau" title="Plaza Degetau">Plaza Degetau</a>) continued to be called "Plaza Mayor."<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> When referring to either plaza it is common to just say "Plaza Las Delicias". For example, it may be said that the <a href="/wiki/Feria_de_Artesan%C3%ADas_de_Ponce" title="Feria de Artesan\xc3\xadas de Ponce">Feria de Artesan\xc3\xadas de Ponce</a> is to take place at Plaza Las Delicias, when in effect it takes place at Plaza Degetau;<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> or to say that <a href="/wiki/Fiestas_patronales_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Fiestas patronales in Puerto Rico">Fiestas Patronales</a> are taking place at Plaza Las Delicias when in effect they are centered mostly at Plaza Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Name">Name</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Mu%C3%B1oz_Rivera&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Name"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Plaza Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera is situated to the north of the Ponce Cathedral and was originally known as <i>Paseo de La Alameda</i> or, simply, Alameda,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> but today it is named after the famed Puerto Rican statesman Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera. It is so named to honor the prominent poet, journalist, and politician by that name born in <a href="/wiki/Barranquitas" class="mw-redirect" title="Barranquitas">Barranquitas</a> and whose statue stands prominently in the center of this plaza. The statue is the product of the foundry of Italian sculptor Luiggi Tomassi<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> in Pietrasanta, Italy.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera <a href="/wiki/Statue" title="Statue">statue</a> is made in <a href="/wiki/Bronze" title="Bronze">bronze</a> and was unveiled in 1923. <a href="/wiki/Luis_Yord%C3%A1n_D%C3%A1vila" title="Luis Yord\xc3\xa1n D\xc3\xa1vila">Luis Yord\xc3\xa1n D\xc3\xa1vila</a>, mayor of Ponce at the time, was one of the main proponents of the monument.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In addition to the statue of Munoz Rivera, this section of Plaza Las Delicias also has two fountains.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Mu%C3%B1oz_Rivera&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Plaza Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera is bounded on the north by Plaza Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera street (also called Reina street and Isabel street), on the west by Union street, on the east by <a href="/wiki/Paseo_Atocha" title="Paseo Atocha">Atocha street</a>, and on the south by the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Cathedral" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce Cathedral">Ponce Cathedral</a> and <a href="/wiki/Parque_de_Bombas" title="Parque de Bombas">Parque de Bombas</a>. Two hotels, various banks, several boutiques, and the old 3-story Forenza building (now home to the <a href="/wiki/Pontifical_Catholic_University_of_Puerto_Rico_School_of_Architecture" title="Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Architecture">Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Architecture</a>) surround this plaza. It is surrounded by hotels, banks, the <a href="/wiki/Casa_Armstrong-Poventud" class="mw-redirect" title="Casa Armstrong-Poventud">Casa Armstrong-Poventud</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Teatro_Fox_Delicias" title="Teatro Fox Delicias">Teatro Fox Delicias</a>, and various boutiques and cafes.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Setting">Setting</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Mu%C3%B1oz_Rivera&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Setting"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The plaza has wide mosaic-tile sidewalks, well-manicured flower gardens, well-trimmed bushes and <a href="/wiki/Ficus_microcarpa" title="Ficus microcarpa">Indian laurel</a> trees, late 1800s lampposts, and numerous marble benches.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The fountain is made of marble and bronze. \n</p><p>It is surrounded by a multitude of other historic sites such as <a href="/wiki/Paseo_Atocha" title="Paseo Atocha">Paseo Atocha</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Casa_Saur%C3%AD" class="mw-redirect" title="Casa Saur\xc3\xad">Casa Saur\xc3\xad</a> (now Ponce Ramada Hotel), among others. During the day, the plaza hustles with schoolchildren, shoppers, and tourists. After the sun sets, there are often live bands giving concerts to "multigenerational families."<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Gallery">Gallery</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span
