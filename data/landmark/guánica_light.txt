class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></td></tr><tr style="display:none"><td colspan="2">\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Gu\xc3\xa1nica Light</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Faro de Gu\xc3\xa1nica</i>) was a historic lighthouse located in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Gu%C3%A1nica,_Puerto_Rico" title="Gu\xc3\xa1nica, Puerto Rico">Gu\xc3\xa1nica, Puerto Rico</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Gu%C3%A1nica_State_Forest" title="Gu\xc3\xa1nica State Forest">Gu\xc3\xa1nica State Forest</a>. It was first lit in 1893 and deactivated in 1950.<sup id="cite_ref-National_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-National-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The light marked the entrance to Gu\xc3\xa1nica Bay and bridged the gap between <a href="/wiki/Los_Morrillos_Light" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Morrillos Light">Los Morrillos Light</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caja_de_Muertos_Light" title="Caja de Muertos Light">Caja de Muertos Light</a>. \n</p><p>On July 25, 1898, lighthouse keeper Robustiano Rivera spotted the convoy of the American forces that initiated the landing offensive of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_Campaign" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican Campaign">Puerto Rican Campaign</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a>. He immediately gave the alert to the residents of Gu\xc3\xa1nica and marched to <a href="/wiki/Yauco" class="mw-redirect" title="Yauco">Yauco</a> where he broke the news of the invading forces to the city\'s mayor.<sup id="cite_ref-EDG_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EDG-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Atlas_Obscura_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Atlas_Obscura-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>By 2009, the lighthouse was practically in ruins, though some parts of its unique architectural elements were still visible.<sup id="cite_ref-Friends_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Friends-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was listed in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._National_Register_of_Historic_Places" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. National Register of Historic Places">U.S. National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1981,<sup id="cite_ref-National_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-National-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and on the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Register_of_Historic_Sites_and_Zones" title="Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones">Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones</a> in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Puerto_Rico_earthquakes" title="2019\xe2\x80\x9320 Puerto Rico earthquakes">series of earthquakes</a> that occurred in January 2020 in Puerto Rico further damaged the Gu\xc3\xa1nica lighthouse.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span
