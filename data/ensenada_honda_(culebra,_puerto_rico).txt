Honda</b> (English: <span lang="en">Deep Cove</span>), also known as "Puerto Grande" (English: <span lang="en">Big Port</span>) and "Bah\xc3\xada Honda" for its elongated shape, is the largest inlet on the island of <a href="/wiki/Culebra,_Puerto_Rico" title="Culebra, Puerto Rico">Culebra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ensenada_Honda_(Culebra,_Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Since the 19th century, observers have deemed Ensenada Honda among the safest Caribbean harbors.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>a<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hugo_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hugo-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-posesiones_Espa\xc3\xb1olas_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-posesiones_Espa\xc3\xb1olas-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Its irregular coastline features rocky cliffs, sandy beaches, and mangrove forests. Three smaller inlets gate the cavernous cove: "Malena" and "Dakity" to the west, and "Mosquito" to the east. A number of distinct <a href="/wiki/Corals" class="mw-redirect" title="Corals">coral</a> specimens inhabit the area around Culebra, and some communities have flourished at the very bay\'s entrance.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> They form bars barely above sea level and block portions of its opening.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Environmental_history">Environmental history</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ensenada_Honda_(Culebra,_Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Environmental history"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>For most of the 20th century (1902-1975) the bay served as grounds for military training, as wreckage left from the time reveals. Explosions and the resulting contamination left pulverized underwater craters and diminished the health of sea life. But low-tech, community-based programs and coral grafting have expanded the reefs\' fields and increased their bio-diversity.<sup id="cite_ref-Watershed_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Watershed-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>   The bay is today an eco-tourist destination, celebrated for its turtles, and the center of the local fishing industry.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tnone" style="width:auto;max-width:908px"><div class="thumbinner"><div class="noresize" style="overflow:auto"><span typeof="mw:File"><a href="/wiki/File:View_from_Mt._Resaca,_Culebra_Island,_P.R_LCCN2007663310.tif" class="mw-file-description" title="View of Ensenada Honda from Mt.
