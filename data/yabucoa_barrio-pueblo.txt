/></a><figcaption>Central plaza (square) of Yabucoa in Yabucoa barrio-pueblo</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The central plaza, or square, is a place for official and unofficial recreational events and a place where people can gather and socialize from dusk to dawn. The <a href="/wiki/Laws_of_the_Indies" title="Laws of the Indies">Laws of the Indies</a>, Spanish law, which regulated life in Puerto Rico in the early 19th century, stated the plaza\'s purpose was for "the parties" (celebrations, festivities) (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">a prop\xc3\xb3sito para las fiestas</i>), and that the square should be proportionally large enough for the number of neighbors (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">grandeza proporcionada al\nn\xc3\xbamero de vecinos</i>). These Spanish regulations also stated that the streets nearby should be comfortable portals for passersby, protecting them from the elements: sun and rain.<sup id="cite_ref-Santullano_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Santullano-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Located across the central plaza in Yabucoa barrio-pueblo is the <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Parroquia Santos \xc3\x80ngeles Custodios</i></span>, a Roman Catholic church. A wooden church was built across the plaza around 1790, which after many repairs was in disuse by 1880. In 1893, a new church was built and used even after it was damaged by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Betsy_(1956)" title="Hurricane Betsy (1956)">Hurricane Santa Clara</a> in 1956.  In 1963, the damaged church was demolished and rebuilt in 1968. The current church is based on a design by architects Amaral and Morales.<sup id="cite_ref-Mari_Mut_2013_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mari_Mut_2013-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yabucoa_barrio-pueblo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Yabucoa barrio-pueblo was in Spain\'s <a href="/wiki/Gazetteer" title="Gazetteer">gazetteers</a><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> until Puerto Rico was ceded by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_of_1898" class="mw-redirect" title="Treaty of Paris of 1898">Treaty of Paris of 1898</a> and became an <a href="/wiki/Insular_area" title="Insular area">unincorporated territory of the United States</a>.
