The most notorious feature of the museum is the building it occupies. The building is a large four-story structure built in the 1930s for the owner of Ponce\'s <a href="/wiki/Destileria_Serralles" class="mw-redirect" title="Destileria Serralles">Destileria Serralles</a>, one of Puerto Rico\'s largest rum distilleries. The distillery was once also home to Puerto Rico\'s largest sugar-factory called <a href="/wiki/Hacienda_Mercedita" title="Hacienda Mercedita">Central Mercedita</a>, producers of the <i>Snow White</i> sugar brand.   The building, known as <a href="/wiki/Castillo_Serrall%C3%A9s" title="Castillo Serrall\xc3\xa9s">Castillo Serrall\xc3\xa9s</a>, was designed by <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Adolfo_de_Castro" title="Pedro Adolfo de Castro">Pedro Adolfo de Castro</a> in 1930 in the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Colonial_Revival" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish Colonial Revival">Spanish Colonial Revival</a> style.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The museum sits on a 2.5-acre lot and opened on 20 February 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Historia_y_Pasi\xc3\xb3n_2009_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Historia_y_Pasi\xc3\xb3n_2009-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  Visitors can tour the refurbished home, which has been furnished to appear as it did in the 1930s.  There is also a temporary exhibit area for local artists. The Serrall\xc3\xa9s Castle is now part of a large complex that includes the <a href="/wiki/Cruceta_El_Vig%C3%ADa" class="mw-redirect" title="Cruceta El Vig\xc3\xada">Cruceta El Vig\xc3\xada</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Garden" class="mw-redirect" title="Japanese Garden">Japanese Garden</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Butterfly_house" title="Butterfly house">butterfly garden</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Butterfly_house" title="Butterfly house">Mariposario</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-Historia_y_Pasi\xc3\xb3n_2009_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Historia_y_Pasi\xc3\xb3n_2009-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> By September 2010, the museum was receiving around 100,000 visitors per year.<sup id="cite_ref-Castillo_Serrall\xc3\xa9s_2010._Page_11_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Castillo_Serrall\xc3\xa9s_2010._Page_11-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_Castillo_Serrall%C3%A9s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The museum sits on a hilltop overlooking the downtown area (<a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce Pueblo</a>). Built during the 1930s by order of Juan Eugenio Serrall\xc3\xa9s, son of businessman <a href="/wiki/Juan_Serrall%C3%A9s" class="mw-redirect" title="Juan Serrall\xc3\xa9s">Juan Serrall\xc3\xa9s</a>, founder of <a href="/wiki/Destiler%C3%ADa_Serrall%C3%A9s" title="Destiler\xc3\xada Serrall\xc3\xa9s">Destiler\xc3\xada Serrall\xc3\xa9s</a>, the museum structure sits on a 2.5-acre (1.0&#160;ha) exceedingly manicured property.<sup id="cite_ref-Luis_Mu\xc3\xb1oz_Polanco_1980._Page_3_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Luis_Mu\xc3\xb1oz_Polanco_1980._Page_3-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The museum is one of the most iconic structures in Puerto Rico and receives some 100,000 visitors a year.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_Castillo_Serrall%C3%A9s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1237879389"><table class="infobox" style="clear: left; float:left;margin:0 1.5em 1.5em 0;"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="font-size:115%">External audio</th></tr><tr><td colspan="2" class="infobox-full-data" style="text-align: left"><span typeof="mw:File"><span><img alt="audio icon" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Nuvola_apps_arts.svg/16px-Nuvola_apps_arts.svg.png" decoding="async" width="16" height="16" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Nuvola_apps_arts.svg/24px-Nuvola_apps_arts.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Nuvola_apps_arts.svg/32px-Nuvola_apps_arts.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="128" data-file-height="128" /></span></span> You may see a video about Museo Castillo Serrall\xc3\xa9s <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g23LaB-40hc"><b>HERE</b></a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The house was purchased by the city of Ponce from the Serrall\xc3\xa9s heirs. While the castle was valued at $17\xe2\x80\x93$25 million,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> it was purchased for a mere $400,000 and included much of the furniture in the sale.<sup id="cite_ref-periodicolaperla.com_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-periodicolaperla.com-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The city\'s initial intent was to turn it into a museum of Puerto Rican music.<sup id="cite_ref-periodicolaperla.com_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-periodicolaperla.com-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Museum_of_Puerto_Rican_Music" class="mw-redirect" title="Museum of Puerto Rican Music">Museum of Puerto Rican Music</a>, however, was eventually established elsewhere in the city.\n</p><p>The castle was built in 1926 by local <a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">architect</a> Pedro Adolfo de Castro y Besosa.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  \nIt includes two huge <a href="/wiki/Terrace_(building)" title="Terrace (building)">terraces</a>, an outside fountain, and a symmetrical backyard garden. Its interior includes a luxurious hall, a spacious dining room, and an interior <a href="/wiki/Courtyard" title="Courtyard">courtyard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Administration">Administration</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_Castillo_Serrall%C3%A9s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Administration"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The museum is administered and operated by a non-governmental, non-profit, civic organization called <i>Patronato de Ponce.</i> The organization received an <a href="/wiki/Financial_endowment" title="Financial endowment">endowment</a> from the Ponce Municipal Government of $600,000 a year during the 2008-2009 fiscal year, but due to the worldwide economic downturn the Municipality dropped its endowment to $300,000 per year in 2010.  It had also been receiving an undisclosed amount in yearly donations from the private sector, but $75,000 of that was also dropped due to the economy. The museum operates with a $1.2<a href="/wiki/Million" class="mw-redirect" title="Million">M</a> yearly budget.<sup id="cite_ref-Castillo_Serrall\xc3\xa9s_2010._Page_11_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Castillo_Serrall\xc3\xa9s_2010._Page_11-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Displays_and_tours">Displays and tours</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_Castillo_Serrall%C3%A9s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Displays and tours"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Access to the museum is through the side patio/balcony entrance on the southern end of the building.  The museum can only be seen via guided group <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tours</a>, and tour guides are very diligent in enforcing this rule as the administration does not want visitors wandering around the house on their own. The only on-your-own time comes at the gift shop.\n</p><p>The museum has four floors, and the displays on each floor focus on a particular aspect of the museum and the lives of its former owners.\n</p><p>The ground floor, which is the castle\'s former basement, houses all of the museum\'s rum and sugar-related expositions. On its northern end is the castle\'s former 3-car <a href="/wiki/Garage_(house)" class="mw-redirect" title="Garage (house)">garage</a> now turned into the museum\'s <a href="/wiki/Gift_shop" title="Gift shop">gift shop</a>. This level also houses the main entrance to the museum on its southern end. On the main, center area are the former service quarters which used to house mechanical, electrical, and water storage facilities. These have been remodeled to provide for the <a href="/wiki/Turnstile" title="Turnstile">turnstile</a> entry, a restaurant, and an <a href="/wiki/Auditorium" title="Auditorium">auditorium</a> capable of sitting some 50 visitors. There are also some <i>Patronato</i> offices on this floor.<sup id="cite_ref-Patronato_de_Ponce_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Patronato_de_Ponce-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The second floor contains the castle owner\'s former library, a central patio, a <a href="/wiki/Sunroom" title="Sunroom">solarium</a>, the living room, dining room, and the kitchen.  More photos adorn the wall on this level, shown the development of both rum and sugar businesses. This level contains the former formal access to the house via its front main entrance.<sup id="cite_ref-Luis_Mu\xc3\xb1oz_Polanco_1980._Page_2_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Luis_Mu\xc3\xb1oz_Polanco_1980._Page_2-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The third floor contains all the sleeping quarters. These are shown with furniture as well as all types of personal accessories of the former occupants. Throughout this level are wall-hung pictures of the rum-producing family clan. The pictures here are oriented towards the personal and family live of the clan.\n</p><p>The fourth floor is the terrace, from where the most impressive and commanding views of the city are possible. Intended as a relaxed entertainment space, the terrace has both covered and uncovered areas.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Gardens">Gardens</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Museo_Castillo_Serrall%C3%A9s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Gardens"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The city also rejuvenated the surrounding landscape.<sup id="cite_ref-Patronato_de_Ponce_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Patronato_de_Ponce-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The backyard now consists of lush green grass, fountains, manicured shrubs, and lights.  The first floor also houses an outdoor square that is covered by the roof over the second floor.  The city uses this area to showcase the work of local artists and photographers. The area is now used for private weddings, <i><a href="/wiki/Quincea%C3%B1era" title="Quincea\xc3\xb1era">quincea\xc3\xb1era</a></i> celebrations, and other similar social events.\n</p>\n<div
