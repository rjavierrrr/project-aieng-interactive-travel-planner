The neighborhood has many historic <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Colonial_Revival_architecture" title="Spanish Colonial Revival architecture">Spanish Revival</a>-style homes with patios and gardens, and it was listed on the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Register_of_Historic_Sites_and_Zones" title="Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones">Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones</a> in 2007 for its historical and architectural value.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Tranv%C3%ADa_de_San_Juan_02.jpg/500px-Tranv%C3%ADa_de_San_Juan_02.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3150" data-file-height="2292" /></a><figcaption><a <a href="/wiki/Santurce,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="Santurce, San Juan, Puerto Rico">Santurce</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-auto1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \nIt first appears in the 16th century as a spring that fed water to the Spanish outpost of <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Old San Juan, Puerto Rico">Old San Juan</a>. During the Battle of San Juan (17 April \xe2\x80\x93 2 May 1797), the town of Miramar was the site of the British ill-fated British assault on the Spanish colonial port city of San Juan de Puerto Rico during the 1796\xe2\x80\x931808 Anglo-Spanish War.\n</p><p>In the 17th century a wide public street, the Central Highway, (today <a href="/wiki/Avenida_Juan_Ponce_de_Le%C3%B3n" title="Avenida Juan Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n">Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n Avenue</a>) was built that ran through the spring. Commerce flourished since goods that arrived at the port of San Juan had to pass Miramar on their way to the rest of the island. Today two set of bridges known as San Antonio and Dos Hermanos connects the adjoining areas of <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Old San Juan, Puerto Rico">Old San Juan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Condado_(Santurce)" title="Condado (Santurce)">Condado</a>. Its land area measures 0.24 sq. miles (0.62&#160;km\xc2\xb2), and has a resident population of 5,440 according to the <a href="/wiki/2000_United_States_Census" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 United States Census">2000 United States Census</a>.\n</p><p>Miramar, since its beginnings had large wooden plantation-style homes with expansive land lots. In the 1950s well known architects from the time period built extravagant homes in Miramar that are still preserved.<sup id="cite_ref-auto1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Miramar is home to several structures registered at the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Residencia_Aboy-Lompr%C3%A9" title="Residencia Aboy-Lompr\xc3\xa9">Casa Aboy-Lompr\xc3\xa9</a>, a cultural center that was declared a historic building in the year 1989, focusing on the preservation and creation of photographic art.<sup id="cite_ref-5" data-file-height="1848" /></a><figcaption>Historic home in Miramar</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Miramar is an exclusive residential neighbourhood where primarily middle to upper-class families reside, characterized by quiet streets lined with large, mature shade trees. There are still a handful of historical residences with the charm and atmosphere from the bygone era including several <a href="/wiki/Prairie_School" title="Prairie School">Prairie School</a> architectural style buildings by internationally renowned architect <a href="/wiki/Antonin_Nechodoma" title="Antonin Nechodoma">Antonin Nechodoma</a>. Miramar\'s rich architectural legacy is now protected by law as it was designated an historical district of Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The government of Puerto Rico has passed a law declaring Miramar a Historic Town.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Miramar\'s Residents Association has had two legal triumphs in the court system that have helped define zoning laws and development sustainability.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The Justice Department of Puerto Rico (which can be seen in the picture, third condo from right to left, dark colored] is located in Miramar,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and had plans to expand their facilities. Expropriation processes were begun for adjacent lots. The Association took the matter to court and simultaneously began lobbying in the Puerto Rico Legislature. Their endeavors resulted in the passing of a law declaring the historic value of the area, and officially declaring Miramar a historic district. Thus, the courts ruled such zoning laws pertaining to historic districts prohibited the proposed expansion plans. To this day, the adjacent lots are empty, but the department has abandoned their proposed expansion.\n</p><p>The second victory for the Residents was in the halting of construction of a four-lane expressway that was begun on the south part of Miramar. The proposed plans were considered to be an abuse of the environment (hundred-year-old trees would have been cut down), the highway would not meet noise emission levels in regular traffic hours (as established by law for historic districts), and various condos and houses would become isolated from the rest of the town.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In an unexpected move, in 2010 <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Santini" title="Jorge Santini">Mayor Jorge Santini</a> proposed that instead of building the highway, a green concept should be pursued and a light rail program called SaTour was to be built.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The plan is part of the San Juan Walkable City proposed plan, which is intended to connect Miramar to the Train system already in place called <a href="/wiki/Tren_Urbano" title="Tren Urbano">Tren Urbano</a>. When completed, Miramar will have a direct access through train with <a href="/wiki/Hato_Rey,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Hato Rey, Puerto Rico">Hato Rey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guaynabo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guaynabo, Puerto Rico">Guaynabo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bayam%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico" title="Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico">Bayam\xc3\xb3n</a>.\n</p><p>Miramar is home to the coeducational Catholic parochial school <a href="/wiki/Academia_del_Perpetuo_Socorro" title="Academia del Perpetuo Socorro">Academia del Perpetuo Socorro</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Convention_Center" title="Puerto Rico Convention Center">Puerto Rico Convention Center</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Luis_Ribas_Dominicci_Airport" title="Fernando Luis Ribas Dominicci Airport">Isla Grande Airport</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Cruise_ship" title="Cruise ship">Cruise ship</a> Pan-American Cruise Pier, the luxurious yacht harbor of Club Nautico,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Conservatory_of_Music_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Conservatory of Music of Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico Conservatory of Music</a>.\n</p><p>Miramar is located two miles from <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Old San Juan, Puerto Rico">Old San Juan</a>, three miles from shopping mecca <a href="/wiki/Condado_(Santurce)" title="Condado (Santurce)">Condado</a>, and four miles from the international airport <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Mar%C3%ADn_International_Airport" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn International Airport">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span
