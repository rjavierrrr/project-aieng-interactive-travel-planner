title="Plaza">plaza</a> in the city of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.  The square is notable for its <a href="/wiki/Fountain" title="Fountain">fountains</a> and for the various monuments it contains. The historic <a href="/wiki/Parque_de_Bombas" title="Parque de Bombas">Parque de Bombas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Cathedral" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce Cathedral">Ponce Cathedral</a> buildings are located within the plaza, which actually consists of two squares: <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Mu%C3%B1oz_Rivera" title="Plaza Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera">Plaza Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera</a> on the north end, and <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Degetau" title="Plaza Degetau">Plaza Degetau</a> on the southern end. The square is the center of the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce Historic Zone</a>, and it is flanked by the historic <a href="/wiki/Ponce_City_Hall" title="Ponce City Hall">Ponce City Hall</a> to the south, the early 19th-century <a href="/wiki/Teatro_Fox_Delicias" title="Teatro Fox Delicias">Teatro Fox Delicias</a> to the north, the <a href="/wiki/NRHP" class="mw-redirect" title="NRHP">NRHP</a>-listed <a href="/wiki/Banco_Cr%C3%A9dito_y_Ahorro_Ponce%C3%B1o_(building)" title="Banco Cr\xc3\xa9dito y Ahorro Ponce\xc3\xb1o (building)">Banco Cr\xc3\xa9dito y Ahorro Ponce\xc3\xb1o</a> and <a href="/wiki/Banco_de_Ponce_(building)" title="Banco de Ponce (building)">Banco de Ponce</a> buildings to the east, and the <a href="/wiki/Armstrong-Poventud_Residence" class="mw-redirect" title="Armstrong-Poventud Residence">Armstrong-Poventud Residence</a> to the west. The square dates back to the early Spanish settlement in Ponce of 1670. In the case of Ponce, a Catholic church was built on the center of the plaza, thus splitting the plaza into two sections.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Plaza Las Delicias square is, thus, actually made up of two plazas.  The north section of the square is named <i>Plaza <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Rivera" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera</a></i> (<i>Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera square</i>), while the south section is called <i>Plaza <a href="/wiki/Federico_Degetau" title="Federico Degetau">Federico Degetau</a></i> (<i>Federico Degetau square</i>). Plaza Las Delicias measures 8,800 square meters.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The history of Plaza Las Delicias dates back to as far as the creation of the first Catholic chapel in Ponce in 1670.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  It is also known that around 1840 Mayor <a href="/wiki/Salvador_de_Vives" title="Salvador de Vives">Salvador de Vives</a> planted trees as a renovation project for Plaza Las Delicias.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was first lit in 1864.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In addition to the cathedral and the firehouse, Plaza Las Delicias at one point also contained an open dining <a href="/wiki/Moorish_architecture" title="Moorish architecture">Moorish</a>-style Arab kiosk that had been part of the 1882 Fair Exposition.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The kiosk was still present at the time of the American invasion of the island in 1898 as reported by American photo-journalist <a href="/wiki/William_Dinwiddie" title="William Dinwiddie">William Dinwiddie</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> but it was demolished in 1914.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a 2x" data-file-width="1536" data-file-height="2048" /></a><figcaption>The Statue of Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera is the centerpiece of Plaza Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera</figcaption></figure>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Plaza_Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Rivera" class="mw-redirect" title="Plaza Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera">Plaza Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera</a> is the smaller of the two plazas. It is situated to the north of the Ponce Cathedral, and was originally known as <i>Paseo de La Alameda</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and was at a time also called "Plaza Las Delicias" itself,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> but today it is known as Plaza Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera.  It is so named to honor the prominent poet, journalist, and politician by that name born in <a href="/wiki/Barranquitas" class="mw-redirect" title="Barranquitas">Barranquitas</a> and whose statue stands prominently in the center of this plaza. The statue is the product of the foundry of Italian sculptor Luiggi Tomassi.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> in Pietrasanta, Italy.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera <a href="/wiki/Statue" title="Statue">statue</a> is made in <a href="/wiki/Bronze" title="Bronze">bronze</a> and was unveiled in 1923. <a href="/wiki/Luis_Yord%C3%A1n_D%C3%A1vila" title="Luis Yord\xc3\xa1n D\xc3\xa1vila">Luis Yord\xc3\xa1n D\xc3\xa1vila</a>, mayor of Ponce at the time, was one of the main proponents of the monument.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In addition to the statue of Munoz Rivera, this section of Plaza Las Delicias also has two fountains.\n</p><p>Plaza Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera is bounded on the north by Plaza Mu\xc3\xb1oz Rivera street (also called Reina street and Isabel street), on the west by Union street, on the east by <a href="/wiki/Paseo_Atocha" title="Paseo Atocha">Atocha street</a>, and on the south by the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Cathedral" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce Cathedral">Ponce Cathedral</a> and <a href="/wiki/Parque_de_Bombas" title="Parque de Bombas">Parque de Bombas</a>. Two hotels, various banks, several boutiques, a five-story drugstore building (now home to the <a href="/wiki/Pontifical_Catholic_University_of_Puerto_Rico_School_of_Architecture" title="Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Architecture">Pontifical Catholic University of Puerto Rico School of Architecture</a>), and the historic <a href="/wiki/Teatro_Fox_Delicias" title="Teatro Fox Delicias">Fox Delicias theater</a> surround this smaller plaza.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Plaza_Federico_Degetau">Plaza Federico Degetau</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Las_Delicias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Plaza Federico Degetau"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Fountain_of_Lions_in_Ponce.JPG" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Fountain_of_Lions_in_Ponce.JPG/225px-Fountain_of_Lions_in_Ponce.JPG" decoding="async" width="225" height="169" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Fountain_of_Lions_in_Ponce.JPG/338px-Fountain_of_Lions_in_Ponce.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Fountain_of_Lions_in_Ponce.JPG/450px-Fountain_of_Lions_in_Ponce.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2304" data-file-height="1728" /></a><figcaption>The Lions Fountain on Plaza Degetau</figcaption></figure>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Plaza_Federico_Degetau" class="mw-redirect" title="Plaza Federico Degetau">Plaza Federico Degetau</a> is the larger of the two plazas. It was designed by architect <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Porrata-Doria" class="mw-redirect" title="Francisco Porrata-Doria">Francisco Porrata-Doria</a> in 1914.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It is located south of the cathedral and the firehouse. It was originally called Antigua Plaza Real (Old Royal Plaza).<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  This section of Plaza Las Delicias is perhaps the best known and the one most often seen in pictures.\n</p><p>In the center of this plaza lies the famous <i>Fuente de los Leones</i> (Lions Fountain). The large, round-shaped fountain is bounded by a low, marmol and granite wall. The fountain\'s wall boundary is shaped in the form of a regular octagon and built so that one of the vertices of the octagon points towards the historic Ponce City Hall.  The fountain also features four lion statues and water that flows under colored lighting effects.  The four lion statues are located one statue on each alternating vertex of the fountain\'s octagonal boundary wall.  The fountain was purchased in 1939 at the <a href="/wiki/1939_New_York_World%27s_Fair" title="1939 New York World&#39;s Fair">New York World\'s Fair</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The fountain, including a mechanical basement, was remodeled and restored in 1993. Its base was enlarged and a computerized lighting system was installed.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>This plaza also features a statue of native <a href="/wiki/Composer" title="Composer">composer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Morel_Campos" title="Juan Morel Campos">Juan Morel Campos</a>. This statue was also produced at the workshop of Italian sculptor Luiggi Tomassi<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Also in this plaza is an <a href="/wiki/Obelisk" title="Obelisk">obelisk</a> in honor of the <a href="/wiki/Firefighters" class="mw-redirect" title="Firefighters">firefighters</a> that fought in the "Polvor\xc3\xadn" fire (See <a href="/wiki/Monumento_a_los_h%C3%A9roes_de_El_Polvor%C3%ADn_(obelisk)" title="Monumento a los h\xc3\xa9roes de El Polvor\xc3\xadn (obelisk)">Monumento a los heroes de El Polvor\xc3\xadn</a>). The obelisk was unveiled in 1948, in time for the 50th anniversary of the frightful fire.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> There is also a statue, called <i>Blind Justice</i> of a woman on a long dress with her eyes covered by a cloth wrapped around the top of her head. The woman\'s left hand holds a sword inside a shaft resting on the ground and there are two children sitting happily by her feet: one is embracing the lower part of the shaft, the other is playing with an orange tree branch.\n</p><p>Plaza Degetau is bounded on the north by the Our Lady of Guadalupe Cathedral and Parque de Bombas, on the south by Plaza Degetau street (also called Villa street and Comercio street), on the west by Union street, and on the east by Atocha street. It is surrounded by two hotels, the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_City_Hall" title="Ponce City Hall">Ponce City Hall</a>, two banks (<a href="/wiki/Banco_de_Ponce_(building)" title="Banco de Ponce (building)">historic Banco de Ponce</a> and <a href="/wiki/Banco_Cr%C3%A9dito_y_Ahorro_Ponce%C3%B1o_(building)" title="Banco Cr\xc3\xa9dito y Ahorro Ponce\xc3\xb1o (building)">historic Banco Cr\xc3\xa9dito</a>), a long-standing ice cream parlor called "<a href="/wiki/King%27s_Cream" class="mw-redirect" title="King&#39;s Cream">King\'s Cream</a>", and various boutiques and cafes.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Setting">Setting</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Las_Delicias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Setting"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Plaza has wide mosaic-tile sidewalks, well-manicured flower gardens, well-trimmed bushes and <a href="/wiki/Ficus_microcarpa" title="Ficus microcarpa">Indian laurel</a> trees, late 1800s lampposts, and numerous marble benches. It is home to the Lions Fountain, "one of the most beautiful fountains in Puerto Rico."<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The fountain is made of marble and bronze.\n</p><p>It is surrounded by a multitude of historic sites: <a href="/wiki/Paseo_Atocha" title="Paseo Atocha">Paseo Atocha</a>, Old Fox Delicias Theater, and Callejon Amor (<i>Love Alley</i>), among others. During the day, the plaza hustles with schoolchildren, shoppers, and tourists.  After the sun sets, there are often live bands giving concerts to "multigenerational families."<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Venue">Venue</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plaza_Las_Delicias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Venue"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The square is the site of numerous cultural celebrations. Among these are <a href="/wiki/Carnaval_Ponce%C3%B1o" class="mw-redirect" title="Carnaval Ponce\xc3\xb1o">Carnaval Ponce\xc3\xb1o</a>, <a href="/wiki/Las_Ma%C3%B1anitas" title="Las Ma\xc3\xb1anitas">Las Ma\xc3\xb1anitas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fiestas_patronales_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Fiestas patronales in Puerto Rico">Fiestas Patronales</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Many musical events related to the <a href="/wiki/Las_Justas" title="Las Justas">Las Justas</a> sporting event also take place here.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span
