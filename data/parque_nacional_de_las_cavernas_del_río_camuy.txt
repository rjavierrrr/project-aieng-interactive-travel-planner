id="Clara_Cave_and_Empalme_Sinkhole_(Cueva_Clara_y_Sumidero_de_Empalme)"><span id="Clara_Cave_and_Empalme_Sinkhole_.28Cueva_Clara_y_Sumidero_de_Empalme.29"></span>Clara Cave and Empalme Sinkhole (<i>Cueva Clara y Sumidero de Empalme</i>)</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_Nacional_de_las_Cavernas_del_R%C3%ADo_Camuy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Clara Cave and Empalme Sinkhole (Cueva Clara y Sumidero de Empalme)"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>"Cueva Clara de Empalme" or Clara Cave is the main attraction at the Rio Camuy Cave Park. A single chamber of this cave system has been developed for the public, starting in 1986.  Regular cavern guided tour and audio tours are available at the site.  Since November 2010, night tours called ECO NIGHTS have been organized weekly under special reservations. The tours start with the screening a short movie in a 90-seat theater, explaining the Rio Camuy Cave System and site safety rules, followed by a train ride that takes visitors into the entrance of Clara Cave. The huge chamber is 700&#160;ft (212m) in length and 215&#160;ft (65m) high.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Once inside this chamber, visitors are able to see an entrance from the roof of "Sumidero de Empalme" that is 60m from the surface, as well as stalactites, stalagmites, unique formations and thousands of bats.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Spiral_Cave_and_Sinkhole_(Cueva_y_Sumidero_de_Espiral)"><span id="Spiral_Cave_and_Sinkhole_.28Cueva_y_Sumidero_de_Espiral.29"></span>Spiral Cave and Sinkhole (<i>Cueva y Sumidero de Espiral</i>)</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_Nacional_de_las_Cavernas_del_R%C3%ADo_Camuy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Spiral Cave and Sinkhole (Cueva y Sumidero de Espiral)"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Visitors must be in good physical conditions in order to walk into "Cueva Clara" and reach "Sumidero y Cueva Espiral" to see the mouth of this cave.  They must go down to it through a scenic 205-step boardwalk. The challenge is getting the way back to the tram. Once walking down the 205 steps into the sinkhole, the cave itself is off limits to reach.  Only experienced spelunkers can penetrate the cave and the underground river with special permission from the National Parks Company of Puerto Rico. This sinkhole is believed to have once been an enormous cavern that collapsed thousands of years ago. At the opening of the cave there is an impressive sight.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Tri-Towns_Sinkhole_(&quot;Sumidero_Tres_Pueblos&quot;)"><span id="Tri-Towns_Sinkhole_.28.22Sumidero_Tres_Pueblos.22.29"></span>Tri-Towns Sinkhole ("Sumidero Tres Pueblos")</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_Nacional_de_las_Cavernas_del_R%C3%ADo_Camuy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tri-Towns Sinkhole (&quot;Sumidero Tres Pueblos&quot;)"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>"Sumidero Tres Pueblos" is a giant sinkhole measuring 650&#160;ft (195m) wide.  The Camuy River can be seen 400&#160;ft (120m) below from three observation platforms that are located at different points alongside the opening of the impressive crater. The sinkhole is located where the borders of the towns of Camuy, Hatillo and Lares meet.  Each town has its own platform overlooking the sinkhole.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Statistics">Statistics</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parque_Nacional_de_las_Cavernas_del_R%C3%ADo_Camuy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Statistics"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Camuy River Cave Park is home to more than 13 species of bats, and hundreds of other insect, arachnid and frog species.\n</p><p>The R\xc3\xado Camuy is the world\'s third-largest subterranean river.\n</p><p>The park closes once reaches its 1,500 visitors daily capacity.\n</p><p>16 entrances,
