Rico</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Bolera Caribe</b> is a bowling center located in barrio <a href="/wiki/Coto_Laurel" title="Coto Laurel">Coto Laurel</a> in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>, where bowling competitions for the <a href="/wiki/2010_Central_American_and_Caribbean_Games" title="2010 Central American and Caribbean Games">2010 Central American and Caribbean Games</a> (CACG) were held.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The venue is located at the intersection of <a href="/wiki/PR-506" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-506">PR-506</a> and <a href="/wiki/PR-52" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-52">PR-52</a>.  The Center is owned by <i>Bolera Caribe, Inc.</i>, a private company.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The bowling center was inaugurated in November 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolera_Caribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Bolera_Caribe_in_Barrio_Coto_Laurel,_Ponce,_PR_(DSC00587).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Bolera_Caribe_in_Barrio_Coto_Laurel%2C_Ponce%2C_PR_%28DSC00587%29.jpg/240px-Bolera_Caribe_in_Barrio_Coto_Laurel%2C_Ponce%2C_PR_%28DSC00587%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="240" height="180" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Bolera_Caribe_in_Barrio_Coto_Laurel%2C_Ponce%2C_PR_%28DSC00587%29.jpg/360px-Bolera_Caribe_in_Barrio_Coto_Laurel%2C_Ponce%2C_PR_%28DSC00587%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Bolera_Caribe_in_Barrio_Coto_Laurel%2C_Ponce%2C_PR_%28DSC00587%29.jpg/480px-Bolera_Caribe_in_Barrio_Coto_Laurel%2C_Ponce%2C_PR_%28DSC00587%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="768" /></a><figcaption>Bolera Caribe in 2010</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The <i>Bolera de Ponce</i>, as Bolera Caribe was previously called, was inaugurated in Ponce in the mid 1950s in the Mariani sector of barrio <a href="/wiki/Primero_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Primero (Ponce)">Primero</a> of Ponce. As the 1990s progressed, the neighborhood where the <i>Bolera</i> was located showed signs of aging and began to <a href="/wiki/Urban_decay" title="Urban decay">decay</a>.  This was a problem for the company, as it was inconsistent with the image it wanted to portray and the type of <a href="/wiki/Clientele" class="mw-redirect" title="Clientele">clientele</a> it was seeking to continue attracting.\n</p><p>Due to overcrowding in the area, parking also became a problem for the <i>Bolera</i>.  Thus, as overflow, guests made use of the parking area of a <a href="/wiki/Shopping_plaza" class="mw-redirect" title="Shopping plaza">shopping plaza</a> across the street from the Bolera. However, this resulted in several <a href="/wiki/Burglary" title="Burglary">breakins</a> that gave the <i>Bolera</i> a bad reputation as an entertainment destination. To solve the problem, the company attempted to sell the building and move elsewhere, but no buyer could be found. The business continued to lose revenue for several years. And a result the bowling alley opted for closing down while seeking another location. It closed down in the late 1990s, leaving the old building vacant.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="New_building_and_location">New building and location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolera_Caribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: New building and location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 2007, the company found a suitable location for its new bowling center at the intersection of routes PR-506 and PR-52, which provided easy access to Ponce and towns nearby, thus providing it greater exposure as well. Given its new location the <i>Bolera</i> shed its Ponce-only roots and became more of a regional entertainment venue.  In light of this, its name was also changed from <i>Bolera de Ponce</i> to <i>Bolera Caribe</i>, thus reflecting its newly expanded business territory. Caribe means Caribbean in Spanish. This name change was appropriate because not only the city of Ponce but all of its neighboring towns are on the shores of the Caribbean.  The new bowling center was part of <a href="/wiki/List_of_mayors_of_Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="List of mayors of Ponce, Puerto Rico">Mayor</a> <a href="/wiki/Churumba" class="mw-redirect" title="Churumba">Churumba</a>\'s plan for providing an entertainment venue in that part of the municipality.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The venue is currently the only bowling center in the entire Caribbean coast of Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Building">Building</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolera_Caribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Building"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The new Bolera Caribe was designed by CMA Architects &amp; Engineers LLP, the same <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican people">Puerto Rican</a> architectural firm that built the Center for Molecular and Comparative Medicine at the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_School_of_Medicine" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce School of Medicine">Ponce School of Medicine</a>, the 16-story Torre Chard\xc3\xb3n office tower in San Juan\'s <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Am%C3%A9ricas_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Plaza Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas (Puerto Rico)">Plaza Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas</a>, and the underground <a href="/wiki/Tren_Urbano" title="Tren Urbano">Urban Train</a> R\xc3\xado Piedras University Station. The building consists of 2500 square meters of space.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Facilities">Facilities</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolera_Caribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Facilities"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The facility includes:<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>Twenty-four synthetic lanes with Brunswick GSX equipment</li>\n<li>Tournament space for up to 300 spectators</li>\n<li>Billiard room</li>\n<li>Game room</li>\n<li>Cafeteria</li>\n<li>Private conference room with capacity for 25</li>\n<li>A restaurant called Caf\xc3\xa9 Caribe Restaurant-Bar &amp; Grill</li></ul>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Economics">Economics</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Central_American_and_Caribbean_Games">Central American and Caribbean Games</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolera_Caribe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Central approved as "the official stage for the upcoming bowling competitions of the XXI Central American and Caribbean Games to be held in Mayag\xc3\xbcez, Puerto Rico in July 2010."<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Games were celebrated from July 17 to August 1, 2010, in the city of <a href="/wiki/Mayag%C3%BCez,_Puerto_Rico" title="Mayag\xc3\xbcez, Puerto Rico">Mayag\xc3\xbcez</a>, and the schedule of events showed when Games were played at the <i>Bolera</i> .<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The bowling center has a capacity of 300 Game  espectators.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The facilities were offered to the CACG Organizing Committee by the <a href="/wiki/List_of_mayors_of_Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="List of mayors of Ponce, Puerto Rico">Mayor of Ponce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mar%C3%ADa_Mel%C3%A9ndez" title="Mar\xc3\xada Mel\xc3\xa9ndez">Mar\xc3\xada Mel\xc3\xa9ndez</a>, in April 2010: <i>"The events of canoeing, rowing, and bowling will take place in the <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ciudad Se\xc3\xb1orial</a>, this last one at Bolera Caribe."</i><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span
