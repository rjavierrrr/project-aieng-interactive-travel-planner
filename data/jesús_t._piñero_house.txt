Pi\xc3\xb1ero House</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The <b>Jes\xc3\xbas T. Pi\xc3\xb1ero House and Museum</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish</a>: <i>Casa Museo Jes\xc3\xbas T. Pi\xc3\xb1ero</i>), often called the Pink House (<i>Casa Rosada</i>), is a historic house located along <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_3" title="Puerto Rico Highway 3">PR-3</a> just outside of <a href="/wiki/Can%C3%B3vanas_barrio-pueblo" title="Can\xc3\xb3vanas barrio-pueblo">Can\xc3\xb3vanas Pueblo</a> in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Can%C3%B3vanas,_Puerto_Rico" title="Can\xc3\xb3vanas, Puerto Rico">Can\xc3\xb3vanas, Puerto Rico</a>. \n</p><p>The house was built in 1931 for politician and then businessman <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_T._Pi%C3%B1ero" title="Jes\xc3\xbas T. Pi\xc3\xb1ero">Jes\xc3\xbas T. Pi\xc3\xb1ero</a>, the last United States-appointed and first Puerto Rican-born <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Governor of Puerto Rico">governor of Puerto Rico</a> in 1946. He and his wife raised their two children here, although the house was abandoned in 1952 following his sudden death.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The house remained abandoned for a long period and was left to ruin due to vandalism in the 1980s and damages during <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Georges" title="Hurricane Georges">Hurricane Georges</a> in 1998, until it was acquired by the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Puerto_Rican_Culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Institute of Puerto Rican Culture">Institute of Puerto Rican Culture</a> (<i>Instituto de Cultura Puertorrique\xc3\xb1a</i>) and some members of the Pi\xc3\xb1ero family who sponsored its renovation and preservation. The house today is a museum dedicated to the life of Jes\xc3\xbas T. Pi\xc3\xb1ero and to the everyday life of the time between the 1930s and 1950s.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The house was reopened to visitors in 2021 and guided tours are available.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span
