Its campus is home to the UPRH Astronomical Observatory and the college graduates more majors in chemistry, physics, and mathematics than any other higher education institution on the island. UPRH has been accredited by the <a href="/wiki/Middle_States_Commission_on_Higher_Education" title="Middle States Commission on Higher Education">Middle States Commission on Higher Education</a> (MSCHE) since 1965.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Humacao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1962, the University of Puerto Rico at Humacao was founded as the first UPR Regional College providing students from the eastern region of the country access to the University of Puerto Rico and facilitating the transfer to other UPR units with higher levels of academic offerings. The <a href="/wiki/Middle_States_Association_of_Colleges_and_Schools" title="Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools">Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools</a> gave the Regional College its first accreditation in 1965. From 1967 to 1972, the institution established around 8 associate degrees in Administrative Sciences, Health Sciences, and Arts. <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Gonz%C3%A1lez_Ramos" title="Pedro Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Ramos">Pedro Gonz\xc3\xa1lez Ramos</a> was the second Dean of the University of Puerto Rico at Humacao from 1960 to 1972.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:UPRH_Aerial_View.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img alt="The UPR Humacao Campus aerial view." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/UPRH_Aerial_View.jpg/220px-UPRH_Aerial_View.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/UPRH_Aerial_View.jpg/330px-UPRH_Aerial_View.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/UPRH_Aerial_View.jpg/440px-UPRH_Aerial_View.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1334" data-file-height="750" /></a><figcaption> The UPRH aerial view. </figcaption></figure>\n<p>In 1973, the Regional College was renamed as the Humacao University College <i>(also known as CUH)</i> approved by the Legislature of Puerto Rico, and started offering bachelor\'s degrees in Arts and Sciences. Between 1973 and 1987 over 18 bachelor\'s degrees were established and offered. The <a href="/wiki/Council_for_Higher_Education_Accreditation" title="Council for Higher Education Accreditation">Council for Higher Education Accreditation</a> gave the CUH its autonomy permitting the designation of the first rector (chancellor) and directive bodies.\n</p><p>The University of Puerto Rico Board of Trustees changed the institution\'s name in 1999 to the University of Puerto Rico at Humacao. Nowadays, the UPRH is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education and offers over 26 undergraduate programs, including 5 associate degrees.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2010 the campus went on strike as part of the  <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%932011_University_of_Puerto_Rico_strikes" title="2010\xe2\x80\x932011 University of Puerto Rico strikes">2010\xe2\x80\x932011 University of Puerto Rico strikes</a>. In 2017 in response to budget cuts to the university system by the <a href="/wiki/Financial_Oversight_and_Management_Board_for_Puerto_Rico" title="Financial Oversight and Management Board for Puerto Rico">Financial Oversight and Management Board for Puerto Rico</a> the campus students voted to join the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Puerto_Rico_strikes,_2017" title="University of Puerto Rico strikes, 2017">University of Puerto Rico strikes, 2017</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Academics">Academics</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Humacao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Academics"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The UPR Humacao campus offers more than 26 <a href="/wiki/Undergraduate_education" title="Undergraduate education">undergraduate</a> major programs, through 10 departments in <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">Natural Sciences</a>, <a href="/wiki/Public_administration" title="Public administration">Administrative Sciences</a>, <a href="/wiki/Health_Sciences" class="mw-redirect" title="Health Sciences">Health Sciences</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Social_Science" title="Bachelor of Social Science">Social Sciences</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Arts</a>. Based on a semester system, the UPRH academic calendar is composed of three academic <a href="/wiki/Academic_term" title="Academic term">semesters</a> each year. The academic year begins in the fall, running from August to December. The spring semester begins in January and ends in May. The summer semester is optional to the students that generally span around six weeks, from either late in May or early in June until July.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Demographics">Demographics</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Humacao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demographics"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>More than 4,000 students were enrolled at the UPR Humacao campus in the 2016\xe2\x80\x9317 academic year, including approximately 3,628 undergraduate students.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Fall 2016 Freshman class of approximately 990 students earned admission to the university campus with an average <a href="/wiki/SAT" title="SAT">SAT</a> score of 1458 \xe2\x80\x93 1809 (out of 2400). The Library is located in a three-story building that has 88,000 square feet. It has six (6) reading rooms, more than 56 computers, two (2) microfiche readers, eleven (11) individual study rooms, and 148 cubicles. The library can accommodate 589 users. The integration of the IT infrastructure with the bibliographic infrastructure facilitates the creation of skill development information programs that supplements those of the classrooms and the training of users in the handling of electronic resources applied to the academic work. The library personnel and professional librarians have managed to turn the Institution into the home of very important biblio-technological projects, such as CONUCO (Puerto Rican journals database) and EMEUPR (authorities\xe2\x80\x99 Spanish database of the UPR).\n</p><p>The UPRH\'s Library uses the University of Puerto Rico automated system known as Horizon and is connected to the Internet through various means. The MARC U-STAR program provides intensive research training, critical thinking development, communication skills development, interpersonal skills development, and preparation for entrance to <a href="/wiki/Graduate_school" class="mw-redirect" title="Graduate school">graduate school</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Student_life">Student life</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Humacao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Student life"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:UPRH_main_entrance.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img alt="The main entrance of the University of Puerto Rico, Humacao Campus." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/UPRH_main_entrance.jpg/220px-UPRH_main_entrance.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="139" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/UPRH_main_entrance.jpg/330px-UPRH_main_entrance.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/UPRH_main_entrance.jpg/440px-UPRH_main_entrance.jpg 2x" data-file-width="700" data-file-height="441" /></a><figcaption>The main entrance of the University of Puerto Rico, Humacao Campus.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The UPR Humacao campus provides multiple services and necessary resources for students to be successful both in the classroom and in their personal lives. Under the Division of Student Affairs, UPRH students have access to involvement opportunities, dining facilities, recreational outlets, health and wellness services, fitness center, and more.\n</p><p>Also, the campus provides:\n</p>\n<ul><li>internet access</li>\n<li>computers in libraries, student center and labs</li>\n<li>email account provided by school</li>\n<li>career counseling, interest Inventory, and resume assistance</li>\n<li>birth control, personal, academic, and psychological counseling</li>\n<li>library on campus, and non-remedial tutoring</li>\n<li>hospital on campus</li></ul>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="UPRH_Astronomical_Observatory">UPRH Astronomical Observatory</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Humacao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: UPRH Astronomical Observatory"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:UPRH_Observatory.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img alt="The Astronomical Observatory of the University of Puerto Rico at Humacao." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/UPRH_Observatory.jpg/220px-UPRH_Observatory.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="141" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/UPRH_Observatory.jpg/330px-UPRH_Observatory.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/UPRH_Observatory.jpg/440px-UPRH_Observatory.jpg 2x" data-file-width="960" data-file-height="614" /></a><figcaption>The Astronomical Observatory of the UPR Humacao.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The Astronomical Observatory is in the Physics (Science) building on the campus. Donated by <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> to the UPR in the late 60\'s, the UPR Humacao Astronomical Observatory was founded in 1985 being a major island-wide resource in <a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">astronomy</a>. The observatory also serves the <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_Society" class="mw-redirect" title="Astronomical Society">Astronomical Society of Puerto Rico</a>, school teachers, science fair students, radio and television stations. The sun and moon <a href="/wiki/Eclipse" title="Eclipse">Eclipses</a> are broadcast live from the observatory, making it one of the best known facilities in the university and the best attraction of the campus.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Also, it is located on top of the fourth floor of the Science Building; and has a dark/storage room, computers room, and visitors room underneath. The UPR Humacao Astronomical Observatory is open to the entire University community and general public in the island free of any charges on most Thursday nights and the talks can be in Spanish, English or both (depending on the audience), always welcoming questions during the presentation.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Cultural_activities">Cultural activities</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Humacao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Cultural activities"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>UPRH stimulates literary, musical and theatrical creation in the university community, through various events to present the art-works, such as contests, festivals and recitals. Also, provides the opportunity to the talent of the university community through the creation of projects, exhibitions and presentations that serve as artistic experiences that reinforce their learning process in our Institution.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Student_associations">Student associations</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Puerto_Rico_at_Humacao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Student associations"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>There are a wide variety of registered student organizations at UPRH, including academic, professional, special interest, and multicultural groups.
