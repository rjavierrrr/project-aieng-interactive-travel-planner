It is located in the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_area" title="Metropolitan area">metropolitan area</a> of the capital, <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fort_Buchanan_(Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Puerto Rico was part of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a> from the time of <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> until the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a>. United States forces landed in <a href="/wiki/Gu%C3%A1nica,_Puerto_Rico" title="Gu\xc3\xa1nica, Puerto Rico">Gu\xc3\xa1nica</a> on July 25, 1898. The last Spanish unit left the island the following October, and the Department of Puerto Rico was established. Upon the signing of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1898)" title="Treaty of Paris (1898)">Treaty of Paris</a> on December 10, 1898, Spain ceded Puerto Rico to the Congress of the United States. In 1901 the island was designated to be an unincorporated United States territory by the Supreme Court of the United States in a controversial decision in the case of Downes Vs. Bidwell, the first of several Insular Cases.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="The_Puerto_Rico_Regiment">The Puerto Rico Regiment</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fort_Buchanan_(Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Puerto Rico Regiment"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>On July 1, 1899, \xe2\x80\x9cThe Puerto Rico Regiment of Infantry, United States Army\xe2\x80\x9d was created. On July 1, 1901, Lieutenant Colonel James Anderson Buchanan took command of the regiment. He was later promoted to colonel on July 21, 1902, and to brigadier general in 1905. Buchanan served in Puerto Rico from 1898 to 1903 and Fort Buchanan was named after him.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Camp_Buchanan">Camp Buchanan</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fort_Buchanan_(Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Camp Buchanan"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Puerto Rico Regiment was officially designated as the 65th Infantry Regiment in 1920, after defending the <a href="/wiki/Panama_Canal_Zone" title="Panama Canal Zone">Canal Zone</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. A tract of 300 acres approximately six miles on the south shore of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Bay" title="San Juan Bay">San Juan Bay</a> was acquired to provide the regiment with a training area. The site was established in 1923 as Camp Buchanan. It served as a target range and maneuver area for the U.S. Army and National Guard troops from 1923 to 1939. The 1st Battalion <a href="/wiki/51st_Coast_Artillery_Regiment_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="51st Coast Artillery Regiment (United States)">51st Coast Artillery Regiment</a> was also assigned to Camp Buchanan in 1939.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Fort_Buchanan">Fort Buchanan</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fort_Buchanan_(Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Fort Buchanan"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Puerto_Rico,_Camp_Buchanan_(8365155934).jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Puerto_Rico%2C_Camp_Buchanan_%288365155934%29.jpg/220px-Puerto_Rico%2C_Camp_Buchanan_%288365155934%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="170" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Puerto_Rico%2C_Camp_Buchanan_%288365155934%29.jpg/330px-Puerto_Rico%2C_Camp_Buchanan_%288365155934%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Puerto_Rico%2C_Camp_Buchanan_%288365155934%29.jpg/440px-Puerto_Rico%2C_Camp_Buchanan_%288365155934%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="771" /></a><figcaption>Camp Buchanan in 1939, photograph by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Yarnall_Richie" title="Robert Yarnall Richie">Robert Yarnall Richie</a></figcaption></figure>\n<p>In May 1940 the location was renamed Fort Buchanan. It was eventually expanded to 4,500 acres. During World War II, Fort Buchanan housed a depot supplying the Army Antilles Department. It also processed local troops through its replacement center. The industrial complex included pier facilities, ammunition storage areas and an extensive railroad network connecting the military installation to the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_San_Juan" title="Port of San Juan">Port of San Juan</a> at San Juan Bay.\n</p><p>Puerto Rican soldiers being deployed during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a> were transported from the train station to the Army terminal at the Port of San Juan. At the time, Fort Buchanan served as a citizens training camp and soldier processing station.\n</p><p>After World War II the post was gradually reduced to its current size of 746 acres. Fort Buchanan remained a command depot with post facilities, a personnel center, and a special training center until closure as an Army post in 1966. On December 31, 1966, with the deactivation of the Antilles Command, Fort Buchanan passed to the control of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>.\n</p><p>On December 7, 1971, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Central" title="United States Army Central">Third U.S. Army</a> resumed control of Fort Buchanan, and in 1973 the post came under direct control of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Forces_Command" title="United States Army Forces Command">United States Army Forces Command</a> (FORSCOM). Following the Department of Defense Unified Command Plan, Fort Buchanan came under control of <a href="/wiki/United_States_Southern_Command" title="United States Southern Command">United States Southern Command</a> (SOUTHCOM). From 1999 to 2003 it was occupied by <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_South" title="United States Army South">United States Army South</a> (USARSO).\n</p><p>In 2002 Fort Buchanan became part of the Southeast Regional Office of the Installation Management Agency (IMA), and in 2003 it became an IMA Reserve installation. In 2006 it became a <a href="/wiki/Garrison" title="Garrison">garrison</a> under <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Installation_Management_Command" title="United States Army Installation Management Command">United States Army Installation Management Command</a>, Southeast Region (IMCOM-SE).\n</p><p>On October 1, 2008, the Commanding General of the <a href="/wiki/81st_Readiness_Division" class="mw-redirect" title="81st Readiness Division">81st Readiness Division</a> became Fort Buchanan\'s Senior Mission Commander under the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Reserve_Command" title="United States Army Reserve Command">United States Army Reserve Command</a> (USARC).  Based on the December 2008 Unified Command Plan, Fort Buchanan currently falls under the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Northern_Command" title="United States Northern Command">United States Northern Command</a> (USNORTHCOM) area of operations, under the direct control of the Installation Management Command, Atlantic Region (IMCOM-Atlantic).\n</p><p>On October 23, 2009, an explosion at the nearby <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Petroleum_Corporation" title="Caribbean Petroleum Corporation">Caribbean Petroleum Corporation</a> caused two people to suffer minor injuries at Fort Buchanan, and four other people sought help for respiratory problems. The explosion caused minor damages in some facilities of the military installation.<sup id="cite_ref-Explosion_rocks_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Explosion_rocks-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2017 after the wake of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a>, Fort Buchanan served as a command center during the recovery efforts. Military active duty, reserve and National Guard units from the continental United States were mobilized from Fort Buchanan all over the island for the emergency. Thousands of these service members were awarded the <a href="/wiki/Humanitarian_Service_Medal" title="Humanitarian Service Medal">Humanitarian Service Medal</a> for their disaster relief efforts. The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Corps_of_Engineers" title="United States Army Corps of Engineers">United States Army Corps of Engineers</a> also managed operations from Fort Buchanan.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Today">Today</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fort_Buchanan_(Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Today"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>United States Army Garrison Fort Buchanan consists of 746.16 acres between the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Bayam%C3%B3n,_Puerto_Rico" title="Bayam\xc3\xb3n, Puerto Rico">Bayam\xc3\xb3n</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guaynabo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guaynabo, Puerto Rico">Guaynabo, Puerto Rico</a> with a real estate value estimated at $560 million. Fort Buchanan serves a population of approximately 130,000, including military personnel, their dependents, retirees, veterans, and the civilian workforce.\n</p><p>Fort Buchanan is host to a number of tenant activities, most of them branches from the <a href="/wiki/Reserve_Component" class="mw-redirect" title="Reserve Component">United States Armed Forces Reserve Component</a> such as the headquarters for the <a href="/wiki/1st_Mission_Support_Command" title="1st Mission Support Command">1st Mission Support Command</a> (MSC) of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Reserve" title="United States Army Reserve">United States Army Reserve</a>.\n</p><p>Fort Buchanan also hosts a number of non-DoD organizations that provide services to soldiers, their dependents, and community members, including the <a href="/wiki/Pentagon_Federal_Credit_Union" title="Pentagon Federal Credit Union">Pentagon Federal Credit Union</a> <b>PenFed</b>, <a href="/wiki/Popular,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Popular, Inc.">Banco Popular</a> de Puerto Rico, and AAFES Concessionaires. Fort Buchanan also provides support to <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Homeland_Security" title="United States Department of Homeland Security">United States Department of Homeland Security</a> agencies such as the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Customs_and_Border_Protection" title="U.S. Customs and Border Protection">U.S. Customs and Border Protection</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Armed_Forces_Reserve_Center">Armed Forces Reserve Center</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fort_Buchanan_(Puerto_Rico)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Armed Forces Reserve Center"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Armed Forces Reserve Center (AFRC) at Fort Buchanan accommodates the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Army_Reserve" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Army Reserve">U.S. Army Reserve</a> Headquarters and Headquarters Detachment (HHD) of the 339th Battalion, (the 393rd) and a Retention Office. The <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_National_Guard" title="Puerto Rico National Guard">Puerto Rico Army National Guard</a> Recruiting and Retention Division (R&amp;R Div), the 480th Military Police Company and the Medical Section Cell C59. The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps_Reserve" title="United States Marine Corps Reserve">United States Marine Corps Reserve</a> relocated the Detachment 1, Landing Support Company, Combat Logistics Regiment 45 into this facility.  The Fort Buchanan Armed Forces Reserve Center serves about 400 personnel on a rotating basis, with a maximum of 250 members per weekend. This facility was named after senior civilian aide to the secretary of the Army for Puerto Rico Major General (Ret) Felix A.
