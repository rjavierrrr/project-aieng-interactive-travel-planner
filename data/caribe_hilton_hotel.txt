Hilton</b> is located in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>, and is owned by <a href="/wiki/Park_Hotels_%26_Resorts" title="Park Hotels &amp; Resorts">Park Hotels &amp; Resorts</a> and managed by <a href="/wiki/Hilton_Worldwide" title="Hilton Worldwide">Hilton Worldwide</a>. \n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caribe_Hilton_Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In early 1946, as part of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rican</a> industrialization effort known as <a href="/wiki/Operation_Bootstrap" title="Operation Bootstrap">Operation Bootstrap</a>, the Puerto Rico Industrial Development Co. decided to erect a modern luxury hotel. The facility would be owned by the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, but leased to the <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> hospitality operator that provided the best <a href="/wiki/Bid_price" title="Bid price">bid</a>. The hotel would have 300 bedrooms and the necessary ancillary facilities, and be located adjacent to <a href="/wiki/Fort%C3%ADn_de_San_Ger%C3%B3nimo" title="Fort\xc3\xadn de San Ger\xc3\xb3nimo">Fort\xc3\xadn de San Ger\xc3\xb3nimo</a> in Puerta de Tierra.\n</p><p>A competition was held to select the design, with three architectural firms in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> invited: Schimmelpfennig, Ruiz y Gonz\xc3\xa1lez; The Office of <a href="/wiki/Henry_Klumb" title="Henry Klumb">Henry Klumb</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Toro_Ferrer" title="Toro Ferrer">Toro Ferrer</a>. Two additional firms from Florida were also invited to participate: Frederick G. Seelman from Palm Beach and Robert Swartburg from Miami. These two firms submitted <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Renaissance" title="Spanish Renaissance">Spanish Renaissance</a>-style designs, while the three Puerto Rican firms presented modern designs inspired by the International style. The final design chosen was by <a href="/wiki/Toro_Ferrer" title="Toro Ferrer">Toro Ferrer</a>, who also designed the Modern Movement-style <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_Building_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Supreme Court Building (Puerto Rico)">Supreme Court Building</a> in San Juan.<sup id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>To operate the hotel, the Puerto Rico Industrial Development Co. invited seven leading American hotel firms. <a href="/wiki/Conrad_Hilton" title="Conrad Hilton">Conrad Hilton</a>, being from New Mexico, opened his letter back with "Mi estimado amigo" and won over the Puerto Ricans.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The creation of the hotel represented a partnership between the Puerto Rican government and U.S business. Americans viewed the creation of the hotel as a symbol of their power to create material progress. A majority of Puerto Rican citizens did not approve of the decision to build the hotel. They believed that the public funds would have better suited the island inhabitants if they were invested in improving education and social welfare. San Juan\'s fire chief of the time disapproved the government\'s decision to "degrade itself by buying tourists." Articles published by <i><a href="/wiki/El_Mundo_(Puerto_Rico)" title="El Mundo (Puerto Rico)">El Mundo</a></i> in 1952 saw the American tourists as selfish people who did not care about the island and ones who will in the future convince the Puerto Rican people to serve them.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The Caribe Hilton opened on December 9, 1949.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The government-backed Puerto Rico Industrial Development Corp. (<a href="/wiki/PRIDCO" class="mw-redirect" title="PRIDCO">PRIDCO</a>) spent $7 million to build and furnish the hotel. After it was built, the government leased the hotel to the Hilton Corp. on a 20-year lease.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The hotel was the first in Puerto Rico to offer radios in every room and individually controlled air conditioners.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Guests for the opening included <a href="/wiki/Gloria_Swanson" title="Gloria Swanson">Gloria Swanson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Air_Lines" title="Eastern Air Lines">Eastern Air Lines</a> President <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Rickenbacker" title="Eddie Rickenbacker">Eddie Rickenbacker</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Rockefeller" title="David Rockefeller">David Rockefeller</a> and numerous other celebrities and notables.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The hotel claims to be the birthplace of the <a href="/wiki/Pi%C3%B1a_colada" title="Pi\xc3\xb1a colada">Pi\xc3\xb1a colada</a>. In 1954, bartender Ram\xc3\xb3n \xe2\x80\x9cMonchito\xe2\x80\x9d Marrero spent three months creating a mix of rum, coconut cream, and pineapple juice. The drink was first served on August 15, 1954.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The original hotel was expanded over the years from 300 rooms to 646 rooms.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> After managing the hotel for 48 years, Hilton International bought the property in 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Hilton closed the hotel the following year for a complete renovation, lasting nine months.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It reopened on December 25, 1999 celebrating its 50th anniversary after $50 million in renovations.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2005, the hotel was expanded with the huge Paseo Caribe complex, including shops, restaurants, and 264 villas marketed as the Condado Lagoon Villas.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In January 2017, ownership of the hotel was transferred to Park Hotels &amp; Resorts when that company was <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spun off</a> from Hilton Worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In September 2017, the hotel was heavily damaged by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a>, forcing it to close. It reopened in May 2019, following a US$100 million renovation.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Caribbean_Business_2019_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Caribbean_Business_2019-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Famous_guests">Famous guests</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caribe_Hilton_Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Famous guests"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Its first of many famous guests were <a href="/wiki/Gloria_Swanson" title="Gloria Swanson">Gloria Swanson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gertrude_Ederle" title="Gertrude Ederle">Gertrude Ederle</a>. Between the 1960s and 1980s, many important performers either worked or stayed at the Caribe Hilton, including <a href="/wiki/Marco_Antonio_Mu%C3%B1iz" title="Marco Antonio Mu\xc3\xb1iz">Marco Antonio Mu\xc3\xb1iz</a>. Other famous guests have included many world champion boxers during <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>\'s <i>golden era of <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">boxing</a></i>. The national <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> teams of twelve countries also stayed there for 2003\'s pre-<a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic</a> tournament of the Americas. <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caribe_Hilton_Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In popular culture"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Part of the television film <i><a href="/wiki/Wizards_of_Waverly_Place:_The_Movie" title="Wizards of Waverly Place: The Movie">Wizards of Waverly Place: The Movie</a></i> was filmed in the outdoor pool area of the hotel. It is also the setting for <a href="/wiki/Hunter_S._Thompson" title="Hunter S. Thompson">Hunter S. Thompson</a>\'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rum_Diary_(novel)" title="The Rum Diary (novel)">The Rum Diary</a></i>. <i><a href="/wiki/22_Jump_Street" title="22 Jump Street">22 Jump Street</a></i> was also
