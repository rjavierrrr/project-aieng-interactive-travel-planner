class="infobox-data" style="border: 0;">July 30, 1976</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Iglesia Parroquial de San Antonio de Padua</b> (English: <span lang="en">Parish Church of Saint <a href="/wiki/Anthony_of_Padua" title="Anthony of Padua">Anthony of Padua</a></span>) is a historic, <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Roman Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Parish_church" title="Parish church">parish church</a> in <a href="/wiki/Guayama,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guayama, Puerto Rico">Guayama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The parish was erected in 1736 and the first church building completed no later than 1775. The original appearance of the building is unknown, but some of the eighteenth century walls may survive in the present structure. The building was rebuilt twice in the nineteenth century and the present appearance dates from 1874. It takes an overall <a href="/wiki/Romanesque_Revival_architecture" title="Romanesque Revival architecture">Romanesque</a> form, while the details exhibit the <a href="/wiki/Eclecticism_in_architecture" title="Eclecticism in architecture">eclecticism</a> that characterized much Puerto Rican architecture in the later 1800s.<sup id="cite_ref-AntonioGuayamaNom_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AntonioGuayamaNom-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The church was inscribed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1976.<sup id="cite_ref-FRto78_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FRto78-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta
