class="infobox-data" style="border: 0;">25 August 1988</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Cementerio Cat\xc3\xb3lico San Vicente de Paul</b> (English: <span lang="en">Saint Vincent de Paul Catholic Cemetery</span>) is a cemetery in the city of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>. It is the only cemetery in Puerto Rico with a group of niches built forming a basement, in which the burials occurred beneath ground level, thus giving the effect of a <a href="/wiki/Catacomb" class="mw-redirect" title="Catacomb">catacomb</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CemSVDPNom_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CemSVDPNom-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The cemetery is named after <a href="/wiki/Vincent_de_Paul" title="Vincent de Paul">Vincent de Paul</a> (24 April 1581 \xe2\x80\x93 27 September 1660), the French <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> priest who dedicated his life to serving the poor. It was listed on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1988.<sup id="cite_ref-WeeklyList_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WeeklyList-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cementerio_Cat%C3%B3lico_San_Vicente_de_Paul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Cementerio Catolico San Vicente de Paul is located off Puerto Rico Route <a href="/wiki/PR-123" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-123">PR-123</a> in barrio <a href="/wiki/Magueyes_Urbano" title="Magueyes Urbano">Magueyes Urbano</a>. PR-123 used to be signed <a href="/wiki/PR-10" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-10">PR-10</a>, and this last route numbering might still be seen in some older documents about this cemetery. The cemetery is located on a site with a steep terrain <a href="/wiki/Gradient" title="Gradient">gradient</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cementerio_Cat%C3%B3lico_San_Vicente_de_Paul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>During the turn of the 20th century the priests of San Vicente de Paul built a private Catholic cemetery in Ponce. The site was blessed on 15 August 1901<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and opened to the public. The wealthy families of the area bought plots for $60 and began to develop the cemetery with magnificent <a href="/wiki/Grave_(burial)" class="mw-redirect" title="Grave (burial)">graves</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mausoleum" title="Mausoleum">mausoleums</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CemSVDPNom_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CemSVDPNom-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Today (February 2016), the cemetery is "squeezed" between sprawling urban residential developments that now fully surround it.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Background_and_significance">Background and significance</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cementerio_Cat%C3%B3lico_San_Vicente_de_Paul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Background and significance"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Ponce, located in the south coast of Puerto Rico, was one of the wealthiest settlements of the island at the turn of the 20th century. Given the status of area cemeteries at the time, the Catholic cemetery offered an exclusive precinct in which the rich could bury their dead. For this reason almost all the tombs are magnificent in their design, ornamentation, and construction. Some of the best examples of funerary architecture in Puerto Rico of the early 20th century period can be seen in this cemetery. Marble sculptures from <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> and other parts of Europe adorn many of the tombs. Most tombs are covered with Ponce\'s ubiquitous stylish pink marble as well.<sup id="cite_ref-CemSVDPNom_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CemSVDPNom-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The larger mausolea, which belong to the wealthiest families of southern Puerto Rico, are magnificent examples of monumental funerary architecture of the period. Some of the best examples are the mausoleums of the <a href="/wiki/Juan_Serralles_Colon" class="mw-redirect" title="Juan Serralles Colon">Serralles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mario_Mercado" title="Mario Mercado">Mercado</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thomas_Armstrong_Toro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Thomas Armstrong Toro (page does not exist)">Toro</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Guillermo_Vivas_Valdivieso" title="Guillermo Vivas Valdivieso">Valdivieso</a> families.<sup id="cite_ref-CemSVDPNom_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CemSVDPNom-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The cemetery is unique among others in Puerto Rico: "A unique feature of the cemetery is a group of niches that were built forming a basement, in which the burials occurred beneath ground level, thus giving the effect of a <a href="/wiki/Catacomb" class="mw-redirect" title="Catacomb">catacomb</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-CemSVDPNom_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CemSVDPNom-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Design">Design</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cementerio_Cat%C3%B3lico_San_Vicente_de_Paul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Design"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The cemetery has a simple entrance gate. It consists of an iron gate flanked by two masonry <a href="/wiki/Pilaster" title="Pilaster">pilasters</a>. Although <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic</a>, this cemetery never had a <a href="/wiki/Chapel" title="Chapel">chapel</a> by the entrance, or anywhere in the entire precinct.<sup id="cite_ref-CemSVDPNom_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CemSVDPNom-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The layout of the cemetery was done following the Spanish tradition of having two main axes forming a cross. The rest of the <a href="/wiki/Religious_precinct" title="Religious precinct">precinct</a>\'s layout was done in a square grid fashion. The cemetery follows the contour lines of the steep site it is located on. As a results, some of the approach paths were built as stairs.<sup id="cite_ref-CemSVDPNom_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CemSVDPNom-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The main axis culminates with the <a href="/wiki/Temple" title="Temple">pantheon</a> for the priests of the <a href="/wiki/Paulist_Fathers" title="Paulist Fathers">Paul order</a>. "In this truly monumental tomb, sculptural forms are integral part of architecture. An interesting feature in this design with double function is the <a href="/wiki/Baldachino" class="mw-redirect" title="Baldachino">baldachino</a>, which protects not only the altar, but also the tomb. The <a href="/wiki/Baroque_architecture" title="Baroque architecture">baroque</a> style of this structure can be appreciated in the free use of forms, its large scale and sweeping vistas, and the complexity of its composition."<sup id="cite_ref-CemSVDPNom_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CemSVDPNom-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The cemetery has a clear arrangement of larger tombs placed along the major central and secondary axes. The other graves then radiate from there. Many of these larger tombs are mausoleums in different <a href="/wiki/Architectural_style" title="Architectural style">architectural styles</a> ranging from the <a href="/wiki/Classical_revival" class="mw-redirect" title="Classical revival">classical revival</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Art-deco" class="mw-redirect" title="Art-deco">art-deco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CemSVDPNom_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CemSVDPNom-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Architecture">Architecture</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cementerio_Cat%C3%B3lico_San_Vicente_de_Paul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Architecture"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Cementerio_Catolico_San_Vicente_de_Paul.JPG" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Cementerio_Catolico_San_Vicente_de_Paul.JPG/200px-Cementerio_Catolico_San_Vicente_de_Paul.JPG" /></a><figcaption>Cementerio Catolico San Vicente de Paul in March 1988</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The style of the cemetery is characterized more by its architectural than by its sculptural features.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Lugo_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lugo-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 65">&#58;&#8202;65&#8202;</span></sup> The quality of the construction is such that "it is considered the posthumous mansion" of those buried there.<sup id="cite_ref-Lugo_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lugo-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 117">&#58;&#8202;117&#8202;</span></sup> A considerable number of the mausoleum in this cemetery are made of marble, a testimony to the wealth of those interred there.<sup id="cite_ref-Lugo_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lugo-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 210">&#58;&#8202;210&#8202;</span></sup>\n</p><p>Of special significance for their architectural value are the <a href="/wiki/Juan_Serralles" class="mw-redirect" title="Juan Serralles">Serralles family</a> mausoleum composed of a central chapel raised over a raised plaza, the Mario Mercado Montalvo family mausoleum also over a raised plaza, and the <a href="/wiki/Guillermo_Vivas_Valdivieso" title="Guillermo Vivas Valdivieso">Valdivieso family</a> mausoleum. The <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Luis_Toro_Home" class="mw-redirect" title="Fernando Luis Toro Home">Toro family</a> mausoleum also presents a funerary chapel but it is not raised over a plaza nor is the <a href="/wiki/Carlos_E._Chard%C3%B3n" title="Carlos E. Chard\xc3\xb3n">Chardon family</a> mausoleum.<sup id="cite_ref-CemSVDPNom_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CemSVDPNom-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Recent_developments">Recent developments</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cementerio_Cat%C3%B3lico_San_Vicente_de_Paul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Recent developments"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Many of these magnificent tombs have been abandoned after their owners moved corpses of their dead to the <a href="/wiki/Cementerio_Civil_de_Ponce" title="Cementerio Civil de Ponce">Cementerio Civil de Ponce</a>. This was due in part to the high cost of maintenance and the strict regulations set by the administration. Under no condition would they allow a non-Catholic to be buried there. Some families did not want to be separated by this rule and moved their loved ones to the Cementerio Civil. As a result, some graves have been abandoned and cannot be sold by the cemetery administration since they are private property and have a legal, albeit uninterested, owner.<sup id="cite_ref-CemSVDPNom_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CemSVDPNom-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div
