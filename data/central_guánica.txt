Gu\xc3\xa1nica</b> was a sugar mill located in <a href="/wiki/Ensenada,_Gu%C3%A1nica,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ensenada, Gu\xc3\xa1nica, Puerto Rico">Ensenada Barrio</a> in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Gu%C3%A1nica,_Puerto_Rico" title="Gu\xc3\xa1nica, Puerto Rico">Gu\xc3\xa1nica, Puerto Rico</a>. It was one of the largest <a href="/wiki/Sugar_mills" class="mw-redirect" title="Sugar mills">sugar mills</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>, and until <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, it was one of the largest mills in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \nIt ceased operations in 1982.<sup id="cite_ref-Lex_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lex-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_Gu%C3%A1nica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Its owners, the <a href="/wiki/South_Puerto_Rico_Sugar_Company" title="South Puerto Rico Sugar Company">South Puerto Rico Sugar Company</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>, began construction of the Central Gu\xc3\xa1nica sugar mill in 1901. A <a href="/wiki/Company_town" title="Company town">company town</a>, which included a hospital, school, and housing facilities was organized around the sugar mill.<sup id="cite_ref-Lex_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lex-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1967, South Puerto Rico Sugar Company was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Gulf_and_Western_Industries" title="Gulf and Western Industries">Gulf and Western Industries</a>, which later sold the sugar mill during the 1970s.\n</p><p>In 2002, the government of Puerto Rico declared the two chimneys of the sugar mill as historic monuments.<sup id="cite_ref-Lex_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lex-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span
