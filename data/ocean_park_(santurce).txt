within the <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico">barrio</a> of <a href="/wiki/Santurce,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="Santurce, San Juan, Puerto Rico">Santurce</a>, in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. It is one of the forty <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Subdivisions of San Juan, Puerto Rico">subbarrios</a> of Santurce.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Demographics">Demographics</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ocean_Park_(Santurce)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Demographics"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/2000_United_States_Census" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 United States Census">2000</a>, Ocean Park had a population of 1,976 and a land area of 0.52 square kilometres (0.20&#160;sq&#160;mi).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2010, Ocean Park had a population of 1,667 and a population density of 8,335 persons per square mile.<sup id="cite_ref-2010_Census_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2010_Census-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Description">Description</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ocean_Park_(Santurce)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:A_man_in_a_mask_and_in_a_bike.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/A_man_in_a_mask_and_in_a_bike.jpg/220px-A_man_in_a_mask_and_in_a_bike.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/A_man_in_a_mask_and_in_a_bike.jpg/330px-A_man_in_a_mask_and_in_a_bike.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/A_man_in_a_mask_and_in_a_bike.jpg/440px-A_man_in_a_mask_and_in_a_bike.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5568" data-file-height="3712" /></a><figcaption>A man, wearing a mask, rides a bicycle in Ocean Park during the COVID-19 pandemic</figcaption></figure>\n<p>About one kilometer off the beach is the small rocky <a href="/wiki/Island_Stone" class="mw-redirect" title="Island Stone">Island Stone</a> which is also part of the area, measuring about 100 by 50 meters.\n</p><p>Ocean Park is bound on the east by Calle Guerrero Noble and its straight extension to the beach, a short piece of Calle Cacique running east to connect to Calle Teniente Rivera, and the connecting piece of Calle Soldado Cruz to Calle Lo\xc3\xadza. The southern border runs to Calle Lo\xc3\xadza, but excluding same. In the west, it is Calle Mar\xc3\xada Mozco (northern part) and Calle Santa Ana. The north is bordered by the beach and the Atlantic Ocean. Through the center runs Avenida McLeary where cafes, pubs and some services line the street.\n</p><p>Calle Lo\xc3\xadza is a "trendy spot" with new businesses, bars, restaurants and boutiques.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Ocean Park is located between <a href="/wiki/Condado_(Puerto_Rico)" class="mw-redirect" title="Condado (Puerto Rico)">Condado</a> and <a href="/wiki/Isla_Verde,_Puerto_Rico" title="Isla Verde, Puerto Rico">Isla Verde</a> beaches making it an essential tranquil, tree-lined street residential neighborhood, with numerous examples of single-family homes from the decades between 1930 and 1950. Small <a href="/wiki/Bed_and_breakfast" title="Bed and breakfast">bed-and-breakfast</a> inns, which blend in with the residential community, dot the area. Its wide oceanfront and offshore reefs prevent large waves from reaching the beach, which is popular with trendy beachgoers. Strong seasonal onshore <a href="/wiki/Trade_winds" title="Trade winds">trade winds</a> make the area popular for <a href="/wiki/Kitesurfing" class="mw-redirect" title="Kitesurfing">kitesurfing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Windsurfing" title="Windsurfing">windsurfing</a>.\n</p><p>At the right of the barrio is the Parque Barbosa and the residential area of Santa Teresita, forming the eastern part of the barrio. Parque Barbosa, also known as Ultimo Trolley (Last Tram) is the home of the Ocean Park Marlins Futbol Club, a football team with categories from 5-year-old kids to "Superior" (semi-professional) both for female and male footballers. \xc3\x9altimo Trolley got its name because it was the last <a href="/wiki/Electric_streetcar" class="mw-redirect" title="Electric streetcar">electric streetcar</a> that crossed the streets of San Juan from 1901 to 1946 and was placed in the park and operated as a restaurant.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
