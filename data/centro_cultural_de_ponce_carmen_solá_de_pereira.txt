title="Government of Puerto Rico">Government of Puerto Rico</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Centro Cultural Carmen Sol\xc3\xa1 de Pereira de Ponce</b> (English: Carmen Sol\xc3\xa1 de Pereira Ponce Cultural Center) is the cultural center of the city of <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce</a>, and is located at 70 Cristina street in the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Historic_Zone" title="Ponce Historic Zone">Ponce Historic Zone</a>, in Barrio <a href="/wiki/Tercero" class="mw-redirect" title="Tercero">Tercero</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-visit_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-visit-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Center is located in a structure designed in 1870, and it is currently used for educational and cultural activities as well as for art exhibits.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Centro Cultural de Ponce was founded on 12 November 1963.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centro_Cultural_Carmen_Sol%C3%A1_de_Pereira_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Founded in 1963 by Judith Rodr\xc3\xadguez Mirailh and Helvetia Nicole, the organization was originally called <i>La Casa de la Cultura</i> (The House of Culture).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Since its founding, Centro Cultural has been "actively promoting the artistic development on [Ponce]\xe2\x80\x99s rich educational and intellectual environment",<sup id="cite_ref-travel_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travel-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> including the <i>Noches de Galer\xc3\xada</i>, "where renowned as well as promising painters, <a href="/wiki/Artisan" title="Artisan">artisans</a> and sculptors exhibit their works and showcase their talent."<sup id="cite_ref-travel_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travel-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Centro Cultural de Ponce has occupied the structure at 70 Cristina Street since 1996. Prior to that its members met at members\' homes and similar venues.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Offerings">Offerings</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centro_Cultural_Carmen_Sol%C3%A1_de_Pereira_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Offerings"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Among the Center\'s offerings are "Noches de Galer\xc3\xada" during which the organization shows off artistic rendering by Ponce\xc3\xb1os and Puerto Ricans in general. Over 75 of these have taken place on a monthly basis since their inception. This offering started under the leadership of then Centro president and painter Jos\xc3\xa9 Balay Ruiz.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Centro Cultural also organizes and directs the yearly <a href="/wiki/Feria_de_Artesan%C3%ADas_de_Ponce" title="Feria de Artesan\xc3\xadas de Ponce">Feria de Artesan\xc3\xadas de Ponce</a>, which take place at <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Delicias" title="Plaza Las Delicias">Plaza Las Delicias</a> every year during the month of April.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Structure">Structure</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centro_Cultural_Carmen_Sol%C3%A1_de_Pereira_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Structure"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The structure, located at 18\xc2\xb0 0\' 44.244" N, 66\xc2\xb0 36\' 39.9528" W,<sup id="cite_ref-see_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-see-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> was designed in 1870 by the Corsican architect <a href="/wiki/Juan_Bertoli_Calderoni" title="Juan Bertoli Calderoni">Juan Bertoli Calderoni</a>. Its style incorporates elements of <a href="/wiki/Colonial_Spanish" class="mw-redirect" title="Colonial Spanish">Colonial Spanish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Creole" title="Ponce Creole">Ponce Creole</a> architecture. Its courtyard is surrounded by a gallery.<sup id="cite_ref-visit_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-visit-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The building was originally built as the former home of <a href="/wiki/Ermelindo_Salazar" title="Ermelindo Salazar">Ermelindo Salazar</a>, a prominent landowner, merchant, banker, as well as mayor of Ponce in 1880.<sup id="cite_ref-visit_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-visit-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-travel_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travel-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1959 the building became the first home of the <a href="/wiki/Museo_de_Arte_de_Ponce" title="Museo de Arte de Ponce">Museo de Arte de Ponce</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-travel_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travel-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-see_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-see-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Museo de Arte de Ponce moved to its current facility on Avenida Las Americas in 1965, vacating the structure at 70 Cristina Street. In 1990, the <a href="/wiki/Instituto_de_Cultura_Puertorrique%C3%B1a" title="Instituto de Cultura Puertorrique\xc3\xb1a">Instituto de Cultura Puertorrique\xc3\xb1a</a> restored the structure to house the first headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/Museo_de_la_M%C3%BAsica_Puertorrique%C3%B1a" title="Museo de la M\xc3\xbasica Puertorrique\xc3\xb1a">Museo de la M\xc3\xbasica Puertorrique\xc3\xb1a</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The house has the distinction of being the first one in the city to be served by a private telephone line.<sup id="cite_ref-visit_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-visit-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The line exclusively communicated the owners\xe2\x80\x99 family residence with their commercial offices at the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Ponce" class="mw-redirect" title="Port of Ponce">Ponce port</a>. This was 17 years before the service finally arrived to the rest of the City.<sup id="cite_ref-travel_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travel-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Carmen_Sol\xc3\xa1_de_Pereira"><span id="Carmen_Sol.C3.A1_de_Pereira"></span>Carmen Sol\xc3\xa1 de Pereira</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Centro_Cultural_Carmen_Sol%C3%A1_de_Pereira_de_Ponce&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Carmen Sol\xc3\xa1 de Pereira"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The Center was named after Carmen Sol\xc3\xa1 de Pereira, an outstanding educator and a representative from Ponce at the Puerto Rico Legislative Assembly.<sup id="cite_ref-visit_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-visit-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Sol\xc3\xa1 de Pereira dedicated her life to the education of disadvantaged children. She was a teacher and school principal in <a href="/wiki/Sexto_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sexto (Ponce)">La Cantera</a>, an underprivileged community in Ponce, Puerto Rico.<sup
