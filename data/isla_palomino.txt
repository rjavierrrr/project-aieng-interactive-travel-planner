href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Isla Palominos</i>) is a small island located off the coast of the <a href="/wiki/Barrios_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Barrios of Puerto Rico">barrio</a> of  <a href="/wiki/Cabezas,_Fajardo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Cabezas, Fajardo, Puerto Rico">Cabezas</a> in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Fajardo" class="mw-redirect" title="Fajardo">Fajardo</a> to the northeast of the <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Geography of Puerto Rico">main island</a> of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. It forms part of a small chain of <a href="/wiki/Cay" title="Cay">cays</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reef" title="Reef">reefs</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Islets" class="mw-redirect" title="Islets">islets</a> protected by the <a href="/wiki/La_Cordillera_Reef_Nature_Reserve" title="La Cordillera Reef Nature Reserve">La Cordillera Reef Nature Reserve</a>. The island is home to <i>El Conquistador Resort</i>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Palominos_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Palominos is 0.7&#160;mi (1.1&#160;km) long and maximally 1,722 feet (525&#160;m) wide, measures 102 acres (41&#160;ha) in area and reaches a height of 165 feet (50&#160;m).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Coral reefs, seagrass beds, and rocky coast species are among the natural attractions found in Palominos. Another attraction is the neighboring  island of Palominitos, which means tiny Palominos. Located about 2,000 feet (610 meters) south of Palominos, Palominitos now almost sank due to erosion.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Economy">Economy</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Palominos_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Economy"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Palominos is the largest of three private islands near Puerto Rico, the other two being the neighboring <i>Ramos</i> island and <i>Lobos</i> cay. The island is owned by the descendants of <i>Alberto Bachman Glauser, the Bachman Family and the Fuertes Bachman Family</i>. Most of the island is rented to <i>El Conquistador Resort</i> for their beach and water activities. A private company provides private yacht charters to the island.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> A ferry is used to get guests to the island, which takes approximately 12 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Palominos horses can be rented by guests for horse-back riding on the beach.<sup id="cite_ref-3"
