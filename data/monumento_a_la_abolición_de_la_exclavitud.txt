Rico">Puerto Rico</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Monumento a la abolici\xc3\xb3n de la esclavitud</b> (Monument to the abolition of slavery) is a monument at <a href="/wiki/Parque_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n" title="Parque de la Abolici\xc3\xb3n">Parque de la Abolici\xc3\xb3n</a> in Barrio <a href="/wiki/Cuarto_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cuarto (Ponce)">Cuarto</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>, dedicated to the abolition of slavery in Puerto Rico in 1873. It is the only monument in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> dedicated as a remembrance of the abolition of slavery. The monument consists of an <a href="/wiki/Obelisk" title="Obelisk">obelisk</a> and a statue called <i><a href="/wiki/El_Hombre_Redimido" title="El Hombre Redimido">El Hombre Redimido</a></i>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monumento_a_la_abolici%C3%B3n_de_la_esclavitud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The monument is located on Avenida Hostos at the fork of Calle Salud and Calle Marina.  It is the centerpiece of <a href="/wiki/Parque_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n" title="Parque de la Abolici\xc3\xb3n">Parque de la Abolici\xc3\xb3n</a> and it is located on its grounds, at the park\'s southernmost tip.  The monument consists of two main pieces, an <a href="/wiki/Obelisk" title="Obelisk">obelisk</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Statue" title="Statue">statue</a>. The obelisk is known as <i>Obelisco a la libertad</i> (Obelisk to freedom).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  The statue is that of a black male slave with broken chains depicting he is now a free man. Its coordinates are 18.00573, -66.61276.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Properties_and_features">Properties and features</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monumento_a_la_abolici%C3%B3n_de_la_esclavitud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Properties and features"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Mapa_del_Parque_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Mapa_del_Parque_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n.jpg/220px-Mapa_del_Parque_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="300" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Mapa_del_Parque_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n.jpg/330px-Mapa_del_Parque_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Mapa_del_Parque_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n.jpg/440px-Mapa_del_Parque_de_la_Abolici%C3%B3n.jpg 2x" data-file-width="721" data-file-height="984" /></a><figcaption>Map of Parque de la Abolici\xc3\xb3n showing the location of the monument</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The 100-foot high <a href="/wiki/Obelisk" title="Obelisk">obelisk</a> raises just behind the black iron <a href="/wiki/Sculpture" title="Sculpture">sculpture</a> of the freed slave that accentuates and gives instance to the occasion. Immediately north of the obelisk is the outdoors <a href="/wiki/Musical_acoustics" title="Musical acoustics">acoustic</a> <a href="/wiki/Amphitheater" class="mw-redirect" title="Amphitheater">amphitheater</a> known as <i><a href="/wiki/Concha_Ac%C3%BAstica_de_Ponce" title="Concha Ac\xc3\xbastica de Ponce">La Concha Ac\xc3\xbastica</a></i> (English: The Acoustic Shell) which completes the park as a triangular city block. <i>La Concha Ac\xc3\xbastica</i> is an open-air auditorium oftentimes used as a music venue, including presentations by the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Municipal_Band" title="Ponce Municipal Band">Ponce Municipal Band</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Background_and_significance">Background and significance</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monumento_a_la_abolici%C3%B3n_de_la_esclavitud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Background and significance"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Slaves were brought to Puerto Rico from Africa starting in 1513 and through the 18th century to replace the local native "Indian" slaves who had been decimated.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The new slaves worked the <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sugar_cane" class="mw-redirect" title="Sugar cane">sugar cane</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gold_mining" title="Gold mining">gold mining</a> industries in Puerto Rico. During the 18th century, as gold mining ceased to be one of the major industries in Puerto Rico, slaves worked mostly in coffee plantations and sugar cane fields.  By royal proclamation slavery was abolished on 22 March 1873.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Construction_history">Construction history</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monumento_a_la_abolici%C3%B3n_de_la_esclavitud&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Construction history"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1874, a year after the abolition of slavery in Puerto Rico, a group of citizens built a small park in memory of the historic event.  In 1880 <a href="/wiki/Olimpio_Otero" class="mw-redirect" title="Olimpio Otero">Olimpio Otero</a>, Juan Mayoral Barn\xc3\xa9s, and <a href="/wiki/Rom%C3%A1n_Baldorioty_de_Castro" title="Rom\xc3\xa1n Baldorioty de Castro">Rom\xc3\xa1n Baldorioty de Castro</a> were instrumental in creating the concept for a park dedicated to commemorating the abolition of slavery, the only such memorial in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Juan Mayoral Barn\xc3\xa9s brought the idea for the creation of the park to the Ponce Municipal Assembly on 14 March 1880. It was unanimously approved by the Assembly, ratified by the Central Government, and confirmed by Royal Decree on 1 March 1881.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The park had been built in the 1890s and renovated in 1956, under the administration of Ponce mayor <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Grillasca_Salas" title="Andr\xc3\xa9s Grillasca Salas">Andr\xc3\xa9s Grillasca Salas</a>. The renovation included the building of <i>Concha Acustica de Ponce</i> and the addition of the statue to stand at the base of the obelisk, in front of it.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The sculpture of the freed slave is the work of Victor Cott Negr\xc3\xb3n.<sup id="cite_ref-El_Valle_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-El_Valle-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The obelisk is built in marble and has a metal tip.\n</p>\n<div
