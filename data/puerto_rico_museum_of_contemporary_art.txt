border: 0;">Added to NRHP</th><td class="infobox-data" style="border: 0;">August 4, 1987</td></tr><tr style="display:none"><td colspan="2">\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>The Puerto Rico Museum of Contemporary Art</b>, often abbreviated to <b>MAC</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_art" title="Contemporary art">contemporary art</a> <a href="/wiki/Art_gallery" title="Art gallery">museum</a> in <a href="/wiki/Santurce,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Santurce, Puerto Rico">Santurce, Puerto Rico</a>.\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Puerto_Rico_Museum_of_Contemporary_Art&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The museum was founded by artists and sponsors of the <a href="/wiki/Civil_society" title="Civil society">civil society</a> and was officially instituted on October 8, 1984 as a non-profit organization. After its incorporation, the Museum offered its services in public spaces like <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Am%C3%A9ricas_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Plaza Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas (Puerto Rico)">Plaza Las Am\xc3\xa9ricas</a> and amongst offices of known contributors.\n</p><p>In 1988, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Sacred_Heart_(Puerto_Rico)" class="mw-redirect" title="University of the Sacred Heart (Puerto Rico)">University of the Sacred Heart</a> granted the museum a provisional home free of charge at the Magdalena Sof\xc3\xada Barat building. After 15 years, the museum officially petitioned the government to grant them the Labra Historical Building in <a href="/wiki/Santurce,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Santurce, Puerto Rico">Santurce</a>. On October 20, 2002, the museum and Governor <a href="/wiki/Sila_Calder%C3%B3n" class="mw-redirect" title="Sila Calder\xc3\xb3n">Sila Calder\xc3\xb3n</a> signed a contract granting the museum the rights for the building.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Building">Building</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Puerto_Rico_Museum_of_Contemporary_Art&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Building"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Museo_de_Arte_Contempor%C3%A1neo_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Museo_de_Arte_Contempor%C3%A1neo_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg/220px-Museo_de_Arte_Contempor%C3%A1neo_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Museo_de_Arte_Contempor%C3%A1neo_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg/330px-Museo_de_Arte_Contempor%C3%A1neo_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Museo_de_Arte_Contempor%C3%A1neo_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg/440px-Museo_de_Arte_Contempor%C3%A1neo_de_Puerto_Rico.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5200" data-file-height="3904" /></a><figcaption>Museo de Arte Contempor\xc3\xa1neo de Puerto Rico</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The museum building is called Rafael M. Labra Building. It was built in 1916 as part of a project from the Paul G. Miller Commission for the construction of <a href="/wiki/Public_school_(government_funded)" class="mw-redirect" title="Public school (government funded)">public schools</a> in urban zones. The building was designed by architect Adrian C. Finlayson.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The building belonged to the Department of Public Education of Puerto Rico since its construction and in 1987 was registered as the Rafael M. Labra High School on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-nom_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nom-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and was added to the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Register_of_Historic_Sites_and_Zones" title="Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones">Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones</a> in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-:02_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:02-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The architecture is of <a href="/wiki/Georgian_architecture" title="Georgian architecture">Georgian style</a> that prevailed during the 18th century in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. Its restoration was in charge of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican people">Puerto Rican</a> architect Otto Reyes Casanova and started from 1995 until 2002.\n</p><p>It is located about ten minutes walking distance from <a href="/wiki/La_Placita_de_Santurce" title="La Placita de Santurce">La Placita de Santurce</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5"
