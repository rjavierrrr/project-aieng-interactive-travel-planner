2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="1600" /></a><figcaption>A couple walks on a street in Santurce</figcaption></figure>\n<p>Geographically speaking, Santurce is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_peninsulas" title="List of peninsulas">peninsula</a> that is attached to the mainland in the east, where it borders with the <a href="/wiki/Isla_Verde,_Puerto_Rico" title="Isla Verde, Puerto Rico">Isla Verde</a> district of <a href="/wiki/Carolina,_Puerto_Rico" title="Carolina, Puerto Rico">Carolina</a>. It is 7.6&#160;km long from west to east, and up to 3.0&#160;km wide in the eastern part. The peninsula is bounded by the Atlantic Ocean in the north, with more than five km of beaches from the <a href="/wiki/Condado_(Santurce)" title="Condado (Santurce)">Condado</a> peninsula in the west, to a point 600 m east of <i>Punta Las Mar\xc3\xadas</i>, where it borders on the Isla Verde area, and <i><a href="/wiki/Laguna_San_Jos%C3%A9_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Laguna San Jos\xc3\xa9 (Puerto Rico)">Laguna San Jos\xc3\xa9</a></i> and its northern embayment, <i>Laguna Los Corozos</i> to the east.\n</p><p>To the south is the <a href="/wiki/Mart%C3%ADn_Pe%C3%B1a_Channel" title="Mart\xc3\xadn Pe\xc3\xb1a Channel">Mart\xc3\xadn Pe\xc3\xb1a Channel</a>, which separates Santurce from the northern barrios of former municipio <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Piedras,_Puerto_Rico" title="R\xc3\xado Piedras, Puerto Rico">R\xc3\xado Piedras</a>: <a href="/wiki/Hato_Rey_Norte" title="Hato Rey Norte">Hato Rey Norte</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hato_Rey_Central" title="Hato Rey Central">Hato Rey Central</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Oriente,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="Oriente, San Juan, Puerto Rico">Oriente</a>. To the west is <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Bay" title="San Juan Bay">San Juan Bay</a>, where three bridges, Dos Hermanos Bridge (Ave. Ashford), G. Esteves Bridge (<a href="/wiki/Avenida_Juan_Ponce_de_Le%C3%B3n" title="Avenida Juan Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n">Ave. Ponce de Le\xc3\xb3n</a>) and San Antonio Bridge (Ave. Fernandes Juncos) connect Santurce with <i><a href="/wiki/Isleta_de_San_Juan" class="mw-redirect" title="Isleta de San Juan">La Isleta</a></i> (small island) where <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Old San Juan, Puerto Rico">Old San Juan</a> is located.\nIt has a total area of 8.70 square miles (22.5&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) composed of 5.24 square miles (13.6&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) of land and 3.46 square miles (9.0&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) of water area.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Topography" title="Topography">topography</a> is mainly flat with low hills toward the central areas and swampy areas to the south along the Mart\xc3\xadn Pe\xc3\xb1a Channel and to the east near the Laguna San Jos\xc3\xa9 (San Jos\xc3\xa9 Lagoon). The highest point is at <a href="/wiki/Sagrado_Coraz%C3%B3n_/_Monteflores_(Santurce)" title="Sagrado Coraz\xc3\xb3n / Monteflores (Santurce)">Monteflores</a> with 23 meters (75 feet).\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Location">Location</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santurce,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Santurce is located along the north-eastern coast of Puerto Rico. It lies south of the Atlantic Ocean, east of <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Old San Juan, Puerto Rico">Old San Juan</a> and west of <a href="/wiki/Isla_Verde,_Puerto_Rico" title="Isla Verde, Puerto Rico">Isla Verde</a>. The district occupies an area of 5.24 square miles (13.6&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) of land and 3.46 (8.96 km2) of water. It is surrounded by six bodies of water: <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_Bay" title="San Juan Bay">San Juan Bay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Condado_Lagoon" title="Condado Lagoon">Condado Natural Lagoon</a>, the Mart\xc3\xadn Pe\xc3\xb1a Channel, <a href="/wiki/Laguna_San_Jos%C3%A9_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Laguna San Jos\xc3\xa9 (Puerto Rico)">San Jos\xc3\xa9 Lagoon</a>, Los Corozos Lagoon, and the Atlantic Ocean with its respective beaches and estuaries.<sup id="cite_ref-Santurce_District_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Santurce_District-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santurce,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Seaport"><span>edit</span></a><span id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santurce,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Colonial">Colonial</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santurce,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Colonial"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1760, <b>San Mateo de Cangrejos</b> ("<a href="/wiki/Saint_Matthew" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Matthew">Saint Matthew</a>" of the Crabs) is founded and later is named Santurce by Basque immigrants.<sup id="cite_ref-UPRRP_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UPRRP-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It was originally settled by the native <a href="/wiki/Arawak" title="Arawak">Arawak</a> and later by <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_slave_trade" title="Atlantic slave trade">the enslaved people from Africa</a> who were forcibly brought by way of the Atlantic slave trade from neighboring islands now known as the <a href="/wiki/Danish_West_Indies" title="Danish West Indies">Danish West Indies</a>. Throughout the centuries, the district continued to grow due to its location between <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan" title="Old San Juan">San Juan</a> and its <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Piedras,_Puerto_Rico" title="R\xc3\xado Piedras, Puerto Rico">southern suburbs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Santurce_Township_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Santurce_Township-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Spanish_influence">Spanish influence</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santurce,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Spanish influence"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1876, an engineer from the port town of <a href="/wiki/Santurtzi" title="Santurtzi">Santurtzi</a> in Spain\'s autonomous <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)" title="Basque Country (autonomous community)">Basque Country</a> region known as Pablo Ubarri arrived on the island to help in the construction of a railroad system and a steam tramway between San Juan and the town of R\xc3\xado Piedras through the center of San Mateo de Cangrejos which prompted the <a href="/wiki/Gentrification" title="Gentrification">gentrification</a> of the district. Many years after his arrival he was granted the title of Count of Santurce (which is the Hispanicized equivalent of Santurtzi) by the Spanish Crown. With his newly acquired title and influence, the district was renamed after his title (a decision that has caused controversy ever since). The tourist district of <a href="/wiki/Condado_(Santurce)" title="Condado (Santurce)">Condado</a> within Santurce also received its present-name from Ubarri\'s title, as the district\'s name literally translates to "county" (which in medieval tradition is land granted by a monarch to a count).<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Treaty_of_Paris_(1898)"><span id="Treaty_of_Paris_.281898.29"></span>Treaty of Paris (1898)</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santurce,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Treaty of Paris (1898)"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In 1899, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_War" title="United States Department of War">United States Department of War</a> conducted a census of Puerto Rico, finding that the population of Santurce was 5,840.<sup id="cite_ref-OfficeSanger1900_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OfficeSanger1900-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The survey was conducted in the aftermath of te <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1898)" title="Treaty of Paris (1898)">Treaty of Paris</a> which had Spain cede Puerto Rico to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" class="mw-redirect" title="United States of America">United States</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War">Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War</a> as an <a href="/wiki/Insular_area" title="Insular area">unincorporated territory of the United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OfficeSanger1900_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OfficeSanger1900-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a> established <a href="/wiki/Camp_Las_Casas" title="Camp Las Casas">Camp Las Casas</a>, in the area of Las Casas in 1904. The camp was the main training base of the <i>Porto Rico Regiment of Infantry</i> (on January 15, 1899, the military government changed the name of Puerto Rico to <i>Porto Rico</i> and on May 17, 1932, U.S. Congress changed the name back to "Puerto Rico") The <i>Porto Rico Regiment of Infantry</i> was a <a href="/wiki/U.S._Army_Regimental_System" title="U.S. Army Regimental System">U.S. Army Regiment</a> which was later renamed the "<a href="/wiki/65th_Infantry_Regiment_(United_States)" title="65th Infantry Regiment (United States)">65th Infantry Regiment</a>". The 65th Infantry Regiment was segregated. However, a separate division called the 375th Regiment enlisted Black soldiers. The base continued in operation until 1946, when it was finally closed and the <a href="/wiki/Residencial_Las_Casas" title="Residencial Las Casas">Residencial Las Casas</a> now stands.<sup id="cite_ref-HMPR_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HMPR-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="20th_century">20th century</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Teatro_Ambassador_panorama.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img early years, the Ambassador Theatre was the place where kids went to see "Abbott and Costello" movies.</figcaption></figure>\n<p>In the 20th century the conurbation of the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a> <a href="/wiki/Metropolis" title="Metropolis">metropolis</a> expanded rapidly beyond its walled confines of <a href="/wiki/Old_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Old San Juan, Puerto Rico">Old San Juan</a> to incorporate the boroughs of suburban <a href="/wiki/Miramar_(Santurce)" title="Miramar (Santurce)">Miramar</a>, Santurce, <a href="/wiki/Isla_Grande_(Santurce)" title="Isla Grande (Santurce)">Isla Grande</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Condado_(Santurce)" title="Condado (Santurce)">Condado</a>, along the coast, as well as industrial <a href="/wiki/Hato_Rey" title="Hato Rey">Hato Rey</a>, with its large sports stadium and modern <a href="/wiki/Milla_de_Oro" title="Milla de Oro">financial district</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/College_town" title="College town">college town</a> of <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Piedras,_Puerto_Rico" title="R\xc3\xado Piedras, Puerto Rico">R\xc3\xado Piedras</a>, immediately to the southeast.<sup id="cite_ref-Archive_Dot_Org_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Archive_Dot_Org-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Between 1937 and 1948, Santurce along with neighboring district Miramar became one of the most vibrant areas of the capital.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> However, by the 1970s, most of the district had fallen into decay, losing the luster and vibrancy it once had. Many residents left Santurce, fleeing to the <a href="/wiki/San_Juan_metropolitan_area" class="mw-redirect" title="San Juan metropolitan area">suburbs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Peffer_Lonely_Planet_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Peffer_Lonely_Planet-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> By 1980 the San Juan metropolitan area included the surrounding municipalities to the east and west and had about one-third of Puerto Rico\'s total population; that proportion has grown to two-thirds of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-Santurce_Britannica_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Santurce_Britannica-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="21st_century">21st century</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santurce,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 21st century"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>In the early 21st century Santurce saw a period of economic decline coupled with the <a href="/wiki/Great_Recession" title="Great Recession">financial crisis</a> of the local banking<sup id="cite_ref-Scotiabank_may_bag_Firstbank_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Scotiabank_may_bag_Firstbank-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> &amp; mortgage system.<sup id="cite_ref-New_York_Times_02_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-New_York_Times_02-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2009, the district began a period of cosmopolitan revival and economic growth<sup id="cite_ref-400K_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-400K-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> as many local establishments such as bars, clubs, and restaurants opened their doors due to the importance of trade and tourism. What helped was the decrease in rent, it attracted artists to the area.<sup id="cite_ref-Peffer_Lonely_Planet_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Peffer_Lonely_Planet-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Since then, Santurce began experiencing a new wave of <a href="/wiki/Gentrification" title="Gentrification">gentrification</a> and is now hailed by many as Puerto Rico\'s "<a href="/wiki/Hipster_(contemporary_subculture)" title="Hipster (contemporary subculture)">hipster</a> haven".<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2018, twenty-two murals were painted in and around Santurce to illustrate Santurce\'s culture and history.<sup id="cite_ref-El_Nuevo_D\xc3\xada_2018_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-El_Nuevo_D\xc3\xada_2018-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Demographics">Demographics</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santurce,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Demographics"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1236090951">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}@media print{body.ns-0 .mw-parser-output .hatnote{display:none!important}}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Jewish_immigration_to_Puerto_Rico" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish immigration to Puerto Rico">Jewish immigration to Puerto Rico</a> and <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Jews_in_Latin_America" class="mw-redirect" title="History of the Jews in Latin America">History of the Jews in Latin America</a></div>\n<p>Santurce is one of the top ten most-populated areas of Puerto Rico. It includes the neighborhoods of <a href="/wiki/Miramar_(Santurce)" title="Miramar (Santurce)">Miramar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lo%C3%ADza_(Santurce)" title="Lo\xc3\xadza (Santurce)">Lo\xc3\xadza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isla_Grande_(Santurce)" title="Isla Grande (Santurce)">Isla Grande</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barrio_Obrero_(Santurce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Barrio Obrero (Santurce)">Barrio Obrero</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Condado_(Santurce)" title="Condado (Santurce)">Condado</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2010_Census_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2010_Census-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> which are cultural hot spots for <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_art" title="Puerto Rican art">art</a>, <a href="/wiki/Music_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Music of Puerto Rico">music</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_cuisine" title="Puerto Rican cuisine">cuisine</a>, fashion, <a href="/wiki/Tourism_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Tourism in Puerto Rico">hotels</a>, technology, multimedia, <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Puerto_Rico" title="Cinema of Puerto Rico">film</a>, textile and <a href="/wiki/Startup_company" title="Startup company">startups</a>.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census,_2010" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Census, 2010">2010 U.S. Census</a> recorded a total population of 81,251 people living in an area of 5.24 square miles (13.6&#160;km<sup>2</sup>). It is the most populous borough (<i>barrio</i>) in Puerto Rico and one of the most <a href="/wiki/Densely_populated" class="mw-redirect" title="Densely populated">densely populated</a> areas of San Juan, at 15,447.0 residents per square mile (6,931.2/km<sup>2</sup>).<sup id="cite_ref-2010_Census_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2010_Census-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Santurce is home to one of the largest Jewish communities in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> and the Caribbean with over 1,500 people attending two local synagogues. Jews were officially prohibited from settling in the island through much of its history, but many managed to settle in the island as <a href="/wiki/Crypto-Judaism" title="Crypto-Judaism">secret Jews</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Many arrived from <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Jews_in_France" title="History of the Jews in France">France</a>, the <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Jews_in_the_Netherlands" title="History of the Jews in the Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saint-Barth%C3%A9lemy" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint-Barth\xc3\xa9lemy">Saint-Barth\xc3\xa9lemy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Cura\xc3\xa7ao">Cura\xc3\xa7ao</a> after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. A minor portion are descendants of <a href="/wiki/Jewish_Cuban" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish Cuban">Jewish Cubans</a> who came to establishment after <a href="/wiki/Fidel_Castro" title="Fidel Castro">Fidel Castro</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Cuban_Revolution" title="Cuban Revolution">Cuban Revolution</a> of 1959. Like in many former Spanish colonies founded soon after the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Inquisition" title="Spanish Inquisition">Spanish Inquisition</a>, there are some Puerto Ricans who are <a href="/wiki/Crypto-Judaism" title="Crypto-Judaism">Crypto-Jews</a>. Recent DNA ancestry has identified a number of <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Jews_in_Portugal" title="History of the Jews in Portugal">Portuguese descendants</a> who arrived in Puerto Rico after the start of the Portuguese Inquisition in 1536. These are descendants of <a href="/wiki/Converso" title="Converso">Converso</a> families. There are some who maintain elements of Jewish traditions, although they themselves are, or were raised as Christians.\n</p><p>Santurce also has a very big <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic_immigration_to_Puerto_Rico" title="Dominican Republic immigration to Puerto Rico">Dominican community</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Cubans" title="Cubans">Cuban</a>, Colombian, <a href="/wiki/Argentines" title="Argentines">Argentine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chinese_immigration_to_Puerto_Rico" title="Chinese immigration to Puerto Rico">Chinese</a> communities.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Subdivisions_of_Santurce">Subdivisions of Santurce</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santurce,_San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Subdivisions of Santurce"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Santurce has a community of 81,251 of inhabitants living in a land area of 5.24 square miles (13.6&#160;km<sup>2</sup>).
