Transportation and Public Works of Puerto Rico">Department of Transportation and Public Works of Puerto Rico</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>El Monumento de la Recordaci\xc3\xb3n</b> (English: <i>The</i> <i>Monument of Remembrance</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Memorial" title="Memorial">memorial</a> <a href="/wiki/Monument" title="Monument">monument</a> and <a href="/wiki/Monolith" title="Monolith">monolith</a> erected in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> that honors <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican people">Puerto Ricans</a> who have fallen in combat in service of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Armed_Forces" title="United States Armed Forces">United States Armed Forces</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Collins_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Collins-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The monument was unveiled on May 19, 1996 as a granite wall engraved with the names of Puerto Rican soldiers who died during combat.<sup id="cite_ref-Collins_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Collins-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> As of 2009, the monument was etched with the names of more than 2,000 service members.<sup id="cite_ref-Lyle_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lyle-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The monument largely contains the names of soldiers who had a Puerto Rico address at their time of enlistment and/or death. If a soldier had a stateside address their name is most likely not on the monument.<sup id="cite_ref-Puerta_de_Tierra_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Puerta_de_Tierra-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Because so many Puerto Ricans in the military list a United States address, their participation is underrepresented in statistics.<sup id="cite_ref-Lyle_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lyle-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>A sculpture by Victor Gutierrez called <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Llama de la eternidad</i></span> (Flame of eternity) is located in the center of monument.<sup id="cite_ref-Puerta_de_Tierra_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Puerta_de_Tierra-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>A service is held each year on Memorial Day at the <span title="Spanish-language text"><i lang="es">Monumento de la Recordaci\xc3\xb3n</i></span>. In 2019, attendees learned that two fallen soldiers from Puerto Rico had been recently identified as part of the <a href="/wiki/Recovery_of_US_human_remains_from_the_Korean_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Recovery of US human remains from the Korean War">Recovery of US human remains from the Korean War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Korean_War_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Korean_War-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="See_also">See also</h2><span
