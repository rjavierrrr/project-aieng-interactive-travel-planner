The institution "has been recognized by the <a href="/wiki/College_Board" title="College Board">College Board</a> as the best among public and private schools in Puerto Rico during the last 14 years."<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It has been regarded as one of the "most prestigious private schools in the city [of Ponce]."<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colegio_Ponce%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The school was founded in 1926 as <b>Colegio Ponce\xc3\xb1o de Varones</b>, by Miss Maria Serra Gelabert, a Catholic <a href="/wiki/Laywoman" class="mw-redirect" title="Laywoman">laywoman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Catechist" class="mw-redirect" title="Catechist">catechist</a>, as a boys-only Catholic school sponsored by the Bishop of Ponce, Msgr. <a href="/wiki/Edwin_Byrne" title="Edwin Byrne">Edwin Vincent Byrne</a>.  It was located on a rented house on <a href="/wiki/PR-133" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-133">Calle Comercio</a>. It later moved to its own facility on Calle Aurora, and in 1974 it moved to its current facility on <a href="/wiki/PR-14" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-14">PR-14</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>From 1930 to 1968 the school was directed by the <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Mary_(Marianists)" title="Society of Mary (Marianists)">Marianist Brothers</a>. In 1968, the <a href="/wiki/Piarists" title="Piarists">Piarist Fathers</a> (<i>Padres Escolapios</i>) took over its administration, called by Msgr. <a href="/wiki/Juan_Fremiot_Torres_Oliver" title="Juan Fremiot Torres Oliver">Juan Fremiot Torres Oliver</a>, Bishop of Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>Under the Piarist administration the school\'s name was changed to "Colegio Ponce\xc3\xb1o" upon becoming a <a href="/wiki/Mixed-sex_education" title="Mixed-sex education">coed school</a> in 1973. As a Piarist school, Colegio Ponce\xc3\xb1o follows the Catholic educational philosophy of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Joseph_Calasanz" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Joseph Calasanz">Saint Joseph Calasanz</a> and is administered by the Piarist Fathers Province of the United States and Puerto Rico. The school is owned by a Parent\'s Board of Trustees founded by the Bishop of Ponce.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colegio_Ponce%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The school was originally located on Luna Street in downtown Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1973, it moved to its new facilities on <a href="/wiki/PR-14" class="mw-redirect" title="PR-14">PR-14</a>. It is now located in Barrio <a href="/wiki/Coto_Laurel" title="Coto Laurel">Coto Laurel</a> in Ponce, where it boasts one of the largest campuses and most modern school facilities in the Island.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Accreditation">Accreditation</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colegio_Ponce%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Accreditation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Colegio Ponce\xc3\xb1o is accredited by the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Education" title="Puerto Rico Department of Education">Puerto Rico Department of Education</a> and by the <a href="/wiki/Middle_States_Association_of_Colleges_and_Schools" title="Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools">Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools</a>. It is a permanent member of the College Board, serving as test center for the ATP of the College Board. It was recognized in 1991 and 1992 as a U.S. Department of Education Blue Ribbon School.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Mission">Mission</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colegio_Ponce%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mission"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>Since its establishment, the school has upheld high <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic Church">Catholic</a> pedagogical standards and excelled in preparing students for higher education, both in Puerto Rico and the United States mainland. In every graduating senior class, Colegio Ponce\xc3\xb1o has consistently produced students who have gone on to become successful professionals in the fields of medicine, law, engineering, and business, among others. Among its alumni are many outstanding civic, political, sports, and religious leaders in Puerto Rican society.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Directors">Directors</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colegio_Ponce%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Directors"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<ul><li>Rev.
