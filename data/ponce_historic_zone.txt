-66.6139500</span></span></span></a></span></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Ponce Historic Zone</b> (<a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_Spanish" title="Puerto Rican Spanish">Spanish</a>: <i>Zona Hist\xc3\xb3rica de Ponce</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Historic_district" title="Historic district">historic district</a> in downtown <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">Ponce, Puerto Rico</a>, consisting of buildings, plazas and structures with distinctive architectures such as <a href="/wiki/Neocl%C3%A1sico_Isabelino" title="Neocl\xc3\xa1sico Isabelino">Neocl\xc3\xa1sico Isabelino</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Creole" title="Ponce Creole">Ponce Creole</a>, a local architectural style developed between the 19th- and early 20th-centuries.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The zone goes by various names, including <b>Traditional Ponce</b> (<i>Ponce Tradicional</i>),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <b>Central Ponce</b> (<i>Ponce Centro</i>),<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <b>Historic Ponce</b> (<i>Ponce Hist\xc3\xb3rico</i>),<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and <b>Ponce Historic District</b> (<i>Distrito Hist\xc3\xb3rico de Ponce</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Although not yet listed in the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>, the Ponce Historic Zone was added to the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Register_of_Historic_Sites_and_Zones" title="Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones">Puerto Rico Register of Historic Sites and Zones</a> (<i>Registro Nacional de Sitios y Zonas Hist\xc3\xb3ricas</i>) on February 2, 1989.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Location">Location</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_Historic_Zone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<p>The historic zone is located in what is commonly called <i>Ponce Pueblo</i> \xe2\x80\x93 the central downtown and oldest area of the city. While there are several roads that lead to it, the most common point of entry is via <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_1" title="Puerto Rico Highway 1">PR-1</a>, which becomes the Miguel Pou Boulevard, and then into the one-way Isabel Street, leading to the center of Ponce at the <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Delicias" title="Plaza Las Delicias">Plaza Las Delicias</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>In addition to Plaza Las Delicias, with its unique <a href="/wiki/Parque_de_Bombas" title="Parque de Bombas">Parque de Bombas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_la_Guadalupe_Cathedral" class="mw-redirect" title="Nuestra Se\xc3\xb1ora de la Guadalupe Cathedral">Nuestra Se\xc3\xb1ora de la Guadalupe Cathedral</a>, the zone includes landmarks such as <a href="/wiki/Ponce_City_Hall" title="Ponce City Hall">Ponce City Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armstrong-Poventud_Residence" class="mw-redirect" title="Armstrong-Poventud Residence">Armstrong-Poventud Residence</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ponce_High_School" title="Ponce High School">Ponce High School</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pante%C3%B3n_Nacional_Rom%C3%A1n_Baldorioty_de_Castro" title="Pante\xc3\xb3n Nacional Rom\xc3\xa1n Baldorioty de Castro">Pante\xc3\xb3n Nacional Rom\xc3\xa1n Baldorioty de Castro</a>. Numerous other attractions in this historic area are listed in the <a href="/wiki/NRHP" class="mw-redirect" title="NRHP">NRHP</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Banco_de_Ponce_(building)" title="Banco de Ponce (building)">Banco de Ponce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Casa_Paoli" title="Casa Paoli">Casa Paoli</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Casa_de_la_Masacre" class="mw-redirect" title="Casa de la Masacre">Casa de la Masacre</a>.  Others, such as <a href="/wiki/Teatro_Fox_Delicias" title="Teatro Fox Delicias">Teatro Fox Delicias</a>, <a href="/wiki/Teatro_La_Perla" title="Teatro La Perla">Teatro La Perla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Plaza_del_Mercado_de_Ponce" title="Plaza del Mercado de Ponce">Plaza de Mercado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hotel_Meli%C3%A1" title="Hotel Meli\xc3\xa1">Hotel Meli\xc3\xa1</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Paseo_Atocha" title="Paseo Atocha">Paseo Atocha</a> are not listed but possess significant historical value.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  Street corners in most of this zone have <a href="/wiki/Chamfer" title="Chamfer">chamfered</a> corners (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: esquinas de chafl\xc3\xa1n), typical of <a href="/wiki/Barcelona,_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Barcelona, Spain">Barcelona, Spain</a>.\n</p><p>A map of the area covered by the Ponce Historic Zone is available (in 2011) from the government of the municipality of Ponce.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span the origins of the Ponce Historic Zone to an event that took place on 8 June 1893. On that date, La Gaceta de Puerto Rico, the insular government\'s official periodical, published an edict of the Governor of Puerto Rico, Antonio Daban y Ramirez de Arellano, that mandated municipal authorities throughout the Island to divide, for fire control purposes, a town\'s urban center into three zones: stone-built, build with fire resistant materials, and built with combustible materials. No structure could be built, rebuilt or restored within a minimum of 50 meters from <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Las_Delicias" title="Plaza Las Delicias">the town\'s central square</a> unless it was stoned-built or it was to be upgraded to a stone-built structure.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> According to Rosario Natal, those were the roots of what almost 70 years later would be called the Ponce Historic Zone.\n</p><p>On 20 June 1960, governor <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Mar%C3%ADn" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn">Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn</a> amended the law regarding historic zones, making it possible for Ponce to be included in such category.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  On 6 June 1962, the Zone was officially designated as such, and initially included only the center core of the city, but it was later expanded to include a much larger area.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> On that date (6 June 1962) the <a href="/wiki/Instituto_de_Cultura_Puertorrique%C3%B1a" title="Instituto de Cultura Puertorrique\xc3\xb1a">Instituto de Cultura Puertorrique\xc3\xb1a</a>, with the concurrence of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Planning_Board" title="Puerto Rico Planning Board">Puerto Rico Planning Board</a>, approved a resolution creating the Zone. The Zone included a list of 22, mostly contiguous areas, including specific buildings, structures, plaza, streets and sectors that were to be preserved. It also included an area in <a href="/wiki/Barrio_Playa" class="mw-redirect" title="Barrio Playa">barrio Playa</a>, including the ruins of the old <a href="/wiki/Fuerte_de_San_Jos%C3%A9" title="Fuerte de San Jos\xc3\xa9">Fuerte de San Jos\xc3\xa9</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> \n</p><p>It was not long before the creation of the Zone was opposed by local developers and the real estate service industry. Ismaro Torruella, president of the Municipal Assembly, who was originally one of the supporters of the creation of the Zone, succumbed to mounting pressure from local developers, business people and realtors and, by 1962, now favored the elimination, or at least the limitation, of the Zone. "The evidence on this matter is clear. Torruella and his colleagues wanted to make sure the public hearing [of 10 March 1965] was controlled by them."<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Opposing groups sought to make their voices known to the townspeople at large, not just to the Municipal Government and the <a href="/wiki/Instituto_de_Cultura_Puertorrique%C3%B1a" title="Instituto de Cultura Puertorrique\xc3\xb1a">ICP</a>, and formed "Comite de Ciudadanos para el Progreso de Ponce" (Citizens Committee Ponce\'s Progress). This committee included prominent businessmen such as <a href="/wiki/Museo_de_la_Historia_de_Ponce" title="Museo de la Historia de Ponce">Juan Eugenio Candal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rovira_Biscuits_Corporation" title="Rovira Biscuits Corporation">Jose Maria Rovira</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armstrong-Poventud_Residence" class="mw-redirect" title="Armstrong-Poventud Residence">Gustavo Armstrong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Teodoro_Moscoso" title="Teodoro Moscoso">Jose Moscoso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_14" title="Puerto Rico Highway 14">Tito Castro</a>, among several others. To counter this group, supporters of the Historic Zone created their own "Comite Ponce\xc3\xb1o Pro Buen Progreso" (Ponce Citizens\' Committee for Fair Progress)." The debates went on for years with many public hearings taking place. Professional urban planning, traffic, and architectural studies, among others, also took place and recommendations were provided. One study categorized the architectural styles in the Historic Zone into seven groups: <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">Neoclassical European</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Colonial_architecture" title="Spanish Colonial architecture">Spanish Colonial</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ponce_Creole_architecture" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponce Creole architecture">Ponce Creole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vernacular_architecture" title="Vernacular architecture">Criollo Pueblerino</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Vernacular_architecture" title="Vernacular architecture">Criollo Residencial Pueblerino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">Neoclassical Creole</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">Neoclassical Superior</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>On 17 November 2005, then-Governor of Puerto Rico, <a href="/wiki/An%C3%ADbal_Acevedo_Vil%C3%A1" title="An\xc3\xadbal Acevedo Vil\xc3\xa1">An\xc3\xadbal Acevedo Vil\xc3\xa1</a>, signed Executive Order Number 72, approving the historic Ponce center as a Historic Center of First Order.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Plan_Ponce_en_Marcha"><i>Plan Ponce en Marcha</i></h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ponce_Historic_Zone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Plan Ponce en Marcha"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>\n<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:CasaPaoli_JuanLlanesSantosPRSHPO_pic2_MainFacade.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/CasaPaoli_JuanLlanesSantosPRSHPO_pic2_MainFacade.jpg/200px-CasaPaoli_JuanLlanesSantosPRSHPO_pic2_MainFacade.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" Paoli</a></figcaption></figure>\n<p>In the 1990s an intensive $440 million<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> revitalization project called "<i>Plan Ponce en Marcha</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> ("Ponce on the Move Plan") has increased the city\'s historic area from 260 to 1,046 buildings.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The <i>Ponce en Marcha</i> project was conceived in 1985 by then governor <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Hern%C3%A1ndez_Col%C3%B3n" title="Rafael Hern\xc3\xa1ndez Col\xc3\xb3n">Rafael Hern\xc3\xa1ndez Col\xc3\xb3n</a> during his second term in <a href="/wiki/La_Fortaleza" title="La Fortaleza">La Fortaleza</a>. A significant number of buildings in Ponce are listed in the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places_listings_in_southern_Puerto_Rico" title="National Register of Historic Places listings in southern Puerto Rico">National Register of Historic Places</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The nonprofit <i>Project for Public Places</i><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> listed the historic downtown Ponce city center as one of the <i>60 of the World\'s Great Places</i>, for its "graciously preserved showcase of Caribbean culture".<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The <i>Ponce en Marcha</i> project has given even more form to the definition, establishment, and development of the Ponce Historic Zone. The plan is the result of litigation between the Government of the Autonomous Municipality of Ponce and the Government of Puerto Rico.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p><p>The <i>Ponce en Marcha</i> plan projects involve several departments of the Government of Puerto Rico:<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>The Puerto Rico <i><a href="/wiki/Public_housing_in_Puerto_Rico" title="Public housing in Puerto Rico">Departamento de la Vivienda</a></i> must complete the "Riberas del Bucan\xc3\xa1 III" residential project, and rehabilitate housing in the Baldorioty, La Ponderosa, and Lomas de <a href="/wiki/Guaraguao" title="Guaraguao">Guaraguao</a> sectors. It must rehabilitate the homes in the Puerto Viejo sector in barrio <a href="/wiki/Playa_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Playa (Ponce)">Playa</a> plus 200 units of uninhabited lots citywide, restart the program "Tu Ciudad Renace", and demolish the housing project "Residencial Las Terrazas".</li>\n<li>The Puerto Rico <i><a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Transportation_and_Public_Works" title="Puerto Rico Department of Transportation and Public Works">Autoridad de Carreteras</a></i> must comply with the construction of the bridge at the intersection of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_2" title="Puerto Rico Highway 2">PR-2</a> and Punto Oro, the two projects required to complete the <i><a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_9" title="Puerto Rico Highway 9">Anillo de Circunvalaci\xc3\xb3n de Ponce</a></i>, and three bridges over Avenida Maruca.</li>\n<li>The Puerto Rico <i>Autoridad de Edificios P\xc3\xbablicos</i>, must swap land belonging to the Puerto Rico Police Marine Unit in exchange for improvements to "Parque Julio Gonz\xc3\xa1lez", and must complete the construction of the Ponce Headquarters of the Puerto Rico Police.</li>\n<li>The Puerto Rico <i><a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Electric_Power_Authority" title="Puerto Rico Electric Power Authority">Autoridad de Energ\xc3\xada El\xc3\xa9ctrica</a></i> must complete Phase III of the burial of <a href="/wiki/Aerial_cable" title="Aerial cable">aerial cabling</a> within the "Zona Hist\xc3\xb3rica", as well as Phase VI.</li>\n<li>The Puerto Rico <i><a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Aqueducts_and_Sewers_Authority" title="Puerto Rico Aqueducts and Sewers Authority">Autoridad de Acueductos Alcantarillados</a></i> must complete the design of the water filtration plant at barrio <a href="/wiki/Cerrillos_(Ponce)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cerrillos (Ponce)">Cerrillos</a>, the acquisition of land for its construction and its distribution network.</li>\n<li>The Puerto Rico <i><a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Ports_Authority" title="Puerto Rico Ports Authority">Autoridad de los Puertos</a></i> must expand the <a href="/wiki/Runway" title="Runway">runway</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taxiway" title="Taxiway">taxiway</a> at <a href="/wiki/Mercedita_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedita Airport">Mercedita Airport</a> and build the maintenance building.</li>\n<li>The Puerto Rico <i><a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Department_of_Natural_and_Environmental_Resources" title="Puerto Rico Department of Natural and Environmental Resources">Departamento de Recursos Naturales</a></i> must comply with the construction of the <a href="/wiki/Parque_Lineal_Veredas_del_Labrador" title="Parque Lineal Veredas del Labrador">Portugu\xc3\xa9s and Bucan\xc3\xa1 rivers lineal park</a>, as well as the canalization of the <a href="/wiki/Rio_Matilde" class="mw-redirect" title="Rio Matilde">Matilde</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rio_Pastillo" class="mw-redirect" title="Rio Pastillo">Pastillo</a> y <a href="/wiki/Rio_Canas" class="mw-redirect" title="Rio Canas">Canas</a> rivers and the development of the La Matilde Natural Reserve and the <a href="/wiki/Caja_de_Muertos_Light" title="Caja de Muertos Light">Caja de Muertos Light</a>.</li></ul>\n<div style="clear:both;" class=""></div>\n<div class="mw-heading within the historic zone are classified into four categories:<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>The first consists of structures "with a monumental historic value", and part of the city\'s heritage.</li>\n<li>The second is for those structures that while lacking a monumental historic value, possess some qualities of historic, architectural, or cultural interest.</li>\n<li>The third category includes those structures of contextual value. These are those structures that may not intrinsically possess historic, architectural, or cultural value individually, but do enhance the area when they are considered contextually, as part of a larger group, as part of urban characteristic, or as part of the architecture of a section of a street.</li>\n<li>And finally, category 4 is reserved for those structures that exist within the historic zone but whose value has not been assessed, that is, they are unclassified.</li></ul>\n<div style="clear:both;" class=""></div>\n<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 Delicias">Plaza Las Delicias</a>. This is the original historic zone, created in 1962.</li>\n<li>Second Order Zone \xe2\x80\x93 This is a zone that was added in 1989 further increasing the perimeter of the area around Plaza Las Delicias.</li>\n<li>Third Order Zone \xe2\x80\x93 This zone was added in 1992, and it covered the communities of Mariani, Belgica, and parts of Clausells, and Cantera.
